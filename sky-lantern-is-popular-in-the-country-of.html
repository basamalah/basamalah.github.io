25. How Does Sky lantern work? what is the Meaning or symbol of the sky lantern?​


Answer:

A sky lantern works like a hot air balloon. The candle flame bellow the shell warms up the air inside it, causing the lantern to float up and stay airborne until the candle is extinguished. Stiff edges near the bottom of the shell are meant to keep the flame away from the potentially flammable material.

26. Sky Lantern is popular in the Country of____?A.Brunei C.Thailand D.VietnamB.Batik​


C. Thailand

Yi Peng Sky Lantern Festival

The beliefs of the Lanna people are linked to the beginnings of sky lanterns in Thailand. On the full moon of the twelfth month, it is said that people must worship the Buddha relics, Phra That Kaew Chulamanee. The artifact, however, is kept on the heavens' topmost tiers. The Lanna people then utilized the sky lights to worship the Buddha relics during the festival in order for their prayers to reach heaven. A wonderful picture results when lanterns are ignited and launched into the night sky, filling it with sky lanterns. Both Thais and foreigners are familiar with this custom because they are enthralled by the Lantern Festival's singularity.

Further explanation

Sky Lanterns were created out of a desire to revere the relic of Lord Buddha that is housed in heaven. It is believed that setting off sky lanterns is the same as setting off ill luck. Let go of your life's unpleasant parts. The Lanna people consider burning Phang Pratheep to be a way of expressing appreciation to all of their supporters. They are appreciative of practically anything that helps them, including their protective walls and home. Enlightenment and wealth are likewise associated with Phang Pratheep's light. Local dwellings in Northern Thailand will have Phang Pratheep lights on during the event.

Similar principles underlie both the Loy Krathong custom in various regions of Thailand and the Yi Peng tradition. The Goddess Ganga's pardon is sought after as the major goal. And to worship the Lord Buddha, which involves reverencing the Lanna people's sacred objects.

The Lanna people typically substitute "Sapao" for Krathong. The Sapao is a modest, hand-built sailing vessel. At the temple, the locals would manufacture Sapao together. When done, they will put it, together with other offerings, on the bamboo raft. They transported the Sapao to the river in the evening of Yi Peng Day.
