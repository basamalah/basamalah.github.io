29. why is the human person an embodied spirit​


The human person is considered an embodied spirit because it is believed that humans are composed of body and soul.

Human Person as an Embodied Spirit

Each of us has an animating core known as the embodied spirit. It is widely acknowledged to be the impetus behind our actual thoughts, deeds, and words. The interconnections between our experiences and human feelings, emotions, and decisions are in fact reflections of who we are as individuals. When we hear the word "embodied," the common and well-known meaning that comes to mind is always "materialized" or "became flesh" (with physical form). Thus, when we refer to an "embodied spirit," one typically thinks of a spirit taking on physical form.The human person as an embodied spirit has spiritual (soul) and material dimensions (body).Human is made up of corporeal (body) and spiritual substance (soul). The body or the corporeal substance is the tangible aspect of humans. The external and internal senses and the material dimension are part of the corporeal substance. On the other hand, the spiritual substance or the soul is not visible. It is composed of the non-physical activities of humans such as thoughts and feelings, will, and conscience. The physical body is the material component of the human being. The human body comprises a complicated set of organs and biological systems used in performing acts and movements. The soul serves as the spiritual and energetic component of the human being. The soul governs the physical body, human mind, and psyche. A higher form of consciousness governs the soul.The concept of the spirit being embodied specifically refers to the unbreakable bond between the human body and soul in the context and viewpoints of Christian philosophy. Since the body and the soul are inseparable, they always work hand in hand. As a result, one may categorically state that "embodied spirit" refers to the belief that the soul and body are not distinct entities.
