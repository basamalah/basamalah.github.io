9. Determine the empirical and molecular formulas of refrigerant freon containing 9.6% C, 60.0% Cl and 30.4% F. The molecular mass of the compound is 120.919. (Show your solution)​


First, determine the number of moles of each element in the compound:

C: 0.096 mol

Cl: 0.600 mol

F: 0.304 mol

Next, determine the empirical formula by dividing each of the moles by the smallest amount of moles:

C: 0.096/0.096 = 1

Cl: 0.600/0.096 = 6.25

F: 0.304/0.096 = 3.16

Therefore, the empirical formula of refrigerant freon is CCl3F.

To determine the molecular formula, divide the molecular mass by the empirical formula mass:

120.919/84.966 = 1.42

Therefore, the molecular formula of refrigerant freon is CCl3F3.

Answer:

Potential energy = (mass) x (gravity) x (height above the reference)

Relative to the 70th floor, on which the refrigerator is standing,

PE = (75 kg) x (9.8 m/s²) x (0) = 0 joules .

Relative to the 35th floor, halfway up to where the fridge is standing,

PE = (75 kg) x (9.8 m/s²) x (150 m) = 110,250 joules .

Relative to the ground,

PE = (75 kg) x (9.8 m/s²) x (300 m) = 220,500 joules .

25. = Get the empirical and molecular formulas of a refrigerant whose mass is composed of 13.85% carbon, 41.89% chlorine, and 44.06% fluorine and has a molar mass of 315.1 mol​


Explanation:

First, calculate the moles of each element present in one mole of the refrigerant:

Carbon: 13.85 g C / (12.01 g/mol) = 1.157 mol C

Chlorine: 41.89 g Cl / (35.45 g/mol) = 1.185 mol Cl

Fluorine: 44.06 g F / (19.00 g/mol) = 2.315 mol F

Next, divide each of these values by the smallest one to get the empirical formula ratios:

Carbon: 1.157 mol / 1.157 mol = 1

Chlorine: 1.185 mol / 1.157 mol = 1.024

Fluorine: 2.315 mol / 1.157 mol = 2

So, the empirical formula is CCl2F2.

Finally, divide the molar mass of the refrigerant (315.1 g/mol) by the molar mass of the empirical formula (1 x 12.01 g/mol + 2 x 35.45 g/mol + 2 x 19.00 g/mol = 126.08 g/mol) to get the empirical formula mass ratio:

315.1 g/mol / 126.08 g/mol = 2.5

So, the molecular formula is (CCl2F2) * 2.5 = C5Cl10F10.
