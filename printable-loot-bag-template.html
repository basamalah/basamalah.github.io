13. For Trick or Teat. Leo bought 210 candies and 126 stickers. He must make the greatest numberof identical loot bags and he must use all the candies and stickers. How many candies will be ineach loot bags?​


Hi! It looks like your math teacher is staying with the them of the month which is Halloween! I hope you have fun learning at her class. Anyway, let's move on to the problem.

The problem calls for Leo to make loot bags with the equal amounts of candies and stickers each. All you need to do here is to express candies and stickers as a ratio or fraction.

[tex]\frac{210}{126}[/tex]

After that, you need to find the greatest common denominator so you can determine how many bags can be stuffed with equal number of contents.

You can do this by thinking of all the common factors of both numbers. Then multiply the common factors to get the GCF or Greatest Common Factor.

210  {2, 3, 5, 7}

126  {2, 3, 7}

GCF = 2 × 3 × 7

GCF = 42

As an extra step, if you want to find out how many candies and stickers each bag contains, find the reduced or simplest form of the fraction by dividing the numerator and denominator by the GCF.

[tex]\frac{210}{126}[/tex] ÷ [tex]\frac{42}{42}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{5}{3}[/tex]

So, there are 42 bags containing 5 candies and 3 stickers each.

30. A burglar drops a bag of loot fr a window hotel. The bag takes 0.15s to pass the 1.6m tall window of your room as it falls toward the ground. How far.above the top of the window is the burglar who dropped the bag?


Distance between top of the window and the burglar is about 5.0 m

[tex]\texttt{ }[/tex]

Further explanation

Given:

Height of windows = h = 1.6 m

Time Taken = t = 0.15 s

Gravitational Acceleration = g = 9.8 m/s²

Asked:

Distance between top of the window and the burglar = d = ?

Solution:

Firstly, we will calculate the initial speed of the bag at the top of the windows:

[tex]h = ut + \frac{1}{2}gt^2[/tex]

[tex]1.6 = u(0.15) + \frac{1}{2}(9.8)(0.15)^2[/tex]

[tex]1.6 - 0.11025 = u(0.15)[/tex]

[tex]u = 1.48975 \div 0.15[/tex]

[tex]u = \frac{5959}{600}[/tex]

[tex]u \approx 9.93 \texttt{ m/s}[/tex]

[tex]\texttt{ }[/tex]

Next , we could calculate the distance between top of the window and the burglar:

[tex]v^2 = u^2 + 2gd[/tex]

[tex]9.93^2 = 0^2 + 2(9.8)d[/tex]

[tex]d = 9.93^2 \div 19.6[/tex]

[tex]d \approx 5.0 \texttt{ m}[/tex]
