11. before creating infographic, define what is the meaning of infographic?​


Before creating an infographic, define what is the meaning of infographic?​

Infographics (a shortened term of "information" and "graphics") are graphic visual representations of data, facts, or knowledge that are intended to communicate information quickly and effectively. They can improve cognition by improving the ability of the human visual system to see patterns and trends in pictures. Data visualization, information visualization, statistical graphics, information design, and information architecture are all activities that are related. In recent years, infographics have evolved to be utilized for mass communication, and as a result, they are constructed with less assumptions about the audience's knowledge base than other types of visualizations. Isotypes are an early example of infographics that deliver information to the masses fast and simply.

Sure, here are some key points and events from Chapters 5-7 of "Life and Works of Rizal" that you might find useful for your infographic:

Chapter 5: Medical Studies at the University of Santo Tomas

- Rizal enrolled at the University of Santo Tomas to study medicine in 1877.

- He found the teaching methods there to be outdated and ineffective, and he clashed with some of his professors.

- He also witnessed the discrimination and mistreatment of Filipino students by Spanish friars and professors.

- Rizal also became involved in a literary and artistic circle called the "Ustetika" group, which included other talented students like Felix Hidalgo and Juan Luna.

Chapter 6: In Sunny Spain

- Rizal decided to continue his medical studies in Spain in 1882, partly to escape the oppressive environment in the Philippines.

- He arrived in Madrid and enrolled at the Universidad Central de Madrid, where he studied medicine, philosophy, and letters.

- Rizal also became involved in the Propaganda Movement, a group of Filipino expatriates who sought to raise awareness of the plight of Filipinos under Spanish colonial rule.

- Some of Rizal's most famous literary works were written during this time, including "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo."

Chapter 7: Paris to Berlin

- Rizal traveled to Paris in 1885 to study ophthalmology and to conduct historical research.

- He also became part of the Filipino community there, which included fellow Propagandists like Graciano Lopez Jaena and Marcelo H. del Pilar.

- Rizal moved to Germany in 1886 to continue his ophthalmology studies at the University of Heidelberg.

- While in Germany, he also conducted research on Philippine ethnography and collected specimens for the National Museum in Manila.

- Rizal returned to Paris in 1887, where he completed his second novel, "El Filibusterismo," and worked on other writing projects.
