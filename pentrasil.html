20. store on crushed ice. use drip pans to allow for drainage of melted ice. change ice daily. cover container or store in separate box away from other foods. whole fish should be drawn because entrails deteriorate rapidly. cut fish should be wrapped or left in original moisture proof wrap. explanation pls


Answer:

Crushed ice is commonly used to store perishable items, such as fish, because it helps to keep the food at a low temperature and slows down the growth of bacteria. Drip pans are placed underneath the container with the fish to catch any melted ice and prevent it from pooling and potentially contaminating the food. It is important to change the ice daily to ensure that it remains cold and to prevent the growth of bacteria. Whole fish should be "drawn" or gutted and cleaned before storing because the entrails, or internal organs, of the fish can decompose quickly and cause the fish to spoil. Cut fish should be wrapped in moisture-proof wrap or left in its original packaging to prevent drying out and to help maintain its freshness. It is also a good idea to store the fish in a separate container or box away from other foods to prevent cross-contamination.

Astute – means ‘shrewd and discerning, especially where personal benefit is to be derived’ (adj.). Example: Jane is an astute and I feel comfortable when listening to her advice.

Brawl – means ‘a noisy fight, especially in a public place’ (v. or n. depends) Example: Many people are watching the brawl and it’s too late when the guards came to stop it.

Exuberant – means ‘full of happy high spirits and vitality (adj.) Example: I’m always exuberant when it comes to wattpad, you know?

Infamous – means ‘having an extremely bad reputation‘ (adj.) Example: Dave is infamous for being a bully.

Hurtle – means ‘to move or travel at very high speed’ (v.) Example: I have no choice but to hurtle in order to escape.

Gaudy – means ‘brightly colored or showily decorated to an unpleasant or vulgar degree’ (adj.) Example: Paul’s work is gaudy and I don’t like it, it’s unpleasant for me.

Loath – means ‘unwilling or reluctant to do something’ (adj.) Example: Letting my classmates to copy my answers is loath to me, it’s their own grade anyway.

Nimble – means ‘agile, fast, and light in movement’ (adj.) Example: My Dad is kind of nimble that’s why it’s hard for me to defeat him in table tennis.

Perjury – means ‘the telling of a lie after having taken an oath to tell the truth’ (n.) Example: Even if I already know the truth, I asked Gen if she’s the one who took my scissors and I hate her of her perjury, she even swear that she’s telling the truth.

Perturb – means ‘to disturb and trouble somebody’ (v.) Example: I really hate it when someone perturbs me while I’m busy.

Subtle – means ‘slight and not obvious’ (adj.) Example: The beauty of Candice is subtle that’s why many believe she won’t win the pageant.

Void – means ‘having no legal force’ (adj.) Example: Arnel and his wife didn’t divorce yet so Arnel and Sofia’s, his current girlfriend, wedding is void.

Note: Always remember that English is so complicated. Words have a lot of meanings, it depends on the use if the sentence.

28. 1-5 step by step of cleaning fishplèase help, màhina ako sa ganito haha_____ Remôve the scales._____ Cût all the fins and tails._____ Remôve the entrails by making a slıt down the center of the sôft bèlly._____ Cût into pieces as required by the recipe_____ Rinse thoroughlyp.s sorry for the letters, it says it was forbidden but not sure which word is it​


Answer:

Step 1

The first cut should get rid of the head with one swift movement. Yeah, RIGHT, depending on the fish this may be a process in and of itself. The blade should run on a slight angle so that the side fins and the cartilage right behind the gills are removed. On most fish, the cartilage is located under the back edge of the gill plate that cover and protect the gills.

Step 2

The next step is to find the vent, and cut from there up to the front edge of the fish,

Vent

Cut made from vent toward the front of fish

Step 3

Then on smaller fish like this Bluegill, I like take my thumb and, starting at the vent, scrape out the guts as I move my thumb forward toward the front of the fish. Note the film like membrane that is left after the gut has been removed. I cut the membrane so you can see under it.

Thumb removing the guts

Film left after guts are removed

Hole poked in film for clarity

Step 4

The next step is to remove the membrane and clean the gunk out from behind it. I do not know the scientific name for the gunk, but there is a black substance along the backbone under another membrane that should be removed. Having running water is a bonus at this point because the black gunk is easier to remove under running water. If you leave traces of the black stuff, don’t fret. You will live. After it is cooked you won’t even know it is there.

Membrane and black gunk removed. Thumb clean

Rinsed out.

Step 5

The final step is to get rid of the “pretty.” On most fish, the bright colors are created by the scales. The scales need to come off. Most warm water species like sunfish and bass have large scales that are easy to remove by holding the knife horizontal like above but with the blade straight up and down. Then scraping from the tail toward the front of the fish. (You will want to do this outside where making a mess is NOT a problem. The scales will fly everywhere!) Cold water species like trout generally have very small scales that are hard to remove. It can be done, but the final product is less than desirable. Most people who I have fished with for these cold water species skin their fish leaving a nice piece of meat as a final product.

Scaled and rinsed.

This fish is gutted, scaled, and rinsed ready for the frying pan. A little egg, a little cornmeal or flour, and some olive oil…FISH FRY!
