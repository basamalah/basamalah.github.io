Work Immersion is usually considered an elective subject in Senior High School in Philippines.The school personnel designated to supervise the learners during the Work Immersion is the Work Immersion Coordinator/Supervisor.Work immersion is a program in the Philippines that is part of the Senior High School curriculum. It is designed to give students practical, hands-on experience in a real-world work environment, and to expose them to different career paths and industries. During the work immersion program, students are placed in companies, organizations or government offices, where they work as interns, shadowing employees and learning about different job functions and responsibilities.The main goal of work immersion is to provide students with opportunities to apply the knowledge and skills they have acquired in the classroom in a real-world setting. It also aims to expose students to different work environments and cultures, to help them develop their problem-solving, communication, teamwork and other employability skills, and to give them a better understanding of the world of work.A Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) is a legal binding document that is signed by the two parties entering into partnership before implementing a project or program. MOU outlines the terms and objectives of the partnership, but it is not as legally binding as a Memorandum of Agreement (MOA). It's a document that outlines the general terms of an agreement and sets out the basic obligations and expectations of each party.
