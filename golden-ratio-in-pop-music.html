11. 5 examples of golden ratio​


GOLDEN RATIO

-Also known as the golden section, golden mean, and divine proportion. It is approximately equal to 1.618 and occurs in many mathematical concepts.

EXAMPLES

1. Fibonacci Sequence

- The ratios of the numbers approach the value of the golden ratio. The higher the Fibonacci number, the closer their relationship is to 1.618.

2. Shells

Snail shells and nautilus shells follow the logarithmic spiral, just like the cochlea of the inner ear. It can also be seen in the horns of certain goats, and the shape of certain spider’s webs.

3. Typhoons/Hurricanes

- Golden ratio is prevalent in typhoons or hurricanes. Fibonacci spirals are also prevalent.

4. Flowers, plants, trees fruits, and vegetables

- Spiraling patterns can be found on pineapples and cauliflower. Fibonacci numbers are seen in the branching of trees or the number of leaves on a floral stem; numbers like 4 are not. 3’s and 5’s, however, are abundant in nature.

5. Spiral Galaxies

- Spiral galaxies are the most common galaxy shape. The Milky Way has several spiral arms, each of them a logarithmic spiral of about 12 degrees.
