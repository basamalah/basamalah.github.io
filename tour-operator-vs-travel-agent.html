What are travel intermediaries?

Travel intermediaries' main responsibility in the tourism industry is to offer customers travel-related goods and services. They buy them from vendors and resale them to tourists, whether they offer tours, tickets, seats on transit, lodging, or any other associated service.

One of the most important players in the tourist distribution chain is the travel intermediary. The travel and tourist sector is one of the fastest-growing industries globally, in part because of tourism intermediation. A distribution agent who engages in the sale and/or brokerage of goods and services associated with travel and tourism is the simplest definition of a travel intermediary.

The role of a travel intermediary is to operate as a go-between for suppliers and customers, purchasing and reselling travel-related goods and services such vacation packages, airline tickets, tours, lodging, and rental cars. The tourism distribution channel includes principals, wholesalers & aggregators, retailers, and consumers. Travel intermediates can function as middlemen between these companies, resulting in several stages in the supply chain.
