4. Kamatayan ni maria makiling​


Wala siyang kamatayan dahil isa lang siyang MYTHOLOGY dito satin.

Information:

Maria Makiling in Philippine mythology is a diwata (anito) or lambana (fairy) associated with Mount Makiling in Laguna, Philippines. She is the most widely known diwata in Philippine mythology and was venerated in pre-colonial Philippines as a goddess known as Dayang Masalanta or Dian Masalanta who was invoked to stop deluges, storms, and earthquakes.

Maria Makiling is the guardian spirit of the mountain, responsible for protecting its bounty and thus is also a benefactor for the townspeople who depend on the mountain's resources. In addition to being a guardian of the mountain, some legends also identify Laguna de Bay-and the fish caught from it-as part of her domain. She was sent by Bathala to aid the people of the area in their everyday life.

14. Ano ang meaning ng Tutok kalawit gang? ​


Answer:

On an opportune time, members of this gang usually hug or embrace a victim while discreetly poking a sharp object as if they were friends having some fun so as not to attract suspicion from passerby and thereafter divert him of his valuables. The area of operations, are dark streets, parks, mall parking area, bridges and passenger waiting areas.

Explanation:

Sa isang angkop na oras, ang mga miyembro ng gang na ito ay karaniwang yumayakap o yakapin ang isang biktima habang maingat na sinusundot ang isang matulis na bagay na para bang sila ay magkaibigan na nagkakaroon ng kasiyahan upang hindi makaakit ng hinala mula sa dumaan at pagkatapos ay mailipat siya sa kanyang mga mahahalagang bagay. Ang lugar ng pagpapatakbo, ay madilim na mga kalye, parke, lugar ng paradahan ng mall, mga tulay at lugar ng paghihintay ng mga pasahero.
