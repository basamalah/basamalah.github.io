What is a forehand push?

The forehand push is a defensive stroke, where you put your backspin on the ball.

When should you play a forehand push?

You're most likely to play a forehand push when the ball is backspinned, either from an opponent's side or  in the opening stages of a rally. The main reason you're using a forehand push is to make it difficult for youropponent to attack. For example, if your opponent serves a short backspin on your forehand, you  can play a short forehand push back or push back fast and long. Either option will make it difficult for  your opponent to play a powerful attack.

How do you play a forehand push?

1. Start with your bat on the side of your body, just below the height of your chest  

2. Turn your bat so that your playing side is facing upwards.  

3. As the ball approaches, bring your bat forward and bring it down to  

4. Brush under the ball – this is going to generate backspin  

5. Your bat is supposed to finish in front of you, pointing in the direction you want the ball to go.

29. in table tennis will the play continue on the serve if the ball touches the net? ​


Answer:

Yes, during a RALLY, if it touches the top of the net and then otherwise lands as a legitimate hit. BUT not when serving. If a served ball hits the net on the way over and otherwise legally bounces in play, it's a “let” serve and is done over. There is no limit on how many times this can happen.

Net Service Is In Play. A served ball that hits the net does not result in the service team losing its serve. The net serve is considered in-play, unless 1) it does not continue over the net or 2) lands outside the opposing team's court-side untouched by the opposing team.


30. a table tennis ball has diameter of 30mm what is the surface area of the table tennis ball?​


Answer:

5,024mm²

Step-by-step explanation:

Surface Area of the Sphere

The surface area of the sphere is the space accumulated by the curved surface of the sphere. In this particukar problem, we are asked to compute for the surface area of the table tennis ball which is a resemblance of a sphere. The formula for computing the surface area = 4πr². π has an equivalent value of 3.14. The radius is half of the diameter.

Answer:

5,024mm²

Solutions:

Asked: The surface area of the table tennis ball.

Given: d = 40 mm

Operation: division, multiplication

Number Sentence: d = 2r, S.A. = 4πr²

Solutions:

Find r.

d = 2r

40mm = 2r

=

20mm = r

Find the surface area.

S.A. = 4πr²

S.A. = (4)(3.14)(20mm)²

S.A. = (4)(3.14)(400mm²)

S.A. = (12.56)(400mm²)

S.A. = 5,024mm²

Answer:

Therefore, the surface area of the table tennis ball with diameter 40mm is 5,024mm².
