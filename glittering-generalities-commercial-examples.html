Just as name-calling words can be used to make a good idea seem bad, glittering generalities are used to make a bad idea seem good.

Patriotism. Justice. Truth. Hero. Science. Freedom.

These are all glittering generalities. These words sound great, but they mean different things to different people.

For example, consider the word democracy. This is a good word; an important word. But what does it mean? When somebody uses this word in conversation, we fall back on our personal understanding of the term, relying on ideas we learned from our parents, our teachers, our neighbors and our friends. We assume that the other person interprets the term in the same way that we do.

14. Give me an example of Glittering Generalities propaganda with explanation please​


Explanation:

A glittering generality is a type of propaganda technique that uses vague, emotionally-charged words and phrases to appeal to people's values and beliefs without providing any specific information or evidence. The words and phrases used in this technique are often broad and positive, such as "freedom," "democracy," "patriotism," and "the American way," and are meant to evoke a positive emotional response in the audience without providing any real information about the topic being discussed.

An example of glittering generalities propaganda can be seen in political campaigns. A political candidate may use phrases like "Make America great again" without providing any specific details about how they plan to achieve this goal or what it would mean for the country. This phrase is meant to evoke feelings of patriotism and nostalgia in the audience, while not providing any concrete information about the candidate's policies or plans.

Another example is a commercial of a cleaning product that says "Lets make your home spotless" , it doesn't specify how this product can clean your home or what makes it better than other products but it evokes a sense of cleanliness and order that triggers a positive emotional response to the audience.

It's important to be aware of the use of glittering generalities in propaganda and to critically evaluate the information being presented. It's important to look for specific and verifiable information, and to question the use of vague, emotionally-charged words and phrases that are not supported by evidence or facts.
