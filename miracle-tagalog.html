20. enumerate the categories of miracles​


Answer:

The miracles of Jesus are proposed miraculous deeds attributed to Jesus in Christian and Islamic texts. The majority are faith healings, exorcisms, resurrection, and control over nature.[1][2]

In the Synoptic Gospels (Mark, Matthew, and Luke), Jesus refuses to give a miraculous sign to prove his authority.[3] In the Gospel of John, Jesus is said to have performed seven miraculous signs that characterize his ministry, from changing water into wine at the start of his ministry to raising Lazarus from the dead at the end.[4]

For many Christians and Muslims, the miracles are actual historical events.[5][6][7] Others, including many liberal Christians, consider these stories to be figurative.[a] Since the Enlightenment, scholars have taken a highly skeptical approach to claims about miracles.[8]

25. explain the message it had been saved by a miracle -a miracle of faith and love​


Answer:

The phrase "it had been saved by a miracle - a miracle of faith and love" refers to the ability of anything or someone to be delivered from a trying or life-threatening circumstance. The term "miracle" implies that this redemption was unanticipated or improbable and that it can be attributed to a higher power or energy. The expression "miracle of faith and love" alludes to the fact that everyone involved's belief and concern contributed to the outcome, proving the strength of these two qualities to overcome even the most formidable obstacles. Overall, the message emphasizes the value of love and faith in helping us get beyond whatever challenges we may encounter in life.
