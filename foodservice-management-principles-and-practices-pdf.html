27. What are the classifications of foodservice?


Classifications of Food Service

Table Service - This is the type of food service where customers are served at a laid cover.

Assisted Service - The customers served part of the meal at a table and required to obtain part through self-service.

Self Service - The customers are required to help themselves from a buffet or counter.

Single point Service - The customers order, pay and receive the food at a counter.

Specialized Service - The food is taken to where the customer is.

The Process of Each Classification of Food Service

Table Service - In this food service, the customers enter in the area and is seated. The menu is given for orders. The orders are taken by the waiter. Then, the service is done using a laid cover on the table.

Assisted Service - The customers enter in the dining area and help themselves to the food, either from a buffet counter, or may get served partly at the table by waiter. The customers also collect any extras they need from the counter. Eating may be done on either at table, standing or in lounge area.

Self-service - This food service normally exists in canteens, cafeteria, colleges and hospitals. To facilitate quick service, the menu is fixed and displayed on large boards.

Single point Service - This can be take away, vending, food court and kiosk.

Specialized Service - This includes tray service in hospitals, hotels, aircraft, home delivery, room service and drive in.

WHAT IS FOOD SERVICE?

Food service or catering industry defines those businesses, institutions, and companies responsible for any meal prepared outside the home. This industry includes restaurants, school and hospital cafeterias, catering operations, and many other formats.

FACTORS IN DECIDING FOOD SERVICE BUSINESS:

According to the frequency of citation in the studies, the most important factors in the selection of fast-food restaurants were: 1) price; 2) speed of service and quality of the service and/or of the food; 3) hygiene; 4) taste of the food; 5) friendliness and behavior of the employees and convenience.

Other factors include:

Menu items. Use of convenience foods. Type of service. Quantity of meals and number of meal periods. Facility layout and design and production equipment. Work environment and number of hours worked.
