3. aerial plants in the philippines


Answer:

Aerial plants are plants that live in the air or wind in which the wind serves as the water of the plants. These aerial plants are found mostly in tropical regions or those regions near the equator. The three most common aerial plants in the Philippines are orchids, bromeliads and mangrove trees.

Explanation:

1. Orchids are perennial plants that are terrestrial or epiphytic herbs (growing on other plants that deeply rooted in the soil.

2. Bromeliads with colorful foliage and bromeliads with flowers. Bromeliads with foliage grow slowly but are more expensive while bromeliads with flowers grow faster and have variety of use.

3. Mangrove Trees are known as "rainforest of the sea" which are good source of alcohol, medicines and timber.

5. kind of terrestrial plant and aerial plants and aquatic plants​


Answer:

Terrestrial Plants

trees, shrubs, vines, grasses and herbaceous plants.

Aerial Plants

Sky plant,Spanish moss and Pink quill

Aquatic plants

Seaweeds,Water Lily and Water Lettuce

Explanation:

A terrestrial plant is a plant that grows on, in, or from land.

Aerial plants are plants that do not have underground root systems; instead, they are located in areas above the ground.

Aquatic plants are plants that have adapted to living in aquatic environments. They are also referred to as hydrophytes or macrophytes to distinguish them from algae and other microphytes. A macrophyte is a plant that grows in or near water and is either emergent, submergent, or floating.
