Difference between civil wedding and church wedding

Answer:

Civil wedding ceremony is solemnized by a mayor or a judge. It is held at the city hall or at the office of the judge. The solemnizing officer will lecture regarding the laws stated in Family Code of the Philippines and then bless the broom and the bride. there will be an exchange of vows by the couple and presentation of gifts offering like rings, symbolic item, flowers and money While, the church wedding ceremony is actually held at the church. The church wedding can be solemnize by a priest, pastor, and minister. there will also be an exchange of vows by the couple and presentation of gifts offering like rings, symbolic item, flowers and money. A church wedding is witnessed by many people, friends and family the same with the civil wedding, not unless the invited visitors are selected. Usually the invited visitors for the civil wedding are only few. Unlike church wedding, especially if it is held in the province, you have just noticed that the whole people in the Barangay even if the parents do not know their names, they will go to the house of the newly wed couple for dinner Therefore, the wedding in the province is more costly than the wedding held in the city which have only selected visitors.
