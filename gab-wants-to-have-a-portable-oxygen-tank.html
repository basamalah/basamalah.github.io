2. Gab wants to have a portable oxygen tank. a 5.00 liter oxygen gas exerts a pressure of 1.00 atmosphere. how much pressure is needed for this gas to be compressed in a 2.00 liter cylinder, provided there is no temperature change?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_1 = \text{1.00 atm} \\ & V_1 = \text{5.00 L} \\ & V_2 = \text{2.00 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final pressure by using Boyle's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1V_1 & = P_2V_2 \\ P_2V_2 & = P_1V_1 \\ \frac{P_2V_2}{V_2} & = \frac{P_1V_1}{V_2} \\ P_2 & = \frac{P_1V_1}{V_2} \\ & = \frac{(\text{1.00 atm})(\text{5.00 L})}{\text{2.00 L}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{2.50 atm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pressure needed for the gas to be compressed in a 2.00 liter cylinder is 2.50 atm, provided that there is no temperature change.

3. Gab wants to have a portable oxygen tank.A 5.00 liter or oxygen gas exerts a pressure of 1.00 atmosphere.How much pressure is needed for this gas to be compressed in a 2.00 liter cylinder, provided there is no temperature change?


Good Day

Problem:

Gab wants to have a portable oxygen tank.A 5.00 liter or oxygen gas exerts a pressure of 1.00 atmosphere.How much pressure is needed for this gas to be compressed in a 2.00 liter cylinder, provided there is no temperature change?

Given Data:

V₁ (initial volume) = 5 L

P₁ (initial Pressure) = 1 atm

V₂ (final volume) = 2 L

P₂ (final pressure) = ? unknown

Solution:

Boyle's law states that volume and pressure are inversely proportional if the temperature and the amount of gas are constant.

The formula of Boyle's Law is  P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

To solve for P₂,  we can use    P₂ = P₁V₁ /V₂

P₂ = P₁V₁ /V₂

     = 1 atm × 5 L / 2 L

     = 5 atm / 2

     = 2.5 atm

Answer:

The pressure needed is 2.5 atm

8. The gauge on a 15.0 L oxygen gas (O2) tank reads 4750 mmHg. How many liters of oxygen can you get from the tank at a pressure of 800 mmHg?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_1 = \text{4750 mmHg} \\ & V_1 = \text{15.0 L} \\ & P_2 = \text{800 mmHg} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final volume by using Boyle's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1V_1 & = P_2V_2 \\ P_2V_2 & = P_1V_1 \\ \frac{P_2V_2}{P_2} & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ V_2 & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ & = \frac{(\text{4750 mmHg})(\text{15.0 L})}{\text{800 mmHg}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{89.1 L}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 89.1 L of oxygen in the tank.

14. Gab wants to measure the pressure of his portable oxygen tank. Originally, the gas exerts a pressure of 1.02 atm in an unknown volume. When he increased the pressure into 2.5 atm, the volume of the gas becomes 2 L What is initial volume of the gas provided that the temperature remains unchanged?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_2 = \text{2.5 atm} \\ & V_2 = \text{2 L} \\ & P_1 = \text{1.02 atm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the initial volume by using Boyle's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_1 & = \frac{P_2V_2}{P_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{2.5 atm})(\text{2 L})}{\text{1.02 atm}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{4.9 L}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the initial volume of the gas is 4.9 L.

[tex]V_{1} = \text{52 L}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

25. An oxygen tank contains 52 L of oxygen having a pressure of 800atm. What would be the volume if the pressure is 500 atm and the temperature is to remain unchanged?​


(P1 x V1) / T1 = (P2 x V2) / T2

where P1 and V1 are the initial pressure and volume, T1 is the initial temperature, P2 and V2 are the final pressure and volume, and T2 is the final temperature (which is assumed to be constant in this problem).

Using the given values:

P1 = 800 atm

V1 = 52 L

T1 = constant

P2 = 500 atm

V2 = unknown

Plugging these values into the combined gas law equation:

(800 atm x 52 L) / T1 = (500 atm x V2) / T1

Simplifying the equation:

(800 atm x 52 L) = (500 atm x V2)

V2 = (800 atm x 52 L) / 500 atm

V2 = 83.2 L

Therefore, the volume of the oxygen tank would be 83.2 L if the pressure is reduced to 500 atm while keeping the temperature constant

Answer:

[tex]\begin{gathered}\begin{aligned} & P_1 = \text{800 atm} \\ & V_1 = \text{52 L} \\ & P_2 = \text{500 atm} \end{aligned}\end{gathered}[/tex]

Calculate the final volume by using Boyle's law.

[tex]\begin{gathered}\begin{aligned} P_1V_1 & = P_2V_2 \\ P_2V_2 & = P_1V_1 \\ \frac{P_2V_2}{P_2} & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ V_2 & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ & = \frac{(\text{800 atm})(\text{52 L})}{\text{500 atm}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{83.2 L}} \end{aligned}\end{gathered}[/tex]

So, the volume of oxygen in a tank would be 83.2 L.
