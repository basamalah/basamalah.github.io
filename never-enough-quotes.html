1. Read the quote below and explain what it means. "Failure will never overtake me if my determination to succeed is strong enough."​


Answer:

This quote means that if you are determined and committed to achieving success, you will not be overcome by failure. It suggests that even if you face challenges or setbacks, your determination to succeed will be strong enough to keep you moving forward.

The quote suggests that failure is inevitable and will happen to everyone at some point. However, it also suggests that how you respond to failure is what ultimately determines your success. If you are determined and committed to achieving your goals, you will not be defeated by failure, but rather use it as a learning opportunity and continue to strive for success.

Overall, the quote emphasizes the importance of having a strong determination to succeed and not letting failure hold you back. It encourages persistence and resilience in the face of challenges, and suggests that with a strong enough will to succeed, you can overcome any obstacle on the path to achieving your goals.

1.) Check that you’re using the correct wattage in all your fixtures and appliances.

Using the right bulbs can prevent electrical problems, so check all lamps, fixtures and appliances to ensure you’re using the correct wattage. If a light fixture has no wattage listed, use 60-watt bulbs or less. For unmarked ceiling fixtures, choose 25-watt bulbs.

2. Watch out for overloaded outlets to protect your home.

Overloading an electrical outlet is a common cause of electrical problems. Check all outlets to ensure they are cool to the touch, have protective faceplates and are in proper working order. According to ESFI, you can follow these electrical outlet safety tips:

Do not use extension cords or multi-outlet converters for appliances.

Only plug one heat-producing appliance into an outlet at a time.

Hot outlets should be checked by qualified electricians.

Remember that power strips only add outlets—they do not change the amount of power the outlet receives.

Smart plugs can be used to monitor outlet power loads and even shut off appliances should an outlet begin to overheat.
