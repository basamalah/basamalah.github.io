16. what is the ability of firewood?​


Answer:

Firewood is any wooden material that is gathered and used for fuel. Generally, firewood is not highly processed and is in some sort of recognizable log or branch form, compared to other forms of wood fuel like pellets or chips. Firewood can be seasoned (dry) or unseasoned (fresh/wet). It is generally classified as hardwood or softwood.

Firewood is a renewable resource. However, demand for this fuel can outpace its ability to regenerate on a local or regional level. Good forestry practices and improvements in devices that use firewood can improve local wood supplies.

24. what is the biodegradability of firewood


Answer and Explanation:

The biodegradability of firewood refers to how long a piece of wood can be expected to last before it starts to break down and decompose into soil. The longer the lifespan, the more solid and usable the wood is for building things like furniture or even houses.

While most pieces of firewood are biodegradable, other factors that affect their lifespan include how often they have been burned, as well as where they were harvested from.
