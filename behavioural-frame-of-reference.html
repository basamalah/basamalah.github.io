17. Positive, constructive, helpful social behavior is what psychologists refer to as affirmative behaviour.


Positive, constructive, and helpful social behavior is what psychologists refer to as prosocial behavior.

    Prosocial behavior is a type of behavior that is concerned with other people and has positive consequences for others. This behavior is characterized by positive and constructive actions aimed at helping others by lightening the physical or emotional burden. Examples of prosocial behavior are helping victims of natural disasters, helping people in need, and volunteering. Prosocial behavior is the opposite of anti-social behavior which can harm or cause damage to society.

    Meanwhile, affirmative behavior or affirmative action is more directed at policies or behaviors that aim to ensure that certain groups or classes get the same treatment.

Plasticity refers to changes in the brain that enable an organism to adapt its behavior in the face of changing environmental demands. The evolutionary role of plasticity is to provide the cognitive flexibility to learn from experiences, to monitor the world based on learned predictions, and adjust actions when these predictions are violated. Both progressive (myelination) and regressive (synaptic pruning) brain changes support this type of adaptation.
