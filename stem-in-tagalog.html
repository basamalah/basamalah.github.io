4. what are the distinguishing features of a young stem? of an old stem?


Answer:

The nodes bear leaves having axillary buds. The leaf bearing part of the stem or its branch is called shoot. Stem branches are exogenous in origin.

Young stems are green, soft and pliable, older stems are dark brown, hard and woody.

Distinguishing features of a young stem and an old stem

The shoot is the leaf-bearing portion of the stem or its offshoot. The genesis of stem branches is exogenous. Older stems are dark brown, stiff, and woody, whereas younger stems are green, soft, and flexible.

After the root has protruded, the embryonic plant's first rudiment of a young stem, or shoot, emerges from the seed. The terminal bud of the plant is the developing part at the apex of the shoot, and the stem grows in height as this bud and its neighboring tissues continue to develop.

To identify old stem simply count the amount of thick purple rings (bands) outside of the yellow pith to determine the age of this immature stem cross section. Do not count the center core of pith; just count the layers of woody growth (xylem tissue).

In botany, secondary growth is the growth that results from cell division in the cambia or lateral meristems and that causes the stems and roots to thicken, while primary growth is growth that occurs as a result of cell division at the tips of stems and roots, causing them to elongate, and gives rise to primary tissue.

Stem cells are special human cells that are able to develop into many different cell types. This can range from muscle cells to brain cells. In some cases, they can also fix damaged tissues.

In stem cell transplants, stem cells replace cells damaged by chemotherapy or disease or serve as a way for the donor's immune system to fight some types of cancer and blood-related diseases, such as leukemia, lymphoma, neuroblastoma and multiple myeloma. 16. Differentiate Anatomy of Monocot Stem from Eudicot Stem. ​


Answer:

The main difference between monocot stem and dicot stem is that monocot stem contains scattered vascular bundles across the stem whereas dicot stem contains vascular bundles arranged in the form of one or two rings. Monocot stem and dicot stem are the two types of stem structures in flowering plants.

Answer:
