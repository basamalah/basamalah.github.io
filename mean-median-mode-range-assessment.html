18. How to learn. Mean median mode and range


Mean - the 'average'; add the number then divide it to the number of the numbers; for example, we have 10 and 20, the average is 15 (10 + 20 = 30; 30/2 = 15)
Median - middle number; list the numbers, in order then simply find the mid number; if the number of numbers is even, no exact 'middle' just find it's mean or average for example you have 4, 6, 8, 9, the average of 6 and 8 is 7 so that's the answer.
Mode - it is the value the occurs most often; if no value repeated, therefore, there's no mode; 9, 10, 9, 8, 8, 9: mode is 9
Range - the difference between the highest value & lowest value; 9, 10, 9, 8, 9, 7: range is 3 (10 - 7 = 3)

21. Mean,Median,Mode, and RangeVocabulary to Definition ​


Answer:

Mean, median, and mode are three kinds of "averages". There are many "averages" in statistics, but these are, I think, the three most common, and are certainly the three you are most likely to encounter in your pre-statistics courses, if the topic comes up at all.

The "mean" is the "average" you're used to, where you add up all the numbers and then divide by the number of numbers. The "median" is the "middle" value in the list of numbers. To find the median, your numbers have to be listed in numerical order from smallest to largest, so you may have to rewrite your list before you can find the median. The "mode" is the value that occurs most often. If no number in the list is repeated, then there is no mode for the list.

What is the mean of 2, 5, 7 and 8?

2 + 5 + 7 + 10 = 24

24/4 = 6

The mean is 6

Median = The middle number of a set of numbers

For example:

What is the median of 7, 5, 3, 9 and 11?

Arrange the set of numbers in acsending order and get the middle number:

3, 5, 7, 9, 11

The middle number is 7

The median is 7

Mode = The most occurring number in a set of numbers

For example:

Range = the highest number - the lowest number

For example:

What is the range of the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 and 7?

Highest number = 7
