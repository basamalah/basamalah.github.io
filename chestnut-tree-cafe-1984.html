9. Mary, Peter, and Lucy were picking chestnuts. Mary picked twice as much chestnuts than Peter. Lucy picked 2 kg more than Peter. Together the three of them picked 26 kg of chestnuts. How many kilograms did each of them pick?please pasagot po now na.​


Answer:

Peter 6, Mary 12, Lucy 8, kg respectively.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be the amount Peter picked. Then Mary and Lucy picked 2x and x + 2, respectively. So:

[tex]x + 2x + x + 2 = 26[/tex]

[tex]4x = 24[/tex]

[tex]x = 6[/tex]

Therefore, Peter, Mary, and Lucy picked 6, 12, and 8 kg.

Of these phenotypes, only palominos never breed true. cremello palomino h 1/2 cremello 1/2 palomino chestnut palomino h 1/2 chestnut 1/2 palomino 1/4 chestnut palomino palomino h 1/2 palomino 1/4 cremello (a) From the results given above, determine the mode of inheritance by assigning gene symbols and indicating which genotypes yield which phenotypes.


Answer:

a) The mode of inheritance in this situation is Incomplete Dominance, a form of intermediate inheritance in which one allele for a specific trait is not completely expressed over its paired allele. This results in a third phenotype in which the expressed physical trait is a combination of the phenotypes of both alleles.

    Cremello = CC (dominant purebred)

    Palomino = Cc (never breed true)

    Chestnut = cc (recessive purebred)

b) In the first case (Cremello Palomino Horse), the progenies are 1/2 Cremello or 2 Cremello and 1/2 Palomino chestnut horses. The genotypes of these phenotypes are: 2 CC, and 2Cc. From the genetic cross of CC x Cc also.

Next, for the chestnut palomino horse, the offspring are 1/2 chestnut and 1/2 palomino. The genotypes of these phenotypes are: 2cc, and 2Cc. From the genetic cross of cc x Cc.

Lastly, for the Palomino Palomino, the outcomes are 1/4 chestnut palomino, 1/2 palomino, and 1/4 cremello. The genotypes of the these phenotypes are: 1cc, 2 Cc, and 1 CC. From the genetic cross of Cc x Cc.

28. Problem 2 Mary, Peter, and Lucy were picking chestnuts. Mary picked twice as much chestnuts than Peter. Lucy picked 2 kg more than Peter. Together the three of them picked 26 kg of chestnuts. How many kilograms did each of them pick?​


Question:

Mary, Peter, and Lucy were picking chestnuts. Mary picked twice as much chestnuts than Peter. Lucy picked 2 kg more than Peter. Together the three of them picked 26 kg of chestnuts. How many kilograms did each of them pick?

Solution:

Let xx be the amount Peter picked. Then Mary and Lucy picked 2x2x and x+2x+2, respectively. So x+2x+x+2=26x+2x+x+2=26

4x=244x=24

x=6x=6

Therefore, Peter, Mary, and Lucy picked 6, 12, and 8 kg, respectively.
