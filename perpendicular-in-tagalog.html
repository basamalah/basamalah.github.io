9. Perpendicular Construct a line perpendicular to line I that passes through A​


Answer:

To construct a line perpendicular to line I that passes through point A, you can follow these steps:

Draw line I and point A on a piece of paper.

From point A, draw a line segment that intersects line I at a 90-degree angle. This can be done by using a protractor or by using a compass to draw an arc that intersects line I at a 90-degree angle.

Label the point of intersection between the line segment and line I as point B.

Draw a line that passes through points A and B. This line will be perpendicular to line I and will pass through point A.

Label the point where the perpendicular line intersects line I as point C.

Double-check that the angle between the perpendicular line and line I at point C is 90 degrees.

All perpendicular lines form vertical angles. All vertical angles form perpendicular lines. All supplementary angles orm perpendicular lines. 9. All perpendicular lines are intersecting lines. 10. All intersecting lines are perpendicular lines.​


Answer:

1. True

2. True

3. True

4. False

5. False

explanation:

1. Right angles measure 90 degrees. Adjacent right angles would measure 180 degrees, making them supplementary.

2. Vertical angles are opposite angles formed by intersecting lines. Vertica; angles are always congruent according to the Vertical Angle Theorem

3. Linear pairs are pairs of adjacent angles formed by intersecting lines and are always supplementary since they form a straight angle.

4. Two perpendicular lines would always form four (4) right angles measuring 90 degrees

5. Linear pair sums up to 180 degrees. If one measures 90, the other must also measure 90 since 180-90 = 90 degrees.
