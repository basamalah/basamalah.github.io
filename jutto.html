18. what was the basic philosophical foundation of the plot in Junto al pasig? what conflicts were presented in the play?​


Answer:

"Junto al Pasig" is a play written by Filipino nationalist and author Jose Rizal, who is also known for his novels "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo." The play was first staged on March 1880 in Manila, Philippines.

The drama's key themes are national identity and colonial independence. The story highlights 19th-century Filipino cultural and political issues.

This story follows Juan, a young Filipino guy who went home from Europe after finishing his study. Juan's travels in Europe have given him a new perspective on his native country, causing him to question the Philippines' cultural and political standards.

Juan's issue stems from his desire to promote innovation and progress in his country while respecting his people's culture. His affection for Julia, the daughter of a wealthy Spanish priest, exacerbates tensions. Julia's father, Don Custodio, represents colonial power and is corrupt and authoritarian throughout the novel.

Juan and Julia's love story is intertwined with Filipino political and cultural conflicts throughout the play. The drama shows the clash between colonizers and colonized and between progress and tradition.

"Junto al Pasig," a play summary, reflects Rizal's philosophy of Filipino nationalism and liberation from colonialism. The play tackles contradictions between tradition and growth, colonial oppression, and progress.

The fundamental intellectual underpinnings of the drama are based on the idea of national identity and the fight for freedom from colonialism. Rizal utilizes the narrative to bring attention to the cultural and political tensions that were present in the Philippines in the 19th century.

Juan, a young Filipino man who has just returned to his native country after completing his education in Europe, serves as the central protagonist of this drama. Juan's travels in Europe have provided him with a fresh viewpoint on his home country, and as a result, he is starting to have second thoughts about the cultural and political norms that he observes back home in the Philippines.

Juan's dilemma results from his conflicting goals of fostering innovation and forward movement in his nation while also honoring the cultural traditions of his people. His love for Julia, who is the daughter of a rich Spanish priest, only serves to exacerbate the tensions that already exist. Don Custodio, Julia's father, is a symbol of the colonial authority and is presented as a corrupt and repressive man throughout the story.

During the whole of the play, the love story of Juan and Julia is entangled with the political and cultural battles that are taking place in the Philippines. The play depicts the conflict that arises between those who have been colonized and those who do the colonizing, as well as the fight that exists between development and tradition.

Rizal's philosophical underpinning of Filipino nationalism and the battle for independence against colonialism is reflected in the play "Junto al Pasig," which is a synopsis of the play. The drama explores a number of different tensions, including the conflict between tradition and development, the battle against repressive colonial authority, and the conflict between tradition and progress.

26. What are the moral or ethical values being fostered in Junto Al pasig


Answer:

The moral and ethical values being fostered in Junto Al Pasig are respect, responsibility, integrity, compassion, and collaboration. Respect for the environment and for each other is emphasized through activities such as tree planting and community clean-ups. Responsibility is encouraged through initiatives such as waste management programs that help reduce pollution in the area. Integrity is promoted by encouraging people to be honest in their dealings with each other and with the environment. Compassion is fostered by providing assistance to those who are less fortunate or who have been affected by natural disasters. Finally, collaboration between different stakeholders is encouraged to ensure that everyone has a voice in decision-making processes that affect their lives.
