4. A scuba diver’s tank contains 2.50 kg of oxygen gas compressed into a volume of 11.0L. Calculate the gas pressure inside the tank at 10℃


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V = \text{11.0 L} \\ & mass = \text{2.50 kg = 2500 g} \\ & MM = \text{31.998 g/mol} \\ & T = 10^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{283.15 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of the gas.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{mass}{MM} \\ & = \frac{\text{2500 g}}{\text{31.998 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{78.13 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Solve for the pressure of the gas by using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P & = \frac{nRT}{V} \\ & = \frac{(\text{78.13 mol})\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{283.15 K})}{\text{11.0 L}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{165 atm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the gas pressure inside the tank is 165 atm.

Problem:

calculate the final pressure inside a scuba tank after it cools from 1.00 x 10³ °c to 25.0°c.The Initial pressure in the tank is 130.0 atm.

Note:

Kelvin temperature should be used in solving gas laws.

Kelvin = degree celsius + 273.15

Kelvin = 25.0°C  + 273.15 = 298.15 K

Convert 1.00 × 10^3 to decimal notation which is 1000 °C

Kelvin = 1000 °C  + 273.15  =  1273.15 K

Given Data:

P1 (initial pressure) = 130.0 atm

T1 (initial temperature) = 1000 °C  or  1273.15 K

P2 (final pressure) = unknown ?

T2 (final temperature) = 25.0°C  or 298.15 K

Solution:

This problem can be solve using Gay-Lussac's Law with the formula

P1/T1 = P2/T2 amd to solve for P2, we can use P2 = P1T2/T1

P2 = P1T2/T1

      = 130.0 atm × 298.15 K  / 1273.15 K   (cancel the unit kelvin)

      =  38759.5 atm  / 1273.15 K

      = 30.44 atm

Answer:

Pressure is 30.44 atm  (when temperature decreases, pressure decreases)

15. A 10-l tank of helium filled to a pressure of 150 atm. how many 1.50 l balloons can be inflated to a pressure of 1.00 atm from the tank


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_1 = \text{150 atm} \\ & V_1 = \text{10 L} \\ & P_2 = \text{1.00 atm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final volume by using Boyle's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1V_1 & = P_2V_2 \\ P_2V_2 & = P_1V_1 \\ \frac{P_2V_2}{P_2} & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ V_2 & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ & = \frac{(\text{150 atm})(\text{10 L})}{\text{1.00 atm}} \\ & = \text{1500 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of 1.50 L balloons that can be inflated.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{number of 1.50 L balloons} & = \frac{\text{1500 L}}{\text{1.50 L/balloon}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{1000 balloons}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 1000 balloons can be inflated to a pressure of 1.00 atm.

[tex]\begin{gathered} \begin{gathered}\begin{array}{|c|c | }\hline \bold{Initial \: Conditions} & \bold{Final \: Conditions}\\ \hline \bold{V_1 = 48.5 \: L} & \bold{ V_2 = \: ?} \\ \hline \bold{ P_1 = 57.3\:mmHg} & \bold{ P_2 = 45.4\:mmHg} \\ \hline \end{array}\end{gathered} \\ \end{gathered}[/tex]
