26. there is a bell at the top of a tower that is 50 m high.The bell weighs 190N.The bell has energy.calculate it.​


The energy of the bell is 9500 Joule.

From the question, we got the data:

w (weigh) : 190 Newton

h (height) : 50 m

After we get the data, we can use it in the formula for potential energy:

PE = m x g x h

Since the question already gives the weight value (w = m x g), we can eliminate the "m" and "g" in the formula.

PE = w x h

PE = 190 Newton x 50 m

    = 9500 Joule

WHAT IS POTENTIAL ENERGY?

Potential energy is the energy that is stored in an object as a result of its position. For example, the coconut fruit that is still in its tree at a certain elevated, the coconut will have the potential energy.

The equation for the potential energy is:

Potential Energy = m x g x h

In this equation, "m" represents the mass of the object, "h" represents the height of the object's position, and "g" represents the gravitational field strength (9.8N/kg or 10N/kg).
