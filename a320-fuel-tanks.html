24. The pressure at the top of a tank of jet fuel is 180.6 kN/m2. The depth of the liquid in the 'ank is 6.4 m. The density of the fuel is 825 kg/m³​


Answer:

We can use the formula for pressure due to a fluid:

pressure = density x gravity x height

where:

density = 825 kg/m³ (density of jet fuel)

gravity = 9.81 m/s² (acceleration due to gravity)

height = 6.4 m (depth of liquid)

So, we have:

pressure = 825 kg/m³ x 9.81 m/s² x 6.4 m

pressure = 51,361.2 N/m² = 51.36 kN/m²

The pressure at the top of the tank is given as 180.6 kN/m². So, the gauge pressure (pressure above atmospheric pressure) is:

gauge pressure = 180.6 kN/m² - 51.36 kN/m²

gauge pressure = 129.24 kN/m²

Therefore, the gauge pressure at the bottom of the tank (where the depth of liquid is highest) is 129.24 kN/m².
