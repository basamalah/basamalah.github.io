13. The hair in the nose trap the dust particles in the air you inhaleExcretionorFiltration​


The hair on your nose traps dust particles in the air you breathe is filtration.

The nose hair or cilia is a part of the nose structure.

The main function of nose hairs is to prevent dust particles, pollen, and allergens from entering the lungs. In the filtration process, nose hairs work together with the mucus in the nose to become the body's natural barrier against dirt and dust from the air.

Meanwhile, the term excretion in the human respiratory system is the function of the lungs to remove residual gases from the inhalation process, namely CO2 and H2O. This gas will then be expelled through the nose and replaced with oxygen which will be inhaled through a process called inhalation.
