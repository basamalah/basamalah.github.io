22. description of conjugation​


Explanation:

Conjugation is the process by which one bacterium transfers genetic material to another through direct contact. During conjugation, one bacterium serves as the donor of the genetic material, and the other serves as the recipient. The donor bacterium carries a DNA sequence called the fertility factor, or F-factor.

28. differentiate simple and conjugated protein​


Answer:

Simple proteins are made up of amino acid subunits joined together by peptide bonds. ... Examples of simple proteins include albumins, globulins, glutelins and albuminoids. Conjugated proteins are complex proteins composed of simple proteins and a non-protein substance referred to as a prosthetic group or cofactor.

Explanation:

Answer:

Simple proteins are made up of amino acid subunits joined together by peptide bonds. When hydrolyzed by enzymes, simple proteins yield only the amino acids from which they are comprised of.

While Conjugated proteins are complex proteins composed of simple proteins and a non-protein substance referred to as a prosthetic group or cofactor. Most prosthetic groups or cofactors are small organic molecules formed from vitamins.
