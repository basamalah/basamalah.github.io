2. What is pavement marking? Enumerate and discuss the different types of pavement markings and markers,


Pavement marking is the application of various materials or substances on a road surface to provide information, guidance, and warning to road users. Pavement markings are essential for maintaining safety and order on the road network. There are different types of pavement markings, and they serve different purposes. Below are the most common types of pavement markings:

1. Longitudinal markings: These are markings that run parallel to the direction of traffic flow. They include centerlines, edge lines, and lane lines. Centerlines divide the road into two-way traffic, while edge lines define the outer limits of the roadway. Lane lines separate traffic lanes and indicate the direction of travel.

2. Transverse markings: These markings run perpendicular to the direction of traffic flow. They include stop lines, crosswalks, and yield lines. Stop lines indicate where vehicles should stop at intersections and crosswalks. Crosswalks indicate where pedestrians should cross the road, while yield lines indicate where drivers should yield to other traffic.

3. Symbols: These are markings that convey specific messages to road users. They include arrows, chevrons, and bicycle symbols. Arrows indicate the direction of travel, while chevrons indicate a change in the direction of the road. Bicycle symbols indicate where bicycles should travel on the road.

4. Raised pavement markers: These are small raised devices that are installed on the road surface to provide tactile and visual information to road users. They are commonly used to indicate lane boundaries, centerlines, edge lines, and other markings.

5. Colored pavement: Some roads have colored pavement to indicate specific areas or functions, such as bus lanes, bike lanes, or pedestrian areas.

In summary, pavement markings and markers are important tools for maintaining safety and order on the road network. They provide information, guidance, and warning to road users, and they come in different types and forms, including longitudinal and transverse markings, symbols, raised pavement markers, and colored pavement.

Michael said that the pavement is a square with dimensions of (a+6)(a+6) ft. while Mark argued that it is a rectangular with dimensions (a+12)(a+3) ft. Who among the two made the correct conclusion? defend your answer​


You are given two options to answer.

Answer 1:

By investigating whose two arguments is true, let's multiply the two factors (dimensions) to find the product (area). Using FOIL method aids us on how to multiply the algebraic expressions.

From Michael's statement, find the area:

[tex]\sf{}(a+6)(a+6)=a^2+6a+6a+6(6)\\\text{}\hspace{60pt}=a^2+12a+36[/tex]

Whereas from Mark's statement, find also the area:

[tex]\sf{}(a+12)(a+3)=a^2+12a+3a+12(3)\\\text{}\hspace{65pt}=a^2+15a+36[/tex]

Therefore, we conclude that Michael's statement is true.

Answer 2:

By investigating whose two arguments is true, take the polynomial of the area by factoring. The given area is a trinomial of the quadratic expression. To factor this trinomial into two binomials, let's find two numbers that satisfy two given conditions:

From a²+12a+36, the product of these two numbers is equal to the constant term (last number) which is 36; and, the sum of these two numbers is equal to the coefficient of the linear term which is 12.

In this case, 6+6=12 and 6x6=36. Thus,

a²+12a+36 = (a+6)(a+6)

Therefore, we conclude that Michael's statement is true.
