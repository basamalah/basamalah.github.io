9. distinguish between and among informative, journalistic,and literary writing


Answer:

Informative-The purpose of an informative essay, sometimes called expository essay, is to educate on a certain topic. It is not for giving an opinion or convincing someone to do something or change his beliefs.

Journalistic Writing- Although every outlet is different, mass media gatekeepers have traditionally relied on some predictable values to evaluate the newsworthiness of a story. Their decision might impact how the story is covered, including how many resources are spent following the story, and how prominently the story is featured.

Literary Writing- the term “literary Writing” calls to mind works by writers such as Shakespeare, Milton, or Wordsworth. Literary works are primarily distinguishable from other pieces of writing by their creative, or artistic intent.

29. what is the difference of journalistic, literary, and informative writing


• Journalistic Writing is a great way to inform, to persuade, and to entertain your readers. It allows you to explore outside the realm of traditional essay writing by incorporating direct quotations, and your own choice of diction.
• Literary Writing is defined as creating new creative work, such as poems or novels, and compilations or volumes of creative work. 
• Informative Writing educates your reader on a topic. They can have one of several functions: to define a term, compare and contrast something, analyze data, or provide a how-to. They do not, however, present an opinion or try to persuade your reader.
