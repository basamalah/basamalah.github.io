What is religious Festival?

Religious festivals are celebrations that have a significant religious or spiritual meaning to a particular group of people or community. These festivals are usually held in honor of a religious figure or event, and often involve specific rituals, prayers, or other forms of worship. Religious festivals can also serve as an opportunity for people to gather together and celebrate their faith, as well as to connect with their cultural heritage and traditions.

What is non-religious Festival?

Non-religious festivals are celebrations that do not have a religious or spiritual basis. These festivals often mark cultural, social, or historical events, and are usually secular in nature.

18. Religious festival different secular festival


Answer:

In theory, one contains religious rituals, prayers, or motivations, while the other does not. However, the distinction can be blurry; a holiday or festival can be religious for some people while secular/cultural for others. For example, although Halloween originally had a religious dimension, it is currently fairly widely divorced from that origin and is widely perceived as secular; at the same time, because of that religious origin, there are some in the Jewish community who are not comfortable participating in it since they still perceive it to be religious, while many other Jews do not have this objection, seeing it as merely cultural. The same can be said of Diwali and Hannukah; while there are religious dimensions and, for many people, it is a religious holiday, there are also people whose primary motivation is cultural and secular.
