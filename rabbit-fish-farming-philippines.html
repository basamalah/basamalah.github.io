LEGS==============================Question:

There is a total of 16 chicken and rabbits in a farm. The total number of legs (chicken and rabbits) is equal to 50. how many chickens and how many rabbits are there?

Answer:

[tex] \implies \large \sf \orange{7 \: chickens}[/tex]

[tex] \implies \large \sf \orange{9\: rabbits}[/tex]

==============================

Solve:

Let c be the number of chickens and r for rabbits, with a total of 16 animals and 50 legs. Let c multiplied to two because chickens have two legs and four for rabbits.

[tex] \begin{cases} \sf \large c + r = 16 \tt \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: ( \red{eq.1})\\ \sf \large c(2) + r(4) = 50 \: \: \: \! \tt (\red{eq.2})\end{cases} \:[/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

Find the the equation of c or r, but I choose chickens. Use the equation 1 for transposing and equation 2 for substituting.

[tex] \begin{cases}\sf \large c = 16 - r \\ \sf \large(16 - r)(2) + r(4) = 50 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

Solve for the second equation.

[tex] \implies \large\sf(16 - r)(2) + r(4) = 50[/tex]

[tex] \implies \large\sf32- 2r + 4r = 50[/tex]

[tex]\implies \large\sf32 + 2r= 50[/tex]

[tex]\implies \large\sf 2r= 50 - 32[/tex]

[tex]\implies \large \sf 2r= 18[/tex]

[tex]\implies \large \sf \frac{ \cancel2r}{ \cancel2} = \frac{18}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\implies \large \therefore \boxed{\sf \orange{r = 9}}[/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

So there are 9 rabbits on the farm, find the number of chickens by using equation 1.

[tex] \implies \large \sf{c} = 16 - 9[/tex]

[tex]\implies \large \therefore \boxed{\sf \orange{c = 7}}[/tex]

==============================
