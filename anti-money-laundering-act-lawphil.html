1. why anti-money laundering act was entacted​


Answer:

To protect and preserve the integrity and confidentiality of bank accounts and to ensure that the Philippines shall not be used as a money laundering site for the proceeds of any unlawful activity.

Money laundering is a process by which illegally acquired money, or “dirty money,” is made to appear legal, or “clean,” so that criminals can freely spend it. Examples of dirty money might include proceeds from financial crimes, drug trafficking or other illicit activities.

24. Ano ang layunin ng Anti-money laundering law?​ at paano ipinapatupad ang batas na ito?


Malaking reporma sa mga financial institutions lalung-lalo na sa mga banko ang pagkakasa-batas ng Anti-Money Laundering Act of 2001. Sa pamamagitan ng batas na ito, magiging mas madali ang pagtuklas sa mga ilegal na gawain at ang pagkakamal ng salapi sa labag na paraan.

Ang impeachment trial kay dating President Estrada ay nag-iwan ng bahid sa ilang lokal na banko na nagamit sa kahina-hinalang transaksiyon. Napaikot ang malaking halaga ng salapi sa pagitan ng ilang tao at nagawang isantabi ang mga regulasyon ng banko.

Ang Anti-Money Laundering Law ay naglalayong protektahan ang confidentiality ng mga deposito sa banko at ang pangangalaga sa reputasyon ng Pilipinas bilang isang bansang hindi kumakalinga sa mga illegal na gawain.

Sa probisyon ng batas, ang mga depositong nagkakahalaga ng P4,000,000 ay mamatyagang mabuti at maaaring ipag-utos na makumpiska pabor sa gobyerno kung hindi makapagbibigay ng mabuting paliwanag ang may-ari kung saan nanggaling ang ganitong halaga.

Inaasahang sa pagpapatupad ng Anti-Money Laundering Law, lalong mapatatatag ang integridad at reputasyon ng mga banko bilang patunay ng patuloy na pagpapatupad ng mga reporma sa paglilinis ng pamahalaan at ekonomiya.

28. How anti money laundering and SALN protect the country from corrupt practices? Did it prevent the people graft and corruption?​


Answer:

Explanation:

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) laws and the Statement of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth (SALN) are both important tools for combating corruption and preventing the flow of illicit funds in the country. However, their effectiveness in preventing graft and corruption depends on a number of factors, including the level of enforcement, the strength of the legal system, and the commitment of public officials to transparency and accountability.

Moreover, corruption is often deeply entrenched in political systems and can involve complex networks of actors, making it difficult to detect and prosecute. It is important to ensure that AML laws and SALN requirements are accompanied by strong legal and institutional frameworks, as well as a culture of transparency and accountability in government and society as a whole, to effectively combat corruption and prevent the flow of illicit funds.

SEC. 14. Penal Provisions. — (a) Penalties for the Crime of Money Laundering. The penalty of imprisonment ranging from seven (7) to fourteen (14) years and a fine of not less than Three million Philippine pesos (Php3,000,000.00) but not more than twice the value of the monetary instrument or property involved in the offense, shall be imposed upon a person convicted under Section 4(a) of this Act.
