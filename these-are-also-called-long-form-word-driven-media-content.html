The information in this problem is incomplete. You need to know how long at least a part of the pole is. You know that the part of the pole driven into the ground is 4/15

[tex] \frac{15}{15} - \frac{11}{15} = \frac{4}{15} [/tex]

However , you have no idea of how long the total length of the pole is nor any clue to lead you to it. If for example you knew the pole's total length was 30 cm then you could say 30 divided by 15 is 2 and since 2 is now equal to 1/15, 2 times 4 is equal to 8 cm, making 8 equal to 4/15 or the length of the pole driven into the ground.

11. 18. A 9-meter pole is driven into the ground so that 5/6 of the pole is above the ground. How long is the part of the pole driven into the ground?​


Answer:

The length of the part of the pole driven into the ground can be found by subtracting the length of the part above the ground from the total length of the pole.

Since 5/6 of the pole is above the ground, then 1/6 of the pole is below the ground.

The length below the ground is 9 meters x (1/6) = 9/6 = 1.5 meters.
