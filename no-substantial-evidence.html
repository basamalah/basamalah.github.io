1. Issue: Reviving death penalty in the Philippines Proposition premise (PRP) Substantial supporting Evidence 1. 2. 3. Opposition premise (ANTI) Substantial Supporting Evidence 1. 2. 3.


Proposition Premise (PRP): Reviving the death penalty in the Philippines will act as a deterrent for crime, leading to a decrease in criminal activity.

Substantial Supporting Evidence:

Countries with the death penalty have lower crime rates than those without it.The death penalty can serve as a powerful deterrent for those considering committing heinous crimes.Reviving the death penalty can provide closure for victims' families and bring justice for the victims of heinous crimes.

Opposition Premise (ANTI): Reviving the death penalty in the Philippines will not effectively reduce crime, and there are other ways to address the problem.

Substantial Supporting Evidence:

Studies have shown that the death penalty is not an effective deterrent for crime, and that the threat of harsher punishment does not reduce criminal activity.The death penalty is prone to error, and innocent people may be executed.Other methods, such as addressing poverty and inequality, improving access to education and healthcare, and increasing police presence, may be more effective in reducing crime.
