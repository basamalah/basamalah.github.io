It fits with the so-called ASI approach, based on Avoid trips, Shift modes, and Improve technologies.

First of all, I'd try not to expand road infrastructure, since it is known to encourage private transport trips, worsening congestion.

Then, I'd apply the ASI approach. «Avoid» implies to rethink if current transport demand is needed. New technologies may help to reduce unnecessary mobility, such as tele-working, on-line shopping, etc., whereas an optimisation of closeness between inhabitants and production and leisure could also be adopted.

«Shift» implies a change from unsustainable transport to more environmental-friendly transport systems, such as walking and cycling, or public transport. First of all, cycling and bus infrastructures would need expansion. Secondly, tax distortions and unjustified subsidies should be eliminated, whereas urban road pricing schemes could be applied in order to encourage more sustainable transport choices.
