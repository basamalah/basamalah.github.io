26. A cargo container is 5 m wide, 34 m long, and 6 m tall. What is the volume of cargo container?


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

[tex]\underline{\sf\dag{\underline{Given}}:}[/tex]

[tex]\sf Length_{(cargo\:container)} = \purple{34\:m}[/tex]

[tex]\sf Breadth_{(cargo\:container)} = \purple{5\:m}[/tex]

[tex]\sf Height_{(cargo\:container)} = \purple{6\:m}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\sf\dag{\underline{To\:Find}}:}[/tex]

[tex]\sf Volume_{(cargo\:container)} =\:?[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\sf\dag{\underline{Solution}}:}[/tex]

Here, we have dimensions of a cargo container, which is cuboidal in shape. To find out it's volume we use formula of volume of cuboid,i.e, l × b × h , where l = length , b = breadth and h = height.

[tex]\leadsto\quad\sf Volume_{(cargo\:container)} = (l\:\times\:b\:\times\:h)\:m^3[/tex]

★ Substituting all known values :-

[tex]\leadsto\quad\sf Volume_{(cargo\:container)} = (34\:\times\:5\:\times\:6)\:m^3[/tex]

[tex]\leadsto\quad\sf \pink{ Volume_{(cargo\:container)} = 1020\:m^3}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\sf\dag{\underline{More\:to\:know}}:}[/tex]

★ Volume formulae of other shapes :-

[tex]\quad\leadsto\quad\tt Volume_{(cube)} = \red{(Edge)^3} [/tex]

[tex]\quad\leadsto\quad\tt Volume_{(cylinder)} = \red{\pi r^2 h} [/tex]

[tex]\quad\leadsto\quad\tt Volume_{(hollow\:cylinder)} = \red{\pi h(R^2 - r^2)} [/tex]

[tex]\quad\leadsto\quad\sf Volume_{(cone)} = \red{\dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h} [/tex]

[tex]\quad\leadsto\quad\sf Volume_{(sphere)} = \red{\dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3 } [/tex]

[tex]\quad\leadsto\quad\sf Volume_{(hemisphere)} = \red{\dfrac{2}{3}\pi r^3 } [/tex]
