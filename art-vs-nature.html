11. contemporary art vs modern art


Answer:

Let us differentiate Contemporary from Modern Art:

TIMELINE

Modern art is mostly used for art being created today but that is wrong. Modern art refers to art created at the beginning of the 1880s up to the 1960s. Artworks created after the 1960s to the present time are called Contemporary Art.

ARTISTS

Famous artists during the Modern Art movement were Van Gogh, Salvador Dalí, and Manet.

STYLE

Modern Art focuses on the subjective representation of an artwork's subject. This means it doesn't focus on interpreting realism but rather surrealism. An example of this is Salvador Dalí's" The Persistence of Memory" 1931.

Contemporary art uses both traditional and technological methods. It applies and mixes styles from any art period. Contemporary art has more freedom and liberty in expression and experimenting in styles and techniques. Contemporary art also has more social impact than Modern art.

20. conflictcharacter vs charactercharacter vs selfcharacter vs naturecharacter vs society​


Answer:

I'm not sure with the answer that I'm supposed to give so I'll just briefly explain the 4 conflicts

Explanation:

character vs character:

this is when the character's problem/conflict is due to another character (ex.: the main character got in a fight with another character and that's the main problem/conflict in the text)

character vs self:

this is when the character's problem/conflict revolves around their state of mind.(ex.: internal conflicts, psychological, the conflict happens inside their head)

character vs nature:

this is when the conflict was not caused by another character or by themselves but rather, by nature or environment (ex.: natural disasters)

character vs society:

this is when society or the people around the character are the ones causing the conflict. (ex: prejudice of society toward the character, actions or ideas that the character has but society can't accept).

An example of a Character vs. Nature conflict
