20. about 45% of the students at our lady of fatima university play a sport. if there are 375 students in the school, about how many do not play a sport? your answer must be in a whole number. with solution ​


Answer:

206  students

Step-by-step explanation:

Students who play a sport

Given:

Base = 375

Rate = 45% (equivalent to 0.45)

Percentage = ?

Formula: P = B × R

Solution:

P = B × R

P = 375 × 0.45

P = 168.75

Round off the answer to 169 since a student is considered as a whole.

Therefore, there are 169 students who play a sport.

In order to get the students who do not play a sport, subtract the total students in the school from students who play a sport. So,

375 - 169 = 206

Hence, there are 206  students at Our Lady of Fatima University who do not play a sport.

It is important to read critically. Critical reading requires you to evaluate the arguments in the text. You need to distinguish fact from opinion, and look at arguments given for and against the various claims. This also means being aware of your opinions and assumptions (positive and negative) of the text you are reading so you can evaluate it honestly. It is also important to be aware of the writer's background, assumptions and purposes. All writers have a reason for writing and will emphasise details which support their reason for writing and ignore details that do not.

In short, critical readings means actually thinking about the subject, moving beyond what the original essay concluded to the point of how the author reached that conclusion and the degree to which that conclusion is accurate. student who (if not precisely well-read) has read a great many books.

Critical reading means that a reader applies certain processes, models, questions, and theories that result in enhanced clarity and comprehension. There is more involved, both in effort and understanding, in a critical reading than in a mere "skimming" of the text. It is important because as a student we need to be critically thinking to be ready for the future
