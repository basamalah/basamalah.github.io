25. 2. What does Nenita feel for her husband? Why do you think she feels that way?​


The fact that Nenita is a woman in a conventional society that does not place much emphasis on a woman's viewpoint and emotional well-being leads me to believe that she does not love her husband, but rather feels obligated to serve him. Nenita mindlessly fulfills her position as an obedient wife since she believes it is not her responsibility to voice an opinion or to intervene in her husband's behavior.

Nenita is stuck in a loveless marriage with a man who despises and undervalues her very existence and she also downplays and undervalues her own life. As a result, it is possible to interpret Nenita's affections for her spouse as an attempt to make up for the lack of feelings she experiences for herself. In other words, Nenita has empathy for her philandering husband since she herself has none. She welcomes him back with wide arms each time one of his extramarital affairs comes to an end. She tends to his needs and obeys his directions while she nurses him back to health without punishing him for his misdeeds or refusing his demands of her.

The way Nenita reacts to her husband's passing is the evidence that convinces us the most that she does not love him. She escapes the limitations their marriage placed on her through his death. She is no longer required to go through life with the knowledge that he despises her and that she despises him. She is exempt from having to be his obedient wife. Most crucially, she still complies with society's expectations by choosing to be a widow rather than a divorcee, which, in her opinion, is much more respectable and even noble.
