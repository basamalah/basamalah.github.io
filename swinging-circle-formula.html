25. You swing a bucket of water attached to a string in a circle above yourhead. What keeps the water in the bucket?a. frictionb. gravityC. centripetal fored. inertia​


Answer:

D. Inertia

Explanation:

This is an example of centripetal (circular) motion. The water stays in the bucket because of inertia. The water wants to fly off from the circle, but the bucket gets in the way and keeps it in place. This is the same effect you feel when you go around a tight corner in the car and get squished against the door.


26. Tarzan, whose mass is 90.0 kg swings from a vine that is 3.0 meters long. His speed at the bottom of the swing is 4.0 m/s. What is his centripetal acceleration? How much centripetal force acted on him to keep him swinging in a circle? Show your solution and round off your final answer to correct number of significant figures.


Answer:

Tarzan (m = 90 kg) tries to cross a river by swinging from a hanging vine suspended by a tree limb half way across the river. The vine is 3.0 m long. His speed at the bottom of the path (almost touching the water) is 4.0 m/s.  

 

The rope must provide the centripetal force

         Fc = m(v2/r)

         Fc = 90(4^2/3)

         Fc = 480N    

The rope must hold up Tarzan’s weight

         Fw = mg

         Fw = 90 * 3 m/s2

         Fw = 270 N    

Fc = 480 + 270 > 750 N
