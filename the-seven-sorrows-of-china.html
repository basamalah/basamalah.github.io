16. What was the Shang Dynasty? And why was the Huang He often called China's Sorrow?


Answer:

China was ruled by several dynasties. One of the earliest was the Shang. This dynasty started ruling about 1500 BC. The Shang settled along the Huang He, or Yellow River, in northern China. Most of the common people during the Shang dynasty were farmers. They grew vegetables and grains, especially rice, for food. The Huang He was often called "China's Sorrow" because its many floods killed thousands of people and ruined many harvests.

24. 1. What are the Seven Sorrows of Mary? 2. In one whole short bond paper, make a short reflection about the seven sorrows of Mary.​


Answer:

The Seven Sorrows of Mary are:

The prophecy of Simeon: When Mary presented Jesus at the temple, the old man Simeon prophesized that her son would cause the fall and rise of many in Israel and that a sword would pierce her own heart too.

The flight to Egypt: To save her baby from Herod's massacre of the infants in Bethlehem, Mary and Joseph had to flee to Egypt with the baby Jesus.

Losing Jesus in the temple: When Jesus was twelve, Mary and Joseph lost Him on their way back from the temple in Jerusalem. They found Him three days later, in the temple, talking to the teachers.

Meeting Jesus on the way to Calvary: As Jesus was carrying His cross, Mary met Him on the way, and their eyes met, bringing great sorrow to her heart.

The Crucifixion: Mary stood at the foot of the cross and witnessed her son's agonizing death.

Taking down Jesus' body: After Jesus died, Mary held His lifeless body in her arms.

The Burial of Jesus: Mary watched as her son's body was laid in the tomb.

Reflection:

The Seven Sorrows of Mary represent the immense suffering and sacrifice that Mary went through as the mother of Jesus. It is not easy to imagine what Mary went through, watching her son being rejected, persecuted, and eventually killed in such a cruel way. As a mother, Mary must have felt the deepest pain and heartbreak imaginable.

However, Mary's sorrows also reflect her unwavering faith and trust in God. Despite her great suffering, she remained steadfast in her belief that God had a plan for her and her son. Mary teaches us the importance of surrendering our own will to God's will and trusting in His plan for our lives, even when we don't understand it.

Mary's sorrows also remind us of the importance of compassion and empathy. As we reflect on Mary's pain and sorrow, we are called to extend our love and compassion to others who are suffering. Mary teaches us that in times of great sorrow, we can find comfort in the love and support of those around us.

Finally, Mary's sorrows offer us hope and assurance that even in our darkest moments, God is with us. Mary's faith and trust in God inspire us to turn to Him in our own times of suffering and to believe that He will never abandon us.
