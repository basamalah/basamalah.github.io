Abstract

In his play Antigona Vélez, Argentine author Leopoldo Marechal adapted the Greek myth to the social reality of the nineteenth-century Argentine pampas, when new settlers clashed against the original inhabitants of the land. Thus, Marechal examined the forces that helped to shape his nation, exploring the limits of honour and tradition, and producing a moving portrayal of the conflicts that arise when love and obligation are at odds.

Journal Information

Mosaic is a quarterly journal published by the University of Manitoba that brings insights from a wide variety of disciplines to bear on the theoretical, practical, and cultural dimensions of literary works.
