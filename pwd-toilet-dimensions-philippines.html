11. Disadvantages of PWD sa pilipinas​


Answer:

PWD stands for Persons with Disabilities, and while there are various laws and programs in place to support PWDs in the Philippines, there are still several disadvantages that they may face.

One major disadvantage is the lack of accessibility in public places, such as buildings, transportation, and communication. Many establishments and infrastructure are not designed to accommodate PWDs, making it difficult for them to move around independently and participate fully in society.

Another disadvantage is the discrimination and stigma that PWDs may face. This can result in limited opportunities for employment, education, and social interaction, as well as negative attitudes and stereotypes from others.

Additionally, PWDs may face financial challenges, such as higher medical expenses and limited income opportunities. This can make it difficult for them to afford the necessary resources and support to fully participate in society.

Overall, while there are efforts to support PWDs in the Philippines, there are still significant barriers and challenges that they face, which limit their ability to live full and independent lives.

24. Paano ba maging pwd?


Ang PWD o Person With Disability ay isang pangngalan sa mga taong may pagkukulang o higit sa pisikal, mental, intelektwal, o senswal na kalagayan.

A PWD or a Person With Disability is a noun that refers to those people with physical, mental, intellectual or sensual impairment.

Pagiging pipi ( o mute sa ingles)Pagiging bingi ( o deaf sa ingles)Pagiging bulag ( o blind sa ingles)pagkakaroon ng down syndrome, ADHD, Autism at iba pa.
