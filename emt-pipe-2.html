Answer:

EMT is often cut with a sawzall (foot on pipe on 2x4 sloves or a wood faced vise), with a band saw, with a Milwaukee or equivalent power tool designed for EMT cutting, or using a manual pipe cutter that also holds the tubing.

Step 1

Loosen the wing nut near the hacksaw's handle. Loosen the nut by turning it counter-clockwise. Ensure the wing nut is loose enough so you can remove the old blade from the frame.

Step 2

Remove the old blade from the frame. Remove the blade by lifting it off the blade retaining pins at each end of the frame.

Step 3

Install a new blade onto the blade retaining pins. Ensure the blade's teeth point away from the handle.

Step 4

Tighten the wing nut by rotating it clockwise until the blade is tight.

Step 5

Make a test cut on a piece of scrap metal held in a vise. Tighten the wing nut further if the blade binds or flexes during the cut.

5. Discuss the procedures in cutting EMT conduit?​


Answer:

Cutting Conduit

1

Attach a tape measure's tang to the end of the conduit. Draw the tape measure across the length of the conduit. Use a pencil or marker to mark the overall dimension of the cut on the conduit's surface.

2

Rest a hacksaw's blade over the cut mark and saw through the pipe circumference. Position the cut mark over the edge of a work surface, such as a sawhorse or workbench. Periodically check the angle of your cut as you saw; many conduit fittings require a very straight cut for proper attachment. Remove burrs from the finished cut by twisting a reamer around the exposed end of the conduit.

3

Use a pipe cutter as an alternative to the hacksaw. Turn the cutter's knob counterclockwise to open the cutter's mouth. Place the open mouth of the pipe cutter on the cut mark. Turn the cutter's knob clockwise to tighten the cutter's blade against the conduit's surface.
