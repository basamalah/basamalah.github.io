17. An ice cream shop uses a mix of blueberries and cherries on its ice cream sundaes. The shop has 5.75 pounds of blueberries and 4.5 pounds of cherries. The shop mixes the blueberries and cherries and uses .06 pound of the mix on each sundae. Which expression represents the total number of sundaes that the shop can make using all the blueberries and cherries? (5.75 ÷ .06) + 4.5 5.75 + (4.5 ÷ .06) .06 ÷ (5.75 + 4.5) (5.75 + 4.5) ÷ .06


Answer:

The answer is 5.75 + 4.5 ÷ .06.

Step-by-step explanation:

In order to derive the correct expression, consider the following phrases: a mix of blueberries and cherries for the ice cream sundaes, since it is given that there are 5. 75 pounds of blueberries and 4.5 pounds of cherries then the combination of the two will give 5. 75 + 4.5 pounds. Continue with the phrase that the shop uses .06 pound of the mixture of the two for each sundae so, it will be divided by .06/ Therefore, the complete expression for this problem is 5. 75 + 4. 5 ÷ .06.
