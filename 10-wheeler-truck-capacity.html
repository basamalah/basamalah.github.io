1. which has greater momentum? 10 wheeler truck or bicycle?


Momentum depends on the body's mass and velocity. This is reflected in its formula:

p = mv

where:

p = momentum

m = mass

v= velocity

Now, since a 10-wheeler truck is obviously much more massive than a bicycle, we could say that there is a chance that the 10-wheeler truck has greater momentum. Assuming that they are both moving at the same velocity, the truck woud still have a greater momentum.

However, the momentum can also be influenced by the body's velocity. Say for example that the truck is at rest (v=0 m/s) and the bicycle is moving at 2 m/s. In this case, no matter how heavy the truck is, its momentum is still zero because it is not moving. Conversely, the bike, although lighter than the truck, has a greater momentum because it is in motion.

19. . A 10-wheeler truck has a mass of 2 569.6 kg, if it accelerates at 4.65m/s2 what is the net force of the truck?


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

A 10-wheeler truck has a mass of 2 569.6 kg, if it accelerates at 4.65m/s2 what is the net force of the truck?

Note:

The formula for Force is Force  = mass x acceleration ( F = m × a )

Given data:

m ( mass ) = 2569.6  kg

a ( acceleration ) = 4.65 m/s²

F ( Force ) = ? unknown

Solution:

We  can solve the problem using the formula of Force, Force  = mass x acceleration ( F = m × a )

F = m × a

  = 2569.6 kg × 4.65 m/s²

  = 11948.64 kg·m/s²

 = 11948.64 N

Answer:

The net force of the truck is equal to 11948.64 kg·m/s² or 11948.64 N

30. Why is it more difficult to stop a fully loaded ten wheeler truck than an unloaded one


It is more difficult to stop a fully loaded ten wheeler truck than an unloaded one because a fully loaded truck has more mass and momentum that needs to be overcome in order to bring it to a stop. With braking, a more massive object require more energy to be applied to bring it to a stop and since the mass of the truck is greater when it is fully loaded, the brakes will have to work harder to bring the truck to a stop.
