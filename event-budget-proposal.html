9. explain the cost associated with the event budget?​


Answer:PCMA suggests to spend 55% of your budget on food and beverage, audio visual, and speaker/entertainment in total, because these items have a direct impact on the overall attendee experience.

Costs associated with officials needed to run the event may have to be borne by the event organisers. Event participants are generally responsible for their own travel and accommodation costs. In minor or local events travel and accommodation costs are unlikely.

When building your event budget, it’s best to determine what financial success means for your event and how you want to measure it. In order to achieve this, you need to list all the possible expenses and revenues that will likely occur from your event. Always keep your cash flow in mind. Once you’ve developed your budget, you’ll have to track and review it on a regular basis to ensure you stay within the allocated resources.

One of the first steps for every event planner is preparing an event budget and predicting as accurately as possible if the event will result in a profit, loss or break-even. However, determining what financial success means for your event depends on the type of event and objectives. For example, a leadership summit is often a loss leader, association events are usually events that break-even, and trade-shows are often the best opportunity to yield a profit.

Before planning your budget any further, you need to understand your organization’s needs. Your event budget is really a forecast, or projection, of all expenses and revenues that will incur at your event. You won’t be able to forecast all expenses and revenues from the beginning, and some of these might even change over time. Nevertheless, planning your budget with consideration for the most common expenses and revenues is necessary.

14. Patulong po(Budget Proposal that will improve your company? ​


Answer:

How to improve your business budget

Use live data.Budget smaller pieces.Switch to rolling budgets and forecasting.Ditch spreadsheets.Adjust budget to strategy.Expand communication.Make a plan B and C.Assess cash flow.

Answer:

Budget Proposal that will improve your company

1. Use live data

The number one problem with any budgeting process is that it’s always using “dead” data to inform live strategy. Are you using a spreadsheet of data from a month (or, heaven-forbid, a year) ago? Do you know what your team spent last week? Far too many companies are setting budgets based on old data and only looking at the budgets once spending has already occurred.

You need data about what’s happening in your company’s budgets NOW. You should be able to see at a glance how much you’ve spent and how much you have left to spend. The biggest culprits are expense reports and reimbursements, which often take a full 90 days to account, leaving you further and further behind.

2. Budget in smaller pieces

Many corporate budgeting exercises look at the company’s finances from a global, macro scope. While this can be helpful for perspective, it makes actual use of a regular budget less practical. Think of it like this—you don’t plan meals by the year, or even by the month. It makes much more sense to manage your meals (and finances) in smaller chunks.

After creating your yearly or quarterly budget, take it one or two steps further to extrapolate what budgets should be on a micro level, including individual budget allocations.  

​3. Switch to rolling budgets & forecasting

Once you’re using more lively data and budgeting in smaller pieces, you’re ready to bring some flexibility to your budgeting. We all learned the hard way from 2020—we have to be ready for the unexpected. A smart, effective budget is one that can roll with any changes, such as a large increase or decrease in revenue, new availability of inventory, real estate, or talent to hire, etc. Utilize rolling budgets and forecasts that take into account what is happening right now as you plan for upcoming spend.


15. What are the event budgeting rules and what are their importance?


Answer:

Event budgeting rules are guidelines that help event professionals plan, execute and manage a successful event. They help event planners stay within budget and ensure that each event is cost-effective. Event budgeting rules also help event planners to plan and allocate resources efficiently.

The importance of event budgeting rules are:

1. They help event planners to stay within budget and avoid overspending.

2. They ensure that each event is cost-effective and budgeted for appropriately.

3. They help event planners to allocate resources efficiently.

4. They help event planners to create realistic goals and objectives.

5. They help to create an effective timeline and ensure that each aspect of the event is completed on time and within budget.

6. They help to ensure that any potential risks are identified and addressed.

Answer:

Event budgeting rules are guidelines and principles that are used to effectively plan and manage the finances of an event. They are important because they help event planners to make informed decisions about the allocation of resources, and ensure that the event is financially sustainable. Here are some common event budgeting rules:

Explanation:

1. Start with a detailed plan: Before creating a budget, it's essential to have a detailed plan of the event, including the venue, the number of attendees, the duration, the type of activities, and any other relevant details. This will help you to identify all the necessary expenses and estimate their costs.

2. Determine your revenue sources: Once you have a clear idea of the event's expenses, you need to identify the sources of revenue, such as ticket sales, sponsorships, and donations. This will help you to determine the budget limits and allocate resources accordingly.

3. Allocate resources strategically: Allocate resources based on the priorities of the event. Identify the essential expenses and allocate resources to them first, and then allocate resources to other expenses based on their importance.

4. Consider contingencies: Unexpected expenses can arise during an event, so it's essential to include a contingency fund in your budget. Typically, it's recommended to set aside 10-20% of the budget as a contingency.

5. Monitor and adjust: Monitor your budget regularly and make adjustments as necessary. Keep track of your expenses and revenue sources and adjust your budget accordingly.

The importance of event budgeting rules includes the following:

1. Financial sustainability: Budgeting rules help ensure that the event is financially sustainable and can meet its financial obligations.

2. Effective resource allocation: Budgeting rules help allocate resources strategically, ensuring that resources are used in the most efficient and effective way possible.

3. Risk management: Budgeting rules help to identify and mitigate risks, ensuring that the event can overcome any unexpected expenses or issues that arise.

4. Improved decision-making: Budgeting rules provide a framework for making informed decisions about the allocation of resources and identifying areas where adjustments can be made to improve the event.

In conclusion, event budgeting rules are essential for effective event planning and management. They help to ensure financial sustainability, effective resource allocation, risk management, and improved decision-making. By following these rules, event planners can create successful events that meet their objectives and exceed the expectations of attendees.

26. National Budget proposal for 2020 in the Philippines?


Answer:

President Rodrigo Duterte and his Cabinet approved the national budget proposal prepared by the Department of Budget and Management (DBM) during the monthly Cabinet meeting on Monday, August 5.

The proposed 2020 budget is 12% higher than the 2019 national budget of P3.662 trillion. (READ: Winners and losers under Duterte's 2019 budget)

The DBM aims to submit to Congress the proposed 2020 National Expenditure Program before the August 21 deadline.

The budget will then undergo the scrutiny of the newly-convened 18th Congress, then sent to Duterte's desk for approval.

"This budget proposal is designed to respond to the needs of the majority of our countrymen longing to be uprooted from decades of want of basic necessities, inadequate supply of basic services, lack of infrastructures required to spur economic growth, absence of accountability on government coffers, vexing bureaucratic rigmarole, deprived education and unchanged poverty," Presidential Spokesman Salvador Panelo said in a statement on Tuesday, August 6.

As in previous years, the Department of Education has been allocated the largest slice of the pie, followed by the Department of Public Works and Highways. Also getting big allocations are the Department of Transportation and the Department of Health.

Panelo gave assurances that the funds will be "spent wisely" in order to create a "vibrant economy that will be felt by the citizenry."

Newly-named Acting Budget Secretary Wendel Avisado was present at the Cabinet meeting, as well as Undersecretary Janet Abuel who helmed the department while the 2020 budget proposal was being prepared.
