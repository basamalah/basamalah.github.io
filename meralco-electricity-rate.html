Answer:

An electric company is a classic example of a natural monopoly. Once the gargantuan fixed costs involved with power generation and power lines is payed, each additional unit of electricity costs very little; the more units sold, the more the fixed costs can be spread, creating a reasonable price for the consumer.

Step-by-step explanation:

Answer:

To find the average charge for the electrical energy per 100kw-hr, we need to calculate the total charge for the first 500kw-hr and then divide it by 5 (since we have five 100kw-hr units).

For the first 100kw-hr, the charge is 40 centavos per kw-hr, which gives a total charge of 40 centavos.

For the second 100kw-hr, the charge has increased by a constant rate, let's call it "r". So, the charge for this unit is (40 + r) centavos per kw-hr, which gives a total charge of (40 + r) * 100 centavos.

Similarly, for the third 100kw-hr, the charge is (40 + 2r) centavos per kw-hr, which gives a total charge of (40 + 2r) * 100 centavos.

For the fourth 100kw-hr, the charge is (40 + 3r) centavos per kw-hr, which gives a total charge of (40 + 3r) * 100 centavos.

And for the fifth 100kw-hr, the charge is 76 centavos per kw-hr, which gives a total charge of 76 * 100 centavos.

Therefore, the total charge for the first 500kw-hr is:

40 + (40 + r) + (40 + 2r) + (40 + 3r) + 76 = 276 + 6r

And the average charge per 100kw-hr is:

(276 + 6r) / 5 = 55.2 + 1.2r

So, the average charge for the electrical energy per 100kw-hr is 55.2 + 1.2r centavos, where "r" is the constant rate of increase in the charge for each 100kw-hr unit.
