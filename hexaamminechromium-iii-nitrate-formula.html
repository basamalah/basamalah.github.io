Answer:

The formula for iron(III) nitrate is Fe(NO3)3.

Explanation:

The formula for Iron (III) Nitrate is Fe(NO3)3. This compound is commonly used as an oxidizing agent, catalyst, and in the production of other chemicals.

An example of how to use Iron (III) Nitrate is in the synthesis of Iron (III) Oxide. To do this, you would mix Iron (III) Nitrate with Sodium Hydroxide to form a precipitate of Iron (III) Hydroxide. Then, you would heat the Iron (III) Hydroxide until it decomposes into Iron (III) Oxide and water vapor.

Another example is using Iron (III) Nitrate in the production of dyes and pigments. Iron (III) Nitrate can be used as a catalyst to help certain organic compounds undergo reactions to produce dyes and pigments with unique properties.

4. Lead ii chloride + calcium nitrate formula


Lead ii chloride + calcium nitrate formula

Lead(II) nitrate is an inorganic compound with the chemical formula Pb(NO3). It commonly occurs as a colourless crystal or white powder and, unlike most other lead(II) salts, is soluble in water.

Known since the Middle Ages by the name plumb dulcis, the production of lead(II) nitrate from either metallic lead or lead oxide in nitric acid was small-scale, for direct use in making other lead compounds. In the nineteenth century lead(II) nitrate began to be produced commercially in Europe and the United States. Historically, the main use was as a raw material in the production of pigments for lead paints, but such paints have been superseded by less toxic paints based on titanium dioxide. Other industrial uses included heat stabilization in nylon and polyesters, and in coatings of photothermographic paper. Since around the year 2000, lead(II) nitrate has begun to be used in gold cyanidation.

27. A solution is formed by adding 35g of ammonium nitrate to 250g pf water. What is the percent by mass of ammonium nitrate? (show your formula and solution)


Percent by MassDiscussion:

Percent by mass (also called percent by weight or weight percent) is the ratio of the mass of a solute to the mass of the solution, multiplied by 100. The formulas used in solving percent by mass are as follows:

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solution}} × 100}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 100}[/tex]

The percent by mass is a unitless number because it is a ratio of two similar quantities.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

A solution is formed by adding 35 g of ammonium nitrate to 250 g of water. What is the percent by mass of ammonium nitrate?

Given:

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 35 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of solvent = 250 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solute} = \text{ammonium nitrate}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solvent} = \text{H}_{2}\text{O}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\text{percent by mass}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\text{percen by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{percent by mass} = \frac{\text{35 g}}{\text{35 g + 250 g}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by mass} = 12.28\%}[/tex]

1. Caffeine, C8H10N4O2

To calculate the percentage composition of caffeine, we need to find the molar mass of the molecule first:

Molar mass of Caffeine = (8 x 12.01 g/mol) + (10 x 1.01 g/mol) + (4 x 14.01 g/mol) + (2 x 16.00 g/mol) = 194.19 g/mol

Now we can find the percentage composition of each element in caffeine:

Percentage of Carbon = (8 x 12.01 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 49.48%

Percentage of Hydrogen = (10 x 1.01 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 5.19%

Percentage of Nitrogen = (4 x 14.01 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 28.85%

Percentage of Oxygen = (2 x 16.00 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 16.48%

2. Ammonium nitrate, NH4NO3

To calculate the percentage composition of ammonium nitrate, we need to find the molar mass of the molecule first:

Molar mass of Ammonium Nitrate = (1 x 14.01 g/mol) + (4 x 1.01 g/mol) + (3 x 16.00 g/mol) = 80.04 g/mol

Now we can find the percentage composition of each element in ammonium nitrate:

Percentage of Nitrogen = (1 x 14.01 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 17.50%

Percentage of Hydrogen = (4 x 1.01 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 5.03%

Percentage of Oxygen = (3 x 16.00 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 37.51%

Percentage of Ammonium ion (NH4+) = (4 x 1.01 g/mol + 1 x 14.01 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 40.96%

3. Benzene, C6H6

To calculate the percentage composition of benzene, we need to find the molar mass of the molecule first:

Molar mass of Benzene = (6 x 12.01 g/mol) + (6 x 1.01 g/mol) = 78.11 g/mol

Now we can find the percentage composition of each element in benzene:

Percentage of Carbon = (6 x 12.01 g/mol) / 78.11 g/mol x 100% = 92.26%

Percentage of Hydrogen = (6 x 1.01 g/mol) / 78.11 g/mol x 100% = 7.74%
