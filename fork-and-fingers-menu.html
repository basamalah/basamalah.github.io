a) The ratio of the number of knives to the number of forks is 1:7, or 1/7.

b) If there are 7 times as many forks as knives, then for every 1 knife, there are 7 forks. Therefore, the fraction of forks is 7/(1+7) = 7/8, and the fraction of knives is 1/(1+7) = 1/8.

c) Alternatively, you can express the number of forks and knives as fractions of the total number of forks and knives by using the total number of forks and knives as the denominator. If there are 7 times as many forks as knives, and the total number of forks and knives is 7+1=8, then the fraction of forks is 7/8 and the fraction of knives is 1/8.

29. Jackie has 23 blue forks and 21 red forks. She is dividing the forks among 5 tables. If each table will get the same number of forks, how many forks will be left over?


The total number of forks that Jackie has is 23 + 21 = 44.

If she divides these equally among 5 tables, each table will get:

44 / 5 = 8 with a remainder of 4

Therefore, there will be 4 forks left over.
