4. A runner covered a 400-m track,divide into 4 laps of 10m each. She completed the first lap in 15s, the second lap in 10s,the third lap in 13s, the fourth lap in 12s. What her average Speed?​


Answer:

The total distance covered by the runner is 400m.

The total time taken to cover the distance is:

15s + 10s + 13s + 12s = 50s

Therefore, the average speed of the runner is:

Average speed = Total distance / Total time

Average speed = 400m / 50s

Average speed = 8 m/s

Therefore, the average speed of the runner is 8 m/s.

Answer:

Speed is a scalar quantity which doesn’t depend on direction of motion.

=> Average speed = Distance/ Time = 270/30 = 9m/s

Velocity is a vector quantity having magnitude and direction. Since the runner runs 270m and returns to the same spot, net displacement is ZERO.

Average Velocity = Displacement/Time

= 0/30 = 0
