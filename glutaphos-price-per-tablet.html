6. 1. The number of tablets sold by a shop can be modeled by theexpression(t) = Tt - 25and price per tablet is modeled by anexpressionP(t) = 3+2+31-36where t is the number of months in ayear. If we use this model, what is the total amount of revenue generated by theshop at the end of the year?​


Answer:

We know that the amount of revenue generated is equal to the:

Number of items sold x Price per item

Here, Number of items sold N (t) = 7t + 25

Price per tablet P(t) = 3 t ^ 2 +3t +36

Multiply these two expressions together:

(7t + 25) \cdot (3 t ^2 +3t +36)

7t ( 3 t ^2 +3t +36) + 25 (3 t ^2 +3t +36)

21 t ^3 +21 t ^2 +252t +75t ^2 +75t+900

21 t ^ 3 + 96 t ^2 +327 t +900

Put t = 12 in the above expression because in a year there are 12 months:

21 {12} ^ 3 + 96 {12} ^2 +327 (12) +900

36288 + 13824 + 3924 + 900

54936

Hence, the total revenue of the shop for a year is 54936 dollars.

15. During a sale, the price of a tablet was reduced by 15% to Php 3 825. What is the original price of the tablet?a. Php 4 300 b. Php 4 400 c. Php 4 500 d. Php 4 600​


Answer:

C. ₱4500

Step-by-step explanation:

In this problem, we are given the "rate of discount" and the "sale price." We are asked to find the base or the initial price in this problem.

We can find the fractional value of the sale price by subtracting the discount rate from 1.

1 - rate = 1 - 15%

1 - rate = 1 - 0.15

1 - rate = 0.85

Now, we can divide the sale price by the new rate to find the original price.

Base = Amount After / (1 - Rate)

Base = ₱3825 / 0.85

Base = ₱4500

Thus, the original price of the tablet is ₱4500.

DECREASE

==================================

Problem

A tablet's price was reduced from P45,000.00 to P33,500.00. Find the percent reduction in price.

Solution

Determine the reduction percent using the formula given

[tex]\bold{||\:Percent_{Decrease} = \frac{Old\:Value\:-\:New\:Value}{Old\:Value}}[/tex]

[tex]\large\rm{\red{Percent_{Decrease}} = \frac{45, 000-33, 500}{45, 000} }[/tex]

Calculate the decrease percent by finding out the difference between the numbers where we subtract the old value by the new value, after that we divide this 'difference' we got to the old value.

[tex]\large\rm{Percent_{Decrease} = \frac{45, 000-33, 500}{45, 000} }[/tex][tex]\large\rm{Percent_{Decrease} = \frac{11,500}{45, 000}}[/tex][tex]\large\rm{Percent_{Decrease} = 0.25}[/tex]

Now that we got the 'decrease' multiply it by a hundred.

[tex]\large\rm{Percent_{Decrease} = (\red{0.25}) (100) }[/tex][tex]\large\rm\red{Percent_{Decrease} = 25\% }[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The percent reduction in price is [tex]\boxed{\red{25\%}}[/tex]
