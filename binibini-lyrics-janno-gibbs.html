12. The number of steps involved in Graham Gibbs' full structured debriefing


Graham Gibbs - shows and outlined rubric of reflective inquiry which he called structured debriefing.

The stages of a full structured debriefing to the stages of the experiential learning cycle:


#1. Description:  Don’t make judgments yet or try to draw conclusions; simply describe.

#2. Feelings: These are the reactions. Don’t move on to analyzing these yet.

#3. Evaluation: Make value judgments.

#4. Analysis: The sense one can make of the situation. Bring in ideas from outside the experience to help you.

#5. Conclusions: What can be concluded about your own specific, unique, personal; situation or way of working?

#6. Personal action plans: These are the steps that you're going to take on the basis of what you have learnt.

24. How are enthalpy, entropy, and the Gibbs free energy related?


Explanation:

Enthalpy (H), entropy (S), and Gibbs free energy (G) are thermodynamic properties that are related to each other through the following equation:

ΔG = ΔH - TΔS

where ΔG is the change in Gibbs free energy, ΔH is the change in enthalpy, ΔS is the change in entropy, and T is the temperature in Kelvin.

This equation, which is known as the Gibbs-Helmholtz equation, describes the relationship between the thermodynamic properties of a system and the spontaneity of a process. A process will be spontaneous if the change in Gibbs free energy is negative (ΔG < 0), which occurs when the change in enthalpy is negative (exothermic reaction, ΔH < 0) and/or the change in entropy is positive (increase in disorder, ΔS > 0).

If the change in enthalpy is negative (exothermic reaction, ΔH < 0), then the reaction releases heat, which can increase the entropy of the surroundings. This can result in a negative change in Gibbs free energy, making the reaction spontaneous.

Conversely, if the change in enthalpy is positive (endothermic reaction, ΔH > 0), then the reaction absorbs heat, which can decrease the entropy of the surroundings. This can result in a positive change in Gibbs free energy, making the reaction non-spontaneous.

In summary, enthalpy, entropy, and Gibbs free energy are related through the Gibbs-Helmholtz equation, which describes the spontaneity of a process based on the changes in these thermodynamic properties
