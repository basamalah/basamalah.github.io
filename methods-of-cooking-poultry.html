25. what is the moist heat method and dry heat method in cooking poultry dishes ​


Answer:

Moist-heat cooking methods use water, liquid or steam to transfer heat to food. Common moist-heat cooking methods include: poaching, simmering, boiling, braising, stewing, pot roasting, steaming and en papillote. Dry-heat cooking methods involve the circulation of hot air or direct contact to fat to transfer heat.

Answer:

Moist Heat Method:

The moist heat method involves cooking poultry in a liquid, such as water or broth, at a temperature below the boiling point. This method is often used for tougher cuts of meat that require a longer cooking time to become tender. Some examples of moist heat cooking methods for poultry include:

Boiling: In this method, the poultry is fully submerged in boiling water and cooked until it is fully cooked through.

Poaching: This involves cooking poultry in a flavored liquid, such as broth or wine, at a lower temperature than boiling. This method is often used for delicate poultry dishes such as poached chicken breast or turkey breast.

Steaming: Poultry is cooked by steam over boiling water.

Dry Heat Method:

The dry heat method involves cooking poultry without any added liquid or moisture. This method is often used for tender cuts of meat that cook quickly at higher temperatures. Some examples of dry heat cooking methods for poultry include:

Roasting: This involves cooking the poultry in a dry heat oven until it is fully cooked through. The poultry can be seasoned with herbs, spices, or marinades before roasting to add flavor.

Grilling: Poultry can be grilled over an open flame, a gas grill or a charcoal grill. The heat should be high and the poultry should be turned once halfway through cooking.

Searing: This method involves browning the poultry on high heat for a short period of time to lock in moisture and create a crust.

29. Make a short discussion about two methods of cooking poultry products namely: a) moist heat method; and be) dry heat method.​


Answer:

In moist-heat cooking methods, liquid or steam is used to cook the food. Flavored liquids, such as broth or wine, can be used as the heat transfer medium and will also add flavor during the cooking process. Leftover liquids from the cooking process can also be used to make sauce or stock.

Dry heat cooking refers to any cooking technique where the heat is transferred to the food item without using extra moisture. This method typically involves high temperatures—300 F or hotter.
