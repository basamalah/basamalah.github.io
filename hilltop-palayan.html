18. Long narrow hilltops mountain ranges or watersheds


Ridge: a long, narrow hilltop, mountain range, or watershed.

Definition of ridge

Ridges are geological formations that are part of mountains or hills that have a higher ground position than the position of the land on both sides.

Characteristics of ridge

- Location: the ridge is in the formation of the face of the earth that is convex or raised upwards, such as on a mountain or on a hill, both mountains or hills that are above sea level (land) or below sea level (ocean).

-Slope: has a slope that varies, can be very gentle or very steep.

-Sections: the ridge consists of the ridge itself and the crest of the ridge.

-Contours: ridges on the map are indicated by a series of U- or V-shaped contours. The direction of the U or V shape differs by 180°

with valley contours and ridges without rivers.

-Elevation : the ridge is the ground with a higher position than the three directions around it. What is meant by the three directions around it is that if it faces towards the top of the ridge, then the three directions around it (right, left and back) have a higher ground position low.

Classification

-Based on location:

1. Ridge

2. Mountain Range

3. Ocean Ridge

28. An object of mass 45.0kg is placed on a hilltop of height 83.0m. What is the potential energy?


Question:-

An object of mass 45.0kg is placed on a hilltop of height 83.0m. What is the potential energy?

Given data:-

{ Mass = m; Height = h; Acceleration due to gravity = g; Potential energy = P.E}

—› m = 45.0 kg

—› h = 83.0 m

—› g = 9.8 m/s²

Formula:-

Gravitational potential energy of

a body, ( P.E ) = mgh

Solution:-

{ To find P.E we use formula }

—› P.E = mgh

—› P.E = mgh

—› P.E = 45 × 9.8 × 83

—› P.E = 441 × 83

—› P.E = 36603 Joule

Hence, potential energy is 36603 joule.

30. history palayan City​


Answer:

History

It was on March 25, 1952 that the leaders of Nueva Ecija comprising of congressman, board members and all mayors of Nueva Ecija called a meeting with the desire to talk and plan about the transfer of the capital of Nueva Ecija from Cabanatuan to other municipality. There are several locations suggested, some of which are Gapan, San Jose, Santa Rosa, Guimba, Talavera, General Tinio, Baloc (present-day Santo Domingo), Muñoz, and the Government Stock Farm (present-day Palayan City). After the meeting, the majority agreed that the Government Stock Farm be made as the capitol site.

On June 19, 1965, congress of the Philippines enacted RA 4475 creating Palayan City as the new capital of Nueva Ecija. While the stock farm is not virtually rice land, ”Palayan” was chosen as its name, the province being dubbed as the Rice Granary of the region and the country. Finally, the city government of Palayan was constituted December 5, 1965 with then Governor Eduardo L. Joson as its Ex-Officio Mayor while the Board Members Constituted its first City Council
