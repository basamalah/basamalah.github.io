1. letter to my future self essay​


Answer:

Dear Future Me,

I am writing this letter to you as a way to reflect on my present self and my hopes for my future. I am at a point in my life where I am facing new challenges and seeking new opportunities, and I want to take a moment to capture my thoughts and dreams for the years to come.

As I look back on my past, I am grateful for all the experiences I have had, both the good and the bad. I know that these experiences have shaped me into the person I am today, and they have given me the strength and resilience to face whatever lies ahead.

I know that the years to come will bring new challenges and trials, but I also know that I am capable of overcoming them. I believe in myself, and I know that I have what it takes to achieve my goals. I may not always make the right decisions or face the right direction, but I know that I have the ability to learn from my mistakes and grow from them.

In addition to keeping my sense of wonder, I also want to remember to be patient and kind to myself. I know that life can be both fleeting and unpredictable, and I don't want to waste my time beating myself up over past mistakes or stressing about the future. Instead, I want to learn to appreciate the present moment and celebrate all the joys and adventures that life has to offer.

16. IF YOU WERE GIVEN THE CHANCE TO SEE YOUR FUTURE SELF, WHAT THREE LIFE ADVICE WOULD YOU REMIND YOUR FUTURE SELF OF? (Essay 5 paragraph)​


Answer:

Doing this exercise can be a really insightful experience.

Imagine writing to your future self 5 years from now — what would you say? What kind of person would you be? What goals would you want to have achieved? Not only is it mind boggling, but it gets you thinking about your goals 5 years from now. As you write your letter, you will start to think about the actions you should take to realize them in your expected time frame.

Subsequently when you open your letter 5 years down the road, you get to assess the things that match (or don’t match) up vs. your expectations, and reflect why that’s the case. Often times, our goals are subject to a lot of changes due to unanticipated circumstances and changing priorities. Reading your letter lets you see how your life trajectory has changed since writing it. It also makes you pause and think about how you are doing, and whether you are where you want to be in life.
