Answer:

What is Rodrigo Duterte’s leadership style?

Have been out of the country (Philippines) for decades but I still follow what’s going on there from time to time. Before he ran for president, Rodrigo Duterte had a reputation of being a tough mayor in the southern part of the country called Davao…tough on criminals and drug dealers. When he became president, the drug lords were very active all over the country, mostly in densely populated Metro Manila, were even prominent politicians were implicated. The last straw was when the drug dealers started penetrating the elementary and high schools and targeted the children! Duterte have had enough, and warned the drug lords that if they don’t immediately stop their nefarious drug activities targeting innocent children he will have no choice but to “eliminate them”. They did not listen…and predictably “elimination” happened! Duterte did exactly the same thing to the Muslim terrorists who were causing mayhem and killings also in the southern part of the country. The country was no longer the same after that…for the better! Of course other countries criticized him for his “brutal and savage” style of enforcing law and order. In my opinion, Duterte is a man of action! And in a place like the Philippines you need a leader who does what he promised…and delivers…regardless of political and international consequence!

17. Compare and contrast the leadership of FerdinandMarcos and our present president, Rodrigo R. Duterte.​


i’ll give here a short objective comparison of both leaders, not just on their leadership but on their personalities.

In terms of brilliance, no politician here can measure up against Marcos (except maybe Pepe Diokno or Gibo Teodoro). The man was a Bar exam top notcher like them, and had a photographic memory.

In terms of achievement, Marcos again takes the win. There’s also no doubt his leadership before 1972 ushered in the golden age of our country. Culture flourished, safety prevailed and infrastructure was built en masse. The Philippines then was prosperous, second only to Japan and when China, South Korea and Singapore were still impoverished.

Sadly, though, this didn’t last and he along with Imelda was the cause of our fall from grace due to their conjugal dictatorship, as Primitivo Mijares calls it, and the rampant corruption and human rights abuses then during the Martial Law years. You could now see that, in terms of having authoritarian tendencies, Marcos wins by default.

Duterte, on the other hand, I think, is just of average (or above-average) intelligence. I’m not disparaging him since his speeches, despite them laden with cuss words and rape jokes, show he’s well-read and knows what he’s talking about.

In terms of his performance, it’s still too early to judge. Although it can’t be doubted he’s achieved a lot more than his last three predecessors combined, the mistakes and flaws of his government undermine what it has done for us. They have made me lose much of my faith and hope in it. This is exacerbated with Duterte’s character flaws which have come under attack by both the opposition and many of his supporters, me included.

In terms of authoritarian tendencies, Duterte does speak like a dictator, even once claiming he threw someone off a helicopter which reminds me of Pinochet, but he only directs it to those who destroy our country, more specifically criminals.

If you were to ask me which one of them do I support more, it’s Duterte. I don’t support Marcos but I do acknowledge the good he had done before Martial Law. Comparing them is comparing apples to oranges. I may be wrong, though.
