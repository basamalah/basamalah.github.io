2. The total amount paid on a GSIS loan is P39,900. If the loan was for 3 years at 11% simple interest, what was the original loan?​


Answer: P30,000

Step-by-step explanation:

Let the original loan be x.

Using the formula for simple interest: I = P * r * t, where I is the interest, P is the principal, r is the interest rate, and t is the time in years.

The interest on the loan is:

I = P * r * t

I = x * 0.11 * 3

I = 0.33x

The total amount paid is the sum of the original loan and the interest:

x + 0.33x = P39,900

Simplifying and solving for x:

1.33x = P39,900

x = P30,000

Therefore, the original loan was P30,000.

Answer:

Principal amount is Php 62,500.00

Step-by-step explanation:Step 1: List down all the given

Interest amount = 5,000

Rate = 0.08

Time = 1 year.

Step 2: Derive the formula of simple interest.

[tex]I=PRT[/tex] since we are looking for P we have to divide both sides by RT

[tex]\frac{I}{RT}=P[/tex].

Step 3: Substitute.

[tex]P=\frac{5,000.00}{(0.08)(1)}[/tex]

[tex]P=62,500.00[/tex]
