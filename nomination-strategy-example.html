NOMINATION OF COMMUNICATIVE STRATEGY

A speaker conducts nomination in a communicative strategy in order to establish a topic jointly and effectively. Essentially, when employing this method, you attempt to initiate a conversation with the persons you are speaking with. This occurs as a result of presenting a certain topic in a clear, honest, and pertinent manner.

Nomination in communicative techniques is described as the presentation of an issue in a clear and honest manner. This method is typically employed at the outset of a conversation to establish its objective.

The nomination in communicative strategy is used to introduce a theme at the opening of a Communicative Situation. It is a communication tactic that can be used at any time during an interaction to continue the conversation. When using this method, the topic is addressed in a straightforward and honest manner, stating only what is necessary to keep the engagement on track.

In human communication, nomination conversation is the process of proposing a discussion topic. Typically, it begins with a question intended to elicit a response or just as a conversation starter. To put it simply, if you were to inquire, "How was your day?"

2. Nomination communicative strategy example


The nomination communicative Strategy is employed when suggesting a topic at the outset of a communicative situation. It is a tactic that can be used to carry on a conversation at any point in the contact. When this tactic is employed, the subject is introduced in a straightforward and honest manner, stating only what is pertinent to keep the conversation on track. Example of nomination communication:

Have you noticed the weird weather lately? Is this because of climate change?I was late for class again! The busway stopped midway. What is wrong with the bsuway?

Plans, methods, and means of information exchange that are used to accomplish a specific social, political, psychological, or linguistic goal are referred to as communicative strategies. Seven different types of communication techniques:

Nomination - Clearly stating the facts about a given subject and saying only what is pertinentRestriction - limiting the answer or reaction to a particular set of criteriaTurn-taking - Knowing when and how to speak when it is your turn to speakTopic control - maintaining the conversation on topic by asking questions and getting a responseTopic shifting - moving the subject to a new subject before returning to the previous oneRepair - resolving a communication breakdown to transmit messages that are easier to understandTermination is the act of ending a conversation using both verbal and nonverbal cues

A nominative communication strategy is a communication strategy that is carried out by asking questions, giving statements, or explaining matters related to the main topic to be conveyed. This strategy is usually used in public speaking such as seminars or presentations to introduce the topic and attract the attention or focus of the audience.

An example of a Nomination example communicative strategy is:

When presenting the topic of going green or the climate crisis, the speaker asked the audience, "Have you felt the weather getting hotter recently?
