12. Ano ang mga subplots ng pelikulang "Mga Munting Tinig"?


The Subplots of the movie entitled “Mga Munting Tinig”is somewhat a real life status that is happening in the society. It brings back the time whereas the parents dictate and command their own children on what to do and what will going to do. It is a heartfelt movie that touches life and anyone could watch out for. It depicts the life of a teacher wherein she is being abused by her own family because of the job that she choose. The lead character who played the role as Melinda Santiago is a public school teacher that experienced being maltreated not physically but in verbal acts. She used to play a flute to express her mournful feelings. In Malawig, she works as a schoolteacher. The students are being stooped down by their own parents because for them education is not dominant whereas they prefer and wanted them to help out in working on the farm to have a good harvest and in doing household chores. But still, students neglect all the negative reviews that is being thrown upon them. As what the father of Melinda say, “Great rewards comes with great hard work”. Melinda reminded herself regarding that saying and instead, she puts all the attentions to the students to teach them well and reminded to strive hard to have a better future. The weaknesses of the movie are the faculty members and the principal that are not diligent and eager enough to teach the students well because they put their concentration to the things that is not literally important. Poverty is the main reason why the parents do not allow their child to continue in studying. Lastly, its strength is the time that they were invited to join a choral competition and Melinda had an idea in joining the said contest to raise funds and help out the students. They were having a hard time in practicing the song which is entitled Small Voices. But at the end, their patience and hard work were all paid off as they won the competition. They brought home pride to their parents, teachers, and also to the school.

Thus, all challenges and problems just come and go into the lives of people. It is a test that God gives to a person on how he or she will handle it accordingly. The movie indeed is an example of trying and working hard to the things that could make and touch everyone’s lives.

13. Munting tinig ng mahihirap, naririnig nga ba?


Answer:

The phrase "munting tinig ng mahihirap" translates to "the small voice of the poor," and it suggests that the voices or concerns of poor people are often not heard or given much attention. It is possible that this phrase is being used to criticize the way that society or the government treats poor people and the issues that they face.

Explain:

There are many reasons why the voices of the poor may not be heard or given much attention. For example, poor people may not have the same access to education, resources, or social networks as more affluent individuals. They may also have less political power or influence, making it harder for them to advocate for their own needs and interests. In addition, the media and mainstream public discourse may not prioritize the concerns of poor people, leading to a lack of representation and visibility for this group.

Overall, it is important to listen to and consider the perspectives and needs of poor people in order to create a more just and equal society. It is possible to work towards creating more opportunities for poor people to have their voices heard and to advocate for themselves and their communities.
