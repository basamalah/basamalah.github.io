Answer:

The wire undergoes a physical change.

The wire has a slight change in its shape.

An object's shape can be modified while the object's chemical composition remains unchanged. For example, if one folds money in a specific manner, the money remains chemically the same. It is the same for the bent wire that Mang Jose bought. No chemical changes happened in the wire.

Explanation:

Composition distinguishes a physical change from a chemical change (or reaction). A chemical change occurs when the makeup of the substances in issue changes; a physical change occurs when the look, smell, or plain display of a sample of matter changes without the content changing. Despite the fact that we label them "reactions," no bodily reaction is taking place. A change in the elemental composition of the substance in question is required for a reaction to occur. As a result, from now on, we'll simply refer to physical "reactions" as physical changes.

Physical alterations are limited to those that alter the appearance without altering the composition. The following are some examples of common alterations (but not all):

Textures, Color, Temperature, ShapeState Transition (Boiling Point and Melting Point are significant factors in determining this change.)Many other elements of a substance are included in physical attributes. Physical qualities include (but are not limited to) the following.Lusters/ MalleabilityThe ability to be twisted into a thin wireDensityViscositySolubilityMass/Volume

A physical change is defined as a change in these physical attributes.

Chemical transformations, on the other hand, are extremely distinct. When the chemical composition of a substance changes, it is called a chemical change. A chemical change occurs when bonds are disrupted and new ones are created. The following are chemical change indicators:

Temperature VariationColor ChangesIdentifiable Odor (after the reaction has begun)Precipitation FormationBubbles are formed

A chemical reaction is likely to occur when two or more reactants are mixed and a change in temperature, color, or other properties is seen. These are not conclusive indicators; a chemical reaction may or may not be taking place. A chemical shift isn't usually accompanied by a change in color. Answer:

The bent wire underwent a physical change. Specifically, it changes the shape of the tie wire.

Explanation:

Physical Change is a kind of change in matter wherein the identity of the material is the same. Examples of physical change are changes in size, shape, phase of a material. In this kind of change, the chemical composition of the substance remains the same. This kind of change is usually reversible. So just like the bent tie wire, there is a change in the shape of the wire but the kind of material did not change - it is still wire. There is no change in the identity nor the chemical composition. Also, we can still return the bent wire to its original shape. This is the opposite of this is the other type of change materials undergo - the chemical change. In a chemical change, the identity of material is being changed and the chemical composition is changed also. There is a new substance formed. One example of chemical change a tie wire may have is the formation of rust. In this example, a new substance. Specifically, there is a formation of rust. This new substance is formed after a chemical reaction took place. In this case, iron reacts with oxygen. These are just examples of the many changes that occur around us.
