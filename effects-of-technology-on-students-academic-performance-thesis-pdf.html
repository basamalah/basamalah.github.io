Abstract

The objective of this study was to compare the performance of pharmacy students from a Pharmaceutical Care course, taught in both distance education (DE) and campus-based formats using active methodologies. For two semesters, students (n = 82) taking the course studied half the subject in the distance education format and half in person. Questionnaires were applied at the beginning of the semester aimed to outline the demographic profile of the students. Their grade in the course was evaluated to determine their performance. The Module 1 (Information on Medication) average on the campus-based was 7.1225 and on DE was 7.5519, (p = 0.117). The Module 2 (Pharmaceutical Services) average on the campus-based was 7.1595 and on distance education was 7.7025, (p = 0.027*). There was a difference in learning outcomes in the Pharmaceutical Care Course between face-to-face and distant education. Therefore, the student performance was better in the distance education module, indicating distance education can be satisfactorily used in Pharmacy Programs

Thesis statement: Various factors affect the academic performance of athletes, including time management, level of athletic involvement, coaching styles, and academic support systems.

Explanation:

Athletes often struggle to balance their academic responsibilities with their rigorous training and competition schedules. Several factors can contribute to their academic performance, including:

1. Time management: Athletes often have to devote a significant amount of time to their athletic training, leaving less time for studying and completing assignments. Effective time management skills are crucial for maintaining a balance between academics and athletics.

2. Level of athletic involvement: The amount of time and energy an athlete devotes to their sport can impact their academic performance. High levels of athletic involvement may result in greater physical and mental fatigue, leaving athletes with less energy to devote to their studies.

3. Coaching styles: The coaching style employed by the athletic coach can also affect the academic performance of the athletes. Coaches who prioritize academics and provide academic support and guidance to their athletes can positively impact their academic performance.

4. Academic support systems: The presence of academic support systems such as tutors, mentors, and academic advisors can also play a critical role in supporting the academic performance of athletes. These resources can help athletes manage their academic workload and develop the skills necessary for academic success.

In conclusion, the academic performance of athletes is influenced by a variety of factors, including time management, level of athletic involvement, coaching styles, and academic support systems. Addressing these factors can help athletes maintain a balance between their academic and athletic commitments, leading to improved academic performance.

29. example of introduction of thesis in education about the effects of technology on learners motivation and engagement in classroom based learning​


Answer:

Introduction:

The integration of technology in education has brought about significant changes in the way we teach and learn. While technology has the potential to enhance learning experiences and increase student engagement, it is important to carefully consider its effects on student motivation. This study aims to examine the impact of technology on learners' motivation and engagement in classroom-based learning.

Specifically, the study will address the following research questions:

How does the use of technology in the classroom affect students' motivation to learn?

How does technology use impact students' engagement in the learning process?

What factors contribute to the impact of technology on motivation and engagement?

To answer these questions, the study will utilize a mixed-methods approach, including both qualitative and quantitative data collection and analysis. The study will be conducted in a high school setting, with a sample of students and teachers participating in the research.

The findings of this study will provide valuable insights for educators and policymakers, as they work to effectively incorporate technology into the classroom in a way that enhances student motivation and engagement.
