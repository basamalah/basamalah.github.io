11. Beef steak Item cost: php 1350 percentage markup 10% No. of serving: 15% pieces find the selling price per serving?​


Answer:

The markup amount for the beef steak is 1350 x 10% = 135

So the selling price is 1350 + 135 = 1485

The selling price per serving is 1485 / 15 = 99

To find the selling price per serving of the beef steak, we need to calculate the markup amount and add it to the item cost.

First, calculate the markup amount by multiplying the item cost (1350) by the markup percentage (10%) = 135.

Then, add the markup amount to the item cost: 1350 + 135 = 1485.

So the total selling price of the beef steak is 1485.

To find the selling price per serving, we need to divide the total selling price by the number of servings.

The number of serving is 15% pieces. To convert that into a decimal, divide by 100 (15/100 = 0.15)

So the selling price per serving is 1485 / 0.15 = 9900 php

So the selling price per serving of the beef steak is 9900 php.

24. solve the elasticities and describe the significance of the value of elasticities 1. An increase in the price of good B from P 10.00 to P 15.00 cause the quantity demanded for good A to decrease by 20% from level of 80 units. 2. An increase in Jose's income from P 3000 to P 3500 cause his demand for steak to increase from 5 to 6 kilos. 3. steak sell at a price of P 50.00 per kilo. an increase in the price of steak by 10% cause your demand to decrease from 10 to 8 kilos a month. 4. When is demand and supply curve said be; a) perfectly elastic; b) elastic; c) perfectly inelastic; d) inelastic; e) unitary? and why? defend your answer.


Answer:

1.Price Elasticity of Demand (PED) of Good A = -(Percent Change in Quantity Demanded / Percent Change in Price of Good B) = -(20% / (15.00 - 10.00) / 10.00) = -1.33. The PED is elastic, meaning the change in quantity demanded of Good A is relatively large compared to the change in price of Good B.

2.Income Elasticity of Demand (YED) of steak for Jose = (Percent Change in Quantity Demanded / Percent Change in Income) = ((6 - 5) / 5) / ((3500 - 3000) / 3000) = 0.2. The YED is low, meaning the change in quantity demanded of steak is relatively small compared to the change in Jose's income.

3.Price Elasticity of Demand (PED) of steak = -(Percent Change in Quantity Demanded / Percent Change in Price) = -((8 - 10) / 10) / (0.10 * 50.00) = 1.67. The PED is elastic, meaning the change in quantity demanded of steak is relatively large compared to the change in price.

4.a) Perfectly Elastic Demand occurs when the quantity demanded changes infinitely in response to a small change in price.

b) Elastic Demand occurs when the percentage change in quantity demanded is greater than the percentage change in price.

c) Perfectly Inelastic Demand occurs when the quantity demanded does not change in response to a change in price.

d) Inelastic Demand occurs when the percentage change in quantity demanded is less than the percentage change in price.

e) Unitary Elastic Demand occurs when the percentage change in quantity demanded is equal to the percentage change in price.

The significance of the value of elasticities is that it shows the responsiveness of quantity demanded or supplied to changes in price or other factors like income. It helps firms and policymakers in pricing decisions, forecasting future demand or supply, and devising marketing strategies.
