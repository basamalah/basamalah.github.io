6. according to mohandas Gandhi if we are the reach real peace in the world we shall have to begin with the children.Explain with basic examples​


Answer:

Mohandas Gandhi believed that to achieve real peace in the world, we must start with the children. This is because children are the future of our society, and by teaching them to be peaceful and compassionate, we can create a more peaceful world for everyone.

One way to do this is through education. Schools can teach children about the importance of peace and how to resolve conflicts in a peaceful way. For example, children can learn how to listen to others, express their feelings, and find common ground. This can help them to be more understanding of people who are different from themselves.

Another way to promote peace is through community outreach programs. Children can volunteer in their community by helping at a local food bank, planting trees in a park, or visiting elderly people at a nursing home. These activities can help children to develop empathy and learn to appreciate the value of diversity.

By starting with the children and teaching them to be peaceful and compassionate, we can create a more peaceful world for everyone.

26. What kind of peace is illustrated by Kalidasa's poem


Answer:

A sonnet

Explanation:

consists of fourteen lines divided into an eight-line unit known as an octet,

Answer:

"Peace" is a sonnet

Explanation:

A sonnet consists of fourteen lines divided into an eight-line unit known as an octet, and a six-line unit known as a sestet. The octet and sestet can together form a single stanza, or appear as two separate stanzas (which is the case in 'Peace').
