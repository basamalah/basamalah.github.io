5. A 52kg student runs up a flight of stairs of vertical height 3.0m in 4.7s. Calculate the power output


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The power of an object doing a work W in a given time t is given by

[tex]\boxed{P = \frac{W}{t}}[/tex]

where:

P = power

W = work

t = time

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for F

F = weight = mg

F = (52 kg)(9.8 m/s²)

F = 509.6 N

Solving for W

W = Fd

W = (509.6 N)(3.0 m)

W = 1,528.8 J

Solving for P

[tex]P = \dfrac{W}{t}[/tex]

[tex]P = \dfrac{\text{1,528.8 J}}{\text{4.7 s}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P = \text{325.28 W}}[/tex]

Therefore, the power output is 325.28 W.

Answer:

W = 3920 Joules

Explanation:

Formula used: W = mgh

Given: m = 20kg, g = 9.8 m/s², h = 20m

Let us substitute the given:

W = (20kg)(20m)(9.8 m/s²)

Why Joules (J) is the unit used?

One joule is defined as the amount of work done when a force of one newton is exerted through a distance of one meter.

Problem:

a boy has a weight of 500 n and can run up flight of stairs in 5 seconds there are 10 stairs and each of them is of height of 0.10 m what is the boys power​

Note:

The gravitational acceleration on Earth is equal to 9.8 m/s² The formula for power is ( P = W / t ) or ( P = F × d / t )

Given Data:

F ( Force ) = 500 N or 500 kg·m/s²

d ( distance ) =  0.1 m

t ( time ) = 5 s

P ( Power ) = ? unknown

Solution:

The formula for power is ( P = W / t ) or ( P = F × d / t )

P = F × d / t

   =  500 kg·m/s² × 0.1 m / 5 s

   = 50 J / 5 s

   = 10 J/s

   = 10 Watts

24. A 75K jogger runs up a long flight of stairs in 8.5 seconds. The vertical height of the stairs is 15 meters. What is the power output of the person in watts and horsepower? Show your solution. Someone, please help.


Answer:

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The joggers power output approximately 1298.36 Watts and in Horsepower is 1.74 HP

Explanation:

To find the jogger's power output, we have to use the formula below:

[tex]\boxed{\bold{P = \frac{Fd}{t}}}[/tex]

Let,

[tex]P[/tex] be the Jogger's power output [tex]F[/tex] be the mass of the Jogger.

Substitute the following mass, time taken, and displacement to the formula.

[tex]\rm{P = \frac{(75)(9.81)(15)}{8.5}} \\\\ \rm{P=\frac{11036.25}{8.5}} \\\\ \rm{P \approx 1298.38 \ Watts}[/tex]

Now, we convert the Watts to Horsepower. To convert it, we simply divide the given Watts by 745.69.

[tex]\rm{Horsepower = Watts \times \left(\frac{1}{ 745.69}\right) = 1298.38 \times \left(\frac{1}{745.69}\right) \approx 1.74 \ HP }[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The joggers power output approximately 1298.36 Watts and in Horsepower is 1.74 HP
