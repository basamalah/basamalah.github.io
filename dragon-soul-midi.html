16. PA HELP PO!Ben claims that the average midi-chlorians a jedi had in his time was 15,000 with a standard deviation of 1500. Luke doesn’t believe it and claims that it is lesser than that so tested his new set of Jedi students and found out that with 12 Jedi’s they had an average of 16,500 midi-chlorians. Test if the average midi-chlorians is less than 15,000. Use 1% level of significance​


Answer:

The null hypothesis is that the average midi-chlorians of a Jedi is 15,000 and the alternate hypothesis is that the average midi-chlorians of a Jedi is less than 15,000.

The sample size is 12 and the average midi-chlorians of the sample is 16,500.

We can use the Z-test to test the hypothesis.

The Z-score is calculated as:

Z = (x - mu)/(sigma/sqrt(n))

Where x is the sample mean, mu is the population mean, sigma is the population standard deviation and n is the sample size.

Substituting the values, we get:

Z = (16,500 - 15,000)/(1500/sqrt(12)) = 1.11

The critical Z-score for 1% level of significance is -2.33.

Since 1.11 > -2.33, we cannot reject the null hypothesis.

Therefore, we can conclude that the average midi-chlorians of a Jedi is not less than 15,000 with 1% level of significance.

29. Define Rational Soul, Spiritual Soul, Appetitive Soul


Answer:

Definition of Rational Soul, Spiritual Soul, Appetitive Soul

Explanation:

Rational Soul

: the soul that in the scholastic tradition has independent existence apart from the body and that is the characteristic animating principle of human life as distinguished from animal or vegetable life.

Spiritual Soul

: In many religious, philosophical, and mythological traditions, the soul is the incorporeal essence of a living being. Soul or psyche comprises the mental abilities of a living being: reason, character, feeling, consciousness, memory, perception, thinking, etc.

Appetitive Soul

: This is the part that governs desire. It “partakes of reason insofar as it complies with reason and accepts its leadership” (1102b31). It is partly rational (because it can be trained to follow reason) and partly irrational (because it is not itself a faculty of thought).
