5. this assingment is from abm course and the subject is business organization management Why is it important for companies and organizations to maintain corporate integrity?


Answer:

It Gives Your Business a Competitive Edge

Trust is not earned easily these days as clients are becoming warier of the businesses they choose to interact with. For that reason, big companies put plenty of effort and resources into proving they conduct business in a morally sound way.

Most of the time, people just know better.

In search of an alternative to corporate vendors, many clients turn to small businesses, just like yours, to source products and services. That’s why it’s even more important that you run your business with integrity and meet that demand.

Companies that live and breathe their values are the ones people are willing to spend their money with. If you deliver quality, play fair with your clients and offer transparency on top of that, people will keep knocking at your door.

2. It Helps Save Face During a Crisis

Back in 2017, United Airlines got entangled in a series of slipups that gave them plenty of bad press. It all started with a passenger who was injured when he was forcibly removed (the company called it “re-accommodation”) from an overbooked flight.

Instead of owning up to their mistake, the CEO attempted to attack the passenger in a public statement and blame him for causing the ruckus. After a video of the incident went viral, the airline was hit by a wave of criticism with many boycotting United flights altogether.

Had United acted with integrity and admitted wrongdoing, they could’ve avoided much of the aftermath and saved face. The alternative proved highly detrimental and left the company scrambling to regain reputation.

Whenever you find yourself in a situation where you’ve made a bad business decision, don’t try to sweep it under the rug or avoid responsibility. Suck it up and let your clients know you’ve learned your lesson.

3. It Makes Difficult Decisions Easier

Imagine a cloak-and-dagger scenario in which you receive an e-mail from a former employee of your direct competitor. The person claims to have insider information that could potentially ruin that company’s public image.

17. As an ABM student, is it important to know about the concepts of obligation and contract? Why? What have you learned from the course (Business Laws and Regulations)?​


Answer:

As an ABM student, it is important to know about the concepts of obligation and contract because they form the foundation of business transactions and relationships. Understanding the legal principles of obligation and contract can help you navigate complex business situations and make informed decisions that protect your interests and the interests of your organization.

In a business context, an obligation is a legal duty or responsibility that one party has to another. Obligations can arise from a contract, a law, or a moral or ethical principle. Understanding obligations is important because they can affect your business's rights, duties, and liabilities.

A contract is a legally binding agreement between two or more parties that sets out the terms and conditions of a transaction or relationship. A contract can be written, oral, or implied by the actions of the parties involved. Understanding the elements of a contract and how to create, interpret, and enforce them is crucial to conducting successful business dealings.

In the course Business Laws and Regulations, you may have learned about the different types of obligations and contracts, such as express and implied contracts, and how to determine the validity and enforceability of a contract. You may also have learned about the legal principles that govern contracts, such as capacity, consent, consideration, and legality, and how to address contract disputes and breaches. Overall, the course may have provided you with a strong foundation in the legal principles that govern business transactions and relationships, which can be valuable in your career as an ABM student.
