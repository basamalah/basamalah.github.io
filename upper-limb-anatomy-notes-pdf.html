For anatomists, the upper limb consists of the arm (the upper arm), the forearm (the lower arm), and the hand. The arm consists of a single bone, the humerus. The forearm consists of two bones, the ulna and radius. And the hand consists of 27 bones, which are grouped into the phalanges, metacarpals, and carpals.

23. Major veins that drain the upper and lower limbs​


Superior vena cava ⇒ drains blood from the upper limb (upper extremities, head and neck).

Inferior Vena Cava ⇒ Drains blood from the lower limbs and middle body.

Superior vena cava

Is a large vein that receives the deoxygenated blood which comes from the systemic circulation in which the oxygen was already distributed in upper region of the body including the head, neck, shoulder or the upper extremities.

Inferior Vena Cava

Is a large vein that receives the deoxygenated blood coming from the systemic circulation of the lower extremities including the legs and abdomen.

Vein returns to blood to the heart so that it will be passed to lungs for it to be oxygenated and distribute it again in the different parts of the body via the artery.
