7. Steve invested a sum of money at 4%.He invested twice as much at 5%.The total annual income from these investment was $210.Find the amount he invested at each rate​


CALCULATING INTEREST GAINED

The formula for determining the interest gained after investing a certain amount of money can be expressed by this equation:

i = Prt

where:

i = refers to the interest gained after t yearsP = refers to the initial or principal amount of money investedr = refers to the interest rate in decimal formt = refers to the number of time (months, years, etc.)

Let us try solving this tricky word problem involving interest with the help of our knowledge in algebra:

Steve invested a sum of money at 4%. He invested twice as much at 5%. The total annual income from these investment was $210. Find the amount he invested at each rate​.

So, let us represent the variable x as the initial amount of invested money in the first account while 2x in the second account since it was stated that he invested twice as much there. Going back to the formula of interest, we can substitute them to P. For the interest rates, 4% and 5%, they are converted to 0.04 and 0.05 respectively since 4% is equivalent to 4/100 while 5% is 5/100. When a number is in percent form and you want to convert them to decimal or fraction, remember that their denominators will always be 100.

At first investment: i = Prt = x(0.04)(1) = 0.04x

The t is substituted by 1 since t = 1 year.

At second investment: i = 2x(0.05)(1) = 0.10x

Since, it was stated that the total annual income that Steve gained from these investments is 210 dollars, then we will add the two terms and equate it to 210. The equation will become:

0.04x + 0.10x = 210

The next step is to solve for the value of x.

Combining like terms, 0.04x and 0.10x, will result to 0.14x.

0.14x = 210

Let us divide both sides by 0.14 to isolate x. So:

0.14x/0.14 = 210/0.14

x = 1,500

This means that the value of x is 1,500 and this is the amount of money that Steve invested in the first account. To obtain the value of money he invested in the second account, just multiply 1,500 by 2 and so we get 3,000.

Hence, Steve invested 1,500 dollars in his first account with 4% interest rate and 3,000 dollars in his second account with 5% interest rate.

Trading and available-for-sale debt securities should be reported at fair value, whereas held-to-maturity debt securities should be reported at amortized cost.

18. A man has 16000 to invest. He can invest part of it at 8% and the other is 12%. How much should he invest at each rate to earn an income of 9% of his investment?


Let x be the amount invested at 8% and y be the amount invested at 12%. The total investment is $16,000 so:

x + y = 16000 (equation 1)

We know that the man wants to earn an income of 9% on his total investment. So the total income earned from the two investments is:

0.08x + 0.12y = 0.09(16000) (equation 2)

Simplifying equation 2:

0.08x + 0.12y = 1440

Multiplying equation 1 by 0.08:

0.08x + 0.08y = 1280

Subtracting the above equation from equation 2:

0.12y - 0.08y = 1440 - 1280

0.04y = 160

y = 4000

Substituting y = 4000 into equation 1:

x + 4000 = 16000

x = 12000

Therefore, the man should invest $12,000 at 8% and $4,000 at 12% to earn an income of 9%.

Answer:

Let x be the amount invested at 10%.

Then the amount invested at 14% is x - 6000.

The total interest earned from these two investments is 4200, so we can write:

0.10x + 0.14(x - 6000) = 4200

Simplifying and solving for x, we get:

0.10x + 0.14x - 840 = 4200

0.24x = 5040

x = 21000

Therefore, the amount invested at 14% is:

x - 6000 = 21000 - 6000 = 15000

So the answer is that $15,000 was invested at 14%.
