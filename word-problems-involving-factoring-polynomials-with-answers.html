Find the dimension of the garden. Questions: 1. How do you represent the width and length of the rectangular garden? 2. How do you represent the equation? 3. How do you find the dimension of the garden? What factoring method did you used? 4. In what instance, will you find it difficult to determine the answer? 5. How would you know if your solution/answer is correct?


1. Given that the length is 8 ft. more than the width, we can let [tex] w + 8 [/tex] and [tex] w[/tex] be the length and the width of the rectangular garden, respectively.

2. We already know that the area of a rectangle is equal to the product of its length and width. The area of the rectangular garden is [tex] 240[/tex] square feet, so we can represent the equation as

[tex] A = LW \\ 240 = (w + 8)(w) [/tex]

3. Transform the equation into its standard form then factor, if possible.

[tex] w^2 + 8w = 240 \\ w^2 + 8w - 240 = 0 [/tex]

To factor [tex] w^2 + 8w - 240 = 0, [/tex] find two numbers which when added gives [tex] 8 [/tex] and when multiplied gives [tex] -240. [/tex] This factoring method is known as factoring by pairs.

So, [tex] 20[/tex] and [tex] -12 [/tex] are the two numbers, such that;

[tex] 20 + (-12) = 8 [/tex][tex] 20(-12) = -240 [/tex]

Now, we can rewrite the equation as

[tex] w^2 + 20w - 18w - 240 = 0 \\ w(w + 20) - 18(w + 20) = 0 [/tex]

Taking [tex] w + 20 [/tex] as a common factor, we get;

[tex] (w + 20)(w - 12) = 0 [/tex]

Equate each factor to [tex] 0 [/tex].

[tex] w + 20 \\ w_1 = -20 \\ \\ w - 12 = 0 \\ w_2 = 12 [/tex]

We want [tex] w [/tex] to be positive, so [tex] w = 12\ \text{ft.}[/tex] It follows that the length is equal to [tex] 12 + 8 [/tex] or [tex] 20\ \text{ft.} [/tex]

Verifying,

[tex] A = LW \\ 240 = (20)(12) \\ 240 \overset{\checkmark}{=} 240 [/tex]

4. It is difficult to factor a polynomial using this method if the given involves large numbers.

5. By verifying, I would know if my solution/answer is correct.
