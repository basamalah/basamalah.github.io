Answer:

1.See image below(The trajectory is parabolic)

2.Slower,because it encounters air resistance and moves against gravitational acceleration.

3.Decreases,because to conserve mechanical energy,if the potential energy increases(because sipa is ascending and its height is increasing),the potential energy must decrease until it reaches zenith,where PE is maximum and KE=0.

4.Increases

5.Increases,because PE is directly dependent on height.

6.We don't know what t is,but I think t is the zenith or highest point reached,so the answer is kinetic energy,as we said earlier to conserve mechanical energy.

7.Faster,because the acceleration due gravity accelerates the sipa in negative y direction.

8.Increases,because of gravity,its velocity increases,and kinetic energy is dependent on velocity.Therefore,as the sipa moves downward,its KE becoming increasingly larger.

9.Decreases,because it is now headed towards the lowest point,or the ground.

10.Decreases,because since GPE is dependent on height,and height decreases as the sipa moves downward,therefore GPE will decrease until it reaches the ground,where KE is at maximum and GPE is zero,again to conserve mechanical energy.
