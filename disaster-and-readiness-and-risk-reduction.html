22. WARM UP DISCUSSION Question: What are your initial Disaster Readiness and Risk Reduction? Explain.​


What are your initial Disaster Readiness and Risk Reduction?

Disaster risk reduction is the policy goal of anticipating and decreasing risk (DRR). Although disaster risk management (DRM) and disaster risk reduction (DRR) are sometimes used interchangeably, DRM can be considered as the execution of DRR because it outlines the actions that try to achieve the goal of risk reduction.

Because disaster risk is a sign of weak development, lowering disaster risk necessitates incorporating disaster risk reduction policy and practice into long-term development goals.

Dealing with disasters has traditionally focused on emergency response, but as the twentieth century progressed, it became clear that disasters are not natural (even if the associated hazard is), and that the only way to prevent losses and mitigate disaster impacts is to reduce and manage conditions of vulnerability.

26. How important is disaster readiness and risk reduction in one's life? ​


The Importance of Disaster Readiness and Risk Reduction

With a preventive ethic, disaster risk reduction (DRR) seeks to reduce the harm caused by natural disasters such as earthquakes, flooding, droughts, and cyclones.

The definition and method of catastrophe risk reduction is to minimize disaster risks by systematically analyzing and reducing the causes of disasters. Reduced exposure to threats, reduced vulnerability to persons and infrastructure, wise land and environmental maintenance, and improved emergency preparedness are all examples of disaster risk mitigation.

The Importance of It

It aids in the identification and mapping of local capacities to deal with these hazards. Finally, the DRR solution enables one to respond to disasters effectively while reducing the likelihood of future disasters. It also means that by removing or reinstating vital vulnerabilities, our emergency response causes no harm.

DRR seeks to reduce catastrophe risk while also encouraging sustainable growth, protecting people and their infrastructure, livelihoods, health, and cultural and environmental properties. Countries will create disaster resilience by DRR.
