20. The literature in this period includes mythical creatures.


Pre-colonial Period.

The term "pre-colonial literature" refers to any form of written work created before the Spanish colonization of the Americas. This includes chants, proverbs, songs, and folk narratives. All of these were passed on verbally from one generation to the next. There is a wide variety and individuality in Philippine folk narratives.

Tigmo in Cebuano, Bugtong in Tagalog, patototdon in Bicol, and paktakon in Ilonggo are examples of pre-colonial literary compositions passed down through traditional narratives, speeches, and songs. Before the Spanish colonization of the Philippines, the natives used a writing system called baybayin (to spell). Baybayin is the ancestor of some contemporary scripts used in the Philippines.

One may argue that the pre-colonial Philippines was predominately matriarchal, with women's perspectives accorded great importance in political and religious concerns (they also headed the rituals as the babaylans). Even husbands had to walk behind their wives as a sign of respect for the position they held in the family.
