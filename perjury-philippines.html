2. What is Police perjury? ​


Answer:

Police perjury is the act of a police officer knowingly giving false testimony. It is typically used in a criminal trial to "make the case" against defendants believed by the police to be guilty when irregularities during the suspects' arrest or search threaten to result in their acquittal.

Exuberant – means ‘full of happy high spirits and vitality (adj.) Example: I’m always exuberant when it comes to wattpad, you know?

Infamous – means ‘having an extremely bad reputation‘ (adj.) Example: Dave is infamous for being a bully.

Hurtle – means ‘to move or travel at very high speed’ (v.) Example: I have no choice but to hurtle in order to escape.

Gaudy – means ‘brightly colored or showily decorated to an unpleasant or vulgar degree’ (adj.) Example: Paul’s work is gaudy and I don’t like it, it’s unpleasant for me.

Loath – means ‘unwilling or reluctant to do something’ (adj.) Example: Letting my classmates to copy my answers is loath to me, it’s their own grade anyway.

Nimble – means ‘agile, fast, and light in movement’ (adj.) Example: My Dad is kind of nimble that’s why it’s hard for me to defeat him in table tennis.

Perjury – means ‘the telling of a lie after having taken an oath to tell the truth’ (n.) Example: Even if I already know the truth, I asked Gen if she’s the one who took my scissors and I hate her of her perjury, she even swear that she’s telling the truth.

Perturb – means ‘to disturb and trouble somebody’ (v.) Example: I really hate it when someone perturbs me while I’m busy.

Subtle – means ‘slight and not obvious’ (adj.) Example: The beauty of Candice is subtle that’s why many believe she won’t win the pageant.

Void – means ‘having no legal force’ (adj.) Example: Arnel and his wife didn’t divorce yet so Arnel and Sofia’s, his current girlfriend, wedding is void.

Note: Always remember that English is so complicated. Words have a lot of meanings, it depends on the use if the sentence.

A. PD 247, also known as the Anti-Fencing Law, punishes the act of fencing or buying and selling stolen goods. It specifically prohibits the act of buying, selling, or receiving any stolen property knowing it to be stolen. On the other hand, Article 164 of the Revised Penal Code punishes the act of possessing or concealing any stolen goods.

In summary, the difference between the two is that PD 247 punishes the act of buying and selling stolen goods, while Article 164 of the Revised Penal Code punishes the act of possessing or concealing stolen goods.

C. False testimony and perjury are not exactly the same. False testimony is a broader term that refers to any statement made under oath that is not truthful. Perjury, on the other hand, is a specific type of false testimony that involves lying while under oath in a judicial proceeding.

Perjury is a more serious offense than false testimony because it involves intentionally lying in a legal setting, which undermines the integrity of the justice system. False testimony, on the other hand, can refer to any false statement made under oath, including in administrative or legislative proceedings.
