11. difference betweem emissive and non emissive display​


Answer:

Emissive displays are devices that convert electrical energy into light; where the image is produced directly on the screen. Non-emissive displays use optical effects to convert sunlight into graphical patterns and shapes; where the light is produced behind the screen and the image is formed by filtering this light.

14. Give 5 example of Alpha Emission, Beta Emission, Positron Emission


Answer:

Many alpha emitters occur naturally in the environment. For example, alpha particles are given off by radionuclides such as uranium-238, radium-226, and other members of the naturally occurring uranium, thorium and actinium decay series which are present in varying amounts in nearly all rocks, soils, and water.

Many beta emitters occur naturally in the radioisotopes found in the natural radioactive decay chains of uranium, thorium and actinium. Examples include lead-210, bismuth-214 and thallium-206. Beta emitters are also commonly found in the radioactive products of nuclear fission.

In positron emission, the atomic number Z decreases by one while the mass number A remains the same. Magnesium-23 has 12 protons and 11 neutrons. The neutron:proton ratio is 11:12 or 0.92:1. It undergoes positron emission to form sodium-23.

23. 1. Describe the material emission of Shield Volcano. 2. Describe the material emission of Cinder Cone Volcano.3. Describe the material emission of Composite Volcano. ​


[tex] \huge\green{Answer:}[/tex]

1. Describe the material emission of Shield Volcano.

Shield volcanoes have small amounts of pyroclastic material, most of which accumulates near the eruptive vents, resulting from fire fuming events. Thus, shield volcanoes typically form from nonexplosive eruptions of low viscosity basaltic magma. Shield volcanoes have low slopes and consist almost entirely of lavas.

2. Describe the material emission of Cinder Cone Volcano.

Cinder cones are the simplest type of volcano. They are built from particles and blobs of congealed lava ejected from a single vent. As the gas-charged lava is blown violently into the air, it breaks into small fragments that solidify and fall as cinders around the vent to form a circular or oval cone.

3. Describe the material emission of Composite Volcano.

Stratovolcanoes/Composite Volcano show inter-layering of lava flows and pyroclastic material, which is why they are sometimes called composite volcanoes. Due to the higher viscosity of magmas erupted from these volcanoes, they are usually more explosive than shield volcanoes.

25. a country pledges to reduce its annual CO2 emissions by 3% per year. if the emissions in 2022 are 3,030 Mt ( metric megaton), what are the maximum allowable emissions in the year 2028​


Answer:

Assuming the country meets its annual reduction targets, the maximum allowable emissions in 2028 can be calculated as follows:

- In 2022, the emissions are 3,030 Mt.

- To reduce emissions by 3% per year, we need to multiply the previous year's emissions by 0.97 (100% - 3%).

- So, the maximum allowable emissions in 2023 would be 3,030 * 0.97 = 2,937.1 Mt.

- We can repeat this calculation for each subsequent year to find the maximum allowable emissions. For example:

2024: 2,937.1 * 0.97 = 2,845.4 Mt

2025: 2,845.4 * 0.97 = 2,755.8 Mt

2026: 2,755.8 * 0.97 = 2,668.1 Mt

2027: 2,668.1 * 0.97 = 2,582.2 Mt

2028: 2,582.2 * 0.97 = 2,498.1 Mt

Therefore, the maximum allowable emissions in the year 2028 would be 2,498.1 Mt, assuming the country meets its annual reduction targets of 3%.
