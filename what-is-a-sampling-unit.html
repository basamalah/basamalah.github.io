8. Randy is now assigned to the surgical unit. He is given a piece of living tissue and asked to put it in the fluid before taking the sample to the pathology lab. What essential characteristic must such fluid have so that it will not damage any cells in the tissue?


Answer:

Isotonic solution

Explanation:

An isotonic solution is a fluid that has the same solute concentration as living cells. With this level of fluid pressure equilibrium, the living tissue sample that will be tested will not lose fluids towards to solution, causing it it shrink, or absorb the fluids from the solution which may cause the tissue to swell and burst.

In clinical setting, an isotonic solution is achieved by using plain normal saline solution (PNSS), which contain 9 grams salt (NaCl) dissolved in one liter of water, which makes it a close approximation to the osmolarity of NaCl in blood.

Hypertonic solutions are solitons whose solute concentration is greater than that of living cells. When cells are immersed in these solutions, water from the cells move out to the solution, causing the cell to shrink and die. Hypotonic solution, on the other hand, contains less solutes than the cells. This cause water to go inside the cells, resulting to swelling of the cells and lysis.

11. If an impure sample of inert gas is heated from 27 °C to 500 °C with an initial pressure of 7.45 bar, what would be its final pressure (in bar)? Round off your answer to three (3) significant figures and omit the unit.


Calulating the Final Pressure.

Given

Initial Pressure (P₁) = 7.45 barInitial Temperature (T₁) = 27 °CFinal Temperature (T₂) = 500 °CFinal Pressure (P₂) = ?

Converting the Temperatures to °C to °K.

For T₁

°K = °C + 273

°K = 27 + 273

= 300 °K

For T₂

°K = °C + 273

°K = 500 +273

= 773 °K

Use Gay-Lussac's Law to calculate the final pressure.

P₂ = P₁ • T₂ / T₁

P₂ = 7.45 • 773 / 300

P₂ = 5758.85 / 300

P₂ = 19.2 bar
