Here are the SWOT analysis results of the statements provided in the question

SWOT (Strength, Weaknesses, Opportunity, and Threats) analysis is a management strategy which functions to help your organization prosper by analyzing the four aspects which are apparent in your case. Strengths and weaknesses are opposite to each other, but both are something that can be changed. Few examples are the members of the organization, the organization's asset, as well as the location.

Meanwhile, opportunities and threats are more similar to a critical analysis of the situation that the organization is in. However, this is also not limited to other areas as well, for example, an outside factor or aspect that can disrupt or hinder the organization's process. To understand better about SWOT analysis, take a look at the classification of an example of scenario below:

Weakness. The quality of our product is something that can be changed or improved. It is up to us to turn it into the strength in the company (improvisation). Strength. Adequate supply for the working capital is an asset that can be further developed in order to increase the production rate or quality as well.Customer demands are an example of outside factor that have the potential to bring the business to succession or to ruin if not handled correctly. Therefore, this is an example of opportunity.Poor management control is a weakness that should be fixed, because this is something that can be changed.Rising cost is more like a threat that might threaten the business' sustainability.Ability of the owner is something owner. However, as this does not specify whether the ability is good or not, then this is an opportunity.Government's policy that can put the business as a disadvantage is more fit to be categorized as a threat.Materials shortage is a problem that the company can't control. Hence, this is categorized as a threat.Lack of knowledge can be improved, and thus this is a weakness.Cheap raw materials will be an opportunity for an increase of production rate and amount. So, this one is opportunity.
