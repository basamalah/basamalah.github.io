18. Characterization of casein and soy protein 1. Biuret Test: casein and soyprotein + Benedict’s reagent Expected result: 2. Hopkins-Cole Test:casein and soy protein + hopkin’s reagent Expected Result:3. Millons Test: casein and soyprotein + Millon’s reagentExpected result: 4. Xanthoproteic Test:casein and soyprotein + Xanthoproteic reagent Expected Result:​


Answer:

The tests you mentioned are used to detect the presence of proteins in a sample and are not specific to casein or soy protein. However, here are the expected results for each test with casein and soy protein:

Biuret Test:

This test is used to detect the presence of peptide bonds in proteins. When casein or soy protein is mixed with Benedict's reagent and heated, the solution should turn from blue to green, indicating the presence of protein.

Expected result: Green coloration.

Hopkins-Cole Test:

This test is used to detect the presence of tryptophan in proteins. When casein or soy protein is mixed with Hopkins reagent, a deep violet coloration should develop, indicating the presence of tryptophan.

Expected Result: Deep violet coloration.

Millons Test:

This test is used to detect the presence of tyrosine in proteins. When casein or soy protein is mixed with Millon's reagent and heated, a brick-red coloration should develop, indicating the presence of tyrosine.

Expected result: Brick-red coloration.

Xanthoproteic Test:

This test is used to detect the presence of aromatic amino acids (tryptophan, phenylalanine, and tyrosine) in proteins. When casein or soy protein is mixed with nitric acid, a yellow coloration should develop, indicating the presence of aromatic amino acids.

Expected result: Yellow coloration.
