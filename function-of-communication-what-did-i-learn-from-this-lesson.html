Further explanation:KWLH

KWLH is a comprehension technique created to assist students in using existing knowledge, creating questions, and thinking critically about newly acquired knowledge. The abbreviation means:

K: What they may already KNOW about the topic or subject W: What they WANT to learn L: What they LEARN in real-time H: HOW additional learning can occur

KWLH:

Is an effective tool for teachers to help children with their comprehension skills.As a cooperative group activityEncourages students to learn new material and connect it to what they currently know.

A graphic organizer called the K-W-H-L CHART helps students keep track of what they already know what they want to know where they will find the knowledge, and what they have learned about a subject. It is employed prior to, during, and the following reading and/or research.

KWLH Function

K-W-L charts are visual organizers that aid in the pre-, during-, and post-organization of knowledge by students. They can be used to introduce new material, draw on past knowledge, communicate unit goals, and assess students' progress.

Choose a different topic, hang a blank KWL transparency from the overhead, and give each student a copy to help them complete a KWL chart. Let the pupils independently fill out the chart's “Know” area. Discuss responses, generate further ideas, and share individual comments with the class.

The K-W-L technique is a multistep process that involves brainstorming, categorizing, establishing goals through questions, and analyzing the responses to those questions.

Students learn through observation, investigation, and inference from facts during indirect instruction, which is a student-centered method of teaching. Instead of providing direct instruction in this instructional paradigm, academics play the function of a facilitator or supporter. K-W-L diagrams are a helpful teaching tool for primary educators. They were initially developed by Donna Ogle in 1986 as a teaching technique to assist pupils who had trouble comprehending a text.
