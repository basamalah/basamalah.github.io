1. What are the things from the grownups that you really like when you are a child but when youbecame a grownup you envy the child. State three (3).​


Answer:

From a child's standpoint, adults experience a lot of privileges compared to them. The freedom and the capacity of doing a lot of things are more evident especially when the child do not have the ability to experience them. Example of which are having financial capacity to buy the things they like, not being hindered by nap time so they can enjoy play time outside, and not having the pressure of school to burden them. But as time passes by, these said "adulting" goals are not as fun as they seemed to be.  

Explanation:Money

Having the means to buy the things that we want seems like a dream. But this so-called privilege also entails a huge amount of responsibilities. When there is income, paying the bills usually follows.

Sleep

Part of being an adult is having the ability to balance different activities. These activities come in the form of working,  spending time with family and friends, and spending time for leisure activities. With all these activities lined up for adults, having rest time is essential. But often times, this is neglected due to demands from these said activities.

School

Demanding as it seems, adults actually miss the time that  they were in school. Compared to the adult life, attending school appears to be a walk in the park. Also, adults never cease to strive to learn new things as they get older.

11. my favorite symbol Christmas essay​


Answer:

The Christmas season is a time for joy, celebration, and spending time with loved ones. One of the most iconic symbols of Christmas is the Christmas tree, a evergreen tree decorated with lights, ornaments, and other festive decorations. The tradition of bringing a tree into the home dates back to the 16th century, and it is a beloved symbol of the holiday season for many people around the world.

Another important symbol of Christmas is the star, which is often associated with the story of the three wise men who followed a star to Bethlehem to visit the baby Jesus. The star is a symbol of hope, faith, and guidance, and it is often depicted on Christmas trees and other decorations.

Gift-giving is also an important part of the Christmas tradition, and the image of Santa Claus delivering presents to children on Christmas Eve is a well-known symbol of the holiday. Santa, with his red suit, white beard, and jolly demeanor, is a beloved figure for many children and adults alike.

In addition to these symbols, Christmas is also associated with certain colors, such as red, green, and white. These colors are often used in Christmas decorations, and they are thought to represent the warmth and joy of the season.

Overall, Christmas is a time of celebration and joy, and the symbols associated with the holiday help to capture the spirit of the season. Whether it is a Christmas tree, a star, or a gift from Santa, these symbols remind us of the love, hope, and happiness that the holiday season brings.
