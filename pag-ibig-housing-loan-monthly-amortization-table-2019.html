Answer:

To find the monthly payment, you can use the formula:

monthly payment = (r * P) / (1 - (1 + r)^(-n))

where r is the monthly interest rate, P is the principal of the loan, and n is the number of months of the loan.

In this case, the principal of the loan is P3,200,000, the number of months of the loan is 20 years * 12 months/year = 240 months, and the monthly interest rate is 12%/12 months/year = 1%.

Plugging these values into the formula, we get:

monthly payment = (0.01 * 3200000) / (1 - (1 + 0.01)^(-240))

= (32000) / (1 - 1.01^(-240))

= 32000 / (1 - 0.3747)

= 32000 / 0.6253

= P51,236.17.

So the monthly payment is P51,236.17.

7. How much is the monthly amortization on an automobile loan of P900,000 to be amortized over a 5-year period at a rate 9.5% compounded monthly?​


Answer:

P22,811.33

Step-by-step explanation:

To calculate the monthly amortization on an automobile loan, we can use the formula for the fixed-term annuity.

The formula is:

A = P * r * (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1)

where:

A = monthly amortization

P = principal or the amount borrowed (P900,000 in this case)

r = monthly interest rate (9.5%/12 = 0.0079167)

n = number of payments (5 years x 12 months/year = 60 months)

So, plugging in the values:

A = 900,000 * 0.0079167 * (1 + 0.0079167)^60 / ((1 + 0.0079167)^60 - 1)

A = P22,811.33

So the monthly amortization would be P22,811.33.
