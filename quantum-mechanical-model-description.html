5. Compare Quantum mechanical model with Bohr Model?​


Answer:

In the Bohr Model, the electron is treated as a particle in fixed orbits around the nucleus. In the Quantum Mechanical Model, the electron is treated mathematically as a wave. The Bohr model was a one-dimensional model that used one quantum number to describe the distribution of electrons in the atom.

23. quantum mechanical model​


Answer:

The energy of an electron is quantized i.e. an electron can only have certain specific values of energy.

The quantized energy of an electron is the allowed solution of the Schrödinger wave equation and it is the result of wave like properties of electron.

As per Heisenberg’s Uncertainty principle, the exact position and momentum of an electron cannot be determined. So the only probability of finding an electron at a position can be determined and it is |ψ|2 at that point where ψ represents the wave-function of that electron.

An atomic orbital is the wave-function (ψ) of an electron in an atom. Whenever an electron is described by a wave-function, it occupies atomic orbital. As an electron can have many wave-functions, there are many atomic orbitals for the electron. Every wave-function or atomic orbital have some shape and energy associated with it. All the information about the electron in an atom is stored in its orbital wave function ψ and quantum mechanics makes it possible to extract this information out of ψ.

The probability of finding an electron at a point within an atom is proportional to the square of the orbital wave function i.e., | ψ |2 at that point. | ψ |2 is known as probability density and is always positive.


24. Describe the Quantum Mechanical Model. Discuss how the Quantum Mechanical Model describes the energies and positions of the electrons. How do electrons are arranged?​


Describe the Quantum Mechanical Model. Discuss how the Quantum Mechanical Model describes the energies and positions of the electrons. How do electrons are arranged?

The quantum mechanical model is a theoretical framework that describes the behavior of particles, such as electrons, on a very small scale, such as within an atom. In this model, electrons are not thought of as particles with definite positions and energies, but rather as probability waves that spread out in space and can be described by a set of quantum states.

According to the quantum mechanical model, the energy and position of an electron are not fixed, but rather they exist as a range of possible values, known as energy levels or orbitals. These energy levels are quantized, meaning that they can only take on certain specific values. The arrangement of electrons within an atom is determined by the number of protons in the nucleus and the distribution of electrons among the different energy levels.

The quantum mechanical model also describes the behavior of electrons in terms of the Pauli exclusion principle, which states that no two electrons within an atom can have the same set of quantum numbers. This principle determines the way in which electrons occupy the energy levels of an atom, and it is what gives rise to the characteristic arrangement of electrons in atoms.

Overall, the quantum mechanical model provides a framework for understanding the behavior of particles on a very small scale, and it is an essential part of modern chemistry and physics.

Answer:

The Quantum Mechanical Model is a theoretical framework used to describe the behavior of particles, including electrons, on a very small scale. It is based on the principles of quantum mechanics, which describe the behavior of matter and energy at the atomic and subatomic level.

According to the Quantum Mechanical Model, electrons do not have a definite position or energy until they are observed or measured. Instead, they exist in a "cloud" of probability around the nucleus of an atom, and their position and energy can only be described in terms of probabilities. This is known as the Heisenberg uncertainty principle.

The arrangement of electrons in an atom is described using quantum numbers. These numbers describe the energy, angular momentum, and other properties of the electrons in an atom. The first quantum number, known as the principal quantum number, describes the energy of the electron, while the second quantum number, known as the angular momentum quantum number, describes the shape of the electron cloud.

Overall, the Quantum Mechanical Model provides a more accurate and detailed description of the behavior and properties of electrons than the classical models that were used previously. It has revolutionized our understanding of the nature of matter and has had many practical applications, including the development of modern electronics and the understanding of chemical reactions.
