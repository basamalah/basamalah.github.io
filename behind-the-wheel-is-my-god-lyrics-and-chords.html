11. culture in my career wheel​


Answer:

1. Motivation

Imagine working in a company that values profits above all else, and doesn’t give a second thought to the consequences of its operations on the environment or society. You, on the other hand, find this lack of social responsibility a major put-off. You feel a growing discontent and heightening demotivation as you force yourself to go through the daily motions at work.

 

2. Productivity

If you don’t share mutual values or beliefs with the organization, chances are you’ll probably not have much in common with most of your colleagues either. This disconnect will eventually make you indifferent to your job. You won’t find any enjoyment in your daily tasks, which will inevitably cause your productivity to decline.

 

3. Inspiration

We feel most inspired at work when we can relate to the company’s vision and goals, and particularly when those same goals are aligned with our own. This is what creates synergy at the workplace. The magical power of collaboration between like-minded individuals sparks outcomes that would not be achievable by those individuals on their own.

 

4. Camaraderie

Our colleagues can enhance our happiness at work, or they could make us absolutely miserable. If you find that majority of your co-workers love partying away the weekends—your bosses included—but you don’t, you’re not going to have much fun at work. On the other hand, if you’re the social butterfly but everyone else is an introvert, you’re going to feel like the odd one out too. The point is, camaraderie with colleagues is an important element of one’s job satisfaction.

 

5. Growth

Naturally, if you enjoy what you do and love your working environment and colleagues, you’ll perform well at work and find it much easier to scale the corporate ladder. Being in sync with your bosses and peers will inevitably translate into fewer obstacles for your career growth.

Answer:

1. The speaker in the song "Dance with My Father" is the singer, Luther Vandross.

2. The speaker is referring to his father, who has passed away.

3. The title "Dance with My Father" refers to the speaker's longing to dance with his father again, even though he has already passed away.

4. The song "Dance with My Father" is a tribute to Luther Vandross's father, who passed away when he was young. The song is a reflection of his memories and longing for his father, particularly the times when they used to dance together. It is a deeply personal and emotional song that resonates with many people who have lost a loved one.

5. The message of the lyrics in "Dance with My Father" is about the power of memories and the enduring love between a father and son. The speaker in the song longs for the chance to dance with his father again and to relive the memories of their time together. The lyrics express a deep sense of loss and longing, but also celebrate the enduring love and connection between family members, even after death. The song reminds us to cherish the moments we have with our loved ones, as those memories can sustain us long after they are gone.
