1. what are the factors affecting communication process​


Explanation:

FACTORS AFFECTING COMMUNICATION

A number of factors which may disrupt the communication process and affect the overall understanding and interpretaion of what is communicated are :

1. Status / Role

2. Cultural differences .

3. Choice of communication channel .

4. Length of communication .

5. Use of language .

6. Individual Perceptions / Attitudes / Personalities .

7. Known or Unknown Receiver .

Explanation:

1. Status/Role

The sender and receiver of a message may be of equal status within a hierarchy (e.g. managers in an organisation) or they may be at different levels (e.g. manager/employee, lecturer/student, business owner/clients).This difference in status sometimes affects the effectiveness of the communication process.

2. Cultural Differences

Cultural differences, both within or outside the organisation (for example, inter-departmental dealings and communication with outside organisations or ethnic minorities) may impede the communication process.

3. Choice of Communication Channels

Sender should choose the most appropriate channel for a particular purpose and keeping the person/receiver in mind.

Sending messages via inappropriate channels can send out wrong signals and end up creating confusion.

Choosing the appropriate channel needs :

Considering all aspects of the communication process (interpretation, understanding, feedback).

Determining all the possible barriers.

Evaluating the complexity of the message and decide how it might be best conveyed .

Knowing--

Who? ? Characteristics of the receiver(s).

Why? Purpose of the communication.

What? Content of the message.

How? Oral, written, visual or a combination of all three.

Where? Location of the meeting.

When? Timing/time limit/expected response time.

4. Length of Communication

The length of the message also affects the communication process.

Sender need to be sure that it serves the purpose and is appropriate for the receiver.

Is the message too long or too brief?

5. Use of Language

The language used must be familiar to the receiver .

Poor choice of words or weak sentence structure also hampers communication.

Sentences must be framed correctly .

6 .Individual Perceptions/Attitudes/Personalities

Sometimes, the method of communication needs to take into consideration the receiver's personality traits, perceptions , age and preferred style.

EXAMPLE -The elderly and children, have different communication needs and preferences when compared to young adults.

7 .Known or Unknown Receiver

Whether the receiver is known or unknown to sender also plays a major role in determining the effectiveness of the communication.

A known receiver may be better able to understand the message despite having insufficient information as both sender and receiver have common experiences .

An unknown receiver, on the other hand, may require more information and time to decode the message.

3. compose and essay about the possible factors that can affect the process of having an effective communication​


Answer:

The Communicator

The Message

The Channel of Communication, and

The Audience or message receivers.

Each of these factors contributes significantly to effective communication.

Communicator of a message, so much is required from him, before his message can produce desired results. Among these are: his skills, delivery methods, message or messages, body language, and perhaps the Ten Cs. These Cs are:

Clear/clarity of message, make message clear.

Concise: make message simple, straight, and strong.

Compelling: make message compelling, not necessarily being emotional, but make audience or receiver understand the reason behind the message and what is expected of them.

Curious: make message interesting that audience or receiver want to know or listen more.

Compassion: make message very compassionate.

Confidence: be confident in yourself, but not being arrogant, exude personality that will make audience love you, trust your message and would want them talk more about the message, even when you are gone.

Control: be in control of your message, talk less, maintain contact through body language, manage all equipment or apparatus use for message properly. Avoid objects falling off the table, avoid paperwork flying around.

Credence/Credible: let your message be credible, support with data, quote or make references to known authorities and scholars. Never make unsubstantiated facts. And when scientific evidence is required, explain to your audience.

Certitude/Certainty: be very sure of your information and message, be ready to give answers to questions; be ready to explain concepts not understood. And if your message center around new idea or object, take time to explain to your audience. Create a lasting impression in receiver’s mind.

Comforting: let your message be comforting, be like balm on wobble knees. Your tone, you choices of words and demeanor should make your audience listen. Even if the message is unwelcome, make it acceptable to the people.

Effective communication derive strength from RIGHT MESSAGE. Message plays a proper role in effective communication; if wrong message is made, no matter how good a communicator can be, the desired result won't come. For instance, a message meant for farmers, if delivered to physicians, it will fail. Also, message meant for teachers, if presented to journalists, nurses, drivers, lawyers, accountants, administrators, it will certainly fail, even though, most of these individuals may be parents with children in the school.

Proper Channel: channels are the vehicles to transmit information, the choice of channel must align with audience. In certain circumstances, verbal or face to face communication will be preferred to using radio, television or social media. At another time, the social status of your audience may determine the type of channel of communication. Where television channel is needed, don't use radio, where video channel is required, don't use lecture; where social media channel is required, don't use magazines; even with the social media, you need to be specific, because no single social media channel can capture all audience.
