1. Secant tangents segments and sector of a circle


Answer:

A line that intersects a circle in exactly one point is called a tangent and the point where the intersection occurs is called the point of tangency. The tangent is always perpendicular to the radius drawn to the point of tangency. A secant is a line that intersects a circle in exactly two points.

4. A line that intersects a circle at exactly two points and contains a chord of a circle. A. Tangent line B. Secant line C. Tangent segment D. Secant segment ​


Answer:

B. Secant line

Step-by-step explanation:

A line intersecting in two points is called a secant line, in one point a tangent line and in no points an exterior line. A chord of a circle is the line segment that joins two distinct points of the circle. A chord is therefore contained in a unique secant line and each secant line determines a unique chord.

10. it is a region bounded by two radii of circle and its intercepted arca. secantb. sector c. segmentd. tangent​


B. Sector

A sector is a region bounded by two radii of a circle and the intercepted arc of the circle. The angle formed by the two radii is called a central angle. A sector with a central angle less than 180° is called a minor sector. A sector with a central angle greater than 180° is called a major sector.

16. Define the following :1. Secant of A circle 2. Tangent of a circle 3. Segment of a circle 4. Sector of a circle ​


Answer:

1. Secant of a circle: A line that intersects a circle at two points.

2. Tangent of a circle: A line that touches a circle at exactly one point.

3. Segment of a circle: A part of a circle that is bounded by an arc and its endpoints.

4. Sector of a circle: A region of a circle enclosed by an arc and two radii.
