10. 2. LCC Mall paid P15,000 for a set of dishes. Expenses are 18% of the selling price while the required profit is 15% of the selling price. During an inventory sale, the set of dishes was marked down by 30%. a. What was the regular selling price? b. What was the sale price? c. What was the profit or loss?​


Answer:P15,671.64

Step-by-step explanation:

a. To find the regular selling price, we need to first calculate the cost of goods sold (COGS), which is the cost LCC Mall paid for the set of dishes plus the expenses and profit margin.

Expenses = 18% of Selling Price

Profit = 15% of Selling Price

Therefore, COGS = Cost + Expenses + Profit

COGS = 15000 + 0.18S + 0.15S

COGS = 15000 + 0.33S

where S is the regular selling price.

Now, we can set up an equation to solve for S:

S - 0.33S = 15000

0.67S = 15000

S = 15000 / 0.67

S = 22,388.06

Therefore, the regular selling price was P22,388.06.

b. The sale price is 30% off the regular selling price, so we can find it by multiplying the regular selling price by (100% - 30%) or 0.7:

Sale Price = 0.7 x Regular Selling Price

Sale Price = 0.7 x 22,388.06

Sale Price = 15,671.64

Therefore, the sale price was P15,671.64.
