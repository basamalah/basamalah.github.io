Sure, here's an example script for a role play about bullying:

Characters:

Sarah: the victim of bullying
Emma: the bully
Alex: the bystander
Scene:
The scene takes place in a school hallway during lunchtime.

Sarah is walking down the hallway when Emma approaches her.

Emma: Hey there, Sarah! What's up?

Sarah: Hi Emma. Not much, just trying to get to lunch.

Emma: (sneering) Oh, I see. Well, I have something to tell you. I heard from some friends that you're a loser and nobody likes you.

Sarah: (looking down) That's not true, Emma. Why would you say that?

Emma: (getting closer to Sarah) Because it is true. You're pathetic, Sarah.

Alex is walking down the hallway and notices the bullying.

Alex: Hey, Emma, what's going on here?

Emma: (turning to Alex) Mind your own business, Alex. This doesn't concern you.

Alex: Actually, it does concern me. You're bullying Sarah, and that's not okay.

Emma: (laughing) Bullying? I'm just joking around with her. You're such a wimp, Alex.

Sarah: (sobbing) It's not a joke, Alex. Emma's been doing this for weeks. I don't know what to do.

Alex: (to Sarah) I'm sorry you've had to deal with this. (to Emma) And as for you, Emma, bullying is never a joke. You need to stop this right now.

Emma: (getting defensive) Fine, whatever. (to Sarah) Just remember, nobody likes you.

Sarah runs off down the hallway, while Emma and Alex stand there for a moment.

Alex: (to Emma) Why do you do this, Emma? Bullying hurts people, and it's not cool.

Emma: (sighing) I don't know, Alex. I guess I just feel better about myself when I put other people down.

Alex: (nodding) I get it, but that's not a healthy way to feel good about yourself. Maybe you could try building people up instead of tearing them down.

Emma: (thoughtful) Maybe you're right, Alex. I'll think about it.

The scene ends with Emma looking contemplative and Alex walking off down the hallway.
