22. Activity 1.5: A World without Media 4. What would happen with the decisions you usually make? 5. How would it affect the way you live? 6. What would you personally miss most in such a situation? 7. What would society lose in this situation? C​


4. Without media, the decisions that people usually make would likely be based solely on their personal experiences and interactions with others, rather than being influenced by the information and opinions presented in media outlets. This could lead to more independent decision-making, but also potentially less informed decision-making.

5. The absence of media would greatly affect the way people live. Without media, people would have less access to information about current events, entertainment, and cultural trends. Social interactions might also change, as people would have less to discuss and share about news and popular culture. Additionally, many jobs and industries rely heavily on media, such as journalism, advertising, and entertainment, so the economy and job market would likely be impacted as well.

6. Personally, people would likely miss the convenience and entertainment provided by media. Without access to streaming services, social media, and news outlets, people would have to find new ways to entertain themselves and stay informed about the world. Additionally, people might miss the sense of connection and community that can be fostered through shared experiences with media.

7. Society would lose a lot in the absence of media. Media plays a crucial role in informing the public about important issues, holding those in power accountable, and providing a platform for diverse voices and perspectives. Without media, there would be less transparency and accountability, and it would be more difficult for marginalized groups to have their voices heard. Additionally, media can provide a sense of shared culture and community, and without it, society might become more fragmented and isolated.
