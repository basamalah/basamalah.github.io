Chapter 3: Introduction

The Introduction chapter of a research study provides an overview of the research project, including the research question, the purpose of the study, and the significance of the study. It also provides background information on the topic being studied and sets the context for the research.

Research design:

The research design is the plan or framework for conducting a study. It outlines the methods and techniques that will be used to gather and analyze data. In a correlational study, the research design involves collecting data on two or more variables and examining the relationship between them.

Sampling procedure:

The sampling procedure refers to the method used to select a sample of participants for a study. It is important to choose a representative sample in order to ensure that the results of the study are generalizable to the larger population.

Scope and delimitation:

The scope of a study refers to the boundaries or limits of the research, including what will be studied and what will not be studied. The delimitation of a study refers to the limitations or constraints of the research, including any factors that may impact the results or interpretation of the study.

5W1H:

The 5W1H method is a technique used to gather and organize information about a particular topic. It stands for "what," "where," "when," "who," "why," and "how." These questions can be used to gather information about the context, purpose, and details of a research project or other topic.

BPL (Business Permit and Licensing):

A Business Permit and Licensing (BPL) is a document that allows a business to legally operate within a particular jurisdiction. It is typically issued by a local government agency and includes information about the business, such as its name, location, and type of operation.

In the context of research, the BPL may be relevant if the study involves collecting data from businesses or if the research is being conducted as part of a business or organizational context.
