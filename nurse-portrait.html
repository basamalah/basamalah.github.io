3. Why is it the landscape lighting is used instead of PORTRAIT the difference of landscape and portrait painting


Answer:

The main difference between landscape and portrait image orientation is that a landscape image is wider than it is taller while a portrait image is taller than it is wider. In other words, Landscape images are captured in a horizontal layout while portrait images are captured in a vertical layout. Portrait orientation can make the subject feel more imposing, even regal. And, because you're closer, it can give the image more of an intimate feel. In contrast, using landscape mode can make the subject feel smaller, perhaps even vulnerable. But, it could also provide context that would make it seem grander.

26. what is a portrait photography? and what types of portraits can be taken during graduation ​


Portrait photography is a type of photography that captures the likeness of a person or a small group of people, typically focusing on their face and expressions.

During a graduation ceremony, some common types of portraits that can be taken include:

Individual Portraits: These portraits focus on the graduate, capturing their attire and regalia, as well as any personal items that hold special significance, such as a class ring or honor cords.

Group Portraits: These portraits capture the graduate with their classmates or friends, often in front of the graduation venue or a landmark on campus.

Family Portraits: These portraits are taken with the graduate and their family members, often capturing the joy and pride of the moment.

Candid Portraits: These portraits capture natural and unposed moments during the graduation ceremony or celebration, showcasing the emotions and excitement of the day.
