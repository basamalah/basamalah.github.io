25. explain jesus as the good shepherd and the meaning of stewardship​


Jesus is often referred to as the Good Shepherd in the Bible because he cares for and guides his followers like a shepherd cares for and guides his sheep. This metaphor emphasizes Jesus' love, protection, and guidance for his people.

Stewardship, on the other hand, refers to the responsible management and care of something that has been entrusted to us. In Christianity, stewardship is often associated with the idea that everything we have comes from God and that we are called to manage these resources in a way that honors God and benefits others.

As the Good Shepherd, Jesus models stewardship by caring for his followers and guiding them towards a life of service and love. He also teaches his followers to use their resources wisely and generously, to care for the needs of others, and to be good stewards of the earth.

Overall, the metaphor of the Good Shepherd and the concept of stewardship remind Christians of their responsibility to care for and serve others, as well as to use their resources in a way that honors God's love and generosity.
