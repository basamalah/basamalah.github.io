2. is learning korean, speaking korean, writing in hangul make u a koreaboo?​


Not necessarily. I think it's all about how you go about it. It's perfectly normal to want to learn Korean because you love Kpop/Kdrama/etc. and want to be able to interact with it better in its original language. If this is what motivates you to learn, all the more power to you.The way that I see it drifting into Koreaboo territory is when you expect South Korea to be exactly like how it is in K-Dramas and KPOP and it becomes this weird idolization/fetishization of South Korea. It's important to realize that South Korea and Korean Culture as a whole is so much more than KPOP/KDramas/etc. so it's important that you learn about Korea beyond what you consume through mass media. Research a lot online, go to museums, if your still in college, take a class or two on Korean History and culture so that way you are much more well-rounded on the culture.Also realize that South Korea is just like any other country in that it has a lot of great things to it but it also has many flaws. By recognizing that there are many flaws in Korean society it can help make you realize that South Korea is not perfect and that what you've learned about just through mass media paints a very one-sided picture.Lastly, I think it's very important in learning about Korean Culture/History/Language, that you know not to step over native Koreans (I'm assuming that you are not Korean). There are so many times I see non-natives trying to speak for the experience of those who are actually from said country just because they are studying it and that can be incredibly frustrating (Not just with Koreaboos but Weeaboos and Chinaboos as well). Realize that although you are studying the language and culture and may even choose to move to South Korea, you're experiences will never trounce those experiences of people who were born and raised there with Korean parents/ancestors/relatives. In studying the language and culture, recognize that you are a guest in the culture so be respectful and listen to those around you.Best of luck with your studies, hope this helps!

18. Imperyalismo Drama script ​


Answer:

Title: Imperialism

Characters:

1. John - a farmer

2. Mary - John’s wife

3. George - a wealthy businessman

4. Jack - George’s assistant

Scene 1:

(John and Mary are sitting outside their house, enjoying the sunset)

Mary: (smiling) This is such a beautiful evening, John.

John: (nodding) Yes, it is. But I can’t help but worry about our farm. The prices of our crops keep falling and we are struggling to make ends meet.

Mary: (sighing) I know. It’s not just us. All the farmers in the area are facing the same problem.

Scene 2:

(George and Jack enter the stage)

George: (smiling) Good evening, John and Mary.

John: (surprised) Good evening, George. What brings you here?

George: (smiling) I heard about the problems you and the other farmers are facing. I wanted to offer my help.

John: (confused) How can you help us?

George: (smiling) I can buy your crops at a fair price and sell them in the city. You won’t have to worry about finding buyers and you’ll get a good price for your crops.

Mary: (suspicious) What’s the catch?

George: (smiling) No catch. I just want to help the local farmers and make some profit for myself.

Scene 3:

(John and Mary are in their house)

Mary: (worried) I don’t trust George, John. I think he has some ulterior motive.

John: (sighing) I don’t know, Mary. We are struggling to make ends meet. Maybe we should give him a chance.

Mary: (nodding) I understand, John. But I have a bad feeling about this.

Scene 4:

(John and Mary are at a meeting with other farmers)

John: (confidently) I think we should give George a chance. He has offered to buy our crops at a fair price and sell them in the city.

Other Farmers: (murmuring) But can we trust him?

John: (defending George) He seems like a good man. And we need all the help we can get.

Scene 5:

(John and Mary are in their house)

Mary: (angrily) I knew it, John! George has been buying our crops at a low price and selling them in the city at a high price. He’s making a huge profit at our expense.

John: (sighing) I should have listened to you, Mary. We’ve been taken for a ride.

Mary: (determined) We need to stand up against this imperialism, John. We can’t let people like George take advantage of us.

John: (nodding) You’re right, Mary. We need to fight for our rights.

Scene 6:

(John and Mary are leading a group of farmers in a protest against George)

John: (angrily) We won’t let you take advantage of us, George! We demand fair prices for our crops.

Mary: (determined) We won’t let you exploit us for your profit. We won’t be silenced anymore.

George: (smiling) You farmers are so naive. You don’t understand the ways of the world.

Jack: (smiling) Yes, you’re just simple farmers. You can’t compete with us businessmen.

John: (angrily) We may be simple farmers, but we won’t let you trample on our dignity. We are human beings with rights.

Mary: (determined) We won’t let imperialism win. We’ll fight for our rights and our dignity.

(The curtain falls)
