15. Six years from now, Marc will be four times his age today. What isbthe correct equation in terms of Marc's present age?


Answer:

Let's call Marc's present age as "x".

Six years from now, Marc's age will be "x" + 6.

And we know that six years from now, Marc will be four times his age today.

So, (x + 6) = 4x

We can solve for x by isolating it on one side:

x + 6 -x = 4x - x

6 = 3x

x = 2

So Marc's present age is 2.

The equation for this scenario would be x + 6 = 4x, which represents the statement: "Six years from now, Marc will be four times his age today."

Age Word Problem

Age problems are word problems dealing with the ages of people currently, in the past or in the future.

The problem above is asking for the correct equation in terms of Marc's present age.

"Six years from now, Marc will be four times his age today ."

To find the equation, let x be Marc's age today.

Six years from now = x + 6

Four times his age today = 4x

x + 6 = 4x

The answer is letter x + 6 = 4x.

Checking:

x + 6 = 4x

6 = 4x - x

6 = 3x

6/3 = 3x/3
