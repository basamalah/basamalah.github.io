online catalog or OPAC

The online public access catalog, now frequently synonymous with library catalog, is an online database of materials held by a library or group of libraries. Online catalogs progressed from analog card catalogs, and similarly enable searching the library's collection of books and other materials.

manual library card catalog

The card catalog was a familiar sight to library users for generations, but it has been effectively replaced by the online public access catalog (OPAC). Some still refer to the online catalog as a "card catalog".[2] Some libraries with OPAC access still have card catalogs on site, but these are now strictly a secondary resource and are seldom updated. Many libraries that retain their physical card catalog will post a sign advising the last year that the card catalog was updated. Some libraries have eliminated their card catalog in favor of the OPAC for the purpose of saving space for other use, such as additional shelving.

14. You were requested by the company's CEO to write a policy manual on sexual harrassment. What provisions will you include? How shoul they be executed?​


Here are some provisions that can be included in a policy manual on sexual harassment for a company in the Philippines or any company:

Definition of Sexual Harassment: The policy manual should define sexual harassment in the context of the Philippine Anti-Sexual Harassment Act of 1995 (Republic Act No. 7877). It should include unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other physical or verbal conduct of a sexual nature that has the effect of intimidating, humiliating, or offending the victim.

Prohibition of Sexual Harassment: The manual should state that sexual harassment is illegal and strictly prohibited in the workplace, as well as in educational and training institutions.

Reporting Mechanism: The manual should provide clear guidelines on how to report incidents of sexual harassment, including the use of the mandatory reporting form provided by the Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE). The manual should also state that the company has a responsibility to investigate and take action on all reported incidents.

Investigation Process: The manual should outline the process for investigating and responding to reports of sexual harassment. This should include ensuring confidentiality, protecting the victim from retaliation, and providing support to the victim during the investigation process.

Disciplinary Action: The manual should state that sexual harassment is a serious offense and that any employee found guilty of it will be subject to disciplinary action, including suspension or termination of employment.

Training and Awareness: The manual should emphasize the importance of regular training and awareness-raising initiatives on sexual harassment prevention for all employees. The training should include an explanation of the law, the company's policies, and the responsibilities of employees in preventing and reporting sexual harassment.

To execute these provisions, the company should ensure that all employees receive a copy of the policy manual, including the reporting mechanism and investigation process. The company should also provide regular training sessions and awareness-raising initiatives on sexual harassment prevention to all employees, including new hires.

In addition, the company should create a culture of respect and inclusivity in the workplace, where employees feel comfortable reporting incidents of sexual harassment without fear of retaliation. By implementing and enforcing these provisions, the company can create a safe and inclusive work environment for all employees in the Philippines.

Online Public Access Card Catalog

1. The online public access catalog, often abbreviated OPAC

2. Frequently synonymous with library catalog

3. Online database of materials held by a library or group of libraries.

4. Online catalogs progressed from analog card catalogs

5. similarly enable searching the library's collection of books and other materials.

Manual Library Card Catalog

1. A library catalog (or library catalogue in British English) is a register of all bibliographic items found in a library or group of libraries, such as a network of libraries at several locations.

2. A bibliographic item can be any information entity (e.g., books, computer files, graphics, realia, cartographic materials, etc.)

3. It is considered library material (e.g., a single novel in an anthology)

4.Is a group of library materials (e.g., a trilogy)

5. It is linked from the catalog (e.g., a webpage) as far as it is relevant to the catalog and to the users (patrons) of the library.
