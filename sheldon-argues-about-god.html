9. Leonard is 68 years less than quadruple Sheldon's age. In 12 years, Leonard's age will be 24 years less than two times Sheldon's age. What are their 2 ages?


Answer:

Leonard: 21 1/7 ; Sheldon: 16 4/7

Step-by-step explanation:

In this problem, we are given the relationship of two numbers, specifically ages, currently and the information when they are compared a period of time from now.

In these types of problems, our main priority is to find the working equation that can help us give values to our variables. Saying that, let's declare our variables first.

Let x be the age of Leonard. From the first sentence of the question, we can imply that Sheldon's age is 4x - 68.

Now, we can work on the second sentence of the question, which will be very helpful in determining the equation.

If you correctly turn the sentence into symbols and numbers, you will get the following equation:

x + 12 = 2[(4x - 68) + 12] - 24

Solving for x,

x + 12 = 2 (4x - 56) - 24

x + 12 = 8x - 112 - 24

-7x = -148

7x = 148

x = 148 / 7 = 21 1/7

Now, we can use this to find Sheldon's age. Remember that the latter's age is represented as 4x - 68.

We can just substitute 21 1/7 into x to find Sheldon's age.

4x - 68 = 4x - 68

4x - 68 = 4(21 1/7) - 68

4x - 68 = 4(21) + 4(1/7) - 68

4x - 68 = 84 - 68 + 4/7

4x - 68 = 16 4/7

We can check our answers by substituting our values in the working equation.

(21 1/7) + 12 = 2[(16 4/7) + 12] - 24

33 1/7 = 2 (28 4/7) - 24

33 1/7 = 57 1/7 - 24

33 1/7 = 33 1/7

LHS = RHS

Therefore, Leonard's age is 21 1/7, while Sheldon's age is 16 4/7.
