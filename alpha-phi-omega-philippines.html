1. Who is the alpha and omega?


Alpha and Omega refers to the first and last letters in the Greek alphabet. When we say The Alpha and The Omega, we are actually saying The Beginning and The End.

The Alpha and The Omega is not a period of time or an incident in history. This actually refers to God himself. He referred to himself as the Alpha and Omega on Revelations 1:8, Revelations 21:6, and Revelations 22:13. This is because God is the Creator of all things and the start of the universe. He has the power to end all suffering and death.

Who is God then? In the modern Bible, many versions have removed the name of God and replaced it with Lord or simply God. This is why people have misinterpreted Jesus to be God because only his name appears clearly. However, if we look into versions of the Bible with wherein the name of God was not fully removed or have been returned, we can see YWHW or Jehovah. So the Alpha and Omega is no other than Jehovah.

30. What is Phi-Coefficient?​


WHAT IS PHI-COEFFICIENT?

In statistics, the phi coefficient is a measure of association for two binary variables. Introduced by Karl Pearson, this measure is similar to the Pearson correlation coefficient in its interpretation. In fact, a Pearson correlation coefficient estimated for two binary variables will return the phi coefficient.
