2. Amari has a piece of wood that is ¾ of a foot in length. She needs to cut pieces that are 1/6 of a foot long. How many pieces of wood can Amari cut? with solution pls


Answer:

Amari will be able to get [tex]4\frac{1}{2}[/tex] pieces of 1/6 ft. long out of the wood.

Step-by-step explanation:

Dividing Fractions:When a fraction is divided by another fraction, it is the same as multiplying the numerator by the reciprocal of the denominator. The reciprocal is the expression that is multiplied with another expression and the product is unity.

To illustrate, the division of fractions appear as: [tex]\frac{\frac{a}{b} }{\frac{c}{d} } =(\frac{a}{b})(\frac{d}{c} )[/tex]

APPLICATION TIME:

PROBLEM: Amari’s wood board is 3/4 ft. long and must be cut into 1/6 ft. long pieces. How many pieces of wood can Amari cut?

To find how many pieces can be cut, divide the length of the board by the length of the pieces. This is made easier by multiplying the numerator (3/4) by the reciprocal of the denominator which is shown below:

= [tex]\frac{3}{4}[/tex] ÷ [tex]\frac{1}{6}[/tex]

= [tex]\frac{3}{4}[/tex] × [tex]\frac{6}{1}[/tex]

= [tex]\frac{18}{4}[/tex]

= [tex]4\frac{2}{4}[/tex] or [tex]4\frac{1}{2}[/tex]

Therefore, Amari will be able to get [tex]4\frac{1}{2}[/tex] pieces of 1/6 ft. long each out of the wood.
