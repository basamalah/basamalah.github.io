3. 5. An archer tries to hit a target that is 30 m away from him. He can release the arrowat 20 m/s neglecting air resistance, estimate the angle at which the archer shouldaim to compensate for fall of the arrow due to gravity.​


Solution:

The formula for the range of a projectile is

R = vi² sin 2θ / g

where:

R = range

vi = initial velocity

θ = angle of release (from the horizontal)

g = acceleration due to gravity

g = 9.81 m/s² (9.8 m/s² in some books)

Listing the given values

R = 30 m

vi = 20 m/s

Solving for θ

R = vi² sin 2θ / g

30 m = [(20 m/s)²(sin 2θ)] / 9.81 m/s²

(30 m)(9.81 m/s²) = (400 m²/s²)(sin 2θ)

294.3 m²/s² = (400 m²/s²)(sin 2θ)

sin 2θ = (294.3 m²/s²) / (400 m²/s²)

sin 2θ = 0.73575

2θ = Arcsin (0.73575)

2θ = 47.3706°

θ = 47.3706° / 2

θ = 23.69°

θ ≈ 24°

Therefore, the archer should aim at an angle of 24° to compensate for fall of arrow due to gravity.
