11. Activity 6 mark my calendar how long does a regular menstrual cycle last?


Answer:

A regular menstrual cycle is a 28-day cycle.

Explanation:

Menstruation is part of the puberty stage in a female body. When someone reaches this stage, the egg cells will start maturing. This is also the start of your uterus wall thickening for fertilisation and shedding of the uterus wall during your period.

The thickening of the wall simply signifies that you can be pregnant when you have an intercourse and the shedding is the days when you have your period.

23. Explain the significant characteristics of the following musical forms.1. life cycle songs2. Responsorial singing3. Songs for special rituals4. Songs for departed5. Courtship and marriage ceremonials songs6. Song of debates7. Epic songs8. Occupational songs9. Solo songs10. Wedding feast songs​


Answer:

Life cycle songs

  Events depicted through songs such as lullabies, songs related to birth, courtship, wedding rits and lamentation for the dead.

Responsorial singing or leader-chorus type

Used in thanksgiving rituals, in Peshit on anniversary feasts or weddings and Du-udyeng or ta-tamiya on ceremonies for dead.

Songs for special rituals

Performed for various purposes and important feasts.

Songs for the departed

Songs performed for the dead.

Courtship and marriage ceremonial songs

Love songs, like the Manobo courtship songs sangong-sangong which are accompanied by saluray, kugot or kitara.

Song debates or alternating response

This done by male and female singers, the topic of the debate center on everyday life which are witty and humorous in nature.

Epic songs

Chanted at weddings, gatherings or at evening entertainment performances. These songs describe the lives of gods and heroes.

Occupational songs

Songs while farming, fishing, doing some simple chores such as indowan which is to call people to plant rice in Mandaya; sowa which sung to protect the fields from pests, and layad-layad which are harvest songs in Manobo.

Solo songs

Performed unaccompanied by onor or pabubayok in all kinds of occasions.

Wedding feasts

Where there is music making.
