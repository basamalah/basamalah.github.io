A comparative degree is a form of change that occurs in adjectives when used to compare things.

1. Radiant.

Positive: The bride's face looks very happy and radiant.Comparative: His face becomes more radiant after recovering from his illness.Superlative: Among all the people who came that day, I saw my father's face the most radiant because he was very happy with my graduation.

2. Important.

Positive: In order to recover quickly, it is important to take the medicine on time.Comparative: Completing homework now is more important than watching a badminton match.Superlative: My parents are the most important people to me in the world.

3. Courageous.

Positive: My little brother is a courageous person.Comparative: My little brother is more courageous than me.Superlative: Among all my siblings, my little brother is the most courageous person.

4. Healthy.

Positive: My mother is healed and healthy now.Comparative: I have to eat more vegetables to be healthier.Superlative: My sister has never been sick, she is the healthiest member of the family this year.

5. Diligent.

Positive: My sister is a diligent person.Comparative: My sister is more diligent than me.Superlative: Among all my siblings, my sister is the most diligent person.

6. Clean.

Positive: The park that I visited today is very beautiful and clean.Comparative: My sister's room is cleaner than mine.Superlative: My parents' room was the cleanest room in the house.

7. Silent.

Positive: Because it's still early my school is very silent.Comparative: My sister is more silent when she's sick.Superlative: Among all the people in my house, I am the most silent person.

8. Old.

Positive: I have a very beautiful old dog.Comparative: My mother is one year older than my father.Superlative: I am the oldest child in my house.

9. Popular.

Positive: My friend is a popular student at school.Comparative: My friend became more popular at school after winning a singing contest.Superlative: My friend, Lia, is the most popular student in school.

10. Artistic.

Positive: My mother is an artistic person.Comparative: My sister's painting looks more artistic than mine.Superlative: Among all the paintings, this painting is the most artistic.

9. Comparative and superlative​


⇒Answer:

∞Comparatives∞

Comparative adjectives enable us to express the difference between two people or things. In order to say that someone or something has a superior quality, size, or characteristic, you need to use ‘more’. Instead, when you want to say that someone or something has an inferior quality, size, or characteristic, you must use ‘less’.

∞Superlatives∞

We use superlatives to compare one person or thing to several others. For example,

Paris is the most beautiful city I’ve ever seen.

This means that I’ve seen several cities and I consider Paris as the number one in terms of being beautiful.

In a similar way to comparatives, there are two ways to use superlatives. When you want to say that one person or thing is superior to all others in that group, you need to use ‘the most’. When you want to say that one person or thing is inferior to all others in that group, you need to use ‘the least’.

Positive, Comparative and Superlative Adjectives

Adjectives have three forms –

Positive form Comparative formSuperlative form.

The positive degree of an adjective is the simplest form of that particular adjective.

Comparative degree of an adjective denotes a higher degree when compared to a positive degree. It is used to compare two persons or things.

The superlative degree of an adjective denotes the highest degree of the quality. Superlative degree is used to compare more than two persons or things.

Comparative and superlative forms of adjectives are formed by adding r and st or er and est or more and most etc.
