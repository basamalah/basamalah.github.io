5. Statement 1. an executive order passed by the president of the directing the commission on higher education (ched) to regulate the operation of review centers is a valid exercise of police power.


Answer:

It is possible that an executive order directing the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) to regulate the operation of review centers could be a valid exercise of police power. Police power is the inherent power of the state to regulate the affairs of its citizens in order to promote the general welfare and to protect the health, safety, and morals of the community. It is often used to regulate activities that may pose a potential harm to the public.

If the operation of review centers is seen as having the potential to harm the public, such as by providing inadequate or misleading information to students preparing for exams, then the government may have the authority to regulate their operation through the exercise of police power. However, the validity of such an executive order would depend on the specific circumstances and the legal and constitutional framework of the country in which it is issued.

Literature Review -2. Traditional Literature Review -3. Integrative Literature Review -4. Syntactic Literature Review -5. Scoping Literature Review -6. Primary Sources -7. Secondary Sources -8. ERIC -9. WWW-10. Search Engine -​


1. Literature Review - literature review is a document or section of a document that collects key sources on a topic and discusses those sources in conversation with each other (also called synthesis).

2. Traditional Literature Review - analyze and summarize the body of literature.

3. Integrative Literature Review - is a form of research that reviews, cri- tiques, and synthesizes representative literature on a topic in an integrated way such that new frameworks and perspectives on the topic are generated.

4. Syntactic Literature Review -

5. Scoping Literature Review - is a relatively new approach to evidence synthesis and differs from systematic reviews in its purpose and aims. The purpose of a scoping review is to provide an overview of the available research evidence without producing a summary answer to a discrete research question.

6. Primary Sources - may include but are not limited to: letters, manuscripts, diaries, journals, newspapers, maps, speeches, interviews, documents produced by government agencies, photographs, audio or video recordings, born-digital items (e.g. emails), research data, and objects or artifacts.

7. Secondary Sources - are generally scholarly books and articles. A secondary source interprets and analyzes primary sources. These sources are one or more steps removed from the event. Secondary sources may contain pictures, quotes or graphics of primary sources.

8. ERIC - Education Resources Information Center

9. WWW- World Wide Web

10. Search Engine - is a website through which users can search internet content. To do this, users enter the desired search term into the search field. The search engine then looks through its index for relevant websites and displays them in the form of a list.

30. Print preview allows a review of the document and the implementation of necessary changes before printing. True or False?


Answer:

True.

Explanation:

Print preview is a feature in some software applications that allows users to view their document in the format it will appear when printed. This feature allows users to review the document layout, font styles, images, and other elements before printing, which can help them identify any errors or formatting issues that need to be corrected. Additionally, print preview can also provide users with the option to make adjustments to the document, such as adjusting margins or page orientation, before sending it to the printer. Overall, print preview is useful for ensuring that the document is printed correctly and is ready for distribution or dissemination.
