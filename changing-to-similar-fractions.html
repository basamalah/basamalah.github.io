9. Steps on changing dissimilar fractions to similar fractions



Dissimilar fractions - fractions that have different denominators

Similar fractions - fractions that have the same denominator

Example:

[tex] \frac{3}{5} \: and \: \frac{5}{8} [/tex]
1. Get the LCD or least common denominator.

5: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40, 45, 50...
8: 8, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, 56...

LCD = 40

2. Follow the formula:
(LCD divided by denominator) times numerator

[tex] \frac{3}{5} \\ (40 \div 5)(3) \\ (8)(3) \\ = 24 \\ [/tex]

[tex] \frac{5}{8} \\( 40 \div 8)(5) \\ (5)(5) \\ = 25 \\ [/tex]

3. The result of your calculation from the formula will be your new numerator while the LCD will be your new denominator.

[tex] \frac{3}{5} \: and \: \frac{5}{8} [/tex]
becomes:

[tex] \frac{24}{40} \: and \: \frac{25}{40} [/tex]

21. How to change the fraction into similar fraction.


A fraction is considered as similar when they both have the same denominator. All you need to do is get the LCD or Least Common Denominator. Please remember this formula below:

(LCD divided by Denominator) multiplied to numerator

or these steps:

1. Get the LCD.
2. Divide the LCD to the original denominator.
3. The quotient of the LCD and the denominator should be multiplied to the numerator.

Example:
[tex] \frac{1}{5} \: and \: \frac{3}{4} [/tex]
LCD = 20

1st fraction solution
20 divided by 5 = 4
4 multiplied to 1 = 4
= 4/20

2nd fraction solution
20 divided by 4 = 5
5 multiplied to 3 = 15
= 15/20

The new fractions are:
[tex] \frac{4}{20} \: and \: \frac{15}{20} [/tex]
