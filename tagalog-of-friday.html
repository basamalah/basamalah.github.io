19. The probability that is Friday and that a student is absent is 0.03 since there are 5 school days in a week the probability that is Friday is 0.2 what is the probability that a student is absent given that today is Friday? ​


P(A | F) = P(F | A) * P(A) / P(F)

We are given P(F | A) = 0.03 (the probability that it's Friday given that a student is absent), P(A) is unknown, and P(F) = 0.2 (the probability that it's Friday). We need to find P(A | F) (the probability that a student is absent given that it's Friday).

To find P(A), we can use the law of total probability:

P(A) = P(A | F) * P(F) + P(A | not F) * P(not F)

We know that P(not F) = 1 - P(F) = 0.8 and we are not given P(A | not F), but we can assume that it is smaller than P(A | F) since absences may be more likely on Fridays than on other days. Therefore, we can estimate P(A | not F) as 0.02 (assuming a slightly lower absence rate on non-Fridays).

Now we can plug in all the values:

P(A) = P(A | F) * 0.2 + 0.02 * 0.8

P(A) = 0.16 + 0.016

P(A) = 0.176

And then use Bayes' theorem to find P(A | F):

P(A | F) = 0.03 * 0.176 / 0.2

P(A | F) = 0.0264

Therefore, the probability that a student is absent given that today is Friday is approximately 0.0264, or about 2.6%.
