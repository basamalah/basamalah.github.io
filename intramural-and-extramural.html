23. subject: PE( i need this now please answer correctly I'll give you 25 points)similarities of intramural and extramural ​


Intramural and extramural are two types of sports competitions that are often held in schools and universities. Despite their differences, they also share some similarities, including:

1. Both are sport competitions - Intramural and extramural competitions involve different sports disciplines that encourage physical activity and promote health awareness among students.

2. Both foster teamwork and sportsmanship - Both types of competition aim to promote teamwork, camaraderie, and sportsmanship among participants. This is reflected in the ways the teams are formed and in how players interact with each other.

3. Both provide opportunities for students to develop leadership skills - Both intramural and extramural competitions can provide opportunities for students to develop their leadership skills. Students may be given the chance to organize the competitions, manage teams, or take on leadership roles.

4. Both promote school spirit - Both competitions are held within the school or university context and are a way of promoting school spirit among students, faculty, and staff members.

5. Both instill a sense of achievement and pride - Participating in intramural or extramural competitions can lead to a sense of achievement and pride among students. Winning a competition can boost self-esteem and create a sense of accomplishment.

Overall, intramural and extramural competitions provide opportunities for healthy competition, physical activity, and personal growth. They encourage students to engage in sports and promote a sense of school spirit and pride.

A good leader should possess the following qualities:

ii) Decisive - An ability to make prompt and effective decisions.

iii) Good Communicator - An ability to communicate effectively with people at all levels.

iv) Confidence - Self-confidence inspires others to follow.

v) Integrity - Honesty and transparency are important qualities in leadership.

vi) Empathy - A leader should be able to understand the needs and emotions of others.

vii) Accountability - Accepting responsibility is an essential part of leadership.

viii) Creativity - A leader should be able to think outside the box and find innovative solutions.

2. Using a Venn Diagram to describe Intramurals and Extramurals:

Intramurals and Extramurals are two types of sports activities that are offered in schools or colleges. A Venn diagram can be used to represent the differences and similarities between these two types of sports activities.

In the middle of the diagram, there are the common features that both types of sports activities share. This includes:

- Promoting physical fitness.

- Encouraging teamwork and cooperation.

- Developing new skills.

On one side of the diagram, there are the unique features of intramurals. This includes:

- Played within the school or college.

- Opportunities for students to interact with peers.

- Usually smaller in size.

On the other side of the diagram, there are the unique features of extramurals. This includes:

- played outside the school or college.

- opportunities to interact with students from other schools or colleges.

- usually larger in size.

To become a good leader, a student should follow the following steps:

i) Develop good communication skills - Listen attentively and communicate effectively.

iii) Participate in extracurricular activities - Join clubs, sports teams or other groups where you can develop teamwork, cooperation and decision-making skills.

v) Be accountable - Take responsibility for your actions and practice transparency.
