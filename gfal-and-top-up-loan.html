Calculating the interest or monthly payments (sometimes referred to as amortization or installment) on a personal loan, or any sort of loan for that matter, is done by adding up the principal and interest. Depending on the information you require, you should compute for different things.

SOLVE FOR INTEREST

This is the sample computation:

FORMULA:   Interest = (Principal  Amount)(Rate)(Time)

                      I = PRT

                      I = (10,000)(0.05)(5)

                     I = 2,500

Therefore, 2,500 is the compounded interest for 5 years.

Monthly Amortization = (Loan Amount) + (Compounded Interest) / 60mos.

                                   = (10,000) + (2,500) / 60 = 208.33

Therefore, 208.33 is the payables monthly.

Note: 60months = 5 Years

Additional information:

On the other hand, you should consider the monthly payments if you want to determine whether you can afford to return a loan each month. Knowing how to calculate the monthly amortization will help you determine whether your monthly spending will allow you to make your loan payments.

A cost that the lender assesses you for using its funds is known as interest. It is the additional amount you must pay toward the loan each month, on top of the amount you borrowed.

Understanding the components that determine your interest payment—principal, loan period, and interest rate—is essential before learning how personal loan interest is calculated.

The amount you borrow from the lender is known as the loan principal. The more principle you have, the more interest you'll have to pay.

The period it will take you to repay your loan in full is known as the loan term (or tenor). Typically, it is stated in terms of months or years. Longer periods result in cheaper monthly payments, but over the course of the loan, interest costs rise. Shorter durations result in larger monthly repayments but less overall interest expense.
