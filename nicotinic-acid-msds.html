21. Nicotine, a component of tobacco, is composed of C, H and N. A 5.20-mg sample of nicotine was combusted producing 14.242 mg CO2 and 4.083 mg H2O. What is the molecular formula of nicotine if the substance has a molecular weight of 160.0?​


Answer:

[tex]C_{10} H_{14} N_{2}[/tex]

Explanation:

Nicotine ->       [tex]CO_{2} + H_{2} O[/tex]

5.250mg          14.242mg    4.083mg

0.000034mol -> 0.0003       0.0002

that means [tex]1 = \frac{1}{0.000034} * 0.003[/tex]

therefore, 1 mole of Nicotine -> 10 mole of [tex]CO_{2}[/tex] and 7 mole of [tex]H_{2} O[/tex] on combustion

that means Nicotine had 10 carbon and 14 hydrogen -> [tex]2H_{2} O[/tex] contains 2-hydrogen atom.

Total Molar Mass = [tex]10 * 12 + 14 * 1[/tex] = 120 + 14 = 134

162 - 134 = 28g left

As we know, remaining atoms left are nitrogen only.

For nitrogen molar mass N2 = 14

as 28 is left => [tex]\frac{28}{14} = 2[/tex]

So, 2 Nitrogen is present

Total = 10 Carbon + 14 Hydrogen  + 2 Nitrogen

        = 120 + 14 + 28

         = 162 g/mol

Hence, [tex]C_{10} H_{14} N_{2}[/tex] is the molecular formula for Nicotine

29. The molecular weight of nicotine is 162 g. It contains 74.0% C, 8.7% H, and nitrogen. Calculate the formula for nicotine. ​


Solution:

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\text{mass C = 74.0 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass H = 8.7 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass N = 17.3 g}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]n \: \text{C = 74.0 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{12.01 g}} = \text{6.16153 mol}[/tex]

[tex]n \: \text{H = 8.7 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{1.008 g}} = \text{8.63095 mol}[/tex]

[tex]n \: \text{N = 17.3 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{14.01 g}} = \text{1.23483 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_{x}\text{H}_{y}\text{N}_{z}[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles.

[tex]x = \frac{\text{6.16153 mol}}{\text{1.23483 mol}} = 5[/tex]

[tex]y = \frac{\text{8.63095 mol}}{\text{1.23483 mol}} = 7[/tex]

[tex]z = \frac{\text{1.23483 mol}}{\text{1.23483 mol}} = 1[/tex]

Step 5: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_{5}\text{H}_{7}\text{N}[/tex]

Step 6: Represent a molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_{5}\text{H}_{7}\text{N})_{n}[/tex]

Step 7: Calculate the empirical weight.

empirical weight = (12.01 g × 5) + (1.008 g × 7) × (14.01 g × 1)

empirical weight = 81.116 g

Step 8: Divide the molecular weight by the empirical weight.

[tex]n = \frac{\text{molecular weight}}{\text{empirical weight}}[/tex]

[tex]n = \frac{\text{162 g}}{\text{81.116 g}}[/tex]

[tex]n = 2[/tex]

Step 9: Multiply the subscripts by the value of n to obtain the molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_{5}\text{H}_{7}\text{N})_{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molecular formula} = \text{C}_{10}\text{H}_{14}\text{N}_{2}}[/tex]
