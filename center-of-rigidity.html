8. which layer is rigid but molten?


Answer:

The layer of the Earth that is rigid but molten is called the Mantle. The mantle is the thickest layer of the Earth, making about 84% of the Earth's volume. It is made of very hot, and dense rock. This rock layer flows like asphalt because of the temperature difference from the top and bottom of the mantle. Earthquakes in the Earth are believed to be from when the Mantle moves.

Layers Of the Earth

Generally, the Earth has four basic layers: Crust, Mantle, Outer Core and Inner Core.

Crust

The crust is the thinnest layer of the Earth. The crust is around between 5 km thick ( from the ocean floor) to around 70 km thick from the land that we live in. The land that we live in is called the continental crust which is made up or rocks made up on silica and alumina called the "sial"

Mantle

The mantle is the thickest layer of the earth. It's almost 3000 km. thick. It's made of very hot and dense silicate rock which is made of magnesium and iron.

Outer Core

The outer core is made of liquid iron and nickel. The temperature in this layer can reach 5000 Centigrade. The outer core creates what is called the magnetic field. The magnetic field that is generated in the outer core creates a protective barrier around the Earth we live in so that we are shielded from the solar wind that's made by the Sun.

Inner Core

The Earth's inner core is as hot as the surface of the sun, and is the hottest part of the earth. Like the outer core, the inner core is made up of iron and nickel. However, because it is under so much pressure, the iron and nickel are solid even if its scorching.
