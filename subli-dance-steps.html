16. What form of folk dance is subli


What form of folk dance is subli?

It is a religious form of folk dance. Subli is a traditional folk dance of the Philippines which is still quite popular today.

What is Subli?

The term subli owes its meaning to two Tagalog words "subsub" which means to fall with the head and "bali" which means broken. In this dance, the dancers appear to be crooked and lame in the dance. It is a ceremonial worship dance performed in homage to the Holy Cross referred to in the vernacular as Mahal na Poong Santa Cruz.

The answer is letter A. Rural/countryside dances A folk dance is a dance created by people that reflects the life of the people of a particular country or area. Folk dances are not often associated with ethnic dances. Ritual dances or dances of ritual origin, for example, are not called folk dances. Because of their function, ritual dances are often referred to as "religious dances." When it is necessary to highlight the cultural origins of the dance, the words "ethnic" and "traditional" are used. Almost all folk dances are cultural in this way. Although some dances, such as polka, transcend ethnic lines and also the line between "folk" and "ballroom dancing," ethnic distinctions are often significant enough to be mentioned.

Dances of the Philippine Countryside

Dances from the rural Christian lowlands are well known and nearest to the Filipino heart: a country blessed with so much charm. These dances, according to the Filipinos, exemplify the fiesta spirit and a love of life. They share joy in their work, adoration for music, and delight in the simple pleasures of life. Colorful balintawak and patadyong skirts for ladies, and camisa de chino and colored trousers for men, are typical clothing in the Rural Suite.

Example :

Pandanggo Sa Ilaw

This graceful and balanced dance originated on Lubang Island, Mindoro, in the Visayas region. Pandanggo is derived from the Spanish word fandango, which is a dance of energetic steps and clapping that changes in rhythm in 3/4 time. This pandanggo is distinguished by the appearance of three tinggoy, or oil lamps, balanced on the head and the back of each side.

Cariñosa

This seductive dance is well-known in the Philippines. Cariosa is a Latin word that means affectionate, lovable, or pleasant. The dancers perform hide-and-seek gestures and other flirtatious activities with a fan or handkerchief, showing romantic feelings for one another. There are several variations on this dance, but the hide-and-seek moves are shared by both.

Tinikling

This 'Visayan' dance was found in Leyte where this dance originated. Dancers imitate the tikling bird’s legendary grace and speed as they skillfully play, chase each other, run over tree branches, or dodge bamboo traps set by rice farmers. Hence it is named after the bird, tikling. this version of the dance is done between a pair of bamboo poles.

26. 11. The steps of this dance was improvised new steps, it imitates the courtship movements of a localspecies of duck known as "itik."D. TINIKLINGA. PANGALAYB. SUBLIC. ITIK ITIK​


Answer:

C. ITIK ITIK

Explanation:

Itik-itik is a mimetic folk dance in the Philippines. It originated in Surigao in Philippines. Itik-itik was discovered in this town by National Artist for Dance Francisca Reyes-Aquino. Originating from a dance called Sibay and performed to the tune of Dejado, the story was told that an expert young dancer named Cayetana at a barrio of Cantilan during a baptismal party had become so carried away with the rhythm that she began to improvise short, choppy steps similar to ducks and then splash water on their backs. The dance immediately became popular in the province for stage performances and social dancing. Later, Mr. Antonino Arreza, a native of Cantilan and a grandfather of Prospero Pichay, Jr. was believed be the one who compose the lyrics of Itik-itik.
