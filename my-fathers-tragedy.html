"My Father's Tragedy" is a short story by Carlos Bulosan, a Filipino-American author, that tells the story of a boy named Juan who grows up in a rural village in the Philippines. Juan's father is a respected and hardworking farmer who is very committed to supporting his family. But despite his best efforts, Juan's father struggles to make ends meet, and his family will often run out of food and other necessities.

As Juan grows older, he begins to understand the magnitude of his father's struggles and sacrifices. He sees his father being exploited by wealthy landowners and how government policies favor the rich over the poor. Despite these challenges, Juan's father never loses hope and continues to work tirelessly to provide for his family.

Unfortunately, Juan's father dies trying to sell his crops at the market. His death was the result of a combination of factors, including exhaustion from working long hours in the fields, extreme heat, and a lack of medical care.

The story is a powerful portrayal of the struggles faced by working-class families in the Philippines and highlights the injustices often perpetrated against them by those in power. Through the character of Father Juan, Bulosan celebrates the resilience and dignity of ordinary people and sheds light on the systemic problems that keep them trapped in poverty and despair.
