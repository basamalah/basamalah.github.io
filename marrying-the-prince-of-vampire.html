23. Write a script about a poor girl who later meets the prince and becomes the queen when they get married(NOT MORE THAN 10 SCENES)


Scene 1: Introduction of the poor girl

(Interior of a small house, the girl is sweeping the floor)

Girl: (whispering to herself) I need to sell more flowers today if we want to have enough food for dinner.

(She walks to the door and opens it, she sees a group of people walking by)

Girl: Good morning! Would you like to buy some flowers? (she offers a small bouquet)

Scene 2: A chance encounter with the prince

(The prince and his entourage walk by and stop at the girl's stand)

Prince: (smiling) Good morning. I'll take these. (he hands her a handful of coins)

Girl: (surprised) Thank you, your highness!

Prince: (looking around) This town seems to be in need of some help. (he looks at the girl) What's your name?

Girl: My name is Sarah, your highness.

Scene 3: The prince's proposal

(Interior of the prince's castle, the prince is pacing back and forth)

Prince: (thinking to himself) I can't get Sarah out of my mind. She's kind, hardworking, and brave. I need to ask her to marry me.

(He walks to the door and opens it, the girl is waiting outside)

Prince: Sarah, would you do me the honor of becoming my queen?

Girl: (taken aback) Your highness, are you serious?

Prince: More serious than I've ever been. Will you marry me?

Scene 4: The wedding

(Exterior of the castle, the wedding ceremony is taking place)

(The prince and Sarah exchange vows in front of the kingdom)

Prince: I, (prince's name), take you, Sarah, to be my lawfully wedded wife.

Sarah: I, Sarah, take you, (prince's name), to be my lawfully wedded husband.

(They exchange rings and kiss, the crowd cheers)

Scene 5: The new queen

(Interior of the castle, Sarah is getting dressed for her coronation ceremony)

Sarah: (looking in the mirror) I never thought I'd be a queen.

(She walks outside to the ceremony)

(The prince places the crown on her head)

Prince: (smiling) From this day forward, you are the queen of this kingdom.

Scene 6: The transformation

(Interior of the castle, Sarah is holding a meeting with her advisors)

Sarah: (firmly) We need to do something about the poverty in this kingdom. I want to implement policies to help the poor and create equality for all.

Advisor: (skeptical) Your highness, the old guard won't like these changes.

Sarah: (determined) I don't care. It's time for a change.

Scene 7: The old guard resistance

(Interior of the castle, Sarah is in a heated argument with a group of old guard members)

Old Guard Member: (angrily) These changes will disrupt the balance of power. We won't stand for it.

Sarah: (calmly) I understand your concerns, but I'm doing what's best for the kingdom and its people.

(The old guard members storm out of the room)

Scene 8: Overcoming obstacles

(Interior of the castle, Sarah is meeting with a group of citizens)

Citizen: (excitedly) Your highness, the new policies have helped so many of us. We are forever grateful.

Sarah: (smiling) It's my pleasure. I will continue to work hard to bring positive changes to this kingdom.

(The citizens cheer and clap)

Scene 9: A growing family

(Interior of the castle, Sarah and the prince are in their bedroom, holding a baby)

Prince: (smiling) Our family is growing.

Sarah: (teary-eyed) I never thought I'd have this much happiness.

(They kiss and hold each other close)

Scene 10: The legacy

(Exterior of the castle, a group of people are gathering to remember Sarah and her impact on the kingdom)

Speaker: (emotionally) Queen Sarah will always be remembered for her kindness, compassion, and tireless efforts to help the kingdom and its people. Her legacy lives on, inspiring future generations.

(The crowd nods and cheers in agreement)

During many weeks the queen could hardly eat or sleep, so sorely did she grieve for him, and the prince feared that she would die also if she went on weeping; so he begged her to go with him to a beautiful place that he knew of on the other side of the forest, and after some time she consented.
