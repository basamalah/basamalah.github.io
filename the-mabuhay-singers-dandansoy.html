3. why do you think the singer in the song dandansoy had to leave?​


Answer:

"Dandansoy" is a traditional Filipino Visayan folk song that tells the story of a man bidding farewell to his lover named Dandansoy. The reason why the singer had to leave is not explicitly stated in the song, but it is implied that he is going away for work or some other reason.

The opening line of the song says, "Dandansoy, bayaan ta ikaw" which translates to "Dandansoy, I have to leave you." The singer then goes on to express his sadness at having to leave his beloved and promises to return someday. He also asks Dandansoy to remember him while he is away and to wait for his return.

Overall, the reason why the singer had to leave is not the focus of the song. Instead, the song emphasizes the deep emotions of separation and longing that arise when two people are forced to part ways.

Here are the lyrics to "Dandansoy" in its original Visayan language and an English translation:

Visayan:

Dandansoy, bayaan ta ikaw

Pauli ako sa Payaw

Ugaling kon ikaw hidlawon

Ang Payaw imo lang lantawon.

Dandansoy, kon imo apason

Bisan tubig di magbalon

Ugaling kon ka malipayon

Sa gugma ko'g saad, damgo ko'y hinumdumon.

English Translation:

Dandansoy, I have to leave you

I'm going back to Payaw

But if you miss me

Just look towards Payaw.

Dandansoy, even if you stir the waters

It won't cause me to turn back

But if you find happiness

Remember the promise of my love and my dreams.
