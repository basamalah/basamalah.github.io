2. short story using of adverb of time​


Answer:

Once upon a time, there was a young girl named Sarah who lived in a small village at the edge of a forest. Every day, she would wake up early in the morning and go to the forest to collect firewood. She would spend most of the day gathering enough wood to last her family for the week.

One day, as she was walking through the forest, she heard a strange noise. She looked around, but she couldn't see anything. She continued walking, but the noise grew louder and more persistent. Suddenly, she saw a bright light shining through the trees.

Curiously, she followed the light until she came to a clearing. There, she saw a group of fairies dancing and singing around a magical tree. The fairies welcomed Sarah and invited her to join in their dance.

For hours, Sarah danced and sang with the fairies. As the sun began to set, she realized that she had lost track of time. She knew that she needed to hurry home before it got dark. She said goodbye to the fairies and ran back to her village as fast as she could.

When she arrived home, her family was worried about her. They had been looking for her all afternoon. Sarah explained what had happened, and her family was amazed. They had never heard of fairies in the forest before.

From that day on, Sarah would often go to the forest to dance and sing with the fairies. However, she always made sure to keep track of the time, so she wouldn't worry her family again. And every time she saw the fairies, she knew that she had to hurry home before it got dark.

3. write a short story with five adverbs of manner and five adverbs of time and five adverbs of place​


Answer:

Once upon a time, there was a young girl named Lily who lived in a small town. She had always dreamed of exploring the world, but she was too afraid to leave the safety of her home.

One day, as she was walking through the forest, she heard a rustling sound. Curiosity getting the best of her, she cautiously walked towards the sound. Suddenly, she saw a brightly colored bird fly past her with incredible speed.

Amazed by the bird's speed, she ran after it as quickly as she could, jumping over logs and dodging trees. Eventually, the bird led her to a beautiful waterfall, where it perched on a nearby rock. Lily stood there, awestruck by the natural beauty of the place.

As the sun began to set, she realized that she had been out for hours and it was starting to get dark. Quickly, she gathered her things and started to make her way back home, using the light of the moon to guide her.

Breathlessly, she arrived home just as the clock struck midnight. Exhausted, but filled with a sense of adventure and wonder, she fell asleep dreaming of all the incredible places she would explore in the future.

Adverbs of manner: cautiously, quickly, awestruck, breathlessly, exhausted

Adverbs of time: once, always, eventually, hours, midnight

Adverbs of place: small town, forest, waterfall, nearby rock, home

15. write a short story on your own and underline the adverbs ​


Answer:

Once upon a time, in a far-off land, there lived a kind and gentle prince. He spent his days exploring the lush, verdant forests and sparkling, crystal clear lakes. One day, as he was wandering through the woods, he heard a faint, pitiful cry for help. Intrigued, he followed the sound until he came upon a tower, hidden deep in the forest.

Climbing the steep, winding staircase, he soon reached the top of the tower, where he found a beautiful princess, who was being held captive by a fierce dragon. Without hesitation, the brave prince drew his sword and charged towards the dragon, who let out a deafening roar.

The prince fought bravely, striking the dragon with precise and powerful blows. Despite the dragon's fierce resistance, the prince was able to deliver the final blow, and the dragon fell to the ground with a thunderous crash.

The princess was overjoyed and thanked the prince profusely. She told him that she had been held captive for many years by the dragon, and that she had given up hope of ever being rescued. She then asked the prince to take her back to his kingdom where they would live happily ever after.

The prince, overjoyed, agreed and together they returned to his kingdom, where they were married in a grand ceremony attended by all the people of the land. And from that day on, the kingdom was filled with peace and prosperity, and the prince and princess lived happily ever after.
