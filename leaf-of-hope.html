4. Difference between monocot leaf and dicot leaf


Answer:

DIFFERENCE BETWEEN MONOCOT LEAF AND DICOT LEAF ??

Monocot Leaf

Monocotyledons or Monocots are flowering plants with seeds having a single cotyledon or embryonic leaf. There are about 60000 species of monocots found worldwide.

Monocot leaves are slender and elongated with parallel veins. Compared to other forms of veins, in parallel venation, the veins are small in size with even smaller veins connecting them.

Monocots have flower parts in sets of three. Their roots are fibrous. A few examples of Monocots are Bananas, Palm trees, Grasses, water plantains, Lilies, and Orchids.

Dicot Leaf

Dicotyledons or Dicots are flowering plants with seeds having two cotyledons or embryonic leaves. There are 175000 known species of dicots.

The leaves of a dicot plant have veins distributed in a net-like or reticulated pattern. In such leaves, the veins appear like a finely branched network throughout the leaf blade, with thin veins reticulating between the prominent veins.

The flower parts in Dicots are tetramerous or pentamerous, i.e. in multiples of four or five. Dicots have a tap root system with a long deep primary root growing into finer secondary branches.

Some examples for the Dicots are Oaks, Elms, Maples, Mango, Papaya, Radish, Rose, Castor, and Guava.

9. how does a monocot leaf differ from a dicot leaf​


Answer:

Both monocots and dicots form different leaves. Monocot leaves are characterized by their parallel veins, while dicots form “branching veins.” Leaves are another important structure of the plant because they are in charge of feeding the plant and carrying out the process of photosynthesis.

24. How does a simple leaf differ from a compound leaf?


NOTE: For better understanding of the differences, check out the simple and compound leaf diagram provided below.

Simple and compound leaves have lots of differences, listed below are the major differences in each leaf.

In simple leaves, the leaf blade is undivided into smaller sections. Compounds leaves are usually have divisions on them. These small leaves in compounds leaves are called leaflets.In simple leaves, the bud is found in between the petiole and the stem (called axil.) The leaflets of the compounds leaves do not have axil. The buds are placed on the axil of the midrib.Lateral buds occur at the base of the petiole for the simple leaves. In compound leaves, these lateral buds are absent in each of the leaflet.

Surface area- leaves are flat and thin thus providing more surface area for exchange of gases.
