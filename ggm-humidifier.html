28. The air that we breathe has different pollutants. How does the body prevent dirt, dust, and other tiny particles from getting into the body?A. The lungs get rid of these particles.B. The larynx and the pharynx trap these particles.C. The bronchi and bronchioles screen these particles.D. The cilia, mucous membrane, and trachea filter and humidify these particles.


Answer:

D. The cilia, mucous membrane, and trachea filter and humidify these particles.

Explanation:

The body has several mechanisms in place to prevent dirt, dust, and other tiny particles from entering the body. One of these is the trachea, which is a tube that carries air to and from the lungs. The trachea is lined with tiny hairs called cilia, which work to sweep particles and mucus away from the lungs. The mucous membrane, which lines the trachea and other respiratory passages, also produces mucus that helps to trap and remove particles. Together, the cilia, mucous membrane, and trachea work to filter and humidify the air that we breathe, helping to protect the body from harmful particles.
