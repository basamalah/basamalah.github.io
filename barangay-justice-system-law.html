2. Crime is an action or omission that constitutes an offense that may be prosecuted by the state and is punishable by law.

4. Justice is a concept on ethics and law that means that people behave in a way that is fair, equal and balanced for everyone.

5. Criminal justice administration focuses on the leadership and operations of the various aspects of the criminal justice system, such as the courts and law enforcement, which work together to help prevent and punish crime.

6. The adult criminal justice system is comprised of four components; legislation, law enforcement, courts, and corrections.

23. why is Law Enforcements considered as one of the pillars of Criminal Justice System? Why is it relevance?​


Answer:

Law enforcement is considered one of the pillars of the criminal justice system because it is responsible for the initial investigation into suspected crimes, the apprehension of suspects, and the enforcement of the laws enforced by the governmental agencies. As such, law enforcement plays a critical role in determining the course of criminal proceedings, from investigation to trial and, if necessary, to punishment. In addition, law enforcement is critical for deterring, preventing, and solving criminal activity. The work of law enforcement is essential for providing a safe and orderly environment for people to live and work in, and for enhancing the public’s perception of security.
