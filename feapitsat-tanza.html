24. discuss the main difference qualitative research Competitive State Anxiety and Self Confidence among the Student-Athletes of Tanza​


Answer:

Qualitative research is a type of research that focuses on exploring and understanding the underlying reasons, opinions, and motivations of individuals or groups. The main difference between Competitive State Anxiety and Self Confidence among the Student-Athletes of Tanza in qualitative research lies in the research questions, the data collection methods, and the analysis process.

Research Questions:

In studying Competitive State Anxiety and Self Confidence among the Student-Athletes of Tanza, qualitative research may seek to answer questions such as:

•What factors contribute to the competitive state anxiety experienced by student-athletes during competition?

•How do student-athletes perceive and define self-confidence?

•How do student-athletes manage their competitive state anxiety and cultivate self-confidence in their athletic pursuits?

Data Collection Methods:

Qualitative research typically involves collecting data through methods such as interviews, focus groups, observations, and document analysis.

For Competitive State Anxiety, data collection methods may include interviewing student-athletes about their experiences of anxiety during competition, observing their behavior and reactions in stressful situations, and examining coach/player communication during games.

For Self Confidence, data collection methods may include interviewing student-athletes about their perceptions of self-confidence, observing how they behave when they feel confident, and examining the role of supportive relationships in building self-confidence.

Analysis Process:

Qualitative research involves analyzing data through techniques such as thematic analysis, content analysis, and discourse analysis.

For Competitive State Anxiety, analysis may involve identifying common themes and patterns in how student-athletes describe their experiences of anxiety, examining the language they use to describe their feelings, and exploring the social and environmental factors that contribute to their anxiety.

For Self Confidence, analysis may involve identifying key themes and patterns in how student-athletes define and cultivate self-confidence, exploring the role of social support in building confidence, and examining the ways in which confidence affects athletic performance.
