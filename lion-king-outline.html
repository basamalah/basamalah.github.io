9. short summary of the lion king


After being anointed as the next king of the jungle, young lion cub Simba is every bit as eager to take his proud father Mufasa's place as the king. However, this makes Simba's villainous uncle Scar jealous of him. But when Mufasa is suddenly murdered by Scar, Simba feels responsible for his death and runs away from home. Meeting up with two outcasts named Timon and Pumbaa, Simba embraces their ways of 'Hakuna Matata'. Years later, approached by his childhood friend Nala and the wise baboon Rafiki, Simba must return home to stop Scar's evil reign and fulfill his destiny as the new king.

21. critique of the story of the lion king​


Answer:

The story of "The Lion King" is a classic tale of good versus evil and includes many humorous moments and catchy musical numbers. It has a strong moral foundation, and its focus on themes such as family, friendship, duty, and courage make it a story that can be appreciated by all ages. While the visuals and music make this movie a delight to watch, some critics have argued that the story can be a bit formulaic at times and may not appeal to everyone. Additionally, the dialogue can feel a bit forced, and the characters may seem too perfect in terms of their motivations and objectives for some viewers.
