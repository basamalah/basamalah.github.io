1. D. Together, Gino and Gary have P183. If Gino has P6 less than twice as much as Gary, how much money money does Gino have?​


Let's start by setting up some equations to represent the given information:

Gino's amount of money: x

Gary's amount of money: y

Together, they have P183: x + y = 183

Gino has P6 less than twice as much as Gary: x = 2y - 6

We can use the second equation to substitute for x in the first equation:

(2y - 6) + y = 183

Simplifying the left side:

3y - 6 = 183

Adding 6 to both sides:

3y = 189

Dividing both sides by 3:

y = 63

So Gary has P63. We can substitute this back into the second equation to find Gino's amount:

x = 2y - 6

x = 2(63) - 6

x = 120

Therefore, Gino has P120.
