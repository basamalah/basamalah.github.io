4. How to use Personal Protective Equipment (PPEs)​


Answer:

1. Identify and gather the proper PPE to don. Ensure choice of gown size is correct (based on training).

2. Perform hand hygiene using hand sanitizer.

3. Put on isolation gown. Tie all of the ties on the gown. Assistance may be needed by other healthcare personnel.

4. Put on NIOSH-approved N95 filtering facepiece respirator or higher (use a facemask if a respirator is not available). If the respirator has a nosepiece, it should be fitted to the nose with both hands, not bent or tented. Do not pinch the nosepiece with one hand. Respirator/facemask should be extended under chin. Both your mouth and nose should be protected. Do not wear respirator/facemask under your chin or store in scrubs pocket between patients.*

- Respirator: Respirator straps should be placed on crown of head (top strap) and base of neck (bottom strap). Perform a user seal check each time you put on the respirator.

- Facemask: Mask ties should be secured on crown of head (top tie) and base of neck (bottom tie). If mask has loops, hook them appropriately around your ears.

5. Put on face shield or goggles. When wearing an N95 respirator or half facepiece elastomeric respirator, select the proper eye protection to ensure that the respirator does not interfere with the correct positioning of the eye protection, and the eye protection does not affect the fit or seal of the respirator. Face shields provide full face coverage. Goggles also provide excellent protection for eyes, but fogging is common.

6. Put on gloves. Gloves should cover the cuff (wrist) of gown.

7. Healthcare personnel may now enter patient room.

7. Give the uses of the personal protective equipment (PPE) written below. Use a separate sheet of paper for your answer. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Facemask Hand Gloves Apron Working Clothes Goggles Boots Uses of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)​


Answer:

Facemask: A facemask is used to protect the wearer from inhaling harmful particles, such as dust, smoke, or harmful chemicals. It is commonly used in healthcare settings, construction sites, and other industries where the worker may be exposed to harmful particles.

Hand Gloves: Hand gloves are worn to protect the hands from cuts, scrapes, or other injuries. They are also used to protect the hands from harmful chemicals, heat, or cold. They are commonly used in healthcare, construction, and industrial settings.

Apron: An apron is worn to protect clothing and skin from spills, stains, or other types of damage. They are commonly used in healthcare, food service, and laboratory settings.

Working Clothes: Working clothes are worn to protect the worker from hazardous conditions. They may be made of special materials to protect against fire, chemicals, or other hazards. They are commonly used in construction, industrial, and emergency services.

Goggles: Goggles are worn to protect the eyes from harmful particles, dust, or chemicals. They are also used to protect the eyes from bright lights, glare, or UV radiation. They are commonly used in healthcare, construction, and industrial settings.

Boots: Boots are worn to protect the feet and toes from injuries. They are also used to protect the feet from slips, trips, and falls. They are commonly used in construction, industrial, and emergency services.

18. invest in the right ppe(personal protective equipment)​


Investing in the right personal protective equipment pays off. Choosing the right personal protective equipment to reduce exposure to hazards and conforms safety to all personnel using it.  Basically, it is the employer’s responsibility to provide clean personal protective equipment and replaced it when needed. As a worker, it is your right to have personal protective equipment available to complete your job duties. Personal protective equipment (https://brainly.ph/question/1818419) is composed of gloves, foot and eye protection, a mask or respirators, and a full-body gown. Other items included as personal protective equipment for some situations are protective hearing devices, hard hats or other head covers, and full bodysuits.

Proper Way of Wearing Personal Protective Equipment

Below is one example of a proper way of wearing PPE:

Prepare all the personal protective equipment in one place and make sure that the gown is fitted to the user base on the training attended. Perform hand washing before touching the personal protective equipment. Put the gown in isolation and secure all ties with the help of other healthcare personnel. Put on an approved facemask or respirator. Put on face shields or goggles for eye protection. Put on gloves. Make sure it covers the gown’s cuff Workers may now come into the area. Proper Way of Taking-off Personal Protective Equipment

Below is one example of a proper way of taking-off PPE:

Remove gloves using glove-in-glove or birds beak technique .Untie all ties, reach up to the shoulders, and cautiously take-off gown away from the body.  Dispose of the gown in the designated garbage bin. Workers may now leave the area. Perform hand washing before touching any object. Remove the eye protection by pulling the strap away from the head. Do not touch its front area. Properly remove and dispose of respirator or facemask in the designated bin. Repeat handwashing before touching any object.

PPE can be classified into categories:

eye and face protectionhand protectionbody protectionrespiratory protection hearing protection.

PPE should be used when all other measures are inadequate to control exposure. It protects only the wearer, while being worn, and can be worn in construction sites or in hospital nowadays.

4.) What can be done to ensure proper use of personal protective equipment?

All personal protective equipment should be safely designed and constructed, and should be maintained in a clean and reliable fashion. It should fit comfortably, encouraging worker use, and protect the user.
