22. Showing the proper storing of stocks, soups, and sauces, and how it is reconstituted.​


Answer:

Storing Stocks:

1. Store stocks in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

2. If you plan to store the stock for longer than 3 days, freeze it in an airtight container or freezer bag.

3. Label the container with the date and type of stock before storing it.

Reconstituting Stocks:

1. Place the frozen stock in a pot and heat over medium-low heat until it is completely thawed and heated through.

2. Stir occasionally to ensure that the stock is evenly heated throughout.

3. Once heated, use as desired or store in an airtight container for up to 3 days in the refrigerator or up to 6 months in the freezer.

Storing Soups:

1. Store soups in an airtight container or freezer bag in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or freeze for up to 6 months.

2. Label the container with the date and type of soup before storing it.

Reconstituting Soups:

1. Place frozen soup into a pot and heat over medium-low heat until it is completely thawed and heated through, stirring occasionally to ensure that it is evenly heated throughout.

2. Once heated, use as desired or store in an airtight container for up to 3 days in the refrigerator or up to 6 months in the freezer

Storing Sauces:

1. Store sauces in an airtight container or jar in the refrigerator for up to 1 week or freeze for up to 6 months.

2. Label the container with the date and type of sauce before storing it

Reconstituting Sauces:

1. Place frozen sauce into a pot and heat over medium-low heat until it is completely thawed and heated through, stirring occasionally to ensure that it is evenly heated throughout

2 .Once heated, use as desired or store in an airtight container for up to 1 week in the refrigerator or up to 6 months in the freezer
