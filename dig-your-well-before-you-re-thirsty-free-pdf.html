27. When you are very thirsty, you drink water continuously and catch your breath afterwards. Why is this so? ​

When you drink water, the epiglottis close the larynx so that water will not go down to the lower respiratory tract and instead it goes to the esophagus down to the stomach. This mean also that there are is no oxygen enters the lungs for gas exchange and distributed in the different part of the body just for a couple of seconds. As a result, you have to compensate by increased in breathing to pay those oxygen dept.

This is a compensatory mechanism so that enough oxygen will be deliver in the different organs of the body. Oxygen is the vital ingredients in making ATP molecules. ATP molecules serves as the energy of the cells in order for them to do their tasks.
