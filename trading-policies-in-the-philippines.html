2. trade policies and the Philippine economy summary ​


Explanation:

Trade policies play a crucial role in shaping the Philippine economy, as the country heavily relies on international trade to support its growth and development. Over the years, the Philippine government has pursued various trade policies aimed at promoting exports, attracting foreign investment, and protecting local industries.

One of the key policies is the Generalized System of Preferences (GSP), which grants duty-free and preferential treatment to Philippine exports to selected countries such as the United States, Japan, and the European Union. This policy has helped to boost the country's exports of goods and services, particularly in the electronics, automotive, and business process outsourcing sectors.

The government has also implemented various measures to attract foreign investment, such as offering tax incentives, streamlining business registration procedures, and improving infrastructure. The Philippine Economic Zone Authority (PEZA) has been instrumental in promoting foreign direct investment by providing a range of incentives to foreign investors who locate their operations in designated economic zones.

However, some trade policies have also faced criticism for their potential negative impacts on the Philippine economy. For example, import tariffs and protectionist measures have been criticized for hindering competition and limiting access to cheaper goods, which can ultimately hurt consumers and small businesses.

Overall, trade policies remain a vital component of the Philippine economy, and the government must continue to balance the interests of various stakeholders, including exporters, local businesses, and consumers, to achieve sustainable and inclusive growth.

10. in your own words and understanding.Discuss Trade policy.​


Answer:

Trade policy refers to the regulations and agreements that control imports and exports to foreign countries.

The Philippines maintains a two-tiered tariff policy for sensitive agricultural products including rice, corn, pork, chicken meat, sugar, and coffee. These products are subject to a tariff rate quota (TRQ) and all imports outside of the minimum access volume are taxed at a higher out-of-quota rate.

For example, if a policy change leads to the import of bananas, and bananas were previously not imported, bananas will be considered a new product. If bananas were already imported, but a trade policy change leads to imports from a new country, such as Philippines, Philippine bananas will be referred to as a new variety.

Answer:

Ang Trade Policy ay mga batas na may kaugnayan sa pagpapalitan ng mga kalakal o serbisyo na kasangkot sa internasyonal na kalakalan kabilang ang mga buwis, subsidyo, at regulasyon ng import at export. Ang ilang mga bansa ay nagpapahintulot ng mabibigat na taripa sa mga na-import na kalakal upang maprotektahan ang kanilang lokal na industriya. Ang mataas na buwis sa pag-import ay nagpapalala ng mga presyo ng mga na-import na kalakal sa mga lokal na merkado, tinitiyak na ang mga lokal na produkto ay mas hinahangad.
