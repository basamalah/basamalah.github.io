Another way of thinking about "86 take away 35" is to replace the words "take away" with a subtraction symbol (-). When we turn "86 take away 35" into a subtraction equation, we can solve it like any other math problem. We have written the equation below:

86 - 35 = ?

13. A world record holder ran 800 meters in 86 seconds. What was the average speed of the athlete when rounded to the nearest tenth?​


Answer:

To find the average speed of the athlete, we need to divide the distance covered by the time taken. In this case, the distance covered is 800 meters and the time taken is 86 seconds.

Average speed = Distance ÷ Time

Average speed = Distance ÷ TimeAverage speed = 800 meters ÷ 86 seconds

Average speed ≈ 9.3023 meters/second (using a calculator)

Rounding this value to the nearest tenth, we get:

Average speed ≈ 9.3 meters/second

Therefore, the average speed of the athlete rounded to the nearest tenth is 9.3 meters/second.
