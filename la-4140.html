2. Find the first three terms of the arithmetic series in which a1 = 6, an = 201, and Sn = 4140.


Answer:

6, 112, 218

Step-by-step explanation;

In mathematics, an arithmetic sequence also called as arithmetic progression define as a sequence of numbers with a common difference where the difference between the consecutive terms is constant.

Arithmetic SequenceHas a common difference (d), difference of two consecutive termFormula for nth term is an= a1 + (n-1)dFormula for sum of all term is Sn= n[1/2(a1+an)] Example of arithmetic sequence: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11,..Set of consecutive even numbers is example of arithmetic sequence, alsoSet of consecutive odd numbers is another example of arithmetic sequence.

Given Problem: Finding First 3 Terms

Use the two formula an= a1+(n-1)d, and Sn=n[1/2(a1+an)], where

Sn= sum of termsan= nth terma1= first termn= number of termd= common difference

Solution

Sn=n[1/2(a1+an)]

4140= n[1/2(6+201)]

4140= n[1/2(207)]

4140= 103.5n

n= 40

Find d

an= a1+(n-1)d

4140= 6+(40-1)d

4140 - 6 = 39d

4134= 39d

d= 106

Find the 3 consecutive terms

First term is 6

a1= 6

a2= 6 + 106

a2= 112

a3= 112 + 106

a3= 218

The first three terms of the arithmetic sequence are 6, 112, and 218.

x = 120 tickets

x+y = 300

y = 300-x

y = 300-120

y = 180 tickets

19. 4. Find the first three terms of the anthmeticseries in which a, = 6, an = 201, and sn =4140A. 6. 11. 16C.5. 10.15B. 6, 15, 40 D. 6, 46, 86​


Answer:

A. 6, 11, 16

Step-by-step explanation:

Sn = n/2(a sub 1 + a sub n)     Formula for arithmetic series

Substitute the variables with values

4,140 = n/2 (6 + 201)

4,140 = (207n)/2

8,280 = 207n

(8,280)/207 = (207n)/207

40 = n

There are 40 terms

a sub n = a sub 1 + (n-1)d    Formula for arithmetic sequence

201 = 6 + (40-1)d

201-6 = 39d

195 = 39d

(195)/39 = (39d)/39

5 = d

The common difference is 5

How many sides does a polygon have if the sum of its interior angles is 4140°?

[tex]\bold{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

We will use the formula [tex]\scriptsize\boxed{\tt 180(n-2)=4140°}[/tex] where n is the number of sides.

[tex]\begin{array}{l}\large\tt 180(n-2)=4140\\\\\large\tt180n-360=4140\\\\\large\tt180n=4140+360\\\\\large\tt 180n=4500\\\\\large\tt\frac{180n}{180}=\frac{4500}{180}\\\\\large\red{\boxed{\tt n=25}}\end{array}[/tex]
