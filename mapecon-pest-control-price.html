12. 1. What is the difference between Pest Management and Pest control?​


Answer:

What is Pest Control?

Pest control experts determine the source and severity of the infestation and where the pests are breeding and living. They understand the life cycle of pests, and which natural factors both inside and around your property might be encouraging their growth. A professional pest control technician will then suggest services available for your specific pest issue, which should include safe, eco-friendly pesticides. Once a treatment plan has been agreed upon, the technician will then treat affected areas to eliminate existing pest issues.

What is Pest Management?

Pest management is professional care that prevents potential pest problems by identifying factors that may lead to future infestation. Professional companies will recommend lifestyle changes (e.g. removing open food sources, cleaning surfaces, clearing garbage surrounding a property), and, if necessary, apply a chemical or natural solution as an additional preventative measure. Follow-up inspections and treatments may be required to prohibit additional pest issues.

Their Differences

While pest control is a treatment that should eliminate infestation after it has already occurred, pest management seeks to limit the survival and reproduction of potential pests by removing sources of water, food, and shelter.

Pest control is typically a one-time treatment; pest management can be ongoing to prevent pest issues before they occur. Pest management is a valuable service for both home and business owners who have concerns about insects or furry animal pests in the area, and the recurrence of infestation.

When combined, pest control and pest management can create a long-term, effective solution for any pest problem.

19. Example of pest control​


Answer:

Pest control refers to the management or regulation of pests, which can cause damage to crops, harm human health, and create nuisance. Here are some examples of pest control:

1. Chemical control - The use of chemicals, such as insecticides, herbicides, and rodenticides, to eliminate or control pests. This method involves the application of pesticides to the affected area, which can kill or repel pests.

2. Biological control - The use of natural predators or parasites to control the population of pests. This method involves the introduction of natural enemies of the pest species, such as predators or parasites, to the affected area to control the pest population.

3. Cultural control - The use of cultural practices, such as crop rotation, sanitation, and planting resistant varieties, to prevent or reduce pest infestations. This method involves altering the environment or the behavior of the host to prevent or reduce pest damage.

4. Physical control - The use of physical barriers, traps, or devices to prevent or control pests. This method involves the use of physical barriers, such as netting or screens, to prevent pests from entering an area, or the use of traps to capture pests.

5. Integrated pest management - The use of a combination of different pest control methods to achieve effective and sustainable pest management. This method involves the integration of different pest control strategies to create a comprehensive and sustainable pest management program.
