16. A. What are the guidelines in checking the freshness of fish? B. What are the guidelines in checking the freshness of shellfish?​


Answer:

A. guidelines in checking the freshness of fish

- Fresh fish should have a mild scent and moist flesh, and appear freshly cut. Don't purchase fish that has a strong, fishy odor. Whole fish should have bright, bulging eyes and bright red or pink gills. Frozen fish should meet the fresh-smell test and have taut packaging with no evidence of ice or blood.

B. guidelines in checking the freshness of shellfish

- The shells should be clean and free of dirt and mud. Should be nice and clean. Feel them if they have a film or are slimy do not purchase. They should be covered in ice in the display case.Each form and species has different quality signs to examine. Live clams, oysters, or mussels should have shells that look moist and are tightly closed. If the shells gape slightly, have your retailer tap them. If the shells do not close or are cracked, do not purchase them.

29. What is the difference of Fresh and not fresh Chicken?


Answer:

The main difference between fresh and not fresh chicken is their smell and how they look .Fresh raw chicken is generally a light pink color with white chunks of fat, has low to no odor, and is soft and moist. Fresh cooked chicken is usually a dark pink color with white pieces of fat, has little to no odor, and is soft and moist. If your chicken is slimy, smells terrible, or has become a yellow, green, or gray hue, it has gone bad.
