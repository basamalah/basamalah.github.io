11. A 50-kg fish is swimming with a speed of 4 m/s.A.) What is its KE? B.) If the fish suddenly speeds up to 12 m/s to catch a small fish what is the value of KE at this point? ​


A. Kinetic Energy is 400 J

B. Kinetic Energy is 3.6 KJ

Explanation:

Problem #1 (4 m/s)

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

m = 50 kg

v = 4 m/s

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Kinetic Energy

Step 3: Solve for Kinetic Energy

Solution:

The formula for Kinetic Energy is given by:

KE = ½ mv²

where: KE - Kinetic Energy

m - mass

v - velocity

Substituting the value of m and v in the formula,

KE = (½)(50 kg)(4 m/s)²

KE = 400 kgm²/s² or 400 Nm or 400 J

Problem #2 (12 m/s)

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

m = 50 kg

v = 12 m/s

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Kinetic Energy

Step 3: Solve for Kinetic Energy

Solution:

The formula for Kinetic Energy is given by:

KE = ½ mv²

where: KE - Kinetic Energy

m - mass

v - velocity

Substituting the value of m and v in the formula,

KE = (½)(50 kg)(12 m/s)²

KE = 3600 kgm²/s² or 3600 Nm or 3.6 KJ
