he meaning of the word hazard can be confusing. Often dictionaries do not give specific definitions or combine it with the term "risk". For example, one dictionary defines hazard as "a danger or risk" which helps explain why many people use the terms interchangeably.

There are many definitions for hazard but the most common definition when talking about workplace health and safety is:

A hazard is any source of potential damage, harm or adverse health effects on something or someone.

Basically, a hazard is the potential for harm or an adverse effect (for example, to people as health effects, to organizations as property or equipment losses, or to the environment).

Sometimes the resulting harm is referred to as the hazard instead of the actual source of the hazard. For example, the disease tuberculosis (TB) might be called a "hazard" by some but, in general, the TB-causing bacteria (Mycobacterium tuberculosis) would be considered the "hazard" or "hazardous biological agent".

30. How did our ancestors' belief in different spirits of woods, seas, and earth affect their way of life?


Answer:

I believe that our ancestors had a lot more respect for nature because of this. Their belief system is very different than present society, which is becoming much clearer now due to the impact of climate change that we are now experiencing.

Our ancestors believed that spirits resided in the seas, the land, and the woods. They worshipped these entities and asked them for help. Our forebears believed that they will be punished if they did not show our environment the respect that it deserves. Many of them did not take more than they needed to feed their families.

As a matter of fact, there were nomadic people who would move from one place to another in search of food. While this can be attributed to their lack of knowledge in agriculture, some of them did this because of their beliefs as well.

You can even say that, in their own way, they understood that we have a finite amount of resources. They did not pillage the land and extract as much of it as they could like we are doing right now.
