5. life and works of grace Fatima soriano​


LIFE AND WORKS OF GRACE OF FATIMA SORIANO

Fatima Soriano is a vocalist who cannot see. Despite being born blind, Fatima was determined to achieve the impossible. Her kidneys stopped working when she was nine years old. After a 23-year-old kidney donor appeared, Fatima's television fame led to a text brigade in 2004 that generated P1.2 million to enable her have a kidney transplant. At first, doctors were concerned that Fatima's life may be in jeopardy if the surgery went forward since the donor's adult arteries and veins were only 90 percent compatible with Fatima's considerably smaller blood vessels. During the procedure on February 28, 2004, however, this fear was proved to be unfounded, as they turned out to be a perfect match.

Fatima was able to recuperate following surgery, but her experience under the knife made it extremely difficult for her to continue singing in the choir. Nothing is impossible, though, for her confidence in God's love demonstrated it once more.

For the last four years, Fatima Soriano has been encouraging ill and disturbed individuals. Religious organizations seek her out to bless their events. She lectured in the Philippines, Indonesia, Korea, and Canada in radio and television presentations, Holy Masses, and prayer rallies, reviving trust in defenseless people. Trust in God's grace is something she always tells others to do. There was a teleplay on her that was made. For her efforts, Fatima Soriano has earned several accolades, the most prestigious of which was the highest honour granted by the Catholic Church.

28. Mr. and Mrs. Soriano budget their monthly income so that they will be able tosave 25% of it. If Mr. Soriano's net salary is P22,500.00 and Mrs. Soriano's netsalary is P18,500.00, what is their monthly savings? How much is their budget forthe following expenses after deducting their savings?​


Answer:

Mr. and Mrs. Soriano's monthly saving is P10,250.00 and their budget for their expenses is P30,750.00

Step-by-step explanation:

• first, add both of their salaries:

22 500 + 18 500 = 41 000

• their monthly salary is P41,000.00

• now, get the 25% for their savings

note: 25% is equivalent to 1/4

• multiply their salary to 1/4:

(1) 41 500 × 1 = 41 500

(2) 41 500 ÷ 4 = 10 250

• P10,250 is their monthly saving because it is the 25% of P41,000 (their monthly savings)

• the rest aelre for their expenses. to get it, simply subtract 10 250 to 41 000:

41 000 - 10 250 = 30 750

• P30,750 is the total budget of Mr. and Mrs. Soriano that was left after deducting their monthly savings.
