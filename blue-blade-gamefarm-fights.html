12. The total area of 3 blades of a ceiling fan is . Two of the blades form an angle of 45° and the third blade forms an angle of 60° at the center. Find the radius of the blade.


Answer:

Let the radius of the ceiling fan be denoted by r.

The total area of the three blades is given as:

Total area = πr^2

Since two of the blades form an angle of 45 degrees at the center, their combined area is:

(2/3) x πr^2 x (45/360) = πr^2/4

Similarly, the area of the third blade, which forms an angle of 60 degrees at the center, is:

(1/3) x πr^2 x (60/360) = πr^2/18

Thus, we can write the equation:

Total area = Area of blade 1 + Area of blade 2 + Area of blade 3

πr^2 = πr^2/4 + πr^2/4 + πr^2/18

Simplifying the equation, we get:

πr^2/2 + πr^2/18 = πr^2

9πr^2/18 + πr^2/18 = πr^2

10πr^2/18 = πr^2

10/18 = 1/r^2

Solving for r, we get:

r^2 = 18/10

r = √(9/5)

r = 1.34 (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the radius of the ceiling fan blade is approximately 1.34 units.
