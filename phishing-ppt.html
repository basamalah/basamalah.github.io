7. what is phishing in online network ?


Phishing in online network is an act of deceiving an online user to get his or her personal information.  

Deceiving means, tricking the user to make him provide his information by means of giving out a link to a website that appears to be legit and trustworthy to make the victims believe that they are authentic sources. Phishing in a form of a website is one type. Many users can easily believe that the website or form they are giving their information to is from a trusted source since they could see logos and links. But if they checked the URL completely, they could verify that this is not the real website. Banks and other services-oriented websites doesn't ask for your information after registration. It is because they already have your information and if there is something that they needed you to update they would normally inform you through your phone and would ask you to personally visit the bank to make the changes in your information. Banks also practice double PINs and authentication measures by providing OTPs in their phones for confirmation on changes of data. These form details are redirecting to a database owned by hackers or someone who is in control.

Sometimes, an email message will receive by the victim that would tell him or her a story of wealthy man or woman looking for someone to inherit his / her riches. But in reality, it is just a hacker or someone who wants to get your information to use your account for malicious intent. It is easy to spot a phishing email. Phishing emails normally contains grammar errors and has no contact details or names.  

There are ways to prevent yourselves to be a victim of phishing.  

You must stay educated about phishing activities.  Be cautious as always. Never give out your information to anyone. Always check website's url before your provide your information.
