7. Why were the Spaniards defeated by the native Filipinos?


Answer:

Explanation:

the Spaniards were defeated by the native Filipinos when in the Battle of Mactan There lived two rival chiefs — Zula and Lapu-Lapu. While the former welcomed the Spanish conquistador and submitted to Spain, Lapu-Lapu strongly opposed Magellan's proposal and Humabon's orders. Thus, sparking the Battle of Mactan, Magellan sent a message to the natives saying that if they still refused to recognize the Spanish king and pay them tribute, they would demonstrate how effective their swords were at wounding people.
