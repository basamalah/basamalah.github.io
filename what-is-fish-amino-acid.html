11. Suppose you eat a serving of fish. What reactions must occur for the amino acid monomers in the protein of the fish to be converted to new proteins in your body?​.


After consuming a serving of fish, the protein in the fish must first be broken down into its constituent amino acids through the process of digestion. These amino acids are then absorbed into the bloodstream and transported to the cells throughout the body. Within the cells, the amino acids are used to synthesize new proteins according to the specific needs and instructions of each cell. This process of protein synthesis involves assembling the amino acids in a specific sequence according to the genetic code encoded in DNA, and requires the input of energy and various enzymes and cofactors. Ultimately, the new proteins produced by the cells can serve a variety of functions in the body, such as providing structural support, catalyzing chemical reactions, transporting molecules, and communicating signals between cells.
