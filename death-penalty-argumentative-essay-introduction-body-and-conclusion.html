3. make an argumentative essay ... should death penalty be used in penalizing criminals? compose with thesis statement and evidences and introduction, body,conclusion at least 150 words ​


Answer:

The death penalty, also known as capital punishment, is a controversial topic that has been debated for decades. While some argue that it is a necessary tool to punish the most heinous crimes and deter criminal behavior, others believe that it is an ineffective and inhumane punishment that should be abolished. In this essay, I will argue that the death penalty should not be used in penalizing criminals.

First and foremost, the death penalty is ineffective as a deterrent to crime. Many studies have shown that states with the death penalty have higher rates of murder than those without it. This suggests that the threat of the death penalty does not effectively prevent people from committing crimes. In addition, the death penalty is often applied unfairly and disproportionately to certain groups of people, such as people of color and those with mental illnesses. This is a clear violation of the principle of equal justice under the law.

Furthermore, the death penalty is costly and time-consuming. The appeals process for death penalty cases is lengthy and expensive, and it often takes years or even decades for a condemned person to be executed. This means that a significant amount of taxpayer money is being spent on a punishment that has been proven to be ineffective.

In conclusion, the death penalty is an ineffective, inhumane, and costly punishment that should be abolished. Instead of focusing on retribution, we should be investing in programs that address the root causes of crime and provide support and rehabilitation for those who have committed crimes. By doing so, we can create a more just and humane society for all.

- capital punishment, also known as the death penalty, is the state-sanctioned killing of a person as punishment for a crime. The sentence ordering that someone is punished with the death penalty is called a death sentence, and the act of carrying out such a sentence is known as an execution.

WHAT ARE THE ARGUMENTS IN FAVOR OF DEATH PENALTY?

- aguments in favour of capital, punishment: retribution, deterrence, rehabilitation, prevention of re-offending, closure and vindication, and incentive to help police.

11. write an argumentative essay compost by 6 paragraph and 350 wordsTOPIC : DEATH PENALTY​


Answer:

The death penalty, also known as capital punishment, is a highly debated and controversial issue in many parts of the world. Supporters of the death penalty argue that it is necessary for maintaining law and order, while opponents argue that it is a cruel and inhumane form of punishment that has no place in a civilized society. In this essay, I will argue that the death penalty should be abolished.

Firstly, the death penalty is not an effective deterrent to crime. Many studies have shown that the threat of execution does not significantly reduce the incidence of violent crime. In fact, some studies suggest that it may actually increase the rate of violent crime, as individuals who are facing the death penalty may be more likely to commit further crimes in an effort to avoid capture and prosecution.

Secondly, the death penalty is often applied unfairly. Minorities, the poor, and those with mental disabilities are more likely to receive the death penalty than other defendants, even when the evidence against them is weak. This is a clear violation of the principle of equal justice under the law.

Thirdly, the death penalty is irreversible. There have been numerous cases of individuals who were wrongly convicted and sentenced to death, only to be exonerated years later. If these individuals had been executed, there would be no way to undo the injustice that had been done.

Fourthly, the death penalty is a costly and inefficient system. It is far more expensive to prosecute a death penalty case than it is to prosecute a case where the defendant is facing life in prison. Additionally, appeals in death penalty cases can drag on for years, costing taxpayers millions of dollars.

Fifthly, the death penalty is a barbaric and inhumane form of punishment. There is no humane way to kill a human being, and the methods used in executions, such as lethal injection and electrocution, are often painful and cruel.

Finally, the death penalty is morally wrong. It violates the sanctity of human life and is inconsistent with the principles of a just and merciful society. Rather than seeking revenge, we should strive to create a system of justice that seeks to rehabilitate offenders and reduce crime.

In conclusion, the death penalty is an outdated and ineffective form of punishment that should be abolished. It is not a deterrent to crime, it is often applied unfairly, it is irreversible, it is costly and inefficient, it is barbaric and inhumane, and it is morally wrong. We should instead focus on creating a system of justice that seeks to rehabilitate offenders and reduce crime, rather than one that seeks revenge.

17. write aN essay about Death penalty Using The INFORMATIVE , PERSUASIVE & ARGUMENTATIVE ​


Introduction:

The death penalty is a legal process whereby a person is put to death by the state as a punishment for a crime. The use of the death penalty has been a subject of debate for many years. Some people argue that the death penalty is necessary to deter crime and punish heinous crimes. Others argue that it is inhumane and ineffective. This essay will discuss the death penalty from three different perspectives: informative, persuasive, and argumentative.

Informative:

The death penalty is a controversial issue that has been debated for many years. Supporters of the death penalty argue that it is necessary to deter crime and punish heinous crimes. They also argue that it is a just punishment for murderers and other violent criminals. Opponents of the death penalty argue that it is inhumane and ineffective. They also argue that it is racially biased and unfairly targets the poor.

The use of the death penalty varies from country to country. Some countries, such as the United States, China, and Iran, continue to use the death penalty. Other countries, such as Canada, Australia, and most European countries, have abolished the death penalty.

Persuasive:

The death penalty is a necessary punishment for heinous crimes such as murder, rape, and terrorism. It serves as a deterrent to potential criminals and provides closure for the families of the victims. The death penalty is also a just punishment for those who commit these heinous crimes.

Opponents of the death penalty argue that it is inhumane and ineffective. However, there are methods of execution, such as lethal injection, that are humane and painless. Additionally, studies have shown that the death penalty does deter crime, particularly murders.

Another argument against the death penalty is that it unfairly targets the poor and minorities. However, this argument is not supported by the facts. The death penalty is applied equally to all races and socioeconomic groups. In fact, studies have shown that the death penalty is more likely to be applied to those who commit the most heinous crimes, regardless of their race or socioeconomic status.

In conclusion, the death penalty is a necessary punishment for heinous crimes. It serves as a deterrent to potential criminals, provides closure for the families of the victims, and is a just punishment for those who commit these crimes.

Argumentative:

The death penalty is an inhumane and ineffective punishment for heinous crimes. It is a violation of human rights and does not serve as a deterrent to potential criminals. Additionally, the death penalty is racially biased and unfairly targets the poor.

One of the main arguments against the death penalty is that it is inhumane. The use of the death penalty is a violation of the right to life and human dignity. It is also a cruel and unusual punishment that does not fit the crime. The use of the death penalty also carries the risk of executing innocent people.

Another argument against the death penalty is that it is ineffective. Studies have shown that the death penalty does not deter crime, particularly murders. In fact, states that have abolished the death penalty have seen a decrease in the murder rate.

The death penalty is also racially biased and unfairly targets the poor. Studies have shown that the death penalty is more likely to be applied to African Americans and those who cannot afford a good defense. This is a clear violation of the principle of equal justice under the law.

In conclusion, the death penalty is an inhumane and ineffective punishment for heinous crimes. It violates human rights, does not deter crime, and is racially biased. The use of the death penalty should be abolished in all countries.
