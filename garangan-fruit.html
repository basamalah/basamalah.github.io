2. At a fruit stall, ⅛ of the fruits are apples,¼ are oranges,and ⅖ of the remainder are mangoes, if there are 72 mangoes,what is the total number of friuts​


Answer:

There are 247 fruits in the stall..

How did I get the answer?

Given:

1/8 = apples

1/4 = oranges

2/5 of the remainder = mangoes

Amount:

Apples = ?

Orange = ?

Mangoes = 72

Solution:

1/8 (apples) + 1/4 (oranges) + 2/5 (mangoes) + ( 5/5 - 2/5 = 3/5) ( remainder )

Make all of them into similar fractions by finding the LCD

1/8 = 5/40 = apples

1/4 = 10/40 = oranges

2/5 = 16/40 = mangoes

3/5 = 24/ 40

LCD: 40

Then, we will find how many fruits are in 1/40 by doing the following:

72/ 16 = 4.5

4.5 = 1/40

5/40 = apples = 22.5

10/40 = oranges = 45

16/40 = mangoes = 72

24/ 40 = remainder = 108

108 + 72 + 45 + 22.5 = 247.5 or 247 ( no one would eat a half fruit lol )

Let,

M = # of Mangoes

A = # of Apples

Total Fruits = 20

Condition:

The mangoes are 4 times the number of the apples.

M = 4A

A = ?

If there are 20 fruits in all.

M + A = 20

solve for A,

M + A = 20

but M = 4A

4A + A = 20

5A = 20

A = 20/5

A = 4

then solve for M,

M = 4A

M = 4(4)

M = 16

Hence, there are 4 apples and 16 mangoes.
