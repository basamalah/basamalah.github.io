John purchased roofing materials worth P20, 000 from RCV Construction Company owned by Rico and paid the latter a check in the said amount. The following day, Rico deposited the check but it was returned dishonored because it was drawn against a closed account. John failed to make good the said check despite written demands. Atty. Torredes, counsel for Rico, filed two complaints against John with the Office of the Provincial Prosecutor, one for estafa under Article 315 of the Revised Penal Code and another for violation of BP Blg. 22. Atty. Luardo, counsel for John, claimed that if his client was at all liable, he could only be liable for violation of BP 22 and not for estafa under Art. 315 of the RPC because one precludes the other and because BP 22 is more favorable to the accused as it carries a lighter penalty. The investigating prosecutor, on his resolution, stated that only one crime was committed, namely, the complex crime of estafa under Art. 315 of the RPC and another under BP 22. Is the investigating prosecutor, correct? Explain.

7. differentiate what is crime of estafa and crime of malversation​


Answer:

The crimes of estafa and malversation are similar in nature in that both involve misappropriation of funds or property, the difference being that in estafa, the property or funds misappropriated is private in character whereas in malversation, the property constitutes public funds or property for which the accused as a public officer is accountable by reason of the duties of his office. In the case of U.S. vs. Cardel, 23 Phil. 207, and U S. vs. Mesina, 42 Phil. 66, it was held that estafa was consummated in the place where the accused is required to render an accounting and failed to do so. Applying the same ruling in the instant malversation case and Section 14(a), Rule 110 of the Revised Rules of Court hereinbefore quoted, We hold and rule that the present case of malversation may be tried in Manila since the offense charged was consummated in Manila.
