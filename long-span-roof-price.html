1. a prefabricated triangular shaped unit for supporting roof loads over long spans? 1.Truss 2.Shingles 3.Rise 4.Ridge​


Truss is a prefabricated triangular shaped unit for supporting roof loads over long spans.

According to the terms in building construction, this truss is a structure consisting of a collection of branches or trunks that form a solid triangular structure and are connected to each other and support the load on the joints.

Trusses are widely used or found as a framework for the construction of bridges, roofs, and towers.

In general, a two-dimensional truss or mild steel frame consists of 3 structural parts, namely the top chord (the top), the bottom chord (the bottom) and the web (the middle).

 These three structures are built so that the truss stands firm, stable, and does not easily collapse so as not to endanger the owner of the house, and can provide comfort because the changes in shape that occur are not very large.

 

Types

 •Pratt Truss

The first type of truss is the Pratt truss. The Pratt truss has been used for the last two (2) centuries as one of the most effective truss methods.

•Warren Truss

The Warren Truss is another very popular truss structure system and is easily identified by its construction of equilateral triangles.

One of the main advantages of the Warren Truss is its ability to spread the load evenly across a number of different members. However, in some cases, the Warren Truss can spread the load when the structure is subjected to a stretched load (the load becomes distributed).

•K. Truss

The K Truss is a slightly more complicated version or variant of the Pratt Truss. The main difference is that the vertical members have become shorter, which increases their resistance to buckling.

•Howe Truss

Basically, Howe Truss is the opposite of Pratt Truss in terms of geometry. In fact, looking at the Pratt truss upside down will visualize a kind of Howe truss.

The overall structure is still relatively the same, but the diagonal braces now occupy opposite or unoccupied joints. A switch in this diagonal member position has a very structurally important effect.

•Fink Truss

 Fink Truss, the shape has a web member that follows a V pattern that can be repeated several times. When the top chord slopes downward from the middle, the V pattern becomes smaller. 

•Gambrel Truss

 On the outside, the gambrel truss frame has two different slopes, where the slope is steeper from the middle.

 Due to its protruding shape, the gambrel truss frame is effectively installed with a hollow center, which can be used as storage.

2. 1. The roof has a slant height of 13 meters and the span is 24 meters. what is the vertical height of the roof?​


Answer:

5 meters

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's call the vertical height of the roof "h".

Using the Pythagorean theorem, we can relate the slant height, span, and vertical height of the roof:

h^2 + (24/2)^2 = 13^2

h^2 + 144 = 169

h^2 = 169 - 144

h^2 = 25

Taking the square root of both sides, we get:

h = 5

Therefore, the vertical height of the roof is 5 meters.

Let: a = vertical height of the roof

c = slant height

b = span

Solution:

b = 24/2 = 12

c² = a² + b²

(13)² = a² + (12)²

169 = a² + 144

a² = 169 - 144

a² = 25

√a² = √25

a = 5

The vertical height of the roof is 5 meters
