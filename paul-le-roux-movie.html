3. Movie Review Introduction on the movie "Les Miserables"


Answer:

Les Misérables is a 2012 musical film based on the 1862 French novel of the same name by Victor Hugo. The film tells the story of Jean Valjean, a French peasant, and his quest for redemption after serving nineteen years in jail for stealing a loaf of bread to feed his sister's starving child. It stars Hugh Jackman, Anne Hathaway, Russell Crowe, Amanda Seyfried, Eddie Redmayne, and Helena Bonham Carter. The movie was nominated for eight Academy Awards and won three, including Best Supporting Actress for Anne Hathaway. It is a beautiful, passionate story of love and sacrifice that is sure to be remembered as an all-time great.

Answer:

"Les Misérables" is a musical film adaptation of the popular stage musical of the same name, which is based on the classic novel by Victor Hugo. The movie was released in 2012 and directed by Tom Hooper. The film features an all-star cast, including Hugh Jackman as Jean Valjean, Russell Crowe as Javert, Anne Hathaway as Fantine, Amanda Seyfried as Cosette, and Eddie Redmayne as Marius.

Plot Summary

The movie is set in 19th century France and follows the story of ex-convict Jean Valjean as he tries to start a new life after serving 19 years in prison. He is being pursued by the ruthless inspector Javert, who is determined to bring him back to prison. Along the way, Valjean encounters the struggling single mother, Fantine, and takes care of her daughter, Cosette. Valjean raises Cosette as his own and eventually becomes embroiled in the Paris student revolution of 1832.

Review

"Les Miserables" is a powerful and emotional film that brings the classic story to life in a truly magnificent way. The musical numbers are beautifully sung, especially Anne Hathaway's powerful performance of "I Dreamed a Dream". The film also boasts excellent cinematography and a strong score. The cast delivers strong performances, especially Hugh Jackman, who brings a great deal of emotion and depth to the role of Jean Valjean.

In conclusion, "Les Miserables" is a touching and memorable film that will appeal to fans of musicals and the stage production. The film is a powerful reminder of the timeless themes of love, sacrifice, and redemption, and is a must-see for anyone who loves musicals or the original novel.

The film's depiction of 19th-century France is both grandiose and gritty, capturing the poverty, desperation, and social upheaval of the time. Jackman's performance as Valjean is powerful, conveying the character's internal struggle and growth over the course of several decades. Crowe's portrayal of Javert is less successful, but the supporting cast, including Anne Hathaway as the tragic Fantine and Amanda Seyfried as her daughter, Cosette, more than make up for it.

30. Explain the different stages of roux 1. White roux 2. Blond roux 3. Brown roux


Answer:

White Roux- A white roux retains its initial color and is only cooked slightly to remove any starchiness from the roux. Blond Roux- A blonde roux is caramelized slightly to give it a darker blonde color. Brown Roux– A brown roux is cooked until almost burnt and highly caramelized, it also has a nice nutty flavor to it.
