1. Why is it important to know the range of research topics in the area of inquiry?​


Through inquiry, students engage in research around interesting ideas and essential questions. Questioning, critical thinking, and the creative development of new knowledge through inquiry are as important (if not more so) to learning as information finding through research.

Inquiry is a behavior involved in human efforts to rationally explain phenomena that provoke curiosity. In other words, inquiry is related to active activities and skills that focus on seeking knowledge or understanding to satisfy curiosity.

The Inquiry Method allows students to find the information needed to achieve their learning goals, because the Inquiry Method involves students in mental processes to discover a concept based on the information provided by the teacher.

The inquiry method supports the development of scientific literacy and understanding of scientific processes, vocabulary knowledge and understanding of concepts, critical thinking, and a positive attitude. It can be said that the inquiry method not only increases students' understanding of concepts in science, but also forms a scientific attitude in students.

Thus the purpose of applying the inquiry method in research makes students able to a process of how knowledge can be created. To achieve this goal students are faced with a problem that is not yet known, but interesting. But it must be based on an idea that can be found.

29. Give mixed method topic in inquiries pls huhu research design yan​


Answer:

Topic: Understanding the Effectiveness of Online Learning During the COVID-19 Pandemic: A Mixed Methods Study

Research Question: How has the shift to online learning during the COVID-19 pandemic affected the academic performance and mental health of university students, and what factors contribute to their experiences?

Methodology:

1.Quantitative Phase: A survey will be conducted to collect data on the academic performance and mental health of university students who have experienced the shift to online learning during the COVID-19 pandemic. The survey will include standardized measures of academic achievement, such as GPA, and validated measures of mental health, such as the Depression Anxiety and Stress Scale (DASS).

2.Qualitative Phase: Follow-up interviews will be conducted with a sub-sample of survey participants to gain a deeper understanding of their experiences with online learning during the pandemic. The interviews will be semi-structured and will explore factors such as technological barriers, social isolation, and academic support.

3.Data Integration: The quantitative and qualitative data will be integrated through triangulation and complementarity methods to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the research question.

Expected Outcome: The mixed-methods approach will enable a holistic understanding of the effectiveness of online learning during the COVID-19 pandemic, and provide insights into factors that contribute to students' experiences. The findings of the study can inform future educational policies and practices, particularly in terms of ensuring the academic success and mental well-being of students during times of crisis.
