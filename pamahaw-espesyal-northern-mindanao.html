15. What is the 5 greetings in Northern Mindanao?​


Answer:

Northern Mindanao, also known as Region X, is composed of 5 provinces and 2 cities in the Philippines. The region has a diverse mix of cultures and languages, with several ethnic groups residing in the area. Here are five common greetings in Northern Mindanao:

"Maayong buntag" - This means "Good morning" in Cebuano, which is widely spoken in the region.

"Adlaw nga maayong" - This means "Good day" in Cebuano and can be used any time of the day.

"Kumusta ka?" - This means "How are you?" in Filipino and is a common greeting throughout the Philippines.

"Marajaw na adlaw" - This means "Have a good day" in Higaonon, an indigenous language spoken in some parts of Northern Mindanao.

"Salam" - This means "Peace" in Maranao, another indigenous language spoken in the region.

These greetings are just a few examples of the many languages and dialects spoken in Northern Mindanao. By using these greetings, visitors to the region can show respect and appreciation for the diverse cultures and traditions of the area.
