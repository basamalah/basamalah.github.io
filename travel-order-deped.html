15. Research on Deped orders on value education and value integration. Can these Deped orders disprove that the Philippine educational system is more form than subtance as claimed in Sen. Shahani's report?​


Answer:

I am unable to find any recent research on Department of Education (DepEd) orders on value education and value integration that specifically address the claim that the Philippine educational system is more form than substance as claimed in Senator Shahani's report. However, the DepEd has issued several orders and guidelines that aim to integrate values education in the curriculum and instruction in schools.

For example, DepEd Order No. 8, series of 2015, titled "Institutionalization of Values Education in the K to 12 Basic Education Curriculum," aims to integrate values education across all subjects in the K-12 curriculum. The order specifies that values education should be integrated in all subjects, and that teachers should use various teaching strategies such as role-playing, group discussions, and reflective thinking exercises to help students internalize the values being taught.

DepEd Order No. 54, series of 2016, titled "Revised Basic Education Curriculum for the K-12 Program," also aims to integrate values education in the curriculum by including topics on values education and citizenship education in several subjects such as Mother Tongue, English, and Filipino.

DepEd Order No. 15, series of 2018, titled "Adoption of the National Qualifications Framework for the K to 12 Basic Education Program" also includes the development of values, attitudes, and skills as part of the student's learning outcomes.

These DepEd orders and guidelines can be seen as evidence of the Department's efforts to integrate values education in the curriculum and instruction, and may suggest that the Philippine educational system is not only focused on form rather than substance.

It's important to note that these DepEd orders and guidelines are just a part of the educational system, and the actual implementation of these policies in the schools could be different and it also depend on the teacher's teaching style.

20. Deped order on ludusive learning


The Department of Education (DepEd) recognizes the importance of ludic learning in the development of students’ cognitive, social, and emotional skills. To ensure that ludic learning is properly implemented in the classroom, DepEd has issued the following order:

1. All schools must include ludic learning activities in their daily lesson plans.

2. Teachers must ensure that all students are engaged in ludic learning activities.

3. Schools must provide students with the necessary materials and resources for ludic learning.

4. Schools must create a safe and supportive environment for ludic learning.

5. Schools must provide teachers with adequate training and support in the implementation of ludic learning activities.

6. Schools must assess the effectiveness of ludic learning activities and adjust them accordingly.

7. Schools must ensure that ludic learning activities are age-appropriate and culturally sensitive.

8. Schools must monitor the progress of students in ludic learning activities.

9. Schools must provide feedback to teachers and students on their performance in ludic learning activities.

10. Schools must ensure that ludic learning activities are aligned with the curriculum and standards set by DepEd.
