10. Why do we need to swab test? ​


Answer:

Testing for COVID-19 involves inserting a 6-inch long swab (like a long Q-tip) into the cavity between the nose and mouth (nasopharyngeal swab) for 15 seconds and rotating it several times. The swabbing is then repeated on the other side of the nose to make sure enough material is collected. The swab is then inserted into a container and sent to a lab for testing.

18. what is the difference bet swab test and rapid test​


Answer:

PCR (Swab) test – throat/nose swab tests for presence of the actual virus. Usually accurate when you first start developing symptoms but this is dependent on taking the swab correctly.

Rapid (Serology) test – blood test which looking at immune response specifically to the virus. Looks for both short term (IgM) and long-term (IgG) antibodies.
