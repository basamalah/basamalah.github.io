How does aHUS affect different levels of organization?

Explanation:

aHUS (atypical hemolytic uremic syndrome) is a rare and potentially life-threatening disorder that affects multiple levels of organization in the body. It is characterized by abnormal blood clotting that can lead to damage in the blood vessels of vital organs, such as the kidneys, heart, and brain.

At the cellular level, aHUS is caused by a deficiency or dysfunction of the proteins that regulate blood clotting, known as complement proteins. This leads to excessive activation of the complement system and inflammation, which can damage blood vessels and cause clots to form.

At the organ level, aHUS can affect several organs, particularly the kidneys, which play a key role in filtering waste products from the blood. Damage to the kidneys can lead to kidney failure, which can be life-threatening if not treated promptly. aHUS can also affect other organs, such as the heart, brain, and lungs, which can lead to serious complications.

At the individual level, aHUS can have significant impacts on a person's physical health and well-being. It can cause symptoms such as fatigue, shortness of breath, and abdominal pain, and can lead to long-term health problems such as kidney damage and high blood pressure. It can also have a significant impact on a person's emotional and mental health, as managing a chronic illness can be stressful and overwhelming.

At the societal level, aHUS is a rare disorder that affects a small number of people. However, the costs associated with treating aHUS and managing its complications can be significant and may place a burden on the healthcare system.

Answer:

1. False (it is not quadrilateral; quadrilateral is a shapes that has four sides, like square, rhombus, parallelogram, trapezoid, rectangle, etc.)

2. True (When a triangle has three congruent sides, we call the triangle an equilateral triangle. The angles in an equilateral triangle are always 60°. When a triangle has two congruent sides it is called an isosceles triangle. The angles opposite to the two sides of the same length are congruent.)

3. False (An obtuse triangle (or obtuse-angled triangle) is a triangle with one obtuse angle (greater than 90°) and two acute angles. Since a triangle's angles must sum to 180° in Euclidean geometry, no Euclidean triangle can have more than one obtuse angle.)

4. True (A right triangle is a triangle in which one angle is a right angle. The relation between the sides and angles of a right triangle is the basis for trigonometry. The side opposite the right angle is called the hypotenuse)

5. True (Scalene. A scalene triangle has three different angles and none of its sides are equal in length.)
