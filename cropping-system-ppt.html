5. What are the different systems in planting fruit bearing crops​


There are seven different system of planting fruit bearing crops and are as follows:

1. Square System: it is the simplest way of planting crops. The land or plot is divided into a square and a tree or crop is planted in each corner of the square

2. Rectangular System: The plot of land is divided into a rectangle. The trees are planted in straight rows in the corners

3. Triangle System: The plot is divided into a triangle and a tree is planted in each corner of each angle

4. Hexagonal System: A tree is planted at each corner of an equilateral triangle. Six trees form the hexagon and the seventh tree is in the middle

5. Quincunx System: This is a modified form of the square system, there is a tree planted in the middle but only serves as a filler tree or a temporary tree and will be removed when the 4 other trees are mature

6. Contour System: This is usually done in hills and following an imaginary line along the hill or slope. This prevents soil erosion

7. Freestyle system: This is planting with no geometrical pattern or system
