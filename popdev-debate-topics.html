6. 1 debatable topic about employment essay


Answer:

One debatable topic about employment is whether or not the government should provide a guaranteed basic income for all citizens. Proponents of this idea argue that a basic income would provide a safety net for those who are unable to find work or who are unable to earn enough to support themselves and their families. It would also provide a financial cushion for those who are facing unforeseen expenses or who are struggling to make ends meet.

Opponents of a guaranteed basic income argue that it would be too expensive and would create a disincentive for people to work. They argue that providing a basic income would reduce the incentive for people to seek employment, and it could lead to higher levels of unemployment and economic stagnation.

Overall, the idea of a guaranteed basic income is a complex and controversial topic, with valid arguments on both sides. While some believe that it would provide important support for those in need, others argue that it would be too costly and would undermine the principles of work and personal responsibility.

The Philippines is a country with a wide variety of issues, ranging from economic and social issues to political topics. One of the most controversial topics in the Philippines today is the issue of extrajudicial killings. Extrajudicial killings are extrajudicial executions of individuals suspected of a crime, typically carried out by the police or other law enforcement agencies without the approval of the courts. While the Philippine government claims that extrajudicial killings are necessary to combat crime, many human rights activists, lawyers, and citizens argue that this type of policy violates basic human rights and should be abolished.

The most commonly cited justification for extrajudicial killings is that they are necessary to combat crime. The Philippine government has argued that crime rates have fallen significantly since President Duterte took office and that extrajudicial killings have played a large part in this reduction. However, human rights activists have countered this argument by pointing to the numerous cases of innocent people being killed by the police without due process. They have also argued that the root cause of crime in the Philippines is poverty, and that extrajudicial killings do not solve the underlying problems that lead to crime.

In addition, human rights activists argue that extrajudicial killings violate basic human rights, such as the right to due process of law and the right to a fair trial. They point to numerous cases of individuals being killed by the police without any chance to defend themselves in a court of law, or even knowing the charges against them. Furthermore, activists argue that extrajudicial killings are often carried out in a way that is excessively violent and gruesome.

Ultimately, the debate over extrajudicial killings in the Philippines is a complex one. On one hand, the Philippine government claims that extrajudicial killings are necessary to combat crime and keep the country safe. On the other hand, many human rights activists and citizens argue that extrajudicial killings violate basic human rights and should be abolished. It is up to the Philippine government to balance the need to combat crime with the need to protect the rights of its citizens.

28. make an argumentative essay any topic but debatable​


Topic: The Internet Should Be Regulated

In the present day, the internet has become an integral part of our lives. From work to entertainment, nearly all aspects of our lives can be accessed and completed through the internet. Despite its many benefits, the internet has also become a dangerous place, with numerous threats to people’s safety and privacy. To protect users from these dangers, it is necessary for the government to regulate the internet.

One of the primary reasons for why the internet should be regulated is to protect people from cyber-crimes. Cyber-crimes have become increasingly common in recent years, with criminals using the internet to commit a wide range of offenses, from identity theft to hacking into bank accounts. By regulating the internet, the government can make it more difficult for criminals to use the internet for malicious purposes, thus protecting citizens from cyber-crimes.

Another reason why the internet should be regulated is to protect users from online scams and malicious websites. The internet is full of scams and malicious websites that seek to take advantage of unsuspecting users. By regulating the internet, the government can take steps to prevent these scams and websites from operating, thus protecting users from financial losses and other negative consequences.

Finally, the internet should be regulated to protect users’ privacy. With the rise of social media and other online services, users are increasingly sharing their personal information online. This personal information can be easily accessed by hackers and other malicious actors, who can then use it for various nefarious purposes. By regulating the internet, the government can take steps to ensure that users’ personal information is kept safe and secure.

In conclusion, the internet should be regulated by the government in order to protect users from cyber-crimes, online scams, and the loss of their privacy. While some may argue that such regulation is an infringement on freedom, the protection of citizens’ safety and privacy is more important than the right to unrestricted access to the internet.
