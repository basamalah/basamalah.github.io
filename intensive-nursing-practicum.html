17. what are the requirements of the confidentiality of the identify of community members about your field practicum ​


Answer:

As an student from UPD, I don't have personal experience with field practicum, but here are the general requirements of confidentiality in social work field practicum:

1. Informed Consent: you should obtain written consent from community members before collecting, using or disclosing their personal information.

2. Anonymity: protect any identifying information of individuals by using pseudonyms, code numbers or other measures.

3. Shared Information: only share confidential information with those who are required to know in order to provide services, and only share the minimum necessary information.

4. Security: use appropriate technology and physical security measures to prevent unauthorized disclosure or intentional or accidental breaches of confidentiality.

5. Professionalism: follow ethical and legal requirements for confidentiality, understand agency policies, use only the approved forms of communication, and respect the privacy of clients.

It is essential to maintain confidentiality to build trust with community members, to avoid legal and ethical liability and to ensure quality service delivery.


18. What i can recommend for the improvement of the practicum program ojt?


On-the-Job Training Program or OJT is curriculum required by schools and universities from students to prepare them into entering the business world once they have graduated. It is an opportunity offered by companies to train students that have a potential on being a new employee in the future. This way, the company will be saved from the hassle on finding and training a new employee. But not all training programs have an effective way of training the students but here is the list of recommendations to improve the OJT practicum:  

1. The school must have a list of involvements required to be experienced by the intern like hands-on usage of ERP system, training with proper answering of phone calls, assisting in organizing an event, etc. This way, the student will have an interactive experience with the company and this will give them an idea of what the work really is about.  

2. The intern must have an hourly journal booklet to monitor the things he or she did for the whole day and this booklet must be submitted to the facilitator at the end of each day for comments and evaluation.  

3. The company conducting the on-the-job training must circulate the OJTs into the different departments of the company depending on the course taken by the student. This way, the OJT will have a first hand experience on the different functions of each department and they will have more idea how a company work together to function well.

23. What is your opinion about the Islamic Counseling Practicum?​


Answer:

Counseling practicum is a vital part of counselor education through experiential learning where trainees learn how to

reflect and analyze their concrete experience gained in practicum to form and expand their configuration of meaning

which they could further validate through action and experimentation (Kolb, 1984). The main aim of this process of

experiential learning is to transform thinking and action of themselves as counselor so that they could effectively affect

thinking and action of their clients. In other words counseling practicum gives trainee counselors an opportunity for

change and growth personally and professionally. Education and Technical Training Committee and Board of Counselor

Malaysia (2003) define counseling practicum as a supervised clinical experience of trainee counselors for the purpose of

building and expanding their basic counseling skills and integrating their theoretical professional knowledge into practice.

Counseling practicum provides the opportunity to evaluate trainees’ ability to apply knowledge and skills acquired from

course works with clients at the school setting. During the counseling practicum, trainees will experience applying

appropriate theoretical knowledge to appropriate cases; practicing their counseling skills; building rapport with clients and

other stakeholders; negotiating their own perception towards others and others’ perceptions towards them; dealing with

their own psychological needs and challenges in the process of fulfilling expectation of others and their own selves.
