20. a pool is filled with 270 cubic meters of water .the base of the pool is 15m long and 9m wide. what is the height of the water in the pool?


Answer: Height = 2m

Step-by-step explanation:

To solve for the cubic meter, we must multiply the length (L), width (W), and height (H).

Cubic meter (m³) = L x W x H

Since we already have the length and width, we can divide those from the given cubic meters in order to get the height.

15m (L) x 9m (W) x __ (H) = 270m³135m² x __ (H) = 270m³H = [tex]\frac{270}{135}[/tex]H = 2m

23. the length of a rectangular pool is 1m less than twice width, the area of the rectangular pool is 45m2 find the length and the width of the pool


✏️RECTANGLE===============================

Problem: The length of a rectangular pool is 1m less than twice width, the area of the rectangular pool is 45m², find the length and the width of the pool.

Solution: Represent l and w as the length and width of the rectangular pool respectively. Make equations of the given statement.

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w - 1& \green{(eq. \: 1)} \\ 45 = l \cdot w & \green{(eq. \: 2)}\end{cases}[/tex]

- Substitute l from the first equation to the second equation in terms of w.

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w - 1 \\ 45 = (2w - 1) \cdot w\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w - 1 \\ 45 = {2w}^{2} - w \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w - 1 \\2 {w}^{2} - w - 45 = 0 \end{cases}[/tex]

- Solve the quadratic equation from the second equation by using the quadratic formula. Make sure that gives the positive solution.

[tex] \begin{gathered}w = \frac{ \text - ( \text - 1 )+ \sqrt{ {1}^{2} - 4(2)( \text - 45) } }{2(2)} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered}w = \frac{1+ \sqrt{1- 4(2)( \text - 45) } }{2(2)} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered}w = \frac{1+ \sqrt{1- 4 (\text -90) } }{4} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered}w = \frac{1+ \sqrt{1- (\text -360) } }{4} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered}w = \frac{1+ \sqrt{1 + 360} }{4} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered}w = \frac{1+ \sqrt{ 361} }{4} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered}w = \frac{1+ 19 }{4} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered}w = \frac{20 }{4} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]w = 5[/tex]

- The width of the rectangular pool is 5 meters, Substitute it to the first equation to find the length of the rectangular pool.

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2(5) - 1 \\ w = 5\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 10 - 1 \\ w = 5\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 9 \\ w = 5\end{cases}[/tex]

- Therefore, the length of the rectangular pool is 9 meters, while the width of the rectangular pool is 5 meters.
