2. Viscosity is a measure of a liquid's resistance to flow. It is the measure of a liquid's internal friction. The more viscous a liquid is, the more resistant it is to flowing and the thicker it is.

3. The viscosity of a liquid has a direct relationship with the velocity of an object as it moves in the same liquid. The higher the viscosity of a liquid, the slower an object will move in it, and vice versa. This is because a more viscous liquid creates more friction and resistance to flow, which slows down the velocity of the object moving through it.
