The magnitude of the impulse of the parachutist on the snow is 3,025,200 kg·m/s

Explanation:

Here, we are going to use the equation of Impulse which is also equivalent to the change in momentum.

Impulse:

I = F· Δt

where

F     is the force

Δt   is the change in time

Change in momentum:

Δp = m(vf - vi)

where

Δp     is the momentum

m       is the mass

vi, vf       is the initial and final velocity respectively

Now, the impulse is equal to the change in momentum (I = Δp), therefore,

F· Δt = m(vf - vi)

Here, we can solve first for Δt.

Solving the problem

Let us use the given equation

F· Δt = m(vf - vi)

Then, substitute the given data

1.2 x 10⁵ N · Δt = (85 kg)(56 m/s)

Divide both sides by (85 kg)(56 m/s), therefore

Δt = 25.21 seconds

Now, we can solve for the impulse by using the formula

I = F· Δt

I = (1.2 x 10⁵ N)(25.21 s)

I = 3,025,200 kg·m/s

Therefore, the impulse of the parachutist on the snow is 3,025,200 kg·m/s
