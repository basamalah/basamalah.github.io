2. explain prospective application of administrative rules and regulations​


Answer:

As a general rule, laws shall have only a prospective effect and must not be applied retroactively in such a way as to apply to pending disputes and cases. This is expressed in the familiar legal maxim lex prospicit, non respicit (the law looks forward and not backward), and is conformable to Article 4 of the Civil Code. The rule is intended to the tendency of retroactive legislation to be unjust and oppressive on account of its liability to punish individuals for violations of laws not yet enacted, unsettle vested rights or disturb the legal effect of prior transactions, which is unconstitutional.

Explanation:

The principle of non-retroactivity finds application in various aspects of the legal system. Thus, the rule is that the jurisdiction of a court depends on the law existing at the time an action is filed; a statute continues to be in force with regard to all rights that had accrued prior to its amendment, a new doctrine laid down by the Supreme Court overturning an existing doctrine is to be applied prospectively, and not to parties relying on the old doctrine and acting on the faith thereof; and many others.

9. Discuss what is Prospecting


Answer:

Prospecting is the first stage of the geological analysis (followed by exploration) of a territory. It is the search for minerals, fossils, precious metals, or mineral specimens. It is also known as fossicking.

Traditionally prospecting relied on direct observation of mineralization in rock outcrops or in sediments. Modern prospecting also includes the use of geologic, geophysical, and geochemical tools to search for anomalies which can narrow the search area. Once an anomaly has been identified and interpreted to be a potential prospect direct observation can then be focused on this area.[1]

In some areas a prospector must also make claims, meaning they must erect posts with the appropriate placards on all four corners of a desired land they wish to prospect and register this claim before they may take samples. In other areas publicly held lands are open to prospecting without staking a mining claim.

25. explain the three characteristics of criminal law using your own wordgeneral, territorial and prospect​


Answer:

Criminal law has three main characteristics: generality, territoriality, and prospectivity.

Generality refers to the fact that criminal laws apply to all members of society, regardless of their individual characteristics or circumstances. This means that criminal laws are not limited to specific groups of people or specific types of conduct.

Territoriality refers to the fact that criminal laws apply within a specific geographic area, such as a country or state. This means that criminal laws are not enforceable outside of the jurisdiction in which they were created.

Prospectivity refers to the fact that criminal laws only apply to conduct that takes place after the law has been enacted. This means that criminal laws cannot be retroactively applied to conduct that took place before the law was enacted.

In summary, criminal laws are general in that they apply to all individuals, territorial in that they only apply within a specific jurisdiction, and prospective in that they only apply to conduct that takes place after the law is enacted.
