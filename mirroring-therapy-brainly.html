26. was it easy or difficult?why when you doing an activity mirroring therapy​


Mirroring Therapy

Mirror therapy is a type of motor imagery in which the patient moves his unaffected limb while watching the movement in a mirror; this sends a visual stimulus to the brain, causing the affected limb to move. Mirror therapy has already been shown to have some effects on the brain.

Mirror therapy is a type of therapy that is used to strengthen arms and hands that have been weakened by a stroke. We use mirror therapy to "trick our brain" into thinking the weaker arm is moving by moving the stronger hand and arm.

Is Mirroring Therapy Easy or Difficult?

Mirror therapy is actually quite simple to perform at home, and many people find it a fun way to supplement their hand and arm therapy.

How long does it take for mirror therapy to work?

A typical mirror therapy session should last about 30 minutes, but patients can divide this time into three 10-minute sessions or two 15-minute sessions if they prefer.

How frequently should you use mirror therapy?

Mirror Therapy should be done three to five times per day. At first, you may only be able to see the mirrored hand's image and possibly make small movements. Try to make larger and smoother movements with both arms over time.

When does mirror therapy come into play?

Mirror therapy is used to help people regain motor function after a stroke. A mirror is placed in the person's midsagittal plane during mirror therapy, reflecting movements of the non-paretic side as if it were the affected side.
