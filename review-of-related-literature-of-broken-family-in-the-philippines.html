6. Explain each expression the way you understood them in relation to research. 1. Review Related Literature 2. Traditional review of Literature 3. Systematic review of Literature​


Answer:

1. Review Related Literature: This expression refers to the process of examining existing literature or scholarly works that are relevant or related to a specific research topic or question. The review may involve analyzing and synthesizing various sources of information, such as academic journals, books, reports, and online resources, to identify key themes, gaps, and debates in the literature. The goal of reviewing related literature is to establish a strong theoretical foundation for the research, to identify research questions or hypotheses, and to situate the research within a broader scholarly context.

2. Traditional review of Literature: This expression refers to a type of literature review that is based on a non-systematic or non-structured approach to collecting and analyzing literature. In a traditional review of literature, the researcher may conduct a broad search of relevant literature using general search terms, and then use their own judgment to select and interpret the sources. The traditional review may be subjective and prone to bias, as the researcher's personal perspectives and experiences may influence their selection and interpretation of the literature.

3. Systematic review of Literature: This expression refers to a rigorous and structured approach to reviewing literature that involves a systematic search, selection, and analysis of relevant literature according to specific criteria and methods. A systematic review of literature aims to minimize bias and enhance the reliability and validity of the review by using explicit and transparent methods to identify, assess, and synthesize the available evidence. Systematic reviews may involve the use of statistical methods, meta-analysis, or other advanced analytical techniques to synthesize the findings of multiple studies and to provide a comprehensive overview of the research on a specific topic.

17. synthesized review of related literature​


A synthesized review of related literature is a comprehensive and critical evaluation of existing research studies and literature that are relevant to a specific topic or research question. It involves analyzing, summarizing, and synthesizing information from various sources, such as academic journals, books, and online databases, to provide a coherent and well-supported understanding of a particular phenomenon or area of inquiry.

In a synthesized review of related literature, the researcher identifies the key themes, theories, concepts, and findings from the existing studies and integrates them into a cohesive narrative that answers the research question or supports a particular argument. This type of literature review requires a systematic and rigorous approach to identify relevant literature, extract and analyze data, and present the findings in a clear and organized manner.

Overall, a synthesized review of related literature provides a critical synthesis of the existing knowledge and gaps in the field, which can inform future research and guide practical applications.

Here are some examples of synthesized reviews of related literature:

1. A synthesized review of related literature on the effectiveness of cognitive-behavioral therapy for depression in adolescents. This review would analyze and synthesize studies that have investigated the efficacy of cognitive-behavioral therapy for depression in adolescents, including the key components of the therapy, its effectiveness in reducing symptoms, and the long-term outcomes.

2. A synthesized review of related literature on the impact of social media on mental health. This review would examine and synthesize studies that have investigated the relationship between social media use and mental health, including the potential positive and negative effects, the mechanisms by which social media affects mental health, and the demographic factors that may influence this relationship.

3. A synthesized review of related literature on the benefits and drawbacks of remote work. This review would analyze and synthesize studies that have investigated the impact of remote work on employee productivity, job satisfaction, work-life balance, and overall well-being, as well as the challenges and drawbacks associated with remote work.

25. what's is the similarities and differences of review of related studies and review of related literature ​


Answer:

Answer: You asked what the similarities of reviews of related literature and reviews of related studies are.

In terms of related literature, you are reviewing literature that other researchers will also use to support their studies in the same field of research as you are doing, but they may be researching on a different research topic from yours.

For instance, other researchers’ study topic may be to find out how service providers may best deliver quality hotel service to international tourists from Europe, while you may be researching on a topic relating to how best to deliver quality service to guests in budget hotels.

Clearly, since, the research in both cases relate to the delivery of quality service, although one set may be for guests in International hotels, and another set, looking at quality service delivery for guests in Budget hotels. Therefore, in this example, many of the literature which both research groups will be reviewing, will be the same literature - that is, those literature related to the field of service management (Related Literature).

However, note that other researchers will be looking at literature more specifically in quality service management for European guests in international hotels, while you are looking at guests at Budget hotels, so clearly, some of the later literature reviewed by you will be associated only from studies relating to Budget hotels (closely related studies - budget hotels).

You will ignore literature on studies of quality service delivery at international hotels (Why? It is because this set of literature does not inform you as well on findings pertaining to international hotels).

The other researchers will review literature closely associated with international hotels catering for guests from Europe, and will ignore literature associated with quality service delivery for guests Budget hotels (Why? It is because, the set of literature on Budget hotel studies does not inform you on findings pertaining to international hotels).
