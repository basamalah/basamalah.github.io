3. Solve the 3 following problems using the formula of Newton’s second law of motion CORRECT ANSWER = BRAINLIEST WRONG ANSWER = REPORT


SOLUTION:

Newton's Second Law of Motion refers to a constant force in which acts on a body, it causes it to accelerate. It changes its velocity, at a constant rate. In other words, when a force applied on an object at rest, it causes to accelerate in a direction of the force.

In order to solve the following problems, simply use Newton's Second Law formula below:

[tex] \boxed{\bold{F = ma}} [/tex]

Always remember that the [tex] F [/tex] stands for Force (N) , the [tex] m [/tex] stands for mass (kg) , and the [tex] a [/tex] stands for acceleration (m/s²).

Using GAFSA method.

For item #1:

Given:

[tex] m [/tex] = 2kg[tex] a [/tex] = 6 m/s²

Asked:

Amount of unbalanced force

Formula:

[tex] \boxed{\bold{F = ma}} [/tex]

Solution:

[tex] \rm{F = ma = (2)(6) = 12N} [/tex]

Answer:

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The amount of unbalanced force is 12N.

[tex]\\\\[/tex]

For item #2:

Given:

[tex] F [/tex] = 20500N[tex] a [/tex] = 4 m/s²

Asked:

Mass of the car

Formula:

(Note: When the situation is finding for mass, we can rewrite the formula as shown below)

[tex] \boxed{\bold{m = \frac{F}{a}}} [/tex]

Solution:

[tex] \rm{ m = \frac{F}{a} = \frac{20,500}{4} = 5,125kg} [/tex]

Answer:

Hence, the mass of the car is 5,125kg

[tex]\\\\[/tex]

For item #3:

Given:

[tex] F [/tex] = 350N [tex] m [/tex] = 70kg

Asked:

Rate of the swimmer accelerates to the wall.

Formula:

(Note: When solving for acceleration, you may use the formula shown below)

[tex] \boxed{\bold{ a = \frac{F}{m}}} [/tex]

Solution:

[tex] \rm{a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{350}{70} = 5 \ m/s^2} [/tex]

Answer:

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The rate of the swimmer accelerates to the wall is 5 m/s²

26. the second Newton's law of motion is called "Law of _______".​


Answer:

law of acceleration

Explanation:

Newton’s second law of motion, also known as the law of acceleration, states that the magnitude of the force acting on an object is equal to the product of the mass of that particular object and the acceleration with which it moves.

Newton’s second law of motion pertains to the behaviour of objects for which all existing forces are not balanced. The second law informs us that the acceleration of an object depends on two variables – the net force acting on the body and the mass of the body.

The acceleration of an object depends directly upon the net force acting upon the object, and inversely upon the mass of the object. As the force acting upon an object is increased, the acceleration of the object is increased. As the mass of an object is increased, the acceleration of the object is decreased.
