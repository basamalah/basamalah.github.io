2. the sun and the moon filipino folktale


Answer:

The Sun and the Moon were married, but the Sun was very ugly and quarrelsome. One day he became angry at the Moon and started to chase her. She ran very fast until she was some distance ahead of him, when she grew tired and he almost caught her. Ever since he has been chasing her, at times almost reaching her, and again falling far behind.

The first child of the Sun and Moon was a large star, and he was like a man. One time the Sun, becoming angry at the star, cut him up into small pieces and scattered him over the whole sky just as a woman scatters rice, and ever since there have been many stars.

Another child of the Sun and Moon was a gigantic crab. He still lives and is so powerful that every time he opens and closes his eyes there is a flash of lightning. Most of the time the crab lives in a large hole in the bottom of the sea, and when he is there we have high tide; but when he leaves the hole, the waters rush in and there is low tide. His moving about also causes great waves on the surface of the sea.

The crab is quarrelsome like his father; and he sometimes becomes so angry with his mother, the Moon, that he tries to swallow her. When the people on earth, who are fond of the Moon, see the crab near her, they run out of doors and shout and beat on gongs until he is frightened away, and thus the Moon is saved.

5. A major event that happened in the sun and the moon story? (tingguian folktale ver)


Answer:

One major event in the Sun and the Moon story is when the Sun and the Moon had a disagreement about who was more important. The Sun claimed that it was more significant because it provided warmth and light to the earth, while the Moon argued that it was crucial because it controlled the tides and provided light during the night.

The argument escalated and they ultimately decided to switch places for a day to prove who was more important. However, when the Moon took the Sun's place, it shone too brightly and caused the crops and flowers to wither. Meanwhile, when the Sun took the Moon's place, it didn't provide enough light for people to navigate at night.

Realizing their mistake, the Sun and the Moon quickly switched back and apologized to each other. They then agreed that both of them were equally important in their own ways and decided to work together to ensure the earth was always properly illuminated.

27. what is the moral of the story in the sun and the moon story? (tingguian folktale ver)


Answer:

The Tingguian folktale "The Sun and the Moon" teaches us the moral lesson of the importance of cooperation and balance in the world. In the story, the Sun and the Moon were once married, but they could never be together because one had to be in the sky while the other was on the ground. They eventually decided to live apart, but this caused problems on Earth, as there was never a time of complete darkness or light. The animals and plants were confused and could not thrive.

The moral of the story is that the Sun and the Moon needed to work together to create balance and harmony on Earth. It teaches us that in our own lives, we need to work together with others to achieve our goals and create a better world. We should not let our differences or individual desires get in the way of cooperation and balance.

30. 1. What is the lesson in the story of the cycle of the sun and the moon?2. What is the conflict in the story of the cycle of the sun and the moon?3. What is the plot of the folktale the cycle of the sun and the moon?


The Cycle of the Sun and Moon

The Lesson of the Story

The lesson of the story is clearly the importance of following or obeying commands (https://brainly.ph/question/109340). In the story, the sun disobeyed her wife Moon. His wife told him not to touch any of their children, the stars, while she was away washing clothes in the spring. Sun did not follow his wife and touches their children. The very hot Sun caused the perish of some of their children. Apart from the lost of some of their children, the Sun's disobedience made Moon feel angry which caused quarrel between the couple. We can infer now how important obeying is. If we obey, we can avoid unwanted incidents (https://brainly.ph/question/1830230).

The Conflict in the Story

The conflict in the story is the Sun's burning of their children because of his hotness. Sun is too hot for their children so Moon forbade Sun to touch their children making the sun to feel so sad.

The Plot of the Story

Once upon a time, the sun and the Moon got married and got so many children who were the stars. The Sun is very ecstatic with his children. However, everytime he hugs or embraces any of his children, he burned them. This leads the wife Moon to decide to forbade Sun from going near their children, Sun got sad about this. One day, Moon asks her husband Sun not to touch any of their children while she will be away. Sun refused to follow and they lost some of their children and the couple ended up quarreling. Sun chases her wife Moon and they have been going ever since.
