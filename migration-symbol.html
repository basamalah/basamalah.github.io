7. internal migration vs international migration (2 differences 1 similarity)


Explanation:

Distance: Internal migration typically involves shorter distances than international migration. People who migrate internally may move within a city, a region, or a country, while people who migrate internationally may move across continents.

Legal status: Internal migration is typically easier to achieve than international migration, as it does not involve crossing national borders and obtaining visas or other legal documents.

Similarity:

Motives: Both internal and international migration can be motivated by a range of factors, such as economic opportunities, social and family connections, and personal preferences.

Migration

Temporary kind of migration is usually dominated by both a and b (C). Immigration to a country for a brief and limited amount of time, usually with a specific goal in mind, is referred to as temporary migration. Temporary migrants are accepted into host countries for the sake of employment, education, travel, business, and exchanges and visits related to religion or culture. The temporary migration related to employment or labor is the main topic of this entry.

The landscape of labor mobility now prominently includes temporary labor migration. Either in the scholarly literature or at the level of policy, there is no precise definition of transitory migration. A worldwide statistical picture is challenging to obtain despite efforts made by various nations to track their own temporary migrants through visa systems. Registration procedures differ from country to country.

Student migration refers to the movement of students who study for a year or more outside of their country of birth or citizenship. Wages and educational quality are the two main factors that influence student migration. It should be emphasized that nations with the best education systems do not necessarily have the highest wages. The internationalization of higher education has grown significantly during the age of globalization and has turned into a business-driven endeavor.

26. Many of the birds (migrate, migrates)  to the west during winter.​


Answer:

The correct verb form in this sentence is "migrate". "Migrate" is a transitive verb that means to move from one place to another, especially seasonally or periodically. In this sentence, it refers to the movement of birds from one location to another during the winter months. "Migrates" is the third person singular form of the verb, but the subject of the sentence, "birds", is plural, so the correct verb form is "migrate".
