Bio Info Board:

History of Cell:

The first discovery of cells can be traced back to the 17th century by Robert Hooke who first observed and named them using a microscope.

Later on, Anton van Leeuwenhoek improved the microscope and observed living cells in various organisms.

The development of cell theory was a gradual process and was finally established in the 19th century by scientists such as Schleiden, Schwann and Virchow.

Parts and Function of a Cell:

Cell membrane: acts as a barrier that controls the movement of substances in and out of the cell.

Nucleus: contains genetic material (DNA) and controls the cell's activities.

Cytoskeleton: provides shape and support to the cell.

Mitochondria: produces energy for the cell.

Ribosomes: manufacture proteins for the cell.

Endoplasmic reticulum and Golgi apparatus: involved in protein and lipid metabolism.

Lysosomes: break down and recycle waste materials in the cell.
