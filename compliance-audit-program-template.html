19. Make an essay on interrelationship of audit, control and compliance in risk management and corporate governancepahelp please.​


Answer:

Introduction

Businesses are faced with different forms of risks originating from internal and external sources. For example, employees may be involved in fraudulent activities, hence affecting the organisation’s financial stability. Secondly, firms’ financial stability may be affected by economic changes such as the occurrence of financial crises (Anand 2007). Such issues may limit an organisation’s ability to achieve its profit and wealth maximisation objectives. In an effort to protect their interest, shareholders delegate the daily operation of their businesses to managers, hence leading to the creation of an agency-relationship between shareholders and managers.

In the course of executing their duties, managers have an obligation to ensure that they act in the best interest of the shareholders (Adeyemi & Olowu 2013). In a bid to attain this goal, managers have to take into account different issues that might affect their role in day-to-day running of an organisation. One of these issues includes the prevailing risks. Cohen and Sayag (2006) argue that managers have an obligation to ensure that such risks are avoided or eliminated where possible. Adopting an effective corporate governance system is one of the strategies that businesses can integrate in order to minimise and eliminate risks (Rand & Chambers 2011).

Adeyemi (2006, p. 52) defines corporate governance as ‘the structures, mechanisms and modalities through which firms goals and objectives are set with regard to the shareholders’ interests’. Subsequently, corporate governance aims at enhancing the shareholders’ value by promoting an organisation’s financial stability. The need to implement corporate governance arises from the existence of separation between managers and shareholders.

27. This involves managing the point of sale activities (reservations, ticketing, hotel and com rental vouchering) and monitoring quality assurance plus the appropriate internal audit procedures to monitor compliance and tracking non-compliance​


The statement refers to the responsibilities of a person or department that is in charge of managing the point of sale activities in a hospitality or travel-related business. The point of sale activities refer to the transactions that take place between the business and the customers, such as reservations, ticketing, hotel and car rental vouchering.

The person or department in charge of managing the point of sale activities is responsible for ensuring that these transactions are carried out efficiently and accurately. This involves monitoring the quality of the services provided, as well as ensuring that the appropriate internal audit procedures are in place to monitor compliance with the business's policies and procedures.

Quality assurance refers to the process of ensuring that the services provided meet or exceed the customers' expectations. This can involve monitoring customer feedback, conducting regular inspections of the facilities and services, and implementing corrective actions when necessary.

Internal audit procedures refer to the processes and procedures that are in place to ensure that the business is complying with its own policies and procedures, as well as with any external regulations or standards. This can involve conducting regular audits of the business's financial and operational processes, as well as monitoring compliance with any relevant laws or regulations.

Tracking non-compliance refers to the process of identifying instances where the business is not complying with its own policies or procedures, or with external regulations or standards. This can involve investigating the root causes of the non-compliance, implementing corrective actions, and monitoring the effectiveness of these actions over time.

Overall, managing the point of sale activities in a hospitality or travel-related business is a crucial responsibility that involves ensuring that the business is providing high-quality services to its customers, while also complying with its own policies and procedures, as well as any external regulations or standards.
