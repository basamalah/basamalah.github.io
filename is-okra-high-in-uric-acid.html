5. _____ includes the nitrogen compounds-urea in mammals and uric acid in birds and other species.


It includes the nitrogen compounds-urea in mammals and uric acid in birds and other species

Nitrogenous Waste

When animals, such as mammals, metabolize ammonia, the end product excreted in urine is rea, a nitrogenous waste material.

Urea is primarily produced by mammals, including humans. They are known as ureotelic animals because they secrete urea as their primary nitrogenous waste product. Urea is crucial in the metabolism of nitrogen-containing compounds by animals. It is the primary nitrogen-containing substance found in mammalian urine. Urea is a colorless, odorless solid that is highly soluble in water and is almost non-toxic. It neither acidic nor alkaline when dissolved in water. It is used by the body in a variety of processes, the most notable of which is nitrogen excretion. As convenient source of nitrogen, urea is widely used in fertilizers. It is also a critical raw material

Nitrogenous wastes in the body produce toxic ammonia, which must be excreted.

Urea is excreted by mammals such as humans, whereas uric acid is excreted by birds, reptiles, and some terrestrial invertebrates.

Uricothelic organisms typically excrete uric acid waste as a white paste or powder.

The conversion of the ammonia to uric acid requires more energy than the conversion of ammonia to urea.

Producing uric acid rather than urea is advantageous because it is less toxic and reduces water loss and subsequent water requirements.

28. Explain the biochemical formulation of uric acid​


Answer:

Uric acid is a heterocyclic compound with the chemical formula C5H4N4O3. It is a product of purine metabolism, which is a type of nucleotide metabolism that occurs in living organisms. Purines are organic compounds that are essential components of nucleic acids, such as DNA and RNA, as well as ATP (adenosine triphosphate), which is the primary source of cellular energy.
