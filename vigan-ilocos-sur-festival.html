2. A non- religious festival in honor of the thanksgiving & celebration of peoples’ weaving industry held around may in vigan, ilocos sur.


Viva Vigan Binatbatan Festival of the Arts

Throughout the first week of May, the Viva Vigan Festival of Arts is held. The Save Vigan Ancestral Homes Association, Inc. (SVAHAI) began it in 1993 with the goal of raising awareness of the historic town's importance and bolstering commitment to conserve and maintain this cultural landmark. The arts festival has been effective in drawing attention to Vigan's ancestral homes for the last sixteen years.

The festival has also been successful in highlighting other facets of Vigan with the aid of national and local organizations, as well as media, arts, and non-governmental sponsors. Its fame has even boosted the entire northern tourism industry by attracting hundreds of curious local and foreign visitors.

The seven-day celebrations of Viva Vigan are significant for both religious and secular reasons. It all begins on May 1st, when the nation observes Labor Day and Vigan honors its own Isabelo de los Reyes, who established the nation's first labor federation. St. Joseph, the patron saint of workers, is also remembered by the catholic faithful on this day. The Binatbatan Festival celebrations, which feature a street dancing competition, come after the first-day memorial.

Binatbatan dancing is related to abel Iloco craft in Vigan. The dance illustrates the process of beating cotton pods with bamboo sticks to separate the cotton fluff known as batbat from its seed. The festival was established in 2002 to highlight this ancient weaving technique that is thought to have existed before the advent of the Spaniards.

3. what is the history of salindeg vigan city ilocos sur


Salindeg Vigan City, Ilocos Sur has a long history that dates back to the Spanish colonial era. The town was founded in 1590 by Juan de Salcedo, a Spanish conquistador who explored the northern part of the Philippines. He named the town after his hometown in Spain, Salcedo de Vigo, which is located in Galicia. The town was initially populated by Spanish settlers and indigenous peoples who lived in the area. The town served as a center of economic activity in the region, and it was a major port of commerce. The town also had a fortress, which was used to protect the town from pirates and invaders. During the American period, the town was transformed into a modern town, with new roads, bridges, and infrastructure. Today, Salindeg Vigan City is a vibrant and progressive city, with a thriving economy and diverse culture.

19. magdalena gamayo is an ilocano master weaver who came from a. vigan ilocos sur b. baluarte ilocos sur c. pinili ilocos norte d. laoag ilocos norte​


Answer:

Magdalena Gamayo

Magdalena Gamayo is an Ilocano master weaver who came from Pinili, Ilocos Norte. Therefore, the correct answer is letter C.

Explanation:

At the young age of 16, Magdalena Gamayo already knew how to weave. She learned the tradition of creating inabel from her aunt, and since learning the craft at a young age, she applied different techniques to create beautiful crafts. The traditional patterns like binakol, inuritan, kusikos, and Sinan-sabong were mastered by her. These traditional patterns can be found in different textiles. Through the years of perfecting her craft, she became known as a master weaver, and her specialty is creating the Sinan-sabong weaving pattern. She is currently one of the National Living Treasures of the Philippines, with the award being conferred on November 8, 2012.
