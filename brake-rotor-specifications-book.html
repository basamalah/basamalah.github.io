21. What is a stator & what is a rotor?


Answer:

A stator is the stationary part of a rotary system, such as an electric motor, alternator or pump. It typically consists of a set of electromagnets arranged around a cylinder, with the poles facing outward. The stator is used to provide a static magnetic field around which the rotor can rotate. It consists of a series of laminations that have been stacked together and wound with copper wire. The stator provides the magnetic field that interacts with the current in the rotor to create motion.

A rotor is the rotating part of a rotary system, such as an electric motor, alternator or pump. It typically consists of a shaft or drum with permanent magnets or electromagnets arranged around its circumference. The rotor is used to convert electrical energy into mechanical energy. It is usually made from magnetic materials such as iron, steel or aluminum and contains a series of windings of insulated copper wire. When an electrical current passes through these windings, a magnetic field is created, which interacts with the stator’s magnetic field to create motion.
