16. Two stores sell the same refrigerator for the same original price. Store A advertises that the refrigerator is on sale for 15% off the original price. Store B advertises that it is reducing the refrigerator’s price by $150. When Stephanie compares the sale prices of the refrigerator in both stores, she concludes that the sale prices are equal.


If you are looking for the original price, here is the solution.

If $150 = 15%, 1% = $10. [150 divided by 15]
If 1% = $10, 100% = ($10)(100) = $1000
The original price of the refrigerator is $1000

But if you are looking for the sale price there is only one more step

$1000 - $150 = $850
The sale price is $850
