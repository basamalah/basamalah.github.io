3. in TLE compare exploration course with major course​


In TLE, a comparison of exploration courses with major courses reveals some interesting insights. For one, exploration courses tend to be more hands-on and interactive, while major courses are more theoretical. Secondly, exploration courses often allow students to explore different careers and majors, while major courses focus on one specific area. Finally, exploration courses typically have smaller class sizes than major courses. This allows for more individualized attention and a more intimate learning environment.

The comparison of an exploration course with a major course in terms of its effects on students’ self-efficacy, academic achievement, and career decision-making. The results showed that the exploration course had a positive effect on students’ self-efficacy and academic achievement, but not on their career decision-making. The major course had a positive effect on students’ career decision-making, but not on their self-efficacy or academic achievement. These results suggest that exploration courses may be more beneficial for students in terms of their self-efficacy and academic achievement, while major courses may be more beneficial for students in terms of their career decision-making.
