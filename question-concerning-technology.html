7. What are the and similarities and differences between Heidegger's The Question Concerning Technology and Hickel's article?.


"The Question Concerning Technology" and Jason Hickel's article "Why It's Time to Reject the Myth of Progress" are both works that address the theme of technology and its impact on society and the human condition. Both works explore the ways in which technology has shaped and continues to shape human culture, and both raise important questions about the role of technology in shaping our understanding of the world and our place in it.One similarity between "The Question Concerning Technology" and "Why It's Time to Reject the Myth of Progress" is that both works discuss the ways in which technology has shaped our understanding of the world and our place in it. Heidegger argues that technology is not simply a neutral tool or means to an end, but rather has a profound influence on the way we think and perceive the world around us.Hickel similarly argues that technology has shaped our understanding of progress and our relationship to the natural world, and has contributed to the myth of progress that pervades modern society.

However, there are also some significant differences between "The Question Concerning Technology" and "Why It's Time to Reject the Myth of Progress."One difference is that Heidegger's essay is more philosophical in nature, while Hickel's article is more focused on empirical evidence and current events. Heidegger's essay is also more abstract and theoretical, while Hickel's article is more concrete and grounded in specific examples and case studies. Additionally, while both works raise important questions about the role of technology in society, Heidegger's essay is more focused on the broader implications of technology for human culture and experience, while Hickel's article is more focused on the specific consequences of technology for the natural environment and for social justice.
