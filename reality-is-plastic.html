11. A construction-based client would like to develop an application that can analyze an image of machinery and overlay information that will assist in the repair. Which form of Extended Reality can best assist the client in this situation? O Haptic Reality O Mixed Reality O Augmented Reality O Virtual Reality


Answer:

Augmented Reality (AR) can best assist the client in this situation.

Augmented Reality overlays digital information on the real world, allowing users to see and interact with virtual objects in the context of their physical environment. In this case, an AR application can analyze an image of the machinery and overlay information such as instructions for repair or parts diagrams, providing the repair personnel with the information they need to complete the task. Additionally, AR can also provide real-time guidance, troubleshooting, and remote assistance.

Haptic Reality focuses on providing tactile feedback to users, not visual, so it's not the best option for this task. Virtual Reality (VR) immerses users in a completely artificial environment, while mixed reality (MR) combines elements of both AR and VR, allowing users to see and interact with both digital and physical objects in the same environment, but the client's goal is to overlay information on real machinery, so VR and MR are not the best options either.

14. How does mixed reality expand on augmented reality?


Answer:

Mixed reality (MR) can be thought of as an extension of augmented reality (AR) that goes beyond simply overlaying digital objects on top of the real world. Mixed reality blends virtual and real environments, allowing users to interact with both digital and physical objects in a seamless manner.

In mixed reality, digital objects are not simply placed on top of the real world, but instead are anchored to specific physical locations in the user's environment, making it possible for users to walk around and interact with these objects as if they were real. This is achieved through the use of advanced sensors and computer vision techniques that allow the device to understand and map the user's environment in real time.

Mixed reality is also capable of creating a more immersive experience than AR, as it allows for digital objects to interact with real-world objects in a way that AR cannot. For example, a mixed reality headset could create a virtual ball that bounces off of a real table and reacts to gravity just as a real ball would.

Overall, mixed reality is an evolution of augmented reality that provides a more interactive and immersive experience by seamlessly blending virtual and real environments.
