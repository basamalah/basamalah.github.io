1. Which is a run-on sentence?Clean your camera lens there's a fingerprint on it both of the candidates radiate confidence in camera​


ANSWER:________________

Clean your camera lens there's a fingerprint on it is a run-on sentence. It has two independent clauses that are joined without end punctuation: Clean your camera lens and There's a fingerprint on it.

REMEMBER!_______________

A run-on sentence, also known as a run-together sentence, is made up of two independent clauses that are joined without end punctuation or with just a comma.

To fix a run-on sentence, separate it into two sentences. Add end punctuation after the first sentence, and capitalize the second sentence.

You can also fix a run-on sentence by rewriting it as a compound sentence. A compound sentence is made up of two independent clauses joined by a comma and a coordinating conjunction such as and, but, or, or so.

16. WHAT ARE LATENT FINGERPRINTS?


Answer:

Latent prints are impressions produced by the ridged skin, known as friction ridges, on human fingers, palms, and soles of the feet. Examiners analyze and compare latent prints to known prints of individuals in an effort to make indentification or exclusions.

26. .Suppose one of identical twins commits a crime and only DNA, Fingerprints, and an    eyewitness account are evidence in the case. Which of the following pieces of evidence can actually be useful. Explain and defend your answer?* ​


Answer:

In this scenario, the most useful piece of evidence would likely be the DNA evidence. DNA evidence can be used to identify an individual with a high degree of accuracy, and it is generally considered to be very reliable in criminal investigations.

Fingerprints can also be useful in identifying an individual, but they are not as reliable as DNA evidence because they can be left by someone other than the person who committed the crime, and they can be easily contaminated or damaged.

An eyewitness account can be useful in some cases, but it is generally considered to be less reliable than physical evidence because eyewitness testimony can be influenced by a variety of factors, such as stress, emotion, and the passage of time.

Overall, DNA evidence is likely to be the most useful piece of evidence in this case because it is highly accurate and reliable, and it can provide conclusive proof of an individual's involvement in a crime.
