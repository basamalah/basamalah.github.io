21. definition of taal volcano


TAAL VOLCANOPHYSICAL AND GEOGRAPHICAL CHARACTERISTICS

- The Taal Volcano is regarded as a complex volcano in the middle of Taal lake.

- It is located 50 km south of Manila, the capital city of the Philippines.

- It is located in the province of Batangas.

- It is part of a chain of volcanoes that are situated at the western edge of Luzon.

- This chain was formed by a subduction plate in the Eurasion Plate, which was also underneath the Philippine Mobile Plate.

- It has a 311 m elevation, making it one of the lowest volcanoes in the world.

- It is often considered as an island within a lake.

- Due to recent events concerning the volcano, the lake that was situated in the crater of Taal Volcano has now evaporated.

SIGNIFICANT ERUPTION HISTORY OF THE MAIN CRATER

1.) 1572

- The first eruption that was ever recorded by humanity was in 1572. This was the year wherein Augustinian Friars founded Town Taal, which is now called San Nicolas, Batangas.

2.) 1591

- A mild eruption that emitted great amounts of smoke from the crater was recorded on this date.

3.) 1753

- In the year 1753, violent activity was recorded in the volcano, increasing in frequency as the year progressed.

4.) The Eruption of 1754

- This was met with one of the most massive explosions of the volcano, which is now regarded as the 200 Day Eruption of 1754.

- It lasted from 15th of May to 12th of December.

- With the eruption, it forced multiple towns from Tanauan, Taal, Lipa, and Sala to relocate.

- It also blocked the Pansipit River, which led to an increase in the lake's water level.

- Almost nothing was left under the layers of mud and lahar except for a few structures.

5.) March of 1808

- Although it was not as severe as the one from 1754, the surrounding was immediately covered with 33 inches of ash.

- The explosion changed the physical characteristics of the volcano, making the volcano shorter, its crater widened, the pond within it massively reduced, and more.

6.) July 19, 1874

- Gas and ash emitted killed the livestock in the surrounding area of the volcano.

7.) January 1911

- Before the 30th of January, aggressive volcanic activity was already being recorded by the Manila Observatory.

- People were surprised when the volcano finally erupted on the 30th of January.

- It formed a huge mushroom cloud that was similar to the cloud that a nuclear device formed, and the island itself was already destroyed from the damage.

- According to official reports: there were 1,100 casualties and 199 injured individuals, but it is believed that there were more undiscovered bodies beneath the layers of hardened lava, lahar and mud.

- Barangays that were nearby the island were completely destroyed, and were covered by the lava flow.

8.) January 12, 2020

- On the aforementioned date a phreatic (underwater) eruption occured from the main crater.

- It spewed ash that spread to most of Luzon.

- Due to this, classes from multiple cities in Luzon were cancelled.

- It also interfered with work schedules, and even cancelled flights.

- The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology issued a Level 4 warning, meaning that a possible hazardous eruption would soon occur.

- Due to its very aggressive behaviour, Alert Level 4 was issued in less than a day.

- Near the end of the month, activity slowly decreased, and the alert levels were lowered.

- There were very few casualties, most of them resulting from respiratory failure due to the ash that spread in the area.

23. taal volcano location


Answer:

Taal Volcano

Taal volcano is located in the province of Batangas and is one of the most active volcanoes in the Philippines, second only to Mayon Volcano in Albay. It is a large caldera wherein the water that filled it turns into a lake called Taal Lake. It consists of 47 craters and 4 maars, volcanic craters caused by an eruption, with the main crater found at the center where previous eruptions occurred.

It already has more than 34 confirmed eruptions in 448 years with the latest happened on January 12, 2020 which caused panic and wide destruction of crops and death of animals.

Location or Geography

The volcano is located about 50 kilometers (31 mi) south of Manila, the capital of the Phillipines. Main Crater Lake was temporarily displaced in the 2020 Taal Volcano eruption but reformed after months by rain when the volcanic activity in the area decreased.
