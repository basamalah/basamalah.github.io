3. introduction to Quantitative research​


Answer:

Quantitative research is the process of collecting and analyzing numerical data. It can be used to find patterns and averages, make predictions, test causal relationships, and generalize results to wider populations.

Quantitative research is the opposite of qualitative research, which involves collecting and analyzing non-numerical data (e.g. text, video, or audio).

Quantitative research is widely used in the natural and social sciences: biology, chemistry, psychology, economics, sociology, marketing, etc.

Chapter 3: Introduction

The Introduction chapter of a research study provides an overview of the research project, including the research question, the purpose of the study, and the significance of the study. It also provides background information on the topic being studied and sets the context for the research.

Research design:

The research design is the plan or framework for conducting a study. It outlines the methods and techniques that will be used to gather and analyze data. In a correlational study, the research design involves collecting data on two or more variables and examining the relationship between them.

Sampling procedure:

The sampling procedure refers to the method used to select a sample of participants for a study. It is important to choose a representative sample in order to ensure that the results of the study are generalizable to the larger population.

Scope and delimitation:

The scope of a study refers to the boundaries or limits of the research, including what will be studied and what will not be studied. The delimitation of a study refers to the limitations or constraints of the research, including any factors that may impact the results or interpretation of the study.

5W1H:

The 5W1H method is a technique used to gather and organize information about a particular topic. It stands for "what," "where," "when," "who," "why," and "how." These questions can be used to gather information about the context, purpose, and details of a research project or other topic.

BPL (Business Permit and Licensing):

A Business Permit and Licensing (BPL) is a document that allows a business to legally operate within a particular jurisdiction. It is typically issued by a local government agency and includes information about the business, such as its name, location, and type of operation.

In the context of research, the BPL may be relevant if the study involves collecting data from businesses or if the research is being conducted as part of a business or organizational context.

— It is very important that research can be replicated, because it means that other researchers can test the findings of the research. Replicability keeps researchers honest and can give readers confidence in research. If the research is replicable, then any false conclusions can eventually be shown to be wrong. — There are two main categories of research methods: qualitative research methods and quantitative research methods. Quantitative research methods involve using numbers to measure data. Researchers can use statistical analysis to find connections and meaning in the data. Quantitative research deals with numbers and statistics, while qualitative research deals with words and meanings.

29. when making the chapter one for a qualitative research, do you need to include a solution to your introduction?​


In qualitative research, the purpose of the introduction is to provide background information on the research topic, explain the importance of the research, and identify the research questions or objectives. It is not necessary to include a solution to the research problem in the introduction.

Qualitative research often focuses on exploring and understanding complex phenomena and experiences, rather than providing a specific solution to a problem. Therefore, the research questions or objectives are typically framed to guide the research process and to help the researcher gain a deeper understanding of the topic under investigation.

However, in some cases, qualitative research may aim to develop or test a theoretical framework, or to inform the development of interventions or policies. In these cases, the research may have a more practical or applied focus, and the introduction may include some discussion of potential solutions or implications of the research.

Overall, the content and structure of the introduction in a qualitative research study will depend on the specific research questions, objectives, and context of the study.
