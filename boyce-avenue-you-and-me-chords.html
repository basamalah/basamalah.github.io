13. Why Cameron Boyce has an Epilepsy Siezure?


Answer: Cameron Boyce and SUDEP in epilepsy

Disney Channel actor Cameron Boyce was a star in the making - until his tragic passing from Sudden Unexpected Death in Epilepsy (SUDEP) on 6th July 2019. Boyce, who starred in the Descendants franchise, was just 20 when he died.

Boyce’s death brought the issue of SUDEP in epilepsy to the attention of the world. A year after his death, take a moment to learn more about SUDEP.

SUDEP is defined as: “when a person with epilepsy dies suddenly and prematurely and no reason for death is found”. SUDEP in epilepsy often occurs during sleep, either during or just after an epileptic seizure.

At present, scientists are still not sure exactly why people die from SUDEP although they believe it could be because of:

Breathing problems: During a seizure, the person may pause their breathing (called apnea). If this goes on too long, they may not have enough oxygen in their bloodSuffocation: Many people die from SUDEP in epilepsy during their sleep, so some researchers believe their airways get blocked by pillows or sheetsHeart problems: Seizures may cause dangerous heart rhythms

Answer:

When splitting a table or removing non-key dependencies to bring it into Boyce-Codd Normal Form (BCNF), the following should be considered:

1. Identify the determinants (candidate keys) of the table, which are the attributes that uniquely identify a row in the table.

2. Check for any functional dependencies (FDs) where a non-key attribute is dependent on a proper subset of the candidate key. If such an FD is found, the table should be split into two or more tables to remove the non-key dependency.

3. Repeat the process of checking for non-key dependencies and splitting tables until the table is in BCNF.

4. When splitting the table, make sure that the original table can be reconstructed from the new tables (i.e., the new tables are in 3NF) and that each new table is in BCNF.

5. Keep in mind that BCNF is a more restrictive form of normalization than 3NF, so it is possible to be in 3NF but not in BCNF.
