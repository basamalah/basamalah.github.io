Answer:

The observations made by Carolyn suggest that the Elodea plant is undergoing photosynthesis. Photosynthesis is a process by which plants use energy from sunlight to convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen gas. During this process, oxygen gas is released as a by-product.

When Carolyn observed the Elodea in the freshwater aquarium on her desk, she counted 32 bubbles that formed on the plant's leaves and were released into the water. This indicates that the plant was producing oxygen gas through photosynthesis.

When Carolyn moved the aquarium to a sunlit window and observed the Elodea again, she counted 194 bubbles that formed and were released by the plant into the water. This increase in the number of bubbles suggests that the plant was undergoing more photosynthesis and producing more oxygen gas in the presence of sunlight.

In summary, Carolyn's observations suggest that the Elodea plant is undergoing photosynthesis and producing oxygen gas as a by-product, and that the amount of photosynthesis is influenced by the availability of sunlight.
