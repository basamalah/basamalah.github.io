15. Discuss the sources of Data : Primary and Secondary Discuss the advantages of primary data over secondary data. Discuss the advantages of secondary data.


Answer:

Primary data:

Primary data is collected by the researcher for a specific purpose. This type of data is original and is collected through various methods such as surveys, interviews, observation, experiments, etc. The main sources of primary data are:

Surveys: Surveys are one of the most common methods of collecting primary data. They involve asking questions to a sample population to gather information about their opinions, attitudes, behavior, etc.

Interviews: Interviews are conducted in person or over the phone and can be structured, semi-structured or unstructured. They are useful in gathering in-depth information on a specific topic.

Observation: Observation involves watching and recording the behavior of individuals, groups or events. It can be done in a natural or controlled environment.

Experiments: Experiments involve manipulating variables to test a hypothesis. They are useful in establishing cause-and-effect relationships.

Advantages of primary data over secondary data:

Accuracy: Primary data is more accurate than secondary data as it is collected for a specific purpose and directly from the source.

Relevance: Primary data is more relevant to the research problem as it is collected specifically for that purpose.

Control: The researcher has complete control over the collection of primary data, which ensures that the data collected is relevant and reliable.

Secondary data:

Secondary data is data that has already been collected by someone else for a different purpose. This type of data is obtained from various sources such as books, journals, government reports, company reports, websites, etc. The main sources of secondary data are:

Government sources: Census data, economic reports, demographic data, etc.

Commercial sources: Company reports, market research reports, etc.

Academic sources: Journals, books, conference proceedings, etc.

Advantages of secondary data:

Cost-effective: Secondary data is generally less expensive than primary data as it is readily available.

Time-saving: Secondary data can be obtained quickly as it already exists.

Large sample size: Secondary data often has a large sample size, which can increase the reliability of the data.

Longitudinal analysis: Secondary data can be used for longitudinal analysis, which involves studying trends over time.

In conclusion, both primary and secondary data have their advantages and disadvantages. While primary data is more accurate and relevant, it can be expensive and time-consuming to collect. Secondary data, on the other hand, is cost-effective and readily available but may not always be relevant to the research problem. The choice between primary and secondary data depends on the research question and the resources available.
