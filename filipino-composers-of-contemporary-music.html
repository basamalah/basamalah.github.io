8. song analysis choose one contemporary and traditional composition written by a filipino contemporary composer ​


Explanation:

Contemporary Composition: "Tatsulok" by Bamboo Mañalac

"Tatsulok" is a song by Filipino rock band Bamboo, composed by the band's lead vocalist, Bamboo Mañalac. It was released in 2005 as part of their self-titled debut album.

The song's lyrics revolve around the themes of social inequality and the struggle for justice. The title "Tatsulok" means "triangle" in English, which is a metaphor for the unequal distribution of power and resources in Philippine society. The lyrics describe how the rich and powerful exploit and oppress the poor and powerless, creating an unjust and unequal society.

The song starts with a haunting guitar riff and gradually builds up to a powerful and energetic chorus, with Bamboo's powerful vocals driving the song forward. The music perfectly complements the lyrics, creating a sense of urgency and intensity that reflects the song's message.

Overall, "Tatsulok" is a powerful and socially conscious song that tackles important issues in Philippine society. Its catchy melody and driving rhythm make it a memorable and effective piece of social commentary.

Traditional Composition: "Pamulinawen" by Felipe Padilla de Leon

"Pamulinawen" is a traditional Ilocano folk song composed by Filipino composer Felipe Padilla de Leon. It is a love song that expresses the singer's deep affection for a woman named Mulinawen.

The song is known for its beautiful melody and poetic lyrics. The opening lines, "Pamulinawen, pusok imdengam man / Toy umas-asug agrayo ita sadiam," describe the singer's yearning for Mulinawen, comparing her to a beautiful flower that he longs to possess.

The song's melody is simple and sweet, with a gentle rhythm that perfectly captures the song's romantic and nostalgic mood. The use of traditional Ilocano instruments, such as the kudyapi and the banduria, further adds to the song's traditional flavor.

Overall, "Pamulinawen" is a timeless and beautiful love song that has been enjoyed by generations of Filipinos. Its simple melody and poetic lyrics make it a classic example of traditional Filipino music.

16. What are the contributions of the filipino composers in the development of contemporary music in our country?​


Answer:

RAMON P. SANTOS, PH. D.

      Contemporary music in the Philippines usually refers to compositions that have adopted ideas and elements from twentieth century art music in the West, as well as the latest trends and musical styles in the entertainment industry. This brief introduction covers only the works written by the art music composers.

      The modern Filipino repertoire consist of pieces that have been written in twentieth century idioms that have evolved out of such stylistic movements as impressionism,  expressionism, neo-classism, as well as the so-called avant-garde and New Music. A good number of these works have utilized the standard formats of classical European music such as the concerto, the symphony, the symphonic and tone poems, cantata, etc. and may therefore be broadly categorized as neo-classic. At the same time, they have also been greatly influenced by the textural colors of Debussy’s music and the ambiguous and “dissonant” tonalities of early 20th century expressionist compositions. Moreover, the sounds of non-Western instruments have been added to the tonal fabric.

      The first modern works that belong to the above descriptions are attributed to Nicanor Abelardo, who from 1931 till his death in 1934 produced works that show a dramatic departure from his highly chromatic–tonal idiom to dissonant and ambiguous tonalities and complex rhythmic textures. Some of the representative works of that period are the “Sinfonietta for Strings” and the “Cinderella” Overture. In Panoramas, a chamber music suite, Abelardo also experimented on unorthodox instrumental combinations (flute, violin, viola, celesta, piano). Two short pieces for piano Dancing Fool and Malikmata by Antoni Molina, Philippine Suite by Ramon Tapales, and Mindanao Sketches by Antonio Buenaventura were isolated works that assumed some degree of modernistic structural elements, immediately following Abelardo’s output.

23. WHAT 'S MORE TASK 2 :name one famous filipino contemporary composer and discuss his life and musical contribution.​


Answer:

Ryan Cayabyab

also known as Mr. C, is a Filipino musician, composer and conductor. He was the Executive and Artistic Director for several years for the defunct San Miguel Foundation for the Performing Arts. He was named as National Artist of the Philippines for Music in 2018.[4]

His works range from commissioned full-length ballets, theater musicals, choral pieces, a Mass set to unaccompanied chorus, and orchestral pieces, to commercial recordings of popular music, film scores and television specials.

Cayabyab's current project includes the Ryan Cayabyab Singers (RCS), a group of seven young adult singers comparable to his group Smokey Mountain in the early 1990s. After FreemantleMedia decided not to renew the Philippine Idol franchise, Cayabyab transferred to rival show Pinoy Dream Academy (season 2), replacing Jim Paredes as the show's headmaster. PDA 2 started on June 14, 2008. He also became the chairman of the board of judges for GMA Network's musical-reality show To The Top.

He is the executive director of the PhilPop MusicFest Foundation Inc., the organization behind the Philippine Popular Music Festival. This songwriting competition for amateurs and professionals puts the spotlight on songwriters and encourages Filipinos to preserve their unique musical identity.

The Traditional Music of Philippines

This short outline focuses solely on the works of art music composers.
