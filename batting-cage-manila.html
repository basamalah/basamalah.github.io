8. Joe has a cage for his chickens and rabbits .He counts 40 heads from the top of the cage and 130 legs from the bottom of the cage .How many chickens and how many rabbits does he have in the cage?​


Answer:

Let R = rabbits and C = chickens.

Rabbits and chickens each have one head apiece.

So your heads equation is

R + C = 40

But rabbits have four feet and chickens two.

So your feet equation is

4R + 2C = 130

or

2R + C = 65 after dividing by 2.

Now, if you subtract your heads equation from your feet equation you get

R = 65 - 40

R = 25

That's your number of rabbits.

40 - 25 = 15

Meaning you have 15 chickens.

10. a bird inside the cage. what do you think is the difference if the bird is inside the cage and if it is outside?​


Answer:

Is your question based on the feelings of the bird or their awareness? All birds are aware that they are in a cage, however how they feel about being in a cage is the issue. I have been given wild birds that have been injured and the trauma of being caged is as much a threat to their health as the actual injury. I can tell this is the case because as time passes and the bird realizes that it is safe, it’s anxiety diminishes. On the other hand, birds that are raised in a cage view the cage as “safe territory” and this is why they voluntarily go back in . My various birds wait close by while I am cleaning their cages and once I am finished they zoom back in there. I always have treats placed around the cage for them to enjoy. In order to maintain this perception of safe territory, always move slowly when placing food or water inside their space.
