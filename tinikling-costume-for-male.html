2. TINIKLING 1. Why is the dance called Tinikling? 2. Music and Count (Time Signature)3. Costume (MALE & FEMALE)​


TINIKLING

Tinikling is a traditional Philippine folk dance and the national dance of the Philippines originated in the island of Leyte in the Visayas during Spanish colonial era. The performance involves the rhythmic clapping, tapping, and sliding of two bamboo poles against each other while one or more dancers step over in between the poles timed to the beat.

WHY IS THE DANCE CALLED TINIKLING?

Tinikling is said to be named after a long-legged bird called TIKLING.

The dance imitates the movement of the tikling birds as they dodge the bamboo traps set by the farmers. Dancer mimics bird's grace and agility by dancing between large bamboo poles.

MUSIC AND COUNT

The time signature of Tinikling is ¾ so we have 1, 2, 3, counts in every measure.

COSTUME

MALE- Barong Tagalog or camesa de chino and long red trousers.

FEMALE- Balintawak or Patadyong.

12. tinikling origin:time signature:costume;classification:​


Answer:

ORIGIN:

The Tinikling is one of the oldest dances from the Philippines, and originated in the islands of Leyte in the Visayas. According to legend, the Tinikling was started by people who worked in the paddies and farms of the Philippines.

TIME SIGNATURE:

TIME SIGNATURE OF TINIKLING 3/4 time signature - think about it, the dance is two steps within the bamboo poles and the 3rd step out, in-in-out, in-in-out, in-in-out, etc.

COSTUME: COSTUME OF TINIKLING Women wear the balintawak, a dress with wide-arched sleeves and a panuelo or handkerchief on the shoulder. Some women wear the patadyong, a checkered skirt paired with a blouse made out of pineapple fiber. And the men wear common formal attire called the barong Tagalog.

CLASSIFICATION: The name tinikling is a reference to birds locally known as tikling, which can be any of a number of rail species, but more specifically refers to the slaty-breasted rail (Gallirallus striatus), the buff-banded rail (Gallirallus philippensis), and the barred rail (Gallirallus torquatus).

TINIKLING DANCEOrigin

The island in Leyte in the Visayas

Description

Tinikling is a traditional Philippine folk dance which originated during the Spanish colonial era.

Adaption

When performed by dance troupes or in cultural shows, Tinikling is typically performed in the "Rural Suite" which includes dances originating from Filipino Christians that have a more "folksy" character.
