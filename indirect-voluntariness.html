Answer:

The answer is yes. An indirect voluntary act can still be considered as voluntary. The reason is that we have our own decision making ability. We have to remember that everything we do is out of our own will. Even if we didn't initially mean our actions, we must still face the consequences.  

Indirect voluntary acts are actions not intended for its own sake
