(a) unicellular cells vs. multicellular cells

- Unicellular organisms are made up of only one cell that carries out all of the functions needed by the organism, while multicellular organisms use many different cells to function.

(b) prokaryotic cells vs. eukaryotic cells

- eukaryotic cells have a membrane-bound nucleus and prokaryotic cells do not.

(c) cyptoplasm vc. karyoplasm

- cytoplasm is (cytology) the contents of a cell except for the nucleus it includes cytosol, organelles, vesicles, and the cytoskeleton while sarcoplasm is (anatomy) the interfibrillar cytoplasm of striated muscle.

(d) rough endoplasmic reticulum vs. smooth endoplasmic reticulum

​- Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum is known for stocking the lipids and proteins. It is not bounded by ribosomes. While, the Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum is bounded by the ribosomes and also stores proteins.

Similarities And Differences Between Aerobic And Anaerobic Respiration

Cellular respiration is a process that takes place inside the cells where energy is released by the breakdown of glucose molecules. The process can be conveniently divided into two categories based on the usage of oxygen, namely aerobic and anaerobic respiration.

Aerobic and anaerobic respiration both have few similarities and differences. Let us read about it.

Similarities

In both aerobic and anaerobic respiration, food is broken down to release energy. Both take place inside cells. Both produce byproducts.Carbon dioxide is produced in both reactions. Energy is released in both reactions.

Differences

The difference between the aerobic and anaerobic respiration is tabulated below.

Aerobic Respiration:

It is the process of cellular respiration that takes place in the presence of oxygen gasIt involves the exchange of gases between the organism and the outside environment. It can be found in the cytoplasm and the mitochondria Glucose breaks down into carbon dioxide and water. All higher organisms such as mammals have this type of respiration.

Anerobic Respiration:

It is the process of cellular respiration that takes place in the absence of oxygen gas.Exchange of gases is absent.It can be found in the cytoplasm and the mitochondria It can be found only in the cytoplasmGlucose breaks down into ethyl alcohol, carbon dioxide, and energy Lower organisms such as bacteria and yeast use this type.

23. venn diagram of ionic vs covalent bonding


Explanation:

IONIC BOND

Ionic bond, also called electrovalent bond, type of linkage formed from the electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions in a chemical compound. Such a bond forms when the valence (outermost) electrons of one atom are transferred permanently to another atom.

COVALENT BOND

A covalent bond is a chemical bond that involves the sharing of electron pairs between atoms. These electron pairs are known as shared pairs or bonding pairs, and the stable balance of attractive and repulsive forces between atoms, when they share electrons, is known as covalent bonding.

Hi there! It looks like you are looking for information on the differences and similarities between animal and plant cells. It's a great topic to explore, as both types of cells have some common features but also some distinct characteristics.

Animal cells and plant cells have some similar features, including a cell membrane, cytoplasm, and ribosomes. They both have a nucleus, which contains genetic material, and mitochondria, which produce energy for the cell.

However, there are some important differences between the two types of cells. Animal cells do not have a cell wall, while plant cells do. Plant cells also have additional structures, such as chloroplasts, which are responsible for photosynthesis, and a large central vacuole, which stores water and other substances. In addition, plant cells are usually larger than animal cells and have a more rigid shape due to the cell wall.
