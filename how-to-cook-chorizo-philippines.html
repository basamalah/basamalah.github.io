The story says that this dish was originally created out of leftover ubiquitous types of menudo from carinderia where diners never choose as it was everywhere resulting in recycling it to become this wonderful dish. As time progressed it was prepared from fresh ingredients rather than leftovers. Everlasting, also known as Marikina’s meatloaf which is prepared with minced pork, Chorizo de Bilbao, capsicum, raisins, pickle relish, onions, and eggs steamed in a llanera, a type of oval aluminum mould popular in the Philippines. Ingredients 650 g minced pork (15% fat) 1 large chorizo, thinly sliced 2 boiled eggs, cut in half 1/2 cup grated quick melt cheese or processed cheese 1/2 red capsicum, sliced 1/4 cup raisins 3 tbsp sweet pickle relish 2 tbsp tomato paste 2 tbsp soy sauce 4 eggs, beaten 6 cloves garlic, minced 1 onion, finely chopped Salt oil freshly ground black pepper margarine Instructions • In a wok add oil then sauté garlic and onions. • Add minced pork and chorizo then cook for 10 minutes. • Add the red capsicum, tomato paste, soy sauce and pickle relish then simmer in low heat for 10 minutes. • Mix in the raisins and cheese, season with salt and freshly ground black pepper, turn off heat then set it aside then let it cool. • Mix in the eggs then set aside. • Prepare four llaneras, generously grease insides with margarine. Place half an egg on each llanera then pour meat mixture into the mould. Cover llanera tightly with an aluminum foil. • Place in a steamer then steam for an hour.
