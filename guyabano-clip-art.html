5. explain the predators in adding clip art design within a slide​


CLIP ART DESIGN

Clip Art Design a type of computer graphic art that comprises on simple graphics rather than photographic pictures. In most cases, clip art is offered in a variety of file formats, including bitmaps and vector drawings. Clip art is a set of ready-to-use, usually non-copyrighted illustrations that can be cut and pasted or inserted as artwork from a website, or sold in books, as part of a software package, or in a book. Microsoft's PowerPoint application offers a library of media files (pictures, videos, music, and animation files) called clip art. A clip art file is a collection of images or photos that can be added to a document or used in a computer application.

STEPS ON HOW TO INSERT CLIP ART DESIGN

Place your cursor over the slide where you wish to place a clip art file.Click Online Pictures in the Images group on the Insert tab. (Clip Art is the name of this option in PowerPoint 2007/2010.)Enter your search criteria in the Bing.com field in the Insert Pictures dialog box (Clip Art task pane in PowerPoint 2007/2010) and press "Enter."The task pane displays the results of your search.Double-click on the clip art you wish to use in your presentation, or pick "Insert" from the drop-down menu.

19. how do graphics and/or clip art affect slide presentation?​


Answer:

.

.

.

The most common slide element used after text is a graphic, usually a photograph or a vector drawing. The old saying goes, "A picture is worth a thousand words" and it is true. Just make sure that the thousand words being spoken by the graphics you use on your presentation slides are words that will increase the impact of your message, not detract from it.

Answer:

The real reason for using a graphic is to add some flavor to the text that is on the slide. It gives the audience visual variety, which keeps their attention raised. A good choice of graphic can make a point come alive in the audience’s mind. A poor choice of graphic will leave the audience wondering what the point really is since the graphic is inconsistent with the message.
