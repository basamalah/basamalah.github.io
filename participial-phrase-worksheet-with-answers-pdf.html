1. ANSWER THE FOLLOWING QUESTIONS • What is a participial phrase? • Give five examples of participial phrases​


PARTICIPIAL PHRASE

A participial phrase is a group of words made up of a participle, a modifier, and/or nouns, pronouns, or noun phrases that serve as the direct objects, indirect objects, or complements of the action or state expressed in the participle.

A participial phrase is a participle modified by an adverb. The entire phrase acts like an adjective.

Here are the 5 Examples:Students planning to study nursing must first meet with the dean.Studying carefully, she learned much about Native American people.Ron loved his new hiking shoes, wearing them even to church.Theresa observed her father jogging along the path.The man wearing the war bonnet was a chief of the Sioux people.
