8. What is folklor? What


Answer:

the traditional beliefs, customs, and stories of a community, passed through the generations by word of mouth.

Answer:

Folklore is the expressive body of culture shared by a particular group of people; it encompasses the traditions common to that culture, subculture or group. These include oral traditions such as tales, proverbs and jokes. They include material culture, ranging from traditional building styles to handmade toys common to the group. Folklore also includes customary lore, the forms and rituals of celebrations such as Christmas and weddings, folk dances and initiation rites. Each one of these, either singly or in combination, is considered a folklore artifact.

12. solar eclipse folklore


Explanation:

In different parts of the world, their folklore is different.

In India, the demon spirit Rahu steals and consumes the nectar of immortality but is beheaded before he can swallow it. His immortal head flies into the heavens. The Sun and Moon had alerted the gods of his theft, so he takes revenge on them: When Rahu swallows an orb, we have an eclipse-but the orb returns to view because Rahu has no body.

In Transylvanian folklore, an eclipse stems from the angry Sun turning away and covering herself with darkness, in response to men's bad behavior.

In Norse culture, an evil enchanter, Loki, was put into chains by the gods. Loki got revenge by creating wolflike giants, one of which swallowed the Sun thus causing an eclipse. Another of the giant wolves changed the Moon trying to eat it.
