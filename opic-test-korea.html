Answer:

Korean painting includes paintings made in Korea or by overseas Koreans on all surfaces. The earliest surviving Korean paintings are murals in the Goguryeo tombs, of which considerable numbers survive, the oldest from some 2,000 years ago (mostly now in North Korea), with varied scenes including dancers, hunting and spirits.[1] The Takamatsuzuka Tomb in Japan, from the 7th-century end of the Goguryeo period, has paintings in Goguryeo style that were either done by Korean artists, or Japanese one trained by Koreans.[2] But more often influences came into Korea from China. 19. A. ExplanationPerformance Task: Make a short paragraph based on the text-type purpose anText Purpose: Classificationopic Difference among Enhanced Community Quarantine, Modifiedcommunity Quarantine. ❤️❤️​


Answer:

A community quarantine is a public health measure designed to limit the spread of communicable diseases by limiting the movement of individuals within an area. A modified community quarantine may limit some people's freedom of movement and contains restrictions such as only allowing individuals who are free from symptoms of a disease outside the quarantined area. The two types differ in their severity: enhanced community quarantines limit external travel more than modified ones, and can be expanded if deemed appropriate.

Enhanced community quarantines (ECQ) may apply to individuals with two conditions: symptoms of a disease, and a history of an acute infectious disease.

Individuals subject to an enhanced community quarantine are limited in terms of travel within the quarantined area. A person who comes into contact with anyone in the affected area is considered contaminated and would be confined within the quarantine area. A person who has been in contact with someone potentially infected will be placed on modified community quarantine for 21 days (or 28 days during an outbreak).

Modified community quarantines (MCQ) are applied in a broad range of public health situations, and can be imposed more widely than ECQs. MCQs may be applied early in an outbreak of a disease to prevent infection spreading, or to limit the spread of infections within a hospital. Modified community quarantines are less restrictive than ECQs; visitors are allowed, for example. In some situations, no quarantine restrictions may apply if the person has no symptoms and has not come into contact with an infected person.

Some types of modified community quarantines may not be imposed in some situations. For example, a hospital quarantine is usually applied to staff and visitors only if they have been tested for the disease, or if there has been a confirmed case of infection.

28. Opical fishes .7. Massive production of coral may endanger the marine life.​


Answer:

Human threats to coral reefs

Overfishing and the use of destructive fishing methods have decimated fish populations on reefs and destroyed their habitats as well. Additionally, elevated surface temperatures caused by worldwide climate change lead to coral bleaching more frequently.

How does coral affect marine life?

Thousands of marine animals depend on coral reefs for survival, including some species of sea turtles, fish, crabs, shrimp, jellyfish, sea birds, starfish, and more. Coral reefs provide shelter, spawning grounds, and protection from predators. They also support organisms at the base of ocean food chains.

What causes the lives of corals to be endangered?

Coral reefs are endangered by a variety of factors, including: natural phenomena such as hurricanes, El Niño, and diseases; local threats such as overfishing, destructive fishing techniques, coastal development, pollution, and careless tourism; and the global effects of climate change—warming seas and increasing levels
