1.The Bible is the holy scripture of the Christian religion, purporting to tell the history of the Earth from its earliest creation to the spread of Christianity in the first century A.D.

2.The traditional author is James the Just, "a servant of God and brother of the Lord Jesus Christ". Like Hebrews, James is not so much a letter as an exhortation; the style of the Greek language-text makes it unlikely that it was actually written by James, the brother of Jesus.

The Bible was written over a period of about 1400 years. And yet, though many of the authors never met each other, they all wrote about a unified theme. Christ is at the center of both the Old and New Testaments.

5. What is the consistent message of the Bible from the different authors of the books in the Bible?​


The Bible's consistent message, as expressed by its various authors and books, is one of God's love and plan for humanity's salvation. The Bible conveys a message of God's sovereignty, justice, mercy, and grace throughout the Old and New Testaments. This message is primarily expressed in the Old Testament through the history of God's interaction with humanity and the nation of Israel, as well as through the laws and prophecies given to them. This message is more fully revealed in the person and work of Jesus Christ, as well as the apostles' teachings, in the New Testament.

According to the Bible, humanity was created in the image of God and is thus intrinsically valuable, but that all people have sinned and fallen short of God's standards. The Bible also teaches that Jesus Christ is the Savior of the world and that humanity can be reconciled to God and have eternal life through his death and resurrection. The Bible's consistent message is one of hope and redemption, and it invites all people to trust in Jesus Christ and follow him as Lord and Savior. The Bible encourages Christians to live out their faith through love, obedience, and service to others, as well as to spread the gospel message throughout the world.

30. Who are the persons behind the collection of the books of the bible? Who are these persons behind the compilation of the bible into a single book (old and new testament)?


Answer:

Second-century Syrian writer Tatian attempted to create a canon by weaving the four gospels together as the Diatessaron. The Muratorian Canon, which is believed to date to 200 A.D., is the earliest compilation of canonical texts resembling the New Testament.

The Old Testament contains 39 (Protestant), 46 (Catholic), or more (Orthodox and other) books, divided, very broadly, into the Pentateuch (Torah), the historical books, the "wisdom" books and the prophets.

The first step in assembling the Bible involves the 39 books of the Old Testament, also referred to as the Hebrew Bible. Beginning with Moses, who wrote the first five books of the Bible, these books were written over the centuries by prophets and leaders. By the time of Jesus and his disciples, the Hebrew Bible had already been established as 39 books. This was what Jesus meant when he referred to "the Scriptures."
