1.A Lion lay asleep in the forest, his great head resting on his paws. A timid little Mouse came upon him unexpectedly, and in her fright and haste to get away, ran across the Lion's nose. Roused from his nap, the Lion laid his huge paw angrily on the tiny creature to kill her.

2. "Spare me!" begged the poor Mouse. "Please let me go and some day I will surely repay you."The Lion was much amused to think that a Mouse could ever help him. But he was generous and finally let the Mouse go.

3.Some days later, while stalking his prey in the forest, the Lion was caught in the toils of a hunter's net. Unable to free himself, he filled the forest with his angry roaring. The Mouse knew the voice and quickly found the Lion struggling in the net. 5. what is the theme and plot of the short story of the lion and the mouse fable aesops fables​


Answer:

The moral of the story is that mercy brings its reward and that there is no being so small that it cannot help a greater. Later English versions reinforce this by having the mouse promise to return the lion's favor, to its sceptical amusement.

Summary of the Fable "The Lion and the Mouse" in Five Sentences

Here is a sample summary of the fable "The Lion and the Mouse" in five sentences:

There was a day when a sleeping lion was run over his nose by a mouse which disturbed his sleep. The lion was angry but the mouse said sorry with the promise that the latter would help him one day. However, the lion wasn't convinced as the mouse is too small; the mouse eventually ran away. A year later, the lion was trapped by a net while walking in the jungle and he roared out for help which was heard by the mouse.The mouse came to free him by chewing the net while the lion realizes that, although little, the mouse was kind.

Narrative Elements in the Summary of the Fable "The Lion and the Mouse"

Below are the narrative elements one could get from the summary of the fable "The Lion and the Mouse":

CharacterLionMouse

SettingOnly the jungle was mentioned

Conflict The lion did not believe that the mouse would help him as the latter is only little. The lion was trapped.

Plot (refer to the summary above) Introduction - 1st sentenceRising action - 2ndConflict - 3rd & 4thFalling action - 4th & 5thResolution - 5th

ThemeThe story teaches about not judging others' character based on what they look like on their appearance."The Lion and the Mouse" also tells about compassion and forgiveness.
