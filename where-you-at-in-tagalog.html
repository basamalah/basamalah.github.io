Explanation:

All humans are naturally creative, especially when creativity is viewed as a problem-solving ability. To put it another way, creativity is sparked when a problem needs to be solved. When viewed as a problem-solving act, creativity can be viewed as a skill—rather than an inborn talent or natural "gift"—that can be taught as well as learned. Every day, we are required to solve problems, from performing mundane tasks to carrying out complex projects. The good news is that we can always improve our problem-solving and creative-thinking abilities, even if we don't consider ourselves artists or "creatives."
