21. step pattern and count of waltz​


Answer:

Waltz is a smooth dance, traveling around the line of dance. It is characterized primarily by its rise & fall action. The shoulders move smoothly, parallel with the floor, not up and down.

three beats

All Waltzes have three beats in a measure. That means that when you are looking at a sheet of music for a Waltz (example below), the top number at the very left of the staff will be a three.

Six steps make up the basic box step, which will be repeated as the couple makes their way around the room. This will be two full counts of one-two-three. Leader steps forward with his left foot. Follower steps back with her right.

27. what is the basic step pattern of waltz​


Answer:

The basic step for waltz is a box step. It's named after a pattern it creates on the floor (box or square) and forms the foundation of the dance. A box step can be divided into two parts - a forward half box and a backward half box.

28. Step pattern used in Waltz Dance.​


Answer:

The basic box step pattern uses three counts - slow, quick, quick, which is repeated twice to create the box step.

Explanation:

The follower performs the opposite – she starts with the right foot and executes a backward half box, followed by a forward half box. The basic box step pattern uses three counts - slow, quick, quick, which is repeated twice to create the box step. Timing is 1,2,3,1,2,3 or 1,2,3,4,5,6.
