7. ways to address global inequality​


Answer:

Today, more than ever, the key to reversing this inequality trend lies clearly in improving the employment situation. Unemployment during the crisis has reached unprecedented levels. We currently have close to 49 million people unemployed in the OECD area, nearly 14 million more than before the crisis. The young have been particularly affected with 16.5% of them out of work in the OECD. This rate even surpasses 50% in countries such as Greece and Spain. Look at Italy: your unemployment rate is 11.5%, while youth unemployment currently stands at 38.4%!

We need to urgently focus on creating more and better jobs and on offering real career prospects for these youth. We must, for example, focus on up-skilling the workforce and removing barriers to higher labour force participation of women; thereby strengthening gender equality.

The provision of public services such as education and health is also essential in reducing inequality. Improving the quality of these services is therefore crucial. With PISA, the OECD provides a powerful tool to assess the quality of education and the experience of 34 OECD member states and 37 partner countries to improve on it.

The reform of tax and benefit policies is also central to our goals. Over the last two decades, many countries moved away from highly progressive income tax rates and net wealth taxes. Viable and effective policy avenues do exist, such as: raising marginal tax rates on the highest income brackets; improving tax compliance; eliminating tax exemptions; and reassessing the role of property and wealth taxes.

Inequalities are threatening the viability of our economies and eroding social trust. People are increasingly calling into question the strength of the “social compact”. Re-igniting growth and putting people back to work is our utmost priority, but this has to be implemented and achieved in a sustainable and inclusive manner. To achieve this, we must ensure that the young receive the job security they deserve; that our fiscal policies ensure that everyone is paying their fair share; that gender equality in the workplace is never questioned; and that the benefits of economic growth do trickle down!

12. Explain the concept of global inequality​


What is Global Inequality?

    International disparity refers to the notion of inequality between nations. This can be compared to the global disparity of people across countries. This could be due to economic disparities between countries. As well as disparities in medical treatment and education.

Causes of Global Inequality

    Historical cycles such as revolutions, industrialisation, imperialism as well as current trade structures, financial systems and global politics had an effect on inequalities between governments, individuals and classes within countries.

Effects of Global Inequality

    Products of income inequality, scholars have shown, include higher rates of health and social issues, lower rates of social goods, lower population-wide satisfaction and pleasure, and even lower levels of economic growth as human capital is ignored for high-end consumption.

How Global Inequality Occurs in our Society?

    Inequality arises where a person's place in the social hierarchy is related to different access to services and relies primarily on inequalities in income. ... Though stratification is most generally identified with socio-economic class, society is often stratified by identity such as race and gender.

Examples of Global Inequality

Access to healthcare. Economy.  Political stability.  Basic human rights and equality.  Geography.  Education and access to information.  Access to water, food, and sanitation facilities.

26. reaction paper to global inequalities​


Answer:

Global inequalities are a major issue in the world today, with significant differences in livelihoods, power, and opportunities between people in different countries. In many ways, it is a result of economic and political systems that uphold power for certain individuals and groups at the expense of others.

Global inequalities result in issues such as poverty, lack of access to education and healthcare, and limitations on human rights. It also causes problems in relationships between countries and within countries, and can lead to conflict and turmoil.

To address global inequalities, action is needed from individuals, organizations, and governments. We must work together to improve economic and political systems to provide a more equitable opportunity for all people. We must also work together to strengthen and develop programs and agencies that aid and empower communities affected by global inequalities. Through these actions and collaboration, we can address and solve the problem of global inequalities.
