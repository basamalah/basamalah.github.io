4. epekto ng Kakulangan ng Pasilidad sa Pag aaral ng mga Estudyante pa help yung chapter 2 rrl wala kasi kami mahanap​


Ang kakulangan ng pasilidad sa pag-aaral ay maaaring magdulot ng iba't ibang epekto sa mga estudyante. Narito ang ilan sa mga epekto nito:

1. Kakulangan sa kagamitan - Kung kulang ang mga kagamitan tulad ng libro, computer, projector, at iba pa, maaaring mahirap para sa mga estudyante na maunawaan ang mga konsepto at magawa ang kanilang mga gawain.

2. Kakulangan sa espasyo - Kung kulang ang espasyo sa silid-aralan, maaaring magdulot ito ng siksikan at hindi komportableng kapaligiran para sa mga estudyante. Ito ay maaaring makaapekto sa kanilang konsentrasyon at pag-aaral.

3. Kakulangan sa mga guro - Kung kulang ang mga guro, maaaring hindi maibigay ang sapat na atensyon at tulong sa mga estudyante. Ito ay maaaring magdulot ng pagkabigo sa pag-unawa ng mga estudyante sa mga konsepto at maaaring magdulot ng pagkabigo sa kanilang pag-aaral.

4. Kakulangan sa mga pasilidad sa kalusugan - Kung kulang ang mga pasilidad sa kalusugan tulad ng mga clinic at first aid kit, maaaring magdulot ito ng panganib sa kalusugan ng mga estudyante. Ito ay maaaring magdulot ng pagkakasakit at pagkabigo sa kanilang pag-aaral.

Sa kabuuan, ang kakulangan ng pasilidad sa pag-aaral ay maaaring magdulot ng pagkabigo sa pag-aaral ng mga estudyante. Kaya't mahalaga na maglaan ng sapat na pondo at suporta upang mapabuti ang mga pasilidad sa mga paaralan.

This article focuses on the development of mentalizing communities as a way to prevent bullying in schools. The authors discuss the Peaceful Schools experiment, which aimed to reduce bullying by creating a supportive and nurturing school environment. The article also provides strategies for identifying and managing bullies in schools.

This book provides a comprehensive overview of bullying in schools and strategies for prevention and intervention. The author, Dan Olweus, is a pioneer in the field of bullying prevention and has conducted extensive research on the topic. The book covers topics such as the prevalence of bullying, the impact on victims and bullies, and effective prevention and intervention strategies.

Cross, D., Shaw, T., Hearn, L., Epstein, M., Monks, H., Lester, L., & Thomas, L. (2009). Australian Covert Bullying Prevalence Study (ACBPS). Child Family Community Australia, 25, 1-28.
This study focuses on the prevalence of covert bullying in Australian schools and provides recommendations for prevention and intervention. The authors discuss the need for a whole-school approach to bullying prevention, including education and training for teachers, parents, and students. The study also emphasizes the importance of taking action against bullying, including developing clear policies and procedures and providing support for victims.

Answer:

The decision to choose a strand in Senior High School, such as Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM), is often influenced by several factors, which can be traced back to Junior High School. Generally speaking, students' experiences in their Junior High years can significantly impact their perspective in choosing a career pathway. Here are some of the reasons that influence students in selecting the ABM strand:

Firstly, students who demonstrate good performance in Mathematics, Economics, and Business-related subjects during Junior High are likely to choose the ABM strand. These subjects provide basic knowledge and skills needed in the strand, making it easy for them to excel in ABM's curriculum and help them prepare for their future business career.

Secondly, career awareness activities in Junior High play an essential role in decision-making for the ABM strand. Career talks, workshops, seminars, and counseling sessions provide opportunities for Junior High students to learn more about different careers and industries, including the ABM strand. Exposure to the business industry at this stage can help students solidify their interests in the industry and encourage them to pursue it later on.

Moreover, guidance and advice from parents, relatives, and friends who work in the business industry can influence students in choosing the ABM strand. Experience and knowledge from people who work in the industry provide a practical perspective on the profession's challenges and opportunities, which can help students make informed decisions.

Another factor that affects students' ABM strand decisions is the availability of learning materials and resources. Junior High institutions that offer comprehensive learning resources that cater to ABM-related subjects can positively impact students' interests in the strand. Accessible materials and a supportive learning environment can enhance students' learning experiences and help them develop their skills in the business industry.

Lastly, personal interest and passion are critical factors that influence students' ABM strand decisions. Junior High students who have a natural talent in managing finances or are into entrepreneurship are likely to choose the ABM strand. Such interests and passions can drive them to pursue careers in the business industry.

In conclusion, Junior High School experiences, such as performance in relevant subjects, career awareness activities, guidance, access to learning materials, and personal interest, play an essential role in influencing students' ABM strand decision. These factors can help students determine if the ABM strand is the right fit for their interests, skills, and career aspirations.

16. How does an RRL for quantitative differ from an RRL for qualitative?​


Answer:

Qualitative Research: an operational description

Purpose: explain; gain insight and understanding of phenomena through intensive collection and study of narrative data

Approach: inductive; value-laden/subjective; holistic, process-oriented

Hypotheses: tentative, evolving; based on the particular study

Lit. Review: limited; may not be exhaustive

Setting: naturalistic, when and as much as possible

Sampling: for the purpose; not necessarily representative; for in-depth understanding

Measurement: narrative; ongoing

Design and Method: flexible, specified only generally; based on non-intervention, minimal disturbance, such as historical, ethnographic, or case studies

Data Collection: document collection, participant observation, informal interviews, field notes

Data Analysis: raw data is words/ ongoing; involves synthesis

Data Interpretation: tentative, reviewed on ongoing basis, speculative

The Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM) strand is a popular choice among senior high school (SHS) students in the Philippines. Researchers have conducted studies to identify the reasons that influence incoming senior high school students in choosing this strand.

According to a study conducted by Garcia (2019), one of the primary reasons that influence incoming seniors high school students in choosing the ABM strand is the potential it holds for future job opportunities. The students recognize that the strand will provide a foundation for pursuing higher education in business management, accounting, finance, and entrepreneurship, among others. The students believe that graduating from the ABM strand will prepare them to become successful in the business sector.

Another reason identified in the study is the interest of incoming senior high school students in the subject. Students who have an inclination towards accountancy, business, and management are likely to choose the ABM strand. They believe that the ABM strand will help them develop their skills and achieve career aspirations.

In another study conducted by Gonzales (2018), peer influence was identified as a factor that affects the decisions of incoming senior high school students in choosing the ABM strand. The students tend to follow the recommendations of friends or family members who have had previous experiences in the ABM field.

Furthermore, social media trends and advertisements also play a significant role in the decision-making process of incoming senior high school students. They are influenced by the images of successful businesses, job opportunities, and prestige that the ABM strand represents in the industry.

26. is there a similarity between traditional rrl and systematic rrl​


Answer:

Traditional reviews

Authors: One or more authors usually experts in the topic of interest

Study protocol: No study protocol

Research question: Broad to specific question, hypothesis not stated

Search strategy: No detailed search strategy, search is probably conducted using keywords

Sources of literature: Not usually stated and non-exhaustive, usually well-known articles. Prone to publication bias

Selection criteria: No specific selection criteria, usually subjective. Prone to selection bias

Critical appraisal: Variable evaluation of study quality or method

Synthesis: Often qualitative synthesis of evidence

Conclusions: Sometimes evidence based but can be influenced by author’s personal belief

Reproducibility: Findings cannot be reproduced independently as conclusions may be subjective

Update: Cannot be continuously updated

Systematic reviews

Authors: Two or more authors are involved in good quality systematic reviews, may comprise experts in the different stages of the review

Study protocol: Written study protocol which includes details of the methods to be used

Research question: Specific question which may have all or some of PICO components (Population, Intervention, Comparator, and Outcome). Hypothesis is stated

Search strategy: Detailed and comprehensive search strategy is developed

Sources of literature: List of databases, websites and other sources of included studies are listed. Both published and unpublished literature are considered

Selection criteria: Specific inclusion and exclusion criteria

Critical appraisal: Rigorous appraisal of study quality

Synthesis: Narrative, quantitative or qualitative synthesis

Conclusions: Conclusions drawn are evidence based

Reproducibility: Accurate documentation of method means results can be reproduced

Update: Systematic reviews can be periodically updated to include new evidence
