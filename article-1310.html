ACCELERATION

Problem:

A 214 kg boat is sinking in the ocean. The force of gravity that draws the boat down is partially offset by the buoyant force of the water, so that the net unbalanced force on the boat is 1310 N. What is the acceleration of the boat?

Given:

f=1,310 N

m=214 kg

a=?

Solution:

a=f/m

a=1,310/214

a=6.121 m/s²

Hence, the acceleration of the boat is 6.121 m/s²

29. A pipe is observed to resonant at three successive frequencies 1310 Hz, 1834 Hz, and 2358 Hz. Determine the best model for the pipe, namely, is the pipe closed at one end or open at both ends? Justify your answer with both sentences and equations. Find the fundamental frequency of the pipe. Find the length of the pipe. Assume the speed of sound in air to be 343 m/s.


Answer:

[tex]L=0.327m, f_{1}=262Hz,[/tex], the pipe is closed at one end.

Step by step explanation:

The pipe is resonating thanks to a standing wave inside of it.

The frequencies, the pipe can resonate in, are given by the formulas:

1. [tex]f_{n}=nf_{1}, n=1,2,3,...[/tex] the harmonics in a pipe with both ends open or both ends closed.

2. [tex]f_{n}=(2n-1)f_{1}, n=1,2,3,...[/tex] the harmonics in a pipe with one end open and one closed(doesn't matter which).

where [tex]f_{1}[/tex] is the fundamental frequency.

Now we will test the values of the given frequencies and verify through trial and error:

We will choose the second case where the values given by the formula are:

[tex]f_{2}=3f_{1}[/tex] ⇔[tex]f_{1}=f_{2}/3[/tex]

[tex]f_{3}=5f_{1}[/tex] ⇔[tex]f_{1}=f_{3}/5[/tex]

[tex]f_{4}=7f_{1}[/tex] ⇔[tex]f_{1}=f_{4}/7[/tex]

etc...

For [tex]n=3,4,5[/tex] and we get:

[tex]1310/5=262,1834/7=262,2358/9=262[/tex]

So all of them are equal to the same number which verifies that:

The pipe is closed at one end.

The fundamental frequency is [tex]f_{1}=262Hz[/tex]

And the frequencies given are: [tex]f_{3}=1310Hz,f_{4}=1834Hz,f_{5}=2358Hz[/tex]


Now, we want to find the length of the pipe:

The formula for the pipe with one open end is:

L=(2n-1)λ/4, n=1,2,3,...

For n=3, L=5λ/4, where λ here is given by the wave equation and the respective frequency:

u=λf⇔λ=u/f

L=5λ/4 ⇔L=5u/(4f)=5*343/(4*1310)=0.327m


Note:

We can try and test different harmonics, different models of the pipe, and find the answer at some point. A faster choice can be made through observation of the given values or by intuition.
