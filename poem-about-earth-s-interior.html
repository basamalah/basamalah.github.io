Through the studies of seismic waves from earthquakes, scientist identify the different layers of the interior of the earth. These waves pass through the earth which gives us vital information about the nature and composition of the internal structure of the earth. As seismic waves pass through the Earth, they are refracted, or bent, like rays of light bend when they pass through a a glass prism.

Because the speed of the seismic wavesl depends on density, we can use the travel-time of seismic waves to map change in density with depth, and show that the Earth is composed of several layers. Waves also enables us to know the composition of the layers of the earth. P-waves and S-waves vary in terms of velocity and effect on rock masses where they pass through. P-waves or primary waves are so called because they are the first to be recorded in a seismograph. Their velocity depends on the elastic property of the rock through which they travel.

20. facts about earth interior


The Earth's interior is composed of four layers, three solid and one liquid—not magma but molten metal, nearly as hot as the surface of the sun. The deepest layer is a solid iron ball, about 1,500 miles (2,400 kilometers) in diameter. Although this inner core is white hot, the pressure is so high the iron cannot melt.
