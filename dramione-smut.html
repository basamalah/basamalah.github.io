11. Which of the following is the South African statesman who introduced the term "holism" in a book he wrote in 1926? Select one: a. Donald Davidson b. Ludwig Wittgenstein c. Gottlob Frege d. Jan Smuts


Answer:

Holism

Holism is a theory which states that 'systems must be viewed not only as parts, but as a whole.' This was an idea introduced in a book of a South American statesman, Jan Smuts, titled Holism and Evolution in 1626.

It came from the Greek word holos which means, whole, all, or entire. It is an idea that directly opposes the idea of atomism. Where atomism is a theory that a whole must be broken down into parts to study and analyze its relation to other parts and function in the whole, holism states that parts can only be understood with its relation to the whole.
