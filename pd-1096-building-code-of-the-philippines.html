PD No. 1511, also known as the Philippine Environmental Policy, was issued on June 11, 1978, with the aim of integrating environmental concerns into the country's development plans and activities. Here are sections 1-4 of the policy:

Section 1: Declaration of Policies and Objectives

This section declares the government's policy to attain and maintain a balance between economic growth and environmental protection. It recognizes the need to preserve the country's natural resources and to promote their rational and judicious use for the benefit of the present and future generations. The policy also recognizes the need to promote public awareness and participation in environmental protection.

Section 2: Definition of Terms

This section defines various terms used in the policy, such as "environment," "pollution," "conservation," "preservation," "waste," and "natural resources."

Section 3: Creation of the National Environmental Protection Council (NEPC)

This section creates the NEPC, which is tasked to coordinate and monitor the implementation of the policy. The NEPC is composed of representatives from various government agencies, as well as from the private sector and non-government organizations.

Section 4: Powers and Functions of the NEPC

This section outlines the powers and functions of the NEPC, which include:

Formulating and recommending policies, plans, and programs for environmental protection and management;

Conducting research and studies on environmental issues and problems;

Monitoring and evaluating the implementation of environmental laws and regulations;

Promoting public awareness and participation in environmental protection;

Recommending measures to prevent or mitigate environmental damage and pollution;

Coordinating and collaborating with other government agencies, as well as with the private sector and non-government organizations, in the implementation of environmental programs and projects;

Providing technical assistance and training to government agencies, private sector organizations, and communities on environmental management and protection;

Establishing and maintaining a comprehensive information system on environmental management and protection.

These sections are just the beginning of the Philippine Environmental Policy, which goes on to detail various measures and programs aimed at protecting the country's environment.

20. Ano ang PD 1586 o Philippine environmental impact statement system


Presidential Decree No. 1586


ESTABLISHING AN ENVIRONMENT STATEMENT SYSTEM, INCLUDING OTHER ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT RELATED MEASURES AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES

WHEREAS, the pursuit of a comprehensive and integrated environmental protection program necessitates the establishment and institutionalization of a system whereby the exigencies of socio-economic undertakings can be reconciled with the requirements of environmental quality;

WHEREAS, the regulatory requirements of Environmental Impact Statements and Assessments instituted in pursuit of this national environmental protection program have to be worked into their full regulatory and procedural details in a manner consistent with the goals of the program.

NOW, THEREFORE, I, Ferdinand E. Marcos, President of the Philippines, by virtue of the powers vested in me by the Constitution do hereby order and declare:

SECTION 1. Policy. – It is hereby declared the policy of the State to attain and maintain a rational and orderly balance between socio-economic growth and environmental protection.

SEC. 2. Environmental Impact Statement System. – There is hereby established an Environmental Impact Statement System founded and based on the environmental impact statement required, under Section 4 of Presidential Decree No. 1151, of all agencies and instrumentalities of the national government, including government-owned or controlled corporations, as well as private corporations, firms and entities, for every proposed project and undertaking which significantly affect the quality of the environment.

SEC. 3. Determination of Lead Agency. – The Minister of Human Settlements or his designated representative is hereby authorized to name the lead agencies referred to in Section 4 of Presidential Decree No. 1151 which shall have jurisdiction to undertake the preparation of the necessary environmental impact statements on declared environmentally critical projects and areas. All Environmental Impact Statements shall be submitted to the National Environmental Protection Council for review and evaluation.

SEC. 4. Presidential Proclamation of Environmentally Critical Areas and Projects. – The President of the Philippines may, on his own initiative or upon recommendation of the National Environmental Protection Council, by proclamation declare certain projects, undertakings or areas in the country as environmentally critical. No person, partnership or corporation shall undertake or operate any such declared environmentally critical project or area without first securing an Environmental Compliance Certificate issued by the President or his duly authorized representatives. For the proper management of said critical project(s) or area(s), the President may by his proclamation reorganize such government offices, agencies, institutions, corporations or instrumentalities including the re-alignment of government personnel, and their specific functions and responsibilities.

Section 1 states that it is the policy of the State to "protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthful ecology in accord with the rhythm and harmony of nature". Section 2 declares that it is also the policy of the State to "attain and maintain a rational and orderly balance between socio-economic growth and environmental protection". Section 3 provides for the creation of an Environmental Impact Statement System which requires all agencies or instrumentalities of the government or any private entity intending to undertake projects or activities which may significantly affect the quality of the environment to file an environmental impact statement. Section 4 provides for penalties for violations of any provisions of this decree.
