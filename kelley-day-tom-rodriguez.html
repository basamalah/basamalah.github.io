5. If the Rodriguez family used 520 kilowatt-hours of electricity in 12 days, how many kilowatt-hours should they use in 6 days at the same usage rate?​


Answer:

To find out how many kilowatt-hours the Rodriguez family should use in 6 days at the same usage rate, we need to divide the total number of kilowatt-hours used in 12 days by the number of days and then multiply that number by 6.

520 kilowatt-hours ÷ 12 days = 43.333 kilowatt-hours per day

43.333 kilowatt-hours per day x 6 days = 260 kilowatt-hours

So, at the same usage rate, the Rodriguez family should use 260 kilowatt-hours of electricity in 6 days.
