18. True or False: When treating wounds, it is essential to clean the wound with gentle soap and water.


The statement "When treating wounds, it is essential to clean the wound with gentle soap and water." is true. To avoid bacteria and infection, it is proper to clean around the wound with gentle soap and running water. It is also essential to make sure that your hands are clean and properly-washed before touching the wound. In connection with this, other details that are related to cleaning the wound is indicated below.

If someone gets a wound, it is essential to attend to it and clean it properly. This is to prevent dirt, bacteria and infection. Here are some notes to consider:

Always wash your hands thoroughly before and after cleaning the wound. Avoid touching the wound except during cleaning. Avoid scatching the wound. When cleaning the wound, rinse the wound with running tap water as this will lower the risk of infection. Use a gentle soap to wash around the wound. Do not use hydrogen peroxide or iodine to avoid irritation. It is better if you apply a thin layer of petroleum jelly or antibiotic. This will prevent scarring because it will make the surface of the wound moist. Get a tetanus shot if you haven't taken one in the previous five years and the wound came from dirty or rusty material.

Considering the steps indicated above, we can then conclude that the statement "When treating wounds, it is essential to clean the wound with gentle soap and water." is true.

21. When is the right time for a first aider to loosen the tightness of a bandage? a. if the wound starts to swell b. if the wound becomes reddish c. if the wound becomes bigger d. if the wound is so painful


Answer:

D. If it is too painful

Explanation:

A first aider should not do any more harm or discomfort to a patient. If the wound is too painful, it means that the dressing is pressing too much on the skin activating the pain receptors. If this happens, the circulation might be cut off and it would lead to the death of the body part where blood flow is restricted. Hence it is important that the bandage is only of the right tension.

If the wound starts to swell or become reddish, it could mean that an infection is developing. It is important that the patient is brought to the nearest hospital right away as any break in the skin is an open invitation for bacteria and other pathogens.
