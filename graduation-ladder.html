10. a ladder makes an angle of 52° with the floor. if the top of the ladder is 5 metres from the floor what is the length of the ladder?​


✒️TRIGONOMETRY

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \:\: \rm \approx 6.34 \: meters [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

By this, we can represent the angles and the sides of a right triangle.

52° is the referred angle.5m height is opposite to the refered angle.The ladder's length is the hypotenuse.

Using trigonometry, we can solve for the hypotenuse by using the sine ratio. Let x be the length of the ladder

[tex] \rm \sin \theta = \frac{opposite}{hypotenuse} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sin 52\degree = \frac{5m}{x} \\ [/tex]

[tex] x = \frac{5m}{\sin 52\degree} \\ [/tex]

[tex] x \approx 6.34m[/tex]

Therefore, the length of the ladder is approximately 6.34 meters

28. A ladder is leaning against a wall. The foot of the ladder is 6.5 feet from the wall. The ladder makes an angle of 74° with the level ground. How high up the wall does the ladder reach?


Answer:

The height of the wall that the ladder reaches can be calculated using trigonometry. The ladder, the ground and the wall form a right triangle. The distance between the foot of the ladder and the wall is the adjacent side of the angle between the ladder and the ground. The height of the wall that the ladder reaches is the opposite side of this angle. We can use the tangent function to find the length of the opposite side: tan(74°) = opposite / 6.5. Solving for opposite, we get opposite = 6.5 * tan(74°) ≈ 23.4 feet. So, the ladder reaches approximately 23.4 feet up the wall.

We can use trigonometry to solve this problem. Let's call the height up the wall that the ladder reaches "x". Then, we can use the sine function to relate the angle of 74° to the opposite side (x) and the hypotenuse (the length of the ladder).
