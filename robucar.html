5. 6. What part of the flower is the stigma, and how does it help in the reproduction of a plant? Stigma isA. the stalk that carries foods and nutrients to the leaves, roots and fruitsB. the male reproductive structure that protects the entire flower from the insectsC. the expanded and lobular structure at the tip of the filament that produces pollenD. the female reproductive structure on which the pollen grains land during pollinationPlease help


Answer:

C

Explanation:

The stigma is the sticky knob at the top of the pistil. It is attached to the long, tubelike structure called the style. The style leads to the ovary that contains the female egg cells called ovules. The male parts are called stamens and usually surround the pistil.

17. 5. Locating the position of trees, roads and buildings in the orchard are being established. toylau bus robur​


Answer:

Lay outing / Layout

Explanation:

Layout of an orchard is very important. Layout means fixing the position of trees,

roads, buildings, etc. in an orchard being planned. Systems of layout refer to the design of

planting the trees. It is desirable to have the trees planted in a systematic way because: (1)

orchard operations like intercultural and irrigation are carried out easily; (2) it makes possible

the distribution of areas equally for each tree; (3) it results in maximum utilization of an area

according to different kinds of trees; and (4) it makes supervision more easy and effective.
