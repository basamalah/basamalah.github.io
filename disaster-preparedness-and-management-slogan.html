14. Discuss the main goal of disaster awareness, preparedness, and management.


The main goal of disaster awareness, preparedness, and management is to minimize the damage or risk that occurs due to a disaster as much as possible. In addition, it also has the goal to minimize the number of victims of a disaster. Therefore, the focus of the steps taken from disaster awareness, preparedness, and management is on the steps taken before and during a disaster.

Examples of awareness, preparedness, and management steps:

Prepare tools that can detect as early as possible the possibility of a disaster.Provide outreach to the community about how to first protect themselves and evacuate in the event of a disaster.Build facilities or buildings that can be more resistant to disastersProviding information and early warning about possible disasters and others steps.

19. is disaster awareness preparedness and management significant​


Answer:

Disaster preparedness refers to measures taken to prepare for and reduce the effects of disasters. That is, to predict and, where possible, prevent disasters, mitigate their impact on vulnerable populations, and respond to and effectively cope with their consequences.


20. Why disaster preparedness and management important?​


Answer:

Disaster preparedness refers to the preventive measures taken to reduce the severity of a disaster's effects. Disaster preparedness has the potential save the maximum number of lives and property during a disaster, and it aims to return the affected populations to normalcy as quickly as possible.

22. Discuss the main goal of disaster awareness, preparedness, and management.


The Main goal of disaster awareness, preparedness, and management.

Safeguard and make vital materials, supplies, and equipment available to ensure records' safety and recovery from predictable disasters.

Reduce the risk of disasters caused by human error, deliberate destruction, and building or equipment failures. Be better prepared to recover from a major natural catastrophe.

Preparing for calamities saves numerous lives, speeds up people's rehabilitation , and saves money. The IFRC supports National Societies to consistently develop their local preparation and response capacity—ultimately avoiding and minimizing the consequences of disasters on communities.

Disaster management refers to the effective administration of resources and responsibilities that will aid in decreasing the effects of the disaster. It requires a well-planned plan of action so we may make effective attempts to decrease the hazards created by the disaster to a minimum.

By involving youth in disaster preparedness and recovery efforts, youth-serving agencies can help not only increase youths' awareness of particular hazards but can also enhance the chance that they openly discuss how to adequately protect their families and loved ones and understand how to seek help.
