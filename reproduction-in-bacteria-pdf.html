6. mode of asexual reproduction of bacteria, fern, yeast, starfish, kataka-taka?


A kind of reproduction wherein an individual only arises from a single organism or individual and inherits what that parent organism has is called asexual reproduction. This reproduction has a several types or modes.

Binary fission is the mode of reproduction for bacteria.

Fragmentation is the mode of asexual reproduction of ferns where a certain part of them breaks away from the main plant to grow into a new one.

Budding is the type of asexual reproduction of yeast wherein a small tuber grows from the parent cell and the parent cells’ nucleus divides and goes to the daughter cell which is the small tube or bud.

For starfish (seastars), their mode of asexual reproduction is called fission of autonomy of arms. With this, seastars can reproduce clones in which an arm breaks and can live alone and can grow its own arms and disc. For kataka-taka, their mode of asexual reproduction is called vegetative reproduction, in which the parent kataka-taka plant grows new plants through its vegetative part – leaves.

22. Which of the following types of asexual reproduction would bacteria reproduce? a. Budding b. Fission d. Regeneration d. Vegetative reproduction


Answer:

A. Budding

Explanation:

Another type of asexual reproduction is called budding. Budding is when a new organism, or the offspring, grows off the side of the adult through a part called a bud. The new baby will stay attached to the original adult until it reaches maturity at which point they break off and become its own independent organism. A single adult can have many buds and many offspring at the same time.

Both unicellular organisms, like yeast, and multicellular organisms, like hydra, can undergo budding. Again, the offspring are clones of the parent unless some sort of mutation happens during the copying of the DNA or cell reproduction.
