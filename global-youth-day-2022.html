26. how the global initiatives specifically reduce the use of alcohol and tobacco among the youth? ​


Answer:

There are several global initiatives that aim to reduce the use of alcohol and tobacco among the youth. These initiatives include:

The World Health Organization's (WHO) Framework Convention on Tobacco Control (FCTC) is a global treaty that aims to reduce the use of tobacco among the youth by implementing measures such as increasing taxes on tobacco products, banning advertising and sponsorship of tobacco products, and requiring graphic health warnings on cigarette packages.

The Global Youth Tobacco Survey (GYTS) is a tool used by the WHO and other organizations to monitor the use of tobacco among the youth. The GYTS collects data on the prevalence of tobacco use, attitudes towards tobacco, and exposure to secondhand smoke.

The Global Alcohol Policy Alliance (GAPA) is a coalition of organizations that aims to reduce the use of alcohol among the youth by advocating for evidence-based alcohol policies, such as increasing taxes on alcohol and limiting the availability of alcohol.

The Global Initiative to Reduce the Harmful Use of Alcohol (GIRHA) is an initiative led by the WHO that aims to reduce the harmful use of alcohol through a combination of policy, advocacy and technical support.

Many countries also have their own initiatives and laws in place to reduce the use of alcohol and tobacco among the youth, such as increasing the legal age for purchasing these products and enforcing strict regulations on advertising and marketing.

In conclusion, there are various global initiatives and laws that aim to reduce the use of alcohol and tobacco among the youth. These initiatives include implementing measures such as increasing taxes on tobacco and alcohol products, banning advertising and sponsorship, and enforcing strict regulations on advertising and marketing. Additionally, organizations such as WHO and GAPA advocate for evidence-based policies and provide technical support to countries in order to reduce the harmful use of these products among the youth.

Among the many effects of globalization on the lives of young people, the UN identified four areas that are profoundly affected by this modern phenomenon. These are the distribution of employment opportunities, migration, youth culture and consumerism, and global citizenship and activism. Globalization has numerous consequences for youth cultures. The increase in media streams has resulted in global consumerism. Through television, music videos and movies, American and European-produced content is increasingly dominating entertainment around the world. It causes Filipinos to consume cheaper import products and their own products left unsold or sold at a much cheaper price. Globalization allows privatization of social institutions (e.g. schools, hospitals and other social centers) and services (e. g. water and light), that are much needed by the poor majority. In general, globalization decreases the cost of manufacturing. This means that companies can offer goods at a lower price to consumers.
