15. The ratio of the amount of money leo has to vero is 3:5. Leo gives half his money to vero , vero then gives £75 pounds back to leo. They now have the same amount of money. How much money did leo have at the start?


Leo:Vero = 3:5

Common factor: x

Leo's money: 3x

Vero's money: 5x

After giving money to each other:

Leo's money: 3x - 3x/2 + 75

Vero's money: 5x + 3x/2 - 75

New ratio  1:1  or equal amount of money

Solve  for the common factor, x:

3x - 3x/2 + 75 = 5x + 3x/2 - 75

Multiply each term by their LCD 2:

(2) [ 3x - 3x/2 + 75 = 5x + 3x/2 - 75] (2)

6x - 3x + 150 = 10x + 3x - 150

3x + 150 = 13x - 150

13x - 3x = 150 + 150

10x/10 = 300/10

x = 30

Substitute 30 to x to solve for the original amount of money:

Leo's money: 3(30) = £90

Vero's money: 5(30) = £150

ANSWER: At the start, Leo had £90 while Vero had £150.

Check:

When Leo gives half his money to Vero and Vero gives £75 pounds back to Leo, they have equal amount of money.

90 - (90/2) + 75 = 150 + (90/2) - 75

90 - 45 + 75 = 150 + 45 - 75

45 + 75 = 75 + 45

120 = 120  (True)

Original amount of money:

90:150 = 3:5

3:5 = 3:5  (True)   GCF: 30
