According to the Philippine Statistics Authority, as of 2015, more than 26% of the population lives below the poverty line. Another reason why the Philippines is considered a third-world nation is because of its low HDI ranking. There are several solutions that can be implemented in order to address the issues of poverty, low HDI ranking, and susceptibility to natural disasters. One solution is to increase investment in education. This can be done by increasing the budget for education, and by making education more accessible to all. Another solution is to increase investment in infrastructure. This can be done by building more roads, bridges, and schools. Lastly, the Philippines needs to strengthen its disaster preparedness and response capabilities.
