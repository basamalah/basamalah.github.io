4. A garden wall with a height of 3 meters and a length of 7 meters is to be constructed, how many Concrete Hollow Blocks (CHB) are to be ordered by the contractor if each hollow block has a dimension of 8 inches by 16 inches and a thickness of 4 inches?​


Answer: 255 concrete hollow blocks

Step-by-step explanation:

The first step is to find the area of the garden wall having a height of 3 meters and length of 7 meters

So, A = L×W = 7 meters × 3 meters

A = 21 square meters or m²

The next step is to find the area of the the concrete hollow block (CHB). We just need the length and width. The thickness is not important here in solving the area.

So, A = L×W = 8 inches × 16 inches = 128 square inches or in².

Then, we have to convert m² to in².

1 m² = 10,000 cm²

1 in² = 6.4516 cm²

So, let us convert 21 m².

21 m² × [100 cm/ 1 m]² × [1 in./2.54 cm]²

= 21 × 10,000 / 6.4516

= 32,550.0651 in².

We can finally divide this one to the area of the CHB.

32,550.0651 / 128 = 254.30 = 255

We need to round up.

Therefore, the contractor needs to buy 255 pieces of CHB for the garden wall.

9. If the distance of two post is 3 meters how many pieces of chb are needed for two layers of each chb is 0.40 meters long?​


Answer:

15 pcs of CHB

Step-by-step explanation:

Distance between 2 post: 3 m

Height of one CHB: 0.20 m

Since 2 layers is required, then height will be equal to 0.40 m.

By Area Method:

Area of Wall = Distance × Height

A = 3 × 0.40

Wall Area = 1.20 sq. m.

Multiply wall area to no. of CHB pcs per sq. m, which is equal to 12.5 pcs / sq. m.

TOTAL PCS OF CHB = 1.20 × 12.5

TOTAL NO. OF CHB = 15 PCS
