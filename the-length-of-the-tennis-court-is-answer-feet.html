Tennis Court Dimensions and Measurements

The quick answer for the size of the singles tennis court is 78 feet by 27 feet. Double, 78 feet  by 36 feet.  

The International Tennis Federation (ITF) shall determine the official dimensions and measurements of  tennis courts. Here are the dimensions for the regulation, the adult tennis court.

Singles Match Court Size

Single players use a narrower width for the size of a single play tennis court (designated by the sidelines  inside singles). The length of the court is the same for singles and doubles matches.Court length: 78 feet (23.77 meters) in length.  The width is different, though. Court width: 27 feet (8.23 metres) wide

Doubles Match Court Size

Double match rules allow players to use full-court width, all the way to the outer doubles sidelines.  The double court extends 4.5 feet beyond each side of the single court sidelines.  Court length: 78 feet (23.77 meters) in length.  Court width: 36 feet (10.97 meters) in width. At 36 ft wide, there are 9 feet more to cover than the singles court.

Service Area

The center service line divides the service area on either side of the net into two rectangles.  These areas are the space in which the ball served must land. The size of each area is the  same for both single and double matches.  Location of the service line: 21 feet (6.4 meters) from the net.  Size of service area: 13.5 feet wide x 21 feet long (4.1 meters wide x 6.4 meters long)

Tennis Net Size

The tennis net stretches across the court, dividing it equally. The net is attached to posts  on either side of the courthouse. Due to its weight, the center point of the net is about 6 inches lower than its height at the posts. The net posts are located 3 feet (.91 meters) outside the sideline for both single and double matches.  Tennis court net height at posts: 3 feet 6 inches high (1.07 metres). Tennis court net height in the center: 3 feet high (.91 meters).

22. the length of the rectangular basketball court is 6 feet less than twice its width and the area is equal to 165 square feet.​


Answer:

Length = 15.42 ft, width = 10.71 ft

Step-by-step explanation:

Area of a Rectangle

The area of a rectangle can be calculated by the formula:

A = l x w

where l is the length while w is the width.

In this problem, we are given the relationship of the length and the width and the area of the rectangle of 165 square feet. We need to calculate the length and the width of the rectangle.

Step 1: Translate the mathematical statement given into a working mathematical equation.

Statement: The length is 6 feet less than twice its width

Equation 1: l = 2w - 6

Step 2: Substitute the given value of the area and the derived equation 1 to the formula.

A = l x w

165 [tex]ft^{2}[/tex] = (2w - 6) x w

165 [tex]ft^{2}[/tex]  = 2[tex]w^{2}[/tex] - 6w

2[tex]w^{2}[/tex] - 6w - 165 [tex]ft^{2}[/tex] = 0

Step 3: Notice that our resulting equation is in a form of a quadratic expression. Use the quadratic formula to solve for the value of w.

w = [tex]\frac{-b +- \sqrt{b^{2} - 4ac } }{2a}[/tex]

where a = 2, b = -6 and c = -165

Substitute the given constants to the formula.

w = [tex]\frac{-(-6) + \sqrt{(-6)^{2} - (4 x 2 x -165) } }{2 x 2}[/tex]

w = 10.71 ft

w = [tex]\frac{-(-6) - \sqrt{(-6)^{2} - (4 x 2 x -165) } }{2 x 2}[/tex]

w = -7.7

We have two resulting values of w, we will use the positive root.

Step 4: Substitute the value of w to equation 1 to get the value of l.

l = 2w - 6

l = (2 x 10.71) - 6

l = 15.42 ft

Therefore, the length of the rectangle is 15.42 ft while the width is 10.71 ft.

What is tennis?

Tennis is a racket sports also known as lawn tennis. It can be played in two ways: singles or one-on-one or doubles, by teams of two players. The things needed for this sports are tennis rackets for each player, one rubber ball, and a net. This is played in a tennis court with dimensions 78 by 27 feet for singles match and 78 by 36 for a doubles match.

Why is it called tennis?

The word tennis comes from the French word "tenez" meaning "hold", "receive", or "take". In addition, the word racket is from the Arabic word "rakhat" which means the palm of the hand.
