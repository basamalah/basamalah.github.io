Drop a sheet of paper and a crumpled paper at the same time. Observe the objects.

What happened when you dropped the crumpled paper and the flat sheet of paper?

ANSWER: The crumpled paper falls fast while the flat sheet of paper falls more slowly than a crumpled one.

Which of the papers reach the floor first? Why?

ANSWER: The crumpled paper reach the floor first, Because the crumpled paper has a smaller surface area, and which is not affected by the air so it move's faster.

Why is that a sheet of paper falls more slowly than a crumpled one?

ANSWER: The sheet of paper falls more slowly than a crumpled one because of air resistance. The surface area of the sheet of paper is affected by the air and causes it to drop slowly.
