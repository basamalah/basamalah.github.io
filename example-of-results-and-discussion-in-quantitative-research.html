14. discuss the strengths of quantitative research​


Strengths of Quantitative Research

1. A larger sample size is possible with a quantitative approach.

It is easier to get an accurate generalized conclusion when you have the option to research a larger sample size for any hypothesis. Because the statistical analysis has more depth to analyze, the additional data you acquire from this effort provides the outcome more confidence.

2. Quantitative research allows you to get knowledge rapidly.

Researchers gather data for quantitative research in real-time scenarios so that statistical analysis can take place virtually instantly.

3. Randomized samples are used in quantitative research.

Personal bias might enter the data spectrum when research participants perceive a study is trying to attain a specific conclusion. As a means of manipulating the job, the answers offered on the supplied papers are either half-truths or outright lies.

4. When conducting quantitative research, it is easy to duplicate results.

When opinions are accepted as viable alternatives to facts, anything is conceivable. Quantitative research solves this difficulty by focusing solely on actual data.

5. Quantitative research can concentrate on a single fact or a sequence of facts.

Researchers can utilize the quantitative technique to concentrate on a specific fact in the overall population that they want to investigate. When a set of data points is particularly wanted within a population, this strategy is also useful.
