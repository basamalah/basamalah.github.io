25. The mouse deer has shorter for than its counterpart deer in the Arctic Region. What characteristics is exhibited in this situation


The mouse deer is much smaller in size and have shorter antlers than its arctic relatives because of evolution. Mammals that inhabit islands and dense jungles tend to become smaller as opposed to reptiles which grow larger. Tigers, bears, elephants, and rhinos that live in Sri Lanka, and Borneo and Sumatra are considered pigmies compared to their mainland counterparts, while Komodo dragons, Galapagos and Aldabra tortoises have become giants while evolving in isolation in their little islands.  

There is a benefit to being small in a dense jungle, running through the vines, branches, and leaves would be much quicker, which is what a mouse deer do. While the large antlers and size of the arctic variety of deer could protect it from wolves, cougars, and bears. So it is all about evolution. The near rodent dimension of a mouse deer is well suited for life in the jungle,  while a large animal like the reindeer, the elk, and the moose survives well in the Arctic region.

28. how is the deer ecosystem​


Answer:

Deer are seen as a "keystone species" for forests. This means that they can completely change an ecosystem through their behavior. ... Deer tend to prefer certain species of plants as food and they overgraze those species. When deer overgraze those species, they leave ecological niches open for other species of vegetation.
