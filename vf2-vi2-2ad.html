Problem:

Fred is playing with a ball on the top of a building but the ball fell and hits the ground after 2.6 seconds. What is final velocity of a ball just before it hits the ground and how high is the building?

Given Data:

Vi ( initial velocity ) = 0 m/s

t ( Time ) = 2.6 s

a ( acceleration (g) ) = 9.8 m/s²

Vf ( final velocity ) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can solve the problem using the formula ( a = Vf-Vf/t ), since Vi = 0 m/s,  we can use the formula Vf = at.

Vf = at

    = 9.8 m/s² × 2.6 s

    = 25.48 m/s

Answer:

Final velocity of a ball just before it hits the ground is 25.48 m/s

Solving for the Height of the Building

d = 1/2 (Vf + Vi)t

 = 1/2 ( 25.48 m/s + 0 m/s) × 2.6 s

  = 1/2 ( 25.48 m/s × 2.6 s)

  = 33.12 m

Answer:

The height of the building is 33.12 m
