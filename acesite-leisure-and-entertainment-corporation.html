10. Contrast the attitudes toward sport and other uses of leisure that were found in ancient Greece with those found in the Roman Empire. How did their philosophies differ, and how did the Roman philosophy lead to a weakening of that powerful nation? Could you compare between the approach to leisure and entertainment in ancient Rome and that in the present-day United States?​


Greek and Roman leisure.

Ancient Greece and Rome had different attitudes towards sports and leisure activities. In ancient Greece, sports were an integral part of daily life, and competitions were held regularly. The Greeks believed that physical fitness was as important as mental strength and that participation in sports promoted discipline and teamwork. They also viewed leisure activities as a time for reflection and intellectual pursuits, such as philosophical discussions and reading.

On the other hand, in ancient Rome, sports were used as a form of entertainment, and competitions were often held in arenas for public amusement. The Romans saw leisure time as an opportunity for indulgence in excess, such as extravagant banquets, drinking, and other forms of hedonistic pleasure.

The difference in philosophy between the Greeks and Romans can be attributed to their respective cultures and values. The Greeks valued individual excellence, while the Romans emphasized the collective glory of the empire. The Roman philosophy of "bread and circuses" - providing free food and entertainment to the masses - was a way to distract the populace from their problems and maintain control.

However, the Roman emphasis on entertainment and leisure led to a decline in the empire's military and economic power. The pursuit of pleasure and excess led to a lack of discipline and focus, weakening the nation from within.

In comparison, present-day United States has a diverse range of leisure activities that reflect modern cultural values. Sports remain an important part of American culture, and there is a strong emphasis on physical fitness and healthy competition. However, there is also a significant emphasis on entertainment, with a wide range of options available, from movies and television to video games and social media. The current approach to leisure and entertainment in the US seems to strike a balance between healthy activities and indulgence, with an emphasis on personal choice and individual preferences.

Olympic games

The Olympic Games, conducted every fourth year between 776 B.C.E. and 395 C.E. in honor of Zeus, the greatest god of Greek mythology, drew spectators from all around Greece. Running, discus throwing, and the long jump were among the events that drew large crowds.

Olympia

Olympia's athletic activities were the oldest and most prominent of Greece's four major athletic festivals. The games began in 776 B.C.E. and were conducted every four years until 776 B.C.E., but they were most likely held much earlier. The hero Herakles is credited with inventing the running races at Olympia to commemorate the completion of one of his twelve labors, according to Greek mythology.

The Games were held in honor of Zeus, whose most important sanctuary was Olympia. A statue of Zeus was used to provide sacrifices and gifts, and athletes took oaths to follow the laws. The games were publicized by heralds who traveled to all of the main Greek cities throughout the Mediterranean, and conflicts were prohibited during the year leading up to the Games.

what does leisure mean in the poem leisure by W.H Davies?William Henry Davies (1871 – 1940)

This fourteen line poem can be regarded as a sonnet comprising of seven rhyming couplets. It is a simple poem to memorise and people of a certain vintage may have come across this poem in their school days.

This is a poem about time and the use of time that use being defined as ‘Leisure’. Leisure implies taking time out from being busy. The iambic rhythm has been likened to a heartbeat and heartbeats are a measure of time. Also the repetition of the two syllables ‘no time’ in each line reinforces the passage of time.

The first couplet has become memorable to the extent that William Henry Davies is mainly known for this one poem. Each of these lines comprise of eight single syllable words (iambic tetrameter). Single syllable words have more strength in immediate mind absorption because they stand alone and start and finish quickly. This couplet poses the question on the use of time.
