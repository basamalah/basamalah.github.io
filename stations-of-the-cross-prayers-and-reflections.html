Recall a time when you were falsely accused of something? How did you feel?

Prayer: Lord, darkness had its hour but it could not over come you because….

Response: By your holy cross you have redeemed the world.

Prayer: Lord, the love you always showed to others won because….”

Answer:

1. Who invented the Stations of the Cross?

Saint Leonard

Their founder Saint Leonard held the Passion of Christ in special veneration and is said to have been the first person to receive stigmata. In 1217, St. Francis also founded the Custody of the Holy Land to guard and promote the devotion to holy places.

2. Where would you normally expect to find the pictures of the Stations of the Cross?

Instead, on this day, most Catholic churches recite the Stations of the Cross (these are normally found an pictures around the inside the walls of all churches).

3. Where is the first station of the cross?

As you walk through the souks in the Muslim Quarter, the first station is on your left. It is believed that it was here where Jesus was condemned to death. Continue on the Via Dolorosa until you reach the Ecce Homo Convent, location of the second station.

4. How long is Stations of the Cross?

The final stations of the crucifixion and burial are within the Church of the Holy Sepulchre. The route is in the Old City of Jerusalem. The Via Dolorosa, which means "way of sorrows," is about a half-mile long, or just under 0.8 kilometer.

5. Can you pray Stations of the Cross at home?

Yes, The ritual consists of 14 stations that walk you through the crucifixion, which you can pray over on your own, during a church service, or with a guide. There isn't a standard prayer for each station, but you can find many examples in guides or talk to your priest about example prayers.

28. make your own 14 stations of the cross with your own title that can be compared in the present time (kahit wala nang prayer)​


Answer:

Here are my 14 stations of the cross with titles that reflect contemporary issues and challenges:

1. Station of Displacement: Jesus is forced to flee his home and become a refugee. In the present time, this station reminds us of the millions of people around the world who are displaced from their homes due to war, conflict, and persecution.

2. Station of Injustice: Jesus is condemned to death despite being innocent. This station represents the ongoing struggle for justice and equality in our society, particularly for marginalized and oppressed communities.

3. Station of Betrayal: Jesus is betrayed by one of his own. In the present time, this station reminds us of the impact of betrayal and broken trust in personal relationships and in larger institutions like government and corporations.

4. Station of Suffering: Jesus is beaten and abused. This station represents the reality of physical and emotional abuse, and the need for healing and support for survivors.

5. Station of Poverty: Jesus carries the cross, representing the burden of poverty and economic injustice that many people face today.

6. Station of Oppression: Jesus falls under the weight of the cross, symbolizing the struggle against oppression and systemic injustices that still exist in our society.

7. Station of Empathy: Jesus is helped by Simon of Cyrene. This station reminds us of the importance of empathy and compassion, and the power of community support.

8. Station of Forgiveness: Jesus forgives those who have hurt him. In the present time, this station represents the power of forgiveness and the need for restorative justice in our society.

9. Station of Courage: Jesus meets the women of Jerusalem, reminding us of the strength and courage of women who have been marginalized and oppressed.

10. Station of Healing: Jesus is stripped of his garments, symbolizing the need for physical and emotional healing in our society.

11. Station of Solidarity: Jesus is crucified alongside two criminals. This station reminds us of the importance of standing in solidarity with those who are marginalized and oppressed.

12. Station of Compassion: Jesus speaks to his mother, showing compassion and care for those we love.

13. Station of Redemption: Jesus dies on the cross, representing the hope for redemption and transformation, both individually and collectively.

14. Station of Resurrection: Jesus rises from the dead, symbolizing the possibility of new life and the promise of hope, even in the midst of suffering and darkness.
