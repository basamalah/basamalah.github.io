4. why were frias considered powerful figures in politics and goverment during the spanish colonial period.​


Answer:

Friars were influential figures in politics and government during the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines because they were representatives of the Catholic Church, which had significant power over Spanish colonial policy. They acted as intermediaries between the Spanish authorities and the Filipino population, held high-ranking government positions, and were involved in trade and commerce. Their religious, educational, and economic influence made them powerful figures in colonial society.
