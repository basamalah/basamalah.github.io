Electrical resistance

Electrical resistance is a force that opposes the flow of electricity.

Resistance affects the flow of electricity. his page offers basic knowledge of electrical resistance, along with detailed explanations of topics such as calculation methods and resistors.

Electrical resistance is a force that opposes the flow of current. So, resistance serves as an indicator of how difficult it is for a current to flow. The resistance value is expressed in ohms .

When there is a difference in electrons between the two terminals, electricity will flow from high to low. here is resistance to the flow of current. The greater the resistance, the smaller the current. Conversely, the lower the resistance, the greater the current.

How to Calculate Resistance

Electrical resistance can be calculated as a value using the voltage and current in the circuit.

Voltage/current = Resistance

This formula is known as Ohm's Law. If the voltage is held constant, the resistance value will decrease as the denominator current increases. On the other hand, the resistance value will increase as the current decreases. In other words, the electrical resistance is low in circuits that carry large currents and high in circuits that carry small currents.
