1. ano ang taal church?


Taal Church

Ang Taal Church ay mas kilala sa tawag na Taal Basilica o Minor Basilica of Saint Martin of Tours. Ito ay matatagpuan sa bayan ng Taal sa Batangas. Ito ang kinikilalang pinakamalaking simbahan sa Pilipinas at sa Asya na may taas na 88.6 na metro at 48 na metro ang lapad.

History

Ang unang gusali ay itinayo noong 1575. Ang lokasyon nito noon ay hindi tulad ng sa kasalukuyan. Ito ay giniba noong 1642 at itinayong muli gamit ang mas matitibay na materyales ngunit, ito ay nasira muli noong 1754 dahil sa pagputok ng Bulkang Taal na malapit dito. Binuo itong muli noong 1755 ngunit mas inilayo na ito sa bulkan. Muli, sa kasamaang palad, ito ay niyanig ng lindol noong 1852. Ang kasalukuyang nakatayong simbahan ay muling ginawa simula noong 1856 at natapos noong 1878.

Founder

Ang Taal Basilica ay pinlanong buuin ng mga Agustinians noong 1572.

Architecture

Ito ay Baroque at Neoclassical. Dahil ito ay binuo noong 1800's, mas makikita rito ang Neoclassical architecture.

2. History of eruption of taal volcano


Taal Volcano eruption history:  

In 1911, the eruption killed 1334 people and caused ash fall as far as Manila City. Due to its devastating potential, Taal Volcano was declared as one of the “Decade Volcanoes” in the Decade Volcanoes program of the 1990s in order to incentive research and monitoring of the volcano.  

Another increase in seismic activity under Taal was recorded in November 2006, followed by an increase in hot springs in the crater on the following year.  

Due to its active activities, Taal Volcano is one of the most closely monitored volcano in the region.  

More detailed timeline of eruption:  

1731 – An underwater eruption happened in Pira-Piraso, or the eastern tip of the island. This was classified as phreatomagmatic. Flowing mixture of gas and ejected rock fragments were reported, as well as falling of rock particles.

August 11, 1749 – The main crater had a phreatomagmatic eruption and was recorded as “very violent” by Phivolcs. It affected residents of Taal Volcano Island and lakeshore towns of Taal, Sala, and Tanauan.

May 15 to December 5, 1754 – A phreatomagmatic/plinian eruption took place, characterized by a “very violent” explosion accompanied by falling and ejection of rock particles. A plinian eruption is characterized by continuous gas blasts and explosive ejection of viscous lava.

This incident is considered Taal Volcano’s biggest eruption yet and lasted almost 7 months. It buried 4 towns in Batangas under ash, volcanic rocks, and water. Ashfall registering 100 to 110-centimeter deposits was reported, as well as shock waves and acid rain. (READ: Taal 1754 eruption: Will history repeat itself?)

1790, 1808, 1825, 1842, 1873, 1874, 1878, 1903, 1904 – Separate eruptions occurred at the main crater. The 1808 and 1874 eruptions were classified as phreatomagmatic, while the 1878 and 1904 were phreatic.

January 27 to February 10, 1911 – A phreatic and “very violent” eruption occurred at the main crater, causing rock particles and fragments to fall and shoot out of the volcano. Phivolcs recorded 1,335 casualties in this incident.

There were also 25 to 80-centimeter thick deposit of ashfall, acid rain, shock waves, and cracking and sinking of land.

September 28 to 30, 1965 – Taal Volcano erupted at Mt Tabaro, leaving 200 casualties, according to Phivolcs. The eruption was phreatomagmatic and violent, characterized by falling of rock fragments, ashfall with 25-centimeter thick deposit, and acid rain.

July 5, 1966; August 16, 1967 – Separate phreatomagmatic eruptions occurred again at the Mt Tabaro crater. Both incidents caused rock particles to fall from the volcano and shoot out in projectile motion.

January 31, 1968; October 29, 1969 – The only Strombolian eruptions of Taal Volcano were recorded on these dates, which both happened at the Mt Tabaro crater. Lava flow and fountaining happened in both incidents.

September 3, 1970; September 3, 1976; October 3, 1977 – The last 3 eruptions that occurred before the 2020 explosion. These eruptions were similar in nature – they all took place at the Mt Tabaro crater and were all phreatic.

Taal Volcano latest eruption was on January 12, 2020.

Ang Bulkang Taal ay makikita sa Batangas na matatagpuan sa bansang Pilipinas.

Ang Bulkang Taal ay isa sa mga aktibong bulkan dito sa Pilipinas. Sa ngayon, ang huli nitong pagsabog ay noong ika-12 ng Enero, taong 2020. Inside Taal Lake, there is an island called Volcano Island, which is the location of Taal Volcano, an active stratovolcano.

What makes Taal Volcano unique is that within the caldera, or the large volcanic crater, of Taal Volcano, there is another smaller lake called the Crater Lake. This smaller lake is also sometimes referred to as "Main Crater Lake" or "Volcano Island Lake". So, in summary, Taal Volcano is located on an island called Volcano Island, which is located in Taal Lake, which is situated on the larger island of Luzon in the Philippines. 29. taal volcano location


Answer:

Taal Volcano

Taal volcano is located in the province of Batangas and is one of the most active volcanoes in the Philippines, second only to Mayon Volcano in Albay. It is a large caldera wherein the water that filled it turns into a lake called Taal Lake. It consists of 47 craters and 4 maars, volcanic craters caused by an eruption, with the main crater found at the center where previous eruptions occurred.

It already has more than 34 confirmed eruptions in 448 years with the latest happened on January 12, 2020 which caused panic and wide destruction of crops and death of animals.

Taal Volcano

Taal Volcano is the second most active volcano in the Philippies after Mt. Mayon. It is known for its small size and its recent eruption.

Location or Geography

The whole volcano is located in the province of Batangas. The northern part of the volcano is located in Talisay while the other half is part of San Nicolas. The main crater of the volcano was a lake but the recent eruption, January 2020, evaporated the water. It is the largest lake on an island in a lake on an island in the world. It used to have a small island inside but due to its volcanic activity, it disappeared but reappeared this March 2020 acording to PHIVOLCS.

2020 Eruption

It started to erupt on January 12, 2020, 43 years after the 1977 eruption. Ashfall was felt even on Metro Manila and Calabarzon region and caused cancellation of flights and classes. Thunderstorms were reported and forced evacuation on nearby areas happened. 39 people died due to refusion to forced evacuation and health problems.
