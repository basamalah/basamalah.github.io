13. Feedback that can be given to the company or institution in ojt


It  is important for the university or institute to provide feedback to the company who manages trainees under internship or on-the job-training. The following aspects can be used when giving feedback to them.
1. Work Ethics
Students under the on-the-job-training must be exposed to work ethics. This can be nurtured/developed by the company by means of establishing rules. Are the students trained to be punctual at work, during meetings and task submission? Do the assigned company managers train the students to commit to work and submit quality output?
Here is a sample feedback:
We are satisfied with the way you help and guide our students to have good work ethics. They have become more disciplined and precise in submitting work. They have become more professional in behaving.
2. Employability Skills
The students are undergoing an on-the job-training precisely for the purpose of employment. Are the students being trained to organize systems, use office machines properly, and work with colleagues? Are the students being trained to serve and handle customers professionally?
Here is a sample feedback:
We are happy that are students have equipped themselves with basic office and meeting skills. They have learned how to operate office equipment. They have improved their soft skills.
3. Opportunity for Development
Does the assigned company give opportunities to the trainees to work out of their comfort zone? Are they given chances to build their confidence?
We are glad that the students have learned additional knowledge  such as organizing CSR events for the company. This will surely widen the exposure and experience of the students.

Answer:

On-the-job training programs (OJT) strengthen your skills in preparation for the job role. For fresh graduates, OJT programs are a ticket to gaining work experience. But there is more to OJT.

Your academic background aside, a specific job role has its unique dynamics, requirements and workflows. An on-the-job training program weans you onto the job’s dynamics and workflows and, broadly, the company culture.

Essentially, an on-the-job training program bridges the gap between academia and industry. The two sectors operate on different planes, as theory and practice often do not meet at eye level.

OJT applies nearly to all disciplines across degree levels. Whether you have an administrative associate degree, a bachelor’s degree in fashion or a master’s in data science, going through an OJT is an essential process to help you succeed in your job.

To shed critical light on this matter, this article reveals all the intricate details about this vital OJT process. It discusses the definition, types, and benefits of on the job training.
