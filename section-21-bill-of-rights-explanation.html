7. article III section 12 bill of rightsplease explain ​


Answer:

Ang Article III, Section 12 ng Konstitusyon ng Pilipinas ay naglalaman ng mga probisyon sa Bill of Rights na naglalayong protektahan ang mga karapatan ng tao sa bansa. Narito ang kabuuan ng seksyon na ito:

"Section 12. (1) Any person under investigation for the commission of an offense shall have the right to be informed of his right to remain silent and to have competent and independent counsel preferably of his own choice. If the person cannot afford the services of counsel, he must be provided with one. These rights cannot be waived except in writing and in the presence of counsel.

(2) No torture, force, violence, threat, intimidation, or any other means which vitiate the free will shall be used against him. Secret detention places, solitary, incommunicado, or other similar forms of detention are prohibited.

(3) Any confession or admission obtained in violation of this or Section 17 hereof shall be inadmissible in evidence against him.

(4) The law shall provide for penal and civil sanctions for violations of this section as well as compensation to and rehabilitation of victims of torture or similar practices, and their families."

Explanation:

Sa seksyon na ito, nakasaad ang mga karapatang dapat na bigyan ng proteksyon sa panahon ng imbestigasyon at paglilitis ng isang tao. Kabilang dito ang karapatan sa pagkakaroon ng impormasyon tungkol sa kanilang karapatan na manahimik at magkaroon ng abogado, ang karapatang magkaroon ng abogado kahit walang kakayahan na magbayad para sa serbisyo nito, at ang karapatang hindi magpakawala ng kanilang mga karapatang ito maliban na lamang kung nakasulat ito sa papeles at kasama ang kanilang abogado.

Sa seksyon na ito rin, ipinagbabawal ang paggamit ng torture, pwersa, karahasan, o anumang iba pang paraan upang labagin ang malayang kagustuhan ng isang tao.

10. Choose 5 section in the bill of rights and explain how it promote values in society.​


The Bill of Rights, the first ten amendments to the United States Constitution, contains several sections that promote important values in society. Here are five sections and how they promote these values:

1. First Amendment - The First Amendment guarantees freedom of speech, religion, press, assembly, and petition. This promotes the values of individual freedom and liberty, as well as the importance of free expression and the free flow of ideas in a democratic society. It also allows for peaceful protests and the ability to hold the government accountable for its actions.

2. Fourth Amendment - The Fourth Amendment protects citizens from unreasonable searches and seizures by the government. This promotes the values of privacy and personal autonomy, as well as limiting the power of government over individuals. It ensures that citizens are protected from arbitrary or abusive actions by the state.

3. Sixth Amendment - The Sixth Amendment guarantees the right to a fair and speedy trial, the right to an impartial jury, the right to be informed of the charges against oneself, the right to confront witnesses, and the right to an attorney. This promotes the values of due process and fairness in the legal system, as well as the protection of the rights of the accused. It ensures that citizens are not subject to arbitrary or unjust treatment by the government.

4. Eighth Amendment - The Eighth Amendment prohibits cruel and unusual punishment and excessive bail. This promotes the values of human dignity and respect for individuals, as well as limiting the power of the government to inflict harm on citizens. It ensures that punishment is proportional to the crime committed and that citizens are protected from arbitrary or excessive punishment.

5. Tenth Amendment - The Tenth Amendment reserves powers not delegated to the federal government by the Constitution to the states or to the people. This promotes the values of federalism and limited government, as well as respecting the authority of state and local governments. It ensures that power is not concentrated in the hands of the federal government and allows for greater flexibility in governance.
