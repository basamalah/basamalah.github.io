Answer:

3 legged stools = 45

4 legged stools = 27

Step-by-step explanation:

The carpenter used 72 legs to make two products. The 3 legged and 4 legged stools. So I divide 72 by 2.

  72 / 2 = 36

Then out of 72 legs, he made three more of the 3 legged stools. So I multiply 3 by 3.

  3 * 3 = 9

Then if 3 legged stools is greater than 4 legged stools, so I add 9 to the 3 legged stools and subtract 9 to the 4 legged stools.

3 legged stools = 36 + 9 = 45

4 legged stools = 36 - 9 = 27

15. it can be due to constipation or diarrhea hard dry stools on one day and loose watery stools on another


Answer:

Severe constipation can cause a blockage in your bowel. Because of this, the bowel begins to leak out watery stools around the blockage from higher up in the bowel. The leak from the bowel can look like diarrhoea. It's called 'overflow diarrhoea.

If you have diarrhea, you'll also have loose or watery stools. However, if you have loose stools from time to time, it doesn't mean you have diarrhea. In order for loose stools to be considered diarrhea, they have to occur repeatedly. If you have loose stools three or more times per day, then it's diarrhea.
