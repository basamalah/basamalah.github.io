Medical Technologist Job Responsibilities and Duties:

Medical Technologist duties and responsibilities

Medical Technologists are mainly responsible for collecting and testing samples. Their duties and responsibilities include the following:

Use established lab protocol to perform laboratory tests and correctly log the results of those tests

Determine specimen validity within the first 20 minutes of receiving the specimen

Inform clients of recollection protocols if a specimen is rejected

Identify, diagnose and repair issues that may arise with the laboratory equipment

Verify all test results and ensure that they are correctly matched with the specimen

Label and organize all tests appropriately to make sure that they are easily retrievable

22. a medical technologist handling specimen of suspected covid 19 patient​


A medical technologist handling specimens of a suspected COVID-19 patient is responsible for collecting, handling, and testing samples to determine whether the patient is infected with the virus. This involves following strict safety protocols, wearing appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE), and using specialized equipment to prevent contamination and ensure accurate results.

The medical technologist will typically collect specimens such as nasal swabs, throat swabs, or sputum samples from the patient, and then process and analyze them in a laboratory setting. They will use techniques such as polymerase chain reaction (PCR) testing to detect the presence of the virus in the samples.

It's important for medical technologists to follow all safety protocols and guidelines when handling COVID-19 specimens to minimize the risk of contracting the virus and spreading it to others. This includes proper use and disposal of PPE, frequent hand washing and sanitizing, and maintaining a clean and sterile work environment.
