Early life and political career

From the 13th century onward, Machiavelli’s family was wealthy and prominent, holding on occasion Florence’s most important offices. His father, Bernardo, a doctor of laws, was nevertheless among the family’s poorest members. Barred from public office in Florence as an insolvent debtor, Bernardo lived frugally, administering his small landed property near the city and supplementing his meagre income from it with earnings from the restricted and almost clandestine exercise of his profession.

Bernardo kept a library in which Niccolò must have read, but little is known of Niccolò’s education and early life in Florence, at that time a thriving centre of philosophy and a brilliant showcase of the arts. He attended lectures by Marcello Virgilio Adriani, who chaired the Studio Fiorentino. 10. what is Machiavellian Concept of Power ​


Answer:

The Machiavellian concept of power is based on the writings of Niccolò Machiavelli, an Italian Renaissance writer and philosopher who is best known for his book "The Prince." According to Machiavelli, the acquisition and maintenance of power require a leader to be willing to use any means necessary, including deception, manipulation, and even violence.

Machiavelli believed that the ideal leader is one who is feared rather than loved, as fear is a more reliable motivator for obedience and loyalty. He also argued that a leader should prioritize the maintenance of power above all else, even if this requires breaking promises or acting immorally.

Another key aspect of Machiavelli's concept of power is the importance of appearing virtuous and compassionate to the public, even if the leader's actions are in fact cruel or ruthless. Machiavelli believed that a leader who is perceived as virtuous and compassionate will be more likely to win the loyalty of the people, making it easier to maintain power.

Overall, the Machiavellian concept of power emphasizes the importance of strategic thinking, ruthless decision-making, and the ability to manipulate others in order to acquire and maintain power. While Machiavelli's ideas have been criticized for promoting immoral behavior, they have also been influential in shaping political theory and practice throughout history.  >-<

17. A. Ipaliwanag ang sumusunod: 1. Secularism 2. Machiavellian doctrine 3. Renaissance man 4.individualism5. Humanities ​


Answer:

1.SECULARISM-ay ang prinsipyo ng paghahangad na magsagawa ng mga gawain ng tao batay sa sekular, naturalistikong mga pagsasaalang-alang. Ito ay pinakakaraniwang tinukoy bilang ang paghihiwalay ng relihiyon mula sa mga gawaing sibiko at ng estado — na alinsunod sa pluralismo ng relihiyon ay tumutukoy sa sekularismo bilang neutralidad (ng estado o hindi sektaryan na institusyon) sa mga isyu ng relihiyon kumpara sa ganap na pagsalungat ng relihiyon sa pampublikong parisukat sa kabuuan — habang ang ibang mga pananaw ay maaaring palawakin ito sa isang posisyon hinggil sa pangangailangang alisin o bawasan ang papel ng relihiyon sa anumang pampublikong globo.

3.RENAISSANCE MAN- .Ang salitang renaissance ay isang salitang French na literal na nangangahulugang “pagkabuhay muli.” Tinawag na panahon ng renaissance ang pagbangon ng Europe mula sa tinatawag na Dark Ages at Middle Ages.

4.INVIDUALISM-.ay ang moral na paninindigan, pilosopiyang pampulitika, ideolohiya at pananaw sa lipunan na nagbibigay-diin sa intrinsic na halaga ng indibidwal.[1][2] Itinataguyod ng mga indibidwal ang paggamit ng mga layunin at pagnanasa ng isang tao at pinahahalagahan ang pagsasarili at pag-asa sa sarili[3] at itinataguyod na ang mga interes ng indibidwal ay dapat makamit ang pangunguna kaysa sa estado o isang panlipunang grupo[3] habang sinasalungat ang panlabas na panghihimasok sa sariling interes ng lipunan o mga institusyon tulad ng gobyerno.

5.HUMANITIES-.Ang humanities ay mga akademikong disiplina na nag-aaral ng mga aspeto ng lipunan at kultura ng tao. Sa Renaissance, ang termino ay kaibahan sa pagka-diyos at tinutukoy ang tinatawag ngayong mga klasiko, ang pangunahing lugar ng sekular na pag-aaral sa mga unibersidad noong panahong iyon.
