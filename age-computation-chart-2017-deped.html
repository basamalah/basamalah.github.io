8. 52. Which among the choices is not an APA format?A. (Zobel 287) B. Ebuetada (2017) C. (Ebuetada, 2017) D. According to Ebuetada (2017)​


APA FORMAT

Only letter A is the only one that is not written in APA format among the following choices:

A. (Zobel 287) B. Ebuetada (2017) C. (Ebuetada, 2017) D. According to Ebuetada (2017)

What is the APA format?

APA stands for American Psychological Association.It is used as a standard format for citations in almost every social science-related field research.This format is created to create standard scientific writing.This aids the authors of research to be free from plagiarism and avoid severe consequences if they ever accidentally plagiarize something from a book, article, or any other form of literature.If it concerns the name of the author and the date the literature was published, we have three ways in APA in writing it.Parenthetical citation, narrative citation, and reference list entry are the only APA formats for author-date-related citations.Parenthetical Citation: (Ebuetada, 2017) Narrative Citation: Ebuetada (2017) Reference List Entry: According to Ebuetada (2017)

What are other forms of citations?

We have several citation styles but here are three common styles: APA, MLA, and Chicago/Turabian style.MLA stands for Modern Language Association. This format is often used by people researching Humanities.Chicago/Turabian is commonly used by business-related people, historians, and artists.

The Evolution of Traditional to New Media

→ You can see the table in the picture below.

THE EVOLUTION OF MEDIA Pre-industrialIndustrialElectronicNew/ Informational erasAGE PRE-INDUSTRIAL (BEFORE 1700)

People made paper from plants, discovered fire, and used stone, bronze, copper, and iron to manufacture tools and weapons.

Examples of Media Formats

Cave art (35,000 BC); Papyrus found in Egypt (2500 BC) Acta Diurna (Rome, 130 BC)Dibao (China, 2nd Century)Codex (Maya region) are only a few examples (5th Century) The wooden block printing press (220 AD)

INDUSTRIAL AGE (1700S TO 1930S)

Humans harnessed the power of steam, created machine tools, established iron production, and began producing a wide range of goods (including books through the printing press)

Examples of Media Formats

A printing press for volume printing (1900) The London Gazette newspaper (1740)Telephone (1800) Typewriter (1876) Motion picture projection and photography (1890) Industry-related motion movies (1913) Sound-accompanied video (1926) Punch cards and telegraphs

ELECTRONIC Era (1930S TO 1980S)

The electronic age began with the development of the transistor. Transistors' power was harnessed by humans, which resulted in the transistor radio, electronic circuits, and the first computers. Long-distance communication became increasingly effective in this era.

Examples of Media Formats

OHP, LCD projectors;Transistor RadioTelevision (1941) Large Electronic ComputersMainframe Computers

NEW/INFORMATION AGE (1900s–2000s)

The Internet cleared the door for social networks to be created and for speedier communication. With the development of personal computers, mobile devices, and wearable technology, people increased their usage of microelectronics. Additionally, data, voice, image, and sound are all digitalized. Our current era is the information age.

Examples of the information age

Web browsers (1995) BlogsSocial media platformsVirtual and Augmented Reality Search EnginesMobile devices
