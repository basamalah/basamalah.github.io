11. Task 8.2 A QUOTE ON QUOTE


The phrase “quote unquote” means speaking a quotation on a word for word basis. You end the quotation with the word “unquote” to indicate that it’s finished. On the one hand, the phrase “quote on quote” is a mispronunciation of “unquote.” The correct phrase is “quote unquote.” This indicates that what you’re saying is a repetition of someone else’s statement.


12. James bought a laptop and a mobile phone. The laptop cost 3 times as much as the mobile phone. The cost of the laptop was $3642.


Let x be the cost of the mobile phone.

According to the problem, the cost of the laptop is 3 times the cost of the mobile phone. So:

Cost of laptop = 3 * Cost of mobile phone

$3642 = 3x

Dividing both sides by 3, we get:

x = $1214

Therefore, the cost of the mobile phone is $1214.

To check:

Cost of laptop = 3 * Cost of mobile phone

Cost of laptop = 3 * $1214 = $3642

So the cost of the laptop is indeed $3642.

Answer:

In this problem, we are given that James bought a laptop and a mobile phone, and we know that the laptop cost 3 times as much as the mobile phone. We are also told that the cost of the laptop was $3642.

To find the cost of the mobile phone, we can use algebra. Let x be the cost of the mobile phone. Then, we can write an equation based on the information given in the problem:

cost of laptop = 3 times cost of mobile phone

$3642 = 3x

To solve for x, we can divide both sides of the equation by 3:

$3642 ÷ 3 = x

Simplifying the right-hand side, we get:

$1214 = x

So, the cost of the mobile phone is $1214.

We can check our answer by verifying that the cost of the laptop is indeed 3 times the cost of the mobile phone:

3 times $1214 = $3642

Therefore, our answer is correct.
