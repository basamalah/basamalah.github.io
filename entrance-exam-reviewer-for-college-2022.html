16. In a college entrance test, 450 students took the exam and the results yielded u= 125 and σ = 30.50. How many students passed the entrance exam if only those whose score is at least above Z = -0.5 will pass?​


Answer:

=> 311 students passed the entrance exam.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

n = 450

u = 125

σ = 30.5

z = -0.5

Step 1: Calculate the Z-score for the passing mark of the entrance exam:

z_p = ( 125 - (-0.5) - 125 ) / 30.5

z_p = 0.50

Step 2: Calculate the proportion of students who have scored above the passing mark:

p = (1 - NormalCDF(-0.5, 0.50))

p = 0.6915

Step 3: Calculate the number of students who passed the entrance exam:

Number of students who passed the entrance exam = p * n

Number of students who passed the entrance exam = 310.73

=> 311 students passed the entrance exam.

What is an entrance examination?

A college admission exam is a standardized aptitude test that assesses your abilities in areas like verbal, math, analytical, and writing. These examinations aren't meant to assess what you've learned in school; rather, they're meant to assess your potential to succeed in the future.

Your high school courses will assist you in studying for these tests. Taking practice examinations, on the other hand, is an extra option to prepare because they will help you become familiar with the types of questions offered, their format, and the time required to complete each part.

Exams for college entrance aim to assess your numerical, verbal, analytical, and writing abilities. These tests are used by several four-year institutions and universities. ACT or SAT scores are also required by some community and technical institutions, especially if you plan to enroll in a program that prepares students to transfer to a four-year institution.

The structures and scoring methods of the ACT and SAT are covered in this article to assist you manage standardized testing. You can also learn more about the AP, CLEP, PSAT, and TOEFL exams. The book also includes advice on how to ace college entrance examinations.

How to pass an entrance examination of a university?

Understand the fundamentals.

You'll need a good knowledge of the foundations, from basic math formulas to common grammar principles, to be able to use them effectively. You've most likely learned all of this if you've attended typical college-bound courses. However, if such classes were taken a year or two ago, your memory may be faulty.

Before taking the test, familiarize yourself with the instructions.

For years at a time, the ACT and College Board (SAT) do not revise their instructions. This provides the astute pupil a leg up! Before you take the test, make sure you understand the directions.

Wear a watch

Sure, there's a clock in the room, but is it functional? Is it anything you'll be able to see from your seat? The condition of the clock in the room becomes meaningless if you wear a watch. You can also use a watch to establish a time-management strategy during the test.

Keep track of your time.

Of course, it's critical to arrive early for the test and pay attention to the start and stop times for each part. You should also be aware of the most effective pacing tactics for each subtest so that you have plenty of chances to exhibit your knowledge.
