3. SWOT analysis for flower shop​


Answer:

Strengths:

Strong reputation for high-quality flowers and excellent customer service.Wide variety of flowers and arrangements, including rare and exotic varieties.Experienced and knowledgeable staff, including florists with a passion for creating beautiful arrangements.Strong relationships with local flower growers and suppliers, ensuring a steady supply of fresh flowers.

Weaknesses:

Limited online presence, with only a basic website and no social media presence.Limited delivery options, with deliveries only available within a certain radius of the shop.High overhead costs, including rent, utilities, and staff wages.

Opportunities:

Growth in the demand for flowers as a result of the increasing popularity of flower subscriptions.Expansion of the business through the opening of additional locations or the acquisition of other flower shops.Development of an online ordering and delivery system to reach a wider customer base.Increase in the events and Wedding Business.

Threats:

Increasing competition from online flower retailers and big box stores that offer flowers at a lower cost.Seasonal fluctuations in demand for certain types of flowers.Economic downturns result in reduced consumer spending on luxury items like flowers.

Conduct a SWOT analysis of a business of your personal choice here in Lipa City. It could be a Restaurant, Milk Tea Business,Fast Food, Lomi Businesses, Pares, Bakery, Water Refilling Stations, Motorcycle Shops, Autoshop, etc. Also write the names and exact location of that business and a photo if possible.​


QUESTION:

ANSWER:

SWOT Analysis is a tool that can help you to analyze what your company does best right now, and to devise a successful strategy for the future. SWOT can also uncover areas of the business that are holding you back, or that your competitors could exploit if you don't protect yourself.

A SWOT analysis examines both internal and external factors – that is, what's going on inside and outside your organization. So some of these factors will be within your control and some will not. In either case, the wisest action you can take in response will become clearer once you've discovered, recorded and analyzed as many factors as you can.

SWOT seems simple, but, if used carefully and collaboratively, it can be very revealing. For example, you may be well aware of some of your organization's strengths, but until you record them alongside weaknesses and threats you might not realize how unreliable those strengths actually are. Equally, you likely have reasonable concerns about some of your business weaknesses but, by going through the analysis systematically, you could find an opportunity, previously overlooked, that could more than compensate.
