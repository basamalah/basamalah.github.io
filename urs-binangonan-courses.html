1. kasysayan ng Binangonan​


Answer:

HISTORY OF BINANGONAN:

A number of theories has been advanced on the origin of Binangonan yet none could exactly point the real beginning of this town. Some observations can be noted thus:

Binangonan is a pure Tagalog word for the place where someting or someone has voluntarily risen.

All barangays and sitios from Tayuman to Talim, with the exception of Janosa, have pure Tagalog names indicating that these places could have existed even before the Spanish rule.

A village in Infanta, Laguna was called Binangonan de Lampon which led some historians to theorize that settlers of the lake region could have originated from this place.

The history of Taytay includes a place called Batasin whose inhabitants were believed to be the ancestors of the few remaining tribes of Negritoes. There is no Batasin in Taytay at present but there is one in Binangonan.

Aetas inhabited Binangonan until 1928. Evidence of their culture are found in Lungga Ita in Pila-pila and in Aeta words like Wawa and Ithan.

But there is one landmark that stands out and has brought these towns together: the famous windmill. The Cardona-Binangonan Wind Farm, located in Sitio Halang, Brgy. Talim, Binangonan, Rizal, is the first and only wind farm in the Philippines. But how did Cardona-Binangonan Rizal become famous for its windmill?

The windmill project began in 2008 when the National Power Corporation (NPC) proposed the development of a wind farm in the area. The project aimed to harness the strong winds in Laguna de Bay and generate renewable energy for the province. After several years of planning and construction, the wind farm was finally inaugurated in 2015.

The wind farm consists of 27 wind turbines, each standing 125 meters tall with a rotor diameter of 87 meters. These turbines generate a combined capacity of 67.5 megawatts, enough to power around 50,000 homes. The turbines' blades are made of fiberglass reinforced with carbon fiber, and their towers are made of steel. These materials are designed to withstand the strong winds and harsh weather conditions in the area.

The wind farm's turbines are located on a ridge overlooking the Laguna de Bay, providing a breathtaking view of the lake and the surrounding mountains. The area has become a popular spot for tourists and photographers, who flock to the site to capture the windmills' majestic beauty.

The wind farm has also brought economic benefits to the towns of Cardona and Binangonan. The project has created job opportunities for the locals, and the taxes generated from the wind farm's operations have been used to fund various community projects and infrastructure developments.

In conclusion, the Cardona-Binangonan Wind Farm has put these two Rizal towns on the map, becoming famous for its pioneering use of wind power in the Philippines.
