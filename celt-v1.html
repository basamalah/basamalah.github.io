12. Celsius to KelvinT1=27°cT2=-78°cV1=113LV2=?Pasagot diko alam pano gagawin sa V1 tas V2 ​


Answer:

The volume at -78°C is approximately 76.72 L.

Explanation:

To convert Celsius to Kelvin, you need to add 273.15 to the Celsius temperature.

For T1 = 27°C:

T1 in Kelvin = 27 + 273.15 = 300.15 K

For T2 = -78°C:

T2 in Kelvin = -78 + 273.15 = 195.15 K

Using the combined gas law equation:

(P1 × V1)/T1 = (P2 × V2)/T2

We can rearrange the formula to solve for V2:

V2 = (P1 × V1 × T2) / (T1 × P2)

Assuming constant pressure, we can use the average atmospheric pressure at sea level, which is 101.3 kPa.

Substituting the values:

V2 = (101.3 kPa × 113 L × 195.15 K) / (300.15 K × 101.3 kPa)

V2 = 76.72 L

Therefore, the volume at -78°C is approximately 76.72 L.

Leading/Lagging deals with the phase relationship between two sine or cosine wave.

v1 has a phase angle of -84°. i has a phase angle of +99°. So we look at counter-clockwise rotation.

To get the angle between the two waveforms, we subtract the most positive from the other.

So +99° - (-84°) = 183°. So i leads v1 by 183°. If we subtract from 360° - 183° = 177°. So if we look at v1 going first, i lags v1 by 177°.

Second one we have sin and cosine. We have to convert our sin to cos.

cos t = sin(t + 90°) <-- trigonometric identity.

So -3° - (-84°) = 81°
