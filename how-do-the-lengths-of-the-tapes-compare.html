Acceleration is the change in speed with respect to time. The difference in length between two successive tapes provides the object's acceleration or its change in speed or velocity for a time interval of 1 second. The longer the type the higher the acceleration and the shorter the tape the shorter the acceleration.

25. how will you compare the changes in the lengths of two successive tapes?


Activity : “Doing Detective Work”

Instruction: Cut the strip at each drop, starting from the first to the last drop, and paste them side by side on a graph paper to form a tape chart

Question: How will you compare the changes in lengths of two successive tapes?

In this activity, the length of tape represents the distance traveled by the object in 1 second. The strips of tape also provide information on the speed of the object. As we know that the speed is the distance traveled for a specific time.  

If you were going to observe carefully the distance traveled by the object in every second which is represented by the tape, you can notice that the distance traveled by the object does not change as the length of the two successive tape does not change. If the distance traveled by every second is the same, this will imply that the speed is increased by the same amount in every second. Thus, we can conclude that the speed is constant.  

Another information that the difference in the length of the successive tape will provide is the object’s acceleration. Acceleration will give you the information on the change in the speed or velocity of the object in a given time.
