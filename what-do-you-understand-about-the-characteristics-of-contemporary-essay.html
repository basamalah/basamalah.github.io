29. what do you understand about the characteristics of contemporary??​


What do you understand about the characteristics of contemporary?

To put it simply, this is the art of today. From painting to sculpture, and from photography to performance, it can take as long as it is created in today's period. In the lack of a particular medium or apparatus, contemporary art has been viewed widely in various periods and regions. This form of art is thought to have began towards the close of the art age of modernism in the late 1960s. Characteristics: The most influential feature of contemporary art is that it has no distinguishing feature or characteristic. It is characterized by the artist's ability to invent and create a new masterpiece. Here are a few of the technical characteristics:

1. Innovation in Art:

Contemporary artists have been pioneers in bringing modern styles of creativity to the world. Different styles of art have been merged or newer ideas have been used to carry forth newer types of art.

Collage painting is an example of creativity that has proven to be a perfect example of modern contemporary art. In the same way, mixed styles of assemblage, various variants of cinematic art as well as different categories of painting, animation and land art are the products of bringing together newer concepts.

2. Using New Materials:

Another characteristic that was used to bring on modern art was the use of diverse objects, materials that appeared worthless to the average man, but were commonly used by modern artists. For example, painters used newspaper articles as a background to their canvases and produced masterpieces of contemporary art from them. It was unprecedented to use in a painting anything like a newspaper. Similarly, the sculptors used junk pieces to produce what is now known as junk art. Popular objects such as vehicles, benches, boxes have also been masterfully used to build assemblies.

3. Use of Color:

Color has traditionally been seen as a way of bringing truth to paintings and works of art. Modern artists, however, worked with colors and unconventionally used them to create new textures and themes and used them in their works of art.

Expressionism was often focused on the vivid and imaginative use of colors as a medium for innovating their paintings.

4. Newer Techniques:

Modern art is not only about playing and experimenting with colors and objects, it has also given rise to newer techniques that form the basis of contemporary art today. Chromolithography is a prime example of a technique invented by Jules Cheret. In the same way, abstract artists have brought automatic drawing to the art world. Decalcomania and Frottage are both vital methods that have progressed over time and are used in the production of contemporary art.
