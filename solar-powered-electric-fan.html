22. How you do differentiate the mechanism and process of electricity generation from solar power which uses Photovoltaic Cells and Concentrated Solar Power?( Photovoltaic Cells VS Concentrated Solar Power)own idea​


Answer:

The mechanism and process of electricity generation from solar power can be differentiated based on the technology used, namely photovoltaic cells (PV) and concentrated solar power (CSP).

Photovoltaic cells (PV):

1. Mechanism: PV cells convert sunlight directly into electricity using the photoelectric effect.

2. Process: When sunlight strikes the surface of a PV cell, it excites electrons in the cell and causes them to flow in a specific direction, creating an electric current. This electric current can then be used to power electrical devices or stored in batteries.

Concentrated Solar Power (CSP):

1. Mechanism: CSP systems use mirrors or lenses to focus and concentrate sunlight onto a small area, creating high temperatures.

2. Process: The concentrated sunlight is directed onto a heat-transfer fluid or a solid material, which absorbs the heat and uses it to generate steam. This steam then drives a turbine, which generates electricity.

In summary, the mechanism of electricity generation from solar power using photovoltaic cells involves direct conversion of sunlight into electricity, while concentrated solar power uses mirrors or lenses to focus and concentrate sunlight onto a small area to create high temperatures, which are then used to generate steam and drive a turbine to generate electricity.
