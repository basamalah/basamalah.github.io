19. How many atoms of carbon are present in 0.750 mol MSG? The formula of MSG is NaC5H8NO4.


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Note that there are 5 moles of C in 1 mole of NaC₅H₈NO₄ and there are 6.022 × 10²³ atoms of C in 1 mole of C.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{number of C atoms} & = 0.750 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{NaC}_5\text{H}_8\text{NO}_4} \times \frac{5 \: \cancel{\text{mol C}}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{NaC}_5\text{H}_8\text{NO}_4}} \times \frac{6.022 \times 10^{23} \: \text{C atoms}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol C}}} \\ & = \boxed{2.26 \times 10^{24} \: \text{C atoms}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 2.26 × 10²⁴ C atoms in 0.750 mole of NaC₅H₈NO₄.

29. calculate the empirical and molecular formula of monosodium glutamate(MSG) given the following % composition. 33.4%C 4.77%H 37.85%O 8.29%N 13.60%Na (molar mass of MSG = 146g/mol)


Hello!

calculate the empirical and molecular formula of monosodium glutamate(MSG) given the following % composition. 33.4%C 4.77%H 37.85%O 8.29%N 13.60%Na (molar mass of MSG = 146g/mol)

data:

Carbon (C) ≈ 12 a.m.u (g/mol)

Hydrogen (H) ≈ 1 a.m.u (g/mol)

Oxygen (O) ≈ 16 a.m.u (g/mol)

Nitrogen (N) ≈ 14 a.m.u (g/mol)

Sodium (Na) ≈ 23 a.m.u (g/mol)

We use the amount in grams (mass ratio) based on the composition of the elements, see: (in 100g solution)

C: 33.4 % = 33.4 g

H: 4.77 % = 4.77 g

O: 37.85 % = 37.85 g

N: 8.29 % = 8.29 g

Na: 13.60 % = 13.60 g

The values ​​(in g) will be converted into quantity of substance (number of mols), dividing by molecular weight (g / mol) each of the values, we will see:

[tex]C: \dfrac{33.4\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g}{12\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g/mol} \approx 2.783\:mol[/tex]

[tex]H: \dfrac{4.77\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g}{1\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g/mol} = 4.77\:mol[/tex]

[tex]O: \dfrac{37.85\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g}{16\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g/mol} \approx 2.362\:mol[/tex]

[tex]N: \dfrac{8.29\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g}{14\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g/mol} \approx 0.592\:mol[/tex]

[tex]Na: \dfrac{13.60\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g}{23\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g/mol} \approx 0.591\:mol[/tex]

We realize that the values ​​found above are not integers, so we divide these values ​​by the smallest of them, so that the proportion does not change, let us see:

[tex]C: \dfrac{2.783}{0.591}\to\:\:\boxed{O \approx 5}[/tex]

[tex]H: \dfrac{4.77}{0.591}\to\:\:\boxed{O \approx 8}[/tex]

[tex]O: \dfrac{2.362}{0.591}\to\:\:\boxed{O \approx 4}[/tex]

[tex]N: \dfrac{0.592}{0.591}\to\:\:\boxed{O \approx 1}[/tex]

[tex]Na: \dfrac{0.591}{0.591}\to\:\:\boxed{Na = 1}[/tex]

Thus, the minimum or empirical formula found for the compound will be:

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{\boxed{C_5H_8O_4N_1Na_1\:\:or\:\:C_5H_8NO_4Na}}}\Longleftarrow(Empirical\:Formula)\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Let's find the Molar Mass of the Empirical Formula, let's see:

If: [tex]C_5H_8NO_4Na[/tex]

C = 5*(12u) = 60 u

H = 8*(1u) = 8 u

N = 1*(14u) = 14 u

O = 4*(16u) = 64 u

Na = 1*(23u) = 23 u

------------------------------------

Molar Mass of the Empirical Formula -MM(E.F) = 60 + 8 + 14 + 64 + 23

Molar Mass of the Empirical Formula - MM(E.F) = 169 g/mol

Knowing that the Molar Mass of the MM(M.F) Molecular Formula is 146 g/mol, then the number of terms (n) for the molecular formula of the compound will be:

[tex]n = \dfrac{MM_{M.F}}{MM_{E.F}}[/tex]

[tex]n = \dfrac{169}{146}[/tex]

[tex]n = 1.1575... \to \boxed{n \approx 1}[/tex]

Knowing that the Molecular Formula is the Empirical Formula times the number of terms (n), then we have:

[tex]M.F = (E.F)*n[/tex]

[tex]M.F = (C_5H_8NO_4Na)*1[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{\boxed{M.F = C_5H_8NO_4Na}}}\Longleftarrow(molecular\:formula)\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]
