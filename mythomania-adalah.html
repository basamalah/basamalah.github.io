The location of point A on the coordinate plane is coordinate (5,2).

Explanation :

Coordinates are a set of values ​​that help indicate the exact position of a point in the coordinate plane. A coordinate plane is a 2D plane formed by the intersection of two perpendicular lines known as the x-axis and the y-axis. The format for writing coordinates is (x,y).

It is known that point A has the same distance to point P (3,2) and point Q (7,2). It can be seen that at points P and Q each have a different x-axis (3 and 7) and the same y-axis point (2).

This means that if it is known that point A is at the same distance as point P and point Q then the y-axis coordinates at point A are 2. And to find the x-axis you only need to find the midpoint between the x-axis point P (3) and the x-axis point y ( 7).

Then the coordinates of point A are (5,2).

29. benda bermassa 1 kg ditarik oleh gaya mendatar sebesar 2 Newton dari keadaan diam,jarak yag ditempuh benda dalam waktu 10 Sekon adalah


Answer:

The distance is 100 meters

Explanation:

In our case, objects with a given mass were pulled along the horizontal axis with a given force. This problem statement is a combination of Newton's 2nd Law of motion and Motion in a Straight Line.

To solve this, we will use the formula:

a. F = ma

where

F     is the force, unit is in Newtons (N)

m    is the mass, unit is in kg

a     is the acceleration, unit is in m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

b. [tex]x=v_{0} t+\frac{1}{2} at^2[/tex]

where

x     is the distance traveled by the object, unit is in meter (m)

[tex]v_{0}[/tex]    is the initial velocity, unit is in m/s

t      is the time, unit is in seconds (s)

For the given information

a. The mass of the objects is, m= 1 kg

b. The force  pulling the objects is, F = 2N

c. The time covered in pulling the objects were, t = 10 seconds

Solving the problem

1. First, use the formula for force

F = ma

Substitute the given information

2N = (1kg) a

Divide both sides of the equation by 1 kg, we have:

[tex]\frac{2N}{1kg} =\frac{(1kg)a}{1kg}[/tex]

Then to solve for the acceleration, we have:

a = 2 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

2. Now, we are ready to solve the distance.

Use the second formula and substitute the given information, we have:

[tex]x=v_{0} t+\frac{1}{2} at^2[/tex]

[tex]x=0+\frac{1}{2} (2m/s^2)(10)^2[/tex]

Take note that [tex]v_{0} t[/tex] is zero because [tex]v_{0}[/tex] = 0 (or the initial velocity starts from rest).

Simplifying the equation, we have:

[tex]x=\frac{1}{2} (2)(10)^2[/tex]

x = 100 meters

Therefore, the distance traveled by the 1 kg objects in 10 seconds is 100 meters.
