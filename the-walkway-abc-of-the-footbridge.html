10. "A square Garden is a surrounded by a walkway . 26m wide. If the area of the walkway is 58 sqm. what are the dimensions of the garden? "​


Answer:

Let's assume that the side length of the square garden is x meters. Then, the length of one side of the larger square, which includes the walkway, is (x+26) meters.

The area of the larger square is equal to the area of the garden plus the area of the walkway:

(x+26)^2 = x^2 + 58

Expanding the left side of the equation gives:

x^2 + 52x + 676 = x^2 + 58

Subtracting x^2 from both sides and simplifying gives:

52x = -618

Dividing both sides by 52 gives:

x = -12

Since the side length of the garden cannot be negative, this solution is extraneous and should be discarded.

Therefore, there is no valid solution to the problem as stated. It is not possible to have a walkway with an area of 58 square meters surrounding a square garden with positive dimensions.

The general formula of an ellipse is :

x^2/b^2 + y^2/a^2 = 1

where b is the minor axis

and

a is the major axis.


This general equation is applicable to an ellipse with the vertex located at (0,0). Since the walkway is 2.5 meters wide, the bigger ellipse would now have the new dimensions:

Length = 15 +2*2.5 = 20 m

Width = 8 + 2*2.5 = 13 m

Consequently,

a = 20/2 = 10

b = 13/2 = 6.5

The equation would then be:

x2/10^2 + y2/6.5^2 = 1

16. A pool measuring 10 feet by 20 feet will have a cement walkway around it. The area covered by the pool and the cement walkway is 600 square feet. If the walkway must have a uniform width around the entire pool, what is the width of the walkway?​


Answer:

Let x be the width of the cement walkway around the pool.

The total area covered by the pool and the walkway is the sum of the area of the pool and the area of the walkway. The area of the pool is given by the product of the length and the width:

10 feet x 20 feet = 200 square feet

To find the total area covered by the pool and the walkway, we add the area of the pool to the area of the walkway. The area of the walkway is equal to the difference between the area of the larger rectangle and the area of the smaller rectangle:

(L + 2x)(W + 2x) - LW

where L and W are the length and width of the pool, respectively.

Setting this expression equal to 600 square feet, we get:

(10 + 2x)(20 + 2x) - 200 = 600

Expanding the left-hand side and simplifying, we get:

4x^2 + 60x - 200 = 0

Dividing both sides by 4, we get:

x^2 + 15x - 50 = 0

Using the quadratic formula, we get:

x = (-15 ± sqrt(15^2 + 4(50)))/2

x ≈ 2.07 feet (to two decimal places)

Since the width of the walkway must be uniform around the entire pool, the width of the walkway is approximately 2.07 feet.
