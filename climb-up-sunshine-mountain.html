x = the time John needs to climb up

1 - x = the time John needs to go down

Since John is passing the same distance up and down, and distance = speed*time we have:

10x = (1 - x)*5

x = 1/3 hr

The distance John climbed was:

10*(1/3) = 10/3 = 3.33 miles.

16. A man is climbing up a mountain that is inclined. He has to travel 100 km to reach the top of the mountain. Every day He climbs up 2 km forward in the daytime. Exhausted, he then takes a rest there at night time. At night, while he is asleep, he slips down 1 km backward because the mountain is inclined. Then how many days does it take him to reach the mountain top?


Answer: ☕

It takes 99 days for him to reach the mountain top.

Lattelle's Solution:

He climbs up 2 km everyday.

2 KM ( Addition / + )

He slips 1 km when sleeping.

1 KM ( Subtraction / - )

He has to travel 100 km to reach the mountain top.

100 KM ( ? )

To find out, we must multiply.

2 km x 100 km = 200 km

1 km x 100 km = 100 km

Now, we use subtraction against 200km. We will use 100 km on 200 km.

It still results as a 100km. If we could pretend that the kilometer is actually days, we could find the actual answer. We will use 1 day against 2 days.

1 day - 2 days = 1 day.

It becomes 1 day. Now, we use 1 day against 100 days.

1 days - 100 days = 99 days

We have reached our conclusion. The answer is 99 days.
