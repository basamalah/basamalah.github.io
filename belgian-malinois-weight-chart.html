18. How did Belgian imperialism affect the indigenous people living in the Congo Free State? apex


Imperialism can be described as the policy of acquiring colonies and dependencies. One such example is the the European powers taking over Congo in the 1870 and enslaving the natives. Here some of the long term effects of imperialism in Congo.


Congo Free State was a colony that was put under King Leopold II of Belgium in 1885. It was not until 1908 that the colony was taken over by the Belgium Government and was at the same time renamed to Belgium Congo. Often, the Belgium Congo is cited as one of the most exploitative and brutal colonial regimes in today's history. It depicts the cruelty of the European colonization in Africa for economic gains.


When looking at the history of Belgium Congo colonization, we can not miss to mention some of the imperialism effects on this nation. The effects were both negative and positive. Some of the major effects include:

Positive Effects

Improved Economy

There was an increase in the production of Palm oil from 2500 tons to an amazing 23,000 within a period of 43 years. This was also the case with cotton.


Culture and Education

the imperialism brought about formal education systems with many indigenous languages being taught in schools sponsored by the government.


Healthcare

When Congo Free State changed its name to Belgium Congo, the healthcare sector was greatly improved.


Negative Effects

-The Congolese people faced a lot of discrimination, and were subjected to curfews and were banned from the white lands

-The indigenous people were forced into joining religious groups

-Reduced population as a result of deaths from starvation, war, forced labor and diseases

-the profits from the mines and crops in Congo was sent back to Europe leaving the Congolese people with nothing.
