A marketing manager tests a new sales strategy by randomly selecting 100 consumers with less than Php 150,000 gross income and 100 consumers with gross income of at least Php 150,000. The type of sampling technique use is Stratified random sampling.

Stratified random sampling

Stratified random sampling is a sampling technique with regard to a level (strata) in the population elements. Population elements are divided into several levels (stratification) based on the characteristics attached to them. In stratified random sampling, the population elements are grouped at certain levels with the aim that the sampling will be evenly distributed at all levels and the sample represents the character of all heterogeneous population elements.

Basic Concepts of Stratified Random Sampling

In general, the populations used as research objects tend to be heterogeneous. Because if it is known that the character of the population element is homogeneous, the sampling procedure does not need to be complicated, there is no need to use difficult sampling techniques and the sample size is taken quite a bit.

For example, when you are cooking vegetables, you just need to stir the vegetables and take a spoon to make a sample whether it is salty enough or not. Another example you can see in the case of blood sampling.

28. In a traditional format income statement, the gross margin is sales minus cost of goods sold.


Answer:

Yes, that is correct. In a traditional format income statement, the gross margin is calculated by subtracting the cost of goods sold from the total sales. The gross margin represents the amount of profit that a company has made from the sale of its products or services before taking into account other expenses, such as selling, general, and administrative expenses. The gross margin can be expressed as a percentage of total sales, and is used to measure the efficiency of a company's operations and to compare the profitability of different products or services.
