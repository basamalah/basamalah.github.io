2. What happen in hacienda luisita massacre?


On November 16, 2004, during the violent dispersal of the Hacienda Luisita workers' picket, fourteen (14) people were killed including two children aged 2 and 5 years old who died of suffocation from teargas lobbed by the police and army dispersal teams.


3. What happened to hacienda luisita massacre?


It was on November 16, 2004, when farmers from the Central Azucarera de Tarlac sugar mill wanted to have a ₱9.50 increase on their weekly wage, but only faced with guns, tear gas, and military vehicles to disseminate them, dispatched by then Labour Secretary Patricia Santo Tomás.

Seven of the people participating in the rally were killed, an estimated number of 121 people, adults, and children were injured, and 133 people were arrested and detained

In the months following the Hacienda Luisita massacre, eight more people who either supported or provided evidence to the farmers’ cause were found murdered. After the incident, Deputy Speaker Benigno ‘Noynoy’ Aquino III, Representative of Tarlac, claimed that the dispersal done by the enforcers was justified.

27. Difference between encomienda and hacienda


THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN ENCOMIENDA AND HACIENDAEncomienda was a labor system established by Spanish crown in the American colonies. A Spanish encomendero in the encomienda system was granted a number of native laborers who would pay tributes to him in exchange for his protection.

Haciendas were originally profit-making enterprises owned by hacendados. The granting of land by the Spanish crown first started in the form of haciendas to the Spanish conquistadors (those soldiers or explorers of Spain responsible for colonizing new lands in the name of the Spanish Crown)
