29. Definition of contemporary arts in the philippines


The Definition of Contemporary Arts

The word "contemporary art" refers to art created in the present day. The artists are usually still living and working. Rather than being just aesthetic, contemporary art is often about concepts and issues (the look of the work). Artists work with various theories and materials in various ways. Non-specialists can conflate the words "modern art" and "contemporary art" since "modern" and "contemporary" are synonyms in common English.

In its strictest sense, the word "contemporary art" refers to art created and generated by living artists today. Today's artists work in and adapt to a culturally complex, technologically advanced, and multifaceted global world. Contemporary artists use a variety of mediums to draw on and comment on contemporary culture. Viewers are asked to put aside questions like, “Is a piece of art good?” while engaging with contemporary art. or "Does the job have a pleasing aesthetic?" Rather, people decide whether art is “difficult” or “interesting.” Contemporary artists can challenge conventional notions of how art is defined, what constitutes art, and how art is created, while engaging in conversation with—and, in some cases, rejecting—predecessor styles and movements.

Contemporary postmodern artists oppose the idea of popular art and promote the concept of "artistic pluralism," or the adoption of a wide range of artistic intentions and styles. Contemporary artists use an infinite range of materials, sources, and forms to make work, whether they are inspired by or grounded in performance art, pop art, Minimalism, conceptual art, or film.
