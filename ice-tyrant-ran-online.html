15. Explain why tyrants came into power in ancient Greece AND how tyrants‚Äô rule led to the development of Oligarchy and Democracy governments in ancient Greece.


Answer:

The emergence of tyrants in ancient Greece was a direct result of the breakdown of traditional systems of governance previously known as the ‚Äòarete system‚Äô in which aristocrats, who had proven their excellence, were granted a certain amount of authority in their respective cities. In times of crisis or social unrest, citizens would select one person who they entrusted with increased powers, creating a tyrannical government. As the power wielded by the tyrant increased, they often abused their authority to the point where they had almost absolute control.

This tyranny eventually led to the development of other forms of government in ancient Greece, namely oligarchy and democracy. Oligarchies existed when the power was produced and maintained by only a few individuals in society. Democracy, on the other hand, was based on the principle of ‚Äòequality‚Äô where all citizens had an equal say in the governing of the state and discussed matters in the Assembly (an open-air forum). The tyranny of the few led to the citizens of Greece to seek out alternative forms of government in order to establish a more egalitarian and fair system. Thus, it can be said that the emergence of tyrants in Greece ultimately led to the development of oligarchy and democracy governments in ancient Greece.
