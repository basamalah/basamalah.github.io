2. problems that the characters "experience" in the summer Solstice by nick joaquin​


Answer:

Explanation:

Gender roles and expectations: The story portrays the rigid gender roles and expectations prevalent in Philippine society during the time period. Women were expected to be submissive and obedient to their husbands, while men were considered superior and dominant. The female characters in the story, particularly Doña Lupeng, struggle with this societal pressure to conform to these gender norms.

Social class and status: The story also explores the social class and status differences between characters. Doña Lupeng and her husband, Don Paeng, belong to the upper class, while their househelp, Amada, belongs to a lower social status. The story highlights the power dynamics and inequalities that exist between individuals of different classes.

Power struggle: A power struggle between Doña Lupeng and her husband is also evident in the story. Doña Lupeng desires to break free from the patriarchal constraints of her society and assert her independence, while Don Paeng wants to maintain his authority and control over her. This conflict creates tension and drama in the story.

Tradition vs. modernity: The story also touches upon the tension between tradition and modernity. Doña Lupeng's desire for independence and freedom conflicts with the traditional beliefs and customs of her society. Her desire to participate in the Tadtarin festival, which is traditionally a male-only festival, represents her desire to break free from the shackles of traditional gender roles and expectations.

Overall, "The Summer Solstice" explores themes of gender, power, social status, and tradition in a patriarchal society. The characters face various challenges as they navigate through these societal constraints, creating a compelling and thought-provoking story.

Explanation:

Nicomedes "Nick" Márquez Joaquín (May 4, 1917 – April 29, 2004) was a Filipino writer and journalist best known for his short stories and novels in the English language. He also wrote using the pen name Quijano de Manila. Joaquín was conferred the rank and title of National Artist of the Philippines for Literature. He has been considered one of the most important Filipino writers, along with José Rizal and Claro M. Recto. Unlike Rizal and Recto, whose works were written in Spanish, Joaquin's major works were written in English despite being a native Spanish speaker.

15. three generations by nick joaquin


Three Generations

by: Nick Joaquin

Plot and Setting:

Nick Joaquin’s full name is Nicomedes Marquez Joaquin. Born on the 4th of May, 1917 and lived in Paco, Manila. He was a writer, historian, and journalist. He wrote short stories and novels in English and used the pen name Quijano de Manila. He started writing stories, poems, and essays in 1934. Many of them were published in Manila magazines.

Summary:

the story "Three Generations" by Nick Joaquin follows Celo Monzon and his terrible childhood. He reflects on the unhappiness he experienced as a child after his own son decides to enter the priesthood. The story focuses on themes of sexuality, inheritance, traditions and acceptance as Monzon comes to terms with his grandfather's behavior during his childhood.

The text’s plot is an example of Man in a Hole.The setting of the text is on the time when patrimony ruled over anything. It is the time during the pre-Spanish period that men ruled their houses.
