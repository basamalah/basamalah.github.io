Capillaries

The link between the vessels that take blood out from the heart (arteries) and the vessels that return blood to the heart (capillaries) is formed by capillaries, the tiniest and most numerous of the blood vessels (veins). The exchange of materials between blood and tissue cells is the major function of capillaries. The metabolic activity of bodily tissues affects the distribution of capillaries. Because they are metabolically active and require an adequate supply of oxygen and nutrients, tissues including skeletal muscle, liver, and kidney have vast capillary networks. Connective tissue, for example, has a smaller number of capillaries than other tissues. A capillary network exists only in the epidermis of the skin, as well as the lens and cornea of the eye.

4. Differentiate between affrent and efferent neuron


Answer:
Afferent neurons, also known as sensory neurons, are responsible for transmitting information from sensory organs (such as the eyes, ears, nose, and skin) to the central nervous system (brain and spinal cord). These neurons are activated by stimuli from the environment or internal body changes, and they send signals towards the central nervous system. While Efferent neurons, also known as motor neurons, are responsible for transmitting information from the central nervous system to the muscles and glands in the body. These neurons are responsible for initiating movement, controlling glandular secretion, and providing control over various organs in the body. Efferent neurons are activated by the central nervous system and transmit signals away from the central nervous system towards the muscles and glands.

11. Renal corpuscles histology slides afferent and efferent arteriole


Answer:

"Renal corpuscles" are a component of the kidney, a vital organ responsible for filtering waste and excess substances from the blood. The renal corpuscle comprises the glomerulus and the Bowman's capsule. The glomerulus is a network of tiny blood vessels responsible for filtering blood. The Bowman's capsule is a sac-like structure that surrounds the glomerulus and collects the filtered fluid.

The "afferent arteriole" is a small artery that carries blood into the glomerulus, and the efferent arteriole is a small artery that carries blood away from the glomerulus. The afferent and efferent arterioles are essential in regulating blood flow and filtration in the renal corpuscle.

"Histology slides" are thin slices of tissue mounted on a glass slide and stained with a dye to make specific structures more visible under a microscope. A histology slide of a renal corpuscle would show the different components of the renal corpuscle, including the glomerulus and Bowman's capsule, as well as the afferent and efferent arterioles.
