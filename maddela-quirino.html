6. what is the festival in quirino​


PANAGDADAPUN FESTIVAL Quirino Province celebrates its annual Festivity, dubbed as “PANAGDADAPUN FESTIVAL” an Ilocano word that means assembly or the gathering of every Quirinian. A highlight of this annual celebration is the grand parade which was enjoined by different agencies found in the province together with the convergence of all tribes thru street dancing portraying the different cultures and history of the province. It is celebrated every month of September which coincides with the Araw ng Quirino Foundation every Municipality has its festival which features its unique culture and practices. It is being celebrated every September 6-10 of the year.

19. datos ni Elpidio Quirino​


Answer:

Elpidio Rivera Quirino (November 16, 1890 – February 29, 1956) was a Filipino lawyer and politician who served as the sixth president of the Philippines from 1948 to 1953

A lawyer by profession, Quirino entered politics when he became a representative of Ilocos Sur from 1919 to 1925. He was then elected as a senator from 1925 to 1935. In 1934, he became a member of the Philippine Independence Commission that was sent to Washington, D.C., which secured the passage of Tydings–McDuffie Act to the United States Congress. In 1935, he was also elected to the 1935 Constitutional Convention that drafted the 1935 Philippine Constitution for the newly established Philippine Commonwealth. In the new government, he served as secretary of the interior and finance under the cabinet of President Manuel L. Quezon.

After World War II, Quirino was elected vice-president in the April 1946 presidential election, consequently the second and last for the Commonwealth and first for the Third Republic. After the death of incumbent President Manuel Roxas in April 1948, he succeeded to the presidency. He won a full term under the Liberal Party ticket, defeating Nacionalista former president José P. Laurel as well as fellow Liberalista and former Senate President José Dira Avelino.

The Quirino administration was generally challenged by the HukBaLaHap, who ransacked towns and barrios. Quirino ran for president again in November 1953 but was defeated by Ramon Magsaysay in a landslide.

22. elpidio quirino biography


Elpidio Rivera Quirino (born Elpidio Quirino y Rivera; November 16, 1890 – February 29, 1956) was a Filipino politician of ethnic Ilocano descent who served as the sixth President of the Philippines from 1948 to 1953.

A lawyer by profession, Quirino entered politics when he became a representative of Ilocos Sur from 1919 to 1925. He was then elected as senator from 1925–1931. In 1934, he became a member of the Philippine independence commission that was sent to Washington, D.C., which secured the passage of Tydings–McDuffie Act to American Congress. In 1935, he was also elected to the convention that drafted the 1935 constitution for the newly established Commonwealth. In the new government, he served as secretary of the interior and finance under President Manuel Quezon's cabinet.

After World War II, Quirino was elected vice-president in the 1946 election, consequently the second and last for the Commonwealth and first for the third republic. After the death of the incumbent president Manuel Roxas in 1948, he succeeded the presidency. He won the president's office under Liberal Party ticket, defeating Nacionalista vice president and former president José P. Laurelas well as fellow Liberalista and former Senate President José Avelino.

The Quirino administration was generally challenged by the Hukbalahaps, who ransacked towns and barrios. Quirino ran for president again in 1953 but was defeated by Ramon Magsaysay.

24. administrasyon roxas?Administrasyon Quirino?​


Answer:

ADMINISTRASYONG ROXAS

Sa panahon ng pamamahala ng Roxas, ang konsepto ng pagtatag ng Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas ay binuo upang mapangalagaan ang ekonomiya ng bansa.

Bilang karagdagan, nakatuon din ang Roxas sa Sector ng Agrikultura. Ipinahayag niya na gawing epektibo ang Rice Share Tenancy Act ng 1933 sa buong bansa, na binago ng Republic Act 1946 o the Tenant Act.

Nagbigay din ng amnestiya si Roxas sa lahat ng mga akusado at sinasabing nakipagsabwatan sa mga Hapon, kasama na ang dating Pangulong Jose P. Laurel.

Ang desisyon na ito ni Pangulong Roxas ay nagresulta sa muling pagsasama ng mga Pilipino.

Bagaman ang pag-aalsa ang tema ng bawat aksyon ng Administrasyong Roxas, nabalot ito ng kontrobersya. Kabilang dito ang katiwalian sa kanyang gobyerno at pang-aabuso sa militar sa kanayunan na nagpalakas sa kilusang kaliwa-ang People's Army Against Japan o HUKBALAHAP, na pinamunuan ng kanilang kataas-taasang si Luis Taruc.

ADMINISTRASYONG QUIRINO

Ang sistema ng irigasyon ay napabuti din para sa paglaki ng Sektor ng Agrikultura.

Ang Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas at mga bangko sa kanayunan ay itinatag din sa panahon ni Quirino. Sila ang nagpapahiram sa mga magsasaka para sa kanilang suporta sa pananalapi.

Itinatag din ng Quirino Administration ang Social Security Commission at ang Action Committee ng Amelioration ng Pangulo upang magbigay ng tulong at pautang sa mga mahihirap na Pilipino.

Ang mga benepisyo ng mga programang ito ay kasama ang seguro sa pagtanda, aksidente at permanenteng kapansanan. Bilang karagdagan, ang katiyakan ng pagbibigay ng tulong para sa mga problema sa trabaho, ang kalusugan ng mga tao, lalo na ang mga ina.

Ngunit sa kabila ng mga positibong hakbang na ito, nabigo si Quirino na malutas ang problema ng agrarian at pantay na pamantayan ng pamumuhay lalo na sa mga liblib na lugar ng bansa. Ito ang naging tagapag-alab ng nabago na pag-igting ng paghihimagsik ng HUKBALAHAP.

Sa panahon ng termino ni Quirino, ang Integrity Board ay nabuo upang tumugon sa lumalaking panawagan para sa reporma ng gobyerno ng estado laban sa katiwalian, isang bagay na pati si Pangulong Quirino ay nadungisan.

Si Quirino ang kauna-unahang Pangulo na sumubok na paalisin siya sa pwesto sa pamamagitan ng proseso ng impeachment. Kasama sa mga akusasyon laban sa kanya ang nepotism at ang kabiguang gamitin ang kaban ng bayan sa wastong pamamaraan.

27. pamahalaan ni ipildio quirino​


Answer:

Si Elpidio Quirino y Rivera (Nobyembre 16, 1890 - Pebrero 29, 1956) ay isang abugadong Pilipino at pulitiko na nagsilbi bilang ikaanim na Pangulo ng Pilipinas mula 1948 hanggang 1953.

Isang abugado sa pamamagitan ng propesyon, si Quirino ay pumasok sa politika nang siya ay naging kinatawan ng Ilocos Sur mula 1919 hanggang 1925. Noon ay nahalal bilang senador mula 1925 hanggang 1931. Noong 1934, siya ay naging miyembro ng komisyon ng kalayaan ng Pilipinas na ipinadala sa Washington, DC, na sinigurado ang pagpasa ng Tydings – McDuffie Act sa American Congress. Noong 1935, nahalal din siya sa kombensiyon na bumalangkas ng konstitusyong 1935 para sa bagong itinatag na Komonwelt. Sa bagong gobyerno, nagsilbi siyang kalihim ng panloob at pananalapi sa ilalim ng gabinete ni Pangulong Manuel Quezon.

Matapos ang World War II, si Quirino ay nahalal bilang bise-pangulo noong halalan noong 1946, dahil dito ang pangalawa at huli para sa Commonwealth at una para sa pangatlong republika. Matapos ang pagkamatay ng nanunungkulang pangulo na si Manuel Roxas noong 1948, humalili siya sa pagkapangulo. Nanalo siya sa tanggapan ng pangulo sa ilalim ng tiket ng Liberal Party, tinalo ang bise presidente ng Nacionalista at dating pangulong José P. Laurel pati na rin ang kapwa Liberalista at dating Pangulo ng Senado na si José Avelino.

Ang pamamahala ng Quirino sa pangkalahatan ay hinamon ng mga Hukbalahaps, na sinalakay ang mga bayan at baryo. Si Quirino ay tumakbo muli bilang pangulo noong 1953 ngunit natalo ni Ramon Magsaysay.

28. elipidio quirino programs​


Answer:

Agricultural Land Reform - Quirino established the Agricultural Land Reform Code in 1950, which aimed to redistribute land to tenant farmers and promote agricultural productivity.

Import Substitution Industrialization - Quirino pursued a policy of import substitution industrialization to promote domestic manufacturing and reduce reliance on imported goods.

Social Welfare Programs - Quirino introduced various social welfare programs, including the establishment of the Social Security System, the National Vocational Rehabilitation Center, and the National Mental Hospital.

Education Reform - Quirino initiated reforms in the education sector, including the expansion of the public school system and the establishment of the Philippine Science High School.

Foreign Policy - Quirino pursued a policy of non-alignment in foreign affairs and established diplomatic relations with several countries, including the United States, Japan, and China.

Overall, Quirino's programs aimed to promote economic development, social welfare, and education, and his administration is remembered for its efforts to uplift the lives of Filipinos during a time of post-war reconstruction and economic growth.
