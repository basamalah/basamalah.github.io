26. What are the three basic characteristics of motivation and What are the 4 types of motivation?​


Answer:

The three basic characteristics of motivation are:

Activation: This refers to the initiation or start of a behavior or action towards a goal.

Persistence: This refers to the continuation of behavior or action towards a goal despite obstacles and challenges.

Intensity: This refers to the level of effort and concentration put into the behavior or action towards a goal.

The four types of motivation are:

Intrinsic motivation: This is the motivation that comes from within an individual. It is driven by personal interest, enjoyment, or satisfaction.

Extrinsic motivation: This is the motivation that comes from external factors, such as rewards or punishments.

Achievement motivation: This is the motivation to achieve success, attain goals, and perform well.

Social motivation: This is the motivation that comes from social influence, such as approval or recognition from others.
