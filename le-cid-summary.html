Answer:

"Les Misérables" is a 2012 musical film adaptation of the stage musical of the same name. The film is set in Paris, France during the 19th century and follows the story of ex-convict, Jean Valjean, as he tries to start a new life while being relentlessly pursued by the ruthless inspector Javert. Along the way, Valjean befriends a struggling single mother named Fantine and promises to care for her daughter, Cosette.

The film deals with themes of justice, love, redemption, and revolution. Valjean's journey to become a better person is central to the story, as he transforms from a hardened criminal to a compassionate and selfless individual. His encounter with the bishop who shows him mercy sets him on a path of redemption and leads him to adopt Cosette and raise her as his own daughter.

Javert, on the other hand, represents the strict adherence to the law and represents the corrupt and oppressive society of the time. His pursuit of Valjean becomes a symbol of the struggle between justice and mercy.

The film also depicts the growing unrest in France, leading up to the 1832 June Rebellion, and the struggle of the working-class people to better their lives. Characters like the students Marius and Enjolras, and the factory worker Gavroche, embody this struggle and the fight for justice and equality.

In the end, Valjean and Javert's final confrontation serves as a powerful commentary on the human condition and the importance of compassion and forgiveness. The film's beautiful musical numbers and powerful performances make it a moving and impactful portrayal of Victor Hugo's classic novel.

In conclusion, "Les Misérables" is a timeless story that touches on universal themes and provides a powerful commentary on the human condition. Its masterful blend of music, drama, and themes of love, justice, and redemption make it a film that will continue to be cherished and enjoyed for generations to come.

7. siya ay ang sumulat ng el cid noong 1207 ​


Answer:

Rodrigo Díaz de Vivar:

Rodrigo Díaz de Vivar (c. 1043 – 10 July 1099) was a Castilian knight and warlord in medieval Spain. Fighting with both Christian and Muslim armies during his lifetime, he earned the Arabic honorific al-sīd, which would evolve into El Cid (“the lord”), and the Spanish moniker El Campeador (“the champion”). He was born in Vivar del Cid, a village near the city of Burgos. As the head of his loyal knights, he came to dominate the Levante of the Iberian Peninsula at the end of the 11th century. He reclaimed the Taifa of Valencia from Moorish control for a brief period during the Reconquista, ruling the principality as its Prince (Señorío de Valencia) from 17 June 1094 until his death in 1099. His wife, Jimena Díaz, inherited the city and maintained it until 1102 when it was reconquered by the Moors.

Les Miserables is a novel by French author Victor Hugo, first published in 1862. The novel is set in France in the early 19th century and tells the story of Jean Valjean, a criminal who is released from prison and must find a new way to live his life. The novel is considered one of the greatest works of French literature and has been adapted for stage and screen many times.

The novel begins with an introduction to the characters and setting. We are introduced to Jean Valjean, a man who has recently been released from prison after serving nineteen years for stealing a loaf of bread. Valjean is given a second chance in life by a bishop, but is soon pursued by a police inspector named Javert. Valjean takes refuge in a convent, where he meets a young girl named Cosette. Cosette's father, a man named Fantine, has abandoned her and she is living in poverty. Valjean decides to take Cosette away from the convent and raise her as his own daughter.

The rest of the novel tells the story of Valjean's life, as he tries to escape Javert's pursuit and protect Cosette. Along the way, he meets a number of interesting characters, including the revolting Thénardier family and the heroic Éponine. The novel concludes with the Battle of Waterloo, in which Valjean plays a key role.

Overall, Les Miserables is a powerful and moving story about redemption and second chances. It is a testament to the human spirit and the ability of people to overcome adversity.

Through their struggles, Hugo paints a vivid picture of French society during a tumultuous period of history. Valjean breaks his parole and starts a new life under a new identity, becoming a successful businessman and mayor of a small town. However, his past catches up with him when he is pursued by Inspector Javert, a relentless police officer who is determined to capture Valjean and bring him to justice. As Valjean tries to evade capture, he encounters Fantine, a destitute woman who was once employed in his factory. A summary is a short overview of the main points of a text. The purpose of a summary is to quickly give the reader or listener an idea of what this material is saying. You may find it helpful to create summaries of your own work, but more often, you will create summaries of material by other authors, such as articles, plays, films, lectures, stories, or presentations.

Why Summarize?

At some point in your classes, you will likely be given an assignment to summarize a specific text, an assignment in which summary is the sole intent. You will also use summaries in more holistic ways, though, incorporating them along with paraphrase, quotation, and your own opinions into more complex pieces of writing. You might summarize for several reasons, both in your time as a student and in your life outside of education.

Here are some common ones:

A summary can show your understanding of the main points of an assigned reading or viewing, so your instructor might ask you to summarize in order to know that you’ve understood the material.

You might summarize a section from a source, or even the whole source, when the ideas in that source are critical to an assignment you are working on and you feel they need to be included, but they would take up too much space in their original form.

You might also summarize when the general ideas from a source are important to include in your work, but the details included in the same section as those main ideas aren’t needed for you to make your point. For example, technical documents or in-depth studies might go into much, much more detail than you are likely to need to support a point you are making for a general audience. These are situations in which a summary might be a good option.

Summarizing is also an excellent way to double-check that you understand a text–if you can summarize the ideas in it, you likely have a good grasp on the information it is presenting. This can be helpful for school-related work, such as studying for an exam or researching a topic for a paper, but is also useful in daily life when you encounter texts on topics that are personally or professionally interesting to you.

What Makes Something a Summary?

When you ask yourself, after reading an article (and maybe even reading it two or three times), “What was that article about?” and you end up jotting down–from memory, without returning to the original article to use its language or phrases–three things that stood out as the author’s main points, you are summarizing. Summaries have several key characteristics.

You’re summarizing well when you

Use your own words.

Significantly condense the original text.

Provide accurate representations of the main points of the text they summarize.

Avoid personal opinion.

Summaries are much shorter than the original material—a general rule is that they should be no more than 10% to 15% the length of the original, and they are often even shorter than this.

It can be easy and feel natural, when summarizing an article, to include our own opinions. We may agree or disagree strongly with what this author is saying, or we may want to compare their information with the information presented in another source, or we may want to share our own opinion on the topic. Often, our opinions slip into summaries even when we work diligently to keep them separate. These opinions are not the job of a summary, though. A summary should only highlight the main points of the article.

25. mga pangyayari na may kaugnayan kay el cid​


El Cid Campeador

El Cid Campeador El Cid Campeador ay isang mahal na tao at lider sa militar mula Castille. Siya rin ay kilala para sa kanyang kadalubhasaan sa mga braso at labanan ang kanyang mga diskarte. Ipinanganak sa 1043 bilang Rodrigo Diaz de Vivar, El Cid ay desterado sa pamamagitan ng Hari Alfonso VI ngunit sa kalaunan ay tinatawag na bumalik upang magsilbi bilang isang lider hukbo. Ang kanyang pagpapatapon ay dumating tungkol sa bilang isang resulta ng isang lagas sa Alfonso VI sa ibabaw ng isang paglalakbay-dagat sa Granada na ang hari ay hindi approve.El Cid ay interesado sa nakasulat na mga gawa tungkol sa militar diskarte. Siya tangkilikin ang mga gawa ng iba't ibang mga klasikong mga manunulat mula sa Rome at Greece. El Cid ginamit ang kaalaman nagkamit siya mula sa pagbasa ng panitikan tulad ng mga diskarte para sa labanan. Ang kanyang pinaka-malakas na taktika ay sikolohikal na digma na kanyang ginamit upang takutin at makuha ang mga kaaway. Siya pinamamahalaang upang lupigin lugar tulad ng Valencia, Alucidia at Castejon.El Cid ay may-asawa sa Jimena Diaz, ang mga anak na babae ng Count Gomez de Gormaz sa Hulyo 1075. Sama-sama sila magbutas dalawang anak na babae at isang anak na lalaki. Ang kanilang mga anak na lalaki Diego Rodriguez sumali sa hukbo at pinatay sa Labanan ng Consuegra na ina-fought laban sa Almoravids. El Cid ng dalawang anak na babae Maria at Cristina ay kapwa may-asawa sa mataas na lipunan. Tulad ng kanyang anak Diego, El Cid ng buhay natapos na kapag siya ay namatay sa panahon ng labanan matapos ang Almoravids invaded Valencia kung saan siya ay naninirahan. Siya ay inilatag upang magpahinga sa Castile pagkatapos ng kanyang asawa tumakas sa kanyang body.Due sa kanyang militar kabutihan, El Cid ay remembered sa kasaysayan sa pamamagitan ng panitikan at pelikula. "El Cantar del Mio Cid" ay isang sikat na tula na kung saan ay pinaniniwalaan na isang representasyon ng mga buhay ng El Cid.
