6. the alpine fault in new zealand is an example of what plate boundary?​


Answer:

Alpine Fault. The Alpine Fault is a geological fault that runs almost the entire length of New Zealand's South Island (c. 480 km) and forms the boundary between the Pacific Plate and the Indo-Australian Plate. The Southern Alps have been uplifted on the fault over the last 12 million years in a series of earthquakes.

22. what are you likely to find in an alpine tundra


If a person were to travel to an Alpine Tundra, he or she will likely find that it has very little to no trees at all. Below the snow-capped mountains are many low flowering plants and some specialized ones, depending on which alpine tundra was visited. Once there, a person is subjected to twice the amount of UV radiation from the sun, despite the cooler air. Although the alpine tundra gets occasional visits from large animals for grazing opportunities, none but small rabbit-sized creatures would choose to make it their permanent home.  

An Alpine Tundra is a delicately balanced ecosystem that has developed for millions of years upon its reliance on its secluded nature. So much so that hikers are advised to step on rocks whenever they can to avoid crushing the inconspicuous low lying plants.

28. Why are greenhouse gases like the glass in the greenhouse?


Kasi ganito yan yung mga greenhouse gases ang nagpapapasok ng heat from sunlight at sya din ang nagta-trap ng heat sa mga heat na gustong mag bounce back/reflect na mga heat. Parang sa glass lang din ng greenhouse nakakapasok yung heat sa loob kahit may glass then ita-trap din nung glass yung heat. A greenhouse lets sunlight pass through so that the plants inside would receive the necessary nutrients to survive, and the glass helps trap that heat inside. This is exactly how greenhouse gases in the Earth work.
