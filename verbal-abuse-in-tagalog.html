16. What is the effect of an verbal abuse?​


Answer:

Verbal abuse can have a profound and lasting effect on a person's emotional and psychological well-being. It can cause feelings of shame, humiliation, anxiety, and depression, and can erode a person's sense of self-worth and self-esteem. Verbal abuse can also lead to feelings of powerlessness, helplessness, and isolation, and can damage interpersonal relationships, leading to social withdrawal and difficulty in trusting others. In severe cases, verbal abuse can lead to post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), which can have long-lasting and debilitating effects on a person's mental health. It is important to seek support and help if you or someone you know is experiencing verbal abuse.

26. what is the purpose of verbal abuse?​


Answer:

Verbal abuse is a form of psychological aggression in which someone uses language to hurt, demean, or control another person. The purpose of verbal abuse can vary depending on the individual and the situation, but it generally involves exerting power and control over another person, causing emotional harm, or manipulating someone's behavior.

Some possible purposes of verbal abuse include:

1. To intimidate or control: Verbal abusers may use language to make others feel afraid or to control their behavior. They may threaten physical harm, or use insults or put-downs to keep others in line.

2. To cause emotional pain: Verbal abusers may use language to deliberately hurt another person's feelings, to undermine their self-esteem, or to make them feel inferior.

3. To manipulate: Verbal abusers may use language to get others to do what they want, or to get them to feel a certain way. They may use guilt, blame, or other forms of manipulation to achieve their goals.

4. To vent anger or frustration: Verbal abuse can also be a way for someone to release their anger or frustration. They may lash out at others with insults or criticisms, even if those things are not justified or accurate.

It's important to note that verbal abuse is never acceptable or justified. It can have serious consequences for the victim's mental health and well-being, and it can damage relationships and social connections. If you are experiencing verbal abuse, it's important to seek help and support from a trusted friend, family member, or mental health professional.

28. verbally abuse by your teacher? why?


Answer:

WHY TEACHERS VERBALLY ABUSE THEIR STUDENTS?

Teachers choose to humiliate students for several reasons: to gain control over them, because the teacher is desperate; to frighten other students; or because they're over-compensating for their own lack of confidence.

A big part of determining whether or not you are being bullied is by reflecting on how your interactions with that person make you feel. If you feel anxious, isolated, put down, humiliated, etc., then you are probably being bullied.

Bullying happens when someone treats you poorly over and over. If your teacher makes you upset one time, that doesn’t really count as bullying. Think back over your interaction with your teacher and consider if there have been repeated instances when they made you feel bad or afraid.

Sometimes teachers have to discipline students or make them do things they don’t want to do. This is part of their job and isn’t considered bullying as long as they make the entire class do the same thing.

Think about how the teacher has treated you. Have they made insulting or derogatory comments to you? Are they punishing you when you're not doing anything wrong, blaming you for things you didn't do, or giving you harsher punishments than your classmates? Is this happening again and again? If so, you're probably being bullied and need to speak to your parents right away

If you're being punished for something, just accept your punishment, and if you think you were truly treated unfairly, tell someone about it later (like the principal or your parents). But if the teacher is genuinely bullying you, like verbally or physically abusing you, just get up and leave the room. They cannot force you to stay there.

When a principal tells a teacher about a complaint such as bullying, they will generally be nicer to the student because they know their job is on the line. Some of them will begin to bully again once they think they have been nice to their bully victims long enough to save their job. Always do what you need to do so you can save yourself and others from bullying.

While they are bullying you, or doing what you think is bullying you, try to remain calm. If you know what they claim you are doing or need to do is untrue or the remedy (or any remedy) is unrealistic or unnecessary, just ignore them.

When a teacher is a bully, they almost always bully some other students. Ask other students how they feel about the teacher and share your experiences. #BrainlyFast
