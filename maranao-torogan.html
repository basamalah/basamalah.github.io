3. Torogan is a house of upper class maranao true or false​


➪True

The Torogan is the ancestral house and the royal residence of the upper-class Maranao. It is commonly found in Marawi City and other areas in Lanao del Sur province. Within the sultanate, no one can have a house bigger than the Torogan which is the dwelling place of the datu along with his wives and children.

The Maranao are an indigenous people of the southern Philippines who hail from the shores of Lake Lanao in the province of Lanao del Sur on the island of Mindanao. Most of the Maranao people are Muslim. They are known for their art, weaving, wood, plastic and metal crafts, as well as the epic Darangen. They are ethnically and culturally closely related to Iranun and Maguindanao, and all three groups are speakers of the Danao languages.

The Maranao culture can be characterized by:

Lanao Lake.Sarimanok (Papanoka Mra and Mara-patik).Torogan, the tallest form of the royal house of Maranao, whose architecture is called the most aesthetic in all of the Philippines.Darangen, UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage.Send, pre-Hispanic handwriting based on Arabic letters with 19 consonants and 7 vowels. Singkil, traditional dance.Okir, the art of carving on wooden artifacts and brass utensils

Sarimanok (Papanoka Mra) is a legendary Maranao bird which has become a symbol in their art. Sarimanok takes the form of a bird with colorful wings and tail, and holds a fish in its beak or claws. Sarimanok's head is shaped like the head of a hud-hud bird (Balatoc in maranao) and is decorated with scroll, leaf and spiral motifs (okir).

The Tausugs people generally work as fishermen and collect marine products such as pearls, sea jelly, gamat and trade by barter between North Borneo (Sabah) and Zamboanga and with other areas around Southeast Asia. Their trading activities extend outside the area around the waters of the Sulu Sea to the Sulawesi Sea and the South China Sea. The Tausug people make the ocean their source of life.

13. ang sarimanok ay makikita sa bahay na torogan ng maranao?​


Answer:

TAMA

Explanation:

Ang Torogan ay isang tradisyonal na bahay na itinayo ng mga Maranao sa lalawigan ng Lanao, Mindanao, Pilipinas.[1] Ang torogan ay isang tanda ng mataas na katayuan sa lipunan. Ito ay isang noo'y tahanan sa mga Sultan o Datu sa pamayanang Maranao. Sa kasalukuyan, mga bahay na yari sa konkreto na ang mahahanap sa mga buong pamayanang Maranao, ngunit may mga natitira pang mga torogan na sandaang taong gulang na. Ito'y mahahanap sa Dayawan at sa lungsod ng Marawi, pati na rin malapit sa lawa ng Lanao. Hindi kumpleto ang isang torogan kapag wala ang maalamat na ibong Sarimanok na dapat makikita sa loob ng bahay.

It comes from the words "sari" and "manok." "Sari" means "assorted" or "various" while manok originally meant "bird" as evidenced by early Spanish colonial sources, but came to mean only "chicken", which is how it is understood today (i.e. the creature is a "bird/chicken of assorted colors").
