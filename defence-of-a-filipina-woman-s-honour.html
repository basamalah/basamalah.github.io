3. why Amorsolo painted Defense of the filipina woman's honor


Answer:

Because during the Japanese occupation of the Philippines during World War II, Amorsolo’s customer base dissipated due to the hard economic times. It was a real challenge to him to acquire painting supplies. The transportation to rural places were difficult to find and so it deprived Amorsolo from visiting these places where he normally painted his popular landscapes. Typical pastoral scenes were replaced by the depictions of a war-torn nation. He spent his days at his home near the Japanese garrison, where he sketched war scenes from the house's windows or rooftop. He documented the destruction of many landmarks in Manila and the pain, tragedy and death experienced by Filipino people, with his subjects.  

Explanation:

About the painting "Defense of the Filipina Woman's Honor"

• It is one of Amorsolo's famous war era painting is the “Defense of a Filipina Woman's Honor”  

• The painting showed a Filipino man holding a bolo about to defend a woman who is either his wife or daughter, from being raped by an unseen Japanese soldier.

Other painting of Amorsolo during World War II

The rape of Manila (1942) Bombing of Intendencia (1942) The Explosion (1944)

Amorsolo frequently portrayed the lives and suffering of Filipina women during World War II.

The wartime paintings of Amorsolo were exhibited at the Malacañang Presidential Palace.
