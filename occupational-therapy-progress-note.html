5. B. Fill in the chart with the correct form of the verb. (Note to the Teacher - Performance Task, 5 points Subject Verb Past Progressive Present Progressive Future Progressive Jona & Mhy walk Carpenters climb Bela swim Pupils clean Jun drive​


Answer:

Past Progressive

Answer:

Jona & Mhy was walking.Carpenters were climbing. Bela was swimming. Pupils were cleaning. Jun was driving.

____________________

Present Progressive

Answer:

Jona & Mhy are walking.Carpenters are climbing. Bela is swimming. Pupils are cleaning. Jun is driving.

Answer:

Future Progressive

Jona & Mhy will be walking.Carpenters will be climbing. Bela will be swimming.Pupils will be cleaning.Jun will be driving.

Explanation:

The PAST PROGRESSIVE TENSE indicates continuing action, something that was happening, going on, at some point in the past. This tense is formed with the helping "to be" verb, in the past tense, plus the present participle of the verb (with an -ing ending): I was riding my bike all day yesterday.

The present progressive tense (sometimes called the present continuous) is a tense which describes an action which began in the past and is still going on now.

The future progressive tense is used to describe an ongoing activity in the future.

28. what is the difference between gene therapy and cell therapy​


Answer:

Gene therapy involves the transfer of genetic material, usually in a carrier or vector, and the uptake of the gene into the appropriate cells of the body. cell therapy involves the transfer of cells with the relevant function into the patient. Some protocols utilize both gene therapy and cell therapy.
