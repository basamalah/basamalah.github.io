The three perilous encounters that Perseus experienced during his adventure.

Perseus is a demigod in Greek mythology because he was the son of Zeus, the god of thunder, and Dana, the daughter of Acrisius of Argos. As a baby, he was tossed into the sea in a box with his mother. This is because of Acrisius, his grandfather, who had predicted that his grandson would slay him. The box would be picked up on the island of Seriphus where Perseus would grow up.

1. Meeting with Graiae - On his mission to slay Medusa, he forced Medusa’s sister, the Graiae, to assist him. Perseus seized Graiae’s one eye and one tooth and refused to return them until Graiae provided him with winged sandals, the Hades cap, a sickle to decapitate Medusa, and a bag in which to conceal the head.

2. Slaying Medusa - Upon reaching the place where Medusa is, Perseus used the shield Athena gave him to look at Medusa’s reflection and beheaded Medusa while she is sleeping.

3. Saving Andromeda - An oracle told Andromeda's father, King Cepheus, that exposing Andromeda to a monster would cure her illnesses, which he did. Passing by, Perseus noticed the princess and fell in love with her. He turned the monster into stone using Medusa’s head and married Andromeda.
