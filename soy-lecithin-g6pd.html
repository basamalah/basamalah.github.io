26. what is g6pd defficiency​


G6PD deficiency is an inherited condition

Explanation:

It is when the body doesn't have enough of an enzyme called G6PD (glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase). This enzyme helps red blood cells work correctly. A lack of this enzyme can cause hemolytic anemia. This is when the red blood cells break down faster than they are made.


27. 7. It is a severe deficiency of thyroid hormone in newborns and previously known as cretinism.A. galactosemiaB. g6pd def.C. congenital hypothyroidism (CH)D. congenital adrenal hyperlapsia (CAH)​


IT IS A SEVERE DEFICIENCY OF THYROID HORMONE IN NEWBORNS AND WAS PREVIOUSLY KNOWN AS CRETINISM.

ANSWER: C. Congenital hypothyroidism (CH)

A severe shortage of thyroid hormone in infants is known as congenital hypothyroidism, sometimes known as cretinism. It causes physical abnormalities, poor growth, and neurological problems. An issue with the baby's thyroid gland or a shortage of iodine in the mother's body during pregnancy could cause the disease.

Iodine is required for the production of thyroid hormones in a baby's body. Thyroid hormones are necessary for the development of the brain, neurological system, and other parts of the body to grow properly.

The basic treatment is to administer the thyroid hormone to the baby (levothyroxine). Without treatment within the first four weeks following birth, the intellectual handicap could become permanent.

29. what is the cooperative solvent effect of lecithin and albumin?​


Explanation:

Not sure what you mean by "cooperative solvent effect".

However, lecithin is an emulsifier consisting of a variety of ingredients, the active components being phospholipids - these are long chain polar lipids. The emulsifying properties of lecithin result from this combination of a polar (hydrophilic) group attached to a non-polar (hydrophobic) lipid chain, and means that they can help disperse an oil phase into an aqueous solution or vice versa, creating an emulsion (oil-in-water or water-in-oil respectively). These emulsions are not solutions, but rather are very well dispersed micro-droplets of the secondary phase, and this explains why emulsions are opaque rather than completely transparent (think of a vinaigrette).

Albumins are water-soluble proteins - massive polar molecules - and I expect work by a similar mechanism.
