2. A 3-0.6 pole 50 Hz induction motor is running at 5% slip. What is the speed of motor? a. 950 rpm b. 850 rpm c. 900 rpm d. 1000 rpm​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The rotor speed of a motor is

[tex]\boxed{N_{r} = \frac{120f}{P}(1 - s)}[/tex]

where:

Nr = rotor speed

f = frequency

P = number of poles

s = slip

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Listing the given values

f = 50 Hz

P = 6

s = 5% = 0.05

Solving for Nr

[tex]N_{r} = \dfrac{120f}{P}(1 - s)[/tex]

[tex]N_{r} = \dfrac{120(50)}{6}(1 - 0.05)[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{N_{r} = \text{950 rpm}}[/tex]

Therefore, the rotor speed of the motor is 950 rpm

Answer:

a. 950 rpm

5. A small steel roulette ball rolls around the inside of a 30 cm diameter roulette wheel .It is spun at 150 rpm, but it slows to 60 rpm over an interval of 5.0s how many revolutions does the ball make during these 5.0s?​


Answer:

-5.18 revolutions

Explanation:

To calculate the number of revolutions the ball makes during the 5.0s interval, we need to determine the angular velocity of the ball at the beginning and end of the interval, and then use that information to calculate the angular displacement of the ball.

First, we'll convert the initial angular velocity from RPM (revolutions per minute) to radians per second. To do this, we'll use the formula:

Angular velocity (rad/s) = 2 * pi * RPM / 60

where pi = 3.14

So, the initial angular velocity of the ball is:

2 * 3.14 * 150 / 60 = 10.47 rad/s

Next, we'll convert the final angular velocity of the ball from RPM to radians per second:

2 * 3.14 * 60 / 60 = 6.28 rad/s

Now that we have the initial and final angular velocities, we can calculate the angular displacement of the ball during the 5.0s interval using the formula:

Angular displacement = (final angular velocity - initial angular velocity) * time

So, the angular displacement of the ball during the 5.0s interval is:

(6.28 - 10.47) * 5.0 = -32.35 rad

To convert this angular displacement to the number of revolutions, we'll divide by 2 * pi:

-32.35 / (2 * 3.14) = -5.18

So, the ball makes -5.18 revolutions during the 5.0s interval.

The answer is a negative value because the ball is slowing down over the 5.0s interval.

19. Through how many degrees does a 33 rpm turntable rotate in 0.27 s?


Solution:

Convert the rotational speed from rpm (revolutions per minute) to rad/s.

[tex]33\:\dfrac{\text{rev}}{\text{min}} \times\dfrac{1\text{ min}}{60\text{ s}} \times\dfrac{2\pi\text{ rad}}{1\text{ rev}} =3.456\text{ rad/s}[/tex]

The angular speed is

[tex]\omega=\dfrac{\Delta\theta}{t}[/tex]

Solving the angular rotation and converting it from radians to degrees, we get

[tex]\Delta\theta=\omega t=(3.456\text{ rad/s})(0.27\text{ s})=0.933\text{ rad}\\\\=0.933\text{ rad}\times\dfrac{360^\circ}{2\pi\text{ rad}} =\boxed{\bold{53.4^\circ}}[/tex]
