15. how is the creed a trinitarian?​


Answer:

The Nicene Creed is considered trinitarian because it affirms the belief in the three persons of the Holy Trinity: the Father, the Son (Jesus Christ), and the Holy Spirit. The creed states that the Father is the creator of all things, that Jesus is the Son of God and was born of the Virgin Mary, suffered, died, and rose again, and that the Holy Spirit is the giver of life who proceeds from the Father and the Son. By acknowledging these three distinct persons as one Godhead, the Nicene Creed asserts the central doctrine of the trinitarian belief.
