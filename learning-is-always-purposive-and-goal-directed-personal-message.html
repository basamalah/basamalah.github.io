3. how can the goal and purpose of communication affect the content of the message​


Answer:

The goal and purpose of communication can significantly affect the content of the message because it determines the focus of the message and the information that needs to be conveyed.

For example, if the goal of the communication is to inform, the content of the message will likely focus on providing accurate and relevant information. The language and tone of the message may be more formal and objective, and the message may be structured in a logical and straightforward way to convey the information effectively.

On the other hand, if the goal of the communication is to persuade, the content of the message will likely focus on presenting arguments and evidence to support a particular position or point of view. The language and tone of the message may be more emotive and persuasive, and the message may be structured in a way that is designed to appeal to the emotions and beliefs of the audience.

In both of these cases, the goal and purpose of the communication are driving the content of the message and shaping the way that it is conveyed. Understanding the goal and purpose of a message can help you to more effectively communicate your own ideas and to more effectively understand and interpret the messages that you receive from others.

16. Pa explain po at example 1. THE SOURCE - in a teaching learning situation the teacher is the SOURCE of communication by originating or perceiving an idea or purpose which she wants to communicate in order to produce the particular response in the learner2. THE MESSAGE - The goal intent or purpose to be communicated by the teacher is express through a message, in reality the message is a physical form of words it is the translation of the ideas ​


Explanation:

The communication process consists of several elements that work together to convey a message from a sender to a receiver. The two elements you mentioned are the source and the message. I will explain each of them and give an example.

1. The source: The source is the person who originates or perceives an idea or purpose that they want to communicate. In a teaching and learning situation, the teacher is the source of communication since they are the ones who have the knowledge and skills that they want to pass on to their students. The teacher uses various methods like verbal instructions, visual aids, and demonstrations, to convey their message to the students.

For example, a teacher teaching a science class will be the source of information for the students. They will explain scientific concepts and theories to the students, and provide examples to help them understand the topic better.

2. The message: The message is the information that the source wants to communicate. It is the goal, intent, or purpose that the source wants to achieve through communication. In a teaching and learning situation, the message is usually in the form of words, but it can also be conveyed through visual aids, demonstrations, or other means.

For example, the message that the science teacher wants to communicate to the students may be about the process of photosynthesis in plants. They will explain the process in detail, using words and diagrams to help the students understand the concept better. The message will be conveyed through the teacher's words and actions, and the students will receive the information and process it to gain knowledge and understanding.
