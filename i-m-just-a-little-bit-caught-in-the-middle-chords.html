5. How do rocks turn into soil? Just asking, im a little bit confused


Answer:

Soil is formed through the process of rock weathering. Weathering is the breakdown of rocks into smaller particles when in contact with water (flowing through rocks), air or living organisms. Weathering can occur physically, biologically or chemically.

Answer:

Rocks turn into soil when they break down by different factors that help this to happen.

This is called weathering.

Weathering is the process of breaking rocks down into smaller pieces, and it is classified into 2 types:

Physical, in other words mechanical, and chemical.

Physical Weathering

    This type of weathering breaks down rocks without changing their chemical properties or their composition. It only changes the size and shape of rocks.

:: Agents of Physical Weathering:

-Water

-Wind

-Temperature

-Plants

-Animals and Human Activity

As for Chemical Weathering:
    It breaks down rocks by changing its chemical properties or composition. Not only does it change the size and shape, but also its composition.

::Agents of Weathering:

-Carbon Dioxide

-Oxygen

-Hydrolysis

-Lichens

Conclusion:
Chemical and mechanical weathering are both types of weathering, but they have lots of differences.
