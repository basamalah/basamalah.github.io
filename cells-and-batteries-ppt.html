11. give 2 characteristics each for wet cell and dry cell battery


Wet cell and dry cells are essentially two types of energy sources. To differentiate each, let's go through them one by one, including their advantages and disadvantages.

Wet Cells

Wet cells are the predecessor of dry cell batteries. Wet cells use liquid electrolytes placed in separate containers. The electrodes are dropped then into the liquid electrolytes to ensure the flow of current.Wet cells also use highly acidic compounds such as sulfuric acid as a source of electrolytes.

ADVANTAGE: Wet cells are fairly cheap and have a longer lasting cycle for charge and discharge. This makes them ideal for use in motors and cars.

DISADVANTAGE: Wet cells have to be stored properly and upright. The electrolyte solution used in wet cell batteries are also highly corrosive.

Dry Cells

Dry cells use a paste-like solution as the source of electrolytes. It can be compounds like potassium hydroxide or sodium hydroxide which then reacts with manganese dioxide when the circuit is complete.Dry cells cells can also be made up of a combination of zinc and carbon ( the black part inside of a small dry cell) and it is placed inside a metal container.

ADVANTAGE: Dry cells are usually on-the-go. They are portable and can be used in most modern devices.

DISADVANTAGE: There is a risk of combustion if these are not stored properly or are carelessly placed in places with metals. This results in an unintended discharge of energy.
