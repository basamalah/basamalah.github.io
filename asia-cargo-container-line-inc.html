1. Why are cargo units containing hanging loads usually located on the center line, towards amidships?​


Cargo units with hanging loads and very high cargo units are especially prone to tipping, so they should be placed in positions with the least movement, such as on the center line, amidships, or on a deck near the waterline.

Cargo is defined as bulk goods or merchandise transported by ship, plane, or vehicle. Cargo is derived from the Spanish verb cargar, which means "to load," and is divided into two categories: general cargo and bulk cargo. General cargo is unitized, which means it is transported in defined load units, whereas bulk cargo is not.

[tex]\quad\leadsto\quad\sf Volume_{(cone)} = \red{\dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h} [/tex]

[tex]\quad\leadsto\quad\sf Volume_{(sphere)} = \red{\dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3 } [/tex]

Explanation:

The equation for volume is as follows, where l is length, w is width, and h is height :

l ⋅ w ⋅ h = V

Knowing this, we can input the given length, width, and height from the equation:

50 ⋅ 10 ⋅ 8 = V

After simplification, volume is found to be 4000 cubic feet:

4000 = V

As the question demands the answer in cubic yards, the volume of 4000 feet must be converted. The ratio of feet to yards is below.

3 : 1

In other words, every yard is three feet.

To find the value of 4000 cubic feet in yards, we must divide by three. Y is yards.

=y

Therefore, the volume in cubic yards is roughly 1333.3333.
