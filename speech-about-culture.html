Culture also means various performing arts like dance, instrumental music, singing, painting, drama, creating writing, craft making, debating, elocution and many more. These are often characteristic of a civilization.

conclusion :

11. Goodwill speech example about culture


“Culture is the total way of life that people in the society are blessed with. Culture is the characteristics of a particular group of people, defined by everything from language, religion, cuisine, social habits, music and arts. Through culture we are governed by norms and customs, which are the pillars of any society. If these pillars are not properly followed in our society, our culture will resemble a perfume with no smell. It’s a gradual existence of presenting those norms and customs to people who surround us through music, dances, arts, crafts, food, dressing style, languages just to mention a few.

Culture has been known since man came to an existence and till today we are still practicing it. Through culture we learn how to be better people in the society like living as brothers and sisters. It’s very hard for a person to live without culture because it’s like an antelope at the middle of hungry lioness. A human being is a very complex creature. Every individual human has a distinguished attitude, behaviors as well as traits. It is only through culture where human beings act uniformly and live as a society. Thus, in a simple definition of culture we all agree that culture is a totality way of life of a certain given society as they live and continue to struggle against the nature.  

Africa is believed to be the cornerstone of culture and every year, thousands of people from all over the world visit Africa to witness the beautiful culture, history and other amazing natural phenomena which Africa is blessed with and which it cherishes, for instance the earth surface features, wild animals, water bodies as well as hospitality of the people in the continent. Also, food, songs, costumes just to mention a few. Just as we are going to witness in this very day.

The way, we Africans handle our lives and whatever surrounds us; that is actually our culture, and it is through which we are identified. However, there are many different cultures from other communities and from other parts of the world, which remain to be the part of great adventure and identity to those people who live in those communities. We also recognize and appreciate their cultures. Wherever you are and wherever you live, your culture is of great value since it is the one which made you to be who you are today. Under no circumstance, a brave should not abandon his or her culture because if one abandons his or her culture, I dare to say he or she would abandon him or herself.

It is high time for us to protect all what we have in our culture and whatever is good should be taken to great heights and we should try all our level best to eliminate all those practices which are against human rights even if they have been practiced in our culture for centuries. Practices such as female genital mutilation, discrimination of any kind, torture to women and children and any kind of maltreatment to our society members. Culture is not static, it changes as the human being develops so let us not be prisoners of our own culture.

For those who have been here for four years will agree with us that we have been improving always from our first Cultural Day in 2010, 2011 and last year. The event is about commemorating our culture and other cultures beyond our boarders and beyond our continent. It must be well understood that, preserving our culture doesn’t mean to disrespect other peoples’ cultures. Cultures create and develop identities.

God bless Africa, God bless Tanzania, and God bless St Jude.

Remember, “Jasiri haachi asili,” The brave do not abandon their culture.”

12. In less than 300 words, write a reflection about the culture, belief, and tradition in your community/ house. Cite a Cohesive Devices in a various types of speech. ​


Answer:

Culture refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, and practices that define a group of people. Every community has its unique culture that shapes their way of life and interactions with others. It influences their language, food, clothing, music, arts, and religion.

Beliefs are the ideas or convictions that a community holds to be true. They can be religious, political, social, or personal. Beliefs can shape attitudes, behaviors, and decision-making processes.

Traditions are the customs or practices that have been passed down from generation to generation. They can be cultural, religious, or family-oriented. Traditions help to maintain the identity and heritage of a community and provide a sense of continuity and belonging.

Cohesive devices are linguistic tools used to connect sentences, paragraphs, and ideas within a text. They include pronouns, conjunctions, adverbs, and transitional phrases. Cohesive devices help to create a flow and coherence in writing and improve its readability.

For example, the use of conjunctions such as "however," "therefore," and "in addition" can connect contrasting or complementary ideas in a text. The use of pronouns such as "they" or "it" can refer to previously mentioned subjects, avoiding repetition and maintaining coherence. Adverbs such as "nevertheless" or "consequently" can signal a shift in the direction or tone of a text.

In conclusion, the culture, beliefs, and traditions of a community are essential aspects that define their way of life and interactions with others. The use of cohesive devices in writing can help to connect ideas and create coherence, making it easier to convey these aspects to others.

22. Education for knowledge about history and culture 2. Speech and conduct Characteristics​


Education for knowledge about history and culture should not only focus on the acquisition of information but also on the development of speech and conduct characteristics. This means that learners should not only be knowledgeable about the past and the traditions of their culture, but they should also be able to express their thoughts and ideas about it in a clear and respectful manner.

Speech characteristics refer to the way individuals communicate their thoughts and ideas. In the context of education for knowledge about history and culture, learners should be taught how to express their opinions about the past and traditions of their culture in a manner that is respectful of others. They should also be taught how to listen to other people’s opinions and be open to different perspectives.

Conduct characteristics refer to the way individuals behave and interact with others. In the context of education for knowledge about history and culture, learners should be taught how to conduct themselves in a way that is in line with the values and traditions of their culture. They should also be taught how to show respect to other cultures and traditions.

In summary, education for knowledge about history and culture should not only focus on the acquisition of information but also on the development of speech and conduct characteristics. This will help learners become knowledgeable and respectful individuals who are able to express their thoughts and ideas in a clear and respectful manner, while also being able to interact with others in a respectful and culturally sensitive way.

29. Give a speech about the following quote:"Providing Learner's Resilience Through Showcasing Skills and Talents in Culture and Arts and Technology"​


Dear friends,

Today, I want to talk about the importance of resilience in learning and how showcasing skills and talents in culture and arts and technology can contribute to building this resilience.

As we all know, learning can be a challenging and sometimes even a frustrating process. We encounter difficulties and setbacks that can make us want to give up. However, resilience is the ability to bounce back from these setbacks, to persevere through challenges, and to continue moving forward towards our goals.

One way to develop resilience is through the showcasing of our skills and talents in culture, arts, and technology. When we have the opportunity to demonstrate our abilities in these areas, we are building our self-confidence and self-esteem. We are also able to receive feedback and recognition for our accomplishments, which can motivate us to continue pushing ourselves and developing our skills.

Culture and arts can provide a unique outlet for individuals to express themselves and to connect with others. By participating in cultural activities and creating art, we can develop a sense of belonging and community. These activities can also be a source of inspiration and comfort during difficult times, helping us to cope with stress and adversity.

Technology is another area where individuals can showcase their skills and talents. In today's digital age, technology is an essential part of our lives, and those who are proficient in this area have a distinct advantage. By learning to code, develop apps, or design websites, individuals can create innovative solutions to real-world problems, and make a positive impact on their communities and society.

In conclusion, showcasing skills and talents in culture and arts and technology can help individuals develop resilience in learning. By participating in these activities, we can build self-confidence, receive recognition for our accomplishments, develop a sense of community, and create innovative solutions to real-world problems. Let us embrace these opportunities and continue to grow and develop as individuals. Thank you.
