4. how to clean steamer​


Answer:

To clean a steamer, follow these steps:

1. Unplug the steamer: Make sure the steamer is unplugged and has cooled down before cleaning it.

2. Empty the water: Pour out any remaining water from the steamer.

3. Clean the water tank: Fill the water tank with equal parts of water and white vinegar. Let the solution sit for about 30 minutes, then empty the tank and rinse it with clean water.

4. Clean the steaming basket: Remove the steaming basket and wash it with warm, soapy water. Rinse it thoroughly and let it air dry.

5. Clean the exterior: Wipe the exterior of the steamer with a damp cloth. If there are any stubborn stains, use a non-abrasive cleaner.

6. Clean the heating element: Use a soft-bristled brush to clean the heating element. Be careful not to damage the element.

7. Reassemble the steamer: Once all the parts are dry, reassemble the steamer.

12. A steamer goes downstream and covers the distance between two ports in 4 hrs., while it covers the same distance upstream in 5 hrs. if the speed of the stream is 2km/h , find the speed of the steamer in still water.


Answer:

Correct option is C. 18 km/hr

Let the speed of the steamer in still water be x km/h.

Then, the speed downstream =(x+2) km/h

and the speed upstream =(x−2) km/h

Given, distance covered in 4 hours downstream = distance covered in 5 hours upstream

∴4(x+2)=5(x−2)

⇒4x+8=5x−10

⟹4x−5x=−10−8

[Transposing 5x to LHS and 8 to RHS]

⟹−x=−18 or x=18km/h

23. what is the method of heat transfer in bamboo steamer​


Answer:

Bamboo steamers consist of interlocking baskets that stack on top of each other, with a lid on top. The entire setup is placed over a wok or pot of simmering water, and the steam rises through the open ridges at the bottom of each basket, up through the layers to cook whatever's inside.
