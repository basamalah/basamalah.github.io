10. A pump must move a discharge of 1.1 cfs against a head of 30 ft. If the pump is driven by a 5 kW motor with an efficiency of 75%, what is the brake horsepower


Answer:

Brake horsepower = 5 hp

Explanation:

Given:

Volumetric flow rate of water, [tex]Q_{w} =1.1cfs[/tex]

Head, [tex]h=30ft[/tex]

Motor power, [tex]P_{m} =5 kW[/tex]

Pump efficiency, [tex]e_{m}=75%[/tex]

Required: Brake horse power [tex]P_{b}[/tex]

Solution:

The pump efficiency is taken as the ratio of water horsepower (output) to the brake horsepower (input).

[tex]e_{p}=\frac{P_{w}}{P_{b}}[/tex]

Solving for water horsepower first:

[tex]P_{w}=\gamma_{w}Q_{w}h[/tex]

Note: The specific weight of water at 4°C is [tex]9.81 \frac{kN}{m^{3}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{w}=9.81\frac{kN}{m^{3}}(1.1\frac{ft^{3}}{s})(\frac{1m}{3.28ft})^{3}(30ft)(\frac{1m}{3.28ft})[/tex]

[tex]P_{w}=2.7970 kW[/tex]

Converting to horsepower:

[tex]P_{w}=2.7970kW(\frac{1hp}{0.746kW})=3.7493hp[/tex]

Inserting this back to the efficiency formula, we get

[tex]e_{p}=\frac{P_{w}}{P_{b}}[/tex]

[tex]0.75=\frac{3.7493hp}{P_{b}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{b}=5hp[/tex]

22. Pump discharges into a 3m per side cubical tank the flow rate is 300 liters/min, and the fluid has a density 1-2 times that of water (density of water p=1000 kg/m^3 Determine (a) the flow rate in kg/s (L) the time it takes to fill the tank​


Answer:

a) To determine the flow rate in kg/s, we first need to convert the flow rate from liters/minute to m^3/s. We can use the conversion factor of 1 liter = 0.001 m^3.

Q = 300 L/min x (0.001 m^3/L) = 0.3 m^3/s

Since we know that the density of the fluid is 1-2 times that of water, we can assume that the density of the fluid is 1500 kg/m^3.

We can then use the density and flow rate to calculate the flow rate in kg/s:

Q_kg = Q x p = 0.3 m^3/s x 1500 kg/m^3 = 0.45 kg/s

b) To determine the time it takes to fill the tank, we can use the formula:

t = V / Q

where t is the time in seconds, V is the volume of the tank in m^3, and Q is the flow rate in m^3/s.

The volume of the tank can be calculated as:

V = 3m x 3m x 3m = 27 m^3

So the time it takes to fill the tank is:

t = 27 m^3 / 0.3 m^3/s = 90 sec or 1.5 min

In summary, the flow rate of the fluid is 0.45 kg/s and it will take about 1.5 minutes to fill the tank.

[tex]\boxed{P = \frac{W}{t}}[/tex]

P = power

W = work

t = time

t = 180 s

[tex]P = \dfrac{\text{3920 J}}{\text{180 s}}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}\sf:\implies m& =\sf V \times d \\& =\sf 500 \times 0.8\\& = \sf 400\: kg\end{aligned}[/tex]

Next, we can calculate the gravitational potential energy gained by the fuel oil:

[tex]\begin{aligned}\sf:\implies GPE& =\sf mgh\\& =\sf 400 \times 9.8 \times 20\\& =\boxed{\bold{\:\: 78,400\: J\:\:}}\:\:\:\green{\checkmark}\end{aligned}[/tex]

Therefore, the gravitational potential energy gained by the fuel oil when pumped to a height of 20 m is 78,400 J.
