What is convection current?

Energy is moved from one location to another during the convection current process known as convective heat transfer as well. A fluid movement known as a convection current is caused by variations in temperature or density within a material. Due to the immobile nature of solid particles, a convection current only occurs in gases and liquids. Energy is transferred from areas of higher energy to areas of lower energy as a result of the difference in temperature.

What causes convection currents in earth's mantle?

The effect of differential heating is convection currents. Material that is warmer and lighter (less dense) rises while cool and heavier (denser) material sinks. Convection currents are the circulation patterns that are produced by this movement in the Earth's atmosphere, oceans, and mantle. Convection currents in the mantle are brought on by the heat produced by the core and the mantle itself. Mantle material rises slowly in hot columns. The hot material at the top of the asthenosphere expands out and pushes the cooler stuff aside. Back in the mantle, this cooler substance sinks.

27. what causes convection currents in mantle convection pamphlet​


Convection Currents in the Mantle

Heat in the mantle comes from the Earth's molten outer core, decay of radioactive elements and, in the upper mantle, friction from descending tectonic plates. The heat in the outer core results from residual energy from the Earth's formative events and the energy generated by decaying radioactive elements. This heat warms the base of the mantle to an estimated 7,230°F. At the mantle-crust boundary. the mantle's temperature is an estimated 392°F.

The temperature difference between the upper and lower boundaries of the mantle requires heat transfer to occur. While conduction seems the more obvious method for heat transfer, convection also occurs in the mantle. The warmer, less dense rock material near the core slowly moves upward.

Relatively cooler rock from higher in the mantle slowly sinks toward the mantle. As the warmer material rises, it also cools, eventually pushed aside by warmer rising material and sinking back toward the core.

Mantle material flows slowly, like thick asphalt or mountain glaciers. While the mantle material remains solid, the heat and pressure allow convection currents to move the mantle material.
