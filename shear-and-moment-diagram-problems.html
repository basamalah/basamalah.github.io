Consider a braced beam that is being loaded uniformly along its length as depicted in the figure below in order to derive the relationships between w, V, and M.

Let the shear force and bending moment at a portion that is x away from the left support be V and M, correspondingly,

and let them be V + dV and M + dM at a section that is x + dx. wdx is the complete load acting through the infinitesimal length's center.

Axial Force

Positive axial forces are those that have a tendency to bind the component at the section under discussion. The component is said to be susceptible to axial tension while such a force is considered tensile. Conversely, an axial force is regarded as negative if it has the tendency to crush the component at the section under consideration.

Shear Force

Positive shear forces are those that have a tendency to shift the section's left or right sides upward or downward, respectively. Similar to this, a shear force that tends to shift the section's left or right sides higher or downward will be categorized as a negative shear force.

Bending Moment

If a bending moment tends to create concavity upward, it is seen as positive (sagging). It will be deemed a negative bending moment if the tendency of the bending moment is to induce concavity downward (hogging).
