21. what is connections in neural and social networkswhat is relationship in neural and social networks ​


Answer:

Explanation:

Neural networks and social networks are two different types of networks with different structures and purposes.

In neural networks, connections refer to the links between neurons that allow them to communicate and exchange information. These connections are created and strengthened through a process called learning, which enables the network to improve its performance over time.

In social networks, connections refer to the links between individuals or entities in a social system, such as friends, family members, colleagues, or organizations. These connections represent social relationships and interactions, and they can be used to study patterns of communication, influence, and behavior.

Relationships in neural networks refer to the ways in which neurons are organized and interconnected to form a functional network. This includes the types of connections between neurons, the strengths of those connections, and the patterns of activity that emerge from the network.

Relationships in social networks refer to the nature and quality of the connections between individuals or entities in a social system. This includes the degree of closeness, trust, and influence between individuals, as well as the patterns of communication and behavior that emerge from these relationships.
