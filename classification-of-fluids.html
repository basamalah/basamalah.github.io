30. Which of the following has lesser density in a convection cell? A. warm fluid materialB. cold fluid materialC. room temperature fluid materialsD. hot fluid mayerials​


A. warm fluid material has a lesser density in a convection cell. This is because the warm material is less dense than the surrounding cooler material. The warmer fluid rises and the cooler fluid sinks, causing a convection current. This process repeats itself and causes the overall density of the warm fluid to decrease.

A convection cell is a fluid motion system in which dense fluid sinks and less dense fluid rises. The sinking of the dense fluid creates a vacuum that the less dense fluid rushes in to fill. This upwelling of the less dense fluid then drives the denser fluid downwards, creating a circulation pattern. In a convection cell, the movement of fluids is driven by differences in density. The denser a fluid is, the more gravity will pull it downwards. The less dense fluid is, the more buoyant it will be and the upwards force will be greater than the force of gravity pulling it down.

The density differential between two fluids can be caused by many things, but one of the most common is temperature. When heated, gas or liquid expands and becomes less dense. When cooled, it contracts and becomes denser.
