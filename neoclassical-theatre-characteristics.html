5. characteristics of the neoclassical paintings​


Answer:

Neoclassical painting is characterized by the use of straight lines, a smooth paint surface, the depiction of light, a minimal use of color, and the clear, crisp definition of forms. The works of Jacques-Louis David are usually hailed as the epitome of Neoclassical painting.

Comparison of Neoclassical Music with Other Art Forms:

Neoclassical music with architecture: Paintings, sculptures, and architectural buildings are only a few examples of great works of art that demonstrate the Neoclassical and Romantic ideals. Neoclassicism (sometimes written Neoclassicism) is a Western cultural movement that draws inspiration from classical ancient art and culture in the decorative and visual arts, literature, theater, music, and architecture.

Neoclassical music with painting: Clear form, muted colors, small spaces, strong horizontal and vertical lines that make the subject matter timeless (instead of temporal as in the vibrant Baroque works), and Classical subject matter are characteristics of Neoclassicism (or classicizing contemporary subject matter). Neoclassical art is distinguished by its use of straight lines, smooth paint surfaces, depictions of light, sparing use of color, and sharp, distinct definition of forms. Jacques-Louis David's creations are frequently considered as the pinnacle of Neoclassical art.

Neoclassical music with sculpture: Neoclassical sculpture is distinguished by its symmetry, colossal scale that ranges from life size, and serious subject matter. The subjects of Neoclassical sculpture included historical heroes, mythological beings, and prominent personalities from the modern era.

Neoclassical music with dance: Features include the use of reduced performance strength, emphasis on contrapuntal rhythms and textures, updated or extended tonal harmonies, and focus on absolute music rather than Romantic program music are examples of how neoclassical impulses are expressed. Ballet is an illustration of neoclassical dance.

Neoclassical music with theatre: Neoclassical theater is distinguished by its grandiosity, as is the era. The décor and clothing were extremely ornate and detailed. Melodramatic gestures and big body language are hallmarks of the performing. The major styles of the 18th century were baroque and rococo; the latter, with its emphasis on asymmetry, vivid colors, and ornamentation, is typically thought to be the exact opposite of the neoclassical style, which is founded on order, symmetry, and simplicity.

27. Compare the characteristics of Greek Theatre and Shakespearean Theatre.Greek TheatreShakespearean Theatre.​


Answer:

THE classic stage and Shakespeare's theatre have, at first sight, nothing in common; for the first was dedicated to unity, the second to variety. The great size of the antique stage made unity essential. A play had but three or four characters and involved but one or two ideas, which were hammered upon during the entire performance. When the heroes ceased speaking, the Chorus took up the thread of the argument. A Greek tragedy, moreover, was of national origin and of religious import. The plot was always taken from a familiar myth; and only great personages, heroes, kings and princes were allowed upon the stage.
