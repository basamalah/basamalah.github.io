Answer:

It will also provide students with a more holistic view of their nation’s history, culture, and economic development. The EPP curriculum will also equip students with the knowledge and skills to become more active and responsible citizens, as well as to become more aware of their responsibilities to the environment. Furthermore, it will also help to foster critical thinking skills, enabling students to make informed decisions about their own lives and society as a whole

Your task is to make a curriculum map using the institutional template and format. Develop an activity that will incorporate differentiation in your instruction. Think of a plan and creative learning activities that will fit or be appropriate to the chosen topic. Below is a sample curriculum map (attached file).​


Answer:

Here's an example of a curriculum map for the topic "Philippine Festivals" for Grade 4 Social Studies:

Grade 4 Social Studies: Philippine Festivals

Unit Goals/Objectives:

Understand the significance of Philippine festivals in promoting culture and traditions.Identify and describe the different types of Philippine festivals.Recognize the importance of participation in festivals in preserving Philippine culture.

Week 1: Introduction to Philippine Festivals

Lesson 1: What are Philippine Festivals?

Students will be introduced to the concept of Philippine festivals, their significance and cultural importance.

Activities:

Brainstorming session on what students know about Philippine festivals.Discussion on the different types of Philippine festivals and their characteristics.Viewing of a video on Philippine festivals.

Lesson 2: Famous Philippine Festivals

Students will learn about the different types of Philippine festivals and their characteristics.

Activities:

Group work on researching and presenting on different Philippine festivals.Sharing of information on different Philippine festivals.

Week 2: Participating in Philippine Festivals

Lesson 1: Preparing for a Festival

Students will learn about the preparations involved in participating in a Philippine festival.

Activities:

Brainstorming session on the different preparations needed for a festival.Group work on creating a checklist of items needed for a festival.

Lesson 2: Participating in a Festival

Students will learn about the different roles and activities involved in participating in a Philippine festival.

Activities:

Simulation of a festival parade.Reflection on the experience of participating in a festival.

Week 3: Preserving Philippine Festivals

Lesson 1: The Importance of Preserving Philippine Festivals

Students will learn about the importance of preserving Philippine festivals in promoting and preserving Philippine culture and traditions.

Activities:

Group work on researching and presenting on the significance of a particular Philippine festival.Sharing of information on the significance of different Philippine festivals.

Lesson 2: Promoting Philippine Festivals

Students will learn about the different ways to promote and support Philippine festivals.

Activities:

Group work on creating a promotional material for a Philippine festival.Sharing of the promotional materials created.

Differentiation:

To incorporate differentiation in instruction, the following activities can be implemented:

For students who have difficulty with writing or oral presentations, they can create visual representations of their research or information, such as posters or infographics.

For students who excel in writing, they can write a detailed essay on the significance of a particular Philippine festival, and how it contributes to the preservation of Philippine culture.

For students who have difficulty with group work, they can work on individual projects that showcase their understanding of Philippine festivals, such as creating a scrapbook or artwork that represents their favorite festival.

Overall, this curriculum map aims to provide students with a better understanding of Philippine festivals, their significance and importance in promoting and preserving Philippine culture and traditions. Through various learning activities and differentiation strategies, students will have the opportunity to showcase their creativity and enhance their understanding of the topic.

27. Explain this curriculum metaphor, “The curriculum is a route over which students will travel under the leadership of an experienced guide and companion”


This representation recommends that the educational plan resembles an excursion, and the understudies are the explorers. Similar to how a route directs travelers, the curriculum provides a path for students to follow. The teacher is like an experienced companion who helps the students navigate the journey in this metaphor.

A teacher helps students achieve their learning objectives by guiding them through the curriculum, just like a guide does for travelers. Similar to how a teacher explains the curriculum's subjects, the guide provides information about the locations along the route. Similar to how a teacher assists students in making decisions regarding their learning, such as which subjects to concentrate on and how to approach them, the guide also assists travelers in making decisions regarding which path to take.

Overall, this metaphor suggests that the curriculum is a planned and organized journey that the teacher is responsible for navigating with the students. The teacher helps students navigate the curriculum and reach their goal of achieving learning outcomes and acquiring knowledge and skills that will serve them well in the future by providing knowledge, support, and direction.
