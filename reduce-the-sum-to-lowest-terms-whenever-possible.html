FURTHER EXPLANATION

Adding Mixed Fractions

The mixed fraction should be changed into an improper fraction.Find the LCM and convert the correct fractions into equivalent ones if the denominators are different.Leave the dominator alone and either subtract or add the numerators.If possible, shorten it to the lowest term.

Example

Question:

2 5/6 + 3/5 = ...

Firstly we convert the mixed fraction 2 5/6 into an improper one.

2 5/6 = 17/6

The fractions 17/6 and 3/5 have different denominators discover the LCM converts them into similar ones, thus.

LCM(6, 5) = 3017/6 × 5/5 = 85/303/5 × 6/6 = 18/30

We may now add them in a way similar to adding fractions.

85/30 + 18/30 = 103/30 or 3 13/20

Then, 2 5/6 + 3/5 =  3 13/20.
