19. 1. In a certain normally distributed set of scores, the score two standard deviations below the mean is 6.2 and the score two standard deviations above the mean is 11.2. Solve for the mean and standard deviation of the set of scores. ​


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

We know that in a normal distribution:

- The mean is the center of the distribution.

- The standard deviation is the measure of the spread of the distribution.

Let's use these properties to solve for the mean and standard deviation of the set of scores:

Given that the score two standard deviations below the mean is 6.2, we can write:

6.2 = Mean - 2 * Standard Deviation   ------(1)

Similarly, given that the score two standard deviations above the mean is 11.2, we can write:

11.2 = Mean + 2 * Standard Deviation  ------(2)

Now we have two equations with two unknowns (Mean and Standard Deviation). We can solve for the unknowns by solving these two equations simultaneously.

First, let's solve equation (1) for Mean:

Mean = 6.2 + 2 * Standard Deviation

Substituting this value of Mean in equation (2), we get:

11.2 = (6.2 + 2 * Standard Deviation) + 2 * Standard Deviation

Simplifying the equation, we get:

11.2 = 6.2 + 4 * Standard Deviation

4 * Standard Deviation = 11.2 - 6.2

4 * Standard Deviation = 5

Standard Deviation = 5 / 4

Standard Deviation = 1.25

Substituting this value of Standard Deviation in equation (1), we get:

6.2 = Mean - 2 * 1.25

6.2 = Mean - 2.5

Mean = 6.2 + 2.5

Mean = 8.7

Therefore, the mean of the set of scores is 8.7, and the standard deviation is 1.25.
