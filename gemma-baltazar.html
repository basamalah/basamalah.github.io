16. A packet of sweets is shared among Kate, Imee and Gemma. Kate receives ⅓ of it, Imee receives 5/7 of the remaining sweets and Gemma takes the rest. If Gemma receives 6 sweets, how many sweets were there in the packet at first? Please answer with solution.


Kate received 1/3 of the sweets which means there is still 2/3 remaining for Imee and Gemma to share.

Imee then gets 5/7 of 2/3. We could get the final fraction share that Imee got by multiplying 2/3 by 5/7. Below is the equation described in this paragraph as well as the answer:
[tex]\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{7} = \frac{10}{21} [/tex]
Now that we know that 10/21 of the sweets went to Imee and Kate got 1/3, we can solve for the portion of sweets that Gemma received by adding Kate's, Imee's, and Gemma's (substituted by "x" since we are solving that value) and equating it to 1.

[tex] \frac{1}{3} + \frac{10}{21} + x = 1[/tex]
[tex]( \frac{(1 \times 21) + (10 \times 3)}{3 \times 21} ) + x = 1[/tex]
[tex] \frac{51}{63} + x = 1[/tex]
[tex] \frac{51 \div 3}{63 \div 3} + x = 1[/tex]
[tex] \frac{17}{21} + x = 1[/tex]
[tex]x = 1 - \frac{17}{21} [/tex]
[tex]x = \frac{1}{1} - \frac{17}{21} [/tex]
[tex]x = ( \frac{(1 \times 21) - (17 \times 1)}{1 \times 21} )[/tex]
[tex]x = \frac{4}{21} [/tex]
Summarizing,
Kate got 1/3 of the sweets;
Imee got 10/21 of the sweets; and
Gemma got 4/21 of the sweets.

Now working backwards with the last given information that Gemma got 6 sweets, we reword the question with this: 6 sweets is 4/21 of a portion of what? We then solve this (know the total sweets) by dividing 6 with 4/21.

The total number of sweets is 31.5.

30. a packet of sweets is shared amkng Kate,Imee and Gemma. Kate recieves 1/3 of it Imee recieves 5/7 of the remaining sweets and gemma takes the rest. If Gemma recieves 64 sweets, how many sweets were there in the packet at first?


Kate received ⅓ of the total packet of sweets so we subtract:
[tex]1 - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3 - 1}{3} = \frac{2}{3} [/tex]

The remaining is ⅔ of the packet. Imee received 5/7 of the remaining so we multiply and subtract:
[tex] \frac{5}{7} \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{5 \times 2}{7 \times 3} = \frac{10}{21} \\ \\ \frac{2}{3} - \frac{10}{21} = \frac{14 - 10}{21} = \frac{4}{21} [/tex]

Gemma takes the rest which is 4/21 of the whole. Gemma received 64 sweets so to find the total number of sweets inside the pack at first:
Let x be the total number if sweets at first.
[tex] \frac{4}{21} x = 64 \\ 4x = 64 \times 21 \\ 4x = 1344 \\ x = \frac{1344}{4} \\ x = 336[/tex]
So the total number of sweets at first is 336 pieces.
