Answer:

Auction chant (also known as "bid calling", "the auction cry", "the cattle rattle", or simply "auctioneering") is a rhythmic repetition of numbers and "filler words" spoken by auctioneers when taking bids at an auction. It is universal in North America but much less common elsewhere. The chant consists of at least the current price, the asking price to outbid and words to keep the audience engaged. Auctioneers typically develop their own style, and competitions are held to judge them. Outside of auctions, the chant has been the subject of music and used in commercials and film.

19. Choose a movie, whether local or international, that won best cinematography in any award winning bodies. This particular award is focused on the visual element of the film in terms of composition, lighting and camera motion. Watch this particular movie and write a descriptive review on why you think this movie deserves the award or otherwise argue why you think the award could have been received by the other nominee. ​


I have selected the movie "1917" directed by Sam Mendes, which won the Academy Award for Best Cinematography in 2020. This movie tells the story of two young British soldiers, Schofield (George MacKay) and Blake (Dean-Charles Chapman), who are given a crucial mission during World War I. They must deliver a message to stop an attack that would lead to the deaths of 1,600 soldiers, including Blake's brother.

The cinematography in "1917" is truly outstanding. The entire movie is shot as if it were one continuous take, which adds to the sense of urgency and tension that permeates the story. The camera follows the two soldiers as they traverse the dangerous terrain of no man's land and navigate through the trenches, all while trying to avoid enemy fire and locate the right battalion to deliver their message.

The lighting in "1917" is also noteworthy. The movie takes place over the course of one day, and the cinematographer, Roger Deakins, masterfully captures the changing light as the sun rises and sets. The use of natural light is particularly effective in the scenes set outdoors, as it adds to the realism and immerses the viewer in the world of the movie.

The composition of the shots in "1917" is also stunning. The camera moves seamlessly from wide shots that capture the vastness of the battlefield to tight shots that focus on the characters and their emotions. The framing of the shots is also carefully thought out, with the camera often positioned at eye level or slightly below, which makes the viewer feel as if they are right there with the soldiers.

Overall, "1917" is a deserving winner of the Best Cinematography award. The technical skill involved in creating a movie that appears to be one continuous take is impressive, and the use of lighting and composition adds to the emotional impact of the story. While there were certainly other worthy nominees, such as "Joker" and "The Irishman," "1917" stands out as a truly remarkable achievement in visual storytelling.

RYAN CAYABYAB

One of the most notable National Artists for Music in the Philippines is Ryan Cayabyab. He was born on May 4, 1954, in Manila and is a composer, conductor, and musician. He is known for his contributions to the development of Original Pilipino Music (OPM), a genre of music that features lyrics in Filipino and other Philippine languages.

Cayabyab started his musical career in the 1970s as a member of the group The Ambivalent Crowd. He then went on to become a musical director and arranger for various artists and bands. In 1982, he released his first album as a solo artist, entitled "Roots to Routes."

Some of his most notable compositions include "Kay Ganda ng Ating Musika," "Tuwing Umuulan at Kapiling Ka," and "Maging Sino Ka Man." He has also composed music for films, television shows, and stage productions.

Cayabyab has received numerous awards for his contributions to Philippine arts and culture. In addition to the National Artist Award for Music, he has also received the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Creative Arts, the CCP Gawad CCP Para sa Sining, and the Aliw Lifetime Achievement Award.

Aside from his musical achievements, Cayabyab has also contributed to the development of music education in the Philippines. He founded the Ryan Cayabyab Music School in 1990, which has since become a leading institution for music education in the country.

Overall, Ryan Cayabyab's contributions to Philippine music and culture have had a significant impact on the country's arts scene. His work has helped to promote the development and appreciation of OPM, and his efforts to improve music education have helped to inspire future generations of musicians and artists.
