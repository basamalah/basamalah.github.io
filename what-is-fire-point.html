30. A rifle is pointed at an angel 45° above the horizontal and fires a bullet with a muzzle velocity 1200 m/s. To what height does the bullet rise and what is its horizontal Range?​


Explanation:

Assuming that the acceleration due to gravity is 9.8 m/s² and neglecting air resistance, we can use the following equations to solve for the height and range of the bullet:

Vertical motion:

v² = u² + 2as

Horizontal motion:

s = ut + 0.5at²

where v is the final velocity, u is the initial velocity, s is the displacement, a is the acceleration, and t is the time.

First, we need to resolve the initial velocity into its horizontal and vertical components. The horizontal component is given by:

u_x = u cos θ = 1200 cos 45° = 849.5 m/s

The vertical component is given by:

u_y = u sin θ = 1200 sin 45° = 849.5 m/s

Now, we can use the vertical motion equation to find the maximum height reached by the bullet:

v² = u² + 2as

At the highest point, the vertical velocity is zero, so v = 0. Therefore:

0 = (849.5 m/s)² - 2(9.8 m/s²)s

s = (849.5 m/s)² / (2 x 9.8 m/s²) = 37,090.2 m²/s² / 19.6 m/s² = 1895.4 m

So the bullet rises to a height of approximately 1895.4 meters.

Next, we can use the horizontal motion equation to find the range of the bullet:

s = ut + 0.5at²

At the highest point, the vertical displacement is equal to the maximum height, which we just calculated. Therefore:

s = u_x t

t = s / u_x = 1895.4 m / 849.5 m/s = 2.231 s

Now we can use the horizontal motion equation to find the range:

s = u_x t + 0.5at²

s = (849.5 m/s)(2.231 s) + 0.5(0 m/s²)(2.231 s)² = 1901.5 m

So the horizontal range of the bullet is approximately 1901.5 meters.
