7. summary about Lesson 1- Jacques Louis David Lesson 2- Jean-Auguste Ingres, Lesson 3- Francisco De Goya Lucientes, Lesson 4- Ferdinand Eugene Delacroix& Jean Louis Andre Theodore Gericault​


Answer:

Lesson 1 is about the artist Jacques Louis David, who was a French painter known for his neoclassical style. David was famous for his historical paintings and portraits of famous figures of his time, such as Napoleon Bonaparte. He was a prominent figure during the French Revolution and created works that celebrated the ideals of the revolution.

Lesson 2 focuses on Jean-Auguste Ingres, another French neoclassical painter. Ingres was known for his precise and detailed style and his ability to capture the beauty of the human form in his portraits. He was also influenced by the classical art of ancient Greece and Rome.

Lesson 3 is about Francisco De Goya Lucientes, a Spanish painter who was active during the late 18th and early 19th centuries. Goya is known for his wide range of subject matter, from portraits and landscapes to dark and disturbing works that explored the darker aspects of human nature. His most famous works include "The Third of May 1808" and "The Black Paintings."

Lesson 4 explores the works of two French Romantic painters, Ferdinand Eugene Delacroix and Jean Louis Andre Theodore Gericault. Delacroix was known for his use of vibrant colors and his depictions of exotic locations and historical events. Gericault, on the other hand, was known for his dramatic and emotional works that often featured horses and other animals. His most famous work is "The Raft of the Medusa."
