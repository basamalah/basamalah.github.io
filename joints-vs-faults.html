3. how is fault different from joint?​


Answer:

What is the difference between a joint and a fault? ... Joints and faults are types of fractures. A joint is a fracture along which no movement has taken place, usually caused by tensional forces. A fault is a fracture or break in the rock along which movement has taken place.

10. fault vs fracture of an earthquake your own explanation, not from google


Answer:

Fault is a break in the Earth’s crust caused by seismic activity, usually occurring along a fault line. Fractures, on the other hand, are breaks in the rock caused by stress, often occurring along the same line as a fault. Fault lines are the areas where two or more of the Earth's tectonic plates meet and move against each other, generating earthquakes. Fractures in the rock occur due to the buildup of stress and can occur in areas both near and far from fault lines.
