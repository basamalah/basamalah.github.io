2. There are supplied 400 kj per cycle to an ideal diesel engine operating on 500 g air, suction pressure, and temperature of 100 kpa and 50c. at the end of compression, the pressure rises to 4000 kpa. determine the compression ratio


Answer:

To determine the compression ratio, we need to find the ratio of the volume of the cylinder during the compression stroke to the volume of the cylinder during the intake stroke.

The volume of the cylinder during the intake stroke can be calculated using the ideal gas law:

PV = nRT

Where:

P = pressure (100 kPa)

V = volume of the cylinder during the intake stroke

n = number of moles of air (0.005 moles, since the mass of air is 500 g and the molar mass of air is 28.97 g/mol)

R = ideal gas constant (8.31 J/mol*K)

T = temperature (50 C, or 323 K)

Solving for V, we get:

V = (nRT)/P = (0.0058.31323)/100 = 0.0136 m^3

The volume of the cylinder during the compression stroke can be calculated in a similar manner, using the pressure at the end of the compression stroke (4000 kPa) instead of the suction pressure:

V = (nRT)/P = (0.0058.31323)/4000 = 0.00034 m^3

The compression ratio is then:

V_compression/V_intake = (0.00034 m^3)/(0.0136 m^3) = 2.5

So the compression ratio is 2.5.

25. A monatomic ideal gas is initially at 1.52 x 10^5 Pa and has a volume of 7.90 x 10^-2 m^3 is compressed adiabatically to a volume of 4.50 x 10^-2 m^3. (a) What is the final pressure? (b) How much work is done by the gas? (c) What is the ratio of the final temperature of the gas to its initial temperature? Is the gas heated or cooled by this compression?


Solution:

Given

[tex]\mathsf{P_1=1.52\times10^5\:Pa;\:V_1=0.079\:m^3;\:V_2=0.045\:m^3}[/tex]

For monatomic,

[tex]\mathsf{\gamma=1.67}[/tex]

a) For adiabatic process,

[tex]\mathsf{P_1V_1^{\gamma}=P_2V_2^\gamma}[/tex]

The final pressure is

[tex]\mathsf{P_2=\dfrac{P_1V_1^\gamma}{V_2^\gamma} }\\\\\textsf{}\quad\:\,\mathsf{=\dfrac{(1.52\times10^5\:Pa)(0.079\:m^3)^{1.67}}{(0.045\:m^3)^{1.67}} }\\\textsf{}\quad\,=\boxed{\mathsf{3.89\times10^5\:Pa}}[/tex]

b) The work done is

[tex]\mathsf{W=\dfrac{1}{\gamma-1}(P_1V_1-P_2V_2) }\\\\\textsf{}\quad=\mathsf{\dfrac{1}{1.67-1} [(1.52\times10^5\:Pa)(0.079\:m^3)- }\\\textsf{}\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\mathsf{(3.89\times10^5\:Pa)(0.045\:m^3)]}\\\textsf\quad=\boxed{\mathsf{-8204.48\:J}}=\boxed{\mathsf{-8.20\times10^3\:J}}[/tex]

c) For adiabatic process,

[tex]\mathsf{T_1V_1^{\gamma-1}=T_2V_2^{\gamma-1}}[/tex]

For ratio T₂/T₁, we get

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{T_2}{T_1}=\bigg(\dfrac{V_1}{V_2}\bigg)^{\gamma-1} }\\\textsf{}\quad\:\:\,\mathsf{=\bigg(\dfrac{0.079\:m^3}{0.045\:m^3}\bigg)^{1.67-1} }}\\\textsf{}\quad\:\,=\boxed{\mathsf{1.46}}[/tex]
