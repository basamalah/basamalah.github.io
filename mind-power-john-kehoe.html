18. where the power transformer lowers the voltage​


Answer:

All transformers have both a primary and a secondary coil. A transformer whose primary coil has more turns than its secondary coil is called a step-down transformer because it reduces voltage — that is, the voltage at the secondary coil is less than the voltage at the primary coil.

21. what is the comparison of lower power and high power​


Explanation:

The most obvious difference between a low power and a high power microscope is that a high power microscope can resolve smaller features, which is usually achieved with objective lenses of greater magnification.

However, there are several non-obvious differences that contribute to the higher resolution of a high power microscope. For example:

Immersion: It is the numerical aperture (NA) that determines optical resolution and the NA is limited by the refractive index of the medium between the lens and the sample. The higher the index of refraction, the higher the resolution. Air is 1.00, pure water is 1.33, and immersion oil is 1.52. High power microscopes have objectives and condensers specifically designed to be used with immersion oil between the objective and the sample.

￼

Light source: The shorter the wavelength, the greater the refractive angle and therefore the greater the resolution. In order to resolve the smallest possible features, the light source illuminating the sample must emit light with a wavelength that is shorter than visible light. This commonly takes the form of UV-emitting light sources such as a mercury-vapor lamp.

Sample stage: This is a minor difference, but high power microscopes have more precise stages. This is because navigating between features occurs at a much smaller scale and with much smaller increments than low power microscopes.

29. john is able to lift a 50kg barbell with the force of 10N and at a distance of 0.5m in 3s. What is his power output?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The power of an object doing a work W in a given time t is given by

[tex]\boxed{P = \frac{W}{t}}[/tex]

where:

P = power

W = work

t = time

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for W

W = Fd

W = (10 N)(0.5 m)

W = 5 J

Solving for P

[tex]P = \dfrac{W}{t}[/tex]

[tex]P = \dfrac{\text{5 J}}{\text{3 s}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P = \text{1.67 W}}[/tex]

Therefore, the amount of power output is 1.67 W.
