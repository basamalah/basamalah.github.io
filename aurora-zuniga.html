6. What is an aurora?welp​


Answer

What is an aurora?

A spectacular natural light show called aurora isbcreated in part by natural magnetism.

Particles from the Sun are attracted to the magnetic poles of the Earth, which give off light as they meet gas in the Earth's atmosphere.

In the northern hemisphere, this is known as aurora borealis, or the 'northern lights'.

10. what is the aurora borealis?


The Aurora Borealis is a light show caused by collisions between electrically charged particles released from the sun that enter the earth's atmosphere and collide with gases such as oxygen and nitrogen. 19. What processes give rise to the Aurora Borealis and Aurora Australis​


Answer:

The auroras, both surrounding the north magnetic pole (aurora borealis) and south magnetic pole (aurora australis) occur when highly charged electrons from the solar wind interact with elements in the earth's atmosphere. Solar winds stream away from the sun at speeds of about 1 million miles per hour.

27. Where aurora can find? AND How aurora formed?


The aurora which is called Aurora Borealis in the north and Aurora Australias in the south can be most strongly seen in the poles of the earth. 

Aurora is formed when solar winds which are large streams of charged particles reach the earth's atmosphere and excite the atoms that are in there. The excited atoms will then occupy a higher energy level and once they go back to their normal energy level, they will emit energy in the form of heat or light which you see in auroras.
