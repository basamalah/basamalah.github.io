Understanding Market Segmentation

Segmentation in product marketing is the mapping of consumers who have different characteristics, purchasing power, needs in certain market situations. These consumers will automatically target product purchases according to their marketing strategy. Usually, marketing segmentation is used as a company strategy to find the right target consumers. Because if this mapping has been mastered, the company can produce goods/services that are really needed.

Definition of marketing segmentation is to unite all broadly targeted consumers, becoming more heterogeneous. The aim is none other than to increase the effectiveness of marketing as well as the utilization of production resources.

Benefits of Market Segmentation

1. Clearer Marketing Ideas

The first benefit is that the marketing idea becomes clearer. Because before supplying goods, the company already has data on consumers who will buy the product.

These data are not only related to the characteristics and needs of consumers. But also the interest or purchasing power of the people. Therefore, companies can determine more appropriate marketing ideas according to consumer data.

2. Easier to Manage Products

With market segmentation, it is certain that product management will be easier. Because the target consumer of the product already exists. So the company just needs to market it.

This is also the reason why post-market segmentation marketing is more effective and efficient. Because the chances of selling products being purchased by consumers are greater than those who do not apply them.

3. Finding Comparison Between Markets

If you apply market segmentation, of course the company has pocketed the characteristics, purchasing power, preferred type of marketing, and customer needs. It is undeniable that in this context, consumers have different sizes.

Because of this, the company can make comparisons between markets by grouping consumers. Later, the results of the comparison of which groups will buy the product more and those who don't.
