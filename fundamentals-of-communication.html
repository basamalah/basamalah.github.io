1. fundamentals of communication​


1. Senders & Receivers

There’s no communication without someone to send a message and someone to receive it. Whether it’s a lecturer orating to their students or an individual making eye contact with someone across a crowded bar, communication is about making a connection. The role of sender and receiver may be fixed, as in a superior officer delivering orders to a private, or fluid, as in an animated conversation between equals.

2. The Message

What is being said, signed, gestured or read? Whether verbal or non-verbal, this is what the sender is attempting to communicate to the receiver. In the previous example of an officer giving orders to a private, the order is the message.

14. what is the fundamental components of data communication circuits? explain


Answer:

Data communication circuits consist of several fundamental components, including a transmitter, receiver, communication channel, and noise source. The transmitter converts the data to be transmitted into a signal suitable for transmission over the communication channel. The receiver then receives the transmitted signal and converts it back into the original data. The communication channel is the medium through which the signal is transmitted, such as a copper wire or optical fiber. Noise sources, such as electromagnetic interference or thermal noise, can also affect the transmitted signal and introduce errors into the received data.

17. what is the fundamental components of data communication circuits? explain in your own


Answer:

The fundamental components of data communication circuits include a transmitter, receiver, and communication channel.

The transmitter takes the data to be transmitted and encodes it into a signal suitable for transmission over the communication channel.

The receiver receives the signal and decodes it back into its original data form.

The communication channel is the medium through which the signal is transmitted, such as a copper wire or a wireless radio frequency.

Additionally, there may be other components such as modems or multiplexers to enable multiple communication channels to share a single communication link
