18. 2. Can the Congress pass a law amending or changing the qualification of a member of Supreme Court or any lower collegiate court in Section 7, Article VIII of the 1987 Constitution, from a natural-born citizen to a citizen of the Philippines. Explain your answer.​


Answer:

Section 7, Article VIII of the 1987 Philippine Constitution states that "No person shall be appointed Member of the Supreme Court or any lower collegiate court unless he is a natural-born citizen of the Philippines." This means that in order for someone to be appointed as a member of the Supreme Court or any lower collegiate court, they must be a natural-born citizen of the Philippines.

To change the qualification of a member of the Supreme Court or any lower collegiate court from a natural-born citizen to a citizen of the Philippines, an amendment to the Constitution would be required. The process for amending the Constitution is outlined in Section 1 of Article XVII of the Constitution, which requires that any proposed amendment be passed by a three-fourths vote of all the members of Congress and ratified by a majority of the votes cast in a plebiscite.

It is worth noting that amending the Constitution is a difficult task and requires a lot of political will and support, so it is unlikely that it would happen easily.
