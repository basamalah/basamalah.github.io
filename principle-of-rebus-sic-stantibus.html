29. Consider the reaction: SiO2 + C → SiC + 2 CO When 100.0 kg of sand (SiO2) is processed, 51.400 kg of SiC is recovered. What is the percent yield of SiC from this process?


Answer:

% yield = 77.02 %

Explanation:

To get the percent yield. We have to calculate the theoretical yield.

First, we convert 100 kg of SiO₂ to moles.

MM  SiO₂=60.08 g/mol

Mole  SiO₂ =100 kg /60.08 g/mol x 1000g/1 kg

Mole  SiO₂ =1664.4474 mole

From the reaction, 1 mole of SiO₂ gives 1 mole of SiC. Using this we know that 1664.4474 mole of  SiO₂ will give 1664.4474 mole SiC.

Converting mole SiC to kg SiC

MM SiC=40.0962 g/mol

kg SiC=1664.4474 mole x 40.0962 g/mol

kg SiC=66,738.01584 g x 1kg / 1000 kg

kg SiC=66.7380 kg

Now we calculate the percent yield:

% yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield ) x 100%

% yield =51.400 kg /66.7380 kg x 100%

% yield = 77.02 %
