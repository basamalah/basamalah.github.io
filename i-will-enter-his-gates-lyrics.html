23. why did the sailor invite the porter to enter the gate?


Is the "The Story of Sindbad the Sailor" you were referring? if thats the case then...

Summary:
In the story of Sindbad the Sailor, there is a tale where Sindbad is traveling and stops in a city where he meets a porter. The porter is carrying a heavy load and is struggling to make his way through the city. Sindbad offers to help the porter and carries his load for him.

Grateful for Sindbad's help, the porter offers to repay him by inviting him to his home for dinner. Sindbad accepts the invitation and follows the porter to his home. When they arrive, the porter leads Sindbad through a grand gate and into a beautiful courtyard filled with fountains and flowers.

Sindbad is amazed by the beauty of the courtyard and asks the porter how he came to live in such a magnificent place. The porter explains that he works as a doorkeeper for a wealthy merchant who lives in the palace.

Sindbad realizes that the porter has mistaken him for someone else and decides to play along. He tells the porter that he too is a doorkeeper and that he would like to meet the merchant.

The porter leads Sindbad to meet the merchant, who is impressed by Sindbad's intelligence and wit. He offers Sindbad a job as a treasurer in his company, and Sindbad accepts.

Note: that is a summary of the particular tale in the story of Sindbad the Sailor where he meets a porter and gains entry to a wealthy merchant's palace.

Answer:

So, to answer the question, the sailor (Sindbad) invited the porter to enter the gate because the porter had offered to repay Sindbad's kindness by inviting him to dinner. They entered the gate to go to the porter's home, where Sindbad would eventually meet the wealthy merchant and begin a new career.
