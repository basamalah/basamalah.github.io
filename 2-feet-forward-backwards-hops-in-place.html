Third Law of Motion "The Law of action-reaction"

Problem:

Which of the following is a reaction when walking?

Answer:

A. Feet pushing the ground backwards

In Newton's Third Law of motion "the law of action-reaction", It states that for every action, there should be an equal and opposite reaction. Example: when we walk, we pushed our feet on the ground backward so that the opposite reaction will happen and that is we move forward. The same with jumping, you pushed your legs to the ground, and the was opposite, you move upward. The amount of force you pushed to the floor, will always have equal opposite reaction. You jump law when you pushed the floor less during jumping, and you jump high when you pushed the floor with higher force.
