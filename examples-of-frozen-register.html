16. 10 examples of frozen register​


"FROZEN REGISTER"

Speech that is sufficiently regimented that it is frequently created through recitation rather than spontaneous speech production is referred to as frozen register. As a result of the identical utterances being made each time, this speech is also frequently referred to as static register and ritualistic speech.

A language's register is its degree of formality, which is governed by the situation in which it is spoken or written. It might be either professional or casual. Speaking informally is conversational or informal, whereas speaking informally is proper.

Additional Language Register:

Formal Register

This language is one-way and utilized in formal settings. This language use typically adheres to a standard structure. Typically, it is stiff and impersonal. Speeches are a typical format for this register. For instance, sermons, speeches, announcements, and rhetorical comments and inquiries.

Advisory Register

It's common to communicate in this way. Users participate in conversations that follow an established structure. Users of this speech are expected to adhere to social norms because it is formal. The language used is formal. For instance, when two strangers speak, when a superior and a subordinate communicate, when a doctor and a patient, a lawyer and a client, a lawyer and a judge, a teacher and a pupil, a counselor and a client, etc.

Temporary Register

Peers and friends use informal language like this. Colloquialisms, slang, and obscene language are common. It's "group" language here. To participate in this registration, one must be a member. For instance, friends, coworkers, conversations, emails, blogs, and letters to friends.

Secret Register

This conversation is confidential. It is only for personal or close family members. a parent and childa, a boyfriend and a girlfriend, a brother and a sister, etc.
