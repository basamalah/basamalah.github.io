4. possible questions to ask in title defense with explanation ​


Here are some questions that could be asked in a title defense with a brief explanation of why they are relevant:

Can you explain your research methodology and the methods you used to gather data?

This question helps the defense committee understand the rigor and validity of the research process.

Can you discuss the contributions of your research to the field?

This question allows the candidate to highlight the significance of their findings and how they advance knowledge in their area of study.

How does your research address limitations or criticisms in previous studies?

This question demonstrates the candidate's awareness of the larger context of their research and their ability to build upon existing knowledge.

Can you discuss any challenges you encountered during your research and how you overcame them?

This question shows the candidate's perseverance and problem-solving skills, and can also provide insight into potential limitations of the study.

Can you discuss any potential implications of your research for practical applications or policy-making?

This question helps the defense committee understand the practical significance of the research and its potential impact outside of academia.

Can you address any ethical considerations that arose during your research?

This question highlights the candidate's awareness of ethical considerations and their responsibility as a researcher.

Can you discuss any future directions for your research or potential avenues for follow-up studies?

This question shows the candidate's long-term thinking and their potential as a future leader in their field.

It has been linked to various negative outcomes, such as depression, anxiety, and low self-esteem. My study aims to explore the impact of cyberbullying on the self-efficacy of selected senior high school students, which is an important aspect of their overall well-being.

What is the scope of your study?

The scope of my study is limited to selected senior high school students who have experienced cyberbullying. I will be using a qualitative research design to gather data through interviews and focus group discussions. The study will focus on the impact of cyberbullying on the self-efficacy of these students, which is a specific aspect of their overall well-being.

What phenomenon were trying to understand with this research?

The phenomenon that I am trying to understand with this research is the impact of cyberbullying on the self-efficacy of selected senior high school students. I want to understand how cyberbullying affects the students' belief in their ability to accomplish tasks and overcome challenges, which is an important aspect of their overall well-being.

Who will be most interested in your research?

My research will be of interest to educators, parents, and policymakers who are concerned about the well-being of senior high school students. It will also be of interest to researchers who are studying the impact of cyberbullying on various aspects of young people's lives.

ADVANTAGES AND DISADVANTAGES of your research?

One advantage of my research is that it will provide insights into the impact of cyberbullying on the self-efficacy of selected senior high school students, which is an important aspect of their overall well-being. One disadvantage of my research is that the scope of the study is limited to selected senior high school students who have experienced cyberbullying.
