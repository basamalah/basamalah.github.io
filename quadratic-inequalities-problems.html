25. How to solve problems involving quadratic inequality?​


Answer:

There are a few steps that can be followed when solving problems involving quadratic inequalities. First, identify the inequality sign. Second, set the equation equal to zero and solve for x. Third, plug in the x-values from the previous step into the original equation to determine which side of the inequality the x-value falls on. Lastly, graph the equation to verify the solution.
