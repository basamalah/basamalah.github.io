3. If you are surveying a plot of land in the shape of a right triangle. The area of the land is 4.000 square meters If one leg of the triangular plot is 18 meters long, what is the other leg of the triangle?​


Since we know that the area of the triangular plot is 4,000 square meters, and the triangular plot is a right triangle, we can use the formula for the area of a right triangle to solve for the missing leg. The formula for the area of a right triangle is:

Area = 1/2 * base * height

where the base and height are the two legs of the right triangle.

Let x be the length of the missing leg. Then we have:

Area = 1/2 * base * height

4,000 = 1/2 * 18 * x

4,000 = 9x

x = 4,000 / 9

Therefore, the length of the missing leg is approximately 444.44 meters (rounded to two decimal places).
