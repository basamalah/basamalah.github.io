8. what is nursery/plant nursery​


Answer:

A nursery is a place where plants are propagated and grown to a desired size.

Explanation:

A nursery is a place where plants are propagated and grown to usable size. They include retail nurseries which sell to the general public, wholesale nurseries which sell only to businesses such as other nurseries and to commercial gardeners, and private nurseries which supply the needs of institutions or private estates. Some retail and wholesale nurseries sell by mail. Although the popular image of a nursery is that of a supplier of garden plants, the range of nursery functions is far wider, and is of vital importance to many branches of agriculture, forestry and conservation biology. Some nurseries specialize in one phase of the process: propagation, growing out, or retail sale; or in one type of plant: e.g., groundcovers, shade plants, or rock garden plants. Some produce bulk stock, whether seedlings or grafted, of particular varieties for purposes such as fruit trees for orchards, or timber trees for forestry. Some produce stock seasonally, ready in springtime for export to colder regions where propagation could not have been started so early, or to regions where seasonal pests prevent profitable growing early in the season.

About Harvesting

Harvesting is a very decisive activity in the activity agricultural production, and is often the most expensive part of the activity production. In simple terms, the term harvesting is defined as effort separate the parts of the plant that have economic value from the parent plant. In fruit plants, the part that is separated is of course the fruit, whereas in vegetable plants, these parts can be leaves, stems, roots and fruit. While in the plantation section the plants that are harvested are various, there are fruits, for example cocoa, coconut; there is flowers eg cloves; there is sap, for example rubber, resin; there is fiber for example rosella and others.

Several important factors related to harvesting and product quality are: determining when to harvest, how to harvest, handling transportation

27. why is it important to prepare the nursery areas before planting any vegetables​


Answer:

A nursery bed needs to be prepared carefully so that uniform and healthy seedlings are obtained for planting. The nursery bed is, generally, used to germinate sown seeds or for rooting of cuttings planted in the soil. Besides nutrition, sufficient moisture and aeration are important factors that affect seedling growth.
