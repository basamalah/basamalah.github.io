14. once roamed in the plains of luzon, mindanao entire philippines archipelago. unfavorable geographical conditions and human exploitation caused their extinction​


Stegodon became extinct due to human exploitation and the unfavorable geographical conditions of the Philippines.

Explanation:

Stegodon is an extinct genus of proboscis, related to elephants. It was originally assigned to the Elephantidae family along with modern elephants but is now placed in the extinct family Stegodontidae. Like elephants, Stegodon had teeth with loph like plates that differed from more primitive proboscideans such as gomphotheres and mastodons. Stegodon became extinct in the Indian subcontinent, mainland Southeast Asia and China at the end of the late Pleistocene period, while Asian elephants still live on mainland Asia today.

Van den Bergh in 2009 on his research say that the extinction of the Late Pleistocene stegodon on Flores is hypothesised to have occurred as a result of a volcanic eruption as stegodon material is found below a volcanic layer. Besides Stegodon, dwarf elephants are also believed to have lived in the Philippines during the Pleistocene period, particularly in Luzon and Panay, and it is believed that Stegodon along with other prehistoric mammals entered Mindanao via a land bridge known as the Sunda Shelf and migrated north.

Stegodon luzonensis is a species of mammal in the family Stegodontidae. This species reproduces offspring by viviparity. Stegodon mindanensis and Stegodon luzonensis is the first large fossil mammal to be scientifically reported in the Philippines. It was first collected by Karl Semper in 1860 in the Agusan River Valley, Mindanao. Stegodon luzonensis lived during the Pleistocene period.
