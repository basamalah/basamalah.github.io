4. Describe the Characteristics of the Following:•Cirrus Cloud •Stratus Cloud •Cumulus Cloud•Stratocumulus Cloud​


Question:

Characteristics of different types of clouds

Answer & Explanation:

Cirrus Clouds - are short, detached, hair-like clouds found at high altitudes. These delicate clouds are wispy, with a silky sheen, or look like tufts of hair.

Stratus Clouds - Stratus clouds are low-level layers with a fairly uniform grey or white color. Often the scene of dull, overcast days in its 'nebulosus' form, can persist for long periods of time.

Cumulus Clouds - Cumulus clouds are detached, individual, cauliflower-shaped clouds usually spotted in fair weather conditions.

Stratocumulus Clouds - Stratocumulus clouds are low-level clumps or patches of cloud varying in colour from bright white to dark grey

25. Search for an example of a worksheet. Explain the different columns included in the worksheet.


Accounting Worksheet Columns Included:

-Unadjusted Trial Balance

-Adjusting Entries

-Adjusted Trial Balance

-Income Statement

-Balance Sheet

Explanations:

Unadjusted Trial Balance

A column contains all the company’s assets, liability, expenses, and revenue accounts, which are used in a concerned year.

Adjusting Entries

All the accounts of the company, which requires the passing of the adjustment entry will get listed in the adjustment column.

Adjusted Trial Balance

The adjusted trial balance will be prepared by combining the entries of the previous two columns, Unadjusted trial balance, and adjustments.

Income Statement

A column contains the values concerning the expenses and revenue accounts only. In this case, if the value of total revenue exceeds the expense column, then the difference will be the net income of the year of the company as it is earning more revenues in the year than what it is spending on its expenses.

Balance Sheet

A column contains the values with respect to the assets, liabilities, and owner’s capital only.
