5. Look for some of your favorite stories/articles. Copy an excerpt of the text then infer(determine) it's mood, tone, and purpose.​


WHY WE SLEEP BY MATTHEW WALKER

Here's an excerpt from the article "Why We Sleep" by Matthew Walker:

"Across these different species, we know that sleep serves to protect an animal against various environmental risks. For example, sleep is associated with a highly efficient restoration process for both the brain and body. In humans, the majority of growth hormone, which is essential for growth and tissue repair, is secreted during deep sleep. Conversely, the reduction in metabolic rate that occurs during sleep, and the associated decrease in core body temperature, may protect against oxidative stress and free-radical damage that accumulate in the brain during wakefulness."

Mood

Mood: Scientific

The passage has a scientific tone since it contains information and facts based on scientific research and investigations, and it use technical language and terminology. The author is talking about the biological processes and functions of sleep, which are based on scientific knowledge of how the human body operates. The language employed is factual and objective, as is customary in scientific writing.Tone

Tone: Informative

While the author's primary goal is to educate and inform readers about the biological importance of sleep, the tone of the passage is instructive. The tone is explanatory rather than persuasive or argumentative, with data presented in a plain and concise manner. The author is providing factual information based on scientific research with no intention of influencing the reader's perspective or advocating a certain point of view. In general, the tone is intended to transmit information and educate the reader on the issue at hand.Purpose

Purpose: To explain the biological importance and protective functions of sleep in various species, including humans.

How do you determine the tone and mood of a speaker?

speaker?The mood is the atmosphere of the story, and the tone is the author's attitude towards the topic. We can identify both by looking at the setting, characters, details, and word choices. By doing so, it will help us find meaning in the story or passage and help us feel more connected to the writing.

How can you tell the mood of a story?

How can you tell the mood of a story?In literature, mood is the feeling created in the reader. This feeling is the result of both the tone and atmosphere of the story. The author's attitude or approach to a character or situation is the tone of a story and the tone sets the mood of the story.

Explanation:

How do you know the mood of a poem?

How do you know the mood of a poem?The writer of the work can create the mood using a number of elements such as, but not limited to, setting, tone, choice of words and theme. To define the mood, the reader should analyze how these different elements interact and what feeling they evoke

What is the speaker's mood?

What is the speaker's mood?Answer: Tone and mood are two different things. The tone is the attitude of the narrator/speaker, and the mood is the emotion or feeling that a piece gives to readers. ... The speaker is describing his feelings, and the reader is along for the ride.

What is the speaker's purpose?

What is the speaker's purpose?Speakers may want to explain an idea or process, share new information, or show how to do something. When a speaker aims to persuade an audience, they want them to adopt a new position or belief, to change their minds, or to be moved to action
