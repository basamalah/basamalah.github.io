9. what is the distinctive characteristics of the last judgement sculpture​


Answer:

The Last Judgment

The Last Judgment has been a popular source of artistic inspiration in the Western world for over 1,000 years. This Christian story of the end of the world involves Jesus coming back to Earth to judge the souls of the living and the dead to determine who goes to heaven and hell for eternity. That's a pretty heavy storyline, and artists have found numerous ways to express their cultural and historic views of this event. In the medieval era, people were consumed with the flaws and potential for sin they saw in humanity. The Last Judgment was a reckoning of the evils of the world and a reminder to everyone that their deeds would be judged one day. After the Renaissance, Europeans had recast human beings as their God's most perfect creations, with humans' capacity for evil in balance with an exhilarating potential for greatness. The Last Judgment's previous value as a tale of warning became less important as artistic expression of the story's drama was given more attention.

The Romanesque Sculpture of Gislebertus

Our first piece is known as Gislebertus' Last Judgment, a Romanesque sculpture made around 1130 CE at the Cathedral of Saint-Lazare, Autun, France. The piece is often attributed to a supposed sculptor named Gisleburtus, but research shows that individual artists rarely signed their work during this time in European history.

25. What are the characters and characterization of The Story "The Last Judgement" By Karel Capek?​


"The Last Judgement" is a short story by Czech writer Karel Capek. The story is a satirical take on the concept of the Last Judgement and the afterlife. The characters in the story include:

The Archangel Michael - Michael is a powerful angel who serves as the chief judge of the Last Judgement. He is described as being "as big as a mountain" and "as bright as a star." He is depicted as being stern and uncompromising in his judgement of the souls of the dead.

The Lawyer - The lawyer is a recently deceased man who finds himself standing before Michael at the Last Judgement. He is described as being "a little man with a bald head and a long, hooked nose." Throughout the story, the lawyer attempts to argue his way out of punishment, but his arguments are ultimately futile.

The Devil - The Devil is a minor character in the story, but he plays an important role. He appears briefly to challenge Michael's authority and to question the fairness of the Last Judgement.

In terms of characterization, Capek uses the characters to satirize various aspects of human nature. The lawyer represents human arrogance and the belief that one can argue their way out of any situation. Michael, on the other hand, represents the unyielding forces of justice and judgement. The Devil serves as a foil to Michael, questioning the fairness of the Last Judgement and suggesting that it is a flawed system. Overall, Capek uses his characters to explore complex philosophical and theological questions in a humorous and satirical way.
