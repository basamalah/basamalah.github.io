9. 1. As a student, how is it important that you know each step in troubleshooting brazing joint? And how will it help you during your hands-on activity?​


Answer:

Step 1: Ensure good fit and proper clearances.

Brazing uses capillary action to distribute molten filler metal between the surfaces of the base metals.

Step 2: Clean the metals.

Capillary action works properly only with clean metal surfaces. If they're coated with oil, grease, rust, scale, or dirt, you must remove these contaminants or they'll form a barrier between the base metal surfaces and the brazing materials.

Step 3: Flux the parts.

Flux is a chemical compound applied to the joint surfaces before brazing. Its use, with a few exceptions, is essential in the atmospheric brazing process.

Step 4: Assemble for brazing.

Once your parts are cleaned and fluxed, hold them in position for brazing. Be sure they remain in correct alignment during the heating and cooling cycles so that capillary action can do its job.

Step 5: Braze the assembly.

The actual brazing involves heating the assembly to brazing temperature and flowing the filler metal through the joint. Be sure when you're heating an assembly to brazing temperature that you don't heat it to the base materials' melting point.

Step 6: Clean the brazed joint.

After you braze the assembly, clean it. Because most brazing fluxes are corrosive, cleaning is essential.

STEPS OF JOINTING:

1. Remove the insulation

2. Bring the two conductors to a crossed position and then make a long bend or twist in each wire.

3. Wrap the end of one of the wires around the straight portion of the other wire, and then do the same for the other wire. Repeat this for about four or five times.

4. Press ends of the wires down close to the straight portions of the wire to prevent the ends from piecing through the insulation tape.

5. Insulate the joint using the tape

STEPS OF SOLDERING:

1. Pre-cleaning

2. Fit the parts

3. Apply flux

4. Heat the desired sections of the parts

5. Apply the solder

6. Cool the soldered joint

7. Post-clean.

JOINT VARIATIONGiven: If y varies jointly as x and z, and y = 36 when x =3 and z= 4, find z when y = 36 and x = 4.

Step 1: Write the correct equation. Joint variation problems are solved using the equation y = kxz.

[tex]y = kxz[/tex]

Step 2: Use the information given in the problem to find the value of k. In this case, you need to find k when y = 36 when x =3 and z = 4.

[tex]36 = k(3)(4)[/tex]

[tex]k = \frac{36}{(3)(4)} [/tex]

[tex]k = \frac{36}{12} [/tex]

[tex]k = 3[/tex]

Therefore, the constant of variation k is 3.

Step 3: Rewrite the equation from step 1 substituting in the value of k found in step 2.

[tex]y = 3xz[/tex]

Step 4: Use the equation found in step 3 and the remaining information given in the problem to answer; in this case, you need to find z when y = 36 and x = 4.

[tex]y = 3xz[/tex]

[tex]36 = (3)(4)z[/tex]

[tex]36 = 12z[/tex]

[tex] \frac{36}{12} = \frac{12}{12} z[/tex]

[tex]3 = z[/tex]
