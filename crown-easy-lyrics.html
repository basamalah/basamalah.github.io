9. It means Crown of Roses​


Answer:

Rosary

Explanation:

In the book The Secret of the Rosary, St. Louis De Montfort explained, “The word Rosary means ‘Crown of Roses,’ that is to say that every time people say the Rosary devoutly they place a crown of one hundred and fifty-three red roses and sixteen white roses upon the heads of Jesus and Mary,” the saint wrote. “Being heavenly flowers these roses will never fade or lose their exquisite beauty.”

The Blessed Virgin Mary has revealed to several people that each time they say a Hail Mary they are handing her a beautiful rose, and that each complete rosary makes her A Crown of Roses.

The rosary is a form of vocal and mental prayer on the mysteries of our redemption, divided into fifteen decades. The recitation of each decade is accompanied by meditation on one of the fifteen events or mysteries. The mysteries consist of four groups. With the rosary, we meditate on the Mysteries of Joy, of Sorrow, the Glory of Jesus, and the new Luminous Mysteries which Saint John Paul II added in October 2002. Since the rose is the queen of flowers, so the rosary is the queen of all devotions. The holy rosary is considered a perfect prayer because within it lies the awesome story of our salvation. Reciting the rosary is one of the most powerful weapons against evil.
