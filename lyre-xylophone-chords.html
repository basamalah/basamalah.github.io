6. Instrument use areanswers:The main instrument used for ATI atihan festival are snare drums, triplets, bass, drum, trumpets, cacophony, xylophone, lyres and gongs ​


The main instruments used for the ATI-ATIHAN festival are:

1. Snare Drums: These are small drums with tight heads that are played with sticks. They create sharp, rhythmic beats and are a prominent instrument in the ATI-ATIHAN festival.

2. Triplets: These are small handheld drums that are played in sets of three. They produce a distinct sound and are used to create complex rhythms and patterns during the festival.

3. Bass Drum: This is a large drum that produces low-frequency beats and provides the foundation for the rhythm in the ATI-ATIHAN festival. It is typically played with a mallet.

4. Trumpets: These brass instruments are used to create melodic lines and add a celebratory tone to the festival. They are often played in groups and create a vibrant and energetic sound.

5. Cacophony: This is a type of instrument made from pieces of metal, such as cans or lids, that are attached to a frame and shaken to produce a chaotic and metallic sound. It adds a unique texture to the overall sound of the festival.

6. Xylophone: This is a percussion instrument with wooden bars that are struck with mallets to produce melodic tones. It is often used in the ATI-ATIHAN festival to create catchy and memorable melodies.

7. Lyres: These are stringed instruments similar to small guitars or ukuleles. They are plucked or strummed to produce melodic lines and are used to add a melodic layer to the music during the festival.

8. Gongs: These large, metallic discs are struck with mallets to produce deep, resonant tones. They are used to create a powerful and majestic sound during the ATI-ATIHAN festival.

These instruments are used in combination to create the lively and rhythmic music that accompanies the vibrant and energetic dances of the ATI-ATIHAN festival.

26. it is wooden xylophone​


Answer:

Wooden xylophone

Explanation:

A xylophone is a long musical instrument with wooden bars that is played by hitting it with small hammer-like mallets. The xylo- part of this word comes from the Greek for wood — xylon — and clues us in to what gives this instrument its unique sound: the graduated bars representing tones of the scale.
