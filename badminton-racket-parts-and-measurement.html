The racket is the piece of equipment used by a player to hit the shuttlecock in the game of badminton. The size, shape, and weight of the racket can vary depending on the player's preference and skill level.

29. Which of the following is not a part of a badminton racket? Answer Face


PARTS OF A BADMINTON RACKET

The face is not part of the parts of a Badminton racket. Below are the parts of a Badminton racket.

FRAME

The frame simply refers to the body of the rocket itself. The frame consists of the head, the stringed area, the throat, the shaft and the handle. The standard length of the frame should be no longer than 680 millimeters or wider than 230 millimeters according to BWF laws. More so, a fully strung frame should weigh within 80 and 100 grams.

HEAD

The head of the racket refers to the ring of material that usually holds the strings in place. The shape of the head can either be oval or more rounded, and the perimeter has holes to which the strings are laced.

STRINGED AREA

Nylon or carbon fiber string that is interwoven to form the face of the racket is what the stringed area is made of. According to BWF rules, the stringed area must not exceed 280 millimeters in length or 220 millimeters in width.

THROAT

The throat is what connects the head to the shaft. The throat may be a separate triangular piece at the base of the head, or may actually be integrated into the head itself.

SHAFT

Shaft is characterized as a long rod between the throat and handle. The shaft is also often made of a composite material such as graphite, more so, shaft can be stiffer or more flexible based on the needs of the player.

HANDLE

The handle usually the ones that holds the racket and it connects to the shaft. A material called grip is what covers the handle. There are two types of grips: the towel grips which are good for absorbing moisture, but may need to be changed frequently; synthetic grips are less absorbent but more durable
