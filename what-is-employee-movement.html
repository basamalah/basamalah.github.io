1. what is employee relations and movement?​


Answer:

Employee relations refers to the relationships and interactions between employers and employees in the workplace. It encompasses the various policies, practices, and procedures that employers use to manage their employees and create a positive work environment. Employee relations can include areas such as employee engagement, communication, performance management, compensation and benefits, employee development, and workplace culture.Employee movement refers to the various types of movement or transitions that employees can experience within a company. This can include promotions, transfers, job rotations, lateral moves, demotions, and terminations. Employee movement is often a key element of talent management and workforce planning, as it helps companies to develop and retain their employees, fill skill gaps, and adapt to changing business needs. Effective employee relations can play a crucial role in facilitating smooth and successful employee movements within an organization.

Answer:

Movement within an organization results from several factors. Movement can result from training programs, expansion, upskilling, termination and the voluntary departure of other employees. The human resources demands of the organization and the availability of employees also drive movement within an organization.
