Answer:

a. Number of committees with no restrictions:

The total number of people to choose from is 15 + 18 = 33. To form a committee of 12 members, we need to choose 12 people out of the 33. The order in which we choose the members does not matter, and we can choose the same person multiple times.

Using the combination formula, we can calculate the number of committees:

nCr = n! / (r! * (n-r)!)

where n = 33 and r = 12

nCr = 33! / (12! * (33-12)!)

nCr = 193536720

Therefore, there are 193,536,720 different committees with no restrictions.

b&c. All committee members are coming from SSG officials and faculty staff:

In this case, we need to form a committee of 12 members using only the faculty staff and SSG officials.

If all the committee members are coming from faculty staff, we can choose 12 members out of 15:

nCr = 15! / (12! * (15-12)!)

nCr = 455

If all the committee members are coming from SSG officials, we can choose 12 members out of 18:

nCr = 18! / (12! * (18-12)!)

nCr = 18564

Therefore, the total number of committees where all members come from either faculty staff or SSG officials is:

455 + 18564 = 19019

d. There are more SSG officials than faculty staff:

In this case, we can choose 12 members out of the 18 SSG officials, and we need to choose the remaining members from the 15 faculty staff. We can choose 0, 1, 2, ..., or 12 members from the faculty staff.

Using the combination formula, we can calculate the number of committees for each case and then add them up:

nCr = n! / (r! * (n-r)!)

15. representatives ssg speech ​


The Supreme Student Government (SSG) Speech

True, I am a newcomer to __________(Your school's name). This may appear to be a disadvantage at first glance. I didn't attend this school during my freshman and sophomore years, and I've only known the majority of you for a few years.

When you think about it, there is actually a benefit to hiring someone who is new to the school. I have new ideas that I can bring in from my previous school.

Are you tired of repeating yourself year after year? Are you willing to try something new?

When I first arrived at this school last year, I noticed that most of the students did not have much say in school decisions.

Have you ever been asked what you wanted to do for a fundraiser? Was it ever brought up to you what you wanted to buy with the money we raised? Who makes these choices?

The student council is in charge of making such decisions. Your choice for president will speak on behalf of the entire senior class. Do you want to pick someone who will make those decisions for you, even if they don't know what you want?

If you elect me as student council president, I promise to consult with you before making any decisions. You should be able to influence the decisions that affect you.

You can all come to me with your ideas, and I promise to listen and speak for you.
