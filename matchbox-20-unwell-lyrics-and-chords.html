7. 1. what happened to the matchbox when it was pushed lightly?2. what happened when you pushed the matchbox with a greater force?3. how did the force applied the acceleration of the matchbox?​


Answer:

Force and Acceleration Activity

1. The matchbox moved slowly and slightly when pushed lightly.

2. The matchbox moved faster when applied with a greater force.

3. The force applied is directly proportional to the acceleration of the matchbox.

Explanation:

Force is affected by the mass of the object to which the force is applied and the acceleration of the object. Force is directly proportional to mass and acceleration. It means that the greater the mass of the object, the greater force should be applied to accelerate it. Or lesser mass must need lesser force to accelerate it. Furthermore, as you increase the force exerted, the greater the acceleration of the object will be. Or less force will result in small acceleration.

In the activity, since you applied different amounts of force to the same object which is the matchbox, the mass remains the same. You just vary the amount of force applied to the matchbox. In the first part of the activity, when you lightly pushed the matchbox, you exerted a small amount of force thus resulting in smaller acceleration also. In the second part of the activity, you pushed the matchbox with a greater force thus resulting in greater acceleration.

What is the distance between two parallel chords of length 12cm and 15cm in a circle of radius 24cm?

Radius of the circle, with centre at O = 24 cm.

Length of chord AB = 12 cm. Mid point is E.

Length of chord CD = 15 cm. Mid point is F.

Case 1. When AB and CD are on the same side of O.

OE = [24^2 - 6^2]^0.5 = [30*18}^0.5 = 23.24 cm.

OF = [24^2 - 7.5^2]^0.5 = [31.5*16.5}^0.5 = 22.80 cm.

The distance between the chords Ab and CD = 23.24–22.80 = 0.44 cm

Case 1. When AB and CD are on the opposite sides of O.

OE = [24^2 - 6^2]^0.5 = [30*18}^0.5 = 23.24 cm.

OF = [24^2 - 7.5^2]^0.5 = [31.5*16.5}^0.5 = 22.80 cm.

The distance between the chords AB and CD = 23.24+22.80 = 46.04 cm

[tex]\qquad\:\:\LARGE\boxed{\tt Volume=lwh}\qquad[/tex]

[tex]\begin{array}{l} \tt Volume=lwh \\ \\ \tt Volume=(10cm)(5cm)(3cm)\\\\\red{\boxed{\tt Volume=150cm ^{3} }}\end{array}[/tex]
