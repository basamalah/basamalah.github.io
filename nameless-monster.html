12. A gorgon is a monster?


Answer:

A Gorgon is a monster figure in Greek Mythology. The Gorgon has three (3) heads, with different characters each. The Gorgons in the Greek Mythology are Stheno, the Mighty, Euryale, the Far Springer, and Medusa, the Queen. They are the daughters of the sea God Phorcys and his sister-turned-wife Ceto.

Explanation:

Know more about Greek Mythology here: https://brainly.ph/question/316983

The Family of Gorgons

The Gorgons were the daughters of Phorcys, a primitive Greek sea God, and Ceto, a hideous monster of the oceans. The Gorgons and their siblings were known collectively as the Phorcydes, along with the Graeae, the Hespirides, the Scylla and Charybdis. They were originally normal human beings, until Medusa was raped by Poseidon in the temple of Athena, who then turned her and her sisters into the repugnant beings they came to be.

You may read through the other characters of Greek Mythology here: https://brainly.ph/question/1319737

The Powers of the Gorgons

According to Greek Mythology, the Gorgons were originally ugly monsters with huge wings, sharp fangs and claws, and their bodies covered with dragon-like scales. They had horrible faces, staring eyes, and writhing snakes as their hair.

Their gaze was so terrifying that anyone who looked straight into them will immediately turn to stone. Medusa was the only mortal Gorgon, here two sisters, Stheno and Euryale, were immortal.

Know more about Medusa here: https://brainly.ph/question/2230149

The three Gorgons were depicted in an ancient Greek painting and sculpture as winged women with broad, round heads, serpentine locks of hair, large staring eyes, wide mouths, lolling tongues, the tusks of swine, flared nostrils, and sometimes short, coarse beards. However, Medusa was humanized in late classical art with the face of a beautiful woman. In mosaic art her round face was wreathed with coiling snakes and adorned with a pair of small wings on the brow.
