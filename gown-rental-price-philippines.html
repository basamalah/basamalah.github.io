2. mrs. curz business is gown rental and sale. if she has 5 mannequins and will display 10 new gowns in her shops window. how many ways can she select the gowns to be displayed​

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad\Large\rm 252 \ ways [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» Since order is not important. We will be using the Formula of Combination.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \bold{\color{lightblue}Formula:} \\ & \quad\boxed{\ \rm _nC_r = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)! \ }} \end{aligned} [/tex]

» Find the number of combinations of 10 gowns taken 5 at a time.

[tex] _{10}C_5 = \frac{10!}{5!(10 - 5)! }\\ [/tex]

[tex] _{10}C_5 = \frac{10!}{5! \ 5! }\\ [/tex]

[tex] _{10}C_5 = \frac{10 \cdot 9 \cdot 8 \cdot 7 \cdot 6 \cdot \cancel{5!}}{5 \cdot 4 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot \cancel{ 5!} }\\ [/tex]

[tex] _{10}C_5 = \frac{ \cancel{10} \ ^{2} \cdot 9 \cdot \cancel{ \: 8 \: } \ ^{2} \cdot 7 \cdot \cancel{ \: 6 \: }}{ \cancel{ \: 5 \: } \cdot \cancel{ \: 4 \: } \cdot \cancel{ \: 3 \: } \cdot \cancel{ \: 2 \: }}\\ [/tex]

[tex] _{10}C_5 = 2 \cdot 9 \cdot 2 \cdot 7[/tex]

[tex] _{10}C_5 = 252[/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] There are 252 ways of 5 mannequins be selected from 10 new gowns.

26. read the following items and identify by writing Ceiling if its about price ceiling or floor if it is about price floor. 1. Imposed by the government to protect farmers from price fluctuation. 2. Setting of minimum wage. 3. Setting the maximum rental price to protect consumers. 4. Imposed by the government to prevent a particular price of a good to drop 5. It is a maximum price that can be charged for a product or service.​


Answer:

1.floor

2.floor

3.price ceiling

4.price ceiling

5.price ceiling

Explanation:

The government can impose various measures to protect farmers from price fluctuations in the market. Some of these measures include:

Minimum Support Price (MSP): The government sets a minimum price at which it guarantees to purchase agricultural produce from farmers. This ensures that farmers get a fair price for their crops and protects them from market fluctuations.

Price Stabilization Fund (PSF): The government can establish a fund to provide financial support to farmers in case of price fluctuations in the market. The PSF can be used to buy agricultural produce from farmers when the market price falls below a certain level.

Crop Insurance: The government can provide crop insurance to farmers to protect them from losses due to natural disasters, pests, and diseases. This helps farmers to manage their risks and ensures a stable income for them.

Export-Import Policies: The government can regulate the export and import of agricultural commodities to stabilize prices in the domestic market. This can be done by imposing export restrictions, import duties, and tariffs.

Overall, these measures aim to protect farmers from price fluctuations and provide them with a stable income, which is crucial for the growth and development of the agricultural sector.
