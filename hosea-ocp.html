21. Why is Hosea's story as a prophet a unique one?​


Answer:

 Hosea reminds us that in Judaism repentance, judgment, personal struggle and measurement are all part of prayer. To pray in Judaism is less about asking God to do something for us, than it is to judge oneself, to see the errors of one's ways; to find a way to correct these errors. Prayer in this regard is the seeking of wisdom, the struggle to attempt to know God's plan for each of us and then put this plan into action. Hosea brings into Judaism the fact that we are more than merely God's creatures and servants; we are also God's partners in the constant unfolding of history.
