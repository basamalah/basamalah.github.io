What is the Dinagyang festival?

The Dinagyang Festival is a well-known nonsecular and cultural festival held each yr on the 4th Sunday of January withinside the metropolis of Iloilo, Philippines, or at once after the Ati-atihan Festival in Sinulog and Aklan in Cebu.

It traces the tale of devotion to the Holy Child Jesus, normally acknowledged to Filipino believers as Sr. Sto. Nino. The pageant additionally commemorates the appearance of  Malay settlers and a mythical barter transaction with indigenous humans known as Ati on Panay Island.

The essence of Dinagyang Festival as a cultural festival is the portrayal and depiction of the manner of lifestyles of the Ilonggo aborigines which is going past the mere use of the bow, arrow, spear, and defense as the principle symbolism in their culture.
