5. Title: The Trojan War Author: Characters: Plot: Exposition: Rising Action: Climax: Falling Action: Resolution:​


Answer:

The Trojan War Author: Homer

Characters:

1. Achilles, the greatest warrior of Greece

2. Agamemnon, the King of Mycenae and the leader of the Greek army

3. Hector, the greatest warrior of Troy

4. Priam, the King of Troy

5. Helen, the wife of King Menelaus of Sparta, whose beauty was the cause of the war

6. Odysseus, the hero of the Odyssey and a clever strategist for the Greeks

7. Paris, a Trojan prince who abducted Helen, starting the war

Plot: The Trojan War is a legendary conflict between the Greek and Trojan army. It was fought over the abduction of Helen, the wife of Menelaus, by Paris. Greece gathered an army led by Agamemnon, the King of Mycenae, to retrieve Helen. They sailed to Troy, a city in modern-day Turkey, and besieged it for ten years.

Exposition: The war begins with the abduction of Helen. Paris, a Trojan prince, falls in love with her and takes her back to Troy. This act of dishonor and disrespect prompts Menelaus to call on his brother, Agamemnon, to gather an army and retrieve Helen.

Rising Action: The Greek army, led by Agamemnon, sails to Troy to retrieve Helen. They face several obstacles during the ten-year siege, including the Trojan army led by Hector, the greatest warrior of Troy. Achilles, the greatest warrior of Greece, also joins the fight and becomes a key player in the war.

Climax: The turning point of the war is when Achilles kills Hector. Troy's best warrior is now dead, and the Trojans' morale is low. They decide to use a Trojan horse to penetrate the Greek army's defenses, but it turns out to be a trap. Greek soldiers emerge from the horse and attack the unprepared Trojans, leading to the fall of the city.

Falling Action: Troy falls, and the Greeks are victorious. The remaining Trojans flee, and many die in the process. Achilles, who was invincible in battle, is killed by Paris, who shoots an arrow into his heel, the only vulnerable part of his body.

Resolution: The war ends with the fall of Troy, and the Greeks return home victorious. However, their journey home is filled with obstacles and tragedy, as described in Homer's famous epic, the Odyssey. The story of the Trojan War has become a timeless tale of love, war, and the human condition.
