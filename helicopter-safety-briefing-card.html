7. A helicopter is flying at a height of 1.5km. If it has 50 x 10 6 Joules of potential energy, what is the mass of the helicopter?


The mass of the helicopter : 3401.36 kg

Further explanation

Potential energy (PE) is the energy that an object has because of the location of that object. Usually, it is related to the force of gravity

 [tex]\rm PE=m.g.h[/tex]

g = acceleration due to gravity (m/s²)

h = height (m)

m = mass (kg)

The higher the object from the surface of the earth and the greater the mass, the greater the potential energy of the object. Earth's gravity also affects the potential energy of objects

Height of helicopter : h = 1.5 km = 1500 m

Potential energy :PE = 50.10⁶ J

The mass of helicopter :

[tex]\rm mass=\dfrac{PE}{g.h}\\\\mass=\dfrac{50.10^6}{9.8\times 1500}\\\\mass=3401.36~kg[/tex]

14. A helicopter’s speed increases from 30 m/s to 40 m/s in 5 seconds. What is the acceleration of this helicopter?


Answer:

2 m/s²

Explanation:

Acceleration is computed by dividing the change in velocity over the change in time. The formula then is as follows,

[tex]\vec{a} = \frac{v_f - v_i}{t_f - t_i}[/tex]

Substitute the given values. Take note that initial time is 0 as it is not stated.

[tex]\vec{a} = \frac{40 - 30}{5 - 0}\\\vec{a} = \frac{10}{5}\\\vec{a} = 2[/tex]

Therefore, the acceleration is 2 m/s².

17. If the mass of a helicopter is 4,500 kg and the net force on it is 18,000 N, what is the helicopter acceleration


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The force acting on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F = ma}[/tex]

where:

F = force

m = mass

a = acceleration

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for a

F = ma

[tex]\dfrac{F}{m} = \dfrac{ma}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{F}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{\text{18,000 N}}{\text{4,500 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{a = \text{4 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

Therefore, the acceleration of the helicopter is 4 m/s².
