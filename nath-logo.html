What is the meaning of "of" in math?

[tex]\bullet[/tex] "Of" in math means "to multiply".

Example:

20% of 100

Multiply 20% or 0.20 by 100.

0.20 × 100 = 20

20% of 100 is 20.

June 1, 1992, Melva Nath began to work in Shangri-la Hotel Manila as Director ofRooms for a probationary period for a minimum of six (6) months. Resident ManagerGerard Sintes met with Nath for their regular weekly meeting on September 4, 1992.Since Nath’s performance was due for evaluation on September 11, Sintes warns Nathof her poor work performance and non-compliance with company rules.Nath neither disputed Sintes' findings nor even if her alleged acts , andomissions. On September 7, Nath called up the hotel and left word that she was sickand couldn’t report for work. Personnel Manager Teresa Lalin returned Nath’s call toremind that a medical certificate was needed to submit by her. Nath informed Lalin thatshe would report for work on September 9. Nath didn’t report on September 9. Lalin andSales Director Gami Holazo visited Nath’s residence. They relayed head Al Wymann’smessage requiring Nath to report on duty and reminded Nath about her scheduledevaluation on September 11. The hotel dismissed Nath on September 14, 1992. Butafterward, Nath filed a complaint for illegal dismissal. Labor Arbiter Ramon Valentin C.Reyes sustained Nath, a decision is hereby rendered declaring the dismissal ofcomplainant as illegal, and thereby ordering respondent Shangri-la Hotel Manila toback the complainant to her former position without loss of seniority rights , and otherprivileges. And to pay complainant, her full back wages counted from September 14 upto until she shall be actually reinstated, moral damages of seventy-five thousand(75,000) peso and punitive damages of fifty thousand (50,000) pesos, and an Attorney’sfees corresponding to ten percent (10%) of the quantity to be recovered. However, thepublic respondent NLRC set aside the appealed decision is hereby set aside and a newone is entered dismissing the complaint for lack of merit. Shangri-la Hotel Manila isordered to pay complainant, her salaries and other related benefits for which she isentitled by reason for her payroll reinstatement for the remaining three (3) months of herprobationary period unless respondent has already done.

Respondent is further ordered to pay the sum of one thousand (1000) pesos bythe way of penalty for respondent’s non-compliance with due process of law. TheRespondent NLRC committed Grave Abuse of Discretion in Upholding the Legality ofPetitioner’s Dismissal despite the fact there was no due process of law and petitioner’salleged substandard work performance was never cited as a ground for termination norraised as a problem , and there was no clear, independent, and satisfactory evidence toprove that petitioner’s work performance was substandard. The dismissal for MelvaNath was for just and authorized cause, but without due process of law. Nath’s case isn't applicable for reinstatement, back wages, damages and attorney’s fees. As the courtruled in Wenphil Corporation vs NLRC, the very fact that the worker wasn 't affordedgroup action doesn 't operate to eradicate just causes that he can be dismissed.

12. About Melva Nath versus Shangri-la manila hotel. Did the company observe the process in the case?


Answer:

Explanation:

Shangrila Hotel Manila didn’t observed due process of law according to this case the company committed grave abuse of discretion from the second division for upholding the legality of petitioner’s (Melva Nath) dismissal despite the fact that: (A) There was no due process and petitioners alleged substandard work performance was never cited as a ground for termination nor raised as an issue; and (B) There was no clear, independent and satisfactory evidence to prove that petitioner’s work performance was substandard.

Under the rules implementing Batas Pambansa Blg. 130 provided:

Sec.2. Notice of Dismissal- Any employer who seeks to dismiss a worker shall furnish him a written notice stating the particular acts or omission constituting the grounds for his dismissal. In cases of abandonment of work, the notice shall be observed at the worker’s last known address.

Sec.6. Decision to Dismissal- The employer shall immediately notify a worker in writing of a decision to dismiss him stating clearly the reasons therefor.

Shangrila Hotel Manila only provided one letter of dismissal to Nath showing the records of her absent days to work and failed to submit any medical certificate. According to the rules requiring the employer to furnish the worker sought to be dismissed with two written notices before termination of employment can be legally effected:(1) notice which apprises the employee of the particular acts or omissions for which his dismissal is sought; and (2) the subsequent notice which informs the employee of the respondent (Shangrila) have failed to furnish petitioner with the first of the required two (2) notices and to state plainly the reasons for the dismissal in the termination letter.

26. Enumerate the different countries of southeast asia nathe musical characteristic of each country.​


Answer:

A general musical division exists between the urban and rural areas of Southeast Asia. Urban centres comprise the islands of Java and Bali and places in Thailand, Laos, Cambodia, and Myanmar, where big ensembles of gong families play for court and state ceremonies. Rural areas include other islands and remote places, where smaller ensembles and solo instruments play a simpler music for village feasts, curing ceremonies, and daily activities. In cities and towns influenced by Hindu epics such as the Ramayana and Mahabharata, shadow and masked plays and dances utilizing music play important communal roles, while in less urbanized areas, in lieu of musical plays, chants and songs in spirit worship and rituals are sung in exclusive surroundings—a ritual procession on the headwaters of Borneo, a drinking ceremony in the jungles of Palawan, a feast in the uplands of Luzon.
