18. What is Rizal's definition of the Latin dictum Vox populi, vox dei? How is it connected to his concept of a nation?​


Answer:

The meaning of Vox populi, vox Dei is "The Friar and the Filipino."

Vox populi, vox Dei is a fundamental principle in Rizal’s moral concept of the nation, which may be characterized as an anti-statist and prescriptive concept. It is anti-statist in that it affirms the separation, indeed opposition, between the people and the state. Thus, we should not conflate Rizal’s concept of the nation with the entirely different concept of the nation-state, which is based on the Enlightenment notion of the "sovereign people."
