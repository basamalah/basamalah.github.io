Answer:

Both the main characters of "President Cleveland, Where are You?" and "Raymond's Run" experience significant changes throughout their respective stories. In "President Cleveland, Where are You?", the main character, Bo, is initially a shy, timid boy who is afraid of change and afraid to stand up for himself. He is extremely self-conscious and is easily embarrassed. Over the course of the story, Bo learns to accept himself and his unique qualities, growing more confident in himself and his abilities. He also learns to stand up for himself and take charge of his life.

Similarly, in "Raymond's Run", the main character, Squeaky, also experiences a transformation throughout the story. At the beginning of the story, Squeaky is a confident, feisty girl who is fiercely protective of her brother Raymond. She faces her fears and learns to be a leader by standing up to other kids and standing up for Raymond. Although she is initially hesitant, Squeaky learns to accept that she can do great things and stops underestimating herself. She also learns to accept Raymond's disability and to take responsibility for him
