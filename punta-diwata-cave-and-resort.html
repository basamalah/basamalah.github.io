23. give the names of the minerals cave painters use as pigments for their cave paintingâ€‹


Answer and Explanation

Prehistoric painters would use natural pigments found in the vicinity of the caves. It is called an "Earth Pigments" because most of the pigments were minerals like calcite (white), limonite (yellowish), hematite (Dark gray), red ochre (red to brown), yellow ochre (yellow, orange, or brown) and umber (natural brown or reddish-brown). Other materials were made out of Charcoal (carbon black) and bones (bone black). These minerals were grounded to powder and mixed with a little water to produce pigments. Aside from Cave Paintings, "Earth Pigments" were also used as body paints for ritual purposes.
