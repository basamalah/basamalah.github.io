14. What is the function of production well in geothermal powerplant


[tex]\huge\pink{ANSWER}[/tex]

What is the function of production well in geothermal powerplant

A production well is drilled into a known geothermal reservoir. Typically, an injection well is also drilled to return used geothermal fluids to the geothermal reservoir. Hot geothermal fluids flow through pipes to a power plant for use in generating electricity.

16. Process in geothermal electric production?


Here are the correct sequence on how to generate electricity in a geothermal power plant.

Wells are drilled deep into the earth to pump steam or hot water to the surface. When the water reaches the surface, the drop in pressure causes the water to turn  into steam. The steam spins a turbine, which is connected to a generator that produces electricity. Cooling tower cools the steam which it condenses back to water. The cooled water is pumped back into the earth to begin the process again.

29. 3. Which of the following is found at the plate boundaries wheremost volcanoes are located?a. Geothermal power plantsb. Geothermal fieldsc. Philippinesd. Pacific plate​


Answer:

D. Pacific Ocean

Sixty percent of all active volcanoes occur at the boundaries between tectonic plates. Most volcanoes are found along a belt, called the “Ring of Fire” that encircles the Pacific Ocean. Some volcanoes, like those that form the Hawaiian Islands, occur in the interior of plates at areas called “hot spots.”

Answer:

D. Pacific plate

Explanation:

Although most volcanoes are found at convergent or divergent plate boundaries, intraplate volcanoes are found in the middle of a tectonic plate. The Hawaiian Islands are the exposed peaks of a great chain of volcanoes that lie on the Pacific plate.


30. what are the economic impacts of geothermal energy production In the Philippines​


Answer:

Geothermal energy is a significant source of power in the Philippines, accounting for around 18% of the country's electricity production. Here are some of the economic impacts of geothermal energy production in the Philippines:

1. Job creation: Geothermal power plants require a skilled workforce, ranging from engineers and geologists to plant operators and maintenance personnel. This creates employment opportunities and generates income for local communities.

2. Energy security: Geothermal energy is a reliable and consistent source of power that can help reduce the country's dependence on imported fuels. This can help stabilize energy prices and reduce the country's vulnerability to supply disruptions.

3. Revenue generation: Geothermal energy production generates revenue for the government through taxes and royalties. This can be used to fund public services and infrastructure projects.

4. Export potential: The Philippines has significant geothermal energy resources and is one of the world's top geothermal power producers. This could create export opportunities for geothermal equipment and expertise.

5. Environmental benefits: Compared to traditional fossil fuel power plants, geothermal energy production has a lower carbon footprint and produces less air and water pollution. This can lead to improved public health and reduced healthcare costs.
