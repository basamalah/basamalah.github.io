12. 1.1 Why is counseling an art and, at the same time, a science? 1.2 Why is counseling defined as a relationship? What are the characteristics of a counseling relationship? 1.3 Why is counseling referred to as an emerging helping profession?​


It is a science because it employs scientific methods in a professional environment to help clients deal effectively with their problems. It is an art because how effective methods may be used, the counsellor must have the knack to handle the client well.

What is the therapeutic relationship? A strong bond is crucial to the success of counselling and psychotherapy. ... Therapy allows clients the chance to explore their relational attachments, bonds and experiences through their relationship with their therapist, which is why this relationship is so important

Patience:

You need to be very patient. Go to the next step of explanation only when the patient/client has clearly understood the content of the information you are giving. Thus you need to have ample time for the client/patient.

Good Listening:

You need to be a good listener. Never interrupt what the patient/client has /is to say. Give your inputs only when the client / patient has finished talking.

Observant:

You need to be very observant and able to interpret non-verbal communication e.g. if the patient/client looks angry, find out the cause of his/her anger first.

Warm:

Provide non-possessive warmth in a counseling environment. Smile and show concern and acceptance to the patient/client.

Knowledgeable:

You should have good knowledge on the topic /problem e.g. compliance to medication. Some people do not take medication for one reason or the other, while others demand drugs/medication. For example, Muslims do not take oral medication when they are fasting while Jehovah’s witnesses do not take blood transfusion. Understanding the factors why people may not do certain activities at specific time will assist to assist them better.

Having empathy with the patient/client:

Try to understand the feelings the patient/client is having in the counseling process. In other words put yourself in his/her position.

Maintaining a therapeutic relationship with a patient:

Give the patient/client the opportunity to make his/her own decision from your message.

Confidentiality:

Although confidentiality is important in health matters it does not apply very much to all situations e.g. most people will openly say what they feel/ the problem they are having. However, ensure that you maintain confidentiality on what the patient/client tells you. The patient/client would feel greatly offended if you disclose any information about him or her to other people. This means that counseling must be done individually and privately.

Personal integrity:

Maintain a high degree of personal integrity, credibility and mutual trust as a counselor.

Counseling is a collaborative effort between the counselor and client. Professional counselors help clients identify goals and potential solutions to problems which cause emotional turmoil; seek to improve communication and coping skills; strengthen self-esteem; and promote behavior change and optimal mental health.

14. Discuss the following areas of specialization where councelors work1. Child development and counseling2. Adolescent development and counseling3. Gerontological counseling4. Marital or relationship counseling5. Health6. Phobia counseling​


Child development and counseling: Counselors who specialize in child development and counseling work with children who are experiencing emotional, behavioral, or social problems. They help children develop coping mechanisms and strategies to manage their emotions and behaviors, as well as provide guidance to parents and caregivers on how to support their child's development.

Adolescent development and counseling: Counselors who specialize in adolescent development and counseling work with teenagers who are experiencing a wide range of issues, including peer pressure, identity development, academic challenges, and mental health concerns. They help adolescents navigate these challenges and develop skills to manage stress, anxiety, and other mental health issues.

Gerontological counseling: Gerontological counselors work with older adults and their families to address issues related to aging, such as physical health concerns, loss of independence, and end-of-life decisions. They help seniors maintain their independence, cope with chronic health conditions, and address issues related to social isolation and loneliness.

Marital or relationship counseling: Counselors who specialize in marital or relationship counseling work with couples to improve their communication skills, resolve conflicts, and strengthen their relationships. They may also help couples navigate difficult life transitions, such as divorce or the loss of a partner.

Health counseling: Health counselors work with individuals who are dealing with chronic illnesses, injuries, or other health concerns. They help clients manage their conditions, develop healthy habits, and address any emotional or psychological issues related to their health.

Phobia counseling: Phobia counselors work with individuals who are experiencing intense fear or anxiety related to a specific object, situation, or activity. They help clients identify the root causes of their phobias and develop coping mechanisms to manage their symptoms. Treatment may involve exposure therapy, cognitive-behavioral therapy, or other specialized interventions.

21. B. Briefly describe the following areas of specialization where counselors work: 1. Child development and counseling 2. Adolescent development and counseling 3. Gerontology counseling 4. Marital or relationship counseling 5. Health 6. Phobia counseling


Answer:

Child development and counseling involves working with children and their families to promote healthy social, emotional, and cognitive development. Counselors may work with children who have experienced trauma or have developmental delays, as well as provide support and guidance to parents and families.

Adolescent development and counseling involves working with teenagers to address the challenges they face during this transitional period, including issues related to identity, self-esteem, peer pressure, and family relationships. Counselors may also work with adolescents who are struggling with mental health issues such as depression, anxiety, or substance abuse.

Gerontology counseling involves working with older adults to address the unique challenges and transitions that come with aging. Counselors may work with older adults who are dealing with chronic illnesses, cognitive decline, or loss of independence, as well as provide support and guidance to caregivers and family members.

Marital or relationship counseling involves working with couples and families to improve communication, resolve conflicts, and build healthier relationships. Counselors may help couples navigate challenges related to infidelity, divorce, or parenting, as well as provide premarital counseling to help couples establish a strong foundation for their relationship.

Health counseling involves working with individuals to promote healthy behaviors and lifestyle changes that can improve physical and mental health outcomes. Counselors may work with individuals who are struggling with chronic illnesses such as diabetes or heart disease, as well as provide support and guidance to those who are dealing with mental health issues such as anxiety or depression.

Phobia counseling involves working with individuals to overcome irrational fears or phobias that are causing distress and interfering with daily life. Counselors may use a variety of techniques such as exposure therapy or cognitive-behavioral therapy to help individuals overcome their phobias and improve their quality of life.
