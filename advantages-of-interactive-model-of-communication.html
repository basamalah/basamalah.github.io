11. Advantages and disadvantages of interactive model of communication​


The interactional or interactive model was developed by Wilbur Schramm (1954) which emphasizes the process of two-way communication between communicators. In other words, communication takes place in two directions: from the sender to the receiver and from the receiver to the sender. This circular process shows that communication is always taking place. The interactional view illustrates that a person can be both a sender and a receiver in an interaction, but not both at the same time.

An important element in this model is feedback or response to a message. Feedback can be verbal or nonverbal, intentional or unintentional. Feedback is very helpful for communicators to know whether their message has been conveyed or not and to what extent the achievement of meaning has occurred. In the interactional model, feedback occurs after the message is received, not while the message is being sent.

The other most important element or part in the concept of interactional communication is characterized by the presence of a person's field of experience, culture or descent that can affect the ability to communicate with others. Each communication participant brings a unique and distinctive experience in every communication behavior that can affect the communication that occurs.

ADVANTAGES :

Circular communication provides an opportunity for both parties to share their opinions.Schramm's communication model is dynamic and always changing so it is very helpful in various communication practices in general.The sender of the message and the recipient of the message exchange messages on the same active level.Semantic disorder is a concept that helps understanding the various problems that can occur during the interpretation of messages.Feedback makes it easy to find out whether the message interpreted by the recipient of the message is in accordance with the purpose or not.The concept of interpretation makes communication effective.Fields of experience or psychological effects help understand the communication process in several ways compared to traditional ways.The concept of context allows various environmental factors to be incorporated into the interpretation of the message and bring about changes in the value of the message.

DISANVANTAGES

Feedback doesn't happen simultaneouslyFeedback is delayed or takes a long timeFeedback is indirectCommunication does not run dynamicallyPredictable communication patternsCommunication becomes linear when the recipient of the message does not provide a response or feedbackThe Schramm communication model cannot be used in multiple levels of communication and complex communication processes.Messages sent and received may be interpreted differently.There are only two sources communicating, multiple sources make the process more complex and the model cannot be implemented.
