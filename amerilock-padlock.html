Answer:

5709

Step-by-step explanation:

The bottom left combination says that there are no correct digits, so that means the only available digits are 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, and 0.

The top left cobination says that there are 2 correct digits, so we know for sure based on the information that we gathered that 5 and 7 are in the code.

The bottom right combination says that it has 2 correct digits, and that both are in the correct place. We know that 4 isn’t the one of the correct digits, and it can’t be 1 either, since if we were to say that 1 is the correct digit and we were to go by the rules in the top right combination, it wouldn’t make sense. So the correct digits that are in postion are 7 and 9.

Going by that, we now know that the digits are 5, 7, 9 and 0, but we need to figure out the order. We know by the rules of the bottom right combination that the code looks like this:

_7_9

We know that 5 is supposed to be the first digit of the cobination, since the rules of the top left combination imply that 5 is not at the third space, and the only other option is at the first space. The same logic applies with 0 and the rules of the bottom right combination. Hence:

5709
