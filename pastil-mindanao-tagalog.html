A staminate flower contains stamens only, whereas only carpels or pistils are found in a pistillate flower. This is the big differentiation. Stamens are male reproductive organs, while pistils are structures of female reproduction.

12. paano gumawa Ng pastil?​

Ingredients

2 cups of powdered milk

1 can (14 oz.) of condensed milk

1/2 cup of sugar

1 tablespoon of margarine

Method  1 : Making No-Cook Pastillas

Image titled Make Pastillas Step 1

1

Pour the powdered and the condensed milk in a bowl. Just pour 2 cups of powdered milk and 1 can (14 oz.) of condensed milk into a bowl. This recipe should make about 80 candies.

Image titled Make Pastillas Step 2

2

Mix the powdered and condensed milk together. The mixture may be a little thick and hard to mix together, so have patience and a thick and sturdy spoon.

Image titled Make Pastillas Step 3

3

Add the margarine to the mixture. Add 1 tablespoon of margarine to the mixture; alternately, you can use real butter. This will help add an extra creamy flavor to the treat. Mix it in with the other ingredients.

Image titled Make Pastillas Step 4

4

Shape your candy into circles or cylinders. Choose the shape you'd like for your pastillas; they can be circular, or they can be more cylindrical, like Tootsie Rolls. Just use your hands to give them their desired shape; you can wear gloves if you want to. Place the shaped candies on a plate.

Image titled Make Pastillas Step 5

5

Pour the sugar on a plate. Pour half a cup of sugar on a plate.

Image titled Make Pastillas Step 6

6

Roll the pastillas into the sugar. Make sure that all part are covered.

Image titled Make Pastillas Step 7

7

Wrap the pastillas in cellophane. You can cut the cellophane in advance to get the desired shape. Then place the pastillas in the cellophane and wrap the ends of it.

Image titled Make Pastillas Step 8

8

Serve. Put the candies on a party plate and enjoy. You can have them for dessert or serve them as a snack whenever you like.

25. paano mag luto ng pastil​


Answer:

Ingredients:

1/2 kg of chicken

1 cup of chicken broth or 1 buoillion cube

1/4 cup of dark soy sauce (silver swan)

3 tbsp of oyster sauce (lee kum kee)

1 tsp white sugar

3 cloves of garlic

1 medium onion

a pinch of ground black pepper

1 tbsp of cooking oil

20 pcs of banana leaves (semi-rectangular cut)

10 cups of cooked white rice

Procedure:

Heat the banana leaves indirectly over your stove to avoid brittleness.

Cook your rice in a pot or in a rice cooker and set aside.

Boil the chicken in a pot until tender. Reserve the broth.

Shred the cooked chicken into small strips.

Heat your wok and pour in cooking oil. Saute the garlic and onion until fragrant. Add the shredded chicken and simmer for minutes. Add a cup of chicken broth and simmer until the liquid evaporates then add a pinch of ground black pepper.

Add the dark soy sauce and simmer for 15 minutes. Pour the oyster sauce and 1 tsp sugar. Add water or broth if needed according to your taste preference.

Once the liquid subsides, Turn off the heat and transfer to serving bowl.

Place the banana leaf in a clean kitchen table. Brush a little oil on the leaf. Put half cup of rice on the leaf and add 1 tbsp of the chicken filling. Wrap neatly and serve.
