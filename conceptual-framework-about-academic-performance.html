Conceptual Framework:

The conceptual framework for studying the impact of using online applications during the pandemic on the academic performance of all General Academic Strand (GAS) students involves several key components:

Independent Variable: Use of Online Applications during Pandemic - This refers to the extent to which GAS students utilized online applications for academic purposes during the pandemic. It includes various online tools and platforms used for virtual classes, online assignments, assessments, and communication with teachers and peers.

Dependent Variable: Academic Performance of GAS Students - This refers to the overall academic performance of GAS students, including their grades, test scores, and other measures of academic achievement. It is the outcome or result that is being investigated to determine how it is affected by the use of online applications during the pandemic.

Mediating Factors: Access to Technology and Internet, Digital Literacy, and Motivation - These are factors that may influence the relationship between the use of online applications and academic performance. Access to technology and internet refers to the availability of devices and reliable internet connection for students to access online applications. Digital literacy pertains to the students' ability to effectively navigate and utilize online tools and platforms. Motivation refers to the students' level of engagement and motivation to learn in an online environment.

Moderating Factors: Socioeconomic Status, Home Environment, and School Support - These are factors that may affect how the use of online applications during the pandemic impacts academic performance differently for different groups of GAS students. Socioeconomic status refers to the economic and social background of students, which may influence their access to resources and support. Home environment includes the availability of conducive learning environment at home, parental support, and other home-related factors. School support pertains to the level of support provided by the school, such as online teaching strategies, resources, and support services.

The relationships among these components will be examined through empirical research, such as surveys, interviews, and data analysis, to understand the impact of using online applications during the pandemic on the academic performance of GAS students, and how various factors may influence this relationship. The conceptual framework serves as a guide for conducting the research and understanding the complex interactions and dynamics involved in this topic.
