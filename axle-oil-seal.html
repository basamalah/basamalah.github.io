12. What is the axle shape and size compared to the wheels What is the axle shape and size compared to the wheels


The shape and size of the axle compared to the wheels can vary depending on the type of vehicle or equipment. In general, the axle is a cylindrical-shaped rod or shaft that supports the weight of the vehicle or equipment and connects the wheels to the vehicle's chassis or frame. The size of the axle and wheels can also vary depending on the load capacity and intended use of the vehicle or equipment. For example, a heavy-duty truck may have a thicker and stronger axle compared to a small car, and the wheels may be larger in diameter to support the weight of the vehicle. In general, the diameter of the axle is larger than the diameter of the wheels it supports.
