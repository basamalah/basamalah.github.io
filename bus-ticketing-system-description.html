4. Show all necessary solutions. Follow the steps in solving word problems involving systems of equations by substitution; The theater club sells a total of 101 tickets to its first play. A student ticket cost $1. An adult ticket costs $2.50. Total ticket sales are $164. How many student tickets were sold?


Answer:

There are 59 student tickets sold.

Step-by-step explanation:

Number of tickets:

Adult tickets: xStudent tickets: yTotal tickets sold:  101

Equation (1):

x + y = 101

y = 101-x  (student tickets)

Cost per ticket:

Cost per adult ticket: $2.50xCost per student ticket: $1yTotal ticket sales: $164

Equation (2):

2.50x + 1y = 164

or

2.5x + y = 164

Solve by substitution where y=101-x:

2.5x + y = 164

2.5x + (101-x) = 164

2.5x -x + 101 = 164

Find x, the number of adult tickets:

1.5x = 164 - 101

1.5x/1.5 = 63/1.5

x = 42  ⇒  number of adult tickets

Find y, the number of student tickets:

y = 101 - 42

y = 59  ⇒  number of student tickets

There are 59 student tickets sold.

Check:

x = 42  and y = 59

Equation (1):

x + y = 101

42 + 59 = 101

101 = 101  (True)

Equation (2):

2.5x + 1y = 164

2.5(42) + (59) = 164

105 + 59 = 164

164 = 164 (True)

10. in how many ways can 10 people line up for a bus ticket ?​


Answer:

10! ways = 3,628,800 ways

Step-by-step explanation:

This is a permutation without replacement (the 10 people can line up in every spot on the line but cannot be in two different spots at the same time). In other words, the number of possibilities for each spot on the line...

10 * 9 * 8 ... * 2 * 1

Each spot has 1 less possibility than the one before it. So you can find the permutations of 10 people lining up simply with 10! = 3,628,800.

Step-by-step explanation:

The correct option is B)

Let Rs. x be the fare of city B from city A and Rs. y be the fare of city C from city A.

Then, 2x+3y=77    (i) and

3x+2y=73      (ii)

Multiplying (i) by 3 and (ii) by 2 and subtracting, we get: 5y=85 or y=17.

Putting y=17 in (i), we get: x=13.
