16. example of reaction paper about sona


Here is a guide to help you write your reaction paper regarding President Duterte’s SONA 2018 (State of the Nation Address) which happened last July 23, 2018.

To write a reaction paper, you need to analyze a topic and then develop a commentary regarding it. You must understand the purpose of the reaction paper, read the topic assigned to you, write down your initial reactions, jot down notes while going through the topic, evaluate the text you are reading, and then draft your paper.

When drafting your paper, do the following steps: free-write your reactions, decide on your side or angle, determine your argument, organize your paper, gather quotations that can support your claims, and structure your paragraphs. Make sure to write your reaction paper by including an introduction, relate your thoughts with greater implications you have discussed in class, follow the instruction on length or number of words, ensure clarity of your thoughts, and proofread and check the spellings of your reaction paper.

To help you regarding your reaction paper of latest SONA of Duterte which was delivered last July 23, 2018, here is a quick summary of Duterte’s speech for reaction paper:

> His introduction for SONA included mentioning the names of key guests during the event, and recalling his oath-taking that happened about two years ago.

> Drugs – He said that the war on drugs will still be “relentless” and he gave criticisms about the human rights groups who are against his anti-drug campaign.

> Corruption – He mentioned that Congress successfully passed the Ease of Doing Business Act. He urged all agencies to stop red tape and make processes efficient. He contradicted "lousy, corrupt bureaucracy". He said he needs to fire friends due to corruption.

> Mindanao – He promised to increase significantly the budget for Mindanao. He also promised to sign the Bangsamoro Law within 48 hours after Congress passes the approved version to Malacanang. He also recognized the soldiers and policemen who died fighting in Marawi City. He said he welcomes with open arms terrorists who will surrender to authorities.

> Foreign policy – He vowed to assert independent foreign policy. He mentioned reenergized ties with China and promises to defend the West Philippine Sea. He said to seek stronger bonds with fellow ASEAN countries.

> Telecommunications and internet – He re-emphasized his promise to ensure affordable, reliable, affordable, and secure internet telecommunications services. He promised to lower interconnection rates between industry players.

> Tax reform – He told Congress that he wants to sign TRAIN Law 2 by end of 2018. He mentioned that TRAIN Law is for the sustainable growth that leaves no Filipino behind.

> Rice – He asked businesses to cooperate in stabilizing rice prices. By the end of July 2018, rice tarrification would have to be submitted to Congress.

> Health – He called Congress to pass the universal health bill. He mentioned that government is currently institutionalizing the unified implementation of “No Balance Billing” policy.

> Federalism – He urged Filipinos to support the shift to a federal system of government. He mentioned that he has no illusions of occupying office one day longer than what the Constitution permits.

On labor –

> Overseas Filipino Workers – He said that the rights of OFWs are the administration’s foremost foreign policy concern.

> Contractualization – He said that the efforts of government resulted to the regularization of 300,000 workers. He urges Congress to pass a law prohibiting all forms of contractualization.

> Coconut Farmers – He urged Congress to pass bills creating coconut farmers’ trust fund.

On environment –

> Boracay Closure – He defended his move of closing the island as an environmental protection measure. He warned Local Governments Units (LGUs) to do their part and not to wait before the national government comes in.

> Land Use Act – He called Congress to pass a law to create a national land use act policy which will address competing land requirement for food, housing, business and environmental conservation.

> Disaster Management – He mentioned that we must learn from Typhoon Yolanda and other disasters. He called Congress to pass a law to create a disaster management department whose resources are way better than existing disaster response agencies.

> Warning to Miners – He warned irresponsible miners to stop destroying watersheds, forests and water forms.

Here is an example of a three-paragraph speech for a SONA:

Dear fellow citizens,

As we gather here today for my State of the Nation Address, I want to begin by expressing my gratitude for the opportunity to serve as your leader. Over the past year, we have faced many challenges as a nation, but we have also made great strides towards building a better and more prosperous future for all Filipinos.

Thank you, and may God bless the Philippines.

28. Which of the following is an example of a frozen speech style? a. Panatang Makabayan b. The President’s SONA c. A commencement speech d. Opening remarks


Frozen Speech Style

A commencement speech (C) is an illustration of a frozen speech style. The "frozen speech," which is the most formal method of speaking, is frequently used during ceremonies and other events that are meant to be courteous. It can also be used to convey uncertainty, boredom, or prejudice. Frozen speech is usually employed in extremely formal circumstances and doesn't require an audience reply. It is the most formal method of communication for respected contexts and It is distinguished by a stern tone, technical language, and lengthy, intricate phrases.

Typically, the frozen speech style is used in print media, rules, or declamation. The speech is well thought out and verbalized because it largely depends on the use of words. It is crucial that words are precise and carefully chosen because this style discourages listeners from providing feedback or asking for clarification. It is a method of communication that hardly ever changes. It uses lengthy sentences with excellent grammar and has a fixed, static language.
