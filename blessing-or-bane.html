12. agriculture boon or bane?​


Answer:

Agriculture can be both a boon and a bane depending on various factors such as the methods used, the scale of production, the types of crops grown, and the impact on the environment and society.

On one hand, agriculture is a boon as it provides food and raw materials for industries, generates income for farmers and farm workers, and contributes to the overall economic growth of a country. Agriculture also plays a vital role in sustaining rural communities and preserving cultural heritage.

On the other hand, agriculture can be a bane when it is practiced using unsustainable methods such as excessive use of pesticides, fertilizers, and water, leading to soil degradation, water pollution, and loss of biodiversity. The large-scale cultivation of monoculture crops can also lead to the depletion of nutrients in the soil and make it vulnerable to pests and diseases.

Furthermore, the agricultural sector can also contribute to climate change through greenhouse gas emissions from livestock production, deforestation, and transportation of goods. In addition, the unequal distribution of land ownership and resources can lead to social inequalities and injustices.

In conclusion, agriculture has both positive and negative aspects, and it is important to ensure that it is practiced in a sustainable and equitable manner to maximize its benefits while minimizing its negative impacts
