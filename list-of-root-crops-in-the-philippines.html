[tex] \large \bold{\blue{PROBLEM:}} [/tex] A sack of root crops hangs from a spring scale, which indicates that its weight is 44.1 N. What is the mass of the sack and the root crops in it?

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{SOLUTION:}} [/tex] Using the Newton's 2nd Law of Motion, determine the mass of the root crops with the given weight of 44.1 N and an acceleration due to gravity.

[tex] \boxed{F = m \cdot a} [/tex]

[tex] 44.1 N = m \cdot 9.8m/s^2 [/tex]

[tex] \frac{44.1 N}{9.8m/s^2} = \frac{m \cdot \cancel{9.8m/s^2}}{\cancel{9.8m/s^2}} \\ [/tex]

[tex] m = \frac{44.1 N}{9.8m/s^2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] m = 4.5kg [/tex]

[tex] \large \therefore \underline{\boxed{\tt \purple{The \: mass \: is \: 4.5kg}}} [/tex]
