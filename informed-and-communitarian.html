15. It is the process can be spontaneouswhen sufficient is available, notneeding control by any external agent.a. Volunteerismb. Self-OrganizationC. Communitarianismd. Social Capital​


Answer:

B

Explanation:

Self-organization, also called (in the social sciences) spontaneous order, is a process where some form of overall order arises from local interactions between parts of an initially disordered system. The process can be spontaneous when sufficient energy is available, not needing control by any external agent. It is often triggered by seemingly random fluctuations, amplified by positive feedback. The resulting organization is wholly decentralized, distributed over all the components of the system. As such, the organization is typically robust and able to survive or self-repair substantial perturbation. Chaos theory discusses self-organization in terms of islands of predictability in a sea of chaotic unpredictability.

20. similarities between feminism and Communitarianism​


Answer:

    There are many similarities between feminist and communitarian critiques of classical political theory, most notably the rejection of the liberal tradition's rational, disembodied subject. Despite the commonalities in the two critiques, the approaches used by communitarian and feminist approaches to representing the topic are very different. The endeavor to articulate the discursive subject, a component of the feminist criticism of the topic, presents the most viable counterargument to the communitarian/liberalism controversy that has divided feminist theory and political theory.
