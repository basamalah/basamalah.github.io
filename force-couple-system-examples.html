12. Two couples act on the frame. If the resultant couple moment is to be zero, determine the distance d between the 40lb couple forces. ​


[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Couple moment produced by the 60 lb couple forces:

[tex]\mathsf{M_c_1=60(\dfrac{4}{5})(4)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{M_c_1=192 \: \: lb.ft} \: \: \textsf{(cw)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Couple moment produced by the 40 lb couple forces:

[tex]\mathsf{M_c_2=40cos(30^\circ)(d)} \: \: \textsf{(ccw)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

The resultant couple moment is given by the equation:

[tex]\mathsf{M_c=M_c_1+M_c_2}[/tex]

Substituting Mc1 and Mc2, we get (cw positive):

[tex]\mathsf{M_c=-40cos(30^\circ)(d)+192}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Since the resultant couple moment is zero,

[tex]\mathsf{M_c_1=M_c_2}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{40cos(30^\circ)(d)=192}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{d=\dfrac{192}{40cos(30^\circ)}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

The distance d, is equal to:

[tex]\large{\boxed{\mathsf{d=5.543 \: \: ft}}}[/tex]

21. what is meant by the phrase "coupled human environment system"?​


Answer:

A coupled human–environment system (known also as a coupled human and natural system, or CHANS) characterizes the dynamical two-way interactions between human systems (e.g., economic, social) and natural (e.g., hydrologic, atmospheric, biological, geological) systems. This coupling expresses the idea that the evolution of humans and environmental systems may no longer be treated as individual isolated systems.
