1. The force exerted on the smaller pistons of a hydraulic lift is 160 N. The areas of the smaller piston and the larger piston are 0.5 m2 and 1.0 m2, respectively. What is the output force of the larger piston?


Given:

[tex]F_{1} = \text{160 N}[/tex]

[tex]A_{1} = 0.5 \: \text{m}^{2}[/tex]

[tex]A_{2} = 1.0 \: \text{m}^{2}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]F_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}} = \frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]F_{2} = F_{1} × \frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]F_{2} = \text{160 N} × \frac{1.0 \: \text{m}^{2}}{0.5 \: \text{m}^{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{F_{2} = \text{320 N}}[/tex]

Question:

movement of the piston power

Answer:

The engine operates on internal combustion; that is, the fuel used for its power is burned inside the engine. This burning occurs inside cylinders. Within the cylinder is a piston. When the fuel is burned, it creates an explosive force that causes the piston to move up and down. The piston is attached, via a connecting rod, to a crankshaft, where the up and down movement of the piston converts to a circular motion. When bicycling, the upper part of a person's leg is akin to the piston. Power from the leg is passed through the pedal in order to turn the crank.

16. on a piston of 10cm diameter a force of 100N is uniformly applied. Find the pressure on the piston in kPa​


Answer:

P=12.732394854 kPa

Explanation:

Give:

d=10cm

F=100N

Find:

A=?

P=? in kPa

Formula:

A=(1/4)πd²

P=F/A

Solution:Find first the Area to proceed in finding the pressure.

A=(1/4)π(10 cm)²

A=(1/4)π(100 cm²)

A=78.53982 cm²×(1m/100cm)²

Cancel cm²

A=78.53982×(1m²/10,000)

A=0.007853982 m²

Now we can proceed in finding the pressure on the piston.

P=F/A

P=100N/0.007853982 m²

P=12,732.394854 N/m²

P=12.732394854 kPa
