I believe the airport represents a "warehouse" for the Balikbayan boxes. Because of the statement "Boxes, boxes, and more boxes are wherever Vince turns." The author compares the airport's environment to a storage facility or warehouse.

Further explanation:Why do Filipinos Love balikbayan boxes?

Filipinos adore balikbayan boxes because they represent the sender's affection through the "pasalubong" or gift, which may include branded appliances, clothing, perfume, shampoos, and many other items from the United States or any other country.

What do you believe the boxes represent? Why do their contents appear to be less essential than their containers?

I believe it represents affection for family and friends. Balikbayan appears to be more significant than their contents since it signifies to the Filipinos that you will always remember them despite the great distance between them.

I believe that the author is proud of his fellow Filipinos. In this anecdote, he demonstrates that we should be proud to be Filipino citizens by elevating the positive attitude of Filipinos toward culture and family, even in tiny things like sending balikbayan boxes.

What do you believe the true meaning of the anecdote or "turban legend" is?

I believe the moral of the story is that Filipinos would always cherish their families by remembering them. Through these balikbayan boxes, it is possible to express that even though you are apart, love will always win.

Do you believe that Filipinos living or working overseas experience homesickness?

In my experience, my aunt who works overseas frequently expresses homesickness and a desire to return home. What is the story's significance?

The significance or essence of the narrative is that, in Filipino culture, family is the most important thing on earth and should always be remembered, even when separated by great distances. The expensive items in the balikbayan boxes are not just a "gift" but also a symbol of the sender's affection for their family.
