Cash accounting: Cash accounting records solely cash transactions made by employees of an organization. For example, if an employee is traveling on a business trip, they can make cash transactions on meals and lodging and incidental expenses. After they make a cash transaction, they hold onto a receipt and report all transactions made to their manager. These are logged in once they're approved. Cash transactions usually don't appear on financial statements but they can still be logged to show proof that a transaction occurred.

Accrual accounting: Accrual accounting is when a bookkeeper records all data from transactions. Thus, it's an expansion of cash accounting because it incorporates credit, debit and other forms of payment for transactions made by employees, with cash included. Accounts payable and accounts receivable also fall under this category, which can represent capital owed to or by a customer. This type of accounting gives a clearer picture of your organization's cash flow and it helps you determine if you have current assets or liabilities.

18. You are a financial Manager of Chevron Corp. You need to assess the effectiveness of working capital management of the company for 2018 using the following data. What is the 2018 Receivable turnover? 2017 Account Receivable = 15,353,000 2018 Account Receivable = 15,050,000 2017 Inventory = 5,585,000 2018 Inventory = 5,704,000 2017 Accounts Payable = 14,565,000 2018 Accounts Payable = 13,953,000 2017 Sales = 134,674,000 2018 Sales = 158,902,000 2017 Cost of Sales = 95,114,000 2018 Cost of Sales = 113,997,000 2017 Purchases = 95,114,000 2018 Purchases = 123,435,000 10.20 O 10.45 10.60​


Answer:

The formula for receivable turnover is:

Receivable turnover = Sales / Average Accounts Receivable

To calculate the average accounts receivable, we add the beginning and ending balances and divide by 2:

Average Accounts Receivable = (Beginning Accounts Receivable + Ending Accounts Receivable) / 2

Therefore, the average accounts receivable for Chevron Corp. in 2018 is:

Average Accounts Receivable = (15,353,000 + 15,050,000) / 2 = 15,201,500

And the receivable turnover for 2018 is:

Receivable turnover = 158,902,000 / 15,201,500 = 10.45

Therefore, the 2018 Receivable turnover for Chevron Corp. is 10.45.
