20. WHAT IUS ITS VELOCITY AT THE HIGHEST POINT


The velocity of an object going upward at the highest point is 0 m/s.

When you throw and object upward. The object will decelerates as the gravity acts on an object at - 9.8 m/s² deceleration. This means that every seconds the object decreases its velocity by 9.8 m/s every seconds and when the object reach the highest point it momentarily stops (0 m/s) before it falls back to the ground. And as the object moving the ground its velocity increases by 9.8 m/s every second. To simplify, the object going upward decelerates due to gravity and  momentarily stops at the highest point then accelerates going downward.

Answer:

I am not aware of any argumentative claim types with these names. The most commonly recognized types of argumentative claims are:

1. Claim of fact: a statement that asserts something is true or false.

2. Claim of definition: a statement that asserts how a term or concept should be defined or understood.

3. Claim of value: a statement that asserts something is good or bad, right or wrong, better or worse, or desirable or undesirable.

4. Claim of policy: a statement that asserts what action should be taken or what policy should be implemented.

5. Counterargument: a statement that addresses potential objections or opposing viewpoints to the main argument.

These are the generally accepted types of argumentative claims in academic and professional settings.
