13. HANS ANDERSEN FAIRY TALES The Tinderbox & The Shepherdess & The Chimney Sweep Illustrated by John Patience Audio Books lans Andersen Fairy Tales​


Answer:

Hans Christian Andersen was a Danish author and poet who is best known for his fairy tales. Some of his most famous fairy tales include "The Tinderbox," "The Shepherdess and the Chimney Sweep," and "The Little Mermaid." These stories have been widely translated and have inspired numerous adaptations, including films, plays, and ballets. "The Tinderbox" is a story about a soldier who finds a tinderbox with magical powers and uses it to win the love of a princess. "The Shepherdess and the Chimney Sweep" is a love story about a shepherdess and a chimney sweep who overcome obstacles and societal expectations to be together. "The Little Mermaid" is a tale about a mermaid who falls in love with a human prince and sacrifices her own happiness to be with him. These stories, and many others by Hans Christian Andersen, have enduring appeal and continue to be enjoyed by readers of all ages.

26. what hans christian oersted contributed


What hans christian oersted contributed

A Danish physicist and chemist who discovered that the electrical current in a wire could deflect a  magnetized compass needle, a phenomenon whose value was quickly recognized and which influenced the development of electromagnetic theory.

During the evening lecture in April 1820, Ørsted discovered that a magnetic needle perpendicularly aligns  itself with a current-carrying thread, a definite experimental proof of the relationship between electricity and magnetism.  

In 1824, he founded a society dedicated to  the propagation of scientific knowledge to the general public.  Since 1908, the company has been awarded the  Ørsted Medal for exceptional achievements by Danish physical scientists.  In the early 1930s, the term oerced was adopted for  the physical unit of magnetic field strength in the centimeter-gram-second system.
