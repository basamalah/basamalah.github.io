Explanation details:

The propaganda technique is one of the techniques used by advertisers to attract the attention of consumers. Based on the example above, we can study several types of propaganda techniques in advertising.

1. Glittering

Glittering is a propaganda technique that uses beautiful, eye-catching words to describe a product. Even sometimes the words used seem excessive.

Ad number 1 is glittering because it uses the words "you will experience unexplainable energy" to describe how good their product is.Ad number 5 is glittering because it uses the word "paradise" to describe the beauty of their tourist spots.Ad number 10 is glittering because it uses the words "a place like no other" and "special delicates experiences" to describe the beauty of their tourist spots.Ad number 4 is glittering because it uses the word "the best energy drink in the market" to describe that their product is the best product.

2. Testimonials

Testimony is a propaganda technique that uses famous people such as artists and influencers to give testimonies or solicitations to influence others.

Ads number 2 and 6 are included in testimonials because those who speak are famous artists and actors.

3. Bandwagons.

Bandwagon is a propaganda technique by taking advantage of other people's FOMO traits so that they feel that they have to do or use the same thing in order to be part of many people.

Ad number 8 belongs to the bandwagon because it uses the word "Most people like them" to say that a lot of people like the band, so you should watch it too.

4. Plain folks.

Plain folks is a propaganda technique that tries to say that these products are ordinary things that we can use and get benefit from.

Ad number 7 is plain folks because the ad is trying to say that we can avoid Covid-19 by using ordinary alcohol.

5. Name-calling.

Name-calling is a propaganda technique to favor one's own product by satirizing or dropping other products indirectly.

Ad number 4 includes name-calling because it uses the word "Unlike ordinary taxi".Ad number 9 includes name-calling because it uses the word "Unlike other apps".
