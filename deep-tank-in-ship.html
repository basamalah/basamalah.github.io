5. a tank measures 0.5 m long, 12 cm wide, and 60 mm deep. calculate the capacity of the tank in liters​


Answer:

3.6 Liters

Explanation:

The unit for volume is m³ and we have to find the capacity of the tank in liters.

So to find m³ to liters, we need to multiply each side by meters.

In order to do that, we have to convert some of the units to meters

Length = 0.5 meters

Width = 12 cm x [tex]\frac{1 m}{100 cm}[/tex] =0.12 meters

Height= 60 mm x [tex]\frac{1 m}{1000 mm}[/tex] = 0.06 meters

The capacity of the tank in cubic meters is 0.5 m x 0.12 m x 0.06

which is 0.0036 cubic meters.

But we are asked to convert it to liters so

0.0036 m³ * [tex]\frac{1 Liter}{1000 m^3}[/tex] = 3.6 liters

30. 1. Water is poured into a conical tank 6 m across the top and 8m deep at the rate of 10 m³/minHow fast is the water level rising when the water in the tank is 5m deep?​


[tex]\mathbb{FORMULA:}[/tex]

Volume of a cone:

[tex]\mathsf{V=\dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h}[/tex]

where:

V = volume

r = base radius

h = height

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Refer to the figure

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{V=\dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2h} \longleftarrow \textsf{equation 1}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

First, we need to reduce equation 1 into one variable. We need to find the relationship between the radius and the height.

Using similar triangles (aka Ratio and proportion):

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{r}{h}=\dfrac{3}{8}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{r=\dfrac{3}{8}h} \longleftarrow \textsf{equation 2}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Substitute equation 2 in equation 1

[tex]\mathsf{V=\dfrac{1}{3} \pi r^2h}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{V=\dfrac{1}{3} \pi \left(\dfrac{3}{8}h\right)^2h}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{V=\dfrac{3 \pi}{64} h^3}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Next, we need to differentiate the volume with respect to time.

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{d}{dt} \left(V=\dfrac{3 \pi}{64}h^3\right)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dV}{dt} = \dfrac{9 \pi h^2}{64}\left(\dfrac{dh}{dt}\right)}[/tex]

When dV/dt = 10 m³/min, h = 5, dh/dt = ?

[tex]\mathsf{10=\dfrac{9 \pi (5)^2}{64}\left(\dfrac{dh}{dt}\right)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dh}{dt}=\dfrac{10(64)}{9 \pi (5)^2 }}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dh}{dt}=\dfrac{128}{45\pi}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

When the water is 5 m deep, the water level is rising at a rate of:

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{\dfrac{dh}{dt}=\dfrac{128}{45\pi} \ or \ 0.9054 \ m/min}}[/tex]
