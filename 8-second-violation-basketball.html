When the violation is called, the offense loses possession of the ball and the opposing team inbounds the ball from the sidelines

Defensive three-seconds: a defensive player staying in the lane for longer than three seconds without guarding anyone

Eight-second violation: the offense fails to cross half court eight seconds after inbounding the ball

Over-and-back violation: the offense returning the ball from the frontcourt to the backcourt without the other team touching it

Leaving the court

Held ball violation: offense holds the ball without doing anything for five seconds

24. Why is the double dribble a violation in playing basketball?


Answer:

In basketball, an illegal dribble (colloquially called a double dribble or dribbling violation) occurs when a player ends their dribble by catching or causing the ball to come to rest in one or both hands and then dribbles it again with one hand or when a player touches it before the ball hits the ground.
