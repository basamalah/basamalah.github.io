Suplementary Brochures -

Often compared to booklets. Considered one of the simplest brochures to create, half-fold consists of two internal panels and a front and back cover.

Promotional Brochures -

Produced by making two bends. The size of the panels must be equal.

Leave-behind Brochures -

One of the obvious advantages is this format’s transformability. It offers more room for the designer’s imagination.

Point of Sale Brochures -

The form of this brochure looks like a zig-zag. Put simply, it includes six parts of equal size, created by double bending.

Direct mail brochures -

This type of brochure is also known as window fold. Its construction is similar to a tri-fold booklet.
