3. 1. why do we need to consider the weather and climate in establishing a horticulture crops?2. what is the importance of nursery in producing horticultural crop? 3. why is it important to select a good site in establishing a horticultural crops? ​


Answer:

1. Weather and climate play a significant role in determining the success of horticulture crops. The type of crops that can be grown, the timing of planting and harvesting, and the frequency and amount of irrigation required are all influenced by weather and climate. By considering the weather and climate, horticulturists can select crops that are well-suited to the local environment and develop management strategies that will maximize yields and minimize risks.

2. Nursery is an important component in producing horticultural crops as it provides a controlled environment for the initial growth of plants. This controlled environment allows for the manipulation of factors such as temperature, humidity, light, and nutrients, which are essential for the proper growth and development of plants. By starting plants in a nursery, horticulturists can ensure that the plants are healthy and well-established before they are transplanted to the field or garden.

3. The selection of a good site is critical in establishing horticulture crops because it can impact the quality and quantity of the harvest. Factors such as soil type, drainage, topography, exposure to sunlight and wind, and the presence of pests and diseases can all influence the growth and development of plants. By selecting a site that is well-suited to the specific requirements of the crops, horticulturists can optimize yields and minimize risks associated with poor site selection.

4. is it important to conduct a soil analysis in preparing crop establishment?if yes why?if no defend your answer.​


Answer:

A soil test is important for several reasons: to optimize crop production, to protect the environment from contamination by runoff and leaching of excess fertilizers, to aid in the diagnosis of plant culture problems, to improve the nutritional balance of the growing media and to save money and conserve energy by applying only the amount of fertilizer needed. Pre- plant media analyses provide an indication of potential nutrient deficiencies, pH imbalance or excess soluble salts. This is particularly important for growers who mix their own media. Media testing during the growing season is an important tool for managing crop nutrition and soluble salts levels. To use this tool effectively, you must know how to take a media sample to send for analysis or for in-house testing, and be able to interpret media test results.

23. what do you call the growing of two (2) or more crops simultaneously in given piece of land?a.multiple croppingb.spatial croppingc.row croppingd.system cropping​


Answer:

A.multiple cropping

Explanation:

In agriculture, multiple cropping or multicropping is the practice of growing two or more crops in the same piece of land during one growing season instead of just one crop. When multiple crops are grown simultaneously, this is also known as intercropping. This cropping system helps farmers to double their crop productivity and income.[1] But, the selection of two or more crops for practicing multicropping mainly depends on the mutual benefit of the selected crops.
