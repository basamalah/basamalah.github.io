30. Misa de Gallo then and now?​


Answer:

"Misa de Gallo" is a Spanish phrase that translates to "Rooster's Mass" in English. It refers to the Catholic tradition of attending Mass at dawn, usually at around 4:00 or 5:00 a.m., during the nine days before Christmas Eve in the Philippines.

The tradition of Misa de Gallo in the Philippines dates back to the Spanish colonial period, when the Spanish friars introduced Catholicism to the country. The practice was a way to encourage the newly converted Filipinos to attend church and celebrate the birth of Jesus Christ.

Today, Misa de Gallo remains an important part of Filipino Christmas traditions. Many Filipinos still wake up early to attend the early morning Masses, which are usually held outdoors or in temporary chapels called "simbang gabi" (night Mass) or "simbang araw" (day Mass) in different parts of the country.

However, there have been some changes to the way Misa de Gallo is celebrated in the modern era. For instance, some churches now offer Masses at different times of the day to accommodate people's schedules, and some Masses are also celebrated online due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Despite these changes, the tradition of Misa de Gallo continues to be an important part of the Christmas season for many Filipinos.
