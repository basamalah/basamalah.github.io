3. how can you help conserve some common minerals?


People can conserve mineral resources by utilizing renewable resources. For example, using hydroelectricity and solar power as sources of energy may conserve mineral resources such as coal. Mineral resources may also be conserved through recycling. A good example is recycling of scrap metal. In addition, the use of new technological methods of mining and the training of miners conserves mineral resources by ensuring minimal wastage during the mining.

13. as a student, how can you help in conserving and preserving mineral?​


Natural resources are essential to human existence, yet not all of them are replenishable. We must take action to conserve what we have in order to promote the health and longevity of both the world and humanity. We rely on natural resources for our food, water, air, and shelter. The dramatic increase in climate change is a result of human activities, particularly the exploitation of nonrenewable resources like fossil fuels. We can lessen greenhouse gas emissions and have a more beneficial effect on the environment by conserving our natural resources.

The fact that products and artifacts manufactured from minerals have assimilated into everyday life and the economy speaks volumes about their significance. Indirectly or directly, minerals are commonly employed in both industry and agriculture. Mineral conservation strategies must be developed by human civilization due to the characteristics of minerals, their scarcity, and escalating demand.

Conserve and Preserve Minerals

There are several ways to conserve minerals, such as:

Save water.
Consider switching to energy-saving equipment if at all possible, and only use your dishwasher or washing machine when there is a full load.Cut the lights off.
After leaving a room, turn off any lights or televisions. When not in use, unplug devices like toasters, coffeemakers, and portable air conditioners because they might still use very little electricity.Utilize green energy.
Renewable energy refills itself, reducing the demand for resource extraction. Our dependency on natural gas can be greatly reduced by using solar or wind power, which will also slow down the gradual depletion of resources.Recycle.
Resources must be used to create new things, but recycling makes it possible to reuse the materials we currently have. Waste is decreased by producing fewer new materials, which aids in lowering groundwater and air pollution. Find a recycling facility that will take goods like plastic bottles, cardboard, or aluminum. To reduce the need for logging and deforestation, switch to paperless billing and buy recycled paper.Invest in reusable items.
Another approach to saving resources is to stay away from single-use plastics. Choose ceramic, metal, or glassware instead of paper plates, plastic cups, or water bottles. Instead of using plastic bags, use your own cotton grocery bags. Reusing products is an excellent strategy to cut waste and prevent more trash from ending up in landfills.

Because the geological process of mineral creation is rather slow, the rate of replenishment is infinitesimally small while the rate of consumption is quite significant, mineral resources should be preserved. The earth's surface has finite and exhaustible mineral resources.
