26. 15 ways to prevent fire incidents


Answer:

1) Keep portable heaters and candles away from furniture and curtains. position safely where they cannot be knocked over.

2) Do not smoke indoors.

3) Do not leave your cooking unattended.

4) Keep matches, lighters and other ignitable substances in a secured location out of the reach of children, and only use lighters with child-resistant features.

5) Always check the kitchen before going to bed or leaving the home to make sure all stoves, ovens, and small appliances are turned off.

6) Keep all potential sources of fuel like paper, clothing, bedding or rugs at least three feet away from space heaters, stoves, or fireplaces.

7) Don't play with fire, especially indoors.

8) Do not use appliances with worn or damaged flexes.

9) Have electric blankets serviced and checked regularly.

10) If an appliance appears faulty stop using it and have it checked at once.

11) Never overload an electric socket.

12) Keep highly flammable liquids away from heat sources.

13) Check power supply regularly.

14) Keep potholders, plastic utensils, towels, or other non-cooking equipment away from the stove, because these items can catch fire.

15) Keep all fires and heaters well guarded, especially open fires.

28. In times of fire incidents, how essential is it to follow fire emergency and evacuation plan?​


Answer:

It is very important because many people simply do not know what to do when they are confronted with a dire emergency. Following fire emergency and evacuation plan can help avoid further injuries to you and people around you. It helps evacuees to efficiently and safely get people away from an area where there is an imminent threat, ongoing threat, or a hazard to their lives or property.

Explanation:

The main objective of fire emergency and evacuation planning is to reduce injuries, protect the community and maintain business continuity. An emergency plan usually includes necessary procedures during a crisis, a clear set of roles and responsibilities and established instructions for local emergency response and body recovery. Following these plans are essential for it will reduce fatality and help you safe.
