7. The following reaction is exothermic. C2H4(g)+Br2(g)⇌C2H4Br2(g) Predict the effect (shift right, shift left, or no effect) of increasing the reaction temperature. 1.) Predict the effect (shift right, shift left, or no effect) of increasing the reaction temperature. a.) shift right b.) shift left c.) no effect 2.) Predict the effect of decreasing the reaction temperature. a.) shift right b.) shift left c.) no effect


Increasing the reaction temperature.b.) shift left

Decreasing the reaction temperature.a.) shift right

Further explanation

In the equilibrium system, according to Le Chatelier, if there is a reaction there will be action

Reaction = - action

The reaction taken by the system is to shift the reaction to the right or left.

The composition of chemical reactions in equilibrium can be influenced by temperature, pressure, and concentration.

If the system temperature is raised, then the equilibrium reaction will reduce the temperature by shifting the reaction in a direction that requires heat (endotherms). Conversely, if the temperature is lowered, then the equilibrium shifts to a reaction that releases heat (exothermic)

The following reaction is exothermic.

C₂H₄ (g) + Br₂ (g) ⇌C₂H₄Br₂ (g)

Because exothermic., Then the value of ΔH is negative (ΔH = -), so the reaction to the right releases heat

1. increasing the reaction temperature.

As the temperature rises, the equilibrium will shift towards the endothermic reaction, so the reaction shifts to the left towards C₂H₄ (g) + Br₂ (g)

2. decreasing the reaction temperature.

At a decrease in temperature, the equilibrium will shift towards the exothermic reaction, so the reaction shifts right toward C₂H₄Br₂
