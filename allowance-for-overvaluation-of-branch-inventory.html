To determine the cost of the merchandise that was destroyed in the fire, we need to calculate the cost of the January 1 inventory and the shipments from the home office up to May 31.

First, we can calculate the cost of the January 1 inventory by dividing the billing price by 1 + the markup percentage:

Cost = P60,000 / (1 + 25%) = P60,000 / 1.25 = P48,000

Next, we can calculate the cost of the shipments from the home office by applying the same markup percentage to the billing price:

Cost of shipments = P240,000 / (1 + 25%) = P240,000 / 1.25 = P192,000

The total cost of the merchandise that was destroyed in the fire is the sum of the cost of the January 1 inventory and the cost of the shipments from the home office, which is P48,000 + P192,000 = P240,000.


17. An investor should buy an asset when it is undervalued and sell the asset when it is overvalued.


Answer:

Should a value investor sell an undervalued stock simply because momentum investors have started to buy?

No. Absolutely not. It is good when momentum investors start to buy. This means the stock has momentum! It is now going up!

Momentum is actually a value investors best friend. For a start, it helps you avoid value traps, stocks that have value relative to current earnings but have poor future prospects. The presence of positive momentum is the market's way of telling you this is unlikely to be the case.

Less appreciated is the fact that one of the ways value stocks make money is when their price appreciates to the point where they are no longer value, they have migrated into growth. The only way this happens is when the price goes up and this inevitably attracts momentum investors. I wrote a blog post on the academic research behind this recently.

Investing should be about making money, not choosing a side in an argument. Don't be a "value investor" or a "momentum investor". Be a successful investor.

28. “How does the overvaluing of the peso keep the exchange rate high"​


Answer:

An overvalued exchange rate means that the countries exports will be relatively expensive and imports cheaper. An overvalued exchange rate tends to depress domestic demand and encourage spending on imports. An overvalued exchange rate is particularly a problem during a period of sluggish growth.
