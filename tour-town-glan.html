8. 1. Jerry takes 1 1/2 hours to drive 30 km in the rush hour. Calculate his average speed in km/hr. 2. Mira cycles 20 miles on her bike in 2 hours. Calculate her average speed in mph. 3. A tour bus left Town A at 9:00 A.M. and arrived in Town B, 120 km away, at 11:00 A.M., find the average speed of the tour bus.​


Answer:

Jerry's average speed can be calculated by dividing the distance he travels by the time it takes him to travel that distance. In this case, he travels 30 km in 1.5 hours, so his average speed is:

Average Speed = Distance ÷ Time = 30 km ÷ 1.5 hours = 20 km/hr

Mira's average speed can be calculated by dividing the distance she cycles by the time it takes her to cycle that distance. In this case, she cycles 20 miles in 2 hours, so her average speed is:

Average Speed = Distance ÷ Time = 20 miles ÷ 2 hours = 10 mph

The average speed of the tour bus can be calculated by dividing the distance it travels by the time it takes to travel that distance. In this case, the tour bus travels 120 km in 2 hours (from 9:00 A.M. to 11:00 A.M.), so its average speed is:

Average Speed = Distance ÷ Time = 120 km ÷ 2 hours = 60 km/hr
