A secondary information resource that will provide a general understanding of technical vocational livelihood and practical education​?

The CP-TLE was increased in the 2010 Secondary Education Curriculum to incorporate more special curricular activities. This brings the total number of programs to six: Special Program in the Arts (SPA), Special Program in Sports (SPS), STEM Program (formerly known as ESEP), Special Program in Journalism (SPJ), Technical-Vocational-Livelihood Education (TVE), and Special Program in Foreign Language (SPFL) (SPFL).

The Technical-Vocational Education-based TLE focuses on the development of technical skills in any field. The exploratory phase (Grades 7 and 8) covers five common competencies based on the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority's (TESDA) training regulations: measurement and calculation, technical drafting, use of tools and equipment, tool and equipment maintenance, and occupational health and safety. Home Economics, Agri-Fishery Arts, Industrial Arts, and Information and Communication Technology are the component areas of Technology and Livelihood Education (TLE), which is one of the study areas of the Secondary Education Curriculum utilized in Philippine secondary schools .

TLE is also known as CP-TLE, which stands for Career Pathways in Technology and Livelihood Education. In the 2010 Secondary Education Curriculum, CP-TLE is allotted 240 minutes each week, or 1.2 units. CP-TLE, on the other hand, is obliged to include community-based job experience, which may extend beyond the school day.

30. What is formal education, elementary education, secondary education, tertiary education, vocational education, non formal education and special education?


Hey dear

So your answer is

•Formal education - It is a kind of education normally delivered by trained teachers in a systematic intentional way within a school, higher education and University.

•Elementary education - it is typically the first stage of formal education coming after preschool and before •Secondary Education.

Secondary education - it is the final and second basic phase of the education which comes before tertiary education.

•Tertiary education - it is commonly referred to as post Secondary Education referred to academic persuits undertaken after High School.

•Vocational education - it is a kind of education that prepares people to work in various jobs, such as trade ,craft or as technician.

•Non formal education - non formal education is about acknowledging the importance of education, learning and training which takes place outside a recognised institution.

•Special education - it is a practice of educating students in a way that addresses their individual differences and needs.
