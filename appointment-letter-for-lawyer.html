18. Mr. Francois Renault, a French resident and a citizen is interested in putting up a Travel Agency in the Philippines, specializing in European outbound tour packages. If you are the lawyer appointed by Mr. Renault to assist him in putting up this kind of business, what advice will you give him?


Answer:

As a lawyer, I will advise Mr. Renault to get an Operating Permit from the Department of Tourism (DOT) for his travel agency business. An operating permit is the basic requirement for an establishment to engage in any business activity.

A foreign national may only operate a business in the Philippines under an operating permit issued by the DOT. The operating permit is issued after application and review by the concerned government agency and grant or denial of issuance thereof by the concerned government agency.

The law provides that: "No person shall engage in any business except through an agent duly registered with the Commission." This means that unless you have an agent or representative who is duly registered with DOT, you cannot open up your own travel agency business here in the Philippines.

The Philippine Tour Operators Association has recommended that all travel agencies should register with DOT so as to avail of its services and benefits, including but not limited to: 1) recognition as authorized tour operators; 2) registration with other regulatory bodies like PTOA and ACTOEI; 3) exemption from paying taxes on gross income; 4) exemption from paying fees for issuing permits; 5) exemption from paying fees for renewing permits; 6) renewal of annual registration
