10. comparison and contrast abt this youth yesterday today and tomorrow 2comparison and 2contrast ​


Answer:

Youth Yesterday:

In the past, youth was often associated with innocence and carefree living. The youth of yesterday was more focused on living in the present moment and enjoying their youth. They were less exposed to technology and social media, so they spent more time outdoors, playing sports, and engaging in social activities with their peers. Young people of yesterday had a more traditional outlook on life and tended to follow the path set by their parents or society.

Youth Today:

The youth of today are more tech-savvy and connected than ever before. They have access to a vast range of information and opportunities through the internet and social media. With the advent of smartphones, social media, and instant messaging, young people today are more connected than ever before. They are more likely to have a diverse set of interests and a more individualistic outlook on life. Youth today are more likely to question authority and challenge traditional norms.

Comparison:

Both youth yesterday and today have a strong desire for social interaction and forming connections with their peers.The youth of yesterday and today have a desire to explore and learn new things.

Contrast:

The youth of today are more tech-savvy and have access to more information and opportunities than youth yesterday.Youth today have a more individualistic outlook on life and are more likely to question authority and challenge traditional norms than youth yesterday.

Answer:

(LIGHT REACTION) 1. Definition. The series of biochemical reactions in photosynthesis that require light energy that is captured by light-absorbing pigments (such as chlorophyll) to be converted into chemical energy in the form of ATP and NADPH

(DARK REACTION) 1. n the dark reaction there is the fixation of carbon-dioxide. During dark reaction, energy released from ATP, NADPH (products of the light reaction) drives the fixation of carbon from carbon-dioxide in the carbon compound (carbohydrates).

(LIGHT REACTION) 2. The importance of pigment in photosynthesis is that it helps absorb the energy from light. The free electrons at the molecular level in the chemical structure of these photosynthetic pigments revolve at certain energy levels.

(DARK REACTION) 2. The main difference between light and dark reaction is that light reaction is the first stage of photosynthesis, which traps light energy in order to produce ATP and NADPH whereas dark reaction is the second stage of photosynthesis, which produces glucose by using the energy form ATP and NADPH produced from the light.

(LIGHT REACTION) 3. The light reaction takes place in the thylakoid discs. There, water (H20) is oxidized, and oxygen (O2) is released. The electrons freed up from water are transfered to ATP and NADPH. ... Note that the dark reaction takes place in the stroma (aqueous fluid surrounding the stacks of thylakoids) and the cytoplasm.

(DARK REACTION) 3. The dark reactions occur in the part of the chloroplast known as the stroma. The purpose of the dark reactions is to take the energy from ATP and energized electrons and hydrogen ions from NADPH and add them to CO2 to make glucose or sugar.

(LIGHT REACTION) 4. The light-dependent reactions use light energy to make two molecules needed for the next stage of photosynthesis: the energy storage molecule ATP and the reduced electron carrier NADPH.

(DARK REACTION) 4. The dark reaction functions if ATP, NADPH and carbon dioxide are present. At the completion of this stage ADP, Phosphorous, and NADP+ are recycled for use in the light reaction.

(LIGHT REACTION) 5. The light reaction takes place in the thylakoid discs. There, water (H20) is oxidized, and oxygen (O2) is released. The electrons freed up from water are transfered to ATP and NADPH.

(DARK REACTION) 5. The dark reaction occurs outside of the thylakoids.

(LIGHT REACTION) 6. An enzyme in photosystem II splits water into hydrogen and oxygen. The energized electron is then passed along through another enzyme called the cytochrome b6-f complex, which in turn pumps positively charged hydrogen molecules across a membrane to create a store of potential energy.

(DARK REACTION) 6. Dark reactions is the biosynthetic phase which is independent of light but depend upon the assimilative powers that is ATP and NADPH2 synthesized during the light reaction. ... So, Dark reaction is not entirely enzymes reaction.
