3. Why is BAHALA NA attitude a negative or unacceptable cultural value​


Answer:

It implies fatalism under the pretext of trusting in Divine providence. By saying “bahala na”, a person resigns himself to luck as he believes the end-result depends ultimately on fate. It is a Filipino's way of avoiding rationalization or good reason.

18. Some people think of the word "Bahala Na" is an unreasonable man's attitude. Explain why is it so called unreasonable attitude? 5 sentences po thank you​


Answer:

The phrase "Bahala na" implies going with the flow in an undesirable situation. Usually, it is said to leave one's future happenings to someone or something else, not bothering to take action to maneuver the circumstances. It is unreasonable for it prompts people to not take control of the situation, and just leave it as it is. One should at least try to take control of their life, even if the undesired is inevitable. You never know if your future can be changed for the better if you don't try.
