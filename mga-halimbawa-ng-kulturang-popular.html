Highbrows are frequently wealthy and occasionally labeled as snobbish or hoity-toity.

Lowbrow, which denotes vulgar and less educated culture and individuals, is the anthisesis of highbrow. a book written by a professor would likely be considered highbrow. as opposed to a book written by a reality TV celebrity.

Explanation:

Who coined the term highbrow?

The New York newspaper The Sun reporter Will Irvin, who "adhered to the phrenological assumption of more educated people having high foreheads," coined the word "highbrow" in 1902. It should have sufficed to mention eugenics and racism in this sentence with just a faint scent.

The words "highbrow" and "lowbrow" originate from phrenology, a nineteenth-century branch of psychology that regarded a person's skull shape as a predictor of intelligence. A "high" forehead denoted intelligence, whereas a "low" one did not. However, prominent instances of bigotry and prejudice were caused by this so-called "science."
