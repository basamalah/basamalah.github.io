1. To look for examples. Read their definitions and look for a sample.• dictionary • atlas • newspaper • manual• thesaurus • almanac • encyclopedia


Answer:

- Dictionary: A reference book that contains words listed in alphabetical order, along with their meanings, pronunciations, and often usage examples. Sample sentence: "I used the dictionary to look up the definition of a new word I came across in my book."

- Atlas: A collection of maps, typically showing geographic features, political boundaries, and other geographical information. Sample sentence: "I used the atlas to locate the capital cities of different countries around the world."

- Newspaper: A printed or online publication that provides news, current events, and other information on a wide range of topics. Sample sentence: "I read the newspaper every morning to stay updated on the latest news and events."

- Manual: A book or document that provides instructions or guidance on how to do something or operate a device. Sample sentence: "I referred to the manual to assemble the new furniture I bought."

- Thesaurus: A reference book that provides synonyms and antonyms for words, often used to find alternative words or expand vocabulary. Sample sentence: "I used the thesaurus to find a more precise word to use in my writing."

- Almanac: A yearly publication that contains information on various topics, such as weather forecasts, astronomical data, and statistical data. Sample sentence: "I consulted the almanac to find out the sunrise and sunset times for my area."

- Encyclopedia: A comprehensive reference work that provides information on a wide range of subjects, typically arranged alphabetically or thematically. Sample sentence: "I used the encyclopedia to research a historical event for my school project."
