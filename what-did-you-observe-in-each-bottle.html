Explanation:

A.

1. In each bottle, we observed the production of gas, which caused the balloon on top of the bottle to inflate.

2. The gas produced in the bottles is carbon dioxide, which is a byproduct of the reaction between baking soda and vinegar. The reaction produces carbonic acid, which quickly breaks down into water and carbon dioxide. As the gas is produced, it fills the bottle and pushes the air out of the balloon, causing it to inflate.

3. Hot water was added to one of the bottles to demonstrate that the reaction between baking soda and vinegar is temperature-dependent. When the temperature is increased, the reaction occurs more quickly, producing more gas and causing the balloon to inflate faster.

B.

1. Yes

2.  Soft drinks also contain carbon dioxide gas, which is produced through a similar reaction to the one between baking soda and vinegar. The gas is added to the drink under pressure, which keeps it dissolved in the liquid. When the bottle or can is opened, the pressure is released, and the gas comes out of the solution.

Pressure plays a crucial role in the set-up because it is the force that pushes the gas out of the bottle and into the balloon. As the gas is produced, it creates a higher pressure inside the bottle than outside, which forces the gas to move from an area of high pressure to an area of low pressure. The balloon on top of the bottle allows the gas to escape while keeping the air out, creating a closed system where the pressure can build up until it is sufficient to inflate the balloon.

Explanation:

A mixture is a combination of two or more substances that are combined physically. Components of the mixture are not in indefinite proportions. Also, the components of the mixtures retain their identities. A mixture can either be a homogeneous and a heterogeneous mixture. A homogeneous mixture or solution is a kind of mixture wherein the components are distributed uniformly thus we see only a single phase. Specifically, in a homogeneous mixture, the solute is completely dissolved in the solvent. Examples of homogeneous mixtures are salt solution, juice, or brass.

In a heterogeneous mixture, on the other hand, the components are not distributed uniformly in the mixture. Components are easily distinguishable. They are made up of two or more phases. Examples of heterogeneous mixtures are soil, vegetable soup, mixed nuts, and smog.

19. 1. What happened to each of the bottles when you flicked them? 2. Which bottle did not topple as you quickly pulled the tablecloth? What do you think accounts for this observation? 3. What is the relationship of the mass of the bottle to its inertia?4. How was inertia demonstrated in this activity?​


Answer:

1. When I flicked Bottle A, it did not move. When I flicked Bottle B, it flicked a little bit. When I flicked Bottle C, it toppled on the table.

2. Bottle A did not topple as the tablecloth is pulled quickly. This is because Bottle A is the heaviest among three bottles. Bottle B and Bottle C are half-filled and empty, respectively which they have less inertia than Bottle A. In addition, Bottle A did not topple because of Newton's first law of motion – the Law of Inertia. Law of Inertia explains that the body or object will stay at rest unless acted upon by an external net force – which means the force exerted on the tablecloth did not affect Bottle A so it remains on its place.

3. Inertia is directly proportional to the mass of the body. The more massive the body is, the greater its inertia. For example, between a bicycle and a truck, the truck is more massive so it has greater inertia, which means it is harder to move when it is at rest, and harder to stop when it is moving in uniform path.

4. In the activity, it shows that when you flicked three bottles, it is harder to move or topple Bottle A because it is more massive and has greater inertia.
