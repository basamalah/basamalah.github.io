2. What is the proper remedy of the hotel management if it later on discovered that the personal information of the current hotel guests were hacked?


Answer:

If a hotel discovers that the personal information of its current guests has been hacked, it should take immediate action to protect the affected individuals and prevent further data breaches. Some specific steps the hotel management might take include:

1. Notifying affected individuals: The hotel should inform all affected individuals as soon as possible, so they can take steps to protect themselves from potential identity theft or fraud.

2.Conducting an investigation: The hotel should launch an internal investigation to determine the extent of the data breach and identify any vulnerabilities that may have contributed to it.

3. Implementing additional security measures: The hotel should take immediate steps to secure its systems and prevent future data breaches. This may include updating software, installing firewalls, and implementing additional security protocols.

4. Reporting the data breach to authorities: Depending on the jurisdiction in which the hotel operates, it may be required to report the data breach to law enforcement or regulatory agencies.

5. Offering compensation to affected individuals: In some cases, it may be appropriate for the hotel to offer compensation to affected individuals to help them recover from any financial losses or other damages resulting from the data breach.

Overall, the hotel should take swift and decisive action to address the data breach and minimize the impact on affected individuals.

20. "ESSAY" As an executive housekeeper/ manager of the hotel, how will you maintain the cleanliness of the hotel in this time of pandemic?


Answer:

Wash all parts of your hands frequently (at least 20 seconds if using an alcohol-based hand rub, and at least 40 seconds with soap and water), including after exchanging objects such as money or credit cards with guests.

Cover a cough or sneeze with a bent elbow or tissue and throw away the tissue in a closed bin.

Maintain at least a 1 metre distance from other staff and guests. This includes avoid hugging, kissing, or shaking hands. If you can’t guarantee the distance, wear a fabric mask. Be sure to check local and national guidelines on the use of masks.

Staying behind plexiglass boards at reception and concierge desks can prevent droplet transmission.

Teleworking may be a possibility for some jobs, which can help reduce physical contact with others.

30. mga terminolohiya ng Hotel and restaurant management? ​


Answer:

Here are some terms that are commonly used in the field of hotel and restaurant management:

Front office: refers to the area of a hotel or restaurant where guests check in, check out, and make reservations.

Back office: refers to the administrative and support functions of a hotel or restaurant, such as accounting, payroll, and human resources.

Housekeeping: refers to the department responsible for cleaning and maintaining the guest rooms and public areas of a hotel or restaurant.

Food and beverage: refers to the department responsible for preparing and serving food and drinks in a hotel or restaurant.

Menu: a list of food and drink items available for purchase at a hotel or restaurant.

Reservation: a request for a specific date and time to visit a hotel or restaurant.

Guest: a person who stays in a hotel or visits a restaurant.

Server: an employee who takes orders and serves food and drinks to guests in a restaurant.

Host/hostess: an employee who greets guests and manages reservations at a restaurant.

Manager: an employee who is responsible for overseeing the operations of a hotel or restaurant.
