1. why are the guest prioritizing and security in a hotel?​


Answer:

The guest, who comes to a particular hotel, comes with an understanding that he and his  belongings both will be safe and secure during his stay at the hotel. At the same time it is also quite important that the hotel staff and assets are protected and secure. Hence it is very important to have a proper security system in place to protect staff, guests and physical resources and assets such as equipment, appliances buildings, gardens of the hotel and also the belongings of the guest.

The management must take care that the security and safety systems cover the following areas:

1)Guest: Protection from crimes such as murder, abduction and health hazards from outsiders, hotel staff, pests, food poisoning etc.

2)Staff: Providing staff lockers, insurances, health schemes, provident funds etc. Protective clothing, shoes, fire fighting drills, supply of clean drinking water use of aqua guards, sanitized wash rooms etc.

3)Guest luggage: Secure luggage store rooms and proper equipment such as luggage trolley and bell hop trolley should be provided.

4)Hotel Equipment: Lifts, Boilers, Kitchen equipment, furniture fitting and building etc. must be protected and for these the security and safety should cover up fire safety equipment, bomb threat security system, water floods security system, earthquake security system , safe vault security system etc.

5)Protection of raw materials, goods, provisions and groceries etc. for this the security system should cover proper storage and pest control systems, apart from the application of total material management system.

2. Without proper hotel security, guests and staff members may be at risk of theft, assault, or other dangerous situations. In addition, the hotel itself may be at risk of damage or loss of property.

3. A lack of security can also lead to loss of trust and credibility among guests and clients, as well as potential legal and financial consequences for the hotel.

4. Electronic locking systems can provide added security and convenience for hotel guests. These systems typically use keycards or electronic codes to control access to guestrooms, which can help prevent unauthorized entry and make guests feel more secure.

5. Ensuring that everyone in a hotel or company plays a role in security can involve a combination of training, communication, and accountability. This can include providing regular security training for staff members, creating clear policies and procedures for security, and holding individuals accountable for following these rules and procedures. It can also involve regularly reviewing and updating security measures to ensure that they are effective and up-to-date.

24. give at least four benefits or advantages of securing hotel accreditation? explain your answer​


A significant differentiator: hotel security is now a key priority for corporates, travel managers and the public. 64 per cent of security professionals believe that the risks facing business travellers have increased (IPSOS MORI) and 89 per cent of hotel guests rank security as their primary concern when travelling (Travelzoo). By demonstrating accreditation to a global standard, hotels can reassure travellers of their commitment to a high standard of security and duty of care

Corporate ressurance: an increasing number of corporations are demanding reassurance about security from hotels before allowing business travellers to stay at their properties. A Global Secure accreditation enables hotels to demonstrate to corporates their commitment to the highest level of duty of care. Corporates can have confidence in their accommodation choice without the need to complete additional forms or have the inconvenience of multiple site security visits for every company.

Futureproof: the ISO 31030 standard for travel risk management is expected to be implemented in 2020. This may require corporate travel managers to seek independent or evidence-based verification of hotel security standards. The GSA standards are likely to be closely aligned with the new ISO standard as members of the management team at Global Secure have been involved in the evolution of the guidance. This offers hotel reassurance that they will be ready for ISO 31030 and can demonstrate compliance quickly and simply.

Trusted: accreditation by Global Secure is regulated by SFJ Awards. SFJ Awards is approved by the Qualification Regulators to provide qualifications in England, Wales and Northern Ireland and works with the British Police Service and the UK Military and Justice Organisations to provide external quality assurance for standards. SFJ regulates the GSA standards and assessment process.
