2. explain the scout oath and scout law the mission and vision out scouting​


QUESTION:

explain the scout oath and scout law the mission and vision out scouting

ANSWER:

Mission

"The Mission of Scouting is to contribute to the education of young people, through a value system based on the Scout Promise and Law, to help build a better world where people are self-fulfilled as individuals and play a constructive role in society." The Mission was adopted at the 35th World Scout Conference in Durban, South Africa in 1999. Illustrating both the local and global impact of Scouting, the Mission of Scouting has been captured in World Scouting’s brand as "Creating a Better World”.

Vision

"By 2023 Scouting will be the world’s leading educational youth movement, enabling 100 million young people to be active citizens creating positive change in their communities and in the world based on shared values." The Vision for Scouting, Vision 2023, was adopted at the 40th World Scout Conference in Ljubljana, Slovenia in 2014.

17. English Question: In what ways have you helped young scouts in the field of scout ideas and character development ?Tagalog na Tanong: Sa anong mga paraan ka nakatulong sa mga batang Scout sa larangan ng mga Ideya ng Scout at Pagbuo ng Karakter​ ?


Answer:

I haven't personally interacted with young scouts, but I can suggest some ways in which one can help young scouts in the field of scout ideas and character development:

Encourage goal-setting: Scouts are taught to set goals and strive to achieve them. As a mentor, you can help them set realistic goals and encourage them to work hard to achieve them.

Foster teamwork and leadership: Scouts often work in groups and learn to lead and follow. You can encourage them to work together and develop leadership skills.

Teach practical skills: Scouts are taught practical skills such as cooking, camping, first aid, and navigation. You can help them learn and practice these skills.

Emphasize character development: Scouts learn to be trustworthy, loyal, helpful, friendly, courteous, kind, obedient, cheerful, thrifty, brave, clean, and reverent. You can help them understand the importance of these character traits and encourage them to practice them in their daily lives.

Be a role model: As a mentor, you can lead by example and demonstrate the qualities and skills that you want the scouts to develop.

25. In school, there are 159 scouts. If 1/4 of them are cub scouts, how mnay are not cub scouts?


Answer: 119.

In order to find out the number of those who are NOT cub scouts, let us first find the number of the cub scouts.

1/4 • 159 = 39.75 or 40 cub scouts

Then, subtract 40 from the number of the scouts:

159 - 40 = 119 remaining scouts

Therefore, there are 119 people who are not cub scouts.
