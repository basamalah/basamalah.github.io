1. you are tasked to cook vegetable recipe but you forgot to buy the complimentary sauce for the recipe,would you still cook the recipe?​


Answer:

yes of course ,

I will substitute any other ingredients (If needed)if they are not available on my kitchen or If I forgot to buy it.Although you may hesitate to change a recipe for fear of ruining it, remember that recipes are simply instructions for combining and processing a group of ingredients.Not only can substitution save the day when you lack an ingredient called for in a recipe, but it also enables you to make a recipe better for you. That’s helpful whether you are trying to improve your overall eating pattern or are cooking for a specific health condition.

Answer:

Definitely YES, I certainly would still cook the vegetable recipe. Since vegetables come in such a wide array of flavourful varieties, it is much easier to find taste matches than it is for meats (since there are only a few types of meats in comparison to types of vegetables).

Some simple herbs and spices can easily make your vegetable recipe come to life. Lemongrass is delicious and versatile, it also smells awesome! Italian seasonings are pretty great too (rosemary, thyme, oregano, basil, garlic salt, pepper, parsley, sage) - you can literally purchase a pre-mixed "Italian Seasoning Blend" from the supermarket/grocery store. Mint is another lovely herb, although it can be a bit more difficult to pair with the right vegetable dishes sometimes. Cumin and cilantro are often used in spicy and in Mexican recipes, but if you're like me (who loves cilantro more than any human being should! lol), it is also a very versatile herb that can be utilized in so many endless ways because it is hard to find a "bad" match for cilantro. Cumin is a very similar spice. You do not need much, as it can add to the saltiness of a recipe. Paprika is another tasty and colourful spice that is quite versatile. I often use it on many vegetables, and sometimes on poultry too.

There are many small staples that can also act as "add-on" vegetables are another great way to cook with flavours. Some of these "add-on" vegetables include: garlic cloves, shallots, green onions/scallions, spanish onions, yellow onions, pabalano,  tomatoes, fennel, hot peppers (such as jalapenos, chilli peppers, ghost peppers - depending on who is eating the recipe and if they enjoy spicy food of course), various kinds of nuts (pine nuts, walnuts, pecans, peanuts, brazil nuts, almonds, etc. Nuts are even tastier if they are candied!).

Even some basic fruits can change a vegetable recipe for the better. Coconut and coconut milk are both excellent ingredients, especially with curries. Lemons and limes (most often their juices) are another wonderful addition to a lot of different vegetable dishes, especially curries and spicy dishes. Oranges (mandarines, tangerines, clementines, etc.) are another great fruit which can be used both as whole slices or for its juices.  Pears (green pears, red pears, asian pears) are yummy additions to certain vegetable recipes, as are apples (red, green, yellow, sweet, sour, so many kinds!).

Ginger is another great root vegetable with such a variety of uses, everything from homemade tea to an addition to a rice or vegetable dish. Brown sugar, agave syrup/nectar, honey, soy sauce - these are also all amazing options for flavouring your vegetable recipe. For example, I make this sweet glazed baby carrot recipe that's been passed down for generations in my family. It involves the use of brown sugar and honey!

Various types of vinegars (apple cider, white wine, red wine, balsamic vinegar) mixed in with some olive oil can add a HUGE boost in flavour to vegetables. You can also make homemade dressing/vinagerette for salads. I usually use apple cider vinegar, some olive oil, salt and pepper, and a couple of different spices I have on hand (differs, depending on what kind of meal we're having and its ingredients).

Noodles and rice. When in doubt, add noodles or rice. It makes for a more filling and satisfying vegetable dish, still meets the vegetable recipe requirements, and you won't be complaining of hunger in an hour or two!

6. why is it important to read a recipe first the recipe before cooking? (10 sentences)​


Answer:

Reading a recipe before cooking is an essential step that should not be skipped. Here are ten reasons why it is important:

Ensures you have all the necessary ingredients: By reading the recipe beforehand, you can make sure you have all the ingredients you need. This saves time and prevents interruptions during cooking.

Helps with meal planning: If you plan your meals ahead of time, reading a recipe will help you decide what to cook and what ingredients you need to buy.

Prevents mistakes: Reading a recipe first can prevent mistakes such as missing steps or adding ingredients in the wrong order.

Saves time: Reading a recipe beforehand allows you to plan out the cooking process and streamline the preparation. You can set up ingredients and equipment ahead of time, saving you valuable time.

Provides insight into the recipe: By reading the recipe, you can gain a better understanding of the cooking process, which can help you adjust and improve the recipe to your liking.

Helps with portion control: By reading the recipe, you can determine the serving sizes and adjust the recipe accordingly.

Ensures proper cooking technique: Reading the recipe can provide valuable information on proper cooking techniques, including cooking times and temperatures.

Helps with dietary restrictions: If you have specific dietary restrictions, reading the recipe can help you determine if the recipe is suitable for you.

Allows for modifications: By reading the recipe, you can determine where you can make modifications, such as substituting ingredients or adjusting cooking times.

Promotes overall success: Reading a recipe before cooking promotes overall success in the kitchen. By following the recipe closely, you can ensure the dish turns out as intended, and you can feel confident in your cooking abilities.

Answer:

To make a pamapa/piypis itum—the burned coconut meat is pounded into a powder together with chilies, ginger, garlic, shallots, lemongrass, turmeric and galangal. And there you have your mirepoix for special Tausug dishes.

answer;

ingredients;

• 1 kg beef brisket, cubed

• 1/2 cup grated coconut meat

• 4 stalks lemongrass, white section only pounded

• 3 birds eye chillies, finely chopped

• 4 cups beef stock

• 1 thumb sized ginger, thinly sliced

• 1/2 thumb sized turmeric, thinly sliced

• 6 cloves garlic, minced

• 3 pcs shallots, thinly sliced

salt

• freshly ground black pepper

• oil

how to cook;

1. Place coconut meat on an aluminium lined baking tray. Place in the oven on the highest grill setting, cook until brunt. It should be black burnt, let it cool then place in a food processor to make into powder.

2. Season beef with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

3. In a pot heat oil then brown beef pieces on all sides, in batches. Remove beef pieces then set it aside.

4. In the same pot add oil if needed, then sauté garlic, shallots, ginger, turmeric, chillies and burnt coconut powder.

5. Add the beef back together with the lemongrass then pour the beef stock. Bring to a boil then simmer for 1 1/2 hours in low heat.

6. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper then serve.

19. Write the steps in preparing or cooking the egg recipe​


STEP 1: Beat your eggs until they're completely blended. Add a little water, cream or milk to make them tender. Use 1 tablespoon of liquid per egg. Add a pinch of salt.

STEP 2: Next, heat a nonstick skillet over a medium-low flame and toss in a pat of butter. Make sure the butter coats the pan.

STEP 3:Then, pour in the eggs. Pause to let them heat slightly — gentle heat is essential.

STEP 4: Move the eggs across the pan like a bulldozer so the eggs cook evenly. This takes a little time, but it's worth it.

STEP 5: As the eggs start to set, add chopped fresh herbs, or bits of ham or cheese.

STEP 6: Turn your eggs onto warmed plates and dig in! Watch our how-to video for more.

Why is a Recipe important?

Recipes provide consistency in the production of menu items. Recipes provide food cost control. Recipes provide knowledge for front of the house staff as a sales tool and to help consumers with dietary concerns and allergies

Why is it important to read a recipe first the recipe before cooking?

It is important to read the whole recipe before you begin cooking. This helps you know how the dish is made. All recipes start with the name of the dish. Next will come the ingredients needed.

What are the purpose of a recipe?

The purpose of A recipe is an instructional text used when cooking or baking food. It tells the person cooking the food, what ingredients they should use, how to use them and any nutritional facts that may be relevant.
