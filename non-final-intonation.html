1. non final rising intonation


Non-final intonation is a pattern within a sentence that includes rising intonation followed by falling intonation in the same sentence. We use non-final intonation for unfinished thoughts, introductory words and phrases, with a series of words, and when expressing choices.

14. give 3 example of non-final intonation ​


Answer:

3 example of non-final intonation:

1. Rising intonation: This is when the pitch of the voice rises towards the end of a sentence. For example, "Are you coming to the party tonight?"

2. Falling-rising intonation: This is when the pitch of the voice falls and then rises towards the end of a sentence. For example, "I'm not sure if I can make it to the meeting tomorrow?"

3. Flat intonation: This is when the pitch of the voice remains relatively constant throughout a sentence. For example, "The weather is supposed to be nice tomorrow."
