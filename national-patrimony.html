5. Explain how this ff words are used in the true decalogue: a) Posterity b) patrimony c) republic d) Monarchy e)annihilate f) frontiers g) cultivate h) latter i) former j)conscience k) strive l) dynasty m) aspiration n) exaltation o) prevails p)subsisted q) solidarity r) decalogue


The True Decalogue inspired by Apolinario Mabini seeks to issue a rallying call to all citizens of the Philippines. Through the Decalogue the revolution leader, lawyer, educator and statesman sought to strengthen the social, religious and economic welfare of the citizens of the Philippines and as such the following words are used to reflect and issue a rallying call to all;

-Posterity; ‘a country is great because of its freedom, and is made prosperous and brilliant by dint of work’. Apolinario Mabini explains that for a country to prosper each and every individual needs to work hard and strive to make the country a better place.

-Patrimony; ‘your country is the only paradise that God has given you in this life; the only Patrimony of your race’. Apolinario describes our country as the only property (Patrimony) that we have inherited from our Ancestors and given by God therefore we have cherish and protect it.

-Republic; ‘Strive that your country be constituted as a republic’. In order to be governed by the constitution and enjoy equality of rights by all. The country needs to be constituted as a republic and not just be a kingdom governed by the elites or a monarchy.

-Monarchy; ‘Strive that your country be constituted as a republic and never as a monarchy’. A monarchy state empowers only individuals or certain families leading to the formation of dynasties that who only take care of their interests.

-Annihilate; ‘if your neighbour is remiss in this sacred duty and makes an attempt on your life, your freedom and your priorities, then you should destroy (annihilate) him’. Citizens who threaten the freedom or rights of a country ought to be disassociated with.

-Frontiers; ‘as long as national frontiers subsist, raised and maintained...’ a rallying call to all citizens to take upon themselves (become frontier’s) and ensure the prosperity and independence of their country.

-Cultivate; ‘Thou shalt cultivate the special gifts which God has granted thee’. Citizens of a country ought to realize and nurture their talents and abilities and use them for the empowerment of the country.

-Latter; ‘Thou shalt strive for a Republic and never for a monarchy in thy country; for the latter exalts one or several families’ The Monarchy or ruling elite families look out for their own interests.

-Former; ‘the former makes a people noble and worthy...’ a constitutional republic is for the people, and by the people, and ensures the respect of rights for all individuals.

-conscience; ‘Thou shalt worship God in the form which they conscience’. Citizens of a country have the right to worship their God in a manner and form which they deem fit.

-Strive; ‘Thou shalt strive for the happiness of thy country’. We ought to ensure that our country is full of peace.

-Dynasty; ‘for the latter exalts one or several families and forms a dynasty’ a monarchy state or a country ruled by the elites is ruled and its future preserved only for the wealthy and mighty.

-Aspiration; ‘by the same joys and sorrows and by common aspirations and beliefs’ citizens of a country ought to help each other as they share the common goals and interests of making their nation great.

-Exaltation; ‘for only thou canst have any real interest in her advancement and exaltation’. We will only pride in ourselves if we are only ruled by a government that is duly elected by its citizens and one which respects the rights of every individual.

-Prevails; ‘for the supreme law of self-preservation prevails’. There is the need for the protection of oneself from death or harm.

-Subsist; ‘Therefore as national frontiers subsist, raised and maintained....’ we should remain in force or support ourselves when pushing for national agendas.

-Solidarity; ‘Thou shalt thou unite in a perfect solidarity of purpose and interest’. We should remain united and focused on our goals of achieving prosperity of our nation.

-Decalogue; The ten instructions or commandments as per an individual.

Cultural heritage conservation involves protection and restoration using "any procedures that prove efficient in keeping that property in as close to its original condition as possible for as long as possible." Cultural heritage conservation is most of the time associated with art collections and museums and includes care of collection and management through tracking, examination, documentation, exhibition, storage, preventative conservation, and restoration.

The scope has greatly widened from conservation of art, involving care and protection of artwork and architecture, to cultural heritage conservation, also including maintaining and care of a broad set of other cultural and historical artifacts. Conservation of cultural heritage can be defined as a type of ethical stewardship.

Conservation of cultural heritage involves simple ethical guidelines:

Minimal interventionAppropriate materials and reversible methodsFull documentation of all work undertaken

Speech Styles

According to Martin Joos (1976; 156) speech style refers to the speaker's use of language, which is defined by the degree of formality.

Types of Communicative Styles Intimate: This is a private style that is used by intimate family members or individuals. For instance, a talk between a husband and wife, a boyfriend and a girlfriend, a conversation between close siblings, a parent and a child, or a conversation between close friends.  Casual: Friends, peers, colleagues, or family members who have a shared knowledge or interest use it in casual or relaxed interactions. Conversations among friends, classmates, colleagues, peers, and family members, for example. Formal: A type of oral presentation or paper that is meant to impart information and follows established professional regulations, standards, and processes while avoiding the use of slang terms. Sermons, rhetorical comments and inquiries, speeches, judicial declarations, and announcements are only a few examples. Consultative: It's usually a dialogue, but it's formal enough that the words are carefully picked.  Frozen: Fixed speech is another name for it. It is the most formal communicating style, and it is frequently utilized in formal situations or ceremonies. Pledge of Allegiance, Lord's Prayer, national song, national pledge, and school creeds are just a few examples.
