25. A boy swimming in a lake smacks the surface of the water with his hand, creating sound waves that travel through the water and air. Which will hear the sound sooner, a fish swimming 5 m below the surface of the water, or a bird flying 5 m above the surface of the water? Justify your answer by discussing how sound travels through a medium


Answer:

The fish swimming 5m below the surface of the water will hear the sound sooner than the bird 5m above the surface of the water.

Explanation:

Comparison through particles between water and air

The particles in water is closer that in air. As sound travels, the particles bumps to each other. Since the particles in water are closer to each other, the vibrations of sound connect each particle easily while in air, since the particles are far apart, the tendency of the particle to bump each other takes a while.

Comparison through formula

To explain this observation through the use of formula. We will use the speed of sound formula.

1. [tex]v_{sound} =\sqrt{\frac{\gamma RT}{M} }[/tex]

where

[tex]\gamma[/tex]       is the adiabatic constant, it is unitless

R       is the universal gas constant, 8.314 J/mol K

T        is the temperature, unit to use is Kelvin

M       is the molecular weight of a quantity, unit is in kg/mol

2. Since the sound varies with temperature, let us assume that in the given problem, we have a room temperature of 20 degree Celsius (293.15 K).

The adiabatic constant for air is [tex]\gamma[/tex] = 1.4.

The molecular mass of air is M = 0.0289647 kg/mol

So, we can solve the speed of sound in air  using the formula for speed of sound, we have:

[tex]v_{sound} =\sqrt{\frac{\gamma RT}{M} }[/tex]

Substitute the given information, we have:

[tex]v_{sound} =\sqrt{\frac{\(1.4)(8.314 J/mol K)(293.15 K)}{0.0289647 kg/mol)} }[/tex]

[tex]v_{sound}=343.31m/s[/tex]

Therefore, the speed of sound in air is 343.31 m/s

3. Now, let us compute the speed of sound in water

We will set the same temperature with water, T = 293.15 K

Adiabatic constant for water is [tex]\gamma[/tex] = 1.33

The molecular mass of water is M = 18.01 g/mol = 0.0181528 kg/mol

Now, let us solve for the speed of sound in water, we have:

[tex]v_{sound} =\sqrt{\frac{\gamma RT}{M} }[/tex]

[tex]v_{sound} =\sqrt{\frac{\(1.33)(8.314 J/mol K)(293.15K)}{0.0181528kg/mol} }[/tex]

[tex]v_{sound} =424.185m/s[/tex]

Therefore, the speed of sound in water is 424.185 m/s

Here in our computation, the speed of sound in water is higher than in air.  This means that the speed of sound travels faster in water than in air.
