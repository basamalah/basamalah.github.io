20. American Marketing Association’s definition of sales management is nearly same as “the management of salesforce.” It is said that this definition is not in line with the broader responsibilities of modern sales managers. Do you agree with this view? Explain with reasons.​


Answer:

I agree with the view that the American Marketing Association's definition of sales management as "the management of the salesforce" is limited and not in line with the broader responsibilities of modern sales managers.

While managing the salesforce is a critical part of sales management, modern sales managers have a more comprehensive set of responsibilities that go beyond simply managing a team of sales representatives. For instance, they are responsible for developing sales strategies, setting sales targets, analyzing market trends and customer needs, managing budgets, and overseeing the entire sales process from lead generation to after-sales support.

Modern sales managers are also expected to work collaboratively with other departments, such as marketing, finance, and product development, to ensure that sales strategies are aligned with overall business objectives. They need to be proficient in data analysis and technology, as the sales process increasingly relies on data-driven decision making and automation.

Therefore, while managing the salesforce is an essential component of sales management, it is not the only responsibility of modern sales managers. They need to have a broader skill set and a more strategic approach to sales management to be effective in today's business environment.
