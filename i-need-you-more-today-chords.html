1. Triad, in music, a chord made up of three tones, called chord factors, of the diatonic scale: root, third, and fifth. The system of diatonic triads is the basis of tonal harmony in music. Triads built on the notes of the C major (and natural A minor) scale.

2. Harmony is two or more notes played together at the same time. As soon as there is more than one pitch sounding at a time, you have harmony. ... In music, harmony is the use of simultaneous pitches (tones, notes), or chords.

3. Polyphony, in music, the simultaneous combination of two or more tones or melodic lines (the term derives from the Greek word for “many sounds”). Thus, even a single interval made up of two simultaneous tones or a chord of three simultaneous tones is rudimentarily polyphonic. Usually, however, polyphony is associated with counterpoint, the combination of distinct melodic lines.
