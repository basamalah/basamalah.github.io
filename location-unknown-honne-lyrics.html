19. the radar detects that there's an unknown ship near coastal area at sin 105° If you are the captain, can you get the exact location of the ship manually? Explain


Answer:

As a captain, it would be possible to get the exact location of the unknown ship near the coastal area using radar and other navigation tools. Here's how:

Triangulation: Using the radar, you can take bearings on the ship from two or more positions, such as the coast and another ship, and then use triangulation to calculate the exact position of the ship. This method works best when the ship is in relatively close proximity to your own ship or the coast.

Dead Reckoning: another method is to use a combination of speed, course and time from the last known position. This process can be done manually by plotting the course and speed of the ship over time on a chart and marking its progress to obtain the estimated position

GPS and Electronic charts : GPS and Electronic charts can also be used to determine the exact location of the ship. This technology uses satellite positioning to accurately determine the position of the ship on a map or chart in real-time.

Visual identification: another method that can be used is visual identification. If the unknown ship is within sight, a visual identification can be used to determine the ship's identity, flag, class or other characteristics and give an idea of its position.

It's important to note that it may not always be possible to obtain the exact location of an unknown ship using these methods, particularly if it is far away or if there are visibility issues like heavy fog or poor weather conditions.

It's also possible to combine multiple methods as a redundancy check and ensure the accuracy of the location of the unknown ship.
