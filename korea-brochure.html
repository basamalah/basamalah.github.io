Korean painting includes paintings made in Korea or by overseas Koreans on all surfaces. The earliest surviving Korean paintings are murals in the Goguryeo tombs, of which considerable numbers survive, the oldest from some 2,000 years ago (mostly now in North Korea), with varied scenes including dancers, hunting and spirits.[1] The Takamatsuzuka Tomb in Japan, from the 7th-century end of the Goguryeo period, has paintings in Goguryeo style that were either done by Korean artists, or Japanese one trained by Koreans.[2] But more often influences came into Korea from China. 25. Definition of -Supplementary brochures -Promotional brochures -Leave-behind brochures -Point of sale brochures -Direct mail brochures nonsense response= immediate report


Suplementary Brochures -

Often compared to booklets. Considered one of the simplest brochures to create, half-fold consists of two internal panels and a front and back cover.

Promotional Brochures -

Produced by making two bends. The size of the panels must be equal.

Leave-behind Brochures -

One of the obvious advantages is this format’s transformability. It offers more room for the designer’s imagination.

Point of Sale Brochures -

The form of this brochure looks like a zig-zag. Put simply, it includes six parts of equal size, created by double bending.

Direct mail brochures -

This type of brochure is also known as window fold. Its construction is similar to a tri-fold booklet.
