20. 16-30. Using Venn Diagram compare and contrast qualitative research and quantitative research based on their characteristics. pahelp naman po​


Answer:

Qualitative and quantitative research are two different approaches to gathering and analyzing data. While they share some similarities, they have distinct differences in their characteristics.

Qualitative Research:

Focuses on exploring and understanding a phenomenon in depth.

Collects data through methods such as interviews, observations, and focus groups.

Relies on subjective interpretation of data and emphasizes the context in which data is collected.

Data analysis involves identifying themes, patterns, and meanings in the data.

Findings are often presented in a narrative form and may include direct quotes from participants.

Sample size is usually small and non-random.

Quantitative Research:

Focuses on numerical measurement and statistical analysis.

Collects data through methods such as surveys, experiments, and structured observations.

Relies on objective measurement of data and emphasizes the generalizability of findings.

Data analysis involves statistical analysis to identify relationships, trends, and patterns in the data.

Findings are often presented in the form of tables, charts, and graphs.

Sample size is usually large and randomly selected.

In summary, qualitative research is characterized by an in-depth exploration of a phenomenon, subjective interpretation of data, and a small, non-random sample size. Quantitative research, on the other hand, is characterized by numerical measurement, objective analysis, and a large, randomly selected sample size.

•Qualitative

Qualitative Research is to provide a complete, detailed description of the research topic. It is usually more exploratory in nature.

Quantitative Research on the other hand focuses more in counting and classifying features and constructing statistical models and figures to explain what is observed.

•Qualitative
