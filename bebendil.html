7. What is..1.Gandingan2. Dabakan3. babandil4. kubing5. palendagPa sagot​


Answer:

1.The gandingan is a Philippine set of four large, hanging gongs used by the Maguindanao as part of their kulintang ensemble. When integrated into the ensemble, it functions as a secondary melodic instrument after the main melodic instrument, the kulintang

2.The dabakan is a single-headed Philippine drum, primarily used as a supportive instrument in the kulintang ensemble. Among the five main kulintang instruments, it is the only non-gong element of the Maguindanao ensemble.

3.The babandil is a single, narrow-rimmed Philippine gong used primarily as the “timekeeper” of the Maguindanao kulintang ensemble.

4.The kubing is a type of Philippine jaw harp from bamboo found among the Maguindanaon and other Muslim and non-Muslim tribes in the Philippines and Indonesia. ... The kubing is traditionally considered an intimate instrument, usually used as communication between family or a loved one in close quarters

5.The palendag, also called Pulalu (Manobo and Mansaka), Palandag (Bagobo), Pulala (Bukidnon) and Lumundeg (Banuwaen) is a type of Philippine bamboo flute, the largest one used by the Maguindanaon, a smaller type of this instrument is called the Hulakteb (Bukidnon).

16. Define the following:1. Kulintang2. Agung3. Gandingan4. Dabakan5. Babandil/babandir​


Answer:

Kulintang: a row of small gongs played with wooden beaters in the Philippines.

Agung: a large, double-headed drum from Mindanao, Philippines.

Gandingan: two large, vertically-laid metal gongs played with wooden beaters in the Philippines.

Dabakan: a cylindrical drum played by hand in the southern Philippines.

Babandil/babandir: a brass gong played with a wooden beater in the southern Philippines.

1.) Kulintang - it is a traditional musical instrument of the Philippines, specifically from the Southern Philippines, made up of a row of small, horizontally-laid gongs that are played using two wooden beaters. It is often used in various indigenous rituals and celebrations.

2.) Agung - it is a large, single-headed drum that is often used in the traditional music of the Philippines. It is typically played with the hands and is known for its deep, resonant sound.

3.) Gandingan - it is a set of four large, vertically-hung gongs that are played using soft mallets. It is commonly used in traditional music from the Southern Philippines, particularly in Maguindanao and Maranao cultures.

4.) Dabakan - it is a cylindrical, single-headed drum that is often used in traditional music from the Southern Philippines, particularly in Maguindanao and Maranao cultures. It is played with the hands and produces a deep, resonant sound.

5.) Babandil/Babandir - it is a small, double-headed drum that is often used in traditional music from the Southern Philippines, particularly in Maguindanao and Maranao cultures. It is typically played with the hands and produces a high-pitched, percussive sound.
