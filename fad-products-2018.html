15. 1. What are the impacts of the fad at the individual, group, and societal levels? 2. Why is the product or activity discussed in the article considered a fad and not a trend?​


ANSWER:

1. The impacts of a fad at the individual level can range from positive to negative, depending on the nature of the fad. For example, if the fad is related to fitness or healthy eating, individuals may experience physical and mental health benefits. On the other hand, if the fad is related to substance abuse, individuals may experience negative health consequences. At the group level, fads can create a sense of community and social belonging, as individuals who participate in the fad share a common interest. At the societal level, fads can influence popular culture and consumer behavior.

2. A fad is typically a short-lived phenomenon that experiences a sudden surge in popularity before quickly fading away. Trends, on the other hand, are more long-lasting and have a lasting impact on society. Fads are often driven by a novelty factor or a desire to be part of the latest trend, while trends are driven by more substantive factors such as changing demographics or technological innovation.

28. how is a fad illustrated through a life of a product?​


Answer:

Fads are temporary periods of unusually high sales driven by consumer enthusiasm and immediate product or brand popularity. A fad may be part of an otherwise normal product life cycle, passing through the product life cycle stages.

When sales start to decline, the product will enter the decline stage. A fad's life cycle is often different. A fad typically has a brief introduction period as its popularity skyrockets, followed by a very brief maturity stage. The product enters decline almost as quickly as it rose in popularity.
