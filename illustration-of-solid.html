10. Illustrate solid liquid gas​


Answer:

Solid is the state in which matter maintains a fixed volume and shape; liquid is the state in which matter adapts to the shape of its container but varies only slightly in volume; and gas is the state in which matter expands to occupy the volume and shape of its container.


11. illustrate the daradigm shift of solid waste management​


Answer:

The traditional, closed-loop waste management system has long relied on landfills, incineration and other approaches to discard of solid waste. This paradigm shift has seen an increase in approaches to solid waste management that focus on recycling and reuse activities.

This shift has been driven by a better understanding of the environmental impacts of traditional waste management practices, and their potential harm to human health and resources. Additionally, today’s increased access to technological advances has enabled users to better track waste streams and monitor the effectiveness of recycling, reuse, and composting activities.

In many countries, governments and other stakeholders have begun encouraging producers of waste to implement product stewardship and extended producer responsibility programs, which consider product and packaging design, production and disposal. This has led to the reduction of superfluous and hazardous material use, in turn curbing the environmental impacts of waste production.

Finally, an improved public awareness about the sources, implications and possible solutions for waste continues to drive the paradigm shift: Communities are showing increased interest in civic-scale composting, volunteering for waste gathering, and dedicating their personal and professional efforts towards progress in solid waste management.

Answer:

The paradigm shift in solid waste management involves a transition from the traditional "end-of-pipe" approach, where waste is collected and disposed of in landfills or incinerators, to a more holistic and sustainable approach that prioritizes waste reduction, recycling, and resource recovery.

This shift recognizes that waste is not just a problem to be disposed of but can also be a valuable resource if managed properly. It also acknowledges that waste management is not just the responsibility of local governments and waste service providers, but also of individual households, businesses, and communities.

Here are some examples of how this paradigm shift is being manifested in solid waste management practices:

1. Waste Reduction: Efforts are being made to reduce the amount of waste generated by individuals and households. This includes promoting the use of reusable bags, bottles, and containers, reducing food waste through composting and meal planning, and encouraging the purchase of durable and long-lasting products.

2. Recycling: Recycling programs are being expanded and improved to increase the amount of waste that is diverted from landfills and incinerators. This includes providing more convenient and accessible recycling options for residents, implementing policies that require businesses and institutions to recycle, and developing markets for recycled materials.

3. Resource Recovery: Technologies are being developed to recover valuable resources from waste streams, such as energy, nutrients, and materials. This includes the use of anaerobic digestion to produce biogas from organic waste, the extraction of metals and plastics from electronic waste, and the conversion of waste into fuel for transportation.

4. Community Engagement: Community-based approaches are being used to promote waste reduction and recycling, such as education campaigns, community composting programs, and zero-waste initiatives. These efforts aim to build awareness and support for sustainable waste management practices among residents, businesses, and local governments.

Overall, the paradigm shift in solid waste management represents a move towards a more circular and sustainable economy, where waste is seen as a resource and not just a problem to be disposed of.

13. Illustrate how molecules are arranged in solid,​


Answer:

Molecules in a solid maintain both their own shape and their own volume. Solids are virtually incompressible and have little diffusion beyond the surface layer. The molecular arrangement in solids is a highly organized, tightly-packed pattern with small spaces and molecular motion reduced to vibration in place.

30. illustrate the kinetic molecular model of solids and liquids.​


Answer:

The kinetic molecular theory of matter states that: ... The average amount of empty space between molecules gets progressively larger as a sample of matter moves from the solid to the liquid and gas phases. There are attractive forces between atoms/molecules, and these become stronger as the particles move closer together.

What is the kinetic molecular model of liquids?

The kinetic molecular theory suggests that the vapor pressure of a liquid depends on its temperature. As can be seen in the graph of kinetic energy versus number of molecules, the fraction of the molecules that have enough energy to escape from a liquid increases with the temperature of the liquid.
