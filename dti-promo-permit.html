7. Government Agency that checks the warranty and price tags of products.A. DepEdB. DTIC. DTID. FDA​


B/C. DTI

About DTI in the Philippines

The Department of Commerce and Industry (DTI) is the driving force behind consumer welfare. It is committed to protecting the rights and interests of consumers and developing policies and programs aimed at sustaining the economic growth and development of the Philippines.

The Department of Trade and Industry (in Filipino called: Kagawaran ng Kalakalan at Industriya, abbreviated as DTI) is the executive department of the Philippine government charged with fostering innovative, competitive, job-creating, inclusive business and empowering consumers.

Checking the product's warranty and price tags fall under CONSUMER RIGHTS, which is part of The Right to Basic Needs. The right ensures adequate food, clothing, shelter, medical care, education, and sanitation for survival. As a result, you can anticipate that consumers will have access to affordable, high-quality essential and luxury goods.

The DTI Consumer Protection Group (DTI-CPG) encourages online and brick-and-mortar merchants to comply with:

- Article 81 of Republic Act No. 7394, or the Consumer Act of the Philippines, requires appropriate tags, labels, or markings indicating the prices of retail-sold consumer products. With these, products cannot be sold at prices higher than stated; with these, products cannot be sold at prices lower than stated.

- According to Section 5 of Republic Act No. 7581 or the Price Act, selling a basic necessity or prime commodity without a price tag is prima facie evidence of profiteering.
