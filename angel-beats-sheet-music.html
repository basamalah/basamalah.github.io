Hip hop is a culture that encompasses a wide range of artistic forms, including graffiti, dance, rap, and music. Hip hop is a much broader cultural expression that includes rap. Beginner dancers can learn and use dance motions or dance steps by isolating, defining, and organizing them. Musicality and lyrical relevance, on the other hand, impact more complicated movements that communicate emotions or connect to a message.

Waacking

Waacking (also spelled waacking, whacking, and whacking) is a Latino and African-American underground dance style that began in the American underground gay disco and gets its name from the English word "waack," which means "raising arms." Waacking is a dancing technique that originated in the 1970s at dance clubs around the west coast of the United States. Waacking is defined by a fast-paced technique that focuses on arm motions to create shapes and positions above and around the head. Waacking is frequently performed to disco music.

Locking

Locking is a funk dancing form that has become connected with hip hop in recent years. The name comes from the concept of locking motions, which entails stopping a fast movement and "locking" in a specific position, maintaining it for a short time, and then continuing at the same speed. Locking is a showman who frequently interacts with the audience by smiling or high-fiving them, and some of his maneuvers are pretty amusing. Locking was first performed in conventional funk music.

Tutting

A hip-hop dance style that stresses the body's ability to produce geometric shapes (such as boxes) and movements, typically using 90-degree angles. It usually concentrates on the extremities of the body, such as the arms, hands, and fingers.

Shuffling

The Shuffle dance is an improvised dance that involves frequently "shuffling" the feet inside, then outwards, while thrusting the arms up and down, or side to side, in rhythm with the beat.

B-boy

Break dancing, also known as breaking and B-boying, is an explosive form of dance popularized by African Americans and Latinos that features stylized footwork and athletic maneuvers including backspins and head spins.
