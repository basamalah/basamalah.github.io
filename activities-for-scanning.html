Answer:

Differences of Skimming and Scanning

Skimming is the process of reading quickly in order to acquire a general overview of the subject. Scanning is the process of reading quickly in order to identify certain data. While skimming tells you what general information is contained within a section, scanning assists you in locating a specific fact. Scanning is the act of reading quickly in order to find specific facts. While skimming shows you what basic information is contained within a section, scanning assists you in locating a specific fact. Skimming is similar to snorkeling, while scanning is more akin to pearl diving.

Similarities between Skimming & Scanning

9. how High resolution scanning microscope/ scanning electron microscope works?​


Answer

Areas ranging from approximately 1 cm to 5 microns in width can be imaged in a scanning mode using conventional SEM techniques (magnification ranging from 20X to approximately 30,000X, spatial resolution of 50 to 100 nm).

The resolution limit of light microscopes is about 200nm, the maximum useful magnification a light microscope can provide is about 1,000x. The resolution limit of electron microscopes is about 0.2nm, the maximum useful magnification an electron microscope can provide is about 1,000,000x.

Scanning is commonly used in everyday life, for example when looking up a word in a dictionary or finding your friend's name in the contacts directory of your phone. Scanning and another quick reading skill, skimming, are often confused, though they are quite different.

Answer:

Skimming and scanning are reading techniques that use rapid eye movement and keywords to move quickly through text for slightly different purposes. Skimming is reading rapidly in order to get a general overview of the material. Scanning is reading rapidly in order to find specific facts.
