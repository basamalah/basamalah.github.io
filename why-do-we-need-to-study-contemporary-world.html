3. why do we have to study the contemporary world?​


Answer:

Part of the Social Sciences, the contemporary world mainly deals with issues and problems related to the environment and the people around it, wealth, power, different types of conflicts, and tensions.

Explanation:

The contemporary world allows learners, who are mostly adults, to learn and develop subject-specific competencies like taking a position on a world issue or being able to interpret world problems. Learners become more aware and sensitive of the different consequences humans act on societies, developing their critical thinking and judgement, and being able to define their understanding of the world.  

Learning the contemporary world can benefit us in the long term because it allows us to fully understand what’s happening in the world. It enables us to decide and think critically.  

The main goals of studying and learning the contemporary world are:

For adult learners to become interested in the problems that the world faces; To develop competencies, construct knowledge, and increase one’s critical judgment; To understand the different natures and relationships built between societies and other organizations of the world; To become aware of the different roles and responsibilities in preparation for the next generation.

13. why do we need to study a contemporary issue? ​


The reason why we need to study contemporary issues

A contemporary issue is an event or problem that is currently happening and has not been resolved. Examples are hate speech, discrimination, human rights, global warming, and others.

There are several reasons why we should know and study contemporary issues:

1. To find out what problems are happening in society

Studying contemporary issues can make us aware of what problems and concerns are happening in society.

2. To develop an attitude of caring, sympathy, and empathy

When we know what problems and concerns people are experiencing, we may also understand and sympathize with what is going on and making us care more about others.

3. Develop critical thinking skills

When we begin to care and sympathize with problems or concerns that are happening in society, we may also try to find out what causes and solutions to them. This thinking process will develop our critical thinking skills.

4. Help understand cultural differences in society

Some contemporary issues are issues related to the culture of a society, such as the issue of discrimination. By studying this issue, it is hoped that we can better understand that there are many people with different cultures in society and all of them must be respected.

5. Develop the ability to express opinions

After knowing or studying some contemporary issues, usually, we will take a position or attitude on the issue along with the reasons. This can develop our ability to have an opinion on something.

Apart from the reasons above, of course, there are many other reasons why it is important to study contemporary issues. But the main thing that is expected from a student who studies contemporary issues is that they in the future can become agents of change who can help solve contemporary issues that are still happening.
