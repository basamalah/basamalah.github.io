15. 1. Marlyn Knows How to Cook Rice Marlyn watches her mother when she cooks rice for their lunch. Marlyn remembered all the steps in doing it for their dinner, she asked her mother to allow her to cook their rice for them. She was allowed so she started preparing for the task She poured the measured rice grain in the pot and washed it with water. After washing the nice grain, she measured the water in the pot and removed the excess water before putting the pot on the stove. The rice was cooked properly, and their family had a great dinner. Since then, she learned to cook rice for their family. Guide Questions What technique did Marlyn use when she separated the water from the rice grain as she washed it before cooking? How did Marlyn apply the technique in separating the water from the rice grain?​


Answer:

The technique that Marlyn used when separating the water from the rice grain as she washed it before cooking is decantation.

Marlyn removed the excess water specifically by pouring excess water out.

Explanation:

A mixture is a kind of material that is made up of two or more substances. These two or more substances are combined physically and do not involve chemical reactions or the formation of bonds. Thus, components in a mixture can be separated. There are many techniques or ways to separate the components of the mixture depending on the characteristics of the components. One way to separate is decantation. Decantation is one separation technique that can be used to separate components of suspension. In the rice-water mixture, rice is heavy and dense that it settles at the bottom. Excess water may then be removed by slowly pouring it out. Through this, the excess water is removed leaving the rice in the pot. Decantation can also be used to separate two immiscible such as oil and water mixture.
