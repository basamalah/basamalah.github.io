There are 400 junior high school students 160 chose online distance learning 240 students chose modular distance learning and 80 chose both if a student is randomly selected, what is the probability that the student chooses online distance learning or modular distance learning?

0.8 is the answer of your question.

[tex]------------------[/tex]

[tex]\huge\color{iron}\bold{Solution:}[/tex]

160 - 80 = 80

So 80 students choose only distance learnings.

240 - 80 = 160

So 160 students choose only modular distance learning.

80 students choose both.

80 + 160 + 80 = 320

So 320 choose online or modular distance learning.

[tex]p = \frac{320}{400} = 0.8[/tex]So, 0.8 is the probability.

16. THE EFFECT OF MODULAR STUDY TO STUDENTS statement of the problem research.​


Modular study is a type of distance learning that involves students working through a series of self-contained modules. Each module covers a specific topic and includes learning materials, such as readings, activities, and assessments. Students can work through the modules at their own pace and in their own time.

There is a growing body of research on the effects of modular study on students. Some studies have found that modular study can be an effective way for students to learn, especially for students who are self-motivated and who have good time management skills. Other studies have found that modular study can be less effective than traditional classroom instruction, especially for students who need more structure and support.

The following are some of the potential benefits of modular study for students:

* Students can learn at their own pace and in their own time.

* Students can focus on the topics that they are interested in and that they need to learn.

* Students can get immediate feedback on their learning.

* Students can learn in a variety of ways, such as through reading, writing, discussion, and problem-solving.

* Students can develop their self-directed learning skills.

The following are some of the potential drawbacks of modular study for students:

* Students may not have the motivation or discipline to work through the modules on their own.

* Students may not have access to the resources that they need to complete the modules.

* Students may not get enough support from their teachers or classmates.

* Students may not learn the material as well as they would in a traditional classroom setting.

Overall, the effects of modular study on students vary depending on the individual student and the specific learning environment. More research is needed to determine the long-term effects of modular study on student achievement.
