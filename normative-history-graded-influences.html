Answer:

3. example of a norm description and influence to media​


Answer:

Media has the power to influence individual beliefs, attitudes, and behaviors. Thus, social norms that influence and encourage gender violence have been targeted by policy interventions using mass media communication. Prior research suggests that media influences through two effects: the individual or direct effect (private) or the social or indirect effect (public). In the individual effect, media information about new norms may persuade individuals to accept them. In the social effect, the information creates common knowledge of a norm and enhances social coordination as individuals more readily accept the information if they believe others have also accepted it. This study examined whether media’s social mechanism has a stronger impact than its individual mechanism on changing violent attitudes against women.

The Spaniards in this ethnic meeting used the term Igorot to refer those tribes they did not settle in Central Luzon. It is derived from the term "Godot," which means "mountain chain," and is supplemented by the prefix "I," which means "live in."

20. . How cultural norms influence politics in different countries?​


Answer:

Religion, politics and culture (norms etc) are everywhere closely interwoven.

One strong argument for that is the difference between US and Nordic politics. Both are religiously defined as protestant, Calvinist vs Lutheran. Those are reasonably close, but with one stark difference.

The Calvinist idea about if God have defined your faith to be rich or poor vs the Lutheran idea that you show love in God by working hard, help other, and thereby control your faith.

So in US if you get rich it is a proof of Gods Love and Purpose for you. If you don’t get rich, it also a proof of Gods Purpose, though not love. It can be viewed as giving wealth to those in need is against Gods Purpose.

In Nordics you show your good heart and love in God by hard work, build fortune and giving those in need, in hope for a better better life after this.

This translate to differences on cultural (social acceptance) and political views on, e.g. how heavily to tax the rich, social security support for poor and if jail is for revenge or to improve the criminals.
