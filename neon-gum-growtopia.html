answer: NON-METAL

Neon is neither a metal nor a metalloid. It is a non-metal.

Neon is a chemical element that has a symbol of “Ne” and an atomic number 10. It is in fact a noble gas element.

Neon is colorless and odorless. Its inert monatomic gas under standard conditions is with about 2/3 of density of air.

It was discovered with krypton and xenon in the year 1898 as one of the three residual rare inert elements remaining in dry air, after these were removed: nitrogen, oxygen, argon and carbon dioxide.

Neon is second of these 3 rare gases to be discovered and immediately, it was recognized as new element with its bright red emission spectrum.

21. Are all neon lights made up of neon gases? Why?


Neon lights are named from the element “neon” where it contains small amounts of neon to none except the red neon light. These lights are made of gases in a rarefied tube, where there is low gas pressure inside the tube. The red neon light is composed mainly of the neon gas and does not have mercury. It is known as the true neon light. Other colored neon lights (that are not reddish in color) do not have neon and are composed of other elements like argon, mercury, phosphor, xenon, helium, and krypton. When noble gases are exposed to high voltage, helium gives off the pink light, the green and yellow light is given off by krypton, xenon gives off a lavender blue light, and the light blue light is given off by argon. Noble gases are nonreactive because their outer shells are composed of a complete set of electrons, but when exposed to voltages, it becomes reactive and shows some colors.
