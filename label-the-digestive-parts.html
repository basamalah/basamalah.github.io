8. Label the part of the Digestive System​


Answer:

Mouth

The anterior opening of the alimentary canal is the mouth. It leads to a buccal cavity or oral cavity, where teeth, tongue and salivary glands are present.

Salivary Glands

They are exocrine glands that produce saliva in the oral cavity. They secrete an enzyme called amylase, which helps in the breakdown of starch into maltose.

Stomach

The esophagus leads to a sac-like organ called the stomach. It is a muscular, hollow organ, having a capacity of 1 litre. The stomach holds food and also is a mixer and grinder. It secretes strong acids and powerful enzymes that help in the process of breaking down of food.

Small Intestine

The small intestine is a long tube, which loosely coils in the abdomen area. Here, enzymes from the pancreas and liver further break down the food.

Pancreas, Liver and Gall Bladder

These organs also play a significant role in the human digestive system. The pancreas secretes enzymes which help in the breakdown protein, fat, and carbohydrate. The liver secretes bile and cleanses and purifies the blood coming from the small intestine. The gallbladder stores the bile that the liver produces. It releases bile into the small intestine to aid in the digestion process.

Large Intestine

The large intestine is a long muscular tube that has different parts, which are caecum, colon, and rectum. The waste that is left over after digestion of food, reaches the rectum through the peristaltic movements of the colon.

Function in Digestion

Mouth - Chews food and mixes it with saliva.

Salivary Glands - Produce saliva, which contains starch-digesting enzyme called salivary amylase

Epiglottis - Closing the laryngeal inlet prior to swallowing in order to prevent the food and liquid accessing the airways

Oesophagus - Carry food and liquid from your mouth to your stomach

Stomach - Mixing and breakdown of food by contraction and relaxation of the muscle layers in the stomach.

Small Intestine - Digests food and absorbs nutrients into blood or lymph

Large Intestine - Turns food waste into stool and passes it from the body when you poòp

Rectum - To receive stool from the colon, let you know that there is stool to be evacuated (pòoped out) and to hold the stool until evacuation happens.

Anus - Opens to allow waste to leave the body

Liver - Makes bile which aids in the digestion and absorption of fat

Gall Bladder - Store bile and release it into the small intestine when needed

Pancrease - Makes pancreatic juices called enzymes. These enzymes break down sugars, fats, and starches. Your pancreas also helps your digestive system by making hormones.

27. label the different parts of the digestive system​


Answer:

Mouth — It includes teeth, salivary glands and tongue. It is the beginning of the digestive tract and the process of digestion begins from the mouth, where teeth help by breaking and grinding the food molecules followed by the saliva mixing with the food particles.

Pharynx –It is a fibromuscular, Y-shaped tube-like structure, attached to the terminal end of the mouth. It is involved in the passage of chewed food from the mouth through the esophagus.

Stomach –It is a hollow and muscular organ, situated towards the left side of the abdominal cavity and beneath the diaphragm. It is a vital organ, which functions by storing the food, provides enough time to digest meals, produces hydrochloric acid and digestive enzymes that maintain the process of digestion.

Small Intestine –It is a thin and long tube-like structure measuring about 9 to 10 feet long
