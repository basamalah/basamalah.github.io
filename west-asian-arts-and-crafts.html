2. What are the characteristics of the arts and crafts of Central Asian arts and West Asian arts?


Answer:

Central Asian Arts

The Turkic, Uighur and Mongol peoples produced a range of arts and crafts. Ceramics, metalworking, jewelry, and artefacts in wood, glass and bone, as well as fabric-making and sculptures, were all important to these areas.

West Asian Arts

Traditional arts such as painting on glass and leather, woodwork, glassmaking, metalwork, textiles, and wall frescoes were considered naive and repetitive, relying less on intellect and more on archaic traditions.

11. Search for the Famous arts and crafts of West Asian Countries.​


Answer:

1. Afghan Carpet Making - Afghan carpets are popular across the world for their intricate designs and vibrant colors. The traditional hand-knotted carpets are made in different regions of Afghanistan.

2. Turkish Ceramics - Turkish ceramics are famous for their beautiful patterns and geometric designs. The art of Turkish ceramics dates back to the Ottoman Empire and is still practiced in various parts of Turkey.

3. Persian Miniature Painting - Persian miniature painting is a traditional form of art that originated in Iran more than 800 years ago. These paintings depict intricate and detailed scenes of life, nature, and mythology.

4. Arabesque Design - Arabesque design is a distinct form of Islamic art that has been used extensively in architecture, textiles, and pottery across the Arab world. This design is characterized by intricate floral and geometric patterns.

5. Lebanese Embroidery - Lebanese embroidery is a traditional art form that uses colorful threads to create intricate patterns on fabrics such as cotton and silk. The designs often depict floral motifs and geometric shapes.

6. Yemeni Jewelry Making - Yemeni jewelry is famous for its unique designs and intricate workmanship. The jewelry is often made from silver and adorned with semi-precious stones.

7. Jordanian Handicrafts - Jordan is known for its traditional handicrafts, which include pottery, embroidery, weaving, and basketry. These crafts are often made using natural materials and traditional techniques.

8. Israeli Glassblowing - Glassblowing is an ancient art form that has been practiced in Israel for thousands of years. Israeli glassblowers are renowned for their innovative designs and techniques.

9. Kurdish Rug Making - Kurdish rug making is a traditional craft that uses natural dyes and hand-knotted techniques to create unique and colorful rugs. These rugs often feature bold geometric designs.

10. Iraqi Calligraphy - Iraqi calligraphy is a form of Islamic calligraphy that has been practiced in Iraq for centuries. The art of calligraphy is a highly respected tradition in Iraq, and Iraqi calligraphers are known for their intricate and beautiful designs.

What are the similarities and differences of the cultures of the South Asian, West Asian, and Central Asi countries in relation to Philippine culture?​


Answer:

1.The traditions of South, West, and Central Asia are still relevant to the modern world in many ways. These traditions encompass various aspects of life, including cultural practices, beliefs, customs, and rituals that have been passed down from generation to generation. These traditions are an integral part of the identity of these regions and their people. They help preserve their cultural heritage and contribute to their sense of community and belonging. Moreover, these traditions have influenced modern-day practices, such as cuisine, fashion, and music, that have gained worldwide popularity.

2.South, West, and Central Asian art reflects the rich cultural heritage of these regions. Art has been an essential part of these cultures, and it has been used to convey messages, tell stories, and express emotions. The art forms of these regions include calligraphy, pottery, carpet weaving, miniature paintings, and sculptures, among others. These art forms have influenced the culture of these regions, shaping their aesthetics, values, and beliefs. They have also contributed to the development of art forms in other parts of the world.

3.Crafts in specific countries in South Asia, West Asia, and Central Asia have developed based on various factors, such as functional requirements, traditional knowledge and expertise, and the availability of resources. For instance, in countries like India and Pakistan, crafts such as weaving, embroidery, and block printing have developed due to the availability of local materials like cotton and silk, and the expertise of the local artisans. Similarly, in countries like Iran and Turkey, crafts such as carpet weaving and pottery have developed due to the availability of local clay and skilled artisans.

The cultures of South Asian, West Asian, and Central Asian countries have many similarities and differences in relation to Philippine culture. For instance, all these regions have rich cultural traditions, which are deeply embedded in their daily lives. Family values and respect for elders are also essential in these regions. However, there are also differences in terms of language, religion, and customs. For instance, South Asian countries have a predominantly Hindu and Muslim population, while West Asian countries have a predominantly Muslim population. Central Asian countries have a mix of different ethnicities and religions, including Islam, Christianity, and Buddhism
