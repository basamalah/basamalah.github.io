6. Juan and Pedro borrowed Php150,000 from Ana, Lorna, and Fe in solidum. Eventually, Ana validly condones or disregards the debt in the amount of Php100,000 as she feels indebted to Juan and Pedro for their parents' benevolence when both of her parents died in an accident since Juan's and Pedro's parents provided moral and financial support to Ana after the tragedy. A. What kind of obligation is involved in the problem? b. What is the effect of Ana's act of condoning the debt in the amount of Php100,000 with respect to Juan's and Pedro's debt? With respect to Lorna's and Fe's credit?.


Answer:

A. Because it involves two or more debtors and two or more creditors, the type of obligation at issue is a collective obligation. Juan and Pedro are the debtors in this situation, and Ana, Lorna, and Fe are the creditors.

B. I believe that Ana's decision to excuse or disregard Juan and Pedro's debt in the amount of Php100,000 will benefit them because they will not be required to repay the full amount of Php150,000 that they borrowed; instead, they will only be required to repay the remaining Php50,000. Additionally, Ana feels obligated to Juan and Pedro because their parents provided Ana with both moral and financial support. Regarding Lorna and Fe's credit, however, I don't believe it is a favor to them because it is their own money and they lent their money in full, which means it must also be reimbursed in full. In addition, Ana need to first discuss her choice with Lorna and Fe as it is their money, not just hers, even if she had the best of intentions. Yet, she made the incorrect choice.
