3. explain in essay form if there is a possibility that after supernova explosion could there is another sun that will replace​


The universe's largest and most potent explosions are known as supernovae. They happen when a huge star reaches the end of its life cycle and explodes, sending a lot of energy and matter out into space. A bright and colorful explosion can be seen in the night sky after a supernova explosion, even from millions of light-years away.

After a supernova explosion, one of the most common questions is whether another sun could take its place. Depending on how the question is interpreted, the response is either yes or no.

On the one hand, it is true that new stars can be made by a supernova explosion. Massive amounts of energy and matter, including heavy elements produced in the dying star's core, are released by the explosion. After that, this matter has the potential to collapse and produce new stars—possibly many at once. In this way, an explosion of a supernova can result in the formation of new suns to take the place of the one that has exploded.

However, it is essential to keep in mind that a supernova explosion also signifies the end of a star's life cycle. The star ran out of fuel and collapsed under the weight of its own gravity, causing the explosion. There is no way to bring the star back to life after the explosion. While new stars might shape from the matter delivered by the blast, these stars are not equivalent to the one that detonated.

In addition, there is no assurance that a supernova explosion will result in the formation of new stars. There are very specific requirements for the formation of stars, and not all of the matter released by the explosion will necessarily result in the formation of new stars. Additionally, it is not an immediate replacement for the exploding star because the formation of new stars can take millions of years.

In conclusion, although an explosion of a supernova can result in the formation of new stars, it is not certain that a new sun will emerge to replace the one that has already exploded. The star's life cycle comes to an end with the explosion, which cannot be undone. However, new stars may form from the matter released by the explosion, though this is not guaranteed and may take millions of years.

11. supernova nucleosynthesis


Answer:

Supernova nucleosynthesis is the nucleosynthesis of chemical elements in supernova explosions.

In sufficiently massive stars, the nucleosynthesis by fusion of lighter elements into heavier ones occurs during sequential hydrostatic burning processes called helium burning, carbon burning, oxygen burning, and silicon burning, in which the byproducts of one nuclear fuel become, after compressional heating, the fuel for the subsequent burning stage. In this context, the word "burning" refers to nuclear fusion and not a chemical reaction.

21. how supernova created?​


As the star runs out of nuclear fuel, some of its mass flows into its core. Eventually, the core is so heavy that it cannot withstand its own gravitational force. The core collapses, which results in the giant explosion of a supernova.

A supernova happens where there is a change in the core, or center, of a star. A change can occur in two different ways, with both resulting in a supernova.

The first type of supernova happens in binary star systems. Binary stars are two stars that orbit the same point. One of the stars, a carbon-oxygen white dwarf, steals matter from its companion star. Eventually, the white dwarf accumulates too much matter. Having too much matter causes the star to explode, resulting in a supernova.

The second type of supernova occurs at the end of a single star’s lifetime. As the star runs out of nuclear fuel, some of its mass flows into its core. Eventually, the core is so heavy that it cannot withstand its own gravitational force. The core collapses, which results in the giant explosion of a supernova. The sun is a single star, but it does not have enough mass to become a supernova.
