11. kiny explain and give example bridging gap- organizatiobs must identify any discrepancies between strategy and its proposed execution​


Answer:

Bridging the gap refers to the process of identifying and addressing any discrepancies or misalignments between an organization's strategic goals and the execution of those goals. In other words, it involves finding ways to ensure that the organization's strategy is effectively translated into action.

For example, let's say that a company's strategic goal is to expand its product line and capture a larger share of the market. However, when the company starts to execute this strategy, it becomes clear that the existing production facilities are not capable of handling the increased demand, and that the company lacks the necessary supply chain infrastructure to effectively distribute the new products.

In this case, the organization needs to bridge the gap by identifying these discrepancies and developing a plan to address them. This might involve investing in new production facilities or partnering with suppliers to improve the supply chain. By bridging the gap, the organization can ensure that its strategic goals are effectively executed and that it is able to achieve its desired outcomes.

22. what makes up a gap analysis?​


Explanation:

A gap analysis typically consists of four components:

1. Identifying the current state of the organization or process.

2. Defining the desired future state of the organization or process.

3. Analyzing the gap between the current and desired states.

4. Developing a plan to bridge the gap and achieve the desired future state.
