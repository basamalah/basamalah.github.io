6. Edna already owns 4 necklaces, and additional necklaces are priced at 2 for a dollar. Is the total number of necklaces Edna owns proportional to the amount of money Edna spends on additional necklaces?


Answer:

Yes.

Explanation:

Two terms are proportional if one term increases with the other, or as one term increases, the other decreases.

Using this basis, we know that the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount of money spent.

As the amount increases by a dollar, the necklaces increase by 2.

We can also determine if this is proportional if we can come up with a linear equation that relates the two terms.

Let x = necklaces; let y = amount spent for additional necklaces.

Using what I previously said in the third paragraph, y = x/2. But, we need to add 4 to this since there are 4 necklaces already to start with. Thus, y = x/2 + 4.

To summarize, yes; the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount spent on additional necklaces.

12. A necklace was sold for Php3150.00 with a 40% discount.what was the original Price of the necklace?​


ANSWER


Php5250.00

SOLUTION

To determine the original price of the necklace, you can use the following formula:

Original price = selling price / (1 - discount%)

Plugging in the given values, you get:

Original price = 3150 / (1 - 40%) = 3150 / (1 - 0.4) = 3150 / 0.6 = 5250

Therefore, the original price of the necklace was Php5250.00.

What is the exposition/introduction of a story "The necklace" by Guy de Maupassant​.

Madame Loisel was born dissatisfied with her lot in life, as the reader can tell from the first few lines of "The Necklace." She was born into an artisan family, but she believes she was destined to be born into a better social class. This character attribute is at the heart of the story's conflict. Madame Loisel wishes and aspires that she was born wealthy, and she devises schemes to achieve that goal, if only in appearance. When her husband returns home with a ball invitation, the core tension was born. Madame Loisel is distraught at the prospect of not looking her best at the ball, so she devises a plan to get a new gown and borrows an expensive diamond necklace from a friend.
