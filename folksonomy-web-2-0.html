28. Explain the concept of Folksonomy and defend its importance.


THE CONCEPT OF FOLKSONOMY AND DEFEND IT'S IMPORTANCE

Folksonomy a system created by the user that uses information such as electronic tags to categorize and organize online content into distinct categories. Folksonomies, also known as social tagging, are collections of metadata that are created by the user. A folksonomy develops when many users create or store content at specific sites and identify what they think the content is about. Users do not intentionally create folksonomies and there is rarely a prescribed purpose.

It lets users to categorize and locate content such as websites, images, documents, and other types of information. Social tagging, collaborative tagging, social classification, and social bookmarking are all terms used to describe Folksonomy.

answer.

taxonomy. 'Taxonomy' refers to a hierarchical categorization in which relatively well-defined classes are nested under broader categories.
