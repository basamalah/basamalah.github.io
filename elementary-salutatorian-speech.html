9. B. DIRECTIONS: 8 A speech about why a town needs a new elementary school


Ladies and gentlemen,

I stand before you today to talk about an issue that is of utmost importance to the future of our town - the need for a new elementary school.

As a community, we all know how crucial education is to the development and well-being of our children. Quality education is the cornerstone of a successful and prosperous society, and it is our duty as adults to provide our youth with the best possible education. Unfortunately, our current elementary school is no longer up to the task. It is old, understaffed, and ill-equipped to meet the needs of the growing number of students in our town.

Not only is this a detriment to our children's education, but it also hinders the town's economic and social development. A lack of education will lead to fewer job opportunities, lower wages, and a general decline in the quality of life for our community. Moreover, if we fail to invest in the education of our children, we are effectively closing the door on their future, and the future of our town.

It is for these reasons that we must seriously consider the need for a new elementary school. By investing in our school, we are investing in the future of our community. We are ensuring that our children are equipped with the knowledge and skills they need to succeed in the modern world, and we are providing them with the foundation for a better future.

Of course, this project will not be easy or inexpensive, but the benefits far outweigh the cost. The long-term benefits to our community will be immense, and the investment in our children's education will pay dividends for generations to come.

In conclusion, the need for a new elementary school in our town is clear. It is crucial for the growth and development of our community, and it is our responsibility as adults to ensure that our children receive the best possible education. Let us not falter in our commitment to our children and our community. Let us work together to make this project a reality, and let us build a brighter future for ourselves and for our children. Thank you.

29. Is the product of two elementary matrices always elementary? ​


Answer:

The product of elementary matrices need not be an elementary matrix. Recall that any invertible matrix can be written as a product of elementary matrices, and not all invertible matrices are elementary.
