26. How often should an organization’s vision/mission be changed in light of strategy evaluation activities?


Organization’s vision/mission be changed in light of strategy evaluation activities:

Answer: one to three years

Board, employees, and volunteers from your organization should develop the practice of evaluating the mission statement every one to three years, particularly in times of substantial transformation for the organization.

It may (and probably will) evolve, alter, and adapt to your expanding company.

You won't need to alter the mission or vision statement until your company or organization substantially changes its emphasis.

You could, however, add more components to your mission or vision statement as the organization or business develops.

Although a company's vision or mission statement may change over time in response to shifts in the sector in which it works, the "why we exist" process that the board of directors and top management engage in every December as they prepare for the upcoming year seldom undergoes revision.

Everyone inside the organization is to strive toward the same objectives and offer direction to your company's activities, according to your vision and mission statements. To be remembered, it should be brief (around 50 words, or two to three lines), and specific.

Criteria for Evaluating Mission StatementsAll staff members, especially the rank-and-file employees, may easily comprehend the mission statement.The organization's business is expressly stated in the mission statement. A single strategic thrust should be the major emphasis of the mission statement.
