20. "Justice delayed is Justice denied" explain this statement​


Answer:

This statement means that if justice is not carried out promptly or efficiently, it is effectively denied to the person seeking it. The idea is that justice should be administered in a timely manner, so that the wronged person can receive the remedy or compensation that they are due. If justice is delayed, it may no longer be relevant or effective in addressing the situation, and the person seeking justice may be left without recourse. Therefore, the saying emphasizes the importance of ensuring that justice is administered swiftly and fairly.

23. What is the impact of restorative justice to Criminal Justice system?​


International research shows that restorative justice significantly reduces imprisonment, reconviction and reoffending. Importantly, it provides significantly greater victim satisfaction.

Restorative justice in the criminal justice system uses victim and offender dialogue to address the harm caused by a crime as well as victims' experiences, interests and needs

Other approaches are available if they are unable or unwilling to meet. Sometimes those meetings lead to transformational changes in their lives.

30. 6. Distinguish Adult Criminal Justice and Juvinile Justice​


Answer:

Juvenile justice is the system of laws and practices designed to protect and rehabilitate adolescents who are accused of committing crimes. This system is based on the premise that children, due to their age and level of maturity, are not fully responsible for their actions and should, therefore, be treated differently than adults. This system is comprised of juvenile courts, probation officers, police, attorneys, and social workers.
