1. what is cash bond means?


In general cash bond means An arrangement wherein a party gives to another an amount of money to secure the fulfillment of an obligation. In cases where the party fails to comply with the obligation, the money is forfeited in favor of the latter.

In law cash bond means a transaction wherein a defendant deposits an amount of money to the court to secure his temporary release from jail. Defendants failing to appear before the court during their trial forfeit the cash bond.

11. When you receive a savings bond worth $100, you can cash it for $100 right away. 1.True 2.False


Answer:

False

Savings bonds are typically purchased at a discount and then mature over time, accruing interest. When a savings bond reaches maturity, it can be redeemed for its full face value, which in this case would be $100. However, if you try to cash a savings bond before it reaches maturity, you may not receive its full value and may be subject to fees or penalties.

20. When the interest payment dates of a bond are may 1 and november 1, and a bond is purchased on june 1, the amount of cash paid by the investor would be


When the interest payment dates of a bond are May 1 and November 1, and a bond issue is sold on June 1, the amount of cash paid by the investor would be increased by accrued interest from May 1 to June 1.

The cash received will be less than the face value of the bond because, in effect, the issuer has sold the bond "on time" and is therefore advanced one interest period. The difference between the amount of cash received by the issuer and the face value of the bond is called the "discount."

When a bond is issued at a discount, the issuer will receive less cash than the face value of the bond.

The market interest rate used to discount the interest payments is called the "yield to maturity." The yield to maturity is the rate of interest that would cause the present value of the bond's interest payments and principal to equal the price at which the bond is selling in the market.
