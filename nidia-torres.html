5. one standard atmosphere atm is equivelent to 75.0 torrtrue or wrong the 75.0 torr?​


Answer:

The statement "one standard atmosphere (atm) is equivalent to 75.0 torr" is false.

Explanation:

One standard atmosphere (atm) is defined as the pressure exerted by a column of mercury 760 millimeters (mm) in height at 0°C at standard gravity (9.80665 meters per second squared). This is equivalent to 101,325 Pascals (Pa) or 101.325 kilopascals (kPa).

On the other hand, 1 torr is defined as 1/760th of a standard atmosphere or 133.32 Pascals (Pa).

Therefore, 75.0 torr is equivalent to 0.0989 atm or 9,890 Pa. It is not equivalent to one standard atmosphere (atm).

8. 400.0 ml of a gas are under a pressure of 800.0 torr. what would the volume of the gas be at a pressure of 1000.0 torr?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_1 = \text{800.0 torr} \\ & V_1 = \text{400.0 mL} \\ & P_2 = \text{1000.0 torr} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final volume by using Boyle's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1V_1 & = P_2V_2 \\ P_2V_2 & = P_1V_1 \\ \frac{P_2V_2}{P_2} & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ V_2 & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ & = \frac{(\text{800.0 torr})(\text{400.0 mL})}{\text{1000.0 torr}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{320.0 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of the gas would be 320.0 mL.

17. a steel cylinder contains a mixture of nitrogen gas at 410 torr oxygen gas at 110 torr and helium gas at 232 torr what is the total pressure of the gas mixture?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_{\text{N}_2} = \text{410 torr} \\ & P_{\text{O}_2} = \text{110 torr} \\ & P_{\text{He}} = \text{232 torr} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the total pressure by using Dalton's law of partial pressures.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_{\text{T}} & = P_{\text{N}_2} + P_{\text{O}_2} + P_{\text{He}} \\ & = \text{410 torr + 110 torr + 232 torr} \\ & = \boxed{\text{752 torr}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the total pressure of the gas mixture is 752 torr.

18. Question 85(with solution)A spray can is used until it is empty except for the propellant gas, which has a pressure of 1344 torr at 23 °C. If the can is thrown into a fire (T=475 °C), what will be the pressure in the hot can? A) 4.28 x 103 torr B) 3.62 x 103 torr C) 3.40 x 103 torr D) 3.57 x 104 torr​


Solution:

According to Gay Lussac’s Law, the pressure of a given amount of gas is directly proportional to its temperature on the Kelvin scale when the volume is held constant. It can be described by the equation:

[tex]\dfrac{P_2}{P_1} =\dfrac{T_2}{T_1}[/tex]

In the case of a can with a propellant, the equation will take the form:

[tex]\dfrac{P_2}{1344} =\dfrac{475+273.15}{23+273.15}\\\\P_2=1344(2.526)\\\\P_2=3394.944\\\\\boxed{P_2=3.40\times 10^{3}\;\text{torr}}[/tex]

Thus, the answer is choice C.

Answer: C) 3.40 x 103 torr

21. If the pressure on 45 mL of gas is changed from 600. torr to 800. torr, the new volume will be


The new volume will be V₂=33.75 ml

Further explanation  

In Boyle's law, it is stated that  

In a closed system, the absolute pressure by an ideal gas is inversely proportional to the volume it occupies if the temperature is constant  

So it can be stated  

P₁V₁=P₂V₂

Given

V₁=45 ml

P₁=600 torr

P₂=800 torr

Required

The new volume,V₂

Solution

V₂=(P₁V₁)/P₂

V₂=(600 x 45)/800

V₂=33.75 ml

25. What volume will 40 ml of a gas at 710 Torrs occupy if the pressure is increased to 760 Torrs?

[tex]P_{1} = \text{710 torr}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{40 mL}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{760 torr}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}[/tex]
