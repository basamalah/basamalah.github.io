Organizational - encountered by institutions, business, or organizations in their decision-making process, at this level the dilemmas that the organizations’ experiences usually affect more than one person and they can be part of the internal group or part of an external stakeholder.

Structural - affect a network of institutions and operative theoretical paradigms like universal care, juvenile laws, and immigration. this type of dilemma can affect a community and even a society at large.

Answer:

In general, a structural dilemma is a problem that arises when there are conflicting goals or values within an organization or system. For example, a company may want to increase profits by reducing costs, but also wants to maintain high quality standards and employee satisfaction. Balancing these goals can be challenging and may require trade-offs.

In a business setting, structural dilemmas can be addressed by identifying the underlying causes and then developing strategies to address them. This may involve gathering data and feedback from stakeholders, analyzing options, and considering the long-term implications of different solutions.

Once a strategy has been identified, it can be implemented through effective communication, training, and monitoring. This may require collaboration and buy-in from all stakeholders involved in the decision-making process.

Ultimately, whether or not someone is happy with how they dealt with a structural dilemma depends on the outcome of the situation and whether or not the solution effectively balanced the competing goals and values involved.
