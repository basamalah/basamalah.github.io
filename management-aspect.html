15. what is the legal aspects of purchasing management? illutrate​


Legal Issues for Purchasing Management
A key management decision with significant legal ramifications is the level of delegated authority or discretion granted to purchasing staff - i.e. are they responsible for routine purchases only or do they control the acquisition of all the company's requirements? This will generally depend on the nature of the goods purchased, levels of available expertise and the degree of autonomy and centralisation of the purchasing function. Other factors to consider include the following:

Authority Limits

It may also be advisable to establish the types of commitment and pre-set financial limits that individual staff are permitted to enter into, with or without a counter-signature or approval from their manager.

Awareness of Contract Law

Purchasing staff should be made aware of the legal issues likely to arise routinely as part of their jobs, especially contract law, and in particular how and when the company may be legally bound and how contracts may be varied or terminated. Training in such areas should be considered.

Quality and Safety

As part of your risk management procedures, you should ensure that only reputable suppliers are selected; that such suppliers comply with their obligations to you under health and safety laws to provide and update safety information for substances and industrial products; and that they co-operate with you generally in your risk management and quality initiatives with respect to customers, employees and others. Such co-operation can be reinforced by including contractual obligations in your purchasing documentation.

Monitoring Supply Contracts

If you have numerous suppliers, it will be necessary to allocate responsibility for ensuring that purchase or performance levels; ordering requirements and deadlines; thresholds; contract dates; renewal, notice and termination dates and other contract parameters are monitored and complied with.

Letters of Intent/Memoranda of Understanding

It is common for goods or services to be supplied before formal contractual arrangements have been concluded to cover their supply. In such circumstances, one party may request a letter of intent to document the understanding reached about the supply of goods/services taking place before the formal contract is signed. Practically, such pre-contract supply may be unavoidable but such arrangements can be treacherous - advice should be taken as to the precise wording of letters of intent so that you do not become contractually committed before you mean to. For further guidance on use of Letters of Intent/Memoranda of Understanding, see our Memorandum of Understanding (Commercial Transactions) and Letter of Intent with Provision for Pre-Contract Services/Works template documents.

Tenders

If a substantial project is involved, you may consider seeking a competitive tender. An important consideration here is the legal status of a tender especially as to whether you are bound to accept the lowest tender. Going to tender may also mean that expert advice is necessary to assist in terms of drafting the tender specification and the comparison of the bids received which may well have been put together on differing bases.

17. Discussion Questions 1. What is the purpose of human resource management? What are its essential aspects? 2. How do the various aspects of human resource management contribute to ensuring efficiency and effectiveness of an organization? 3. In what way do the two aspects of human capital management (talent and knowledge management) complement each other?​


Answer:

1. What is the purpose of human resource management?

Answer: The purpose of Human Resource Management (HRM) is to maximize the effectiveness of an organization's most valuable asset—its people. HRM involves recruiting, training and developing employees, providing compensation and benefits, and motivating employees to reach their full potential. HRM also includes the management of employee relations, performance management, safety, and health. By effectively managing these areas, HRM helps organizations achieve their goals and objectives.

2. What are its essential aspects?

Answer- Quality Assurance: Quality assurance involves creating a system to ensure that products or services meet established standards of quality. Quality assurance involves processes such as conducting audits, measuring performance, testing, and training.

- Cost Control: Cost control is the process of monitoring, controlling, and reducing costs in order to maintain profitability. Cost control involves setting budgets, tracking spending, and making adjustments as needed.

- Risk Management: Risk management is the process of identifying, assessing, and managing potential risks to an organization. Risk management includes developing strategies to reduce or eliminate risks, as well as monitoring and assessing their effectiveness.

- Process Improvement: Process improvement involves analyzing, designing, and implementing effective and efficient procedures and processes to meet organizational objectives. This includes identifying areas of opportunity, creating new processes and procedures, and measuring performance.

- Performance Management: Performance management is the process of setting goals, evaluating progress, and providing feedback to ensure that goals are met. Performance management includes establishing performance measures, monitoring progress, and providing feedback and guidance.

3. How do the various aspects of human resource management contribute to ensuring efficiency and effectiveness of an organization?

Answer- Recruitment and Selection: Recruiting and selecting the right people for the right jobs is essential for ensuring efficiency and effectiveness in an organization. By ensuring that the right people are in the right positions, an organization can maximize the potential of its workforce and ensure that everyone is working towards a common goal.

- Training and Development: Training and development are also important for ensuring efficiency and effectiveness in an organization. By providing employees with the necessary skills and knowledge, organizations can ensure that employees are better equipped to do their jobs. This will in turn help them increase their productivity and enable the organization to be more efficient.

- Performance Management: Performance management is also essential for ensuring efficiency and effectiveness in an organization. By setting performance goals and providing feedback and support, managers can ensure that employees are working to the best of their abilities. This will help the organization to achieve its goals and objectives in a timely and cost effective manner.

- Compensation and Benefits: Compensation and benefits also play an important role in ensuring efficiency and effectiveness in an organization. By offering competitive salaries and benefits, organizations can attract and retain the best talent and ensure that their employees are motivated to do their best.

- Employee Relations: Employee relations are also essential for ensuring efficiency and effectiveness in an organization. By fostering a positive work environment and providing employees with the necessary resources and support, organizations can ensure that employees are engaged and motivated to achieve the organization’s goals.

4. In what way do the two aspects of human capital management (talent and knowledge management) complement each other?

Answer: Human capital management is the process of acquiring, developing, and retaining talented and knowledgeable employees. Talent and knowledge management work in tandem to ensure that an organization has a successful and productive workforce. Talent management focuses on hiring and developing employees while knowledge management focuses on capturing, storing, and leveraging employee knowledge. Together, these two aspects of human capital management help organizations create a strong and productive workforce by identifying, attracting, and retaining the right talent and leveraging the knowledge of employees to maximize their contributions.

26. explain each aspects of disaster management​


Answer:

Disaster management aims to reduce, or avoid, the potential losses from hazards, assure prompt and appropriate assistance to victims of disaster, and achieve rapid and effective recovery. The Disaster management cycle illustrates the ongoing process by which governments, businesses, and civil society plan for and reduce the impact of disasters, react during and immediately following a disaster, and take steps to recover after a disaster has occurred. Appropriate actions at all points in the cycle lead to greater preparedness, better warnings, reduced vulnerability or the prevention of disasters during the next iteration of the cycle. The complete disaster management cycle includes the shaping of public policies and plans that either modify the causes of disasters or mitigate their effects on people, property, and infrastructure.

Often phases of the cycle overlap and the length of each phase greatly depends on the severity of the disaster.

Mitigation - Minimizing the effects of disaster. ...

Preparedness - Planning how to respond. ...

Response - Efforts to minimize the hazards created by a disaster. ...

Recovery - Returning the community to normal.
