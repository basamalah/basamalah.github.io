5. Tiger woods hit a golf ball at a velocity of 80 m/s. the head of his club was in contact with the 50 g ball for 0.40 ms. what is the average force acting on the golf ball during impact?


To calculate the average force acting on the golf ball during impact, we can use the equation:

force = change in momentum / time

We can first calculate the change in momentum of the golf ball by using the formula:

momentum = mass x velocity

The mass of the golf ball is 50 g, or 0.05 kg. Therefore, the initial momentum of the golf ball is:

momentum = 0.05 kg x 80 m/s = 4.0 kg*m/s

Since the final velocity of the ball is 0, the final momentum is 0.

So the change in momentum is:

change in momentum = final momentum - initial momentum = 0 - 4.0 kgm/s = -4.0 kgm/s

The time of impact is 0.40 ms, or 0.0004 s. So the average force acting on the golf ball during impact is:

force = change in momentum / time = -4.0 kg*m/s / 0.0004 s = -10,000 N

So the average force acting on the golf ball during impact is -10,000 N which is a negative value because the force is acting opposite to the direction of motion.

[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The kinetic energy of a moving object is given by

[tex]\boxed{KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}}[/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = speed

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for KE

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}mv^{2}[/tex]

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}(\text{0.6 kg})(\text{2.0 m/s})^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{KE = \text{1.2 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the kinetic energy of the golf ball is 1.2 J.

23. A golf ball with a mass of 0.06 kg is subjected to an impulse that causes it to move with a velocity of 50 m/s. It the golf club and the ball are in contact for 0.00% see, what force acts on a ball?​


We can use the impulse-momentum theorem to solve this problem. The impulse-momentum theorem states that the impulse acting on an object is equal to the change in its momentum. Mathematically, it can be expressed as:

Impulse = Change in momentum

The impulse can be calculated as the product of the force and the time for which it acts. Mathematically, it can be expressed as:

Impulse = Force × Time

The change in momentum can be calculated as the product of the mass and the change in velocity. Mathematically, it can be expressed as:

Change in momentum = Mass × Change in velocity

Substituting these expressions in the impulse-momentum theorem, we get:

Force × Time = Mass × Change in velocity

Solving for force, we get:

Force = (Mass × Change in velocity) / Time

Given that the mass of the golf ball is 0.06 kg, the change in velocity is 50 m/s, and the time for which the force acts is 0.00% sec, which is equivalent to 0 seconds. Therefore, the force acting on the ball is:

Force = (0.06 kg × 50 m/s) / 0 seconds

Since we cannot divide by zero, the force acting on the ball cannot be determined with the given information. In reality, the contact time between the ball and the club is not zero, and it is important to consider this contact time when calculating the force acting on the ball.
