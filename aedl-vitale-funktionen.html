1. What is vital sign? What is the function of vital sign? What is the important of the four vital sign?


Answer:

What is vital sign?

Vital signs are a group of the four to six most important medical signs that indicate the status of the body’s vital functions. These measurements are taken to help assess the general physical health of a person, give clues to possible diseases, and show progress toward recovery.

What is the function of vital sign?

Vital signs reflect essential body functions, including your heartbeat, breathing rate, temperature, and blood pressure. Your health care provider may watch, measure, or monitor your vital signs to check your level of physical functioning.

What is the important of the four vital sign?

The four main vital signs routinely monitored by medical professionals and health care providers include the following:

Body temperature

Pulse rate

Respiration rate (rate of breathing)

Blood pressure (Blood pressure is not considered a vital sign, but is often measured along with the vital signs.)

25. what is vital signs?


What is Vital Signs?

Vital signs(Cardinal Signs) are objective guideposts that provide data to determine a person's state of health and somehow determines the internal functions of the body. It also reflects the body's physiologic status and provides information critical to evaluating homeostatic balance. There are four primary vital signs.

What are the Primary vital signs?

The four primary vital signs are:

1. Body Temperature

The purpose of measuring the body temperature is to know the baseline temperature of the patients and to monitor any abnormalities. The average normal body temperature is generally accepted as 98.6°F (37°C).

2. Heart rate or Pulse

Pulse is a wave of blood flow created by a contraction of the heart. Heart rate is the speed of the heartbeat measured by the number of contractions of the heart per minute.

3. Respiratory rate

Respiration is the act of breathing; it includes the intake of oxygen and the output of carbon dioxide. The respiratory rate is the rate at which breathing occurs. This is usually measured in breaths per minute and is set, and controlled by the respiratory center.

4. Blood pressure

Blood pressure is a measure of the force that your heart uses to pump blood around your body.
