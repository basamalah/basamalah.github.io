2. examples of proverbs expressing system of values


PROVERBS: SYSTEM OF VALUESWHAT ARE PROVERBS?

- Proverbs are basically a simple statement that is often rooted from tradition.

- They are often metaphorical and abstract as to give the person receiving the proverb space for thought.

- It encompasses many topics, but is usually used in situations wherein wisdom is required.

EXAMPLES:

1.) No man rules alone. (You can't rule alone)

2.) No man's an island. (Humans are dependent with one another)

3.) There's no use locking the cage, if the bird has already gone out. (There's no point reminiscing past mistakes)

4.) What you do, what they see. (Your actions will define you)

5.) Close your eyes if you seek peace. (The truth is painful, but ignorance is bliss).

6.) Make sure the lion won't bite when it comes out of the cage. (Think before you say)

7.) Money runs the world. (Money is a very crucial component in our world)

8.) No person lives without motivation. (All people have a goal, no matter how small it may be)

9.) Talent is gifted, skill is gained. (While talent may be inherited, skill can be obtained)

10.) Clutching at straws is a desperate man's resort to life. (You should not put yourself in a situation of desperation).
