8. why is spencer's educational though described as utilitarian?​


Spencer's educational view though describes as utilitarian.

Because, education of Spencer is closely related to normative ethical educational theory which dictates that kindness is an act that maximizes happiness and well-being for all affected individuals.

FURTHER EXPLANATION:

Utilitarianism is a normative ethical theory that dictates that kindness is an act that maximizes happiness and well-being for all affected individuals.

Although there are forms of utilitarianism with different characterizations, the basic idea of ​​ethical utilitarianism is to maximize utility, or what is often defined by the term welfare. Jeremy Bentham, founder of utilitarianism, defines utility as "a character in any object that produces benefit, gain, pleasure, good, or happiness ... [or] to prevent harm, pain, evil, or unhappiness to those whose interests are considered ."

Utilitarianism is a version of consequentialism, an ethical theory that holds that the consequences of an action are the only standard by which to judge right and wrong. Unlike other forms of consequentialism, such as egoism and altruism, utilitarianism considers the interests of all human beings to be equal. Proponents of utilitarianism differ on several issues, such as whether an action should be chosen based on the likelihood of the outcome (action utilitarianism), or whether an agent should act according to utility-maximizing rules (rule utilitarianism). There is also a difference of opinion as to whether the total utility (total utilitarianism), the average utility or the utility of the poorest people should be maximized.

Spencer became one of the main proponents of modern curriculum theory. He created a stir in England with his curriculum theory as the main focus of education continued to be Latin and Greek and literature. In his work “What Knowledge is Most Valuable?” Spencer states that this question needs to be answered before any curriculum is chosen or any instruction begins. Once this question is answered, it must be ensured that the curriculum aids in advancing survival and progress.

To achieve this progress Spencer believed that five activities were needed in the curriculum. These activities assist self-preservation, job performance, child-rearing, social and political participation, recreation and recreation. Again, the main goal is to teach subjects that will contribute to a successful life.

Education today continues to be influenced by Spencer's Social Darwinist theory. In fact, the curriculum activities based on human needs are still implemented in one form or another.

Answer:

A. KARL MARX

Explanation:

A 19th century political philosopher who led the development of communism as a school of thought in economics

Answer:

A. Karl Marx

Explanation:

first purported by Karl Marx, is a theory that society is in a state of perpetual conflict because of competition for limited resources. Conflict theory holds that social order is maintained by domination and power, rather than by consensus and conformity. According to conflict theory, those with wealth and power try to hold on to it by any means possible, chiefly by suppressing the poor and powerless. A basic premise of conflict theory is that individuals and groups within society will work to try to maximize their own wealth and power.
