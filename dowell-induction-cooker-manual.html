Answer:

An induction cooker passes electrical energy into a metal vessel that has to be ferromagnetic by induction from a coil of wire. Under the cooking surface, the coil is mounted, and a high frequency alternating current is passed through it (e.g. 24 kHz). A complex magnetic field is generated by the current in the coil.

Answer:

An induction cooktop uses an electromagnetic field to heat food. When the cooktop is turned on, an electric current passes through a coil of metal, creating a magnetic field. This magnetic field then penetrates the metal of a cooking pan, inducing a current in the pan

28. Ben used (x + 4) cm and 8 cm dowel (diagonals) to build a kite. If its area is 96 cm2, how long is the other dowel? ​


Answer:

the area of a kite is given by:

Area = (diagonal1 * diagonal2)/2

We are given that one diagonal is 8 cm and the area is 96 cm^2.

So, substituting these values in the above formula, we get:

96 = (8 * diagonal2)/2

Simplifying, we get:

96 = 4 * diagonal2

diagonal2 = 96/4

diagonal2 = 24 cm

The sum of the lengths of the two diagonals is equal to the sum of the lengths of the two dowels used to build the kite.

So, we have:

diagonal1 + diagonal2 = (x + 4) + (24)

Substituting diagonal1 = 8 and diagonal2 = 24, we get:

8 + 24 = x + 4

32 = x + 4

x = 28 cm

Therefore, the length of the other dowel is x = 28 cm
