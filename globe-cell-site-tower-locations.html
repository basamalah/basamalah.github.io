28. A particular cell phone tower is designed to service a 12-mile radius. The tower is located at (-3,5) on a coordinate plane whose units represent miles. What is the general equation of the outer boundary of the region serviced by the tower


x²+6x+y²-10y-110=0

Step-by-step explanation:

This is the equation of a circle. Center is the tower, located at (-3, 5)

Standard form of the equation (plug in the values):

(x-h)² + (y-k)² = r²

(x+3)² + (y-5)² = 144

The general form of the equation of a circle is given by: x²+y²+2hx+2ky+c=0 (don't be confused by c; this just represents a constant number)

So your answer should look something like this: x²+y²+x+y+number=0

Now, translate into general equation by expanding:

(x+3)(x+3) + (y-5)(y-5) = 144

x²+6x+9+y²-10y+25 = 144    

(now transpose 144 to equate everything to 0, and then combine like terms)

x²+6x+9+y²-10y+25-144 = 0  

x²+6x+y²-10y-110=0
