DC generator

20. What is the nature of the force responsible in the operation of a dc motor?


The basic working principle of a DC motor is: "whenever a current carrying conductor is placed in a magnetic field, it experiences a mechanical force"

In theory, a DC motor is an electric motor that requires a supply of direct current or DC (Direct Current) voltage to the field coil to be converted into mechanical energy. The field coil on the motor is called the stator, and the armature coil is called the rotor.

DC Motor Parts

DC motors have 3 main parts or components to be able to rotate as follows:

-. Field pole = Usually a simple DC motor only has two field poles, namely the north pole and the south pole.

-. Dinamo = Dynamo in the form of a cylinder, connected to the driving axle to move the load. In the case of a small DC motor, the armature rotates in the magnetic field created by the poles, until the north and south magnetic poles change locations.

-. Commutator = This component is mainly found in DC motors. Its purpose is to transmit current between the armature and the power source.

The main advantage of this DC motor is speed control, which does not affect the quality of the power supply. This motor can be controlled by adjusting the dynamo voltage or field current.
