2. can you summary the legend of black rice?​


Answer:

This is a legend often narrated by the old folks living in the town of Naujan in the island of Mindoro.

According to them, during the early Spanish times, many merchandises flowing to the country were brought in by the huge galleons coming all the way from Mexico. On their return voyage to Mexico, they would in turn ship out locally-made goods such as copra, tobacco and spices out of the country.

Up to now, if one were to go to Naujan, Mindoro once could still gather of these black rice along the shore which tastes sweeter and yummier that oridinary white rice.

8. How is the Turban Legend is titled "Turban Legend"?


Urban legends are modern forms of folklore formed within the context of modern society. 

A turban legend, refers to a person of Middle Eastern descent who displays a type of "legendary" comprehension of subject matters. People who live in the Middle East are known for their use of the turban which they wrap around their head, thus the term "Turban legend"
