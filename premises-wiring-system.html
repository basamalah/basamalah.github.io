Explanation:

On premise catering is when food is prepared and served at the physical location of a certain catering service. This could be a restaurant or a hotel where people are gathered for an event. Off premise catering happens when a caterer prepares food on their premises and have it transported by food trucks to the location of an event where it is served to guests. 13. describe the basic premise of factor endowment theory. how it different from the premise of recardian theory​


Answer:

Factor endowment theory is an economic theory that suggests that a country's economic development is determined by its relative abundance or scarcity of certain factors of production, such as land, labor, and capital. According to this theory, countries that are relatively abundant in certain factors of production will tend to specialize in the production of goods that use those factors intensively, while countries that are relatively scarce in certain factors will tend to specialize in the production of goods that use those factors less intensively.

Ricardian theory, on the other hand, is an economic theory developed by David Ricardo in the early 19th century. It suggests that a country's comparative advantage in trade is determined by its relative productivity in producing different goods. According to this theory, a country will tend to export the goods it can produce relatively efficiently, and import the goods it produces relatively inefficiently. The theory of comparative advantage is the main insight from Ricardian theory

The main difference between factor endowment theory and Ricardian theory is that the former emphasizes the role of a country's relative abundance or scarcity of factors of production in determining its economic development, while the latter emphasizes the role of a country's relative productivity in determining its comparative advantage in trade.
