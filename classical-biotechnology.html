Importance of biotechnology to the following:

A. Health- In human health care, biotechnology products include quicker and more accurate diagnostic tests, therapies with fewer side effects and new and safer vaccines. We can now detect many diseases and medical conditions more quickly and with greater accuracy because of new, biotechnology-based diagnostic tools.

B. Agriculture- Biotechnology is being used to address problems in all areas of agricultural production and processing. This includes plant breeding to raise and stabilize yields; to improve resistance to pests, diseases and abiotic stresses such as drought and cold; and to enhance the nutritional content of foods.

Modern biotechnology is a term adopted by international convention to refer to biotechnological techniques for the manipulation of genetic material and the fusion of cells beyond normal breeding barriers.

22. Describe the "old" and "classical" biotechnology and enumerate at least 5 products


The old and classical biotechnology is the outdated way of engineering new products from raw materials for the usage of humans. It is also defined as the traditional way of improving the lives of people by utilizing biological processes to produce products. To enumerate at least 5 products in early forms of biotechnology, let’s focus on agriculture. The cultivation of plants like rice, wheat and barley were well-maintained for those were good and reliable sources of food. The domestication of animals, on the other hand, produced meat and milk. Bread and dairy products like cheese and yogurt were also produced by utilizing microorganisms. When the process of fermentation was discovered by Louis Pasteur, examples of industrial biotechnology products like beer, wine and alcohol were produced.

More old biotechnology examples were the traditional medicines used in earlier times to treat some ailments. First is soybean curd which was used to treat boils in ancient China. Next is honey, which was believed to contain several antimicrobial compounds so it was used to treat some respiratory problems and as balm for wounds to aid in healing. Ukrainians, on the other hand, use moldy cheese to treat infected wounds. After several years, Alexander Fleming discovered penicillin – the first antibiotics that came from mold.

Other list of industrial biotechnology products were paint solvent, acetone, sugar, detergents and fabrics.
