6. how will you apply ethical guidelines in making good assessment result in experiment or research? essay


Answer:

Ethical guidelines play a crucial role in ensuring that assessments and research are conducted in a responsible and ethical manner. When designing and conducting experiments or research, it is important to consider ethical guidelines to ensure that the study is conducted with integrity, fairness, and respect for human dignity.

One of the key ethical guidelines that should be followed when conducting research is informed consent. This means that participants should be fully informed about the purpose and procedures of the study, including any potential risks or benefits, and be given the opportunity to voluntarily agree or decline participation. Researchers should ensure that participants are aware that they can withdraw from the study at any time without penalty.

Another important ethical guideline is confidentiality. Researchers should ensure that the privacy and anonymity of participants are protected throughout the study. Data collected from participants should be kept confidential and shared only with those who have a legitimate need to access it.

In addition, researchers should ensure that their study does not cause harm to participants, physically or psychologically. If there are any potential risks involved, researchers must take appropriate measures to minimize those risks and ensure the safety of the participants.

Finally, ethical guidelines dictate that researchers should not engage in any fraudulent or deceptive practices, such as misrepresenting data or plagiarizing the work of others. Any conflicts of interest should be disclosed, and researchers should be transparent in their methods and findings.

In conclusion, applying ethical guidelines in making good assessment results in experiments or research is essential for ensuring the integrity, fairness, and respect for human dignity of the study. By following ethical guidelines such as informed consent, confidentiality, minimizing risks, and avoiding fraudulent practices, researchers can conduct their studies in a responsible and ethical manner, leading to reliable and trustworthy results.

9. How can a formative assessment be ensured the ethical use of the assessments and the assessments data to identify a student's strengths and needs?


Answer:

Educational assessment occurs in two major contexts. The first is the classroom. Here assessment is used by teachers and students mainly to assist learning, but also to gauge students’ summative achievement over the longer term. Second is large-scale assessment, used by policymakers and educational leaders to evaluate programs and/or obtain information about whether individual students have met learning goals.

The sharp contrast that typically exists between classroom and large-scale assessment practices arises because assessment designers have not been able to fulfill the purposes of different assessment users with the same data and analyses. To guide instruction and monitor its effects, teachers need information that is intimately connected with the work their students are doing, and they interpret this evidence in light of everything else they know about their students and the conditions of instruction. Part of the power of classroom assessment resides in these connections. Yet precisely because they are individualized and highly contextualized, neither the rationale nor the results of typical classroom assessments are easily communicated beyond the classroom. Large-scale, standardized tests do communicate efficiently across time and place, but by so constraining the content and timeliness of the message that they often have little utility in the classroom. This contrast illustrates the more general point that one size of assessment does not fit all. The purpose of an assessment determines priorities, and the context of use imposes constraints on the design, thereby affecting the kinds of information a particular assessment can provide about student achievement.

Assessment in Practice:

Although assessments are currently used for many purposes in the educational system, a premise of this report is that their effectiveness and utility must ultimately be judged by the extent to which they promote student learning. The aim of assessment should be “to educate and improve student performance, not merely to audit it” (Wiggins, 1998, p.7). To this end, people should gain important and useful information from every assessment situation. In education, as in other professions, good decision-making depends on access to relevant, accurate, and timely information. Furthermore, the information gained should be put to good use by informing decisions about curriculum and instruction and ultimately improving student learning (Falk, 2000; National Council of Teachers of Mathematics, 1995).

Assessments do not function in isolation; an assessment’s effectiveness in improving learning depends on its relationships to curriculum and instruction. Ideally, instruction is faithful and effective in relation to curriculum, and assessment reflects curriculum in such a way that it reinforces the best practices in instruction. In actuality, however, the relationships among assessment, curriculum, and instruction are not always ideal. Often assessment taps only a subset of curriculum and without regard to instruction, and can narrow and distort instruction in unintended ways (Klein, Hamilton, McCaffrey, and Stecher, 2000; Koretz and Barron, 1998; Linn, 2000; National Research Council [NRC], 1999b). In this chapter we expand on the idea, introduced in Chapter 2, that synergy can best be achieved if the three parts of the system are bound by or grow out of a shared knowledge base about cognition and learning in the domain.

Answer:

If I were to put up my own business, the following values would be incorporated in my company's code of ethics:

Integrity: Employees should always act with honesty, transparency, and consistency in all business dealings.

Respect: Employees should treat each other, clients, and partners with respect, regardless of differences in gender, race, age, religion, or social status.

Responsibility: Employees should take full accountability for their actions, and be proactive in addressing any issues that arise.

Professionalism: Employees should maintain a high level of professionalism in their conduct, dress, and language, in order to represent the company in the best possible light.

Innovation: Employees should be encouraged to come up with creative and innovative solutions to business problems, and to constantly improve the products and services offered by the company.

Fairness: Employees should be treated fairly and equitably, with no discrimination based on gender, race, age, religion, or social status.

Environmental Responsibility: The company should strive to minimize its environmental impact, and to promote sustainability in all business operations.

Community Engagement: The company should actively engage with the local community, and contribute to social and economic development initiatives.

These values will help to guide employees in their daily conduct and decision-making, and will ensure that the business is run in an ethical and socially responsible manner.

24. how will you apply ethical guidelines in making good assessment result in experiment or research? essay


When conducting an experiment or research, it is essential to ensure that ethical guidelines are followed to produce reliable and accurate assessment results. Ethical guidelines are a set of standards that are put in place to ensure that research and experiments are conducted in a responsible and ethical manner.

One of the ethical guidelines that should be applied in making good assessment results is the principle of informed consent. This principle requires that participants be given adequate information about the research or experiment, and their rights and privacy be respected. The participants must also give their voluntary and informed consent to participate in the study or experiment.

Another important ethical guideline is confidentiality. Researchers must ensure that the information obtained from participants is kept confidential, and the data collected should be used only for the purpose of the research or experiment. The results should be reported in a manner that does not identify any individual participants, unless explicit permission is obtained from them.

Researchers should also ensure that the participants are not subjected to any harm during the course of the research or experiment. Any potential risks or harm should be identified and minimized. In addition, any benefits that may accrue to the participants should be explained to them.

Finally, researchers should ensure that their work is conducted with integrity and honesty. Data should be collected and reported accurately and truthfully, without any manipulation or falsification. Any conflicts of interest should be disclosed, and the results should be presented in a transparent manner.

In conclusion, the application of ethical guidelines in conducting experiments or research is essential to producing good assessment results. Researchers must ensure that they follow the principles of informed consent, confidentiality, minimizing harm, and conducting their work with integrity and honesty. By doing so, researchers can ensure that their work is responsible, ethical, and contributes to the advancement of knowledge.
