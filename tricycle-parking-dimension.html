3. There are 30 tricycles and cars parked outside a shopping mall. There are a total of 98 wheels altogether. How many tricycles are there?


Answer:

22 cars

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's assume that each tricycle has 3 wheels and each car has 4 wheels.

Let T be the number of tricycles and C be the number of cars. We know that:

T + C = 30 ... (1) (the total number of vehicles)

3T + 4C = 98 ... (2) (the total number of wheels)

To solve this system of equations, we can use substitution. We can solve equation (1) for C in terms of T:

C = 30 - T

Then we can substitute this expression for C into equation (2):

3T + 4(30 - T) = 98

Simplifying and solving for T, we get:

T = 8

Therefore, there are 8 tricycles parked outside the shopping mall. To find the number of cars, we can substitute T = 8 into equation (1):

C = 30 - T = 30 - 8 = 22

So there are 22 cars parked outside the shopping mall.

22. There are bicycles and tricycles in the parking lot. There are 34 wheels and 14 drivers. How many bicycles are there??


Applying the algebraic process, assume that bicycles are b and tricycles are t.

Since there are two wheels on a bicycle and three on the tricycle,

2b+3t=34 wheels.

b+t=14 drivers

Multiplying the second equation to 2, 2b +2t=28.

Comparing it to the first equation and subtracting from it, we will arrive to:

(2b + 3t)-(2b+2t)=t=34-28=6. There are 6 tricycles.

We are looking for bicycles, so simply substitute it to any equation.

b+6=14, b=8.

There are 8 bicycles.
