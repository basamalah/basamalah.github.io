17. do you think it was the companys fault that the women who used their product became affected by such side effects why/why not?​


Answer:

It's difficult to determine fault without more specific information about the company and the product in question. Generally, companies have a responsibility to ensure that their products are safe and to provide adequate warnings about any potential risks or side effects. If a company failed to adequately test their product, provide adequate warnings, or otherwise acted negligently, they may be at fault for any harm caused by the product. On the other hand, if the product was thoroughly tested and warnings were provided, the responsibility may lie with the individual who used the product or with other factors such as pre-existing health conditions. Ultimately, the determination of fault would depend on a thorough investigation and evaluation of the facts and circumstances surrounding the use of the product.
