7. it is to observe the feast days of patron saints.​


A saint’s feast day can be the day of their actual death or a day assigned by the Church. Typically, the Church only assigns a day when the day of death is unknown or if several other saints are already assigned to that day. The number of canonized saints, however, is greater than the number of days in a calendar year. So two or more saints often share the same feast day. Because overlap often occurs, and the Church isn’t sure of the date of death of some saints, other calendar dates are sometimes chosen — such as the day that the saint was canonized.

29. Write at least 10 festivals in the Philippines. It's origin, Month of Celebration and the Saint/Patron of the festival.​


- Sinulog Festival: Celebrated every third Sunday of January in Cebu City to honor the Santo Niño (Child Jesus). It features colorful street dancing and processions.- Ati-Atihan Festival: Celebrated every third week of January in Kalibo, Aklan to honor the Santo Niño. It features people wearing tribal costumes and faces paint and dancing to drum beats.- Dinagyang Festival: Celebrated every fourth Sunday of January in Iloilo City to honor the Santo Niño. It features people dressed as Ati warriors, performing dances and rituals.- Panagbenga Festival: Celebrated every February in Baguio City to celebrate the season of blooming flowers. It features floral floats, street dancing, and garden shows.- Moriones Festival: Celebrated during Holy Week (March or April) in Marinduque to commemorate the passion of Christ. It features people wearing masks and costumes of Roman soldiers (moriones), reenacting scenes from the Bible.- Pahiyas Festival: Celebrated every 15th of May in Lucban, Quezon to honor San Isidro Labrador (Saint Isidore the Laborer), the patron saint of farmers. It features houses decorated with colorful rice wafers (kiping), fruits and vegetables.- MassKara Festival: Celebrated every fourth Sunday of October in Bacolod City to showcase the city's resilience and optimism. It features people wearing smiling masks, street dancing and carnivals.- Higantes Festival: Celebrated every 23rd of November in Angono, Rizal to honor San Clemente (Saint Clement), the patron saint of fishermen. It features giant paper-mache figures (higantes), parading along with a procession of boats.- Giant Lantern Festival: Celebrated every Saturday before Christmas Eve (December) in San Fernando, Pampanga to showcase the city's craftsmanship and creativity. It features vast lanterns made of colorful lights, competing for the best design.- Sinukwan Festival: Celebrated every first week of December in San Fernando, Pampanga to honor Aring Sinukwan (King Sinukwan), a legendary Kapampangan hero. It features cultural performances, street dancing and pyrotechnics.
