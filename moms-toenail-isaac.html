12. what is an ingrown toenail? how it happens?​


What is an ingrown toenail? How it happens?​

What is an ingrown toenail? Ingrown toenails appear when the corner or side of a toenail develops into the delicate skin of the foot. Pain, redness, swelling, and, in certain cases, infection are the outcome. The big toe is typically affected by ingrown toenails.

Ingrown toenails are usually treatable on your own. If the pain is severe or spreading, your doctor can help you reduce the agony and avoid ingrown toenail issues.

Ingrown toenails are more likely to create issues if you have diabetes or another illness that causes inadequate blood flow to your feet.

If you have diabetes, which can cause poor blood flow and nerve damage in your feet, complications can be extremely severe. A small foot injury, such as a cut, scrape, corn, callus, or ingrown toenail, may thus fail to heal correctly and get infected. To avoid tissue deterioration and death, a difficult-to-heal open sore (foot ulcer) may require surgery (gangrene). An halt in blood flow to a part of your body causes gangrene.
