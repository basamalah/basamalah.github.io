Problem:In the equation x²/4 - y²/1 = 1 look for the following components of a hyperbola.

a. Length of the transverse axis

b. Coordinates of the vertices

c. Length of the conjugate axis

d. Coordinates of the co-vertices

e. Distance between the foci

[tex]\[\frac{{{x^2}}}{4} + {y^2} = 1\][/tex]

center is at origin (0, 0)

a. Length of the transverse axis = 4

a = 2

2a = 4

b. Coordinates of the vertices

Vertices(a, 0), (-a, 0)

Vertices(2, 0), (-2, 0)

c. Length of the conjugate axis = 2

b = 1

2b = 2

d. Coordinates of the co-vertices

Co-vertices(0, b), (0, -b)

Co-vertices(0, 1), (0, -1)

e. Distance between the foci

c² = a² + b²

c² = 2² + 1²

c² = 4 + 1

c² = 5

c = √5

distance = 2c

distance = 2(√5)
