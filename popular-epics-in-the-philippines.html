24. list of epics in philippines


Centuries before the Spaniards came, the Filipinos already had their own cultural traditions, folklore, mythologies and epics. They are the folk epics, transferred from one generation to the next through oral tradition, with the use of singers or chanters. These singers or chanters were often high priests or priestesses in their tribes. These epics were a great source of entertainment and inspiration especially to the youth. In Mindanao, they would call the epic singer, "Onor." Some of the epics, because of their length, would take the singer or chanter seven nights of singing or chanting to finish. The singer or chanter, may either be a male or a female, would sing for minimum of two hours to six hours every night. These events were often festal and recreational occasions like wedding ceremonies, wakes, prestige rites, peace pact agreements, when an ancestor's bones are dug out to be blessed, harvest seasons, when a wild boar is caught, or to welcome a guest, coupled with feasts for the community, supported by either the head of the tribe or a rich and prominent family within the community. The epics were later on transcribed and preserved by rich families as family heirloom.

Influences and Similarities of India before to the Philippines

(Last Blog for this week)

For this Blog I am going to talk about the similarities and influences of India in ancient past to the Philippines Culture, Literature and traditions. India has influenced the Philippines in many different ways. They have influenced us in terms of food, religion, literature, culture and many more. Hinduism came to Philippines brought by the Javanese Empire of Majapahit, but unluckily it declined. It declined because of the arrival of Islam in the country. Why talk about Hinduism? Hinduism plays an important role in the influence of India’s Culture and Literature to the Philippines. Hinduism has influenced our country’s culture, beliefs, philosophy and even influenced us economical. But today Hinduism is in the minor part of the religion of this country. But even if Hinduism declined over centuries, Indian culture and literature has traces in Filipino life especially in our ideas, languages and the arts and literature of our country, the Philippines.

First, I would like to point out that India has influenced us in terms of Food. We Filipinos adopted from India the cooking of curry especially chicken Curry. But aside from that, India’s culture and Literature has influenced our National Psyche. Even though Hinduism declined in our country, beliefs and other culture of India was still in the minds of the Filipinos. One example is “Karma”. We Filipinos believe in “karma” which originally one of India’s belief. For us, we concentrate more on the negative side of “Karma” which is, whatever you do wrong, something wrong will happen to you. But for India, it is also the same but they also emphasize on, whatever you do good, it will also happen to you.
