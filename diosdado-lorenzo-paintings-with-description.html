Noong 1636, nasangkot ang kanyang pangalan sa isang krimeng naganap sa Maynila. Kayat siya’y pinaghanap ng pulisya upang litisin. Gumawa siya ng paraan para tumakas sa Pilipinas at sumama sa apat na misyonerong Dominico at isang Hapon. Nagtungo sila sa Okinawa sa bansang Hapon. Ang tangi niyang dahilan sa pagtakas ay dahil sa may nakaaway siyang kastila. Ganoon pa man, siya’y dinakip at ibinilanggo ng mga awtoridad ng Hapon.

Matinding hirap ang dinanas ni Lorenzo Ruiz sa kamay ng mga tagausig na Hapon dahil ipinakita niya ang matatag niyang pananampalataya sa Panginoon. Sinabi niyang walang kailangan kung ihandog man niya ang nalalabi niyang buhay sa Diyos.

Noong 1637, ibinitin siya nang patiwarik, at kalahati ng kanyang katawan ay ibinaon sa hukay hanggang sa makitil ang kanyang buhay sa Burol ng Nishizaka. Pumanaw siya bilang tunay na martir ng pananampalatayang Kristiyano.

Bago ang kanyang kamatayan, kanyang sinabi: “Ako ay isang Kristiyano, at ito ay aking ipahahayag hanggang sa oras ng aking pagpanaw at para sa Panginoon ibubuwis ko ang aking buhay. Kahit na hindi ako nagtungo sa bansang Hapon para maging martir, gayon pa man, bilang isang Kristiyano at para sa Diyos ibibigay ko ang aking buhay.”

Si San Lorenzo Ruiz ay hindi isang Obispo, Pari o Dyakono kundi siya ay isa lang Layko o ordinaryong tao at ganoon din ang ating Pangalawang Santo na Pilipino na si San Pedro Calungsod na kamakailan lang ay ipinahayag na maging Santo ni Papa Benedicto XVI noong ika-21 ng Oktubre, 2012. Ibig sabihin tayong mga sumasampalataya ay tinawag ng Panginoon upang maging banal ngunit sa halimbawa ng ating dalawang pilipinong santo, tayo ay hindi na kailangan maging Pari o Obispo o kahit maging Santo Papa, Madre para sa mga kababaihan upang maging santo. Sina San Lorenzo Ruiz at Pedro Calungsod ay isang ehemplo na dapat parisan nating mga pilipinong kristiyano.

Noong Pebrero 1981, dumalaw sa ating bansa si Beato Papa Juan Pablo II na malapit na rin na maging santo. Ang pinakatampok na gawain niya ay naganap sa Luneta, sa isang solemne at makabagbag-damdaming seremonya ay ipinahayag ng Papa na si Lorenzo at ang kasamang 15 na iba pang martir ay “Beato” (Blessed). Answer:

1.The Basilica di San Lorenzo (Basilica of St Lawrence) is one of the largest churches of Florence, Italy, situated at the centre of the city's main market district, and the burial place of all the principal members of the Medici family from Cosimo il Vecchio to Cosimo III.

2.The Church of the Gesù is the Roman Catholic church of the Ateneo de Manila University campus in Quezon City in the Philippines. The landmark was designed by Jose Pedro Recio and Carmelo Casas.

3.The Sistine Chapel is a rectangular brick building with six arched windows on each of the two main (or side) walls and a barrel-vaulted ceiling. ... The chapel's exterior is drab and unadorned, but its interior walls and ceiling are decorated with frescoes by many Florentine Renaissance masters.

4.Santa Susana is a former railroad town located mostly within the City of Simi Valley. A small portion of the community, outside the Simi Valley city limits to the south of the Ventura County Metrolink rail line, is an unincorporated area and census-designated place (CDP).

5.Peter's Basilica, present basilica of St. Peter in Vatican City (an enclave in Rome), begun by Pope Julius II in 1506 and completed in 1615 under Paul V. It is designed as a three-aisled Latin cross with a dome at the crossing, directly above the high altar, which covers the shrine of St. Peter the Apostle.

17. who is Lorenzo monaco ?


Answer:

Lorenzo Monaco (born Piero di Giovanni; c. 1370 – c. 1425) was an Italian painter of the late Gothic-early Renaissance age. He was born Piero di Giovanni in Siena.

In 1390 he joined the Camaldolese monastery of Santa Maria degli Angeli. He was thenceforth generally known as Lorenzo Monaco (English: "Lawrence the Monk").

In 1414 he painted the Coronation of the Virgin (now at the Uffizi), characterized by a great number of saints and brilliant colours.

CARAVAGIO↓

Caravaggio (byname of Michelangelo Merisi) was a leading Italian painter of the late 16th and early 17th centuries who became famous for the intense and unsettling realism of his large-scale religious works as well as for his violent exploits—he committed murder—and volatile character.

GIAN LORENZO REMINI↓

PETER PAUL RUBENS↓

Peter Paul Rubens is famous for his inventive and dynamic paintings of religious and mythological subjects, though he also painted portraits and landscapes. He is regarded as one of the greatest painters of the 17th-century Baroque period.

REMBRANDT↓

Rembrandt is also known as a painter of light and shade and as an artist who favoured an uncompromising realism that would lead some critics to claim that he preferred ugliness to beauty. Early in his career and for some time, Rembrandt painted mainly portraits.

DIEGO VELASQUEZ↓

Diego Velázquez was one of the most important Spanish painters of the 17th century, a giant of Western art. He had a keen eye and a prodigious facility with the brush. His works often show strong modeling and sharp contrasts of light, resembling the dramatic lighting technique called tenebrism.

25. Write a reflection of Saint Lorenzo​


Saint Lorenzo was a Christian deacon and martyr who lived in the 3rd century AD. He is venerated as a saint by the Catholic and Orthodox Churches.

According to tradition, Saint Lorenzo was serving as a deacon in Rome during the persecution of Christians under the Roman Emperor Valerian. He was arrested and sentenced to death by being roasted on a gridiron, which became one of his most commonly depicted symbols.

Saint Lorenzo is remembered for his bravery and faith in the face of persecution and suffering. His feast day is celebrated on August 10th, and he is the patron saint of cooks and librarians.

His legacy has inspired many works of art, including paintings, sculptures, and stained glass windows. He serves as a reminder of the importance of standing up for one's beliefs and the power of faith in difficult times.
