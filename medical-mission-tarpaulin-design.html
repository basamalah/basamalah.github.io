20. If 30 soldiers represent 1/7 of a battalion, in medical mission, how many did not participate?​


Answer:

There are 180 soldiers of the battalion who did not participate in the medical mission.

Solutions:

Let N be the total number of soldiers.

Given: 30 - number of soldiers who went to the medical mission

           [tex]\frac{1}{7}[/tex] - portion of the battalion in the medical mission

Equation: [tex]\frac{1}{7}[/tex]N = 30

Answer: [tex]\frac{1}{7}[/tex]N = 30

              [tex]\frac{1}{7}[/tex]N = 30

              [tex]\frac{1}{7}[/tex]       [tex]\frac{1}{7}[/tex]

               N = (30)(7)

                          1

               N = 210

Therefore, there are 210 soldiers in the battalion.

Find the number of soldiers who did not participate in the medical mission.

210 - 30 = N

180 = N

Therefore, there are 180 soldiers of the battalion who did not participate in the medical mission.
