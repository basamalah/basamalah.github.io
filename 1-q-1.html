2. whats the answer and solution of (q+1)(q-1)?​


Answer: q2-1

Explanation: (q+1)(q-1)

This could further be expanded to q(q-1)+1(q-1)

Which could be simplified to q2-q+q-1

And this leads you to q2-1

Answer: q^2 - 1

USE THE FOIL METHOD(First, Outside, Inside, Last)

In the problem: (q+1)(q-1)

Multiply the first terms which are q & q that will be q * q = q^2;

Next, multiply the outside terms which are q & -1 that will be q * -1 = -q;

After that, multiply the inside terms which are 1 & q that will be 1 * q = q;

Then, multiply the last terms which are 1 & -1 that will be 1 * -1 = -1

After multiplying, gather all the terms in 1 polynomial expression:

q^2 - q + q - 1

Lastly, combine the like terms.

q^2 (-q + q) - 1 = q^2 - 1

Therefore, the final answer is q^2 - 1

PS: The symbol "^" means to the power of. Therefore, q^2 means q to the power of 2.

8. if P(x)=3x3-4x2+5 and Q(x)=x3-5x-1,find P(1)+Q(1).​


The answer is -1.

Given the two polynomial functions

[tex]\begin{cases} P(x)=3x^3-4x^2+5\\Q(x)=x^3-5x-1\end{cases}[/tex]

Substituting [tex]x=1[/tex] to the functions yields the required sum:

[tex]\begin{gathered} P(1)+Q(1)=\underbrace{[3(1)^3-4(1)^2+5]}_{P(1)}+\underbrace{[1^3-5(1)-1]}_{Q(1)} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]P(1)+Q(1)=(3-4+5)+(1-5-1)[/tex]

[tex]P(1)+Q(1)=4-5=\boxed{-1}[/tex]

Thus, the required value is -1.
