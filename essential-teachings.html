Answer:

The Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) track is a crucial part of the K-12 curriculum in the Philippines, aimed at preparing students for gainful employment in various industries. One of the most popular TVL courses is the tourism track, which is designed to provide students with the necessary knowledge and skills to succeed in the tourism industry.

To achieve this goal, innovative teaching methods and techniques are essential. According to various studies, there is a need to move away from traditional teaching methods, which often rely on lectures and textbooks, and towards more active and engaging learning strategies.

One innovative approach that has been found to be effective in teaching essential skills to TVL tourism students is the use of simulation-based learning. This approach involves creating realistic scenarios that allow students to practice and develop their skills in a safe and controlled environment. For example, students can be given a task to organize a tour for a group of tourists, and they will be required to make all the necessary arrangements, such as booking transportation, accommodation, and activities.

Another approach is the integration of technology into the classroom. Technology has the potential to enhance the learning experience by providing interactive and multimedia-rich content that can engage and motivate students. For example, virtual reality (VR) technology can be used to provide students with an immersive and realistic experience of various tourist destinations.

Moreover, collaboration and teamwork are essential skills in the tourism industry. Therefore, teachers can incorporate group projects and activities that encourage students to work together towards a common goal. This approach not only develops teamwork skills but also fosters creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving abilities.

In conclusion, finding innovative ways to teach essential skills to TVL tourism students is essential for their success in the industry. Simulation-based learning, technology integration, and collaborative learning are some of the effective approaches that can be adopted by teachers to make learning more engaging, interactive, and effective.

Teaching essential skills to TVL (Technical-Vocational Livelihood) Tourism students is critical in the tourism industry. The tourism industry is one of the fastest-growing and most dynamic industries globally, and it provides ample opportunities for employment and entrepreneurship. Therefore, it is essential to provide TVL Tourism students with the necessary skills and knowledge to excel in this field.

One innovative way to teach these skills to TVL Tourism students is through experiential learning. Experiential learning is a process of learning through experience, and it is an effective way to teach skills and knowledge required in the tourism industry. The students can learn about customer service, cultural sensitivity, communication, and teamwork by engaging in experiential activities such as role-playing, field trips, and simulations.

Another innovative way to teach essential skills to TVL Tourism students is through the use of technology. Technology can be used to create immersive and interactive learning experiences that simulate real-world scenarios. For example, virtual reality can be used to create a 360-degree environment that allows students to experience different cultures and customs.

Furthermore, gamification is an innovative way to teach essential skills to TVL Tourism students. Gamification is the use of game design elements in non-game situations, and it can be an effective way to engage and motivate students. Games can be designed to teach teamwork, problem-solving, and critical thinking, which are essential skills in the tourism industry.

In conclusion, finding innovative ways to teach essential skills to TVL Tourism students is critical in preparing them for success in the tourism industry. Experiential learning, technology, and gamification are some of the innovative ways that can be used to teach these skills effectively. Employing these methods can help students to develop the skills and knowledge required to excel in the tourism industry.

8. Does teaching style reflect any of the beliefs of essentialism​


Answer:

Essentialism tries to instill all students with the most essential or basic academic knowledge and skills and character development. Essentialists believe that teachers should try to embed traditional moral values and virtues such as respect for authority, perseverance, fidelity to duty, consideration for others, and practicality and intellectual knowledge that students need to become model citizens. The foundation of essentialist curriculum is based on traditional disciplines such as math, natural science, history, foreign language, and literature. Essentialists frown upon vocational courses. In the essentialist system, students are required to master a set body of information and basic techniques for their grade level before they are promoted to the next higher grade. The content gradually moves towards more complex skills and detailed knowledge. Essentialists argue that classrooms should be teacher-oriented. The teacher should serve as an intellectual and moral role model for the students. The teachers or administrators decide what is most important for the students to learn with little regard to the student interests. The teachers also focus on achievement test scores as a means of evaluating progress. The essentialist classroom is centered on students being taught about the people, events, ideas, and institutions that have shaped American society. Essentialists hope that when students leave school, they will not only possess basic knowledge and skills, but they will also have disciplined, practical minds, capable of applying lessons learned in school in the real world. Essentialism is different from what Dewey would like to see in the schools. Students in this system would sit in rows and be taught in masses. The students would learn passively by sitting in their desks and listening to the teacher. An example of essentialism would be lecture based introduction classes taught at universities. Students sit and take notes in a classroom which holds over one hundred students. They take introductory level courses in order to introduce them to the content. After they have completed this course, they will take the next level course and apply what they have learned previously. English 101 and English 102 are a specific example of essentialism.
