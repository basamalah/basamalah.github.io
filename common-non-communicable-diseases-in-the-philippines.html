5. What is communicable disease and non communicable disease?


COMMUNICABLE VS NON-COMMUNICABLE DISEASESWhat is a Communicable Disease?Communicable diseases are a type of disease that can be transferred between one host to another.They utilize a carrier, which can be anything that has the ability to transfer the disease from one host to another.An example of a carrier may be bodily fluids (e.g. saliva, blood, sweat, etc.)

Examples of Communicable DiseasesFiliarasisIs a parasitic disease that is caused by roundwormsThey spread throught mosquito bites, wherein a mosquito infected by the disease can transfer the disease when they feed on the human host's blood.

MalariaIt's a fatal disease that targets the Red Blood Cells (erythrocytes) of the infected host, which is one of the main components of our blood.Like the example stated above, this disease also spreads through mosquito bites.

What is a Non-communicable Disease?These are diseases that have no ability to transfer between one host and another.Unlike communicable diseases, they usually develop inside the host's body, rather than invading it.This is most often due to the internal malfunction of the body and any of its constituent organs.

Examples of Non-communicable Diseases

PsoriasisThis is a disease that is caused due to the speedy mass production  of skin in a person's body.A person infected by this disease will notice symptoms of having red and scaly patches in their skin.This is mainly caused by genetics, and cannot be spread.

DiabetesIs a disease that is caused due to high amounts of glucose levels (blood sugar levels).This happens when the body's ability to give insulin, a hormone responsible for the distribution of glucose throughout the body, fails.

9. characteristic of communicable disease and non communicable disease​


Answer:

A communicable disease is one that is spread from one person to another through a variety of ways that include: contact with blood and bodily fluids; breathing in an airborne virus; or by being bitten by an insect.

A non-communicable disease (NCD) is a disease that is not transmissible directly from one person to another. NCDs include Parkinson's disease, autoimmune diseases, strokes, most heart diseases, most cancers, diabetes, chronic kidney disease, osteoarthritis, osteoporosis, Alzheimer's disease, cataracts, and others.
