1. How is a debit card similar to an ATM card?


A debit card is similar to an ATM card as stated below:

1. Both can store monetary value equivalent to your balance in your bank account.
2. They have the same size.
3. Both can withdraw in an ATM machine.
4. Both can be used to pay goods and services. You can now use your ATM and debit card in selected outlets like SM to pay for goods and services.
5. Both requires you to maintain an account in a bank.

10. supporting datails of insert your ATM card​


Answer:

An ATM card is a plastic card issued by a financial institution that allows customers to access their bank accounts in order to make cash withdrawals, deposits, transfers, and other transactions. The card is inserted into an automated teller machine (ATM) and the customer is prompted to enter their personal identification number (PIN) in order to authenticate the transaction. The ATM card can also be used at point-of-sale terminals to make purchases or pay bills.

14. Thieves can capture a credit card number by placing a(n) ______ on an atm.


Answer:

SKIMMER

Explanation:

An ATM skimmer is a malicious device that thieves attach to ATMs in order to steal your card information. Usually it’s a magnetic stripe reader stuck on top of or inside the default card reader. It’s frequently paired with a device that retrieves your card’s PIN number like a tiny video camera hidden near the ATM or a fake PIN pad attached on top of the original.

28. Using your ATM card -Advantage-Disanvantage​


Advantage:

•ATM points are conveniently located at multiple locations. You can go the ATM of any bank to withdraw cash – provided your ATM card is linked with that bank.

•There is no need to fill out withdrawal and deposit slips – unlike the practice at the bank branch.

Disadvantage:

•Fraud. Criminals can fit skimming devices and small cameras to ATMs

•Theft Risk. If you go to a bank, you're likely walking into a secured area watched by multiple cameras or a life guard. ATM doesn't have a guard.
