11. why does the holy mass becomes the highest form of prayer? need kopo​


Explanation:

The Holy Mass, also known as the Eucharist or the Lord's Supper, is considered the highest form of prayer in the Catholic Church and many other Christian denominations because it is considered the most intimate union with God.

During the Holy Mass, the bread and wine are consecrated by a priest and believed to become the body and blood of Jesus Christ. This sacrament is considered the source and summit of the Christian life, and it is believed that through the Holy Mass, Catholics and other Christians are able to participate in the sacrifice of Jesus on the cross and receive the grace and forgiveness of God.

Additionally, the Holy Mass is also considered a form of worship and adoration of God, and it is an opportunity for the faithful to come together as a community to praise and glorify God, to listen to His word, to offer their petitions and to receive His grace.

Furthermore, the Holy Mass is also considered as the celebration of the mystery of faith, and the way that we can participate in the Paschal Mystery (the death and resurrection of Jesus) and to be united in the Mystical Body of Christ. It is also an act of thanksgiving, and reparation for our sins, and it helps us to grow in holiness.

In summary, the Holy Mass is considered the highest form of prayer because it is considered the most intimate union with God and the source and summit of the Christian life. It is a sacrament in which the faithful participate in the sacrifice of Jesus, receive the grace and forgiveness of God, and come together as a community to worship and glorify God.
