2. WHAT IS THE STRUCTURE OF SALAD


AnswerThe structure of a salad is the Base, Body, Dressing and Garnish.

Base

The base of a salad is often made up of green leafy vegetables like lettuces, cabbages, kale, etc. However, some salads don't use green leaves like the Greek Salad. Think of it as a foundation of a building.

Body

The main flavor of a salad. Often in proteins like chicken, beef or ham. And are often sliced into chewable sizes.

Dressing

Basically, a dressing is the sauce of the salad. The commonly used ingredients for dressings are Vinaigrette(oil+vinegar) and simple mayonnaise. This helps combine the flavors of the other ingredients.

Garnish

A complimentary ingredient in making a Salad. It is more often used a decor or to add texture and appeal to the salad.

Identify the structure of salad​

The Base, Body, Dressing, and Garnish make up the salad's structure. A prepared salad - imagine ordered - is one salad where it is simply exhibited.

Various types of lettuce, greens, romaine, and other vegetables make up the basis. The human body is mainly made up of proteins such as chicken or ham. A mayonnaise or vinaigrette base might be used for the dressing. Parsley, basil, chives, or olives are common garnishes that complement the salad's hues.

Composed salads can be prepared in a layered form in a clear container, with lettuce as the base, followed by the body (protein), dressing, and garnishing.

Tossed salad are made up mostly of greens and little bite-size vegetables.

Composed salad come in a variety of shapes and sizes, ranging from a tower of fresh fruit to a bowl of mixed greens.
