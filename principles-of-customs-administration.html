Answer:

Henri Fayol's Principles of Management

Early Management Theory

Today's managers have access to an amazing array of resources which they can use to improve their skills. But what about those managers who were leading the way forward 100 years ago?

Managers in the early 1900s had very few external resources to draw upon to guide and develop their management practice. But thanks to early theorists like Henri Fayol (1841-1925), managers began to get the tools they needed to lead and manage more effectively. Fayol, and others like him, are responsible for building the foundations of modern management theory.

Background

Henri Fayol was born in Istanbul in 1841. When he was 19, he began working as an engineer at a large mining company in France. He eventually became the director, at a time when the mining company employed more than 1,000 people.

28. State one (1) principle of fingerprint examination as infallible means of identification that will convince the court, being presented by expert witness in the administration of justice.*​


Answer:

One principle of fingerprint examination that could be presented as an infallible means of identification in court is the uniqueness of fingerprints. Every person's fingerprints are unique and do not change over time, making them an extremely reliable means of identification.

This principle can be demonstrated through the use of statistical analysis, which shows that the likelihood of two individuals having the same fingerprint is extremely low. In fact, the probability of two individuals having the same fingerprint has been estimated to be as low as 1 in 64 billion.

In addition, fingerprint examination techniques have been extensively tested and validated by the scientific community, and they have been used successfully in thousands of criminal investigations around the world.

Overall, the uniqueness of fingerprints, combined with the reliability and validity of fingerprint examination techniques, makes them an extremely convincing means of identification that can be presented as evidence in court.

30. describe briefly the principles of administrative management theory outlined by fayol.​


According to Henri Fayol, the five management functions are: planning, organizing, command, coordination, and control. These five functions make up "management," one of the six industrial activities described by Henri Fayol management theory. Henri Fayol is widely considered to be the father of modern management.
