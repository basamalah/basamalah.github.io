12. a fault form due to the sion on rocks is called?


Answer:

When rocks slip past each other in faulting, the upper or overlying block along the fault plane is called the hanging wall, or headwall; the block below is called the footwall. The fault strike is the direction of the line of intersection between the fault plane and Earth's surface. Faults occur when rocks break due to the forces acting on them. Stress may build up over a period of many years until the fault suddenly moves – perhaps a few centimetres, or even a few metres. When this happens, it releases a huge amount of energy in an earthquake.
