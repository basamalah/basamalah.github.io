6. if 8 moles of potassium superoxide reacts with 5 moles of water how many moles will remain underacted? A. 1 mole KO2 B. 3.0 moles KO2 C. 1.0 mole H2O D. 3.0 moles H2O


Number of unreacted moles : B. 3.0 moles KO₂

Further explanation

A reaction coefficient is a number in the chemical formula of a substance involved in the reaction equation. The reaction coefficient is useful for equalizing reagents and products.

Limiting reagents are reactants that limit the outcome of the reaction. Where if two substances are reacted there is a possibility that both substances are used up or one is used up and the other is left.

The method that can be used to find limiting reagents is to divide the number of moles of known substances by their respective coefficients, and the small quotient will be used up or as a limiting reagent

Reaction

2 KO₂ + 2 H₂O ⇒ 2 KOH + H₂O₂ + O₂

8 moles of potassium superoxide reacts with 5 moles of water, then :

Comparison of both moles:

KO₂ : H₂O = 8 : 5

Comparison of both moles with coefficients :

[tex]\rm KO_2=\dfrac{8}{2}=4\\\\H_2O=\dfrac{5}{2}=2.5[/tex]

So H₂O : limiting reactants

mol KO₂  that reacts :

[tex]\rm mol~KO_2 = \dfrac{coefficient~KO_2}{coefficient~H_2O}\times mol~H_2O\\\\mol~KO_2=\dfrac{2}{2}\times 5\\\\mol~KO_2~=5[/tex]

                2 KO₂ + 2 H₂O ⇒ 2 KOH + H₂O₂ + O₂

initial            8             5

reaction       5             5           5           2.5      2.5

remain         3             0           5           2.5      2.5

19. How many number of moles are present in 200. g of sodium bicarbonate (nahco3)? how many number of moles are present in 200. g of sodium bicarbonate (nahco3)? 4.00 mole 23.8 mole 0.238 mole 2.38 mole?


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of NaHCO₃.

molar mass = (22.99 g/mol × 1) + (1.008 g/mol × 1) + (12.01 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 3)

molar mass = 84.008 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of NaHCO₃.

[tex]\text{moles of NaHCO₃ = 200. g NaHCO₃} × \frac{\text{1 mol NaHCO₃}}{\text{84.008 g NaHCO₃}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of NaHCO₃ = 2.38 mol}}[/tex]
