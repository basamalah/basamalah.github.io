Identifying Figures of Speech

1. The phone rings inside the room. – Onomatopoeia

Onomatopeia is the figure of speech that names a thing or an action by creating a vocal imitation of the associated sound. ‘Buzz’ from the bee's wings and ‘hiss’ from the tongue of the snakes are examples of onomatopoeia.

2. Why do you cry, Willie? Why do you cry? Why, Willie? Why, Willie? Why, Willie? – Alliteration

Alliteration is described as the repetition of consonant sounds. It is usually found on the first letter or syllable of words. It is usually found in poetry and songs. The tongue twister ‘Peter Piper’ uses alliteration.

3. It was cold I saw polar bears wearing jackets. – Hyperbole

Hyperbole can be found when there is extreme exaggeration in the statements or extravagant expressions found in the literary piece. This figure of speech is used to add emphasis on the subject.

4. The team leader moves as bravely as a lion. – Simile

Simile is the simplest figure of speech. It can be observed when there are comparisons of two unlike things using the words ‘like’ or ‘as’.

5. The snow is a white blanket. – Metaphor
