6. mortality rate news?​


Answer:

14.9 million excess deaths associated with the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 and 2021

Explanation:

According to recent estimates from the World Health

Organization (WHO), between January 1, 2020, and December 31, 2021, there were roughly 14.9 million deaths that could be directly or indirectly attributed to the COVID-19 epidemic (range 13.3 million to 16.6 million).

The discrepancy between the number of fatalities that have occurred and the number that would be anticipated in the absence of the pandemic based on data from preceding years is known as excess mortality.

Excess mortality comprises all deaths related to COVID-19, whether they are caused directly by the illness or indirectly by the pandemic's effects on social structures and health care. Deaths that are indirectly linked to COVID-19 are caused by other medical illnesses for which people were unable to receive preventive and treatment due to the pandemic's load on health services. Deaths saved during the pandemic due to lower risks of specific events, including motor vehicle accidents or occupational injuries, can also have an impact on the anticipated number of extra deaths.

22. what is mortality rate?


Answer:

Mortality rate, or death rate,[1] is a measure of the number of deaths (in general, or due to a specific cause) in a particular population, scaled to the size of that population, per unit of time. Mortality rate is typically expressed in units of deaths per 1,000 individuals per year; thus, a mortality rate of 9.5 (out of 1,000) in a population of 1,000 would mean 9.5 deaths per year in that entire population, or 0.95% out of the total. It is distinct from "morbidity", which is either the prevalence or incidence of a disease,[2] and also from the incidence rate (the number of newly appearing cases of the disease per unit of time).

In the generic form, mortality rates are calculated as:

{\displaystyle d/p\cdot 10^{n}}{\displaystyle d/p\cdot 10^{n}}

where d represents the deaths occurring within a given time period, p represents the size of the population in which the deaths occur and {\displaystyle 10^{n}}10^{n}is a conversion factor from fraction to some other unit (such as multiplying by {\displaystyle 10^{3}}10^{3}to get mortality rate per 1,000 individuals).

29. All men are mortal. harold is a man. therefore, harold is mortal. this statement is


This statement is example of deductive reasoning.

Explanation:

Deductive reasoning is a logical approach in which a particular or spesific conclusion develops from several general ideas. Deductive reasoning is the opposite of inductive reasoning, where you start with specific ideas to form general conclusions. Deductive reasoning is also known as deductive logic or top-down reasoning. In deductive reasoning, if something is true of a class of things in general, it is also true for all members of that class. This is also a kind of hypothesis that has been proven by the previous sentence. For example :

All men are mortal (general idea) + Harold is a man (general idea) = Harold is mortal (specific conclusion)
