Answer:

What property of proportion states that "interchanging the means or interchanging the extremes of a proportion results in a equivalent proportion"

Property 2   →Ans.

Explanation:

Property 2: In a proportion, the means or the extremes (or both the means and the extremes) may be interchanged.

That is, if a/b = c/d and a, b, c, and d are all non-zero, then a/c = b/d, d/b = c/a and d/c = b/a.

Again, mixing numerators and denominators is not surprising because you've been doing this in algebra for years! But there is another property of proportions that might be a bit surprising. You have to be careful when you apply this property because it involves adding or subtracting things.
