RELIABILITY

The term reliability in psychological research refers to the consistency of a research study or measuring test.

For example, if a person weighs themselves during the course of a day they would expect to see a similar reading. Scales which measured weight differently each time would be of little use.

The same analogy could be applied to a tape measure which measures inches differently each time it was used. It would not be considered reliable.

The Sampling Distribution of the Sample Mean. If repeated random samples of a given size n are taken from a population of values for a quantitative variable, where the population mean is μ (mu) and the population standard deviation is σ (sigma) then the mean of all sample means (x-bars) is population mean μ (mu).

As you might expect, the mean of the sampling distribution of the difference between means is: which says that the mean of the distribution of differences between sample means is equal to the difference between population means.

What is the mean of the sampling distribution of the sample mean quizlet?

The Sampling Distribution of the Sample Mean is the distribution of all possible sample means of a given sample size. 20. 7. What do you mean by;a fruit of poisonous tree? illustrate in few sentence​


Answer:

Fruit of the poisonous tree is a legal metaphor used to describe evidence that is obtained illegally. The logic of the terminology is that if the source (the "tree") of the evidence or evidence itself is tainted, then anything gained (the "fruit") from it is tainted as well.
