When you visit any UP campus, it is not difficult to see the Oblation. In the UP Diliman campus, the popular statue, measuring 3.5 meters in height, was constructed during the university presidency of Rafael Palma. First time observers, whether part of the UP community or not, usually ask “Who is the model of the famous UP symbol, the Oblation?

The model for the famous UP symbol, the Oblation, is not a single individual, but rather a representation of the Filipino people. The pose of the statue was inspired by a passage in the Bible where Abraham is willing to offer his son Isaac as a sacrifice to God. The figure in the Oblation represents sacrifice and selflessness, qualities that are seen as desirable for Filipinos to embody.

9. . How does the Material Recovery Facility helps out the community in UP DILIMAN?


Answer:

The Environmental Bureau of the Philippines with the help of Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) aims to install Materials Recovery Facility (MRF) in every barangay in the country. It will aid our solid waste problems and at the same time help mother nature. MRF shall receive waste for final sorting according to its type for composting and recycling. It shall be designed to receive, sort, process and store compostable and recyclable material efficiently and in an environmentally sound manner. Our task is to visit MRFs in our barangay in order to know the activities on solid waste management.

Last Monday, we looked for MRFs near UP Diliman because three of our groupmates are dormers. Unfortunately, we were not able to find any, because they’re closed due to the absence of the personnel.

One of our groupmates residing in Makati City was not able to go to their MRF because he said he couldn’t allot time for the activity. Instead, he made up by telling us about their barangay’s strict implementation of the waste segregation scheme, and that the school and the community households within the barangay actively participate in it.
