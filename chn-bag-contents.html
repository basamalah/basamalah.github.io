14. Calculate the concentration of [OH-] and pH of a weak acid in a 0.500 M pyridine (CHN) solution (K 1.7x10")​


Given:

[tex][\text{C}_{5}\text{H}_{5}\text{N}]_{0} = \text{0.500 M}[/tex]

[tex]K_{\text{b}} = 1.7 \times 10^{-9}[/tex]

Required:

OH⁻ concentration

pH

Solution: (OH⁻ concentration)

Step 1: Express the equilibrium concentration of these species in terms of the initial concentration of the acid and a single unknown x, which represents the change in concentration.

[tex]\begin{array}{cccccccc} & \text{C}_{5}\text{H}_{5}\text{N}(aq) & + & \text{H}_{2}\text{O}(l) & \rightleftharpoons & \text{C}_{5}\text{H}_{5}\text{NH}^{+}(aq) & + & \text{OH}^{-}(aq) \\ \text{Initial} \: (M): & 0.500 & & & & 0 & & 0 \\ \text{Change} \: (M): & -x & & & & +x & & +x \\ \hline \text{Equilibrium} \: (M): & 0.500-x & & & & x & & x \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Step 2: Solve for the equilibrium concentration of OH⁻ ions.

[tex]K_{\text{b}} = \dfrac{[\text{C}_{5}\text{H}_{5}\text{NH}^{+}][\text{OH}^{-}]}{[\text{C}_{5}\text{H}_{5}\text{N}]}[/tex]

[tex]1.7 \times 10^{-9} = \dfrac{(x)(x)}{0.500 - x}[/tex]

[tex]1.7 \times 10^{-9} = \dfrac{x^{2}}{0.500 - x}[/tex]

Applying the approximation [tex]0.500 - x ≈ 0.500[/tex], we obtain

[tex]1.7 \times 10^{-9} = \dfrac{x^{2}}{0.500}[/tex]

[tex]x^{2} = (0.500)(1.7 \times 10^{-9})[/tex]

[tex]x^{2} = 8.5 \times 10^{-10}[/tex]

[tex]x = \sqrt{8.5 \times 10^{-10}}[/tex]

[tex]x = 2.9155 \times 10^{-5} \: M[/tex]

To test the approximation

[tex]\text{approx} = \dfrac{x}{\text{initial concentration}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{approx} = \dfrac{2.9155 \times 10^{-5} \: M}{0.500 \: M} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{approx} = 0.0058\%[/tex]

Since the approximation is less than 5%, the approximation is valid. Therefore, the value of OH⁻ concentration is

[tex][\text{OH}^{-}] = x = 2.9155 \times 10^{-5} \: M[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{[\text{OH}^{-}] = 2.9 \times 10^{-5} \: M}[/tex]

Solution: (pH)

Step 1: Calculate the pOH of the solution.

[tex]\text{pOH} = -\text{log} \: [\text{OH}^{-}][/tex]
