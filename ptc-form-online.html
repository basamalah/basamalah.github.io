4. The ability to taste a bitter chemical, phenylthiocarbamide (ptc), is due to a dominant gene. use t and t to symbolize the two alleles of this gene.


Yes, the ability to taste phenylthiocarbamide (PTC) is determined by a dominant gene. The two alleles of this gene are represented by "T" and "t."

The "T" and "t" in Alleles

The T allele represents the dominant gene, which codes for the ability to taste PTC. The t allele represents the recessive gene, which codes for the inability to taste PTC.

Individuals who have at least one T allele will be able to taste PTC, while those who have two t alleles will not be able to taste PTC. If an individual has one T allele and one t allele, they are referred to as carriers of the gene for the ability to taste PTC. They will be able to taste PTC but can also pass on the t allele to their offspring.

The T allele is dominant, meaning that it is the version of the gene that will be expressed if a person has at least one T allele. The t allele is recessive, meaning that it will only be expressed if a person has two t alleles.
