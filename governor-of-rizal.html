12. pahelpGive your own view of Rizal, when he complained to Governor General Primo de Rivera on the brutality inflicted to him by a Spanish Civil Guard. Did such incident plant a seed of hate to the young Jose Rizal against Spanish government in the Philippines? Explain your answer.​


The incident that Rizal complained about to Governor General Primo de Rivera did not necessarily plant a seed of hate towards the Spanish government in the Philippines for young Jose Rizal. Rizal was known for his advocacy for reforms and equal treatment of Filipinos, rather than for promoting hate towards Spanish colonial rule. The incident could have influenced Rizal's later works, such as "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," which exposed the injustices and corruption prevalent during Spanish colonial rule in the Philippines. However, Rizal's works were written to promote change through peaceful means and to educate the people, rather than to incite hate.
