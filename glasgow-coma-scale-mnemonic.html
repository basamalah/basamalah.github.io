6. Why does Moscow have a lower temperature than Glasgow even if they are in the same latitude?


Answer:

Although Moscow and Glasgow are at the same latitude (55 degrees north), Moscow is located much further inland and has a continental climate, while Glasgow has a maritime climate due to its proximity to the ocean. This means that Moscow experiences greater temperature extremes throughout the year, with very cold winters and hot summers, while Glasgow has a milder, wetter climate with smaller temperature fluctuations. Additionally, Moscow's higher altitude and distance from large bodies of water also contribute to its colder temperatures.

12. part of a comet that sorrounds its coma?


Answer:

hydrogen envelope

Surrounding the coma is an invisible layer of hydrogen called the hydrogen envelope; the hydrogen may come from water molecules. It usually has an irregular shape because it is distorted by the solar wind. The hydrogen envelope gets bigger as the comet approaches the sun.
