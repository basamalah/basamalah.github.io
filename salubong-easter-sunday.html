8. 10 filipino practices to celebrate Easter Sunday​


Answer:

Visita Iglesia – Visiting seven different churches is a tradition that is often observed during Holy Week and on Easter Sunday. This practice is meant to represent the Seven Stations of the Cross.Salubong – Salubong is a traditional Easter practice where an image of the Risen Christ and the Blessed Virgin Mary meet each other to signify the reunion of Christ with His Mother.Easter Vigil – The Easter Vigil is a religious service that usually takes place on the night before Easter Sunday. It is a time of prayer, readings, and reflection on the resurrection of Christ.Easter Sunday Mass – Easter Sunday Mass is a celebration of the resurrection of Christ. It is a time for families to come together to attend Mass and give thanks for the gift of salvation.Easter Egg Hunt – Easter egg hunts are popular activities for children during Easter Sunday. It is a fun way to celebrate and spread joy among the younger members of the family.Pabasa ng Pasyon – Pabasa ng Pasyon is a traditional Filipino practice where the passion, death, and resurrection of Christ are narrated through the reading of a book called Pasyon.Offering of Palaspas – Palaspas are palm leaves that are woven into intricate designs and shapes. These are often blessed on Palm Sunday and offered as a symbol of peace and blessing during Easter Sunday.Binyagang Pasko – Baptism is a significant part of the Easter celebration, and it is common for families to have their children baptized on Easter Sunday.Sharing of Food – Easter Sunday is also a time for families to come together and share a special meal. Traditional Filipino dishes such as lechon, pancit, and kakanin are often served.Processions – Religious processions are often held on Easter Sunday, with devotees carrying images of the Risen Christ and the Blessed Virgin Mary. These processions are meant to honor the resurrection of Christ and express gratitude for His sacrifice.
