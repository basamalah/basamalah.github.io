"Ako ang Daigdig" by Alejandro C. Abadilla is a poem that speaks about the interconnectedness of all things in the world. The title of the poem translates to "I am the World," which immediately sets the tone for the poem's message.

The poem begins by describing the speaker's identity as the world itself. The speaker claims to be the land, the sea, the sky, and all the living creatures that inhabit them. This suggests that the speaker sees themselves as a part of something much larger than themselves, and that they are intimately connected to everything around them.

The speaker then goes on to describe the various elements of the world and how they are all interconnected. They describe how the sun's rays nourish the earth, how the earth gives life to the plants, and how the plants provide food for the animals. This cycle of life and death is presented as a natural and essential part of the world.

The poem also touches on the destructive impact that humans can have on the world. The speaker describes how humans have polluted the air and water, destroyed forests, and caused the extinction of many species. This serves as a warning that our actions have consequences and that we must take responsibility for our impact on the world.

Overall, "Ako ang Daigdig" is a powerful poem that reminds us of our place in the world and our responsibility to protect it. It speaks to the interconnectedness of all things and the importance of living in harmony with the natural world.
