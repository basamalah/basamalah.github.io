If so, can the municipal mayor validly use the exercise of Power of Taxation as a defense?


Answer:

The determination of whether a business is a nuisance and whether it should be foreclosed is usually within the discretion of the local government unit, specifically the Municipal Mayor. The Municipal Mayor has the power to regulate and control the operation of businesses within its jurisdiction in order to promote the general welfare of its constituents.

In this case, if the operation of the KTV Bar is causing a nuisance to the community, the Municipal Mayor may order its foreclosure. However, the determination of whether the KTV Bar is a nuisance or not would depend on the specific circumstances of the case.

As for the use of the exercise of Power of Taxation as a defense, it is not clear how this would apply to the situation you have presented. The Power of Taxation is a power of the government to impose taxes on individuals, properties, or businesses. It is not typically used as a defense against a foreclosure order. Nonetheless, it's possible that there are local laws or regulations that may provide for such a defense.
