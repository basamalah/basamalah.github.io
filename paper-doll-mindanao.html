15. The Contribution of Catriona and Lapu lapu Paper doll in Culture ang Philippine History​


Answer:What is the contribution of Catriona Gray?

Catriona Elisa Magnayon Gray (born 6 January 1994) is a Filipino-Australian model, singer, and beauty pageant titleholder who was crowned Miss Universe 2018. She is the fourth Filipina to win the Miss Universe competition. Previously, Gray was crowned Miss Universe Philippines 2018 and Miss World Philippines 2016.

Miss Universe 2018 Catriona Gray was among popular personalities featured in the National Quincentennial Committee’s (NQC) new set of paper dolls, crafted for the country’s quincentennial celebration on April 27.

The doll version of the 27-year-old model-singer-beauty queen comes with a facsimile of the national costume she wore at the Miss Universe contest, including her evening gown.

Aside from Catriona, the Quincentennial Paper Crafts Series also includes historical personages like Lapu-Lapu, Rajah Humabon, Rajah Kulambo, Ferdinand Magellan, Juan Sebastian Elcano, Enrique de Malacca, and Antonio Pigafetta.

Those interested may download the doll for free here: https://nqc.gov.ph/en/quincentennial-paper-crafts/.

21. The Contribution of Catriona and Lapu lapu Paper doll in our Culture in Philippine history​


Answer:

Filipino hero Lapulapu and key characters of the Philippines’ celebrations in 2021 are joined by Miss Universe Catriona Gray in the Filipino Paper Dolls craft series released by the National Quincentennial Committee (NQC).

The Paper Dolls from the Quincentennial Paper Crafts Series was released on July 17, 2020, in preparation for the country’s 500th-anniversary celebrations of the victory in Mactan, the coming of the Christian faith, and the successful circumnavigation of the world, all in the year 2021.

The Quincentennial Paper Crafts Series features the personalities and figures who were part of the Philippines’ written history during the 16th century.

A bonus paper doll features Filipina Miss Universe Catriona Gray wearing her pageant’s Philippine national costume.

The first batch of Quincentennial paper doll series includes the early Visayan Islands natives Lapulapu of Mactan, Rajah Humabon, his wife Juana, and Rajah Kulambo of Cebu.

Also included in the series are Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan, Spanish sailor Juan Sebastian Elcano, Italian chronicler Antonio Pigafetta, and the native translator and interpreter Enrique de Malacca.
