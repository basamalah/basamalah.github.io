1. In an infrared camera, what color indicates warmer temperature?


Answer:

In an infrared camera, what color indicates warmer temperature?

In an infrared camera, warmer objects typically appear as a brighter color, while cooler objects appear as a darker color. However, it's important to note that the specific color scheme used by an infrared camera can vary, and the camera may use false colors to represent different temperature ranges. For example, warmer objects could appear as red, while cooler objects could appear as blue or purple. Ultimately, the color scheme used by an infrared camera is arbitrary and depends on the specific device and its settings

The colors indicating warmer temperatures in an infrared camera are red, orange, and yellow.

Explanation

Thermography is one of the infrared technologies used to detect the thermal distribution contained in an object. To detect infrared radiation emitted by an object based on its temperature, an infrared camera is used to produce an image called a thermogram.

A thermogram can help us to see the temperature variation of an object. In a thermogram, brighter colors such as red, orange, and yellow indicate warmer temperatures (due to more heat and infrared radiation being emitted) and for dark blue, purple, and black colors indicate cooler temperatures (due to less heat and infrared radiation being emitted).

2. In an infrared camera, what color indicates warmer temperature?


In an infrared camera, the bright color such a red, orange and yellow indicates warmer temperature.

Explanation :

Infrared is electromagnetic radiation of a longer wavelength than visible light, but shorter than radio wave radiation. Literally infra red consists of "infra" and "red", red is the color of visible light with the longest wavelength.

In any thermogram, brighter colors like red, orange, and yellow indicate warmer temperatures because more heat waves and infrared radiation are emitted. Meanwhile, purple and dark blue/black colors indicate cooler temperatures where less heat and infrared radiation is emitted.

The color spectrum is directly proportional to the heat emitted, meaning that the order of the red, orange, yellow, green, blue, dark blue, violet and dark color spectrum is the order of the emitted objects from the highest temperature to the lowest temperature.
