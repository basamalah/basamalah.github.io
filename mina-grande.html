7. The ratio of Faye's savings to Mina's savings is 3 : 7. If Faye spends 4/9 of her money, she will have $112 less than Mina. How much is Mina's savings?


Answer:

We can start by using algebra to set up and solve the problem. Let x be the amount of Mina's savings in dollars. Then, based on the given ratio, we know that Faye's savings is 3/10 of x. We can express this as:

Faye's savings = (3/10)x

We also know that if Faye spends 4/9 of her money, she will have $112 less than Mina. So we can write an equation using the information given:

(3/10)x - (4/9)(3/10)x = x - 112

Simplifying the equation gives:

(3/10)x - (4/9)(3/10)x = x - 112

Solving for x, we get:

x = 560

Therefore, Mina's savings is $560.

Problem: Mina is Sana's Mother. Mina is 25 years old. Four years ago. Mina was three times as old as Sana was then. How old is Sana now?

Solution: Represent x and y as the ages of Mina and Sana respectively. Make two equations of the given statement.

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25& \green{(eq. \: 1)} \\ (x - 4) = 3(y - 4)& \green{(eq. \: 2)} \end{cases}[/tex]

- Find the age of Sana by substituting x in the second equation in terms of y.

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ (25 - 4) = 3(y - 4) \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ 21 = 3(y - 4) \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ 21 = 3y - 12 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ 21 + 12 = 3y \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ 33 = 3y \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\ \begin{gathered} \frac{33}{3} = \frac{ \cancel3y}{ \cancel3} \end{gathered} \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 25 \\y = 11 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \therefore[/tex] Sana's age is:

[tex] \large \rm Sana = \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 11 \: years \: old \: }}[/tex]===============================
