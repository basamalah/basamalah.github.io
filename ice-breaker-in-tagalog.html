6. Ice Breaker game that should be related to the Conflict Theory of Karl Marx


1. Divide players into two teams.

2. Have one team stand in a line, shoulder to shoulder.

3. The other team lines up behind the first team, also shoulder to shoulder.

4. Have the first team member in the front line say "go" and everyone in the front line tries to move to the back of the line.

5. The team members in the back line try to stop the players in the front line from moving.

6. The team that moves the most players to the back of the line wins.

Explanation:

The water does not enter the bottle because of the presence of air inside it. When the bottle is dipped inside water, it applies pressure on the air present inside so as to replace it. But the air present inside the bottle applies opposite force on the water as it tries to escape through the only opening present which is surrounded by water. So both the forces cancel each other and maintain an equilibrium due to which the neither the air gets out nor the water gets in.
