  • 15. PHILIPPINE LENT Lent in the Philippines, especially the Holy Week, is one of the unique observances in Christiandom. History shows that the initiation of Lent was implanted by the Spanish conquistadores who came to our country in the middle of the 16th century for the dual purpose of extending the limits of the Spanish empire and for spreading the Catholic faith. Our Philippine Lenten customs and rituals consist of the Peniteneiye, Pabasa, Bieita Iglesiya, Senakule, Siyete Palabras, Salubong and Linggo ng Pagkabuhay. Calling themselves as flagellants or penitents, these men and women have vowed to undertake annual flagellation to cleanse themselves of sins against God and fellowmen. Garbed in black, with their faces covered with a mask or thin cloth, these penitents walk around the streets, flogging themselves and uttering prayers. They flog themselves with switches or dried branches of trees. Sometimes, a flagellant has a com- panion who deals him the blows. The second, though not as popular as the first in the Lenten rituals, is the Pabasa. It is the reading of the passion of Christ, done in a sing-song chant, lasting for 24 hours. Holding this Pabasa is supposed to be in connection with a vow a person made either as form of penitence during Holy Week or to give thanks for blessings received or prayers answered. The Bisita Iglesia is observed in which the devotees make trips to various churches to undertake the stations of the Cross. This is usually done on Maundy Thursday or Good Friday. The Senakulo is nothing more than the stage drama- tization of the Pabasa. It focuses on the highlights of the passion and crucifixion of Christ. Not all communities ob- serve this Lenten practice. Amateur or professional actors compose the dramatis personae of this presentation on s stage.​

1. what is your reflection in Maundy Thursday​


Maundy Thursday Reflection.

I can provide information on what Maundy Thursday is and its significance for those who observe it.

Maundy Thursday is a Christian holy day that falls on the Thursday before Easter Sunday. It commemorates the Last Supper of Jesus Christ with his apostles before his crucifixion, as described in the Bible. During the Last Supper, Jesus shared bread and wine with his apostles, which is now known as the Holy Communion or Eucharist in the Christian faith.

In some Christian denominations, Maundy Thursday is also associated with the washing of feet, a gesture of humility and service that Jesus performed for his disciples during the Last Supper. This act is remembered by some churches through the ritual washing of feet on Maundy Thursday.

Overall, Maundy Thursday serves as a reminder for Christians of the sacrifice and teachings of Jesus Christ, and is an important part of the Holy Week leading up to Easter Sunday.

If the Passion and Death of Jesus happened in our time now, what practical and personal things can you do to ease the pain that he felt?​


1. The most sacred time of the liturgical year is the Triduum, which begins with the Mass of the Lord's Supper on Holy Thursday and ends with evening prayer on Easter Sunday.

2. The 3 C's when we speak of the Paschal Mystery of Jesus are: Crucifixion, Death, and Resurrection.

3. Maundy Thursday is when we remember the Last Supper of Jesus with his disciples, where he instituted the sacrament of the Eucharist and the priesthood.

4. Jesus instituted the practice of humble service and charity by washing the feet of his disciples during the Last Supper.

5. The Last Supper of Jesus with his disciples recalls the feast of the Hebrews called Passover, which commemorates the liberation of the Israelites from slavery in Egypt.

6. Good Friday is when we reflect upon the Passion and Death of Jesus Christ on the Cross, which he endured for the forgiveness of our sins.

7. Jesus' death on the cross brought about our salvation and redemption, and opened the gates of heaven for all who believe in him.

8. We rejoice on Easter Sunday because Jesus Christ rose from the dead, conquering sin and death, and giving us hope for eternal life.

9. The 3 gifts we receive as we commemorate, contemplate, and celebrate the Paschal Mystery of Jesus are: forgiveness of sins, new life in Christ, and the hope of eternal salvation.

10. If the Passion and Death of Jesus happened in our time now, we can ease his pain by being present with him, comforting him, and offering him acts of love and service. We can also offer our own suffering in union with his, and strive to live a life of virtue and holiness in imitation of his example.

15. PHILIPPINE LENT Lent in the Philippines, especially the Holy Week, is one of the unique observances in Christiandom. History shows that the initiation of Lent was implanted by the Spanish conquistadores who came to our country in the middle of the 16th century for the dual purpose of extending the limits of the Spanish empire and for spreading the Catholic faith. Our Philippine Lenten customs and rituals consist of the Peniteneiye, Pabasa, Bieita Iglesiya, Senakule, Siyete Palabras, Salubong and Linggo ng Pagkabuhay. Calling themselves as flagellants or penitents, these men and women have vowed to undertake annual flagellation to cleanse themselves of sins against God and fellowmen. Garbed in black, with their faces covered with a mask or thin cloth, these penitents walk around the streets, flogging themselves and uttering prayers. They flog themselves with switches or dried branches of trees. Sometimes, a flagellant has a com- panion who deals him the blows. The second, though not as popular as the first in the Lenten rituals, is the Pabasa. It is the reading of the passion of Christ, done in a sing-song chant, lasting for 24 hours. Holding this Pabasa is supposed to be in connection with a vow a person made either as form of penitence during Holy Week or to give thanks for blessings received or prayers answered. The Bisita Iglesia is observed in which the devotees make trips to various churches to undertake the stations of the Cross. This is usually done on Maundy Thursday or Good Friday. The Senakulo is nothing more than the stage drama- tization of the Pabasa. It focuses on the highlights of the passion and crucifixion of Christ. Not all communities ob- serve this Lenten practice. Amateur or professional actors compose the dramatis personae of this presentation on s stage.​


Answer:

The Philippine Lent is a unique observance in Christianity that was initiated by Spanish conquistadores in the 16th century. The Lenten customs and rituals include Peniteneiye, Pabasa, Bieita Iglesiya, Senakule, Siyete Palabras, Salubong, and Linggo ng Pagkabuhay. The flagellants or penitents garbed in black, walk around the streets flogging themselves and uttering prayers to cleanse themselves of sins. The Pabasa is the reading of the passion of Christ, done in a sing-song chant, lasting for 24 hours. The Bisita Iglesia is observed, in which the devotees make trips to various churches to undertake the stations of the Cross. These unique Lenten practices provide an opportunity for devotees to reflect and deepen their faith during the Holy Week.
