9. what is the relationship of freedom with democracy


Freedom and democracy are closely related concepts, and they are often seen as interdependent. In a democratic society, freedom is a fundamental value and a necessary condition for the functioning of democracy. Democracy is based on the principles of equality, representation, and the rule of law, and it aims to ensure that every citizen has the freedom to express their opinions, participate in the decision-making process, and hold their elected representatives accountable.

Freedom is essential to the exercise of democracy, as it enables individuals to express their opinions freely, to criticize the government and its policies, and to participate actively in the political process. Without freedom of expression, assembly, and association, democracy cannot function properly, and the voices of the people will not be heard. Moreover, democracy helps to safeguard individual freedoms by providing a system of checks and balances that ensures that the government is held accountable to the people and that its power is limited.

In summary, freedom and democracy are closely intertwined, with democracy providing the framework for the protection of individual freedoms, and freedom being essential to the functioning of democracy.

Freedom is the ability to set your schedule, to decide on the work you do, to make decisions.

Responsibility is being held accountable for your actions. It might involve figuring out how to get paid for your work, owning your mistakes or having others count on you.
