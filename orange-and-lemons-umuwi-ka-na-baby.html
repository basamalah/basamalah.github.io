19. A box contains of 60pcs of fruits with either apple , orange or lemon . The ratio of the apple to orange to lemon is 3:5:4. How many fruits did each have?


Problem:

A box contains of 60pcs of fruits with either apple, orange or lemon . The ratio of the apple to orange to lemon is 3:5:4. How many fruits did each have?

Solution:

✓Suppose that 3:5:4 is the simplest form of the numbers of apple, orange and lemon. Therefore, you need to multipy a number in each of them and we can use n. Then add all of them and equate to 60.

[tex]3n + 5n + 4n = 60 \\ 12n = 60 \\ n = \frac{60}{12} \\ n = 5[/tex]

✓After finding the value of the unknown n, multiply it to each of the numbers in ratio to find its equivalent.

[tex]3n = 3(5) = 15 \\ 5n = 5(5) = 25 \\ 4n = 4(5) = 20[/tex]

Answer:There are 15 apples, 25 oranges, and 20 lemons.

To check:

[tex]3n + 5n + 4n = 60 \\ 3(5) + 5(5) + 4(5) = 60 \\ 15 + 25 + 20 = 60 \\ 40 + 20 = 60 \\ 60 = 60[/tex]

✓Therefore, the statement is true and the answers are correct.
