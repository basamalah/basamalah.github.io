14. 7.) You take out a fire insurance policy on your home. The annual premium is Php 15 000.00. In case of fire, the insurance company will pay you Php 10 000 000.00. The probability of a house fire in your area is 0.0002. a. What is the expected value? b. What is the insurance company’s expected value? c. Suppose the insurance company sells 1,000 of these policies. What can the company expect to earn?


To solve this problem, you will need to calculate the expected value for both you and the insurance company.

a. The expected value for you is the amount of money you expect to receive in case of a house fire, multiplied by the probability of a house fire occurring. In this case, the expected value is 10,000,000 * 0.0002 = Php 2,000.

b. The expected value for the insurance company is the amount of money they will pay out in case of a house fire, multiplied by the probability of a house fire occurring. In this case, the expected value is 10,000,000 * 0.0002 = Php 2,000.

c. If the insurance company sells 1,000 of these policies, they can expect to earn a total of 1,000 * 15,000 = Php 15,000,000 in premiums. The company's expected value is their total expected earnings minus the expected payouts in case of a house fire. In this case, the company's expected value is 15,000,000 - (1,000 * 2,000) = Php 13,000,000.
