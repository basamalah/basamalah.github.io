29. The overall objective remain the same from one audit engagement to another


Answer:

The overall objective of an audit engagement is to provide an independent, objective assessment of an organization's financial statements. This assessment is typically performed by an auditor, who is an independent professional who is trained to evaluate the accuracy and reliability of financial statements. The objective of the audit is to express an opinion on whether the financial statements are presented fairly, in all material respects, in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework.

The overall objective of an audit engagement remains the same from one audit to another, regardless of the specific organization being audited. The auditor's objective is to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence to provide a reasonable basis for their opinion on the financial statements. In order to do this, the auditor must follow established audit standards and procedures, including planning the audit, identifying and assessing the risks of material misstatement, testing the organization's financial information, and evaluating the results of their audit procedures.

The overall objective of an audit is to provide assurance to users of the financial statements that the information contained in the statements is reliable and accurately reflects the financial position and performance of the organization. This objective is important for helping to ensure the integrity and credibility of financial reporting, which is essential for building trust and confidence in the financial system.
