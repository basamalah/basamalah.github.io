1. 3 difference of evangelization and new evangelization ​


Answer:

Evangelization and New Evangelization are two terms that are often used in the context of the Catholic Church. While they both involve spreading the message of the Gospel, there are some key differences between them.

Historical Context:

Evangelization refers to the spreading of the Gospel message in a traditional sense, which has been done since the early days of the Church. In contrast, the concept of New Evangelization emerged in the late 20th century, as a response to the changing cultural and social realities of contemporary society.

Target audience:

Evangelization is primarily focused on non-believers, those who have not yet heard the message of the Gospel, or who are not yet members of the Church. New Evangelization, on the other hand, is directed towards those who have already been baptized but may have become disconnected from the Church or are not actively practicing their faith.

Methods and approaches:

Traditional methods of evangelization often involve preaching, missionary work, and catechesis. New Evangelization, however, involves a broader range of methods, including the use of modern media, interfaith dialogue, and new forms of evangelization tailored to specific cultural contexts.

In summary, while evangelization and New Evangelization share the same goal of spreading the message of the Gospel, the latter takes into account the changing cultural and social realities of contemporary society and seeks to engage with those who are already baptized but may have become disconnected from the Church. It also employs a broader range of methods and approaches to reach its target audience.
