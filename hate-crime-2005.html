Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19) is believed to have emerged in Wuhan, China in late December 2019 and began rapidly spreading around the globe throughout the spring months of 2020. As COVID-19 proliferated across the United States, Asian Americans reported a surge in racially motivated hate crimes involving physical violence and harassment. Throughout history, pandemic-related health crises have been associated with the stigmatization and “othering” of people of Asian descent. Asian Americans have experienced verbal and physical violence motivated by individual-level racism and xenophobia from the time they arrived in America in the late 1700s up until the present day. COVID-19 has enabled the spread of racism and created national insecurity, fear of foreigners, and general xenophobia, which may be related to the increase in anti-Asian hate crimes during the pandemic. Racial discrimination is a serious issue that has been prevalent in society for centuries. It is a form of prejudice and intolerance that results in unfair treatment and negative attitudes towards individuals or groups based on their race, ethnicity, or national origin. It can manifest in many ways, including hate crimes, systemic racism, and individual acts of discrimination.

Efforts to combat racial discrimination have been ongoing, and progress has been made in some areas. Governments and organizations have implemented anti-discrimination laws and policies, and education and awareness campaigns have been launched to promote acceptance and inclusion. However, much work still needs to be done to eradicate racism from our society.

Hate crimes related to race continue to occur, and the rise of online platforms has facilitated the spread of hate speech and xenophobic attitudes. It is essential to continue efforts to educate and raise awareness about the impact of racial discrimination and to take action to stop this type of hate crime. Individuals can play a significant role in combating racial discrimination by speaking out against prejudice and discrimination, educating themselves and others about diversity and inclusion, and supporting policies and initiatives that promote equality and justice for all.
