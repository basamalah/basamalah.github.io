19. What is the meaning of displaying grace or beauty in action? ​


Graceful

Graceful is the meaning of displaying grace or beauty in action. Being graceful refers to the physical quality of exhibiting attractive agility in the form of graceful movement, poise, or balance. The Latin word gratia, derived from gratus, which means pleasant, is the etymological source of the term grace. The term "gracefulness" has been used to refer to something that is visually pleasant.

Further explanation

Edmund Burke wrote that the concept of graceful is similar to that of beauty; it shares many of the same components. The concept of gracefulness is related to movement and posture. For each of them to be beautiful, there must be no indication of effort; a slight body inclination; and a calmness of the parts so as not to encroach upon one another or look split by abrupt and sharp angles.

People are sometimes compared to being "as graceful as a swan" or "as elegant as a ballerina" to express how graceful they are. Both movement and inanimate items are considered to exhibit grace.

Gracefulness is occasionally mistaken for gracility, or slenderness, despite the fact that the latter word comes from a different root: the Latin adjective gracilis (masculine or feminine), or gracile (neuter), which in either form means slender, and when transferred, for example, to discourse, takes the sense of "without ornament," "simple," or various other similar connotations.

The Shorter Oxford English Dictionary makes the following comments on courtesy: Recently abused for Gracefully slim (by connection with grace). This usage is terrible because the adjectives gracile and grace have nothing to do with one another. The Latin word gratia, which means pleasing and has nothing to do with slenderness or thinness, is the etymological root of grace.

Something that is beautiful, well-formed, and glides easily is said to be graceful. A ballet dancer is a perfect illustration of graceful. Elegant means graceful or beautiful in shape, composition, movement, or expression.
