5. Ati atihan festival and its use of music


The Ati-Atihan Festival is a Philippine festival held annually in January in honor of the Santo Niño in several towns of the province of Aklan, Panay Island. The biggest celebration is held during the third Sunday of January in the town of Kalibo, the province’s capital.
The festival uses a festive, vibrant, tribal music, a perfect fit for traditional dances. The music is commonly accompanied by instruments like drums, trumpets, xylophones, gongs, and lyres. Overall, its use of music suits the theme of the festivity.

18. what is the use of the music of the ati-atihan festival?


Answer:

Ati-Atihan Festival is a feast held annually on January in honor of the Santo Niño (Infant Jesus), Held on the third Sunday, in the town of Kalibo Philippines in the island of Panay originally came from Batan, Aklan, then adopted later by some neighboring towns. The name Ati-Atihan means "to be like Atis" or "to make believe Atis", the local name for the Aeta aborigines who first settled in Panay Island and other parts of the archipelago.

The festival consists of tribal dance, music, accompanied by indigenous costumes and weapons, and parade along the street. Christians and non-Christians observe this day with religious processions. It has inspired many other Philippine Festivals including the Sinulog Festival of Cebu and Dinagyang of Iloilo City, both adaptations of the Kalibo's Ati-Atihan Festival, and legally holds the title "The Mother of All Philippine Festivals" in spite of the other two festivals' claims of the same title.

The costumes worn at the festival is patterned after the African tribal design like those seen at the Rio Carnaval.

The main instruments used for the ATI-ATIHAN festival are:

1. Snare Drums: These are small drums with tight heads that are played with sticks. They create sharp, rhythmic beats and are a prominent instrument in the ATI-ATIHAN festival.

2. Triplets: These are small handheld drums that are played in sets of three. They produce a distinct sound and are used to create complex rhythms and patterns during the festival.

3. Bass Drum: This is a large drum that produces low-frequency beats and provides the foundation for the rhythm in the ATI-ATIHAN festival. It is typically played with a mallet.

4. Trumpets: These brass instruments are used to create melodic lines and add a celebratory tone to the festival. They are often played in groups and create a vibrant and energetic sound.

5. Cacophony: This is a type of instrument made from pieces of metal, such as cans or lids, that are attached to a frame and shaken to produce a chaotic and metallic sound. It adds a unique texture to the overall sound of the festival.

6. Xylophone: This is a percussion instrument with wooden bars that are struck with mallets to produce melodic tones. It is often used in the ATI-ATIHAN festival to create catchy and memorable melodies.

7. Lyres: These are stringed instruments similar to small guitars or ukuleles. They are plucked or strummed to produce melodic lines and are used to add a melodic layer to the music during the festival.

8. Gongs: These large, metallic discs are struck with mallets to produce deep, resonant tones. They are used to create a powerful and majestic sound during the ATI-ATIHAN festival.

These instruments are used in combination to create the lively and rhythmic music that accompanies the vibrant and energetic dances of the ATI-ATIHAN festival.

1. Origin Of Ati Atihan Festival

The festivity was originally a pagan celebration to commemorate the Barter of Panay, where the Aeta accepted gifts from Bornean chieftains called Datu, who fled with their families to escape a tyrannical ruler, in exchange for being allowed to dwell in the Aeta's lands.

2. Cultural Of Ati atihan Festival

This is a celebration in honor of the Infant Jesus, the town's patron. Ati-Atihan, observed in honor of the Child Jesus. Ati-atihan means 'making like Atis,' that is, pretending to be like the aboriginal natives that once inhabited Aklan.
