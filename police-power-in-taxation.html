13. A. Definition of Terms. Provide a simple definition of the following terms and concepts. 1. Power - 2. Police Power - 3. Eminent Domain - 4. Taxation - 5. Just Compensation - 6. Public Use - 7. Public Purpose. 8. Taxes - 9. "The Power to Destroy" - 10. Executive Power -​


Power: the ability or capacity to do something, often used in reference to authority or control.

Police Power: the power of the government to regulate and control the behavior of individuals and businesses in order to protect the health, safety, and general welfare of the public.

Eminent Domain: the power of the government to take private property for public use, with just compensation paid to the property owner.

Taxation: the process of levying and collecting taxes on individuals, businesses, and other entities by the government in order to fund public services and projects.

Just Compensation: the fair market value paid by the government to a property owner for property taken through eminent domain or other government actions.

Public Use: a use of property that is for the benefit of the public, such as roads, schools, or parks.

Public Purpose: a purpose or goal that is intended to benefit the public as a whole, such as promoting public health or safety.

Taxes: a compulsory financial charge imposed by the government on individuals, businesses, and other entities in order to fund public services and projects.

"The Power to Destroy": a phrase used to describe the power of taxation, which can be used to destroy or bankrupt individuals, businesses, and other entities if not properly regulated.

Executive Power: the power of the executive branch of government to enforce laws and make decisions related to the administration of government policies and programs.

3. The power of reward is the ability to offer incentives or rewards in exchange for desired behavior or actions.

4. Referent power is the ability to influence others through personal characteristics or charisma.

5. Legitimate power is the ability to influence others through the authority given to a person or position by society or the law.

6. Purchasing power is the ability to influence others through the use of money or financial resources.

7. The power of eminent domain is the ability of the government to seize private property for public use, with compensation.

8. The power of taxation is the ability of the government to collect taxes from individuals and organizations.

9. Police power is the ability of the government to regulate and control the actions and behavior of individuals and organizations for the protection of public health, safety, and welfare.
