Explain the importance relationship of speaker and role and responsibilities of the speaker by creating a tourism slogan about "The Beauty of the Philippines" which would serve as a promotional advertisement to tourists. Create your slogan inside the box provided below: Be guided by the rubric provided which will be used in assessing your tourism commercial slogan.​​


Answer:

The relationship between the speaker and the role of the speaker is important because it helps to establish trust and credibility with the audience. The speaker's words and actions should be aligned with the role they are representing, and they should be able to effectively communicate the message and vision of the organization they are representing.

In the context of promoting tourism to the Philippines, the speaker should be knowledgeable about the country's natural beauty and attractions, and be able to effectively communicate the unique experiences and cultural offerings that tourists can enjoy in the Philippines.

As the speaker, some of your responsibilities would include:

Communicating the key messages and vision of the tourism campaign to the audience.

Engaging the audience and capturing their attention through effective storytelling and engaging visuals.

Providing accurate and up-to-date information about the Philippines, including its natural beauty, attractions, and cultural offerings.

Encouraging the audience to visit the Philippines and experience its beauty for themselves.

Maintaining a positive and professional image for the Philippines as a destination for tourists.

With these responsibilities in mind, a possible slogan for the tourism campaign could be: "Experience the beauty of the Philippines – a tropical paradise waiting for you to discover." This slogan effectively captures the unique natural beauty of the Philippines, and encourages the audience to visit and experience it for themselves. It also incorporates a sense of adventure and discovery, which can be appealing to potential tourists.

Answer:

The Department of Tourism (DOT) is mandated to encourage, promote, and develop tourism as a major socio-economic activity to generate foreign currency and local employment and to spread the benefits of tourism to a wider segment of the population with the support, assistance, and cooperation of both the private and public.
