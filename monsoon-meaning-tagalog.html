5. what does the word monsoon literally mean?


Answer:

The word monsoon comes from the Arabic word mausim, which means weather. Owing to the yearly appearance of torrential rain, indicating a marked shift in weather, mausim gradually became monsoon.

Answer:

The word “monsoon” is from the Arabic word “mausim,” meaning season of winds, which is often applied to the seasonal reversals of the wind direction along the shores of the Indian Ocean and surrounding regions, especially in the Arabian Sea

Question:

Means to mitigate or lessen negative effects of monsoon?​

Answer:

Mangrove forests are vital for stabilizing and mitigating the effects of both summer and winter monsoons. Natural elements such as native woods and grasslands, as well as urban forestry and agriculture projects such as community gardens, will aid in the absorption of heavy rains, soil stabilization, and moisture retention.
