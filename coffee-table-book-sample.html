15. Daniel left his coffee mug on his study table. If the coffee mug weighs 0.2 kg and the height of his study table is 1.4 m, calculate for the potential energy of his coffee mug.


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The potential energy of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{PE = mgh}[/tex]

where:

PE = potential energy

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s²

h = height

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for PE

PE = mgh

PE = (0.2 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(1.4 m)

[tex]\boxed{PE = \text{2.744 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the potential energy of the coffee mug is 2.744 J.

[tex]\\[/tex]
