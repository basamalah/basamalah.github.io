1. The circular wading pool of little Bettina has a radius of 1.5 meters. It Bettina walks around it, how many meters will that be?​


Answer:

The distance Bettina would walk around the circular wading pool can be calculated using the formula for the circumference of a circle, which is:

Circumference = 2 x π x radius

where π (pi) is a mathematical constant approximately equal to 3.14.

Substituting the given radius of 1.5 meters into the formula, we get:

Circumference = 2 x 3.14 x 1.5

Circumference = 9.42 meters

Therefore, Bettina would walk around the circular wading pool for approximately 9.42 meters.
