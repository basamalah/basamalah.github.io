6. What are the basic principles in layout and composition


Answer:

Balance: The distribution of visual weight across a design. A balanced composition can create a sense of harmony and stability.

Contrast: The juxtaposition of elements with varying characteristics, such as color, size, shape, or texture. Contrast can add visual interest and depth to a design.

Proximity: The grouping of related elements together to create a visual relationship between them. Proximity can help to organize information and create a sense of hierarchy.

Alignment: The placement of elements along a common axis or edge. Alignment can create a sense of order and structure, and can help to establish a visual hierarchy.

White Space: The space between elements in a design. White space can help to create a sense of balance, emphasize certain elements, and provide visual breathing room.

Repetition: The use of similar or identical elements throughout a design. Repetition can help to create a sense of unity and consistency.

Proportion: The relationship between the size of elements in a design. Proportion can help to create a sense of balance and harmony.
