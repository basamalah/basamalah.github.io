4. what's the effect of elevation in haircutting


Answer:

The two basic lines used in haircutting are straight and curved. Elevation creates graduations and layers and is usually described in degrees. In a blunt or one- length cut there is no elevation (0 degrees). Elevations below 90 degrees build weight; elevations of 90 degrees or higher remove weight or layer hair

Answer:

Elevation is the up and down (vertical) movement of hair.When hair is lifted or elevated in-between natural fall and 90° from the head shape, it takes weight away from the bottom and builds weight up and away from the head shape.

13. Writing narrative The Haircut by Paul Cadmus​


It was a hot summer day in New York City, and I was in desperate need of a haircut. As I walked down the street, I noticed a small barbershop tucked away in a corner. The sign outside read "George's Barbershop," and I decided to give it a try.

Upon entering, I was greeted by an older man with a bald head and a thick mustache. He introduced himself as George and motioned for me to take a seat in his chair. As he wrapped a cloth around my neck, he asked me how I wanted my hair cut.

I told him that I wanted a short, simple cut that would be easy to maintain. George nodded and began to work his magic. He carefully ran his fingers through my hair, making precise cuts with his scissors. He had a gentle touch, and I could tell that he was experienced in his craft.

As he worked, George told me stories about his life in the city. He had been a barber for over thirty years, and he had seen all sorts of people come and go. He talked about the changes he had seen in the neighborhood, and how much he missed the old days.

As he finished up my haircut, George dusted off my shoulders and handed me a mirror. I looked in the mirror and was pleased with the results. My hair was clean and neatly trimmed, exactly what I had asked for.

As I got up to leave, George handed me a business card and told me to come back anytime. I thanked him and left the barbershop, feeling refreshed and ready to take on the day.

That haircut at George's barbershop may have been just a simple task, but the experience was far from ordinary. It was a reminder that sometimes, it's the small things in life that can make all the difference. And for that, I am grateful to George and his craft.
