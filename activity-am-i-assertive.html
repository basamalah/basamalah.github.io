Explanation:

A basic assertion is a straightforward statement that expresses a belief, feeling, opinion, or preference. For example: “I would like to finish this email before we have our conversation.” or “I would like you to wait until I have finished speaking.” An emphatic assertion occurs when the speaker or writer conveys sympathy or recognition of the other person's position or feelings. This acknowledgement is then followed by a statement that conveys the speaker's own beliefs. Escalating Assertion. Occurs when the other person fails to respond to your basic assertions, and you become increasingly firm. May include the mention of resulting action on your part. For example, "If you don't complete the work on my car by 5:00 tomorrow, I will call the Better Business Bureau." It involves the first person pronoun "I" and is useful for expressing negative feelings.

22. Direction: Compare and Contrast the Three Types of Assertion1. similarities of basic and emphatic Assertion 2. similarities of basic Assertion and I-Language Assertion 3. similarities of emphatic and I-Language assertion 4. similarities of Three Assertion ​


Answer:

1. The similarities between basic and emphatic assertion are:

- Both types involve making a statement or claim about a situation or opinion.

- Both types require confidence and conviction in the statement being made.

- Both types aim to convey a clear message to the listener.

2. The similarities between basic assertion and I-language assertion are:

- Both types involve expressing a personal belief or experience.

- Both types require the speaker to take responsibility for their feelings or thoughts.

- Both types encourage open communication and a willingness to share one's perspective.

3. The similarities between emphatic assertion and I-language assertion are:

- Both types involve expressing a strong opinion or belief.

- Both types require the speaker to take ownership of their statement.

- Both types aim to communicate the speaker's perspective in a clear and assertive manner.

4. The similarities between all three types of assertion are:

- All three involve making a statement or claim about a situation or opinion.

- All three require the speaker to communicate their perspective with confidence and clarity.

- All three aim to create open and honest communication between the speaker and listener.
