18. What is the I on iPhone?​


Answer:

According to an article,  the naming pattern began in 1998, when Steve Jobs first released the iMac. Since then, nearly all new Apple products, up until the launch of the Apple TV and the Apple Watch have been preceded by the lowercase letter. When Jobs first introduced the iMac, Steve Jobs explained the meaning behind the "i" at the computer's launch event (which we can now revisit on YouTube-VIDEO: YOUTUBE, EVERYSTEVEJOBSVIDEO)  . “iMac comes from the marriage of the excitement of the Internet with the simplicity of Macintosh,” he said. “We are targeting this for the no. 1 use that consumers tell us they want a computer for, which is to get on the Internet simply and fast.”

In the beginning, it stood for internet. But it was so, so much more. The prefix also stood for individual, instruct, inform, and inspire. So much meaning in a single letter!
