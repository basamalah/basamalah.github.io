21. Find the angle by which i1 lags v1 if v1 = 120


Leading/Lagging deals with the phase relationship between two sine or cosine wave.

v1 has a phase angle of -84°. i has a phase angle of +99°. So we look at counter-clockwise rotation.

To get the angle between the two waveforms, we subtract the most positive from the other.

So +99° - (-84°) = 183°. So i leads v1 by 183°. If we subtract from 360° - 183° = 177°. So if we look at v1 going first, i lags v1 by 177°.

Second one we have sin and cosine. We have to convert our sin to cos.

cos t = sin(t + 90°) <-- trigonometric identity.

i1 = 6.3 sin (120πt − 93°) A = 6.3 sin (120πt − 93° + 90°) A = 6.3 sin (120πt − 3°) A

So -3° - (-84°) = 81°

i leads v1 by 81°. Counter-clockwise rotation.

If you want to reference it to lagging 360° - 81° = 279°. i lags v1 by 279°.

29. .Maria has two printers – Printer Z and Printer A. She set Printer Z as the default printer. What will happen the next time she prints a document?


What will happen the next time she prints a document? --- The answer is Printer Z will print her documents.

Even when Maria has two printers, since she has set up Printer Z as her default printer, once she has printed her documents, Printer Z will be her default printer, meaning it will be chosen automatically. In any case that she would want to use her other printer, she must select printers on Print options. A default printer is a type of printer which print jobs will be automatically accepted unless she has changed the option.  

To Select default printers, user might navigate to Start Menu > Control Panel > Printers & Scanners then on the printer list, he must right - click on the name of the printer then select Set as Default.
