7. What were your consideration in classifying your study habits?


My consideration in classifying my study habits includes the right attitude, choosing space for studying, identify distractions, setting a realistic schedule, and improving the organizing of my thoughts. Effective study habits will make me study smarter, not harder. The purpose of study habits is to help students take full advantage of the learning process. School success will require time and dedication to enable a student to improve skills such as note-taking, organizing of thoughts, reading strategies, and effective writing style. Giving importance to your prior knowledge is also important in establishing good study habits.

Study Habits

These are the time tested effective study habits of successful students:

Develop a study plan. Make a schedule of a subject to be studied in every session to avoid long hours of study with too much information. Plan specific times devoted only for studying. Establish a routine schedule of studying. Set realistic goals to meet when studying. Avoid waiting for the deadline of submission to feel the rush of studying. Start studying with your most difficult assignment or subject so that your mental energy is enough to focus on the lesson. Review notes before answering an assignment. Keep distractions away during study time. For example, put a smartphone on an airplane or silent mode when studying. Have a study buddy that you can share ideas about the lesson and learn from each other. Have a checklist of all school requirements to be checked over the weekend to make sure that you are ready when classes begin on Monday.

13. what were your considerations in classifying your study habits


Answer:

Here are some factors to consider in classifying your study habits:

1. Exhibiting your right attitude

Maintaining the right attitude while studying keeps you focused and diligent in achieving your study goals. Having the right attitude should include perseverance, competitiveness, focus, flexibility, right mindedness, and good composure.

2. Establishing the right environment

Your environment will surely impact how you are going to approach your study habits and goals. If your environment is noisy and messy, then your study habits will be disorganized and distracted. Therefore, you should create an environment that will be helpful and necessary on how you are going to study.

3. Setting a realistic schedule

Schedules are highly encouraged in making an effective study habit. This will help you keep your progress on track and evaluate whether your study habit is effective or not. If you must study during a certain period of time, then you must stick to your plan, but make sure that the schedule is realistic and achievable.
