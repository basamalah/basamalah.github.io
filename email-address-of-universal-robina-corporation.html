Electronic mail, often known as email or e-mail, is a way for individuals to communicate with one another using electronic devices. At a period when "mail" solely referred to physical mail, email was therefore developed as the electronic (digital) equivalent of, or counterpart to, mail (hence the prefix e- + mail).

Further explanation

An email address is now frequently seen as a fundamental and essential component of many activities in business, trade, government, education, entertainment, and other areas of everyday life in most nations. Email later became a ubiquitous (extremely widely used) communication channel. The communication channel is email, and each message delivered over email is referred to as an email (mass/count distinction).

Email is accessible across both local area networks and computer networks, principally the Internet. The store-and-forward approach is the foundation of present-day email systems. Email servers receive, deliver, forward, and store emails. Users and their computers do not need to be online at the same time in order to send or receive messages; instead, they just need to establish a connection to a mail server or webmail interface.

Multipurpose Internet Mail Extensions (MIME) have allowed Internet email, which was once an ASCII text-only communication tool, to transport attachments with multimedia content and text in various character sets.
