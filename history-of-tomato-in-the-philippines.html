12. Make a summary and conclusion about the prices of tomato in the Philippines ​


Answer:

Tomato is a commonly consumed vegetable in the Philippines, and its price is an important factor for both producers and consumers. The price of tomato in the Philippines can vary depending on a number of factors such as supply and demand, weather conditions, pest and disease, and transportation cost.

According to a report from the Philippine Statistics Authority, the price of tomato in the Philippines has been fluctuating in the past few years. The average retail price of tomato in Metro Manila reached its highest level in 2019, with prices reaching as high as PHP 50/kg. However, the price dropped significantly in 2020 and 2021 due to the oversupply of tomatoes caused by the COVID-19 pandemic.

Factors such as weather conditions, pest and disease, and transportation cost also affect the tomato prices in the Philippines. A dry spell or typhoon can lead to a shortage of supply and a subsequent increase in prices. Similarly, pest and disease outbreaks can also lead to a decrease in supply and an increase in prices.

In conclusion, the price of tomato in the Philippines is affected by a number of factors such as supply and demand, weather conditions, pest and disease, and transportation cost. The price can fluctuate significantly and it's important for producers and consumers to be aware of these fluctuations in order to make informed decisions.
