7. The length of every chord in a circle is always greater than the length of a radius.​


Answer:

False

Step-by-step explanation:

Before we answer if it is true or false, we first define what a chord and a radius are.

A CHORD is a straight Line Segment wherein its endpoints lie anywhere on the circle.

That is, may it pass the center or not, as long as its endpoints are ON the circle, it is a chord.

On the other hand, the RADIUS is also a straight line segment in which one of its endpoints is at the center and the other is anywhere on the circle. It is also half the DIAMETER of the circle - a chord that passes through the center of the circle.

As we all know, the length of the RADIUS anywhere on the circle IS CONSTANT. That is because we draw the line directly from the center to anywhere on the circle.

Now, if we take note, a chord does not have any restrictions to its length, except for the fact that its ENDPOINTS SHOULD BE ON THE CIRCLE.

Thus, no matter how small a chord is, as long as it qualifies to the restrictions, then it is a chord.

Take a look at the drawing provided below.

See that the radius really does remains constant. But the chord can be as long as the diameter and as short as if the endpoints are touching each other.

Thus, the length of every chord in a circle is NOT ALWAYS greater than the radius.
