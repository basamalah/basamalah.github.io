7. debate about cornrow hairstyles (argument part)​


Answer:

Cornrow hairstyles have been a popular choice among people of African descent for many years. They are a traditional African hairstyle that involves braiding the hair into a series of tight, narrow rows that run parallel to the scalp. Some people enjoy cornrow hairstyles because they can be worn in a variety of styles and patterns, and they can be a fun and creative way to express oneself. However, some people have criticized cornrow hairstyles for cultural appropriation, arguing that non-black people who wear cornrows are taking ownership of a hairstyle that has deep cultural significance for black people. This debate often centers around issues of respect, cultural appreciation versus appropriation, and the right of people to express themselves in ways that may be considered culturally significant to others. Ultimately, whether or not someone chooses to wear cornrows is a personal decision that should be made with respect and consideration for others.

25. Why there is a need to analyze the fundamental principles of hairstyling?


PRINCIPLES OF HAIR DESIGN

The five important principles of hair design are: proportion, balance, rhythm, emphasis,

and harmony.

• Proportion- comparative relationship of one thing to another

Body proportion-a general guide for classic proportion os that the

hair should not be wider than the center of the shoulders, regardless

of body structure

• Balance- establishing equal or appropriate proportions to create symmetry;

balance can be symmetrical or asymmetrical

Symmetrical balance- occurs when an imaginary line is drawn

through the center of the face and the two halves form a mirror

image

Asymmetrical balcne- occurs when the two imaginary halves of a

hairstyle have equal visual weight but are positioned unevenly

• Rhythm- the regular pulsation of recurrent pattern of movement in a design; ex:

tight curls are a fast rhythm while long waves are a slow rhythm

• Emphasis (also known as focus)- what draws the eye first before it travels to the

rest of the design

• Harmony- creating unity in a design; most important of the principles

A harmonious design has: a form with interesting lines, and balance

and rhythm that strengthen the design
