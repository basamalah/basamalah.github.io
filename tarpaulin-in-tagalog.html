9. The area of the rectangular tarpaulin is 20 square meters. Its width is 2√2 meters. How long is the tarpaulin? with solution​


RADICALS

The area of the rectangular tarpaulin is 20 square meters. Its width is 2√2 meters. How long is the tarpaulin?

The tarpaulin is [tex] 5 \sqrt{2} [/tex] meters long.

SOLUTION:

We know the area of a rectangle is the product of its length and width, represented by the formula:

[tex] \qquad \large{\boxed{\text{Area = length × width}}} [/tex]

Since the length is of an unknown value, we modify that formula to solve for the length.

[tex] \qquad \large{\boxed{\mathrm{ Length = \frac{Area}{Width} }}} [/tex]

Given:

area = 20 sq. meterswidth = 2√2 meters

Solve:

» Rationalize the denominator.

[tex] \quad \begin{array} {|ccl|} \rm { Length} & = & \frac{20}{2\sqrt{2}} \\\\ & = & \rm { \frac{20}{2\sqrt{2}} \cdot \frac{2\sqrt{2}}{ 2\sqrt{2} }} \\\\ & = & \rm { \frac{20 \cdot 2\sqrt{2}}{2 \cdot 2\sqrt{2 \cdot 2} }} \\\\ & = & \rm { \frac{10 \cdot \cancel{2} \cdot \cancel{2} \sqrt{2}}{ \cancel{2} \cdot \cancel{2}\sqrt{2 \cdot 2} }} \\\\ & = & \rm { \frac{10 \sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{4} }} \\\\ & = & \rm { \frac{10 \sqrt{2}}{ 2 }} \\\\ \large \rm { \therefore \: Length} & = & \large{ \rm{ 5 \sqrt{2} \: meters }} \end{array} [/tex]

Therefore, the tarpaulin is [tex] 5 \sqrt{2} [/tex] meters long.

10. the length of the tarpaulin in 3 feet more than thrice its width and the area is 126ft squared. What is the length of the tarpaulin?


The area of a rectangle has the formula A = lw. The length is 3 ft more than thrice the width, which means l = 3w + 3.

lw = 126 ft²

(3w + 3)(w) = 126

3w² + 3w = 126

w² + w = 42

w² + w - 42 = 0; factor out

(w + 7)(w - 6) = 0

w = -7, 6

w = 6

l = 3w + 3

l = 3(6) + 3

l = 18 + 3

l = 21

The length of the tarpaulin is 21 feet.

16. The length of tarpaulin is 3 ft more than thrice its width and its area is 126ft squared . what is the length of tarpaulin?


L = length of tarpaulin
W = width of tarpaulin

L = 3 + 3W
A = L x W = 126

Substituting the value of L using the first equation, we have:
(3 + 3W) x W = 126
3W + 3W^2 = 126

Divide both sides by 3:
W + W^2 = 42
Or, W^2 + W - 42 = 0

Solving the equation, we get:
(W + 7) (W - 6) = 0
So, W = {-7, 6}
And lengths are never negative so W = 6.

Solving L:
L = 3 + 3W
L = 3 + 18
L = 21

So, L = 21 and W = 6. Area = 21 x 6 = 126
