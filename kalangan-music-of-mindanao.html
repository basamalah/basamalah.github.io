8. kalangan meaning in music


Answer:

Kalangan is Tausug vocal music. It can be divided into narrative and lyric songs, and further into the lugu and the paggabang traditions. The luguh traditio-n denotes unaccompanied religious songs, while the paggabang tradition applies to "more mundane" songs that are accompanied by the gabbang and biyula

Kalangan is Tausug vocal music. It can be divided into narrative and lyric songs, and further into the lugu and the paggabang traditions. 28. Task 2: Describe the following VocalMusic of Mindanao1. Lugu2. Kalangan3. Sa-il/Lunsey4. Dikker5. Bayok6. Ya-Ya​


Answer:

1. Lugu - Chants used in reading his/her Qur'an and other books in his/her Islam religion.

2. Kalangan - Songs used for serenading his/her loved ones.

3. Sa-a.unsey - An important chant to be sung by the wife to be during the ceremony that talks about married life.

4. Dikker - Sacred songs highlighted by the quotations from the Qur'an.

5. Bayok - Semi-generic term for a lyrical rendition of different improvised text. It is also the Maranao term for lullaby.

6. Ya-ya - Is a song of the Yakans to put the baby to sleep.
