Natural calamities and their effects

Explanation:

Their - sa kanila, PAGMAMAY-ARI ng maraming tao, bagay, hayop, o event

There - doon, tinutukoy ay LUGAR

Example:

Their:

1. Their smiles are beautiful.

- sinasabing ang mga ngiti nila ay maganda. Pagmamay-ari ng mga tao ang kanilang smiles.

2. I hate their dance.

- sinasabing ayaw mo sa sayaw nila. Pagmamay-ari ng mga tao ang kanilang dance/dance steps.

3. Their effects are really terrifying.

- sinasabing nakakatakot ang mga epekto/resulta. Pagmamay-ari ng event ang mga epekto/resulta.

There:

1. I went there.

- sinasabing pumunta ka roon. Pumunta ka sa isang lugar.

2. Sunny lives there.

- sinasabing si Sunny ay nakatira doon. Ang bahay ni Sunny ay lugar.

3. There will be calamities next week.

- sinasabing magkakaroon ng mga calamities sa inyo. Tinutukoy ay lugar kung nasaan ka.

8. as a student how will you face these calamities like earthquakes and what are the things that you will do to be prepared in the unexpected calamities? ​


Answer:

pack an emergency preparedness kit

Having an emergency preparedness kit on hand may help you keep your family safe during a disaster. Store supplies in a large waterproof container near a door or in your garage so you can grab it and find shelter quickly. Ready.gov recommends you have at least three days' worth of food, water and medications on hand. They also provide a list of items to include in your disaster kit:

Drinking water (at least one gallon per person per day)

Nonperishable food, such as canned veggies and protein bars

Manual can opener

Flashlights or portable lanterns and extra batteries

First aid kit

A crank- or battery-powered radio

Sanitation supplies: toilet paper, moist towelettes, soap, trash bags and disinfectants

Local maps

Depending on your situation, your kit might also include:

Baby food, bottles and diapers

Pet food

Prescription medications

Extra eyeglasses or contact lenses and solution

Dry clothing and blankets

11. Ano ang pagkaiba ng natural calamities sa man-made calamities?​


Answer:

Ang natural calamities ay ang mga trahedya o mga pangyayaring hindi mapipigilan ng mga tao. Ito ay pangunahing salungat na kaganapan buhat sa likas na proseso ng Daigdig. Halimbawa nito ay lindol, pagguho ng lupa, bagyo, baha, pagputok ng bulkan, etc.

Ang man-made calamities naman ay kagagawan ng mga tao. Tulad ng explosions, fire accidents, road accidents, gas leak, poisoning, oil spill and such.

21. Guide Questions: #1 1. How do these environmental calamities affect the life cycle of animals and other living organisms? 2. Which of the conditions above allows the life cycle of a frog and a cockroach to continue? Why?​


Answer:

1. How do these environmental calamities affect the life cycle of animals and

other living organisms?

All animals depend on plants. ... When the environment changes, some plants and animals survive and reproduce, and others die or move to new locations. All organisms cause changes in the environment where they live. Some of these changes are detrimental to the organism or other organisms, whereas others are beneficial.

All organisms rely on the abiotic factors of their environment in addition to relying on other organisms for survival. Organisms that live in the same environment affect other living organisms within that community. ... They rely on the grasses in their environment as a food source and energy supply.

2. Which of the conditions above allows the life cycle of a frog and cockroach  to continue? Why?

All cockroaches go through three stages of life: egg, nymph, and adult. While these insects usually live about 6 to 15 months, the cockroach lifespan varies based on species, moisture, and temperature in each of these phases.

The life cycle of a frog consists of three stages: egg, larva, and adult. As the frog grows, it moves through these stages in a process known as metamorphosis.
