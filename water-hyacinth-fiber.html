8. What characteristict make the water hyacinth flow in the water​


Explanation:

The leaves of the plant contain air-filled tissue, which gives the plant its unusual buoyancy, allowing it to float completely unsupported. The stems of the plant are filled with the same tissue, which allows the stems to support the leaves without dropping below the surface of the water.

13. life cycle of water hyacinth?​


Life cycle of water hyacinth⬆️

Water hyacinth grows from seed and through vegetative reproduction. Seeds are produced in capsules at the base of each flower. Daughter plants are produced by vegetative reproduction, remain attached to the parent plant until they are broken off by wind or other physical damage.

18. Specialized structures of water hyacinth


Water hyacinth is a rhizomatous and stoloniferous plant with long, pendant and adventitious roots. The leaves arise from the rhizome nodes and stand above the water. They are dark green, ovate and cordate at the base, borne on swollen bladder-like petioles (Pieterse, 1997).
