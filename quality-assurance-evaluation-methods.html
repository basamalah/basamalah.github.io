24. how do i maintain quality control and quality assurance?​


Answer:

Here are some steps you can take to maintain quality control and quality assurance:

Establish standards: Define the quality standards that your products or services must meet, and communicate them to your team members. This can include specifications for product design, manufacturing processes, testing, and customer service.

Implement quality control processes: Put in place quality control processes to ensure that products or services meet the required standards. This can include inspection of materials, monitoring of manufacturing processes, and testing of finished products.

Train employees: Train your employees on the quality standards, quality control processes, and best practices. This will ensure that they understand the requirements and can work to maintain quality.

Monitor performance: Monitor performance regularly to ensure that quality standards are being met. This can include analyzing data, conducting audits, and reviewing customer feedback.

Continuously improve: Continuously look for ways to improve quality by identifying areas for improvement and implementing corrective actions. This can involve reviewing processes, introducing new technology, or updating quality standards.

Foster a culture of quality: Foster a culture of quality within your organization by encouraging collaboration, transparency, and a commitment to continuous improvement. This will ensure that quality is embedded in your organization's culture and processes.

By following these steps, you can maintain quality control and quality assurance, which will help ensure that your products or services meet customer expectations, increase customer satisfaction, and build your reputation in the marketplace.

Though QC is absolutely necessary, QA is perhaps more important. ... Whereas QA is proactive, QC is reactive. QC detects bugs by inspecting and testing the product. This involves checking the product against a predetermined set of requirements and validating that the product meets those requirements.

Which comes first QA or QC?

Quality assurance is a proactive process which starts even before the work on the deliverable has started, while quality control is a reactive process and its activity starts as soon as you start working on the deliverable
