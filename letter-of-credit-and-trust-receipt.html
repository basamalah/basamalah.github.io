9. Percentage of toleranc with letter of credit


The MT 700 Swift message type has a field called percentage credit amount tolerance that is used to specify the tolerance with respect to the documentary credit amount as a percentage plus or minus that amount.

The terms "about" or "about," when used in conjunction with the credit's stated amount, quantity, or unit price, are to be interpreted as permitting a tolerance of no more than 10% above or below the amount, quantity, or unit price to which they apply. If the credit does not specify the quantity in terms of a predetermined number of packing units or individual items, and the total amount of the drawings does not exceed the credit, a tolerance of no more than 5% more or 5% less than the quantity of the goods is permitted.

Even when partial shipments are prohibited, a tolerance of up to 5% less than the credit amount is permitted as long as the quantity of the products, if specified, is transported in its entirety and the unit pricing, if specified, is not altered or rendered inapplicable. When the credit specifies a specific tolerance or makes use of the terms referred, this tolerance does not apply.

26. ACCOUNTING: I HAVE THE ANSWER & SOLUTION, I JUST NEED SHORT EXPLANATION (1 PARAGRAPH) REGARDING THE SOLUTION. How much is the unadjusted book receipts for December? a. P1,098,850 c. P1,113,600 b. P1,110,950 d. P1,116,150 ANSWER: B Solution: Adjusted Book Receipts P1,113,600 NFS Check, returned by the bank and recorded in December (5,200) Credit memo - this month (58,200) Credit memo - last month 60.750 Unadjusted Book Receipts P1,110,950


Answer:

The unadjusted book receipts for December are P1,110,950. This is found by starting with the adjusted book receipts of P1,113,600 and then making adjustments for the NFS check returned by the bank (-P5,200), credit memo for this month (-P58,200), and credit memo for last month (+P60,750). When all of these adjustments are made, the resulting amount is P1,110,950.

Community advocacy can make real change and understanding that change is important. Is it getting more people to take public transportation? Preventing childhood obesity? No matter the issue, knowing what you would like to see changed and being able to articulate it is half the battle. Many people just can’t say what the problem is and how they would fix it. Don't Wait

Waiting for other people to do something about it is a bad idea. Waiting is detrimental to action. Think of the verbal and emotional barriers folks create around change. You will hear folks say things like:

“Oh, that is a good idea, but we don't have the money to do that.” Financial hurdles are big, but money is available if people really want change. Your job is to make that case.

“Someone else is already working on that.” Are they really working on it or is that an excuse? Don’t take someone else’s word for it, do some research for yourself.

“We have always done it this way.” Yes, and this is why we still have a problem. Be respectful, but make the case for why this needs to happen a different way.
