23. Mr. Torreon made a loan from a bank to be used for his upcoming business mounting to 3,000,000.00 at 6% annually if this is to be paid equal payments every year for 13 years, find the annually payment and construct an amortization schedule.​PATULONG PO TY


The annual payment for the loan can be calculated using the formula for the annuity payment:

A = P * r * (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1)

where:

A = annual payment

P = principal (3,000,000.00)

r = annual interest rate (6% = 0.06)

n = number of payments (13)

Plugging in the values, we get:

A = 3,000,000.00 * 0.06 * (1 + 0.06)^13 / ((1 + 0.06)^13 - 1)

A = 619,902.82

An amortization schedule is a table that shows the breakdown of each payment into interest and principal payments over the life of the loan. It will show the remaining balance of the loan after each payment.

To construct an amortization schedule, you can use a spreadsheet or financial calculator.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the annual payment for Mr. Torreon's loan, you can use the formula for the equal payments of a loan:

Annual Payment = (Loan Amount * Interest Rate) / (1 - (1 + Interest Rate)^(-Number of Years))

Where:

Loan Amount = 3,000,000.00Interest Rate = 6% (expressed as 0.06)Number of Years = 13

Substituting the values into the formula:

Annual Payment = (3,000,000.00 * 0.06) / (1 - (1 + 0.06)^(-13))

= 180,000.00

So the annual payment is 180,000.00
