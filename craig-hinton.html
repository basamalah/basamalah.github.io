13. Who is craig mooney in psychology biography?


Answer:

Craig Mooney makes paintings of dramatic moments and heightened emotionality that are known for being expansive and expressive. Though a representational painter, the artist incorporates a myriad of abstract qualities throughout his paintings. In his figurative work, Mooney romanticizes his subjects and presents them in an atmospheric lens that is best described as dreamlike. His paintings appear to be capturing a moment suspended in time. While his work feels familiar, it is not specific. Rather it is, on a very basic level, symbolism of what could have been, has been or will be...

Born and raised in the heart of midtown Manhattan (NY), Mooney’s roots in art go back to his youth. His father, an amateur artist, taught him how to create oil paintings from discarded art supplies found on city streets. To Mooney, the city was an endless source of inspiration at an early age. Though the artist would later take classes in art both in high school and college, he regards this early exposure as the truest form of training he had ever received,

After a brief career in the film industry, the artist moved out of New York in the mid-nineties to rural Vermont. The open and bucolic settings of the countryside allowed Mooney new sources of inspiration.

Today, Mooney devotes himself full time to his art at his studio in Vermont.

A Martian sends a postcard home

The Images that was said or described in the Poem: A Martian sends a postcard home:

1st Image: Telephone- 9th stanza as per written on the book/module

Described as a ghost that needed soothing by the use of our voice and it will calm itself to sleep. But ironically we also wake it up by our touch or tickle of our finger. This may describe a phone or a telephone, whenever it rings we pick it up and speak to the other person on the other end of the line and when we need to call someone we also pick it up and start the conversation first.

3rd Image: Mist-2nd and 3rd stanza as per written on the book/module

It was described as something that makes the sky blurry or hazy, it allows the sky to take a rest on the ground which is often seen as fog or mist especially mornings. It was also described as blurry and groggy as a text under a tissue.

5th Image: Rain-4th stanza as per written on the book/module

Described as if the world turned into a huge television devoid of color and not much movement and the present colors that are seen are mostly pale or darker than it would normally do.

6th Image: Sleeping pair-14th and 15th stanza/line as per written on the book/module

It said that when the colors die or when the night falls people start to go to their respective areas to take a rest or sleep. That is also when “we read ourselves” because we are basically moving our eyes beneath our eyelids as if we really are reading ourselves in color.

8th Image: Car-5th and 6th stanza/line as per written on the book/module

He called it a model T, which is locked and in need of a key in order for it to move. In simple facts, it needed ignition to start and thus it is most probably describing a car or any automobile.

9th Image: Wristwatch-7th stanza/line as per written on the book/module

He said that the time was tied to our own wrist or is even kept in a box that makes it impatient and starts ticking.  

Images that were not described nor mentioned in the Poem: A Martian sends a postcard home:

2nd Image: Fly 4th Image: Night Sky 7th Image: Pineapple 10th Image: Glass of Milk 12th Image: Airplane 11th Image: Bowl of Noodles 13th Image: Toilet 14th Image: Yoyo 15th Image: Pair of eyes

The very idea of the poem A Martian sends a postcard home is to see the world from a different perspective. And in this poem’s case, the author Craig Raine used an alien or a Martian, in particular, to observe the world with brand new eyes and form of understanding and perspectives.  

The Martian starts off as an innocent creature who finds the most simple of things on Earth unnecessarily complicated, as if it’s so simple it’s so complicated to understand why. It described natural phenomena like rain and mist and how people sleep to take a rest. It also described the technological inventions of people like a clock, the phone, and cars.
