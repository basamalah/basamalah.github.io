7. there are 3 flowers in vase A and 2 flowers in vase b 6 more flowers are added to vase a how many flowers must be added to vase b so that the ratio of the number of flowers in vase a to the number of flowers in vase b remains the same as before?


Answer:

6 flowers

GIVEN:

Vase A = 3 flowers

Vase B = 2 flowers

6 more flowers are added to vase a

Step-by-step explanation:

Ratio of Vase A to Vase B = 3:2

Vase A + 6 more flowers

3 flowers + 6 more flowers = 9 flowers

3 : 2 = 9 : x or [tex]\frac{3}{2}[/tex] : [tex]\frac{9}{x}[/tex]

3x = 18

x = 6

6 flowers must be added to vase b so that the ratio of the number of flowers in vase a to the number of flowers in vase b remains the same as before.
