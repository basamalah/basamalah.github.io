20. Suppose you want to make 2000g of a solution of glucose in water that has 2.8% (m/m) concentration. How much glucose should you use?


Answer:

56g of glucose should be used in a 2000g solution of glucose in water to achieve a 2.8% concentration.

How to Calculate for Solute

  1.  Identify the given.

% m/m = 2.8% or 0.028 (glucose concentration)

g of solution = 2000g

 2. Identify which quantity is required.

g of solute = ? (glucose)

 3. List down the equation to be used.

g of solute = (% m/m)(g of solution)

 4. Then, substitute the given quantities in the equation.

g of solute = (0.028)(2000g)

 5. Simplify or solve the equation, and interpret the answer.

g of solute = 56g of glucose

Interpretation: To achieve a 2.8% concentration, a 2000g solution of glucose in water should contain 56g of glucose.

25. Suppose you want to make 2000g of a solution of glucose in water that has 2.8% (m/m) concentration. How much glucose should you use?​


Answer:

The amount of glucose that must be used is 56 g.

Explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

Mass of solution = 2000 g

Percent by mass = 2.8%

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Amount of glucose

Step 3: Solve for the amount of glucose

Solution:

The formula for percent by mass is given by:

[tex]percent \: by \: mass \: = \frac{mass \: of \: solute}{mass \: of \: solution} \times 100[/tex]

[tex]2.8 = \frac{mass \: of \: solute}{2000 \: g} \times 100[/tex]

[tex]mass \: of \: solute \: = \frac{(2000g)(2.8)}{100} [/tex]

[tex]mass \: of \: solute \: = 56 \: g[/tex]
