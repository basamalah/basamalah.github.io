Limiting and Excess Reactants:
The terms limiting and excess reactants refer to the reactant species in a particular chemical reaction, that are, respectively, fully consumed or remain after the reaction proceeds to completion (product side). This means that there is a mismatch of the starting stoichiometric molar quantities of these reactants (mole ratio). To identify which reactant is limiting, you must balance the reaction equation and convert all starting reactant quantities into numbers of moles.
