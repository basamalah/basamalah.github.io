14. During a rescue operation, two ships were sent by the coast guards to rescue the passengers trapped in a lifeboat. To make the rescue operation easier, a rectangular coordinate system was used to map the entire rescue location. Ship A has coordinates (-1, -2) and ship 8 has coordinates (-4, 11. Both ships have spotlights fixated on the same lifeboat. The light beam from ship Atravels along the line y=2x The light beam from ship 8travels along the line y=x+5. As the coast guard personnel on duty, you are tasked to determine the coordinates of the lifeboat to guide the ships. You are to submit to the team leader of the rescue operation an accurate and properly labeled sketch that shows the coordinates of the two ships and the lifeboat. ​


Answer:

To determine the coordinates of the lifeboat using the information given, we need to find the point of intersection between the two lines representing the light beams from the two ships.

The equation for the line from Ship A is y = 2x.

The equation for the line from Ship B is y = x + 5.

To find the point of intersection, we can set the two equations equal to each other and solve for x and y.

y = 2x

y = x + 5

x + 5 = 2x

x = 5

Substituting this value of x back into the first equation, we get:

y = 2(5)

y = 10

Therefore, the coordinates of the lifeboat are (5, 10).

To create a labeled sketch, you would need to plot the coordinates of the two ships and the lifeboat on a rectangular coordinate system and label them accordingly

22. Ship A is at a distance of 3km from the lighthouse, whereas ship B is 4 km away. If the lines of sight from the lighthouse to the two ship form an angle of 60 degrees, then how far are the two ships from each other


Answer:

The two ships are 3.61 km away from each other.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be the distance between the two ships

Using the Law of Cosines, we have

x = sqrt (4²+3²-2(4)(3)cos60°)

x = sqrt (25-24cos60°)

x = sqrt (25-12)

x = sqrt (13)

x ≈ 3.61 km.
