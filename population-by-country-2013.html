4. does the table of values to the populating of the phil from 2005 to 2013 undicates function why?​


Answer:

Yes, the table of values of the Philippine population from 2005 to 2013 is a function.  

It is a one-to-one relation where no first element (x, year) and second elements (y, population) are repeated.

Step-by-step explanation:

A function is a relation where no first elements (x) are repeated.

The years 2005 to 2013 are the first elements (x), and the corresponding populations for each year are the second elements (y).

In the table of values of the year and population, apparently the year are listed from earlier to the latest year and no years are repeated.

Therefore, the table of values of the Philippine population represents a function.

The Philippine Population from 2005 to 2013:

First elements (x) = year

Second elements (y) = population

28. 1. The province has the population of 241 000 in the year 2009. At this time the population was growing exponentially at a rate of 0.9% a year. Assume the population growth follows the law of natural growth. a. Find the population in terms of time. b. Estimate the population of the province in 2013. c. When will the population of the province reach 350 000.​


a. Find the population in terms of time.

   Pn = 241,000 (1.009)ⁿ

b. Estimate the population of the province in 2013 is 249,794.

c. When will the population of the province reach 350 000.​

   The province's population will reach 350,000 around 2051.

Step-by-step explanation:

Be discovered:

The province has the population of 241 000 in the year 2009.

At this time the population was growing exponentially at a rate of 0.9% a year.

Assume the population growth follows the law of natural growth.

Question:

a. Find the population in terms of time.

b. Estimate the population of the province in 2013.

c. When will the population of the province reach 350 000.​

Asked:

Pn = Po (1 + b)ⁿ

a. Find the population in terms of time.

   Pn = Po (1 + b)ⁿ

   Pn = 241,000 (1 + 0,009)ⁿ

   Pn = 241,000 (1.009)ⁿ

b. Estimate the population of the province in 2013.

2009 - 2013 = 4 years

    [tex]Pn = 241,000 (1.009)^n\\\\P_4=241000(1,009)^4\\\\p_4=241000(1.03649)\\\\p_4=249794[/tex]

Estimate the population of the province in 2013 is 249,794.

c. When will the population of the province reach 350 000.​

    [tex]Pn = 241,000 (1.009)ⁿ\\\\350,000=241,000(1.009)^n\\\\(1.009)^n=350,000:241,000\\\\(1.009)^n=1.4523\\\\(1,009)^4^1=1.4439\\(1.009)^4^2=1.4567[/tex]

The province's population will reach 350,000 in about 42 years.

2009 + 42 = 2051

The province's population will reach 350,000 around 2051.
