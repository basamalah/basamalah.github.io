Four values of f are given in the following table: [tex]\quad[/tex]x [tex]\quad\quad[/tex]f(x)[tex]\quad[/tex]13 [tex]\quad\quad[/tex]4[tex]\quad[/tex]10 [tex]\quad\quad[/tex]6[tex]\quad[/tex]7 [tex]\quad\quad[/tex]8[tex]\quad[/tex]4 [tex]\quad\quad[/tex]10Give the definition for f(x).​


Answer:

To find the definition for f(x) as a linear function, we can use the slope-intercept form of a linear equation, which is:

y = mx + b

where:

y is the dependent variable (in this case, f(x))

x is the independent variable

m is the slope of the line

b is the y-intercept (the value of y when x = 0)

To find the slope, we can use the two points (13, 4) and (4, 10):

m = (y2 - y1) / (x2 - x1)

= (10 - 4) / (4 - 13)

= -6/9

= -2/3

To find the y-intercept, we can use the point (13, 4) and the slope we just found:

4 = (-2/3)(13) + b

b = 18.67 (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the definition for f(x) as a linear function is:

f(x) = (-2/3)x + 18.67

Step-by-step explanation:

Since f(x) is a linear function, we can write its equation in the form f(x) = mx + b, where m is the slope and b is the y-intercept.

To find the slope, we can use any two points from the table. Let's use the points (13, 4) and (10, 6). The slope is given by:

m = (6 - 4) / (10 - 13) = -2/3

To find the y-intercept, we can use any point from the table and the slope we just calculated. Let's use the point (13, 4). We have:

4 = (-2/3) * 13 + b

Solving for b, we get:

b = 22/3

Therefore, the equation for f(x) is:

f(x) = (-2/3) x + 22/3

Give the definition for f(x).

SOLUTION & ANSWER:

By the two-point formula, the equation of a line through points [tex]\sf (x₁, y₁), (x₂, y₂)[/tex] is

[tex]\sf y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)[/tex]

where [tex]\sf{\bold{m = \frac{x₂\: -\: x₁}{y₂\: -\: y₁}}},[/tex] the slope of the line. The choice of the two points from the given four is arbitrary, so we will select the middle two ordered pairs.

[tex]\sf x₁ = 10[/tex][tex]\sf y₁ = 6[/tex][tex]\sf x₂ = 7[/tex][tex]\sf y₂ = 8[/tex]

[tex]\sf m = \frac{x₂\: -\: x₁}{y₂\: -\: y₁} = \frac{7\: -\: 10}{8\: -\: 6} = -\frac{3}{2}[/tex]

The first formula becomes

[tex]\sf y - 10 = -\frac{3}{2} (x - 6)[/tex]

Since [tex]\sf{\bold{f(x) = y}},[/tex] solve for [tex]\sf {\bold{y}}.[/tex] First, distribute [tex]\sf -\frac{3}{2}[/tex] throughout the difference at right:

[tex]\sf y - 10 = -\frac{3}{2}x -(-\frac{3}{2})\: 6[/tex]

[tex]\sf y - 10 = -\frac{3}{2}x -(-9)[/tex]

[tex]\sf y - 10 = -\frac{3}{2}x + 9[/tex]

Isolate [tex]\sf{\bold{y}} [/tex] by adding 10 to both sides:

[tex]\sf y - 10 + 10 = -\frac{3}{2}x + 9 + 10[/tex]

[tex]\quad\quad\quad\quad[/tex][tex]\sf y = -\frac{3}{2}x + 19[/tex]

Replacing, the difinition of [tex]\sf {\bold{f(x)}}[/tex] is

[tex]\begin{gathered}{\blue{\boxed{\sf{\purple {\small{f(x) = -\frac{3}{2}x + 19}}}}}}\end{gathered}[/tex]
