10. meaning pandanggo no neneng


Answer:

Pandanggo is a Philippine folk dance which has become popular in the rural areas of the Philippines.

Answer:

Pandanggo is a Philippine folk dance which has become popular in the rural areas of the Philippines. The dance evolved from Fandango, a Spanish folk dance, which arrived in the Philippines during the Hispanic period. The dance is accompanied by castanets.[1] This dance, together with the Jota, became popular among the illustrados or the upper class and later adapted among the local communities. In the early 18th century, any dance that is considered jovial and lively was called Pandanggo.

Col. Antonio R. Buenaventura

Antonino R. Buenaventura (1904-1996), a composer and teacher, was a National Student of the Philippine Musical Arts who devoted seven decades to promoting Filipino music and creating songs based on folk music of various ethnic group in the country.

Buenaventura was born on May 4, 1904 in Bal1wag, Bul4kan and is the offspring of Leocadia Ramirez and Lucino Buenaventura. When he was young, he learned solfeggio from his music teacher in their hometown. He also pl4ys the cl4rinet well and composes music well.

He studied at the University of the Philippines Conservatory of Music and obtained his Teacher's Diploma, majoring in science and composition in 1932. Nicanor Abel4rdo, Francisco Santiago, and Jeno von Takacs were his teachers at UP.

Buenaventura became a band instructor and conductor at the Philippine Military Academy in 1939; conductor of the Philippine 4rmy ‘Band; and co-conductor of Ramon Tapales of the Municipal Symphony Orchestra of Manil4 in its performance in Hongkong, Yokohama, Guam and Hawaii.

He also became the director of music at the University of Santo Tomas Conservatory of Music in 1961 and the University of the East (UE) School of Music and Arts.

On several occasions, he represented the Philippines in various international meetings, including: International Council of Music conference in Rome; 1962 in Hamburg; 1964 as president of the National Music Council; Festival of the Asian League of Composers in Kyoto, Japan; 1974 as vice-chairman of the League of Filipino Composers; and the General Assembly of the International Society for Music Education in Switzerl4nd in 1976.
