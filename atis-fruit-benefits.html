• Since there are a total of 20 fruits, let's equate the sum of the number of guavas and the number of atis.

[tex] \large \tt x + 4x = 20 \\ \large \tt 5x = 20 \\ \large \tt x = 20 \div 5 \\ \huge \boxed {\tt x = 4}[/tex]

ANSWER:

Answer:

B. Php 28

Step-by-step explanation:

let M is the price of mango

let A is the price of atis.

1st eq. 2M + A = 120

find A

A = 120 -2M

2nd eq. M + 3A = 220

substitute A from 2nd eq.

M + 3(120-2M) = 220

M + 360 - 6M = 220

6M-M = 360-220

5M = 140

M = 28 (price of mango)
