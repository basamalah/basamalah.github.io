1. EAPP is grounded in four academic papers the,____paper, concept paper, position paper, and report paper, which are tradionally taught for college freshman​


EAPP is grouped in four academic papers. They are reaction paper, concept paper, position paper, and report paper.

Reaction paper

A reaction paper offers a thoughtful and well-informed view of the world, popular culture, and the arts as well as a rational and well-founded response to it.

Concept paper

The concept paper serves as the initial form of communication between the doctoral student and the doctoral committee, laying the groundwork for the practical dissertation process. The Concept Paper essentially serves as a proposal, giving the doctoral student the chance to specify a research area and get preliminary comments on the proposed study. A carefully thought-out Concept Paper will pique the dissertation committee's attention and lay out a concise strategy for the student's dissertation.

Position paper

A position paper is an essay that takes a stand on a particular topic. On the basis of the information gathered, it presents reasons in favor of the writer's viewpoint.

Report paper

It is a written account of a specific circumstance that includes both qualitative and quantitative information. An effective report has an impartial tone while also conveying humanity.

2. Pa help EAPP subject Give your insights about the 8 features of a concept paper


Here are eight features of a concept paper:

1. Title: A concept paper should have a clear and concise title that reflects the main focus of the proposed project or research study.

2. Background or Introduction: This section provides the context and rationale for the proposed project or research study. It outlines the problem or issue that the project aims to address and provides relevant background information to establish the need for the proposed concept.

3. Objectives: This section outlines the specific goals and objectives of the proposed project or research study. It should clearly state what the project intends to achieve and how it will contribute to addressing the identified problem or issue.

4. Methodology or Approach: This section describes the overall approach and methodology that will be used to implement the proposed project or research study. It should provide an overview of the research design, data collection and analysis methods, or project implementation strategies.

5. Scope and Limitations: This section outlines the boundaries and limitations of the proposed project or research study. It should clearly state what the project will and will not cover, and any constraints or restrictions that may impact the implementation or outcomes of the concept.

6. Expected Outcomes or Deliverables: This section outlines the expected results or deliverables of the proposed project or research study. It should clearly state what will be achieved as a result of the project and how it will contribute to addressing the identified problem or issue.

7. Timeline or Schedule: This section provides a timeline or schedule for the proposed project or research study. It outlines the key milestones, deadlines, and activities that will be undertaken throughout the project's duration.

8. Budget: This section provides an estimated budget for the proposed project or research study. It should include a breakdown of the anticipated costs, funding sources, and any other financial considerations.

These are some of the key features of a concept paper. It's important to note that the specific requirements for a concept paper may vary depending on the purpose, audience, and guidelines provided by the intended recipients of the concept paper, such as funding agencies, academic institutions, or other stakeholders. It's always advisable to carefully review and follow any specific guidelines or instructions provided when developing a concept paper.

1. Title: The title is the first thing that readers will see, so it should be attention-grabbing and accurately reflect the essence of the proposed project or study. It should be concise yet informative, providing a clear indication of the content.

2. Introduction: The introduction should effectively engage the reader and provide a compelling overview of the proposed project or study. It should establish the context and significance of the topic, and create interest in the reader to continue reading further.

3. Problem Statement: The problem statement is a critical component of the concept paper as it identifies the issue or problem that the proposed project or study aims to address. It should be well-defined, specific, and supported by evidence to highlight the need for the project or study.

4. Objectives: The objectives should be clearly stated and aligned with the problem statement. They should be SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound), providing a clear roadmap of what the project or study aims to achieve. Well-defined objectives demonstrate a clear sense of purpose and direction.

5. Methodology: The methodology section should provide a clear description of the approach and methods that will be used to conduct the proposed project or study. It should be well-reasoned, feasible, and aligned with the research design or project plan. Including details about data collection, analysis, and other procedures adds credibility to the concept paper.

6. Literature Review: The literature review should demonstrate a thorough understanding of the existing knowledge and research related to the proposed project or study. It should highlight the gaps or limitations in the literature and provide a rationale for why the proposed project or study is needed to address those gaps. A well-structured literature review strengthens the theoretical foundation of the concept paper.

7. Budget: The budget section should provide a realistic estimate of the financial resources required to complete the proposed project or study. It should be well-justified and transparent, indicating how the resources will be allocated and used. A well-planned budget shows that the project or study is feasible and financially viable.

8. Conclusion: The conclusion should effectively summarize the main points of the concept paper and reinforce the significance of the proposed project or study. It should leave a lasting impression on the reader and emphasize the potential impact or contributions of the project or study. A strong conclusion reinforces the value and relevance of the concept paper.

Answer:

Ang pagsulat ng isang posisyon papel sa EAPP ay pinahihintulutan ang mga mag-aaral na ipahayag ang kanilang mga personal na pananaw sa isang tiyak na paksa. Ang isang position paper ay naglalaman ng isang malinaw na argumento na ginagamit ng isang manunulat upang ipaliwanag ang kanilang mga pananaw. Narito ang ilang hakbang upang makatulong sa iyo na magsulat ng isang epektibong posisyon sa papel sa EAPP:

1. Piliin ang isang paksa. Ang unang hakbang ay upang pumili ng isang paksa na nagpapahayag ng iyong mga personal na pananaw. Maaaring maging isang kontrobersyal na paksa, isang isyu sa lipunan, o anumang bagay na alam mo na interesado ka.

2. Maghanap ng mga resource upang suportahan ang iyong argumento. Ang susunod na hakbang ay upang maghanap ng mga resource na makakatulong sa iyo na ipaliwanag ang iyong mga punto. Makakakuha ka ng mga resource mula sa mga website, libro, artikulo, o anumang iba pang mapagkukunan na maaaring makatulong sa iyo na makapagbigay ng isang malinaw na konklusyon.

3. Lumikha ng isang outline. Ang susunod na hakbang ay upang lumikha ng isang outline ng iyong posisyon papel. Ang outline ay magbibigay sa iyo ng isang malinaw na pagkakasunud-sunod ng mga punto na nais mong ipahayag.

4. Isulat ang iyong position paper. Sa pamamagitan ng paggamit ng iyong outline, bumuo ng isang maikling panimula na nagpapakita ng iyong paksa. Pagkatapos, ipaliwanag ang iyong mga punto at suportahan ang iyong argumento sa pamamagitan ng mga resource na nakuha mo. Sa huli, magbigay ng isang malinaw na konklusyon na nagpapakita ng iyong mga pananaw.
