7. description of a gimbal​


Answer:

A gimbal is a pivoted support that permits rotation of an object about an axis. ... For example, on a ship, the gyroscopes, shipboard compasses, stoves, and even drink holders typically use gimbals to keep them upright with respect to the horizon despite the ship's pitching and rolling.

29. gimbal classification​


Answer:

In industrial applications, gimbals are typically classified as a type of rotary stage and are also referred to as “gimbal mounts,” since they're commonly used for mounting mirrors, sensors, or other direction-sensitive equipment. Gimbal axes are driven with servo, stepper, or even piezo or voice coil motors.
