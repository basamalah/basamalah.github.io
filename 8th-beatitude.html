1. what does the 3rd,4th,5th,6th,7th,and 8th Beatitude tell us?


Answer:

The third, fourth, fifth, sixth, seventh, and eighth Beatitudes are as follows:

3. Blessed are the meek, for they will inherit the earth.

4. Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, for they will be filled.

5. Blessed are the merciful, for they will be shown mercy.

6. Blessed are the pure in heart, for they will see God.

7. Blessed are the peacemakers, for they will be called children of God.

8. Blessed are those who are persecuted because of righteousness, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven.

The third Beatitude tells us that those who are meek or humble will be rewarded with inheriting the earth. This means that they will receive blessings and rewards in this life and in the life to come.

The fourth Beatitude tells us that those who hunger and thirst for righteousness will be filled. This means that those who seek to do what is right and just in the eyes of God will be satisfied and rewarded.

The fifth Beatitude tells us that those who show mercy will receive mercy in return. This means that those who forgive and show compassion to others will also receive compassion and forgiveness from God.

The sixth Beatitude tells us that those who are pure in heart will see God. This means that those who have a sincere and genuine love for God and others will have a clear vision of God's love and presence in their lives.

The seventh Beatitude tells us that those who are peacemakers will be called children of God. This means that those who promote peace, love, and reconciliation will be recognized as God's children and will receive blessings and rewards.

The eighth Beatitude tells us that those who are persecuted because of righteousness will be rewarded with the kingdom of heaven. This means that those who suffer and endure hardships for the sake of doing what is right and just in the eyes of God will be blessed and rewarded in the afterlife.

24. beatitudes support materialism?​


Answer:

beatitude is specifically relevant to people that do not desire wealth or materialistic possessions. They instead focus on long term means of happiness by usually strengthening their relationship with God. People that are poor or defenceless; that have lost all hope usually experience this beatitude at its fullest. The opposite of this beatitude is to focus on one’s wealth or other means of short term happiness. It is speculated that to follow this Beatitude, one must sell all they own and live in financial distress. This, however, is an incorrect statement. One does not have to live in poor conditions or without any luxury to express their love for God. It is advised that they remain focussed on strengthening their relationship with God rather than focussing on wealth and other means of short term happiness. One is allowed to obtain and enjoy luxuries but must be able to thank God for allowing them to have such possessions and remain grateful rather than envious of what others have or over proud of what they have.
