This dish is incredibly simple to prepare. This cuisine will appeal to novice cooks. Begin by sautéing the onion and garlic in a skillet. Cook, stirring occasionally until the onion has softened. Make sure the garlic doesn't burn by keeping an eye on it. Cook until the ground pork has turned light brown. For the ground meat to loosen up, you may want to mix it frequently as it cooks. Pork can be substituted with ground chicken or turkey. Combine the oyster sauce and water in a mixing bowl. Allow coming to a boil. The oyster sauce infuses the entire meal with flavor. Salt and pepper can be added later to adjust the overall flavor. Into the pot, add the chopped pechay. If desired, use more than two bunches. Cook for 3 minutes before sprinkling with salt and pepper and serving.

The layers from top to bottom if you mix together the following materials: water, oil, oyster sauce, one peso coin, coke bottle cap, and foam

Foam < Coke Bottle Caps < Oil < Water < Oyster Sauce < One Peso Coin

Explanation

Density

Density is a measure of mass per unit volume of an object, which can be solid, liquid, or gas. Density is one of the physical properties taught at school in physics lessons. This property can be found in everyday life, such as the buoyant force discovered by Archimedes. Buoyancy force is the ability of an object to float when placed in a fluid. This is related to density. By measuring density, we can identify and distinguish between materials because substances vary in density. If the density of an object is less than the fluid in which it is placed, it will float. If it is greater, it will sink.

From these objects, water, oil, oyster sauce, one peso coin, coke bottle cap, and foam, we will know what the density of each object is. The density of water is 1 g/mL, oil is 0.91-0.93 g/mL, oyster sauce is 1.2 g/mL, one peso coin is 8.9 g/mL, coke bottle caps are 0.92 g/mL, and foam is approximately 0.048 g/mL. If the density of an object is less than the liquid in which it is placed, it will float. If it is greater, it will sink.

If we order these objects from top to bottom (floating to sinking), it becomes like this

Foam (0.048 g/mL) < Coke Bottle Caps (0.92 g/mL) < Oil (0.91-0.93 g/mL) < Water (1 g/mL) < Oyster Sauce (1.2 g/mL) < One Peso Coin (8.9 g/mL)

24. Aunt Cely is trying out a new recipe. The recipe requires 12 ounces of oyster sauce. Her measuring cup measures liquid in milliliters. How many milliliters of oyster sauce does she need for the recipe?


Answer:

To convert ounces to milliliters, we can use the conversion factor of 1 fluid ounce = 29.5735 milliliters.

So, 12 ounces of oyster sauce is equal to:

12 ounces * 29.5735 milliliters/ounce = 354.882 milliliters

Therefore, Aunt Cely needs 354.882 milliliters of oyster sauce for the recipe.
