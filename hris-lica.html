16. From a manager's perspective, an hris (human resource information system) can be used to perform primarily all but one of the following. name the exception.


A human resource information system (HRIS) is a piece of software that gives the human resource management (HRM) team the consolidated repository of employee master data they require to finish core human resource (core HR) procedures. Through the use of technology, an HRIS can aid in improving the efficiency of HR and companies.

Employee data, including names, addresses, national IDs or Social Security numbers, visa or work permit information, and dependant information, is stored, processed, and managed by an HRIS. Additionally, it frequently offers HR services including hiring, applicant tracking, time and attendance management, performance reviews, and benefits administration. Additionally, it might provide features for employee self-service and even bookkeeping.

An HRIS can be thought of as a sophisticated employee data base in several ways. The data contained in the system is made more accessible and usable by the interaction of the data, the operations that can be carried out, and the reporting capabilities.
