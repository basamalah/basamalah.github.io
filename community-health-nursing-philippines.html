22. What are the different fields of community health nursing practice?


Different fields of community health nursing practice are family, school, workplace and gerontology.

About community health nursing

Community health nurses work in a variety of positions with a primary focus on individual clients, families, and communities.

The practice setting in community health nursing is very broad because in all settings, community nurses can provide services with an emphasis on primary, secondary, and tertiary prevention levels.

1. Nurse for the family.

Family health nursing is a level of public health nursing that is centered on the family as a unit that is cared for healthily as a service goal and cared for as an effort.

Family nurses are registered and graduated nurses in the field of nursing who are prepared to practice providing individual and family services throughout the range of health and illness. The role of the family nurse is to carry out family nursing care, participate in and use research results, develop and implement policies in the fields of health, leadership, education, case management, and consulting.

2. Nurse at the School

School nursing is nursing that focuses on children's education arrangements to meet the needs of children by involving families and the school community in service planning.

The main focus of school health nurses is on students and their environment, and the supporting targets are teachers and cadres.

3. Workplace Health Nurse

 Occupational health care is the application of nursing principles in maintaining the health of the workforce in all fields of work.

 Occupational health nurses apply nursing practice in an effort to meet the unique needs of individuals, groups and communities in industrial settings, factories, workplaces, universities and others.

 4. Gerontology Nurse

 Gerontology or gerontology is the science that studies and provides services to the elderly that can occur in various settings and help the elderly to achieve and maintain optimal function.

 The scope of gerontology nursing practice is to provide nursing care, carry out advocacy and work to maximize the ability or independence of the elderly, improve and maintain health, prevent and minimize disability and support the process of dying with dignity.
