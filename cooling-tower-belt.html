3. what is the Function of cooling tower​


Answer:

A cooling tower is used to cool water and is a huge heat exchanger, expelling building heat into the atmosphere and returning colder water to the chiller. A cooling tower receives warm water from a chiller. This warm water is known as condenser water because it gets heat in the condenser of the chiller. The chiller is typically at a lower level, like in a basement. The cooling tower’s role is to cool down the water, so it can return to the chiller to pick up more heat.


4. Describe the process on each ofthe number part.1. Magma2. Reservoir3. Turbine4. Generator5. Electric mains6. Condenser7. Cooling tower​


Answer:

Magma: Magma is not a part of the electricity generation process. It refers to the molten rock material beneath the Earth's surface, which can occasionally be tapped as a source of geothermal energy.

Reservoir: A reservoir is a large artificial or natural lake used to store water that is used to generate hydroelectric power. The water is released through a dam to turn the turbines of a hydroelectric power plant.

Turbine: A turbine is a machine that converts the energy from a moving fluid, such as water or steam, into mechanical energy. In a power plant, a turbine is driven by high-pressure steam produced by heating water with fuel, such as coal or natural gas.

Generator: A generator is a machine that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy. In a power plant, a generator is driven by a turbine to produce electricity.

Electric mains: Electric mains refer to the network of high-voltage power lines and substations that transport electricity from the power plant to homes and businesses.

Condenser: A condenser is a device that removes heat from a substance or system. In a power plant, a condenser is used to cool the steam that has passed through a turbine, turning it back into water that can be reused.

Cooling tower: A cooling tower is a large, open-topped structure used to cool water that has been used to cool steam in a power plant's condenser. The water is sprayed into the top of the tower and allowed to trickle down over a series of baffles, creating a large surface area for the water to evaporate and cool. The cooled water is then pumped back to the condenser to be used again.

23. A power plant has an efficiency of 36 percent with 15 percent of the waste heat being released to the atmosphere as stack heat and the other 85 percent taken away in the cooling water (see Figure below). Instead of drawing water from a river, heating it, and returning it to the river, this plant uses an evaporative cooling tower wherein heat is released to the atmosphere as cooling water is vaporized. At what rate must makeup water be provided from the river to offset the water lost in the cooling tower?


Answer:

401.3 kg/s

Explanation:

The power plant has an efficiency of 36%. This means 64% of the heat form the source (q1) will become waste heat. Of the waste heat, 85% will be taken away by water (qw).

qw = 0.85 * q2

q2 = 0.64 * q1

p = 0.36 * q1

q1 = p /0.36

q2 = 0.64/0.36 * p

qw = 0.85 *0.64/0.36 * p

qw = 0.85 *0.64/0.36 * 600 = 907 MW

In evaporation water becomes vapor absorbing heat without going to the boiling point (similar to how sweating takes heat from the human body)

The latent heat for the vaporization of water is:

SLH = 2.26 MJ/kg

So, to dissipate 907 MW

G = qw * SLH = 907 / 2.26 = 401.3 kg/s
