18. The scores in a 100 item exam resembles a normal distribution with a mean of 78 and a standard deviation of 8. What is the percentage of students that got a score of 86 and above? A. 14.98% B. 15.87% C. 68.15% D. 84.13%​


Answer:

B. 15.87

Step-by-step explanation:

You will see a Z value of 1 = .3413 or 34.13% of the area under the curve.

But… to finish the calculation we need to put all the numbers in the right context.

The value of .3413 is the area under the curve from “0” (zero, the normalized average of 86) to the value of 100 or 72 (since normal distribution is symmetrical).

The number you want is what is the probability of a random student getting a score below 72 (which is 1 standard deviation away from the average, by the way).

What we need to do is calculate the percentage (probability) below 72 by subtracting .3413 from .5000 (.5000 is the probability of what is contained in the lower half of the normal distribution and the .3413 is the probability of what is above 72). So .5000 - .3413 = .1587 or the probability that a student scored below 72 is 15.87%.

To calculate the probability of a student scoring 72 or better just add the probabilities above the 72 level which would be .3413 + .5000 (upper half of normal distribution which was not affected by our calculations) and we get .8413 or 84.13% probability a student scored above 72.

19. A math quiz and is administered to 300 students. The mean score is 85 and the standard deviation is 4. What percentage of the cases falls between the mean and the score of 90? show sulotion ​


Answer:

68% of the students scored between the mean and 90.

Step-by-step explanation:

To solve the problem, we will use the z-score formula:

z = (x - μ) / σ

Where:

x = score of 90

μ = mean score of 85

σ = standard deviation of 4

z = (90 - 85) / 4 = 1.25

Using a standard normal distribution table or calculator, we can find that the area under the curve between the mean and a z-score of 1.25 is 39.44%.

Therefore, approximately 39.44% of the students scored between the mean and 90.

Alternatively, we can also use the empirical rule (68-95-99.7 rule) to estimate the percentage of cases falling between the mean and 90. According to the empirical rule, about 68% of the cases fall within one standard deviation of the mean, about 95% within two standard deviations, and about 99.7% within three standard deviations.

Since 90 is only one standard deviation away from the mean of 85, we can estimate that about 68% of the students scored between the mean and 90.

Answer:

To find the z-score, we use the formula:

z = (x - μ) / σ

where x is the raw score, μ is the mean, and σ is the standard deviation.

In this case, we have:

x = 58

μ = 50

σ = 5

Substituting the values, we get:

z = (58 - 50) / 5 = 1.6

To find the percentage that belongs to the left of a score of 58, we can use a standard normal distribution table or a calculator. Using a calculator, we can find that the percentage is approximately 94.52%. Therefore, about 94.52% of the scores fall below 58.

To answer this question, we need to use the properties of the normal distribution. We know that the mean is 100 and the standard deviation is 15.

We want to find the percentage of the population with IQ scores greater than 130. We can use a standard normal distribution table or a calculator to find the corresponding z-score, which is:

z = (130 - 100) / 15 = 2

This means that an IQ score of 130 corresponds to a z-score of 2. We can then find the percentage of the population with a z-score greater than 2 using a standard normal distribution table or a calculator.

Looking up the value in the standard normal distribution table, we find that the percentage of the population with a z-score greater than 2 is approximately 2.28%. Therefore, we can conclude that approximately 2.28% of the general population have IQ scores larger than 130.
