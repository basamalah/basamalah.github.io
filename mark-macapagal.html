5. Program of diosdado macapagal


Diosdado Macapagal was the 9th President of the Philippines, serving from 1961 to 1965. During his presidency, he implemented several programs and policies to promote economic growth and social justice in the country. Here are some of the notable programs that he introduced:

Land Reform - Macapagal's administration implemented the Agricultural Land Reform Code, which aimed to provide landless farmers with their own lands. The program included the expropriation of private lands for distribution to farmers, as well as the establishment of agricultural cooperatives.
Social Justice - Macapagal's presidency was marked by his commitment to social justice. He established the Presidential Commission on Social Justice, which aimed to address the inequalities in Philippine society. The commission was tasked with promoting fair wages, providing education and training for the poor, and protecting the rights of labor.
Economic Policies - Macapagal's economic policies focused on promoting economic growth and development. He introduced the peso devaluation, which aimed to boost exports and attract foreign investment. He also established the National Economic Council to coordinate economic policies and programs.
Education Reform - Macapagal introduced several reforms in the education system, including the establishment of the Department of Education, Culture and Sports, which aimed to improve the quality of education in the country. He also passed the Magna Carta for Public School Teachers, which provided teachers with better working conditions and benefits.
Foreign Policy - Macapagal's foreign policy was marked by his efforts to promote Philippine sovereignty and independence. He successfully negotiated the Philippine claim to Sabah with the Malaysian government, and he also worked to strengthen relations with other countries in the region.
Overall, Diosdado Macapagal's programs and policies had a significant impact on Philippine society, promoting economic growth, social justice, and national sovereignty.

28. Sino si Diosdado Macapagal? ​


Answer:

Diosdado Pangan Macapagal

(28 Setyembre 1910 – 21 Abril 1997)

Ang ika-9 na Pangulo ng Republika ng Pilipinas (30 Disyembre 1961-30 Disyembre 1965) na makikita sa dalawandaang piso na isyu ng Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas. Siya ay ama ni Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo na naging pangulo rin sa Pilipinas. Inilunsad niya agad ang programa sa dekontrol.Ibig sabihin, wala nang limitasyon sa importasyon at palitan ng piso sa dolyar. Bilang pag-alinsunod sa kahilingan ni Macapagal, ang Kodigo ng Repormang Panlupa, ay ipinasa ng Kongreso. Ito ay nilagdaan ni Macapagal noong 8 Agosto 1963 upang maging ganap na batas. Sa ilalim ng Administrasyon ni Macapagal ay nalipat ang pagdiriwang ng Araw ng Kalayaan sa Hunyo 12 sa halip Hulyo 4, tinawag na lamang na Araw ng Pagkakaibigan ng mga Pilipino at Amerikano ang 4 Hulyo 1946.

Tinagurian si Diosdado Macapagal bilang "Batang Mahirap mula sa Lubao" dahil anak siya ng isang mahirap na magsasaka. Isinilang siya sa San Nicolas, Lubao, Pampanga noong Setyembre 28, 1910 kina Urbano Macapagal at Romana Pangan. Tumira siya sa isang tahanan at pumailalim sa pangangalaga ni Don Honorio Ventura hanggang magtapos ng pagka-Doktor sa mga Batas mula sa Pamantasan ng Santo Tomas noong 1936 at pumasok sa pulitika. Bayaw siya ni Rogelio de la Rosa, embahador ng Pilipinas sa Cambo at siya ay presidente.
