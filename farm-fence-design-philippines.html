Step-by-step explanation:

⇒ [tex]28 = l + w[/tex]

⇒ [tex]28 = l + w[/tex]

⇒ [tex]28 - (l) = l + w - (l)[/tex]

⇒ [tex]w = 28 - l[/tex]

4.) Substitute the variable [tex]w[/tex] in terms of [tex]l[/tex] into the area of a rectangle formula.

⇒ [tex]w = 28 - l[/tex]

⇒ [tex]A = lw[/tex]

⇒ [tex]A = l(28 - l)[/tex]

5.) Since [tex]A[/tex] depicts a quadratic equation ([tex]f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c[/tex]) concerning the variable [tex]l[/tex], we will modify [tex]A[/tex] in function format with the examples in the descending series:

⇒ [tex]A(l) = -l^2 + 28l[/tex]

6.) The graph of [tex]A(l)[/tex] will be a parabola since [tex]a < 0[/tex]. And the equation is quadratic. The parabola will have the highest point as its vertex. The y-coordinate of the vertex will speak to our most noteworthy territory. To continue, we need to find the value of the x-coordinate of the vertex. That is the value of [tex]l[/tex] in our condition.

⇒ [tex]l = \frac{-b}{2a}[/tex] (The values of [tex]a[/tex] and [tex]b[/tex] are the values of the quadratic equation [tex]A(l) = -l^2 + 28l[/tex], where [tex]a = -1[/tex] and [tex]b = 28[/tex].)

⇒ [tex]l = \frac{-(28)}{2(-1)}[/tex] (Substitute the values of [tex]a[/tex] and [tex]b[/tex] to arrive at this equation.)

⇒ [tex]l = \frac{-28}{-2} = \frac{28}{2} = \frac{2 \times 2 \times 7}{2} = 14 \ \textnormal{m} \ \textnormal{or} \ 14 \ \textnormal{meters}[/tex] (Simplify the expression on the Right Hand Side of the equation in lowest terms to get the value of [tex]l[/tex].)

⇒ [tex]A(14) = -[(14)^2] + 2[(14)(14)][/tex]

Many examples of objects that are rectangular shapes around us, namely blackboards, floor mirrors, table surfaces, tile surfaces and others. Rectangles are actually derivatives of quadrilaterals, just like squares, trapezoids, rhombuses, and kites.

1. has 4 sides/plane

2. Has 2 pairs of opposite sides.

3. The opposite or parallel sides have the same size.

4. There are 4 angles that are perpendicular to each other ([tex]90^{0}[/tex]) or called right angles

5. Has 2 diagonal sides that are the same length.

6. The diagonal is the symmetry of the fold.

7. Has 2 axes of symmetry
