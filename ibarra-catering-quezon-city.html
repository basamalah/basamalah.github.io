Quezon City (UK: /ˈkeɪzɒn/, US: /ˈkeɪsɒn, -sɔːn, -soʊn/;[8][9][10][11] Tagalog: Lungsod Quezon [luŋˈsod ˈkɛson]; also known as the City of Quezon, QC, and Kyusi)[12][13][14] is a highly urbanized city and the most populous city in the Philippines. It was founded by and named after Manuel L. Quezon, the 2nd President of the Philippines, to replace Manila as the national capital. The city was proclaimed as such in 1948, though a significant number of government buildings remained in Manila. Quezon City held status as the official capital until 1976[15] when a presidential decree was issued to reinstate and designate Manila as the capital and Metro Manila as the seat of government.[16]

It is the largest city in terms of population and land area in Metro Manila, the National Capital Region of the Philippines. Quezon City is not located in and should not be confused with the province of Quezon, which was also named after the president.

Quezon City hosts a number of government offices, the most important of which are the Batasang Pambansa Complex (the seat of the House of Representatives, the lower house of the Philippine Congress), and the Quezon City Reception House (the current seat of the vice president of the Philippines).[15] Quezon City also serves as home to the University of the Philippines Diliman—the national university—and Ateneo de Manila University.

Also located in Quezon City is the Quezon Memorial Circle, a national park. The park is an ellipse bounded by Elliptical Road. Its main feature is the Quezon Memorial Shrine, a 66-meter-tall mausoleum containing the remains of President Quezon and his wife, First Lady Aurora Quezon. The shrine is considered Quezon City's foremost landmark and is depicted in its city seal.

11. Quezon City is 225 km from your house while Bulacan is 33,525 m. What is the distance in km from Quezon City to Bulacan?​


Answer:

191.475 kilometers

Step-by-step explanation:

The distance from your house to Quezon City is 225 km. The distance of Bulacan from your house is 33,525 meters, equivalent to 33.525 km. ( 33,525 m = 33.525 km)

In order to identify the distance between Quezon City and Bulacan, we are going to subtract 33.525 km from 225 km:

225 km - 33.525 km = 191.475 km

The distance of Quezon City to Bulacan is 191.475 kilometers.

17. QUEZON CITY SEAL MEANING


Answer:

Greatness, Incomparable Leadership, and Educational Development

Explanation:

In summary, the Quezon City seal main components stands for Manuel L. Quezon's Greatness, and Incomparable Leadership and the city's Educational Development.

Learn more about Manuel L. Quezon here: https://brainly.ph/question/37473

The current seal has a yellow background and white triangle frames in the middle. Inside the frame contains the Quezon Memorial Shrine at the center, divided by a red and blue field at the back. Within the red divider is the gavel that represents MLQ's incomparable leadership, and on the blue divider is Lamp of Knowledge.

Within the white triangular frames are the words, "LUNGSOD QUEZON" in all caps, with 3 stars within the texts that over-all describes the 3 main components.

At the bottom inside the white frame contains the words, "Kalakhang Maynila" (Metro Manila). Originally, it was "Punong Bayan ng Pilipinas" as Quezon City was the former capital of the Philippines until the Presidential Decree of former President Ferdinand Marcos on May 1976, then it was changed to Republika ng Pilipinas, then eventually changed to Kalakhang Maynila.

Overall, the colors of the Quezon City seal is also patterned to the colors of the Philippine National Flag.
