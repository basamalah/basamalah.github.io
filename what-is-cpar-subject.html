3. Cite 5 GAMABA Awardees and briefly discuss their significantcontributions.(CPAR SUBJECT)​


GAMABA Awardees and their significant

1. Ginaw Bilog

Ginaw Bilog was a poet from the Philippines who was honored as a National Living Treasure. Bilog, a Hanunuo Mangyan who was born on January 3, 1953, was a native of Mansalay, Oriental Mindoro. He was renowned for his work to uphold the ambahan tradition of Mangyan poetry. In honor of his people's efforts to preserve the ambahan poetry, which is written on bamboo, then-President Fidel V. Ramos presented Ginaw Bilog with the National Living Treasure Award on December 17, 1993. Due to an ongoing illness, he passed away on June 3, 2003, at the age of 50.

2. Masino Intaray

Masino Intaray was a Filipino poet, bard artist, and musician from Palawan who was renowned for his performances of the basal, kulilal, and bagit indigenous customs. Additionally, he has been recognized as a National Living Treasure. Intaray was born in Makagwa Valley on April 10, 1943, and resided at Brooke's Point, Palawan. He is renowned for his proficiency on a variety of native instruments, including the basal (gong), aroding (mouth harp), and the babarak (ring flute).

Intaray is renowned for his performances of vocal music known as bagit as well as kulilal, or songs. The Palawan native had four kids and was married. On November 30, 2013, Intaray passed away from diabetes-related complications, including repeated strokes. His age was 70.

3. Samaon Sulaiman

Awarded the National Living Treasure title is the Filipino musician Samaon Sulaiman. The Maguindanaon is well known for his proficiency with the local kutyapi instrument.

Sulaiman, who was born on March 3, 1953, began learning to play kutyapi when he was about 13 years old from his uncle. He was already well-known in Maganoy by the age of 35 for his proficiency with the instrument and for mentoring aspiring kutyapi practitioners. He is credited for having a positive impact on other regional authorities in his field, including Esmael Ahmad, Bitul Sulaiman, Nguda Latip, Ali Ahmad, and Tukal Nanalon. Along with these instruments, Sulaiman plays the kulintang, tambul, gandingan, palendag, and agong, a hung bossed gong with a wide rim.

4. Lang Dulay

Filipino traditional weaver Lang Dulay, who received the National Living Treasures Award, lived from August 3, 1928, until April 30, 2015. She is recognized with preserving the tradition of weaving T'nalak, a coloured fabric made from processed abaca fiber, among her people.

5. Salinta Monon

One of the two recipients of the National Living Treasures Award in 1998 was the Filipino textile weaver Salinta Monon (December 12, 1920–June 4, 2009). As the "last Bagobo weaver," she was well-known for her Bagobo-Tagabawa fabrics.

29. Look for the meaning of the 7 form of artsGive atleast 1 example eachHelp po sa CPARPainting ArchitectureLiteratureFilmPerforminSculptureMusic​


Painting: A visual art form that uses colors, strokes, and textures to create images on a two-dimensional surface. Examples include oil paintings, watercolors, and acrylics. One example of a painting is the "Mona Lisa" by Leonardo da Vinci.Architecture: The art and science of designing and constructing buildings, structures, and other physical spaces. Examples include the Colosseum in Rome, the Taj Mahal in India, and the Burj Khalifa in Dubai.Literature: Written or spoken art that uses language to express ideas, emotions, and stories. Examples include novels, poems, plays, and essays. One example of literature is "To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee.Film: A visual medium that tells stories through the use of moving images and sound. Examples include feature films, documentaries, and animated movies. One example of film is "The Godfather" directed by Francis Ford Coppola.Performing: Art that involves a live performance, such as theater, dance, and opera. Examples include musicals, ballets, and plays. One example of a performing art is "The Nutcracker" ballet.Sculpture: Three-dimensional art that is created by carving, casting, or modeling materials such as stone, wood, metal, and clay. Examples include sculptures of people or animals, abstract forms, and monuments. One example of a sculpture is Michelangelo's "David."Music: An art form that uses sound and rhythm to convey emotions and ideas. Examples include classical music, rock, pop, jazz, and blues. One example of music is Beethoven's "Symphony No. 9."
