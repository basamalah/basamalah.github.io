6. Assume the role of a human resource manager in organization is it ethical to hire friend or relative from among the pool of many qualified applicants? Elaborate your answer YOUR OWN ANSWERR)


As a human resource manager, it is important to ensure that the hiring process is fair and unbiased. Hiring a friend or relative from among the pool of many qualified applicants may create a conflict of interest and could be seen as unethical.

While it may seem like a good idea to hire someone you know and trust, it can create issues within the organization. It can cause resentment and a perception of favoritism among other employees who were not given the same opportunity, and it can also undermine the credibility of the hiring process.

It is important for the human resource manager to maintain the integrity of the hiring process and to ensure that all candidates are evaluated based on their qualifications, skills, and experience. This is important to ensure that the organization is able to hire the most qualified candidate for the position, and to maintain a fair and equitable workplace.

However, there may be situations where hiring a friend or relative is appropriate, such as in a small family-owned business where relationships are already established and there is a need for trust and familiarity. In such cases, it is important to establish clear policies and guidelines for hiring friends and relatives to ensure that the hiring process is still fair and unbiased.

In conclusion, while it may be tempting to hire a friend or relative from among the pool of many qualified applicants, it is generally not ethical to do so. The human resource manager should maintain the integrity of the hiring process and ensure that all candidates are evaluated based on their qualifications, skills, and experience, in order to hire the most qualified candidate for the position and to maintain a fair and equitable workplace.

Answer:

As a human resource manager, it is important to maintain ethical standards in the hiring process to ensure fairness and avoid any conflicts of interest. Hiring a friend or relative from among the pool of qualified applicants can raise ethical concerns and may create the perception of bias or favoritism.

While it may be tempting to hire someone you know and trust, as a human resource manager, you have a responsibility to ensure that the best candidate is selected for the position based on their qualifications, skills, and experience. Failing to do so can not only undermine the integrity of the hiring process but also negatively impact the organization's overall performance.

It is, therefore, advisable to avoid hiring friends or relatives to ensure that the hiring process is objective and unbiased. If you do need to consider a friend or relative for a position, it is important to ensure that the individual goes through the same hiring process and evaluation as other candidates and that any potential conflicts of interest are disclosed and addressed appropriately.
