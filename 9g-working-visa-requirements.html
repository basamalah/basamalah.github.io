22. (9g + 8h) + (2g + 3h)


Answer:

11g + 11h

Step-by-step explanation:

Remember that the g and the h in the problem are just variables (in this case, they are labels used to group numbers together). In this given example, 9g and 2g are grouped together while 8h and 3h are also put in another group.

Also, the mathematical sign used in the problem is addition (+). You can just discard the parenthesis and go on with solving the problem if it is only addition and subtraction. Multiplication and Division are for another time.

Breaking the problem down,

(9g + 8h) + (2g + 3h)

= 9g + 8h + 2g + 3h

Grouping those with the same labels for easier solving,

= (9g +2g) + (8h + 3h)

And finally adding both the sides,

= 11g + 11h.

Tip:

Do not add or subtract numbers with different labels! Imagine you're buying a bag of Dinurado rice and Sinandomeng rice and you just combined them together. That would be a disaster, and you will get it wrong if you add or subtract them together. However, remember that multiplying or dividing them is on a different level.
