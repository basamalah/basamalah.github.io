4. what is the difference between competitive water sports and individual water sports?​


Answer:

Competitive water sports refer to sports that are typically played against other players or teams, with the goal of winning a competition or match. Examples of competitive water sports include swimming, diving, water polo, and rowing.

Individual water sports refer to sports that are typically performed alone or with limited interaction with others and do not typically involve competition. Examples of individual water sports include surfing, windsurfing, stand-up paddleboarding, and kayaking.

Another difference is that Competitive water sports are more organized with set rules and regulations, while individual water sports have fewer rules and more freedom of expression. Additionally, Competitive water sports usually require more training and skill to perform well.
