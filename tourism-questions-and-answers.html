19. how would you promote or improve tourism in your community?​What does the tourism 2020 vision predict? Plss answer ​


1) How to promote or improve tourism in my community1. Think about what distinguishes your town or city.

Making a list of every activity and attraction that the town now offers is one approach to achieve this. Tourists frequently have more interest in the things they can do and see in your town or city than in where the town or city is located. They will look up an activity online first, then a location. Rock climbing in Bend, Oregon, or fly-fishing near Missoula, Montana, as examples.

2. Consider concentrating on local activities or sights.

A minor or unusual attraction, such as the biggest paper clip in the world or a man-made wave on a river, could draw tourists and draw attention to the community. What makes the town special, you might wonder? What do you offer that a visitor cannot obtain or do elsewhere?

Work with a tourism planning committee to concentrate on the top three attractions in your community. The more detailed you can be, as opposed to being too general, the more probable it is that tourists will find your community interesting.

3. Take a poll of the community's residents.

A survey is an important tool for tourist planning since it allows you to learn more about the community and makes sure everyone can agree on the branding and marketing of the town. Conduct phone or in-person interviews. Ask inquiries like:

What do you believe draws visitors to the area?What kind of guests do you notice coming to our neighborhood?What can we do to enhance the experience for visitors?

4. Take a poll of the town's tourists.

In-person interviews can be held at the neighborhood mall. Additionally, you can send a survey to visitors who join your mailing list. Ask inquiries like:

Who is the visitor's neighbor?What drew the tourist to the neighborhood?How did the visitor learn about the attractions for tourists?What kind of establishments or services did the visitor use?What types of services or accommodations are required?

A third-party endorsement from former or present visitors to the area is a fantastic technique to figure out how to better accommodate guests in the future.
