6. What is the major achievement of the Red Cross, International Court of Justice, Amnesty International, and Greenpeace? support with source.


The major achievements of the Red Cross, International Court of Justice, Amnesty International and Greenpeace are as follows:

1. Red Cross: The major achievement of the Red Cross is its contribution towards humanitarian aid and disaster relief. It provides essential assistance to people affected by armed conflict, natural disasters and other emergencies. It also works to promote and strengthen international humanitarian law and the rights of the most vulnerable people. One of the most significant achievements of the Red Cross is its role in the development of the Geneva Conventions, which have established the standards for the conduct of armed conflicts and the protection of civilians and prisoners of war.

Source: International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC). (2021). About us. Retrieved from https://www.icrc.org/en/who-we-are

2. International Court of Justice: The major achievement of the International Court of Justice (ICJ) is its contribution to the peaceful settlement of international disputes. The ICJ is the principal judicial organ of the United Nations and has jurisdiction over legal disputes between states. Its decisions are binding and have helped to resolve many conflicts around the world. One of the most significant achievements of the ICJ is its role in the development of international law and the promotion of the rule of law at the international level.

Source: International Court of Justice. (n.d.). Overview. Retrieved from https://www.icj-cij.org/en/about-court/overview

3. Amnesty International: The major achievement of Amnesty International is its work in promoting and protecting human rights around the world. It advocates for the release of prisoners of conscience, the abolition of the death penalty, and the prevention of torture and other human rights abuses. Amnesty International's work has helped to secure the release of thousands of political prisoners and to bring attention to human rights abuses in many countries.

Source: Amnesty International. (n.d.). About us. Retrieved from https://www.amnesty.org/en/who-we-are/

4. Greenpeace: The major achievement of Greenpeace is its work in promoting environmental protection and sustainability. It campaigns for the protection of endangered species, the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions, and the promotion of renewable energy sources. Greenpeace's work has helped to raise awareness about environmental issues and to encourage governments and businesses to take action to protect the planet.

Source: Greenpeace International. (n.d.). Who we are. Retrieved from https://www.greenpeace.org/international/about/who-we-are/

7. Discuss the international criminal court from the international court of justice.


Answer:

The International Criminal Court (“the ICC” or “the Court”) is a permanent international court

established to investigate, prosecute and try individuals accused of committing the most serious

crimes of concern to the international community as a whole, namely the crime of genocide, crimes

against humanity, war crimes and the crime of aggression.

Some of the most heinous crimes were committed during the conflicts which marked the twentieth

century. Unfortunately, many of these violations of international law have remained unpunished. The

Nuremberg and Tokyo tribunals were established in the wake of the Second World War. In 1948, when

the Convention on the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide was adopted, the United

Nations General Assembly recognised the need for a permanent international court to deal with the

kinds of atrocities which had just been perpetrated.

The idea of a system of international criminal justice re-emerged after the end of the Cold War.

However, while negotiations on the ICC Statute were underway at the United Nations, the world was

witnessing the commission of heinous crimes in the territory of the former Yugoslavia and in Rwanda.

In response to these atrocities, the United Nations Security Council established an ad hoc tribunal for

each of these situations.

These events undoubtedly had a most significant impact on the decision to convene the conference

which established the ICC in Rome in the summer of 1998

16. Jurisdiction of international court of justice


Answer:

The Court’s jurisdiction is twofold: it decides, in accordance with international law, disputes of a legal nature that are submitted to it by States (jurisdiction in contentious cases); and it gives advisory opinions on legal questions at the request of the organs of the United Nations, specialized agencies or one related organization authorized to make such a request (advisory jurisdiction).
