28. Objective: Volcano Concept Map ​


Answer:

Here are the five eruptive styles and their descriptions:

Hawaiian eruption: A relatively quiet eruption characterized by the effusion of very fluid lava. Hawaiian eruptions typically produce low, broad shield volcanoes with gentle slopes.

Strombolian eruption: A moderate eruption characterized by the ejection of incandescent cinders and ash. Strombolian eruptions typically produce small cinder cones.

Vulcanian eruption: A violent eruption characterized by the ejection of viscous magma, which creates a dense ash cloud that can rise several kilometers into the atmosphere. Vulcanian eruptions can produce pyroclastic flows and surges.

Pelean eruption: A catastrophic eruption characterized by the collapse of a volcano's summit, which triggers a hot avalanche of ash, gas, and rock fragments that can travel at high speeds. Pelean eruptions can produce pyroclastic flows that devastate everything in their path.

Plinian eruption: A massive eruption characterized by the ejection of huge quantities of ash, pumice, and gas, which can form a mushroom-shaped cloud that rises many kilometers into the atmosphere. Plinian eruptions can produce pyroclastic flows and surges that can travel great distances from the volcano.
