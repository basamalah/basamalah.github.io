3. A sample of argon gas at STP occupies 56.2 L. Determine the number of moles of argon and the mass of argon in the sample


SOLUTION: (number of moles)

There are 22.414 liters in 1 mole of an ideal gas at STP.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of Ar} & = \text{56.2 L} \times \frac{\text{1 mol Ar}}{\text{22.414 L}} \\ & = \text{2.50736 mol} \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{2.507 mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 2.507 mol of argon in the sample.

SOLUTION: (mass)

The molar mass of argon is 39.95 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of Ar} & = \text{2.50736 mol Ar} \times \frac{\text{39.95 g Ar}}{\text{1 mol Ar}} \\ & = \text{100.169 g} \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{100.2 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 100.2 g of argon in the sample.


4. 1. An argon gas occupies 56.2 L at 760 torr. If the volume of gas is decreased to ¼ of the original volume, calculate it's final pressure.​


Boyle's law

There are several gas equations used, with various conditions. Boyle's law studies the relationship between the pressure and volume of a gas. If the volume of gas is decreased to ¼ of the original volume, then the final pressure = 3040 torr = 4 atm

Further explanation

Some of the laws regarding gas, can apply to ideal gas :

Boyle's law at constant T, P = 1 / V  Charles's law, at constant P, V = T  Avogadro's law, at constant P and T, V = n  

So that the three laws can be combined into a single gas equation, the ideal gas equation  

PV = nRT  

In Boyle's law, it is stated that  

In a closed system, the absolute pressure by an ideal gas is inversely proportional to the volume it occupies if the temperature is constant  

PV = k  

So it can be stated  

[tex]\boxed{\bold{P_1.V_1=P_2.V_2}}[/tex]

Given

Volume of Argon gas : V₁=56.2 L

Pressure = P₁=760 torr

V₂ = ¼ of the original volume

Required

The final pressure, P₂

Solution

V₂ = ¼ of the original volume

V₂ = ¼ x 56.2 L

V₂ = 14.05 L

Input the value in formula :

[tex]\tt P_2=\dfrac{P_1.V_1}{V_2}[/tex]

[tex]\tt P_2=\dfrac{760.56.2}{14.05}\\\\P_2=\boxed{\bold{3040~torr}}\Rightarrow \boxed{\bold{4~atm}}[/tex]

11. . an Argon gas occupies 56.2 L at 760 torr if the volume of gasses decrease to 1/4 of the original volumes , calculate its final pressure.​


Boyle's LawP1V1 = P2V2

where in, P1 = first pressure

V1 = first volume

P2 = second pressure

V2 = second volume

Given: P1 = 760 torr

V1 = 56.2 L

V2 = 42.15 L

Note: The problem said that the volume of gas decrease to 1/4 of the original volume.

56.2L × 1/4 = 14.05L

56.2L - 14.05L = 42.15L

Unknown: P2 = ?

Formula: P2 = P1V1 / V2

Note: To get the formula, apply division and cancellation of like terms in the original formula of Boyles Law.

P1V1 = P2V2 <--- divide both sides by V2

P1V1 / V2 = P2V2 / V2 <--- cancel like terms

P1V1 / V2 = P2 <--- final formula

Note: Easier method is by Transposition.

P1V1 = P2V2 <--- transpose V2 to the other side

P1V1 / V2 = P2 <--- final formula

Solution: P2 = P1V1 / V2

= (760torr)(56.2L) / 42.15L

= 1014.06torr

Note: "L" is cancelled out, so the unit for Pressure will remain which is "torr"

Reminder: Cancellation of units is applicable when the other same unit is on denominator.

Final Answer: P2 = 1014.06 torr

18. If 0.25 mol of argon gas occupies a volume of 76.2 mL at particular temperature and pressure, what volume would 0.43 mol of argon have under the same conditions?


Answer:

The volume of the argon at 0.43 mol under same conditions is 131.064 mL.

Explanation:

This problem is an application of the Avogadro's Law concerning gases. Avogadro's law for gases describes the relationship of the number of moles of a gas with its volume at constant pressure and temperature. It states that the volume of a gas is directly proportional to its amount in moles.

The mathematical expression for this law is:

[tex]\frac{V1}{n1} = \frac{V2}{n2}[/tex]    where

V1 is the inital volume of the gasV2 is the final volume of the gasn1 is the initial amount of gas in molesn2 is the final amount of gas in moles

Solving for the problem, we have:

Given:

n1 = 0.25 molV1 = 76.2 mL = 0.0762 Ln2 = 0.43 mol

Formula:

[tex]\frac{V1}{n1} = \frac{V2}{n2}[/tex][tex]V2 = \frac{(V1) (n2)}{n1}[/tex]

Substitution:

[tex]V2 = \frac{(0.0762 L) (0.43 mol)}{(0.25 mol)}[/tex]V2 = 0.1311 L or 131.064 mL

Comparing our final value of volume from our computation to the concept of the theory, our final value is logical. The law states the the volume is directly proportional to number of moles, hence, as the number of moles increase, the volume of the gas will also increase. In our problem, the final number of moles is 0.43 and it is larger than the initial number of moles which is 0.25, therefore, the final volume will also increase.

Final answer: The volume of the argon at 0.43 mol under same conditions is 131.064 mL.

27. An argon gas occupies 56.2 l at 760 torr. if the volume of gas is decreased to ¼ of the original volume, calculate its final pressure.


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_1 = \text{760 torr} \\ & V_1 = \text{56.2 L} \\ & V_2 = \frac{1}{4}V_1 = \frac{1}{4}(\text{56.2 L}) = \text{14.05 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final pressure by using Boyle's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1V_1 & = P_2V_2 \\ P_2V_2 & = P_1V_1 \\ \frac{P_2V_2}{V_2} & = \frac{P_1V_1}{V_2} \\ P_2 & = \frac{P_1V_1}{V_2} \\ & = \frac{(\text{760 torr})(\text{56.2 L})}{\text{14.05 L}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{3,040 torr}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the final pressure of the gas is 3,040 torr.
