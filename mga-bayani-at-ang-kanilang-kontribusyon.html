Emilio Aguinaldo

Emilio Aguinaldo is a Filipino revolutionary, politician, and military leader who is officially recognized as the first and most recent President of the Philippines and the first leader of a constitutional republic in Asia.

Antonio Luna

Antonio Narciso Luna de San Pedro y Novicio Ancheta was a general of the Philippine Army who fought in the Spanish -American War, Philippine Revolution, and Philippine -American War before his assassination in 1899

Gregorio del Pillar

Gregorio Hilario del Pilar y Sempio was a Filipino general of the Philippine Revolutionary Army during the Philippine-American War.

.Miguel Malvar

Miguel Malvar y Carpio was a Filipino general who served during the Philippine Revolution and, later, during the Philippine-American War. He eventually led the Philippine revolutionary forces, following the capture of Emilio Aguinaldo by the Americans in 1901.

Macario Sakay

Macario Sakay y de León was a Filipino general who joined the 1896 Philippine Revolution against the Spanish Empire and the Philippine-American War. After the war was declared by the United States in 1902, Sakay continued to fight through leading guerrilla attacks.

Artemio Recarte

Artemio Ricarte y García was a Filipino general during the Philippine Revolution and the Philippine-American War.

Francisco Macabulos

Francisco Macabulos y Soliman was a patriotic Filipino and a revolutionary general who led the Katipunan revolutionary forces during the Philippine Revolution against Spain in 1896.

Vicente Lukban |

Vicente Lukbán y Rilles or Vicente Lucbán Rilles is a General of the Philippines in the Philippine Republican Army. He was also an officer in the staff of Emilio Aguinaldo during the Philippine Revolution and the political-military leader of Samar and Leyte during the Philippine-American War.

Mariano Llanera

Mariano Nunez Llanera was a Filipino revolutionary general from Cabiao, Nueva Ecija who fought in his own province, as well as in the neighboring provinces of Bulacan, Tarlac, and Pampanga.

Apolinario Mabini

Apolinario Mabini was a Filipino revolutionary leader, educator, lawyer, and statesman who first served as a legal and constitutional adviser of the Revolutionary Government, and later as the first Prime Minister of the Philippines when the First Republic of the Philippines was established.

Trinidad Tecson

Trinidad Perez Tecson, known as "Mother of Biak-na-Bato" and "Mother of Mercy", is fighting to achieve Philippine independence. She was given the title "Mother of Biak-na-Bato" by Gen. Melchora Aquino

Melchora Aquino de Ramos is a Filipino revolutionary who became known as "Tandang Sora" because of his age during the Philippine Revolution. She is known as the "Grand Woman of the Revolution" and the "Mother of Balintawak" for her contributions.

GOMBURZA

Gomburza, alternately described as GOMBURZA or GomBurZa, refers to three Filipino Catholic priests Mariano Gomez, Jose Burgos, and Jacinto Zamora who were killed by a garrote on 17 February 1872 in Bagumbayan, by Spanish colonial authorities allegations of subversion originated in the 1872 Cavite mutiny.

Agueda Compassion

Agueda Kahabagan y Iniquinto is a Philippine general in the Philippine Republican Army and a member of the Katipunan. He fought in the Philippine Revolution and the Philippine-American War. Several sources refer to him as General Agueda.

Julian Felipe

Julian Felipe, is the music composer of the Philippine national anthem, formerly known as Marcha Nacional Filipina, now known as Lupang Hinirang.
