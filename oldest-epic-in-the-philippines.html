19. list of epics in philippines


Centuries before the Spaniards came, the Filipinos already had their own cultural traditions, folklore, mythologies and epics. They are the folk epics, transferred from one generation to the next through oral tradition, with the use of singers or chanters. These singers or chanters were often high priests or priestesses in their tribes. These epics were a great source of entertainment and inspiration especially to the youth. In Mindanao, they would call the epic singer, "Onor." Some of the epics, because of their length, would take the singer or chanter seven nights of singing or chanting to finish. The singer or chanter, may either be a male or a female, would sing for minimum of two hours to six hours every night. These events were often festal and recreational occasions like wedding ceremonies, wakes, prestige rites, peace pact agreements, when an ancestor's bones are dug out to be blessed, harvest seasons, when a wild boar is caught, or to welcome a guest, coupled with feasts for the community, supported by either the head of the tribe or a rich and prominent family within the community. The epics were later on transcribed and preserved by rich families as family heirloom.

22. Hinilawod is the oldest and longest epic poem in Panay.


The statement is TRUE.

Hinilawod generally translates to “Tales From The Mouth of The Halawod River”. This epic poem is orally transmitted by the early inhabitants of a place called Sulod in Cental Panay. The epic must have been commonly known to the Visayas of Panay before the conquest and rule of Spaniards in the Phillppines. Spanish colonizers recorded that the Islander’s worship its main protagonists, like Labaw Donggon. They enter a cave in the evening of a certain day of the year, in order to render homage and offer chickens, doves , rice, bananas, and pigs to the ancient Visayan god.

24. The oldest and longest epic poem in Panay. a. Hinilawod b. Balitaw c. Maragtas d. Ambahan


Hinilawod

The correct answer among the listed given is letter a. Hinilawod is known as the oldest and longest epic poem in Panay.

History of Hinilawod

Hinilawod is translated generally to be ''Tales From The Mouth of The Halawod River". This is a story of the exploits of the 3 Sulodnon demigod brothers in ancient Panay namely:

Labaw Donggon- the eldest among the 3 brothers, one of the main protagonist HumadapnonDumalapdap

The original form of this epic would take 3 days to perform that's why it is considered as the longest epic poem in Panay.

This is written by one of the Ilongo writers, Dr. Felipe Landa Jocano.

Some other Characters in the epic HinilawodAlunsina -Goddess of the eastern skyKaptan -King of the gods Datu Paubari -A mortal; mighty ruler of halawod Maklium-sa-twan -God of the plainsSuklang Malayon - Known as the Goddess and guardian of happy homes; known as the sister of Alunsina

Knowing the meaning of other ChoicesMaragtas - is a book that tells a story of the bravery of ten datus who went on a journey to escape from a tyrannical ruler. The information written in this book was originally from the written and oral work of Pedro Alcantara Monteclaro. The Maragtas book contains relevant information on the deep history of Panay from the first inhabitants and the Bornean immigrants. This is where the Bisayan community descended.Balitaw - this is a kind of folk song originally from the Visayas region, which has a similarity to kundiman and kumintang. This is known as a debate or dialogue song where a young woman and a young man have a competition for pitching and competing with the best romantic verses.Ambahan - this is a kind of old traditional poetry which is written on bamboo. This came from the Southern Hanunuo Mangyans of Oriental Mindoro. This is a poetic expression of complementing each other.

30. What is the oldest city of the philippine​


Answer:

Cebu

Explanation:

Cebu City, officially the City of Cebu (Cebuano: Dakbayan sa Sugbo; Tagalog: Lungsod ng Cebu), is a 1st class and highly urbanized city of the island of Cebu in the Central Visayas Region, Philippines. According to the 2015 census, it has a population of 922,611 people,[3] making it the fifth-most populated city in the nation and the most populous in Visayas.

Cebu

Cebu is declared as the oldest city in the Philiippines because the colonizers, particularly Ferdinand Magellan, first set foot in its shores on the year 1521. It was the time when the Spaniards introduced Christianity to the natives, which is why Cebu was called the birthplace of the religion in the eastern part of Asia.

Since it was the first Spanish settlement, it was also declared as the first capital of the Philippines—specifically, it was the Spanish colonial capital. Despite the title, Cebu was only officially chartered as a city in the year 1936.
