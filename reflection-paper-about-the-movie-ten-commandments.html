3. give insights and/or reflections on the Ten Commandments​


Answer:

When I read the Ten Commandments of God which is written in Exodus 20:3-17, I learned that it's about the love of God and the love of man.

The first commandment talks about what God wants us to do. If we love Him, we should not have other gods aside from Him. The second commandment tells us that He is a jealous God and He wants us to worship Him alone.

The third commandment tells us that if we love Him and respect Him, we sill respect His name and we will not use it in vain.

The fourth commandment tells us that if we love Him we should remember the day He rested. It reminds us of how God created the earth and everything on it. Keeping it holy means to dedicate the seventh day for Him to be worshiped. During this holy day, we as His creations, we will praise Him and thank Him for everything blessing that we received.

The fifth commandment tells us to obey and respect our parents which is the first thing we learned since we are young.

The sixth commandment tells us to value life.

The seventh commandment is about the value of relationships. Loyalty and faithfulness to your partner will keep you away from committing

adultery.

The eighth commandment tells us to respect others' possession.

The ninth commandment tells us to be honest and we should only tell the truth. Telling lies against other people is not good.

The tenth commandment tells us to be contented with what we have. Other people may possess the things that we don't, but being content will make our lives happy.

29. The ten commandments​


Answer:

Ten Commandments

Exodus 20:2–14

20:2 I am the LORD thy God, which have brought thee out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of bondage.

20:3 Thou shalt have no other gods before me.

20:4 Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness of any thing that is in heaven above, or that is in the earth beneath, or that is in the water under the earth.

20:5 Thou shalt not bow down thyself to them, nor serve them: for I the LORD thy God am a jealous God, visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth generation of them that hate me;

20:6 And shewing mercy unto thousands of them that love me, and keep my commandments.

20:7 Thou shalt not take the name of the LORD thy God in vain; for the LORD will not hold him guiltless that taketh his name in vain.

20:8 Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy.

20:9 Six days shalt thou labour, and do all thy work:

20:10 But the seventh day is the sabbath of the LORD thy God: in it thou shalt not do any work, thou, nor thy son, nor thy daughter, thy manservant, nor thy maidservant, nor thy cattle, nor thy stranger that is within thy gates:

20:11 For in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is, and rested the seventh day: wherefore the LORD blessed the sabbath day, and hallowed it.

20:12 Honour thy father and thy mother: that thy days may be long upon the land which the LORD thy God giveth thee.20:13 Thou shalt not kill.

20:14 Thou shalt not commit adultery.
