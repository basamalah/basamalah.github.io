Answer:

pH is less than 7 - acidic

pH is greater than 7 - basic/alkaline

pH is equal to 7 - neutral

Explanation:

pH stands for potential hydrogen

In chemistry, pH is a scale used to specify the acidity or basicity of an aqueous solution. Acidic solutions are measured to have lower pH values than basic or alkaline solutions. The pH scale is logarithmic and inversely indicates the concentration of hydrogen ions in the solution.

Remember:

・pH lower than 7 - acidic

・pH greater than 7 - basic

・pH equal to 7 - neutral

Examples:

Acidic Substances

- Citrus fruits (Oranges, Lemons, Limes)

- Soft drinks

- Vinegar

- Yogurt

- Jelly

Basic Substances

- Baking soda

- Toothpaste

- Bleach

- Washing Powder

- Ammonia

Neutral Substances

- Water

- Table salt

- Sugar solution

- Cooking oil

- Sugar

Ways to know the acidity and alkalinity/basicity of a substance:

Litmus paper

- A strip of paper that is chemically treated with a dye named litmus. This strip is placed in the liquid whose pH is being determined. The use of litmus paper, which is red at any pH less than 5 and blue at any pH greater than 8, allows an inspector to determine only whether a solution is acidic or basic.

・red = acidic

・blue = basic

・no reaction = neutral

Titration

- The total alkalinity analysis involves titration. In this test, titration is the addition of small, precise quantities of sulfuric acid (the reagent) to the sample until the sample reaches a certain pH (known as an endpoint). The amount of acid used corresponds to the total alkalinity of the sample.
