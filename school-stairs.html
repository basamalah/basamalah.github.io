4. Stairs leading to the entrance of a mall is designed in the shape of an isosceles trapezoid. The longer base which is at the bottom of the stairs is 6.5m and the shorter base which is at the top of the stairs is 4 3m. Find the width of the stairs halfway to the topHelp braninliest po sumagot thanks​


Answer:

Therefore, the width of the stairs halfway to the top is approximately 4.09 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

We can use the formula for the area of an isosceles trapezoid to solve for the width of the stairs halfway to the top.

Area = [(b1 + b2) / 2] * h

where b1 and b2 are the lengths of the longer and shorter bases, respectively, and h is the height or the width of the stairs.

We can see that the height of the trapezoid is equal to the width of the stairs. Let's denote the width of the stairs halfway to the top as x. We know that the height of the trapezoid is half of the distance between the two bases. Therefore:

h = (6.5 - 4.3) / 2

h = 1.1 m

Now we can plug in the values in the formula and solve for x:

Area = [(6.5 + 4.3) / 2] * 1.1

Area = 5.4 m^2

Area of the trapezoid is also equal to the product of the height and the average of the bases. Thus:

Area = h * [(b1 + b2) / 2]

5.4 = 1.1 * [(6.5 + 4.3) / 2]

Simplifying this equation, we get:

5.4 = 1.1 * 5.4

x = 4.09 m

25. A new obstacle course is home to several new types of challenging obstacles. One such obstacle is traversing a 15-stair staircase. To make it more challenging, the stairs are not uniformly spaced. Rather, the vertical space between consecutive stairs for the first 10 stairs is 113 feet and the vertical space for the last 5 stairs is 134 feet. What is the total vertical distance traveled ascending and descending the entire 15-stair staircase?


Since we are only asked to solve for the vertical distance, then, we simply have to add the vertical space between consecutive stair. The first 10 stairs and the last 5 stairs make up the total of 15 stairs given in the problem.  

Vertical Distance = 113 + 134 = 247 ft

However, take note that this number only covers the distance in one direction. If you descend the stairs, you travelled 247 ft vertically. If you ascend the stairs, you cover another 247 ft.

Therefore, the total vertical distance traveled ascending and descending is:

Total Vertical Distance = 247*2 = 494 ft

30. stairs characteristics​


A staircase is an integral part of a building; residence, office or public utility. It is designed and built according to the local building codes.
