8. We invested $10,000 into two bank accounts. One account earns 14% per year, the other accountcains 8%per year. How much did we invest into each account if after the fust year, the combinedinterest from the twoaccounts is $1238?​


Answer:

$7300 ; $2700

Step-by-step explanation:

Assume that the money in Bank A earns 14% per year, and that of Bank B earns 8% per year.

Now, let x be the amount of money invested in Bank A. Using the first sentence of the question and the previous declaration, we will know that 10000 - x is the amount of money invested in Bank B.

We will now start crafting the equation needed for this problem.

We will multiply 14% to the amount in Bank A and 8% for Bank B to compute for their profits.

Now, we have the expression 14%(x) + 8%(10000-x).

The next step is to equate it into the total profit. Since in this case the total is $1238, we now have the equation 14%(x) + 8%(10000-x) = 1238.

To make the equation simpler, we can multiply 100 into both sides to remove the percent symbol.

Now, we have 14x + 8(10000-x) = 123800.

Solving this,

14x + 80000 - 8x = 123800

14x - 8x = 123800 - 80000

6x = 43800

x = 43800 / 6 = 7300

We now solved how much money was invested into Bank A. Now to find the money invested on Bank B, remember that we used 10000 - x a while ago as a substitution for this. Now, we just have to substitute.

10000 - x = 10000 - 7300 = 2700

Therefore, $7300 was invested in the first account, and $2700 was invested in the second account.
