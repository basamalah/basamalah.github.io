1. what is the symbol of linguistic philosophy?​


Answer:

Linguistic philosophy is the view that many or all philosophical problems can be solved (or dissolved) by paying closer attention to language, either by reforming language or by understanding the everyday language that we presently use better.[1] The former position is that of ideal language philosophy, one prominent example being logical atomism. The latter is the view defended in ordinary language philosophy.[2]

7. What is the difference between Micro-linguistics and Macro-linguistics?


Answer:

What is the difference between Micro-linguistics and Macro-linguistics?

Micro-linguistics is a small scale observation of language, particularly dealing with the concepts of grammar, syntax, and individual words. Macro-linguistics deals with language and extra-lingual related phenomena as a whole, while Micro-linguistics deals with the analysis of specific linguistic data.

25. Similarities between linguistics philosophy and constructivism


Answer:

Linguistics is the study of the human cognitive capacity for language. Linguists study the structure of language, to find what all languages have in common. When linguistics specialists study how language is used in communication, the goal is usually to find the parameters that can give us evidence about human cognition. Communications scholars study the social aspects of communication, which includes language but also includes nonverbal communication, visual communication, rhetoric, journalism and media studies. The relationship between the two is somewhat analogous to that between gastroenterologists and nutritionists, or between ophthalmologists and vision researchers, or between zoologists and veterinarians.
