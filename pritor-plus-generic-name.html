11. find the sum of the first 12 terms of the arithmetic sequence whose general term is a sub n is equal to 3n plus 5.


Answer:

294

Step-by-step explanation:

an = 3n + 5

a1 = 3(1) + 5 = 8

a2 = 3(2) + 5 = 11

a3 = 3(3) + 5 = 14

Formula:

Sn = n/2 [ 2a1 + (n + d)(d)]

Sn = sum of sequence = ?

a1 = first term = 8

d = common difference = 11-8=14-11 = 3

n = number of terms in a sequence = 12

S12 = 12/2 [2(8) + (12 - 1)(3)]

S12 = 12/2 [16 + 11(3]

S12 = 12/2 [16 + 33]

S12 = 12/2 (49)

S12 = 6 (49)

S12 = 294
