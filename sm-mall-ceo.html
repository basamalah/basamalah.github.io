22. SM Mall with "SALE! SALE! SALE!" posters. Is it repetition or emotional words propaganda?​


Answer:

The use of "SALE! SALE! SALE!" posters in an SM Mall can be considered a form of repetition in advertising. Repetition is a common advertising technique that aims to create brand awareness and reinforce messages by repeating them multiple times. In this case, the repetition of the word "SALE" is used to catch the attention of potential shoppers and create a sense of urgency or excitement about the discounted prices or promotions.

However, it can also be argued that the use of emotional words like "SALE!" in all capital letters and with exclamation marks can be seen as a form of emotional or sensational propaganda. This is because it relies on creating an emotional response in consumers, such as excitement, anticipation, or the fear of missing out (FOMO), to influence their purchasing behavior. By using emotional language, the advertising may attempt to manipulate or persuade consumers to make impulsive buying decisions based on their emotions, rather than rational considerations.

Ultimately, whether the use of "SALE! SALE! SALE!" posters in an SM Mall is seen as repetition or emotional propaganda may depend on individual perception and interpretation. It is important for consumers to be mindful of advertising techniques and make informed purchasing decisions based on their needs, budget, and personal preferences, rather than solely relying on emotional cues from advertising messages
