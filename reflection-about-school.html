6. reflection about open high school modular​


Answer:

Open high school modular education is a form of education that allows students to work at their own pace and progress through course material in whatever order they choose. This type of education is particularly beneficial for students who need more flexibility in their learning, as it allows them to take classes on their own schedule and from the comfort of their own homes. It also provides the opportunity to explore various topics in depth, as each module covers a specific topic or skill. Additionally, open high school modular education can help students stay motivated and engaged, as they are able to explore their interests more freely.

8. Write a reflection/ understanding/ learnings about The Role of the School in Human Development.


The Role of the School in Human Development

Every individual has a fundamental right to education. It is the most crucial foundation or building brick that lays a person's future path. Education has the power to create or break a person since it shapes and develops their beliefs, ideas, and values.

Education enables people to think rationally, achieve their objectives and aspirations in life, and live a decent life in society. Education provides us with a clear road to follow, allows us to conduct our lives according to values, and allows us to express ourselves freely. It liberates our thoughts from preconceptions and encourages us to think logically and rationally. It is necessary for the mental and cognitive development of humans. A country's wealth and economic health are determined by its literacy rate.

Every channel of information and wisdom is opened up through education. It opens up a whole new world of information to us, allowing us to develop new things and learn how to make our surroundings a better place to live and put our lives to better use.

Answer:

High school is finally coming to an end. I’ve never pictured myself being “done” with high school let alone going to college.

It's only about seven days until graduation, but it feels like seven weeks. When I was a freshman, I was always the one who couldn't wait to graduate and enter the "real world," but now that I'm a senior, I realize how wrong I was. I'm terrified; I was finally getting used to the idea of high school and my classmates, but now it all has to come to an end. The sad part, I believe, is that more than half of us will not stay in touch, but that's okay. It's a part of life and growing up that, to be honest, makes me nervous.

If I could go back in time and give 14-year-old me advice, I would tell her to live in the moment and make the most of her four years in high school because I will never get those years back. I would tell myself not to be satisfied with Cs at the start of my high school career and to strive for higher grades. I'd tell myself that it's okay to be myself and not try to be someone I'm not.

Since I’ll be starting college this fall, I will follow the advice that I would give myself at the age of 14 because it is not too late to learn important life lessons.
