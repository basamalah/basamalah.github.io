11. Learning Task 5: The dialogue uses conditional sentences. Use the given pattern and complete it by supplying the appropriate conditionals. Write your answers in your notebook. Kenny : If I were the mayor, I would assess the effect of the pandemic. Roger : If I assess its effect, I will plan how to respond on it. Kenny : If I plan our response, I will call for more budget. Roger : If I call for more budget, I will have more resources. Kenny : _________________________________________________ Roger : _________________________________________________ Kenny : _________________________________________________ Roger : _________________________________________________ Kenny : _________________________________________________ Roger : _________________________________________________ Kenny : _________________________________________________


Answer:

Kenny : If I were the mayor, I would assess the effect of the

pandemic.

Roger : If I assess its effect, I will plan how to respond on it.

Kenny : If I plan our response, I will call for more budget.

Roger : If I call for more budget, I will have more resources.

Kenny : If I will have more resources, I will be able to help more community people.

Roger : If I will be able to help more community people, I will inspire private organizations to help too.

Kenny : If I will inspire private organizations to help too, I will inspire private individuals as well.

Roger : If I will inspire private individuals as well, I will be able to lessen the problems caused by the pandemic.

Kenny : If I will be able to lessen the problems caused by the pandemic, I will lessen the burden on the people.

Roger : If I will lessen the burden on the people, I will spark hope among them.

Kenny : If I will spark hope among them, I will lead them to a better tomorrow.

Explanation:

Conditionals describe the result of something that might happen (in the present or future) or might have happened but didn't (in the past).

17. What is the brief explanation of "Even if you're on the right track, you'll get run over if you just sit there." - Will Rogers


Answer:

The quote "Even if you're on the right track, you'll get run over if you just sit there" by Will Rogers means that even if you are doing the right things and making progress towards your goals, if you don't continue to take action and move forward, you will eventually be left behind by others who are more proactive and aggressive in pursuing their own goals. The quote emphasizes the importance of taking action and continuously making progress, rather than just relying on being on the right track. It suggests that even if you have the right approach or plan, you still need to put in effort and take action in order to achieve your goals and avoid being passed by others
