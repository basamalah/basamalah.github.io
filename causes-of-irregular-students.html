13. Suppose that in a population of psychology students of 1600, 500 are 1st years, 400 are 2nd years, 380 are 3rd years, 270 are 4th years, and 50 are irregular students. What is the probability of getting a sample that is a 2nd year student or irregular student?


Answer:

The total number of students in the population is 1600, and the number of 2nd year and irregular students is 400 + 50 = 450.

Therefore, the probability of randomly selecting a 2nd year or irregular student from the population is:

P(2nd year or irregular) = (number of 2nd year or irregular students) / (total number of students)

P(2nd year or irregular) = 450 / 1600

P(2nd year or irregular) = 0.28125

So the probability of getting a sample that is a 2nd year student or irregular student is 0.28125, or 28.125%.
