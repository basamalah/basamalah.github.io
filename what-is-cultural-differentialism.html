11. Form a group with 3 to 5 members among your family. Brainstorm how religion influences the culture in your community. Come up with a creative presentation of your output. 1. Differentiate the psychological, anthropological, historical and geographical 2. What are the two outstanding principles in understanding the geography of religion? Briefly explain each. 3. Explain the model of absorption, transformation and rejection of how religion affect culture. ​


Answer:

1. Religion can influence culture in many ways, depending on the psychological, anthropological, historical, and geographical context. Psychological factors include the personal beliefs, values, and practices of individuals, while anthropological factors include the customs, traditions, and rituals of different communities. Historical factors include the evolution of religion over time and its impact on cultural development, while geographical factors include the geographic distribution and diversity of different religious groups.

2. The two outstanding principles in understanding the geography of religion are spatial diffusion and religious ecology. Spatial diffusion refers to how religions spread and interact with each other over time and space, while religious ecology refers to how religion adapts to and interacts with the natural and cultural environment.

3. The model of absorption, transformation, and rejection explains how religion affects culture. Absorption occurs when a religion is adopted and integrated into an existing culture, while transformation occurs when a religion adapts and modifies an existing culture. Rejection occurs when a culture resists or rejects a new religion, either by maintaining its traditional practices or by adopting a different religion.

Gender related Development Gender stereotypes are the beliefs that people have about the characteristics of males and females. The content of stereotypes varies over cultures and over time. These expectations are often related to the roles that the sexes fulfill in the culture.

A gender stereotype is, at its core, a belief and that belief may cause its holder to make assumptions about members of the subject group, women and or men. In contrast, gender stereotyping is the practice of applying that stereotypical belief to a person.

Gender is a cultural construct and perceptions of men and women differ from culture to culture or even during different periods even in a single culture or society.

Cultural Stereotypes. Generalizations become stereotypes when all members of a group are categorized as having the same characteristics. Stereotypes can be linked to any type of cultural membership, such as nationality, religion, gender, race, or age. Also, stereotypes may be positive or negative.

Gender stereotyping is defined as an overgeneralization of characteristics, differences and attributes of a certain group based on their gender. Gender stereotypes create widely accepted biases about certain characteristics or traits and perpetuate the notion that each gender and associated behaviors are binary.
