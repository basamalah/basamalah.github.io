Direct and Reported Speech

1. Direct Speech

2. Reported Speech

3. Reported Speech

4. Direct Speech

5. Reported Speech

Explanation:

To ways to relay what someone else is saying or has said can be through direct and reported speech.

Quoting the exact words of the person is a direct speech. Quotation marks around their words and adds a speech tag such as "he said" or "she asked" before or after the quote are done in a direct speech. An example of this is:

     1. Mikayla said, "The world needs a hero right now."

Here we can find the exact words of Mikayla as marked by the quotation marks.

Reported speech, on the other hand,  is another way of saying what someone said. The statement doesn't have quotation marks because it doesn't usually repeat the words exactly as the person said them. It typically changes the tense of the verbs. Specifically, if the speaker used a present tense verb, it is changed to the past tense. If the speaker used a past tense verb, it is changed to past perfect. We also change pronouns and time expressions. An example of this is:

    2. The mayor said that the Calasiao LGU had started the contact tracing.

In this statement, the statement of the mayor is just restated and we don't cite the exact words.
