5. Volcano strip comics


Volcano Strip is a comic book series created by Filipino comic book artist Gerry Alanguilan. It tells the story of a group of people living in a small town near a volcano that suddenly erupts, causing chaos and destruction.

The series is known for its realistic portrayal of the effects of a volcanic eruption on a community, as well as its strong character development and social commentary. The story follows a diverse cast of characters, including a young boy named Bong, his mother, a priest, a scientist, and a group of rebels fighting against the corrupt government.

Throughout the series, the characters must navigate the aftermath of the eruption, including dealing with the loss of loved ones, finding shelter and food, and fighting against the government's attempts to control the situation. The series also explores themes of class struggle, corruption, and the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Volcano Strip was first published in the Philippines in the 1990s and has since been reprinted and translated into several languages. It is considered a classic of Philippine comics and a significant work in the genre of disaster comics.
