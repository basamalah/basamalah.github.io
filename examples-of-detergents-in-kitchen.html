23. give an example of sulotion on detergent


A detergent is another name for a chemical compound known as a surfactant. Essentially, these compounds are able to increase the water solubility of traditionally non-water soluble compounds like oil or grease. Detergents are able to do this because they exhibit both polar and non-polar natures.

There are three main classes of detergents. Anionic, Cationic, and Nonionic. Anionic. One natural detergent made by the human body is bile, which is used to increase fat absorption by the small intestine.
