19. spoken poetry about the fairy​


Answer:

Glass Slipper Sonnet

Pity the poor step-sister those big feet

she’ll never stuff in a size-six sonnet,

her flesh so fulsome the slipper seems effete,

unworthy of the labor spent on it.

But try she must, and so she makes a pass,

jams four fat toes in the narrow throat,

the fifth pig smarting, pressed against the glass

(though pain’s no stranger—she knows it by rote.)

The other shoe drops—as it is wont to do—

a second foot is squeezed into the vamp.

She stands up straight and takes a stride towards you,

her footfall heavy as a farm girl’s tramp.

The slipper strains against those excess feet.

She hobbles onward—she has a prince to meet.

21. spoken poetry about pandemic​


'MY CORONA'

The coronavirus looks like a dog toy

or a child’s Koosh ball

with its primary color

and fanciful shape.

How can something so whimsical

be so insidious?

It hasn’t infected me, mind you,

but it has changed me —

morphed into an

odd, complex chimera.

I’ve grown antennae that detect

a six foot field around me.

I’ve developed a fly’s eyes

to see danger on surfaces.

Like a squirrel, I bury food

in nooks and crannies

for a distant time.

I don a carapace

to venture out —

which I shed like a

snakeskin

on return to my door.

The future keeps receding.

Certainty has collapsed.

Sometimes I am like

the bear,

lumbering out of hibernation —

but mostly, I am like

the ground hog,

waiting

still waiting

to see her

shadow.

23. spoken poetry for teachers day


Answer:

What is a teacher? At first, I thought it was just a job, a position or a profession. So I looked up the definition of the word “teacher,” and according to the Oxford dictionary, a teacher is a person who teaches, especially in a school.

The definition may be correct, but I think it is incomplete. Because real teachers don't just teach, they prepare us, their students, for our future. They are people with passion, dedication and patience for teaching. They are people that give up their personal and family time, even working on a weekend to grade papers, prepare lessons and tutor their students. Some people don't realize that some teachers have to buy most classroom supplies out of their pockets.

Being a teacher is not only a profession or a job because good teachers want to see their students succeed. It also takes great patience, endurance and talent to be one.

A teacher is also our second parent. We spent a lot of our time in school with them, mentoring us, guiding us, and teaching us, not only with academics but also teaching us, what is good and what is right.

Teachers make a significant impact on the lives of their students. That is why they should not be taken for granted. We should honour them and thank them for their sacrifices and dedication to their students' future.

So, to my dearest teachers, I want to express my sincerest gratitude for your dedication and sacrifice.  Your passion for what you do has helped me find my passion and curiosity to learn.  It takes a lot to guide so many students, but I can speak on behalf of us all in saying that your impact on our lives is immense.
