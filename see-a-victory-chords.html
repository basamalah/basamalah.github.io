✏️CIRCLE===============================

Problem: The segment of a circle is a region bounded by a chord and the arc subtended bt the chord. Find the area of the segment of the circle with a central angle of 120° and a radius of 64 cm.

Solution: First, we need to find the area of the sector by the product of the circle's area and the ratio of the central angle at 360 degrees.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \bold{ \color{lightblue}Formula:} \\ & \boxed{A_{sec} = \frac{ \theta}{360 \degree} = \pi {r}^{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{A_{sec} = \frac{120 \degree}{360 \degree} = (\pi) (64cm)^{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{A_{sec} = \frac{120 \degree}{360 \degree} = (\pi) 4096cm^{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{A_{sec} = \frac{1}{3} = (\pi) 4096cm^{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{A_{sec} = \frac{(\pi)4096 {cm}^{2} }{3} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{A_{sec} = (\pi)1365.33 {cm}^{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

- Let 3.14 be the approximate value of pi.

[tex]\begin{aligned}{A_{sec} = (3.14)1365.33 {cm}^{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{A_{sec} = 4287.13{cm}^{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

- Second, find the area of a triangle.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \bold{ \color{lightblue}Formula:} \\ & \boxed{A_{ \Delta} = \frac{1}{2}bh} \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{A_{ \Delta} = \frac{1}{2 }(64cm)(64cm)} \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{A_{ \Delta} = \frac{1}{2 }(4096cm^{2} )} \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{A_{ \Delta} = \frac{4096 {cm}^{2} }{2 }} \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{A_{ \Delta} = 2048 {cm}^{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

- Find the area of the segment by subtracting the area of the sector and area of a triangle.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \bold{ \color{lightblue}Formula:} \\ & \boxed{ A_{seg}= A_{sec} - A_{\Delta}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{ A_{seg}= 4287.13 {cm}^{2} - 2048 {cm}^{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned}{ A_{seg}= 2239.13 {cm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

- Therefore, the area of the segment is:

[tex] \large \boxed{ \rm{ \green{ \: 2239.13cm \: }}}[/tex]===============================

26. Which chords are major chords and minor chords ​


ANSWER

MAJOR CHORDS V.S MINOR CHORDS

•A major chord has what's called a “natural third.” It's the third degree of the chord's respective major scale.

•A minor chord has what's called either a “minor third” or a “flat third.” It's the third degree of the chord's respective minor scale.
