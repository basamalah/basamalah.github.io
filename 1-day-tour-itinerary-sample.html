6. A tour guide prepares for the itinerary, commentaries, data and information.(pls elaborate) ​


Answer:

As a tour guide, it is important to prepare thoroughly for a tour to ensure that the itinerary runs smoothly and that the tour participants have a positive experience. This involves preparing commentaries, data, and information that will be shared with the group during the tour.

Itinerary: The tour guide should prepare a detailed itinerary that outlines the schedule for the tour. This should include the start and end times for each activity, as well as any transportation arrangements that need to be made. The itinerary should also include information about any breaks or meals that will be provided.

Commentaries: The tour guide should prepare commentaries that will be shared with the group during the tour. This could include historical or cultural information about the places being visited, as well as interesting anecdotes or stories that will help bring the tour to life. The tour guide should practice delivering these commentaries to ensure that they are engaging and informative.

Data: The tour guide should also gather relevant data that will be shared with the group during the tour. This could include facts and figures about the places being visited, such as population sizes or important dates in history. The tour guide should ensure that this data is accurate and up-to-date.

Information: The tour guide should also prepare other relevant information that will be shared with the group during the tour. This could include practical information, such as the location of restrooms or emergency contact information, as well as information about local customs or traditions that the group should be aware of.

By preparing an itinerary, commentaries, data, and information in advance, the tour guide can ensure that the tour runs smoothly and that the tour participants have an enjoyable and informative experience.

How to Get to Boracay

Located off the northwestern coast of Aklan, Boracay Island is very accessible to the beach-going public. If you’re coming from Manila, flying is the best option. All major airlines fly to either of them.

Two airports serve Boracay-bound passengers. Each airport has pros and cons.

Caticlan Airport and Kalibo Airport

Caticlan Airport and Kalibo Airport

Kalibo is farther, requiring around 2 hours of travel time, but fares are much cheaper.

Caticlan is closer, 15 minutes away, but fares are way more expensive.

Whatever you choose, you need to make your way to the Caticlan Jetty Port. Here’s a brief step-by-step guide:

From Kalibo Airport, take the bus or van to Caticlan Jetty Port. Fare: P200.

If you’re a big group, you may also consider arranging a private van transfer that will wait for you at the airport. I have tried this pre-arranged setup before when I was part of a group of 10. Cost was P3800 roundtrip (P1900 one-way, or P180 per person per way).

Upon arrival at the jetty port, proceed to the booths near the entrance to the building and settle the Terminal Fee (P100), Environmental Fee (P75), and Ferry Fare (P25).

Inside the port building, register, proceed to the Passenger Waiting Area, and wait for boarding announcement.

Board the boat or ferry to the island.

You will dock at either Cagban or Tambisaan Port. Either way, there are tricycles waiting here that can take you to your hotel. If your hotel is in the White Beach Area, cost is P100 per ride.
