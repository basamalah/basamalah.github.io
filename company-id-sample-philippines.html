1. Emphasis –

The theory in art that makes the viewer piece together the plot of a painting is called focus.

A focal point is any object or place of emphasis. The focal point is intended to be the aspect of an artwork that draws the viewer's attention first. Many focal points are possible in artworks. The order in which the focal points are noticed is determined by the degree to which they stand out.

2. Balance

Balance is one of the organizing principles of structural elements in art and architecture. In general, harmony refers to the equilibrium of various components. In art and architecture, however, harmony does not always mean a total visual or even physical equilibrium of forms around a central point of the composition, but rather an arrangement of forms that evokes a sense of balance in viewers. In literature, equilibrium or harmony of elements is accomplished by the reconciliation of competing forces. Balance is one of the fundamental building blocks of visual representations and adds to their artistic effectiveness. Balance can be classified into many groups. The most often used terms in terms of vocabulary include asymmetrical equilibrium, symmetrical balance, and radial balance. These kinds of harmony can be seen in sculpture, architecture, and construction. Their application and production have a tradition as long as human history, but for this text, we will concentrate on the importance of harmony in art and design and include examples mostly from modern and contemporary art.

An art movement is a movement or style of art that is practiced by a group of artists over a certain period of time (usually a few months, years, or decades) or, at the very least, with the heyday of the movement established within a number of years. Problem:

The sides of a triangle are 3,6 and 8. If the largest side of a similar triangle is 16, what is the shortest side?

3:6:8 = x:y:16

3/x = 8/16

8x = 3(16)

x = 6

6/y = 8/16

8y = 6(16)

y = 12

The shortest side is 6 (units).


30. What are the issues on pre-registration for Philippine Identification System (PhilSys) or National ID?​


Answer:

N/A (Free of charge) The Philippine Identification System ID (PhilSys ID) or the Philippine Identification Card (PhilID) (Filipino: Pambansang Pagkakakilanlan) is the official national identity card for Filipino citizens worldwide and foreign permanent residents in the Philippines.

Current concerns on the pre-registration process for PhilSys ID:

1. Privacy

While the intentions of the centralized ID are good, the government has not yet proven its capability in handling and protecting data privacy. Previous instances of hacking of government websites, social media accounts and other digital assets over the years also contributed to the general public’s skepticism and fears over the implementation of a centralized ID system.

2. House-to-house Visit

Some Filipinos raised concerns on the health and security risks of the manual visitation of PSA staff amidst the pandemic.

3. Voluntary Basis

Other Filipinos questioned the purpose for the pre-registration to be on a voluntary basis, citing the government’s goal of convenience to Filipinos, businesses and other organizations.
