8. Project management Instructions:Your task is to plan work on a project called: Implementation of a new healthy food product - apple and banana chips - on the German market write out the scope of work in accordance with the following points before starting the project.• Justification of the project• Stakeholders • Project card - what it should look like • Project requirements• Declaration of the scope of the project • Risk identification• Risk analysis • Create a budget • Project communication • Project progress control • Personal resource​


Answer:

Justification of the project: The implementation of a new healthy food product - apple and banana chips - on the German market is justified by the growing demand for healthy snack options among consumers. The market for healthy snacks is expected to grow significantly in the coming years, and introducing a new product in this segment presents a significant business opportunity.

Stakeholders: The stakeholders in this project include the project team, the company's management, investors, suppliers, distributors, and customers.

Project card - what it should look like: The project card should include the project name, project manager, project objectives, project scope, project timeline, project budget, and key stakeholders.

Project requirements: The project requirements include identifying the target market, developing the product, creating a marketing strategy, identifying suppliers and distributors, ensuring compliance with regulations, and establishing a pricing strategy.

Declaration of the scope of the project: The scope of the project is to introduce a new healthy food product - apple and banana chips - on the German market. The project will involve product development, marketing, distribution, and sales.

Risk identification: The risks associated with this project include market competition, supply chain disruptions, regulatory compliance issues, and consumer preferences shifting towards other products.

Risk analysis: The risks will be analyzed by identifying the likelihood and impact of each risk and developing mitigation strategies to minimize their impact.

Create a budget: The budget for this project will include the costs associated with product development, marketing, distribution, and sales. The budget will be developed based on the project requirements and will be monitored throughout the project to ensure that the project stays within budget.

Project communication: Effective communication is critical to the success of this project. Communication channels will be established with all stakeholders, and regular updates will be provided to ensure that everyone is informed about the project's progress.

Project progress control: Project progress will be monitored through regular status updates, milestone tracking, and performance metrics. Any deviations from the plan will be addressed promptly to ensure that the project stays on track.

Personal resource: The project team will consist of individuals with expertise in product development, marketing, distribution, and sales. The team will be led by a project manager who will be responsible for ensuring that the project is completed on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards.

Three phases of swine production are breeding, farrowing and post-weaning (grow-out period prior to slaughter).  Each of these phases requires special knowledge, good observation skills and attention to detail. Heat detection, breeding, and farrowing sows takes quite a bit of management skill. In the early post-weaning phase, it is essential that pigs are comfortable and that they begin eating solid feed early.  Thus, the first couple of weeks after weaning are important. The input costs in the grow-finish phase are high because of the high feed intake of growing pigs.  But, it’s important to recognize that once pigs are past the post weaning phase, managing the grower finisher pigs is not as demanding as getting sows bred, caring for new born pigs, or managing pigs through the weaning period.
