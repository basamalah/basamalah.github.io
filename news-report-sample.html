1. summarize the sample news report three to five sentences news report-deped sets opening of school year 2022-2023 on August 22​


Answer:

The Department of Education (DepEd) on Friday said it is ready for the scheduled opening of classes on Aug. 22.

"All systems go na tayo for the opening of classes," DepEd spokesperson Michael Poa said in a press conference.

Poa said a total of 24,175 public and private schools nationwide or 46 percent will implement five-day face-to-face classes, while 29,721 schools or about 51.8 percent will implement blended learning modality.

Meanwhile, the remaining 1.29 percent or about 1,004 schools will conduct distance learning.

Poa, citing Aug. 19 DepEd data, said the current total enrollment on the basic education level is 27 million learners, which is 96 percent of the agency's target of 28.6 million.

DepEd Undersecretary Epimaco Densing III earlier said schools will be given options of in-person, blended, or full-distance learning only until Oct. 31.

After this, all enrolled students will be required to attend face-to-face classes, under DepEd Order (DO) 034, series of 2022.

"This is mandatory, they are all required to go to school and the mandate is really to eventually go face-to-face. Everybody who's enrolled should abide by the Department Order, parents are also encouraged to ensure that their students go to school. Again, we're trying to do some educational recovery," he said.

According to the same DO 034, there are 203 days for Academic Year 2022-2023 for public schools, which will begin on Aug. 22, 2022 and will end on July 7, 2023.

Answer:

In a recent report, the rising prices of basic commodities have caused concern among citizens despite the government's claims of addressing the issue. According to data from the Department of Trade and Industry, the prices of food and other essential goods have significantly increased over the past month.

Many have attributed the price hike to the ongoing pandemic and the resulting supply chain disruptions. However, critics argue that the government has not done enough to address the issue and provide relief to those affected by the price increase.

In response to the criticism, the government has announced a series of measures aimed at stabilizing prices and providing financial assistance to those in need. These include implementing price caps on certain goods, increasing importation of essential products, and providing subsidies to low-income families.

Despite these efforts, many remain skeptical of the government's ability to effectively address the issue. Protests and calls for action have been held in major cities, with citizens demanding more concrete solutions to the rising cost of living.

The situation has sparked a heated debate among government officials and the public, with both sides presenting their arguments and suggesting possible solutions. It remains to be seen how the government will address the ongoing crisis and bring relief to those struggling with the rising prices of basic commodities.

29. A 330 mL sample of gas is initially at a pressure of 650 torr and a temperature of 32°C. If this gas is compressed to a volume of 242 mL and the pressure increases to 901 torr, what will be the new temperature of the gas (reported in °C)? [1 torr = 1 mm Hg]


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1 & = \text{650 torr} \\ V_1 & = \text{330 mL} \\ T_1 & = 32^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{305 K} \\ P_2 & = \text{901 torr} \\ V_2 & = \text{242 mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final temperature in kelvin by using combined gas law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} T_2 & = \frac{T_1P_2V_2}{P_1V_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{305 K})(\text{901 torr})(\text{242 mL})}{(\text{650 torr})(\text{330 mL})} \\ & = \text{310 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Convert the final temperature from kelvin to degree Celsius.

[tex]\begin{aligned} t_2 & = T_2 - 273 \\ & = 310 - 273 \\ & = \boxed{37^{\circ}\text{C}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the new temperature of the gas is 37°C.
