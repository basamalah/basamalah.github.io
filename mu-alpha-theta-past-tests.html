4. sin(theta)+tan(theta)/1+cos(theta)=tan(theta)​


Prove that [tex]\sf \frac{sin(\theta)+tan(\theta)}{1+cos(\theta)} = tan(\theta)[/tex]

Answer:

Let [tex]\sf LHS = \frac{sin(\theta)+tan(\theta)}{1+cos(\theta)}, RHS = tan(\theta)[/tex]

[tex]\sf LHS = \frac{sin(\theta)+tan(\theta)}{1+cos(\theta)}[/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf LHS = \frac{\frac{sin(\theta)}{cos(\theta)}cos(\theta)+tan(\theta) }{1+cos(\theta)}[/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf LHS = \frac{tan(\theta)cos(\theta)+tan(\theta)}{1+cos(\theta)}[/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf LHS = \frac{tan(\theta)(cos(\theta)+1)}{1+cos(\theta)}[/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf LHS = tan(\theta)[/tex]

[tex]\therefore \sf LHS = RHS[/tex]

Hence proved :)
