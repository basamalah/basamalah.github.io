26. 1. Explain briefly how the three departments; Finance, Marketing, and Production department collaborating to each other? 2. What do you think why the VP Finance functions is more crucial than the two other functions, the VP Marketing and the VP Production?​


Answer:

1. The three departments, Finance, Marketing, and Production collaborate with each other in order to achieve the common goal of the organization.

a. Finance department provides funds and resources to the marketing and production department for carrying out various activities.

b. Marketing department receives funds from the finance department for advertising and promoting products and services.

c. Production department relies on the finance department to provide funds for purchasing raw materials and manufacturing products.

d. Marketing and production department work together to create demand for the products produced by the production department.

2. The VP Finance function is more crucial than the two other functions, the VP Marketing and VP Production, because the finance department is responsible for managing the financial resources of the organization. The finance department is responsible for financial planning and analysis, budgeting, financial reporting, and managing financial risks. The finance department ensures that the company has sufficient funds to keep the operations running and meets its financial obligations such as paying bills, employees, and suppliers. Additionally, the finance department analyzes the financial data generated by the marketing and production departments, which helps to make informed decisions regarding allocating funds for various activities. Without proper financial management, the organization could quickly run into financial trouble, which could negatively impact the marketing and production department's ability to carry out their functions effectively.


27. discuss briefly the roles of each position identified a. shareholders b.board of directors c.vp for marketing d president (CEO)e. vp for production f. vp for administration g. Vp for finance​


Answer:

Chief Executive Officer (CEO): As the top manager, the CEO is typically responsible for the corporation's entire operations and reports directly to the chairman and the board of directors. It is the CEO's responsibility to implement board decisions and initiatives, as well as to maintain the smooth operation of the firm with senior management's assistance.

Shareholders: A shareholder, also referred to as a stockholder, is a person, company, or institution that owns at least one share of a company’s stock, which is known as equity. Because shareholders are essentially owners in a company, they reap the benefits of a business’ success. These rewards come in the form of increased stock valuations, or as financial profits distributed as dividends. Conversely, when a company loses money, the share price invariably drops, which can cause shareholders to lose money, or suffer declines in their portfolios’ values.

Board of Directors: A board of directors (B of D) is an elected group of individuals that represent shareholders. The board is a governing body that typically meets at regular intervals to set policies for corporate management and oversight. Every public company must have a board of directors. Some private and nonprofit organizations also have a board of directors.

VP for Marketing: Vice President (VP) of Marketing is a marketing expert who takes responsibility for overall marketing results of a company. Vice President (VP) of Marketing oversees marketing strategies and efforts in order to strengthen company's market position and achieve desired business goals.

In order to attract Marketing Director that best matches your needs, it is very important to write a clear and precise Marketing Director job description.

VP for Production: A vice president of production plans, directs and coordinates the development and manufacture of all products made by that company. The person who holds this position should ensure that the company uses the most efficient, effective and economically viable methods for the production of the company and also the products.

VP for Finance: A vice president of finance is in charge of overseeing company financial records and projecting future financial investments and plans. VP of finance is a similar role to the chief financial officer (CFO), and in smaller companies, the roles are combined into one position. In a larger business, the vice president of finance serves under the CFO, and both are responsible for the accounting department or team. This is an executive level job and requires extensive financial and business qualifications.
