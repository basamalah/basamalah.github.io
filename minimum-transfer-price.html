1. Ang price floor ay kilala bilang price support at minimum price policy na tumutukoy sa?​


Answer:

Price Floor– Ito ay kilala rin bilang price supportat minimum price policyna tumutukoy sa pinakamababang presyo na itinakda ng batas sa mga produkto at serbisyo. Itinatakda ito ng mas mataas sa equiblibrium price. Katulad ng price ceiling, isinasagawa ito ng pamahalaan upang matulungan ang mga prodyuser. Kabilang sa sistemang ito ang pagkakaloob ng tinatawag na price supportsa sektor ng agrikultura at ang batas naman na nauukol sa pagtatakda ng minimum wage. Halimbawa, kung masyadong magiging mababa ang equilibrium priceng mga produktong palay sa pamilihan, ang mga magsasaka ay maaaring mawalan ng interes na magtanim dahil maliit naman ang kanilang kikitain mula rito. Kung magaganap ang sitwasyong ito, magdudulot ito ng kakulangan sa supply. Hindi makabubuti sa ekonomiya ang kalagayang ito kung kaya ang pamahalaan ay magtatakda ng price support/price flooro ang pinakamababang presyo kung saan maaaring bilhin ang kanilang ani.

Setting of minimum wage. 3. Setting the maximum rental price to protect consumers. 4. Imposed by the government to prevent a particular price of a good to drop 5. It is a maximum price that can be charged for a product or service.​


Answer:

1.floor

2.floor

3.price ceiling

4.price ceiling

5.price ceiling

Explanation:

The government can impose various measures to protect farmers from price fluctuations in the market. Some of these measures include:

Minimum Support Price (MSP): The government sets a minimum price at which it guarantees to purchase agricultural produce from farmers. This ensures that farmers get a fair price for their crops and protects them from market fluctuations.

Price Stabilization Fund (PSF): The government can establish a fund to provide financial support to farmers in case of price fluctuations in the market. The PSF can be used to buy agricultural produce from farmers when the market price falls below a certain level.

Crop Insurance: The government can provide crop insurance to farmers to protect them from losses due to natural disasters, pests, and diseases. This helps farmers to manage their risks and ensures a stable income for them.

Export-Import Policies: The government can regulate the export and import of agricultural commodities to stabilize prices in the domestic market. This can be done by imposing export restrictions, import duties, and tariffs.

Overall, these measures aim to protect farmers from price fluctuations and provide them with a stable income, which is crucial for the growth and development of the agricultural sector.
