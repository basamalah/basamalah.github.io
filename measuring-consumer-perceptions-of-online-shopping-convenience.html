3. extent/magnitude of the problem the perception of high school teachers towards online shopping​


Answer:

The perception of high school teachers towards online shopping is a relatively new research area and thus, there is limited research available on the extent/magnitude of this problem. However, the COVID-19 pandemic has significantly increased the use of online shopping, and it is likely that high school teachers are also using online shopping services to a greater extent than before.

It is important to study the perception of high school teachers towards online shopping because their opinions may influence the attitudes of their students, who are also likely to be online shoppers. Additionally, high school teachers play an important role in shaping the learning experiences of their students, and their attitudes towards online shopping may have implications for how they teach about e-commerce and online safety.

Further research is needed to explore the extent/magnitude of the problem and to understand the underlying factors that shape high school teachers' perceptions of online shopping. This can help to identify strategies for promoting responsible online shopping behavior and for improving online safety education in schools.

Explanation:

some high school teachers may view online shopping negatively due to concerns about the reliability and safety of online transactions, as well as the potential impact on local businesses. Others may see online shopping as a convenient and efficient way to purchase goods and may have positive perceptions of the practice.

To get a better understanding of the extent and magnitude of the problem, one could conduct surveys or interviews with high school teachers to gather their opinions and attitudes towards online shopping. This would provide insights into the level of awareness and understanding of online shopping, as well as any specific concerns or issues that may be impacting their perception of the practice. Additionally, analyzing data on online shopping trends and its impact on local businesses could provide further context on the issue.
