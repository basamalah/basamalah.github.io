6. K a The ratio of puppies to kittens in a pet shop is 8:3. If there are 18 kittens, how many are puppies?​


✒️PROPORTION

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

The ratio of puppies to kittens in a pet shop is 8:3. If there are 18 kittens, how many are puppies?

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \LARGE \:\: \rm{48 \: puppies} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» Let x be the number of puppies. Determine the proportion between the two ratios.

[tex] 8\::\: 3 = x \::\: 18 [/tex]

» Multiply the means and the extremes.

[tex] (3)(x) = (8)(18) [/tex]

[tex] 3x = 144 [/tex]

[tex] \frac{\,3x\,}{3} = \frac{\,144\,}{3} \\ [/tex]

[tex] x = 48 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] There are 48 puppies in the pet store.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

MATHPopulation vs. Sample

Before we answer this question, let's define what is a population ans what is a sample.

POPULATIONa set of group of things that every member shares common characteristics

SAMPLEa subset of a populationgroup of things that is being tested from a whole population

Now, let's proceed.

By reading the statement above, we could see that Marco wants to specifically study on Labrador, but then he have 5 Labradors and 3 Dalmatians.

Given the definition of what population and sample is, we could easily tell that:

POPULATION = 8 dogs

SAMPLE = 5 Labradors
