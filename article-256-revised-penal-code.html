4. Research Presidential decree of 247 and differentiate the laws punished as compared to Article 164 of the Revised Penal Code. ​


Answer:

Presidential Decree No. 247, also known as the "Anti-Bouncing Check Law," was enacted in 1973 to address the increasing problem of bounced checks in the Philippines. The law provides penalties for those who issue checks without sufficient funds or who stop payment on checks with the intention to defraud.

Under Presidential Decree No. 247, the following acts are punishable:

1.)Issuing a check to apply on account or for value, knowing at the time of issue that the issuer does not have sufficient funds in or credit with the bank for the payment of such check in full upon its presentment, which check is subsequently dishonored by the drawee bank for insufficiency of funds or credit or would have been dishonored for the same reason had not the drawer, without any valid reason, ordered the bank to stop payment.

2.)Stopping payment of a check with the intention to defraud.

The penalty for violating Presidential Decree No. 247 is imprisonment for a minimum period of 30 days or a maximum period of one year, or a fine of not less than the amount of the check nor more than double the amount of the check, or both such fine and imprisonment.

On the other hand, Article 164 of the Revised Penal Code provides penalties for the crime of Estafa through issuance of bouncing checks. Under this law, the following acts are punishable:

1.)Making or drawing and issuing any check to apply on account or for value, knowing at the time of issue that the issuer does not have sufficient funds in or credit with the bank for the payment of such check in full upon its presentment, which check is subsequently dishonored by the drawee bank for insufficiency of funds or credit or would have been dishonored for the same reason had not the drawer, without any valid reason, ordered the bank to stop payment.

2.)Failing to keep sufficient funds in or credit with the drawee bank to cover the full amount of the check when it is presented for payment.

The penalty for violating Article 164 of the Revised Penal Code is imprisonment for a minimum period of 6 months and 1 day to a maximum period of 6 years, or a fine of not less than the value of the check but not more than double the value of the check, or both such fine and imprisonment.

In summary, while both Presidential Decree No. 247 and Article 164 of the Revised Penal Code address the issue of bouncing checks, there are slight differences in the acts punishable and the penalties imposed. The Anti-Bouncing Check Law provides a lower minimum penalty for violators, while the Revised Penal Code imposes a higher minimum penalty but includes the offense of failing to keep sufficient funds to cover the check.

5. define REVISED PENAL CODE OF THE PHILIPPINES?​


The Revised Penal Code contains the general penal laws of the Philippines. First enacted in 1930, it remains in effect today, despite several amendments thereto. It does not comprise a comprehensive compendium of all Philippine penal laws. The Revised Penal Code itself was enacted as Act No. 3815, and some Philippine criminal laws have been enacted outside of the Revised Penal Code as separate Republic Acts.
