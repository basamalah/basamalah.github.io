5. A 430-g soccer ball lying on the ground is kicked and flies off at 25m/s. If the duration of the impact was 0.010s, what was the average force on the ball?


Tools used for this solution:

Impulse = change in momentum

Impulse = (force ) x (time applied)

Momentum = (mass) x (speed)

=================================

Momentum of the ball after the kick = (mass)x(speed) = 0.43kg x 25 m/s = 10.75 kg-m/s .

Impulse exerted by the kicker's toe = 10.75 kg-m/s .

(force) x (time) = 10.75 kg-m/s .

Force x 0.001 sec = 10.75 kg-m/s .

Divide each side by 0.001 sec :

Force = (10.75 kg-m/sec) / (0.001 sec) = 10,750 kg-m/s² = 10,750 newtons.

That's about 2,416 pounds from the kicker's toe, during that ¹/₁₀₀₀ of a second.

30. A soccer ball was kicked at an angle of 300 off the horizontal and with an initial velocity of 23.5 m/s. How long is it in the air and what is the horizontal displacement?


Answer:

a. The total time of flight of the ball is 2.397 seconds

b. The horizontal displacement of the soccer ball is 48.8 meters

Explanation:

In our case, we are to solve for the time which the ball stay in air. This also means for the total time of flight of the soccer ball. We, are also to solve the range or or total horizontal displacement of the ball. Here, we are going to use the principle of Projectile motion.

For the formula, we will use the following:

1. [tex]v_{oy} = v_{o}sin\theta[/tex]

where

[tex]v_{oy}[/tex]     is the initial vertical velocity of the ball, unit is in m/s

[tex]v_{o}[/tex]      is the initial velocity of the ball, unit is in m/s

θ       is the angle created with the horizontal

2. [tex]v_{y}=v_{oy}-gt[/tex]

where

[tex]v_{y}[/tex]     is the vertical velocity at a given time, unit is in m/s

[tex]g[/tex]      is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

[tex]t[/tex]      is the time traveled by the ball, unit is in seconds

3. [tex]v_{ox}=v_{o}cos\theta[/tex]

where

[tex]v_{ox}[/tex]     is the initial horizontal velocity, unit is in m/s

4. [tex]x=v_{ox}t[/tex]

where

[tex]x[/tex]     is the horizontal displacement of the ball, unit is in meter

For the given information

θ = 30°

[tex]v_{o}[/tex] = 23.5 m/s

a. t = ?     total time of flight

b. x = ?    horizontal displacement at total time of flight

Solving the problem

a. First, we will solve the initial vertical velocity of the ball. Let us use the first formula introduced above, we have:

[tex]v_{oy} = v_{o}sin\theta[/tex]

Then substitute the given information, we have:

[tex]v_{oy}[/tex] = 23.5 Sin 30°

[tex]v_{oy}[/tex] = 11.75 m/s

Now, let us solve the time travel of the ball at maximum height. Let us use the next formula, we have:

[tex]v_{y}=v_{oy}-gt[/tex]

Then substitute the given information, we have:

0 = 11.75 m/s - (9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]) t

Take note that at maximum height, the vertical velocity, [tex]v_{y}[/tex] becomes is zero.

Let us now transpose  - (9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]) t to the left side of the equation to solve for t, we have:

(9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]) t = 11.75 m/s

Divide both side the equation by 9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex], we have:

t = [tex]\frac{11.75m/s}{9.8 m/s^2}[/tex]

t = 1.198 seconds

Take note that we should double this time to complete the total time of flight, therefore:

t = 1.198 x 2

t = 2.397 seconds

b. Next, we are to solve for the horizontal displacement of the ball. Let us solve first for [tex]v_{ox}[/tex], we have:

[tex]v_{ox}=v_{o}cos\theta[/tex]

Then substitute the given information, we have:

[tex]v_{ox}[/tex] = 23.5 Cos30°

[tex]v_{ox}[/tex] = 20.35 m/s

Now, we are ready to solve for the horizontal displacement.

Let us use the formula for range, we have:

[tex]x=v_{ox}t[/tex]

Then substitute the given information, we have:

x = (20.35 m/s) (2.397 s)

x = 48.8 meters

Therefore, the time the ball is in the air is 2.397 seconds and its horizontal displacement within this time is 48.8 meters.
