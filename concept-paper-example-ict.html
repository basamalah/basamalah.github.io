7. Write a concept paper what is the PURPOSE? of Contemporary ICT issues such as Cyberbullying, Copyright Infringement, Green Technology, and Internet Addiction ​


Answer:

The purpose of this concept paper is to examine contemporary issues in Information and Communication Technology (ICT) that are of particular relevance to today’s society. In particular, the focus of this paper is four specific issues: cyberbullying, copyright infringement, green technology, and internet addiction. This paper will explore the nature of these issues, their potential impacts on individuals and society, and the measures that are being taken to address them.

Cyberbullying is a form of harassment that occurs online, through social media, email, and other electronic means. It can involve threats, insults, humiliation, and the spreading of private or sensitive information. It can lead to serious emotional and psychological distress, as well as physical harm in some cases.

Copyright infringement involves the unauthorized use of another’s work, typically for commercial gain. It is a serious issue in today’s digital age, with the proliferation of online content, and has serious implications for both businesses and individuals.

Green technology is the application of environmentally friendly and sustainable technologies in order to reduce the environmental impacts of ICT-related activities. It is a growing field, with a focus on reducing energy consumption and waste, as well as the development of renewable energy sources

15. write an reflection about Language concept (ICT)​


Language is a fundamental concept in information and communication technology (ICT) as it plays a vital role in communication between individuals, groups, and machines. The development of ICT has enabled people from different cultures and backgrounds to communicate and exchange information effortlessly, breaking down geographical barriers and promoting global collaboration.

In the context of ICT, language has become more than just a tool for communication; it is also a medium for the creation and transmission of knowledge. The internet, for example, has allowed for the creation of vast amounts of information, much of which is written in various languages. Machine translation and natural language processing (NLP) technologies have made it possible to translate and process this information, making it accessible to people who speak different languages.

However, while ICT has made communication and knowledge exchange more accessible, it has also presented new challenges related to language. For instance, the use of computer-mediated communication (CMC) tools such as instant messaging and social media has given rise to new forms of language use and communication styles that may be difficult for some individuals to understand. Moreover, the use of ICT has also brought about new forms of language-based crimes such as cyberbullying and online harassment, which pose a threat to individuals' mental health and wellbeing.

In conclusion, language is a crucial concept in ICT as it enables communication, knowledge creation, and information exchange. However, the development of ICT has also presented new challenges related to language, which need to be addressed to ensure that everyone can benefit from the advantages that ICT provides.
