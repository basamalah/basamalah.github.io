17. 5 Do’s and Don’t’s in using candles and making woodcraft?


Answer:

DOS:

•Add your fragrance at the right temp: Adding your fragrance oil when your wax is at the proper temperature will help it bind to the wax, which will help give you a stronger scent throw. It is usually recommended to add your fragrance to the wax at 180-185F for soy and paraffin wax 200-205F for palm wax.

•Stir consistently for at least 3-5 minutes: This is also very important. Stirring for a short amount of time can keep your fragrance oil from binding to the wax. This can cause the oil to settle to the bottom of the wax which can also result in a weak scent throw.

•Trim your wick: Not trimming your wick can result in smoke, soot, mushrooming, and can cause a large flame. It's important to keep your wick trimmed so your candle burns properly, and for your safety. It is recommended to trim wicks for paraffin candles down to 1/4th of an inch, 1/8th for soy candles, and just above 1/4th for our wood wicks in any application.

•Preheat your containers: Preheating your containers will help slow down the cooling process & helps prevent the wax from shrinking and pulling away from the container, developing wet spots.

•Use a thermometer: You always want to monitor your temperature throughout the whole candle making process. Doing so will prevent you from scorching the wax and it will ensure that you are adding fragrance and pouring the wax at the recommended temperatures.

23. Paraffin (C22H46) is a wax used in candle-making. During combustion, a 20.0g candle produces 1.42 moles of CO2. How many molecules of CO2 are released in the atmosphere after using the candle? ​


Number of molecules of CO₂ = 8.548 x 10²³

Further explanation  

A reaction coefficient is a number in the chemical formula of a substance involved in the reaction equation. The reaction coefficient is useful for equalizing reagents and products.  

A mole is the number of particles (atoms, molecules, ions) contained in a substance that is the same amount as many atoms in 12 gr C-12  

1 mole = 6.02.10²³ particles  

N = n x No

No = 6.02.10²³ = Avogadro's number

Given

20.0g candle

1.42 moles of CO₂

Required

molecules of CO₂

Solution

Reaction

2C₂₂H₄₆ + 67O₂⇒ 44CO₂ + 46 H₂O

Let's check if 20 g of paraffin produces 1.42 moles of CO₂

molar mass of paraffin: 310.6 g / mol

mol paraffin:

= mass: molar mass

= 20: 310.6

= 0.0644

From the equation above, the mole ratio of paraffin: CO₂ = 2: 44, then the moles of CO₂:

= 44/2 x 0.0644

= 1.4168 ≈ 1.42

Then it's correct, 20 g Paraffin produces 1.42 moles of CO₂

So the number of particles (molecules):  

Number of molecules = 1.42 x 6.02.10²³  

Number of molecules = 8.548 x 10²³

30. you are designing candle-making kits. each kits contains 25 cubic inches of candle wax and a mold for making a pyramid-shaped candle with a square base.you want the height of the candle to be 2 inches less than the edge of the base. what should the dimensions of your candle mold be? show a mathematical procedure in determining the dimensions.


First, recall that the volume of a pyramid is: V = (length)(width)(height)/3.

Now since we have a square base, the length and width are equal, so let's call that "s" for side. Then V = s^2*h/3, where h is the height.

The question asks for a height that is "2 inches less than the edge of the base", and we know that the edge of the base is the side. This means that h = s - 2.

Thus our equation is now V = s^2(s-2)/3.

Since each kit has 25 cubic inches of wax, that should be the volume that we use. Then 25 = s^2(s-2)/3.

Now, we just solve for s:

25 = s^2(s-2)/3

75 = s^2(s-2) , from multiplying both sides by 3

75 = s^3 - 2s^2, from expanding the right hand side

s^3 - 2s^2 - 75 = 0, from transposing the other side

(s-5)(s^2 + 3s +15) = 0, by factoring or synthetic division

Now if we take the roots of s^2 + 3s + 15 = 0, we will get imaginary numbers, so let's only use s-5 = 0, or s = 5.

We now have s = 5. So, the length and width are 5 inches each. Since h = s-2, h = 3, or the height is 3 inches.


Checking, volume = (length)(width)(height)/3 = (5)(5)(3)/3 = 25. Correct.
