15. excerpts whether literature or not?​


Answer:

Excerpt is not a literature.

Explanation:

Excerpt is a clip, snippet, passage or extract from a larger work such as a news article, a film, a literary composition or other media. The definition of an excerpt is a quote from a book or film. ... A passage or segment taken from a longer work, such as a literary or musical composition, a document, or a film.

An excerpt in writing is a quoted passage taken from a longer work, such as a book, or poem, or an article. Whatever the subject of your writing or the type of writing you intend to compose, excerpts can be used to 'show' readers what it is you want them to understand and remember about the subject.
