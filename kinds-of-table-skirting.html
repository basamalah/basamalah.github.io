21. what is the importance of table skirting


Answer:

Importance of Table Skirting

Table skirt is part of a table design. Aside from making the table beautiful and complete, there are other awesome reasons to use table skirting.

Table skirts hide the legs of the table.

It hides the unnecessary things under the table without anyone ever getting to see them.

It improves the look of the table.

Protects the table from any potential damage.

Prevents food or drink to spill on the guests while eating their meals.

Protects the dining table in getting stained.

Table Skirting

Table skirting can be used at home or in restaurants and even in hotels. It is defined as draping a table with a table cloth in order to give the table a formal and elegant look.

Table skirts consist of two components: the cloth table topper and a skirt that fastens to the table edge with clips.

Different Kinds of Table Skirting

Table skirting can be done in different ways. Here are some of its kinds.

Gathered or Shirred

Gathered or shirred table skirts are a popular choice due to the simplicity and reasonable cost. It provides extra body to the skirt. A tighter gather gives a table skirt a full, ruffled appearance.

Knife Pleat

This is one of the most common kinds of pleating for table skirts. A knife pleat is a series of narrow, sharp creases folded in the same direction. It is a classic choice that is the least expensive of the pleated variety.

Box Pleat

It is made from a sequence of back-to-back knife pleats. Box pleated table skirting is a good choice for a professional conference or business meeting.

Fringe

It is a playful addition to party tables.

Fabrics Used in Table Skirting

There are various types of fabrics used in skirting. Some are as follows:

Polyester

This is an inexpensive skirt and is made synthetically. It doesn’t absorb liquids which makes it a great choice.

Plastic

It is another cheap fabric that has a really long lifetime as well.

Linen

It is extremely stretchable and flexible fabric.

Cotton

It is one of the most versatile types in terms of possible colors and patterns.

Vinyl

This is a synthetic material that falls in-between thin plastic and polyester.

Satin

Made from a floated weave and characterized by having one side look shiny while the other side is more matte.
