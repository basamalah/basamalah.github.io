Explanation:

1. Who do you think is speaking in the poem?

The one speaking in the poem is generally assessed as an agent of the state. Since the voice is seemingly authoritarian.  

2.What is meant by self-censorship?Why is it considered as good governance?

Self-censorship is the act of censoring or classifying one's own discourse. This is done out of fear of, or deference to, the sensibilities or preferences (actual or perceived) of others and without overt pressure from any specific party or institution of authority.

It is a mean to assert force and authority.

3.What rules for citizen are mentioned in the poem?

• Let us govern those who undertake the telling of stories.

• Censorship is good governance. ...

• If an actor speaks of God, he will be chastised. ...

• Let us govern those who undertake praise of the next world, since what they say is neither true nor useful to us.

• Our best recourse is to be warlike.

4 Do you agree with the rules given?why or why not?

No, absolutely not. The idea with the rules is tyrannical. It sounds that it is commanding the citizens that their freedom of thought shall not be exercised. If government tries to control what you think and how you behave, this is to preempt the citizens’ right to speak out or complain about the abuses of the state.
