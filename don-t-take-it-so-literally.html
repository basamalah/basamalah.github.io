13. a ball is thrown upward so it's height (t) seconds later is six feet above the earth where is = 96(t) - 16(t)². What is the maximum height that the ball will reach?​


Step-by-step explanation:

It is given that the total time of the journey upward and downward is 6 seconds. As the distance travelled by the ball in both the upward and downward journey is the same so, let us take the distance travelled during both upward and downward motion to be ‘d’ meters each. So as per the 1st equation of motion,

v = u+at

In the case of the upward journey,

v = 0 m/s, a = -10 m/s^2 and since the time of both the upward and downward journies is the same so t for upward journey = t for downward journey = 6/2 = 3s.

as per the above mentioned equation

0 = u - 10 x 3

0 = u - 30

u = 30 m/s

Now as per the 2nd equation of motion,

s = ut + 1/2 at^2

s = 30 x 3 + 1/2 x -10 x 9

s = 90 - 45

s = 45 m

So the greatest height reached by the ball is of 45 m and the initial velocity of the ball is 30 m/s.

27. 6. A gas is compressed at a constant pressure of 0.7 atm from 8 liters t 1.5 liters and releases 600 J of thermal energy. What is the work don by the gas? A. -959.65 J C. -369.66 J D. -758.56 J B. -459.55 J


Answer:

Correct option is C)

Solution:- (C) +396 joule

Since work is done against constant pressure, i.e., the process is irreversible.

Given:-

ΔV=V

2

−V

1

Given that the gas expands from a volume of 2L to the volume of 6L.

∴ΔV=6−2=4L

∴W=−PΔV=−1×4=−4atm=−405.3J

From first law of thermodynamics-

q=ΔU−W

⇒ΔU=q+w

Given that q=800J

∴ΔU=800+(−405.3)=800−405.3=394.7J≈396J

Hence the correct answer is +396J.
