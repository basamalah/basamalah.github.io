15. what is a "rite"?why must proper rites be observed​


Answer:

A rite pertains to a religious act or ritual done with solemnity.

Explanation:

Rites are done with a religious concept in observance of the faith of a group of people. It is synonymous to ritual, sacrament, and service. There is a specific order or sequence how things should be done during the ceremony. Particular words and actions are required as cue for the next action to initiate.  Some of the common rites that we can see are:

baptismsacrifices and offeringsfuneral services

A rite is defined as a religious or other solemn ceremony or act. It is something that has to be respected as this is form parts of culture and tradition of some of the ethnic groups.

Explanation:

A rite is considered as an established, cereonial, more often than not religious, act.

Rites in this respect fall into three major categories such as:

rites of passage, under this category, it generally has the effect of changing an individual's social status, like marriage, adoption, baptism, coming of age, graduation, inauguration, and the like;communal rites, it may of worship, where a certain group of people comes together to worship, like for instance the Jewish synagogue or mass, or of some other character, such as fertility rites and certain non religious festivals;rites of personal belief or devotion, where an individual worship, inclusive of prayer and pilgrimages like the Muslim Hajj, pledges of allegiance, or promises to marry someone.
