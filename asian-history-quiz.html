19. The data set below shows the scores of 8 students in a 15-item History quiz. 12, 9, 8, 15, 8, 7, 5, 10 Find the range, variance, and standard deviation. Round off the answers in the nearest two decimal numbers.


To find the range, subtract the lowest score from the highest score:

Range = 15 - 5 = 10

To find the variance:

1. Find the mean:

Mean = (12 + 9 + 8 + 15 + 8 + 7 + 5 + 10) / 8 = 9.5

2. Subtract the mean from each score and square the results:

(12 - 9.5)^2 = 5.25

(9 - 9.5)^2 = 0.25

(8 - 9.5)^2 = 2.25

(15 - 9.5)^2 = 30.25

(8 - 9.5)^2 = 2.25

(7 - 9.5)^2 = 6.25

(5 - 9.5)^2 = 18.25

(10 - 9.5)^2 = 0.25

3. Add up the squared results:

5.25 + 0.25 + 2.25 + 30.25 + 2.25 + 6.25 + 18.25 + 0.25 = 64.75

4. Divide the result by the number of scores (8):

Variance = 64.75 / 8 = 8.09375

To find the standard deviation, take the square root of the variance:

Standard deviation = √8.09375 = 2.85 (rounded off to the nearest two decimal numbers)
