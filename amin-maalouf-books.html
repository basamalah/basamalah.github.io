1. what is the difference in structure between an aliphatic amine and aromatic amine?


Aliphatic Amine are molecules wherein Amino group is not directly attached to an Aromatic Compound. They Can be straight chain alkanes bonded to amine. While Aromatic Amine are molecules wherein the amino group is directly attached to an aromatic compound, example is Aniline.

2. what is a an amine???


amine, any member of a family of nitrogen-containing organic compounds that is derived, either in principle or in practice, from ammonia (NH3). ... Amines are classified as primary, secondary, or tertiary depending on whether one, two, or three of the hydrogen atoms of ammonia have been replaced by organic groups.

Explanation:

Inorganic derivatives of ammonia are also called amines, such as monochloramine (NClH2). The substituent -NH2 is called an amino group. 11. compare and contrast amine and amide​


Answer:

The addition of nitrogen into an organic framework leads to two families of molecules. Compounds containing a nitrogen atom bonded in a hydrocarbon framework are classified as amines. Compounds that have a nitrogen atom bonded to one side of a carbonyl group are classified as amides.
