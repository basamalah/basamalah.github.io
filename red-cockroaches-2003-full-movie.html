9. distinguishing characteristics of cockroach​


Answer:

Cockroaches are generalized insects with few special adaptations, and may be among the most primitive living Neopteran insects. They have a relatively small head and a broad, flattened body, and most species are reddish-brown to dark brown. They have large compound eyes, two ocelli, and long, flexible antennae.

17. pupa of a cockroach​


Answer:

Nymph. Once a cockroach hatches, the young, small cockroach is called a nymph. The nymphs go through the process known as molting, where they shed their skin and their bodies becomes white and soft. Whenever the cockroach molts, the cockroach also grows in size and color.

Denotation of cockroach: any of numerous orthopterous insects of the family Blattidae, characterized by a flattened body, rapid movements, and nocturnal habits and including several common household pests.
