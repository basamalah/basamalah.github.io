10. What are the types of educational leadership?


Hi there!

Schools and educators have different ways of approaching education and managing schools. Here are five of the known effective educational leadership types:

1. Instructional Leadership

This type of leadership mostly involves planning, evaluating, and improving the system of teaching and learning. Instructional leaders set the standard for the school's instructional program.Solving pedagogical problems is the focus of the leader - they work directly with teachers and give them constructive feedback on how to improve while making sure that the approach to learning is aligned with the school's mission.

2. Constructivist Leadership

In this setting, a teacher is not the "person who teaches" anymore but becomes a "facilitator of learning". The class becomes a shared experience for the teacher and students - a student-centered approach to learning.A "constructivist" leader considers individual learning needs and checking for understanding. One specific example is that they ensure that students understand the lesson by questioning their answer and making them explain it. Student reflection is also encouraged.

3. Transformational Leadership

By taking on a role model figure, leaders empower their school teams to take a collaborative approach to learning/working by building a culture of trust, respect and openness with each other.Leaders in this setting practice compassion and value emotional intelligence - teams under this leadership style is shown to be motivated and inspired to strengthen their personal and professional growth.

4. Servant Leadership

The core principle of this leadership style is that it focuses on the real needs of others and the school community rather than self-interest.Leaders who practice this exhibit listening skills, empathy, conceptualization, and commitment to community-building among others.Engagement in decision-making and fulfilling the vision of the school is high; servant leaders focus on their desire to gain results and improvement while building meaningful relationships with the community.

5. Strategic Leadership

Strategic leaders focus more on long-term planning rather than solving day-to-day problems.Heavily drawing their decisions from evidence, research, and data to evaluate and assess the current school performance, leaders are able to think ahead and create preventative measures and allocate resources.

22. why the NSTP - MAPUA office created the 3 component of NSTP​


Answer:

The Republic Act No. 9163, which is the act that establishes the National Service Training Program for tertiary level students, stipulated that the program will have three components tertiary level students can sign up for with the purpose of enhancing civic consciousness and defense preparedness in the youth by developing the ethics of service and patriotism. These programs are the following:

Reserve-Officers’ Training Corps or ROTC which is a program institutionalized under Sections 38 and 39 of Republic Act No. 7077 intended to provide military training to tertiary level students in order to motivate, train, organize and mobilize them as part of national defense preparednessLiteracy Training Service or LTS which is a program developed to train students to become teachers of reading, writing, and number skills to school children, out of school youth, and other segments of society who are in need of their serviceCivic Welfare Training Service or CWTS refers to programs or activities contributory to the general welfare and the enhancement of life for the members of the community or the betterment of its facilities, especially those devoted to improving health, education, environment, entrepreneurship, safety, recreation and morals of the Filipino citizens.

Mapua, just like other universities, is following the rules and regulations set by the Republic Act No. 9163.
