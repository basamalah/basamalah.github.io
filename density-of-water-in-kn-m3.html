4. The surface tensions of mercury and water at 60C are 0. 47 N/m and 0. 0662 N/m, respectively. What capillary-height changes will occur in these two fluids when they are in contact with air in a glass tube of radius 0. 30 mm? Use angle of contact = 130 deg. For mercury, and 0 deg. For water; specific weight of mercury is 132. 3 kN/m3 and 9. 650 kN/m3 for water.


To calculate the capillary-height change in these two fluids, we can use the following equation:

Δh = 2γcosθ / ρgr

Where:

Δh is the capillary-height change

γ is the surface tension of the fluid

θ is the angle of contact between the fluid and the glass

ρ is the density of the fluid

g is the acceleration due to gravity

r is the radius of the glass tube

For mercury, we can plug in the values given in the question to get:

Δh = 2 * 0.47 N/m * cos(130 deg) / (132.3 kN/m3 * 9.81 m/s2 * 0.30 mm)

= 4.88 x 10-6 m

For water, we can plug in the values given in the question to get:

Δh = 2 * 0.0662 N/m * cos(0 deg) / (9.650 kN/m3 * 9.81 m/s2 * 0.30 mm)

= 1.29 x 10-6 m

Therefore, the capillary-height change in mercury will be 4.88 x 10-6 m and the capillary-height change in water will be 1.29 x 10-6 m.

12. A solid object is made of two materials, one material having density of 2 000 kg/m3 and the other having density of 5 000 kg/m3. If the object contains equal volumes of the materials, what is its average density?


Answer:

The average density is 3,500 kg/[tex]m^3[/tex].

Explanation:

Density is the ratio of mass to its volume. Its unit is in kg/[tex]m^3[/tex], g/L, g/[tex]cm^3[/tex]. The formula for density is:

ρ [tex]=\frac{m}{V}[/tex]

where

ρ    is the density

m   is the mass, unit is in kg

V   is the volume, unit is in liters (L), [tex]m^3[/tex], [tex]cm^3[/tex]

Formula for average density

[tex]\rho _{ave} =[/tex] ∑ [tex]\frac{\rho }{n}[/tex]

where

∑ρ     is the sum of all densities

n       is the total number of densities

Given information

[tex]\rho _{1} = 2000 kg/m^3[/tex]

[tex]\rho _{2} =5000 kg/m^3[/tex]

[tex]\rho _{ave} =[/tex] ?

Solving the problem

Using the formula for average density

[tex]\rho _{ave} =[/tex] ∑ [tex]\frac{\rho }{n}[/tex]

Substitute the given information

[tex]\rho _{ave} =[/tex] [tex]\frac{\rho _{1}+\rho _{2}}{2}[/tex]

We get the sum of the densities and n = 2 since there are only two (2) given densities.

[tex]\rho _{ave} =[/tex] [tex]\frac{2000kg/m^3+5000kg/m^3}{2}[/tex]

[tex]\rho _{ave} =[/tex] 3,500 kg/[tex]m^3[/tex]

20. 2. I. Calculate the specific gravity of a substance with a density of 0.917 x 103 kg/m3. The density of water is 1.000 x 103 kg/m3 A. 0.92 B. 0.08 C. 1.09 D. 1.17 II. Will it float or sink? A. Float B. Sink III. What percentage of the object is submerged in water? A. 8% B. 23% C. 87 D. 92% Show your solution


SOLUTION:

I.

To calculate for specific gravity of the substance, we have to use the formula below:

[tex]\boxed{\bold{Specific \ Gravity = \frac{\rho \ of \ the \ object }{\rho \ of \ the \ H_2O}}}[/tex]

Substitute the density of the substance and water.

[tex]\rm{Specific \ Gravity = \frac{0.917\times \:10^3}{1\times \:10^3} = \frac{10^3\times \:0.917}{10^3} \approx 0.92 \ kg/m^3}[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The Specific Gravity is 0.92 kg/m³ (A.)

II.

Will it float or sink?

- As we can see our Specific Gravity is 0.92 which is less than 1. If our Specific Gravity is greater 1, it will sink. Therefore, it will float because our  Specific Gravity is less than one. (A.)

III.

To find the percentage of the object submerged in water, simply we have to do is convert our Specific Gravity to percentage.

[tex]\rm{Specific \ Gravity \times\left(\frac{100\%}{1}\right) = 0.92 \times \left(\frac{100\%}{1}\right) = 92\% }[/tex]

Hence, the percentage of the object is submerged in water is 92%. (D.)
