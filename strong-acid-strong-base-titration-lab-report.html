5. When an acetic acid solution is titrated with sodium hydroxide, the slope of the titration curve (Ph vs volume of NaOH added) increases when sodium hydroxide is first added. This change shows that a. the pH is not affected until all the acetic acid is consumed. b. the reaction is very slow during this part of the titration c. acetic acid is being converted to sodium acetate. d. a more concentrated solution of NaOH needs to be present to initiate the reaction.


c. acetic acid is being converted to sodium acetate.

When an acetic acid solution is titrated with sodium hydroxide, the slope of the titration curve increases when sodium hydroxide is first added because this is when acetic acid is being converted to sodium acetate. This conversion causes the pH of the solution to change, resulting in an increase in the slope of the titration curve. Options a and b are not correct because the pH of the solution is affected as soon as sodium hydroxide is added, and the reaction is not necessarily slow during this part of the titration. Option d is not correct because the concentration of the sodium hydroxide solution does not affect the slope of the titration curve in this way.

15. 1. What is the importance of acid-base titrations?


Acid-base titration is important which aims to determine the concentration of an acidic solution by titrating it with a known concentration of a base solution, or vice versa, until neutralization. Therefore, air and salt are produced when a strong acid and a strong base react.

What is Acid-Base Titration?

An acid-base titration is the concentration of an acid solution with a known base solution, indicating that this is also included in the titration and must be based on a neutralization reaction. The equivalence point is indicated by a color change in the indicator. On the other hand, the titration status also shows a color change, which is called the end point of the titration. For correct titration results, a method can be used to see the points of difference between the end point of the titration and the equivalence.

Types of acid-base titration

There are two types of acid-base titrations:

Acidimetry is used to determine the value of the concentration of a base solution using a standard acid solution.

Alkalimetric test is used to determine the value of the concentration of a base solution using a standard acid solution.

Application of acid-base titration in daily life

- The food industry

Examples are foods like vinegar, which contain a weak acid called acetic acid. This product is the result of certain manufacturers' using different levels of acetic acid. because the product has a fundamental difference from factory to factory.

- Cosmetics manufacturing

Apart from the food industry, the cosmetic industry is another field where acid-base titration products are often used. Titration determines the exact concentration and amount of ingredients used in the manufacture of cosmetics. From hair dyes, shampoos, conditioners, and detergents to acid and alkaline night creams,etc.

- Wine industrial.

Titration plays an important role in the development of the wine industry and the processes that affect its quality. It is necessary to measure the concentration of some acids, such as tartaric acid, malic acid, and citric acid. They have different effects on the taste, color, and microbes of wine.
