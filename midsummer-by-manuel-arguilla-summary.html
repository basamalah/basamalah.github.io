Manuel Arguilla's short tale "Midsummer" is set in a rural community in the Philippines and addresses the economic and social challenges encountered by the farmers and laborers who tend the land.

One of the key economic themes in the novel is the struggle of the farmers to achieve a decent income.

The narrative demonstrates how the farmers put a lot of effort into growing their crops yet are still unable to provide for their family.

They are compelled to sell their produce at cheap rates to intermediaries who subsequently resell it in the city for more money. The farmers are trapped in an endless cycle of debt and poverty as a result of this economic abuse.

The lack of opportunity for rural areas to attain education and employment is another economic problem that is mentioned in the narrative.

The protagonist, Istak, dreams of leaving the town to study and find a better profession, but his family cannot afford to send him to school. For rural populations, a lack of opportunities and education feeds the cycle of poverty.

The class gap between farmers and landowners is one of the societal concerns that the novel also focuses on. The lives of the farmers are more heavily influenced by the landowners, who also enjoy more social prestige and authority.

They have the power to set agricultural prices and regulate farmers' access to resources like land and water.

Overall, "Midsummer" throws attention on the structural problems that support poverty and inequality as well as the economic and social difficulties encountered by rural communities in the Philippines.
