15. Who is Marcelo De Gracia Concepcion?​


Answer:

M. de Gracia Concepcion was listed on the staff of the Three Stars as an editor in 1931. He was the first Filipino poet to have his collected poems published as a book in the U.S. Published in 1925 by G.P. Putnam's Sons, the book was entitled Azucena, named after a lily found in the Philippines.

28. trinidad,san antonio,concepcion,elena ships of magellan?


Answer:

The ships of Magellan's expedition were the Trinidad, San Antonio, Concepcion, Victoria, and Santiago

Explanation:

Magellan's expedition was a historic voyage that aimed to find a new trade route to the Spice Islands in the East Indies. The expedition consisted of five ships, namely the Trinidad, San Antonio, Concepcion, Victoria, and Santiago. The Trinidad was the flagship of the expedition, and it was the largest and most heavily armed ship. It was commanded by Magellan himself and carried most of the crew and supplies. The San Antonio was the second-largest ship and was commanded by Juan de Cartagena. The Concepcion was a smaller ship commanded by Gaspar de Quesada, and it was used to transport supplies and provisions.

The Victoria was a small caravel commanded by Luis Mendoza, and it was used for scouting and exploration. The Santiago was also a small caravel commanded by Juan Serrano, and it was used for reconnaissance and mapping.

During the expedition, the ships faced many challenges, including storms, mutinies, and hostile encounters with indigenous peoples. Despite these difficulties, the expedition managed to reach the Spice Islands and complete the first circumnavigation of the globe, with only the Victoria returning to Spain with 18 survivors.
