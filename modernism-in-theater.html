5. with the continuous development of modern technology evolved do you still recommend the protection of the presentation of traditional theater such as wayang kulit why or why not​


I can provide some points to consider regarding the protection of traditional theater such as Wayang Kulit.

Wayang Kulit is a traditional form of puppet theater from Indonesia that has been performed for centuries. It is a cultural heritage that has been passed down from generation to generation, and it carries significant value in terms of history, art, and storytelling.

Although modern technology has brought new forms of entertainment and media, traditional theater such as Wayang Kulit should still be protected and preserved. Here are some reasons why:

Cultural preservation: Traditional theater like Wayang Kulit is an integral part of the cultural heritage of a country. It reflects the history, beliefs, and values of the society it originated from. Preserving such art forms helps to keep the culture alive and allows future generations to learn from and appreciate their cultural roots.

Artistic expression: Traditional theater is a unique form of artistic expression that carries a different flavor and beauty than modern theater. It is an excellent example of how art can tell stories and convey important messages in a distinctive way.

Educational value: Traditional theater like Wayang Kulit is often used to teach moral values, history, and cultural knowledge. It can be a valuable tool for educating young people about their cultural roots and promoting cross-cultural understanding.

In conclusion, protecting the presentation of traditional theater such as Wayang Kulit is crucial for the preservation of cultural heritage, artistic expression, and educational value.

15. What would costumes be like in the time of Greek Dramas? Elizabeth Theater? Modern Times?


Answer:

GREEK DRAMAS - Costumes, along with masks and props, helped indicate the social status, gender, and age of a character. Athenian characters wore more elaborate, decorated versions of everyday clothing, such as a tunic or undergarment (chitôn or peplos), a cloak or over-garment (himation).

ELIZABETH THEATER - The dresses were full skirted and sleeved. The sleeves were worn apart and the ensemble accessorized with shoes, capes, hats and reticules. The men's costume at the Elizabethan theatre was a shirt, stockings, britches, doublet which was a well fitted coat, sleeves separate, same stiff high collar and an overcoat.

MODERN TIMES - Street style is a modern phenomenon. In essence the term refers to what regular, everyday people are wearing "on the streets," and thus reflects general trends that many people are wearing. ... Modern street style is about dressing yourself in your own unique style based loosely on common trends.
