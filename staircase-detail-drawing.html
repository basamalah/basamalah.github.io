12. elements arranged in staircase pattern


Answer:

The staircase line indicates the position of the metalloids in the Periodic Table.

Explanation:

The elements along side the line are metalloids. They have both metallic and nonmetallic character.

The elements on the immediate right of the line tend to gain electrons more than lose them. They have relatively high electronegativities.

Silicon, for example, is a semiconductor. It is more of a nonmetal than a metal, but it has some of the properties of both metals and nonmetals.

The elements to the immediate left of the line tend to lose electrons more easily than gain them. These elements have relatively low electronegativities.

Boron, for example, forms positive ions like metals. However, it is not malleable like most metals, but is brittle like a nonmetal.

18. Why is the Stages of Change depicted as a spiral staircase?


#BrainlyFast

The stages of change depicted as a spiral staircase since as we continue living, a lot of changes will be encounter and there is no skipping on it. The process of change is a never ending process as long as we are still living. Some experience will relapse but we still need to continue to moving until we reach the finish line and accomplished the change.

When we a smoker, we know that this behavior is problematic. And in order to make a change, we follow the 5 stages of change if we want to quit smoking. In precontemplation change, the individual have no idea that smoking is problematic and they might continue doing it. In contemplation stage, the person understang that smoking is not good for his health and action is intended in the next 6 months. When he is determined to take a change he then enter the preparation stage in which he is willing to make a change in the next 30 days. In the action stage, he already take action in quitting smoking. In the maintenance stage, he will continue not to smoke. In the termination stage, he no longer have the desire to smoke again and the change of behavior (quitting smoking) is completed.

If any of the stage will not be accomplished, relapse will occur and there will be no change in behavior.

The following are 5 stages of change.

1. Precontemplation - People are unaware that their behavior are somehow problematic. They do not take change for foreseeable future.

2. Contemplation-  Health is the main focus for the future in this stage.

3. Preparation-  have made an attempt to change.

4. Action- People are ready to take action in the next 30 days.

5. Maintenance- Continue doing the change more than 6 months

6. Termination - People will not go back to their problematic behavior and no relapse occur.
