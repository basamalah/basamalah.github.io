17. Cite and explain at least 5 laws of proportion? Perdev po to Perdev = Personality development


The Laws of Proportion in Personality Development

The Laws of Proportion are the style guidelines that highlight your strong features and conceal your bad ones.

List at least 5 laws of proportion, and explain each one.

1. An unbroken line appears longer.

Horizontal stripes, belts, two-piece outfits, clashing hues, skirts and blouses, large bands of color, or any horizontal line make you appear shorter and add breadth to your figure.

2. Being tall makes you appear thin.

If your height and form are proportionate, just breach the vertical line while dressing for height.

3. Light hues increase weight.

Whites and pastels will make it appear as though you are gaining weight. Be sure to only put them where this addition is required.

4. Dark hues reduce weight.

Place the darkest to lightest shades of the color palette on the parts of your body that need to visually lose weight.

5. Fabrics that reflect light and shine weigh more

Brocades, metallic fabrics, satin, and sequins all visually enlarge the wearer's frame. Place them only where they are required as an addition or as tiny fashion details.

30. least favorite topic in (perdev) personal development,then explain why​


Answer:

One topic that some people might find uninteresting or unimportant is time management. They might feel that they already have a good handle on managing their time and don't see the need to focus on it further. Others might find the topic of time management to be too rigid or restrictive and prefer to work in a more organic and flexible way.

Another topic that some people might not be interested in is goal setting. They might find the process of setting and achieving specific goals to be too structured or formulaic, and prefer to focus on more general self-improvement or self-discovery.

A third topic that some people might not be interested in is money management. They might find it to be too mundane or boring, or might feel that it is not relevant to their personal development.

Ultimately, everyone is different and will have their own interests and priorities when it comes to personal development. What is important to one person may not be important to another, and what one person finds uninteresting or unimportant, another person might find to be essential.
