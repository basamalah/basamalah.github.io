6. A reporter based his conclusion on 3000 responses from 5000 questionnaires distributed to men. the type of sampling technique use is


SAMPLING TECHNIQUE

Attached is a picture for each sampling technique below.

The method used to choose the subjects for the sample is known as a sampling methodology and can be identified by name or another means.

Random, systematic, convenient, cluster, and stratified sampling are the five different types. The comparison between random sampling and plucking a few names out of a hat using everyone's names is accurate. There is a fair likelihood that every component of the population will appear.

Type of Sampling Technique

Random Sampling

It is comparable to picking some names from a hat after placing everyone's names in it. In the population, each component has an equal chance of appearing. Although this method of sampling is preferable, it is frequently challenging to carry out. It necessitates obtaining an exhaustive list of every component in the population. Random sampling frequently uses lists that were generated by computers.

Example: A reporter based his conclusion on 3000 responses from 5000 questionnaires distributed to men.

Systematic Sampling

It is simpler to do systematic sampling than random sampling. In systematic sampling, the elements are "counted off" from the list. Every kth element is thus taken. Comparable to having everyone in a line and counting off "1,2,3,4; 1,2,3,4; etc." All those numbered 4 would be used once the list had been completed.

Convenience Sampling

Although convenience sampling is the most straightforward method, it should never be used. Convenient data are utilized in convenience sampling. These are the first individuals the surveyor encounters.

Cluster Sampling

By segmenting the population into clusters, usually geographically, cluster sampling is performed. These units are referred to as blocks or clusters. The clusters are chosen at random, and each component in the chosen clusters is used.

Stratified Sampling

The population is also divided into groups known as strata via stratified sampling. This time, though, it is based on a characteristic rather than a location. The population might be divided, for example, into males and females. Each of these strata is sampled using convenience, systematic, or random sampling techniques.

What is a standardized questionnaire and what is a research made questionnaire​ .

A standardized questionnaire is one that is developed and delivered in such a way that all participants are asked the same questions in the same format and their replies are recorded in the same way. A measure's reliability improves when it is standardized. A formal standardized questionnaire is a survey tool used to collect data on people or a social unit such as a home or a school from individuals. When each responder is subjected to the same questions and the same coding system, the questionnaire is considered to be standardized.

What are the best ways to standardize a questionnaire?

A standardized questionnaire needs to considert these:

Preliminary questionnaire design decisions

Make a list of the information you'll need.

Define your target audience.

Select the method(s) for contacting your target audience.

Make a decision about the content of the inquiry.

Make changes to the question's phrasing.

Put your questions in a logical sequence and structure.

Check the questionnaire's length.

Structured and unstructured questionnaires are the two main forms of questionnaires. The quasi-structured questionnaire, which is commonly used in social science research, is a combination of these two. Pre-coded questions are included in structured surveys, as are well-defined skipping patterns for following the question sequence.

15. Explain different tools used in the different stages of scope management: 1. Meeting minutes template 2. Questionnaires and surveys 3. Prototypes 4. Variance analysis tools (e.g., spreadsheets)


1. Meeting minutes template: This tool is typically used in the planning stage of scope management. During meetings, notes are taken and compiled into a meeting minutes document. This document serves as a reference to help ensure that everyone is on the same page and understands what was discussed and decided upon.

2. Questionnaires and surveys: This tool is usually used in the initiation stage of scope management. These tools help to gather information from stakeholders, such as their requirements and expectations. This information can then be used to define the project scope and identify potential issues that need to be addressed.

3. Prototypes: Prototyping is a tool that is often used in the design stage of scope management. A prototype is a preliminary model or version of the final product. This tool enables stakeholders to visualize the product design and provide feedback that can be used to refine and improve the final product.

4. Variance analysis tools (e.g., spreadsheets): Variance analysis tools are typically used in the control stage of scope management. These tools help to identify differences between actual project performance and the plan. For example, by using spreadsheets to monitor and analyze project costs, project managers can identify areas where the project is over or under budget and take corrective action.

Answer:

Questionnaire design is a multistage process that requires attention to many details at once. Designing the questionnaire is complicated because surveys can ask about topics in varying degrees of detail, questions can be asked in different ways, and questions asked earlier in a survey may influence how people respond to later questions.

Questionnaire length refers to the amount of time it takes a respondent to complete a questionnaire. Survey instruments can vary in length from less than a minute to more than an hour. The length of a questionnaire is important because it can directly affect response rates, survey costs, and data quality.
