6. Give at least five Filipino values and evaluate if it’s a positive or a negative one. Then explain why?​


Answer:

1.Philosophical basis

Filipino values are, for the most part, centered at maintaining social harmony, motivated primarily by the desire to be accepted within a group. The main sanction against diverging from these values are the concepts of "Hiya", roughly translated as 'a sense of shame', and "Amor propio" or 'self-esteem'.[2] Social approval, acceptance by a group, and belonging to a group are major concerns. Caring about what others will think, say or do, are strong influences on social behavior among Filipinos.[3]

2.Models of the Filipino values Edit

F. Landa Jocano identified two models of the Filipino value system. The first is the exogenous model or the foreign model, while the second is the indigenous model or the traditional model. The foreign model is described to be a "legal and formal" model. The indigenous model is described as a "traditional and non-formal" model or guide but is deeply embedded in the subconscious of the Filipinos.[3]

3.Elements and composition of Filipino values Edit

Based on studies, surveys, opinions, anecdotes, and other literatures made by experts and researchers in relation to Filipino social values or Filipino core values, along with the Filipino character or Filipino identity of a person or an individual known as the Filipino, the Filipino value system are found to possess inherent key elements.

4.Enumeration of Filipino values Edit

Human activities Edit

Family orientation Edit

The basic and most important unit of a Filipino's life is the family. Unlike in Western countries, young Filipinos who turn 18 are not expected to move out of their parents' home. When a Filipino's parents are old and cannot take care of themselves, they are cared for in their children's homes and are very rarely brought by their children to homes for the aged. The practice of separating the elderly from the rest of the family, while common in Western countries, is often looked down upon in Filipino society. Family lunches with the extended family of up to 50 people, extending until the line of second cousins, are not unusual. The Filipino culture puts a great emphasis on the value of family and being close to one's family members.

5.Gender-specific values

In relation to parenthood, bearing male and female children depends on the preferences of the parents based on the expected roles that each gender would assume once grown up. Both genders are expected to become responsible members of the family and their society. Women in the Philippines are expected to become caring and nurturing mothers for their own children.[12]

10. do you practice " utang na loob"?is it a positive or negative Filipino value?why?​


Answer:

The essence of utang na loob is an obligation to appropriately repay a person who has done one a favor. The favors which elicit the Filipino's sense of utang na loob are typically those whose value is impossible to quantify, or, if there is a quantifiable value involved, involves a deeply personal internal dimension.[3] This internal dimension, loob, differentiates utang na loob from an ordinary utang (debt); being an internal phenomenon, utang na loob thus goes much deeper than ordinary debt or even the western concept of owing a favor. Filipino psychology explains that this is a reflection of the kapwa orientation of shared personhood or shared self, which is at the core of the Filipino values system.[1]

13. What are the positive and negative implications of the value orientation of Filipinos on national development goals?​


Answer:

The value orientation of Filipinos has both positive and negative implications on national development goals. On the one hand, Filipino values such as family orientation, respect for elders, and close community ties can help promote harmonious social relations and a sense of responsibility among citizens. This can in turn lead to more effective and efficient governance and better implementation of development programs. On the other hand, Filipino values such as patriarchy, social stratification, and fatalism can reinforce existing inequalities and power imbalances in society. This can hinder progress towards national development goals by preventing marginalized groups from accessing opportunities and resources.

18. What are the Positive and negative Filipino values and its advantage and disadvantage to our daily lives?​


Positive Filipino values include respect for authority, strong family ties, and a strong work ethic. Negative Filipino values include discrimination and a tendency toward corruption.

The advantages of these positive values include strong social bonds and a sense of community, which can provide support and help people to achieve their goals. A strong work ethic can also lead to personal and professional success.

The disadvantages of negative values such as discrimination and corruption can include a lack of equal opportunities for all members of society, and a general lack of trust in institutions and authority figures. This can lead to social and economic problems.

Overall, it is important for individuals and society as a whole to strive to uphold positive values and strive to overcome negative ones in order to create a more harmonious and prosperous society.

Kanya-kanya“(to each his own)

This is the Filipino self-centeredness and lack of regard for others. This happens when most people are similarly situated such us in a calamity and in instances where family is involved as “blood is thicker than water”.

In instances however when the others sis not meet the same fate, the Filipino helpfulness comes out. If one is able to help another without danger to himself and his family, they will readily extend a helping hand.

“Balimbing”

A reference to the many-sided star fruit, the term is applied to someone who changes allegiance for personal convenience. The allusion is that the person is not only double-faced, but multiple-faced. This was evident in the Marcos downfall where his cronies and enemies turned against him and embraced the Aquino government. These people are said to be “balimbing”.


29. Give the positive and negative effect of this value of the filipinos​


Answer:

Two positive Filipino values are "pakikisama," which means "group loyalty" and "constantly striving for peace," and "kasi," which means "seeing all sides of a situation to know exactly where failure occurred." Two negative Filipino values are "hiya," which means that you do not try to achieve so you can enjoy peace of mind, and "ningas-cogon," which means that you should procrastinate and be indifferent to promote peace and tranquility.

Filipino standards emphasize smallness and mediocrity. Filipino culture frowns upon grandeur. When it comes to success, Filipinos believe it should come naturally and that one should not exert effort to achieve it. Success should not happen early in life because it signifies hard work. Success should occur very late in life, if at all. Filipinos have high regard for their family and relatives. "Sakop" means that you stand and fall with your family. "Kanya-kanya" means "to take care of your family according to the saying "blood is thicker than water." The popular belief is "saving face," which means that instead of taking responsibility for something offensive you have done, you should save yourself from embarrassment by making a graceful exit. They believe in a superior being, according to Bahala Na, which means "do not rely on your own powers but rely the powers of a superior being." This belief encourages lack of arrogance, modesty and humility. They also believe in Patigasan, which signifies strength. They stand up for their rights and are not forced into submission. Filipinos have a great sense of gratitude and believe in meekness. Their values stem from the afterlife where the weak will be strong, the last will become the first, and the small will become great
