18. Jacob lost his job due to the pandemic. To continue supporting his family, he opened a small online t-shirt printing shop. The production cost for the adult shirts includes Php1000 for design and labor plus Php120 per shirt while for the kid shirts includes Php850 for design and labor plus Php100 per shirt. he sells adult shirts at Php210 each and kid shirts at Php180 each.


Answer:

To compute for the profit of Jacob's small online t-shirt printing shop, we need to compute for the total production cost and the total revenue first.

For adult shirts:

Production cost per shirt = Php1000 + Php120 = Php1120

Selling price per shirt = Php210

Profit per shirt = Selling price - Production cost = Php210 - Php1120 = Php90

Total revenue from selling adult shirts = Number of adult shirts sold x Selling price per shirt

For kid shirts:

Production cost per shirt = Php850 + Php100 = Php950

Selling price per shirt = Php180

Profit per shirt = Selling price - Production cost = Php180 - Php950 = Php70

Total revenue from selling kid shirts = Number of kid shirts sold x Selling price per shirt

To compute for the total profit, we need to subtract the total production cost from the total revenue:

Total profit = Total revenue - Total production cost

Let's say Jacob sold 50 adult shirts and 100 kid shirts. Here's how we can compute for his profit:

For adult shirts:

Total revenue from selling adult shirts = 50 x Php210 = Php10,500

Total production cost for adult shirts = 50 x Php1120 = Php56,000

For kid shirts:

Total revenue from selling kid shirts = 100 x Php180 = Php18,000

Total production cost for kid shirts = 100 x Php950 = Php95,000

Total revenue = Php10,500 + Php18,000 = Php28,500

Total production cost = Php56,000 + Php95,000 = Php151,000

Total profit = Php28,500 - Php151,000 = -Php122,500

Based on the computation, Jacob's small online t-shirt printing shop is incurring losses. He may need to review his pricing strategy, production costs, or marketing tactics to improve his sales and profitability.
