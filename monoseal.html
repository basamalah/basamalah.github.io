28. what do we call the process when a monomer bond with another monomer?​


The process when a monomer bond with another monomer is called Polymerization.

Definition of Polymerization

Polymerization is the process of reacting monomer molecules together in a chemical reaction to form three-dimensional networks or polymer chains.

Polymerization is classified into several systems: addition-condensation system and stepwise chain growth systems. Another form of polymerization is ring-opening polymerization which is similar to chain polymerization. Natural polymers include proteins such as silk, enzymes and muscle fibers. Polymers are also known as macromolecules. Addition polymers for example: polyethylene, teflon, PVC, PVA, and PMMA. Condensation polymers for example: nylon, kevlar, silicon rubber, and polyester.

Polymerization Type

Polymerization is divided into addition polymerization and condensation polymerization. Addition polymerization is a polymerization involving double bond monomers. The monomers will bond to each other and form repeat units by breaking the double bonds.

Examples of polymers formed by addition polymerization are polyethylene (CH2=CH2 –> [CH2-CH2]n) and teflon (CF2=CF2 –> [CF2-CF2]n).

Meanwhile, condensation polymerization is the formation of polymers from monomers that bind by releasing another form of molecule such as H2O, NH3, or HCl. An example of a polymer formed by condensation polymerization is polyethylene-glycol (HOCH2CH2OH –> [OCH2CH2]n + nH2O).
