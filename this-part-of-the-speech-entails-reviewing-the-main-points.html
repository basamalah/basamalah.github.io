My Answer:

Humans did not evolve from monkeys. Humans are more closely related to modern apes than to monkeys, but we didn't evolve from apes, either. Humans share a common ancestor with modern African apes, like gorillas and chimpanzees. Scientists believe this common ancestor existed
5 to 8 million years ago. Shortly thereafter, the species diverged into two separate lineages. One of these lineages ultimately evolved into gorillas and chimps, and the other evolved into early human ancestors called hominids.

Since the earliest hominid species diverged from the ancestor we share with modern African apes, 5 to 8 million years ago, there have been at least a dozen different species of these humanlike creatures. Many of these hominid species are close relatives, but not human ancestors. Most went extinct without giving rise to other species. Some of the extinct hominids known today, however, are almost certainly direct ancestors of Homo sapiens. While the total number of species that existed and the relationships among them is still unknown, the picture becomes clearer as new fossils are found.

Humans and monkeys are both primates. But humans are not descended from monkeys or any other primate living today. Humans did not evolve from chimpanzees or any of the other great apes that live today. We instead share a common ancestor that lived roughly 10 million years ago. They're on an entirely different evolutionary path.

13. What does reading with a point of view entail? Do you read with a point of view or just to acquire point of view? How do you know that you are engaged in one and not the other?​


Answer:

Reading with a point of view entails approaching a text with a particular perspective, opinion, or attitude that influences how you interpret and understand the information presented. This means that you are not reading neutrally, but rather with a preconceived idea or bias that shapes your understanding of the text.

To read with a point of view, you need to have a clear understanding of your perspective and be able to identify how it influences your reading. You may read with a point of view to gain support for your existing beliefs or to challenge them and consider alternative viewpoints.

Whether you are reading with a point of view or just to acquire a point of view depends on your intentions and approach. If you are reading with the sole purpose of acquiring new information and perspectives without any preconceived ideas, then you are likely reading neutrally. However, if you approach a text with a specific perspective and are looking for evidence to support or challenge your ideas, then you are reading with a point of view.

To know if you are engaged in one or the other, you can reflect on your reading experience and consider your mindset and attitude towards the text. Are you actively looking for evidence to support your existing beliefs or are you open to new ideas and perspectives? Are you critically analyzing the information presented or accepting it without question? These are some questions that can help you identify if you are reading with a point of view or neutrally.

In reality, it is natural for individuals to approach reading with a point of view, as we all have unique perspectives and biases that influence how we interpret information. However, it is important to be aware of our biases and actively work to challenge them to ensure that we are critically analyzing the information presented and not just accepting it without question.

Explanation:

Or in simpler terms

Reading with a point of view means looking at a text with a particular opinion or attitude that affects how you understand it. It's like putting on a pair of glasses that changes the way you see things.

When you read with a point of view, you might be trying to find evidence to support what you already think, or you might be looking to challenge your beliefs and consider new ideas.

Sometimes people read to learn about different points of view and don't have a preconceived idea. Other times, people read with a particular opinion already formed.

To know if you are reading with a point of view or not, think about whether you are actively looking for evidence to support what you already think or if you are open to new ideas. Are you carefully thinking about what the author is saying, or are you just accepting it without questioning it?

It's normal to read with a point of view, but it's important to be aware of your biases so that you can make sure you are thinking critically and considering all perspectives.
