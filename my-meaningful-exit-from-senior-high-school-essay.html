Senior year was my favorite year above all – I experienced how great prom is, even get the chance to dance with my friends. Also, that was the first time that my parents allowed me to go out with my friends and had fun with the after party.

It helped me more to know what I want to be in life and to know my purpose. Our school even provided us an event and invited different successful people to share their knowledge and give us tips on how to become successful in life. Junior High School and Senior High School are two distinct educational levels in the Philippine education system. Both have their unique features and offer different opportunities for students. In this essay, we will discuss the comparison and contrast of Junior High School and Senior High School.

Junior High School or JHS is composed of grades 7 to 10, while Senior High School or SHS is composed of grades 11 to 12. The main goal of JHS is to provide a basic education foundation for students, while SHS aims to equip students with the necessary skills and knowledge for their chosen career paths. One of the significant differences between JHS and SHS is the curriculum. JHS follows the K-12 curriculum, which includes general education subjects such as Math, Science, English, and Social Studies. Meanwhile, SHS offers specialized tracks such as Academic, Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL), Sports, and Arts and Design. The tracks provide students with a more focused education that will help them prepare for their future careers.

Another difference between JHS and SHS is the length of time spent in school. JHS has a total of four years, while SHS only has two years. In JHS, students spend more time in general education subjects. In contrast, in SHS, students spend more time in specialized subjects and practical training. The reduced time in SHS allows students to graduate early and pursue higher education or enter the workforce earlier than JHS students.

Moreover, JHS students are required to take the National Achievement Test (NAT) to measure their academic proficiency, while SHS students are required to take the Philippine Educational Placement Test (PEPT) and Career Assessment Examination (CAE). These tests are essential for students to identify their strengths and weaknesses and choose the appropriate career track that suits their interests.

Furthermore, the teaching methods used in JHS and SHS are different. In JHS, the teaching approach is more theoretical, while in SHS, it is more practical. SHS students undergo On-The-Job-Training (OJT) or apprenticeships, giving them hands-on experience in their chosen fields.

In conclusion, JHS and SHS have significant differences in their curriculum, length of time spent in school, assessments, teaching methods, and overall goals. JHS aims to provide students with a general education foundation, while SHS focuses on providing specialized education to prepare students for their future careers. Regardless of these differences, both JHS and SHS play essential roles in the Philippine education system, providing students with a solid foundation for their future.
