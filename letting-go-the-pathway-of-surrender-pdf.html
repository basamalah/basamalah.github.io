25. 3. From the main entrance of a park, there are two pathways where visitors can walk along going to the circular garden. The pathways are both tangent to the garden whose center is 40m away from the main entrance. If from the center of the garden to endpoint/s of the pathway is 28m, how long is each pathway?


Answer:

[tex]4\sqrt{51}[/tex] m.

Step-by-step explanation:

I tried to draw your problem and I came up with the image attached.

Point E is the entrance to the park and BE and AE are the paths to the circular garden whose center is point C. BE is tangent to circle C at point B, and AE is tangent to the circle at point A. CE = 40m (given).

AC and BC are radii of the circle since both are lines from the center of the circle to the point on the circle. AC and BC are both 28m (given). We are looking for AE and BE.

The tangent of a circle is perpendicular to the radius of the circle at the point of tangency. On the figure, you can say that BE ⊥ BC and AC ⊥ AE. That means that ΔCBE and ΔCAE are right triangles with ∠CBE and ∠CAE being the right angles.

On ΔCBE, ∠CBE is the right angle. That means that BE and BC are the legs, while CE is the hypotenuse. We know that BC is 28, and CE is 40. We can solve for BE via the Pythagorean theorem.

[tex]BC^{2} +BE^{2} =CE^{2} \\28^{2} + BE^{2} =40^{2} \\\\784+BE^{2} = 1600\\BE^{2} =1600-784\\BE^{2} = 816\\BE = \sqrt{816} \\BE = \sqrt{16*51}\\BE = 4\sqrt{51}[/tex]

On ΔCAE, ∠CAE is the right angle. That means that AE and AC are the legs, while CE is the hypotenuse. We know that AC is 28, and CE is 40. We can solve for AE via the Pythagorean theorem.

[tex]AC^{2} +AE^{2} =CE^{2} \\28^{2} + AE^{2} =40^{2} \\\\784+AE^{2} = 1600\\AE^{2} =1600-784\\AE^{2} = 816\\AE = \sqrt{816} \\AE = \sqrt{16*51}\\AE = 4\sqrt{51}[/tex]

Both AE and BE are equal to [tex]4\sqrt{51}[/tex]  m.
