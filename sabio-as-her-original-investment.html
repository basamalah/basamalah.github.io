11. When invested at an annual interest rate of 5%, the amount invested earned ₱20,500 simple interest in two years. How much money was originally invested


Problem:

When invested at an annual interest rate of 5%, the amount invested earned ₱20,500 simple interest in two years. How much money was originally invested?

Given:

Interest earned (I) = 20,500

Simple Interest rate (R) = 5% or 0.05

Time (T) = 2 years

Principal (P) or amount invested = unknown

Solution:

Solve for P using the formula, the amount invested:

L = PRT20,500 = P(0.05)(2)20,500 = P(0.1)20,500 = 0.1P0.1P/0.1 = 20,500/0.1P = 205,000

Answer:

∴ Therefore, the amount originally invested was 205,000.

17. dodong made an investment of 8% compounded annualy. it grows to 702,000.00 at the end of the one year. hom much was his original investment?


Answer is 650 000.


We're given the Future Value (A) = 702 000, the rate (r) = 0.08, the time (t) = 1 year, and the compounding period (n) = 1 (because it compounds "annually" or "once every year").  We're looking for the Principal Amount (or Present Value, P).

Since this is a Compound Interest case, we can use the Future Value formula,

A = P(1 +r/n)^(nt)

Inputting the known values, we'll have

702 000 = P(1 + 0.08/1)^(1*1)

We can simplify some of the parts and we end up with

702 000 = P(1.08)

To get just "P", we can divide both sides of the equation by "1.08", and we'll have

702 000/1.08 = P

After performing the division, we'll get 650 000 = P.
