6. in a paragraph form, differentiate BIAS and PREJUDICE​


Answer &

Explanation:

BIAS

Bias is a natural inclination for or against an idea, object, group or individual. It is often learned and is highly dependent on variables like a person's socioeconomic status, race, ethnicity, educational background, etc. At the individual level, bias can negatively impact someone's personal and professional relationships; at a societal level, it can lead to unfair persecution of a group.

PREJUDICE

Prejudice refers to a preconceived judgment, opinion or attitude directed toward certain people based on their membership in a particular group. It is a set of attitudes, which supports, causes, or justifies discrimination. It also means to pre-judge others.

An important skill of critical reading is the ability to detect an author's Bias and Prejudice. The reason you need to be able to do this, of course, is that bias and prejudice may invalidate an author's claim.
