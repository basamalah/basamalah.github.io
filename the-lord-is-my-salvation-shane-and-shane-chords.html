23. Shane is 6 years older thzn her brother she will be twice as old as her brother in 4 years how old is shane. now?


First off, proper punctuation could have been used so the problem could make more sense. A suggested and corrected problem could read:

Shane is 6 years older than her brother. She will be twice as old as her brother in 4 years. How old is Shane now?

Notice that the problem is describing Shane's and her brother's ages at present and in the future. The first sentence describes their present ages (notice the use of the verb is) and the second sentence describes their ages in four years (notice the use of the verb will).

Now if Shane is 6 years older that her brother, we can represent her brother's age as x and Shane's as x+6.

If those are the representations of their ages at present, then we only need to add 4 to each of their ages to represent how old they will be in 4 years. So her brother would x+4 and Shane would be x+10 by then.

 Now, we can create an equation using the description of the ages the second sentence: She will be twice as old as her brother in 4 years.

[tex] x+10=2(x+4)[/tex]
[tex] x+10=2x+8[/tex]
[tex]10-8=2x-x[/tex]
[tex]x=2[/tex]

Going back to how we represented their ages, Shane is [tex]x+6=2+6=8[/tex] years old and her brother is 2 years old.
