12. The municipal government of Rodriguez granted 50 households a livelihood project, 18 took Kabute-culture project, 26 in candle making, and 2 take both. How many households did not pursue with any of the 2 projects? ​


Answer:

8 households did not pursue any of the two projects.

Step-by-step explanation:

TOTALhouseholds = 50

TOTALkabute = 18

TOTALcandle = 26

Both = 2

Kabute-only = 18 - 2 = 16

Candle-only = 26 - 2 = 24

2 + 16 + 24 = 42 households took projects

50 - 42 = 8

15. Discuss some livelihood projects for victim-survivors​


Discuss some livelihood projects for victim-survivors

WOMEN'S PROTECTION AND LIVELIHOODS PROGRAM  As a result of the continuing conflict in CAR, which started in December 2012 and continues today1, there are reportedly over 107,000 displaced people from CAR in Southern Chad, with more than half (54%) of them being women. Women and girls' vulnerability to gender-based violence (GBV) is substantially increased during humanitarian crises2, and this is frequently exacerbated during displacement. The IRC's Women's Protection and Livelihoods Program in Southern Chad led to GBV response, safely restoring livelihoods, and preventing and reducing the risk of GBV, including sexual harassment of women and girls, or spending time in areas where women and girls are more vulnerable to seek provisions, which could expose women and girls to additional risks of violence. The Women's Protection and Livelihoods Program integrated the provision of supportive resources for GBV survivors, such as case management, psychosocial counseling and health treatment, community understanding of GBV, and cash transfers and income-generating activities, with cash transfers and income-generating activities. Between September 2014 and September 2016, the curriculum was implemented in two locations in Southern Chad: Maingama and Kobiteye. The program specifically worked to: Train volunteers for community outreach; Raise collective awareness of gender-based violence and programs available; Conduct protection checks at two different locations; Open two women's centers for events and supportive environments, including: o recreational and community psychosocial activities for 3,071 women and girls; o case management and psychosocial counseling for 542 survivors of GBV at women's centers.  4 327 women and girls would receive respect packages. Equip and educate 34 support providers, including medical personnel (clinical treatment for perpetrators of sexual violence), officers, and religious leaders. Form collaborations with other agencies to offer integrated health and GBV programs. Engage in gender dialogue groups of 256 couples; Assist 250 poor women with their livelihoods:

Unconditional cash allocations in the first year of the programme to supplement World Food Program food rations; In the second year of the programme, skills training on income-generating activities (IGAs) and cash transfers will be provided to help execute a business strategy. The disadvantage status of the 250 women chosen for subsistence assistance was determined by community members who were given instructions about how to choose beneficiaries.

Beneficiaries had to fulfill at least one of the following five criteria:

A GBV survivor with little access to specific food products Female head of household with four or more under-five-year-old children Older women who have limited access to basic food products o Widows with under-five-year-old twins.
