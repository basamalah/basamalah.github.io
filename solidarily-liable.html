13. The crust of the earth is brittle and is liable to breaking true or false ?


Earth's crust is the thin outermost layer of the Earth. It ranges from about 10 to 65 km in thickness worldwide. The uppermost 15-35 km of crust is brittle enough to produce earthquakes.

The crust of the Earth is broken into many pieces called plates. The plates "float" on the soft, plastic mantle which is located below the crust. These plates usually move along smoothly but sometimes they stick and build up pressure. The pressure builds and the rock bends until it snaps. This will result to an earthquake.

The crust is composed of granite and basalt.Crust is divided into two:oceanic crust and continental crust.
