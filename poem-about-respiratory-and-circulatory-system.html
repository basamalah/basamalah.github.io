Of the Heart and Lungs

Our Respiratory System serves as the one who cleans and distributes oxygen all throughout our body. It enables for a healthy blood flow and our capabilities to absorb other substances that will be beneficial to our proper function as a human. The Respiratory consists of:

Lungs- the one that contracts in order to help circulate the air and cleanse it from impurities Pharynx and Larynx- in charge of the regulation of objects of any other substance or air to go inside the lungs, also affiliated to our voice box. Nasal Cavity- serves as the passageway of the air we breathe and to the lungs Trachea- passageway for the air to come and go form the lungs or to the lungs

Our Circulatory System is the one who enables blood flow and to give the necessary substance requirements from every organ. The blood cells carry the important substance that we humans need it involves:

Nutrients Oxygen Waste

Other than the beneficial things our blood carry it can also transfer waste, but only bound to the physicality of the blood cells. The Circulatory System consist of the:

Heart- the one that pumps out blood in order to have a blood flow Blood- the one that carries the substances that will be distributed to every parts of our body Veins/ Capillaries- the path ways of the blood all over the body

There are two types of Veins:

Systemic Veins- delivers blood towards the lungs to be oxygenated Pulmonary Veins- delivers the oxygenated blood to the heart and then it pumps it out

These two systems in our body serve more on the supply and maintenance of our most intake of the environment. Unlike other systems, it is mostly focused on the distribution of the necessary factors that enable our other organs to function and to be healthy. The Respiratory System is for the air we intake, and the Circulatory System is for our blood.
