5. it is true? "anthro-"means human and "ethno-"means culture​


Answer:

Yes, it is correct.

Explanation:

Anthro o Anthropology is from the New Latin word anthropologia (“the study of humanity”) and shares its ultimate root in Greek, anthrōpos (“human being”), with a number of other words in English, such as anthropomorphize, philanthropy, and misanthrope.

Ethno is a combining form meaning ”race,” “people,” or “culture,” used in the formation of compound words: examples - ethnography; ethnogenic.

The difference between Anthro and Furry

When used as nouns, anthro means anthropology, whereas furry means an animal character with human characteristics. When used as adjectives, anthro means anthropomorphic, whereas furry means covered with fur, or with something resembling fur. ... An anthropomorphic character
