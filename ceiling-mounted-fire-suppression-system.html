For whatever it may be worth, my opinion is that all modern so-called 'democratic' countries suppress democracy. What that means is that the people do not govern themselves, as is implied by the word democracy, but are ruled by small groups of unelected individuals who control the political infrastructure.

In place of democracy, power-seeking individuals institute a form of pseudo-democracy where control of the government is vested in organizations called political parties. The parties claim to represent the people, or, more properly, certain subsets of the people, but, instead, they only provide a power-base for their leaders. The party leaders control the government by controlling the officials the people elect to represent their interests.

The parties control the elected officials by arrogating to themselves the right to choose the candidates for public office and providing the finances and infrastructure necessary for the candidates' election. Since the elected officials are indebted to the parties for their selection and election, the parties are able to control the government. One of the subtle beauties of the system (from the party leaders' perspective) is that it allows the political leaders to create the myth that the people govern themselves because they are allowed to choose between the offerings of the competing parties.

The parties lure the money they need for the power they crave by selling the nation's laws to vested interests - which leads to the quip that "We have the best laws money can buy." In this sense, it is fair to say that suppressing democracy means the rise of corrupt government.
