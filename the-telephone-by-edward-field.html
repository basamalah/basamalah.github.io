11. Reaction paper about the telephone poem by Edward Field ​


Answer:

"Telephone" by Edward Field is a thought-provoking and insightful poem that touches on the theme of communication and how it can both bring people together and push them apart. As I read through the poem, I found myself reflecting on my own experiences with technology and how it has impacted my relationships with others.

The poem starts with an image of two people using a telephone to connect with each other, despite being separated by a great distance. It highlights the power of technology to bridge gaps and bring people together in ways that were once impossible. However, as the poem progresses, it becomes clear that this connection is not always positive. The speaker notes that the telephone is a "magic wand" that can be used for both good and bad. In some cases, it can lead to a breakdown in communication, causing misunderstandings and hurt feelings.

What struck me most about this poem is its relevance to our modern-day lives. With the rise of social media and instant messaging, we have more ways than ever to connect with others, but at the same time, we are becoming increasingly isolated. We can easily misinterpret messages or say things we don't mean, leading to conflicts and broken relationships. As the speaker in the poem notes, the telephone can be a "tool of insult" that can do more harm than good.

In terms of style, Field's use of metaphor and personification helps to bring the telephone to life, turning it into a character with its own motivations and desires. The repetition of the phrase "I am the telephone" adds to the sense of personification and creates a haunting, almost menacing tone.

Overall, "Telephone" is a powerful poem that encourages us to think more deeply about the role of communication in our lives. It reminds us that while technology can be a powerful tool for connection, it is not without its pitfalls. As we navigate our increasingly digital world, it is important to remember the value of face-to-face communication and to be mindful of the impact our words can have on others.

19. what literary device is used in the poem the telephone by edward field


The literary device used in the poem Telephone by Edward Field is simile.

In the lines,

Without it I was like a bear in a cave
Drowsing through a shadowy winter
It rings and springs has come
I stretch and amble out into the sunshine
Hungry again as I pick up the receiver

The absence of the telephone was compared to the life of a bear during winter.
