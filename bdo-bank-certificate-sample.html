6. Which are the best examples to include in a career portfolio? photograph, a current resume, and a birth certificate ethnic identity, professional recommendations, and a cover letter birth certificate, current work samples, and a photograph work samples, professional recommendations, and a resume


A career portfolio should have a good sample of the person's work so that any potential employer could make the right decision when thinking of hiring. In the Philippines, what is usually needed is a resume, sample works (of course the best ones), photographs of work samples, professional recommendations and an application cover letter. There is no need for an ethnic card since we do not have that anyway, unless part of the IP in the Philippines. The same goes for a birth certificate or an ID  photo. These could be asked for a part of the employee record but should not as part of the work portfolio.

When assembling a career portfolio make sure you include the best work you have ever done. If the work could have a short description, better. Also, make sure you have professional recommendations or testimonials from previous clients. Also make sure that your Facebook profile looks clean and professional. Do not use dummy accounts or accounts with inverted names (like NauJ Aled Zurc).

7. how certificate of assignment works in banking?


Answer:

Certificate of assignment is a legal document that a bank may use to transfer ownership of a loan obligation from one person or entity to another. The certificate of assignment provides evidence that the bank has assigned and transferred its rights to receive payment on the loan to the new owner. The new owner can then take legal action to enforce the loan, if necessary, and collect any outstanding payments. The certificate of assignment is typically recorded with the relevant government agency to ensure that the new owner's rights are properly documented and protected.

18. GENERAL ANNUITY (FINDING THE FUTURE VALUE) BDO bank pays interest at the rate of 3% compounded quarterly. How much will have in the bank at the end of 6 years if Ms. Rona deposits P5,000 every month?​


Answer:

To find the future value of an annuity, you can use the formula:

future value = payment * (((1 + interest rate/n)^n*t - 1) / (interest rate/n))

Where:

payment is the amount deposited each period (in this case, P5,000 per month)

interest rate is the rate at which the annuity earns interest (in this case, 3% per year)

n is the number of times the interest is compounded per year (in this case, quarterly, so n = 4)

t is the number of years the annuity is invested (in this case, 6 years)

Plugging in the values given in the problem, we get:

future value = 5000 * (((1 + 0.03/4)^(4*6) - 1) / (0.03/4))

= 5000 * (((1.0075^24) - 1) / 0.0075)

= 5000 * ((1.212161552 - 1) / 0.0075)

= 5000 * (0.212161552 / 0.0075)

= 5000 * 28.2948872

= P141,474.44

So at the end of 6 years, Ms. Rona will have P141,474.44 in the bank if she deposits P5,000 every month and the bank pays interest at a rate of 3% compounded quarterly.
