2. explanation Judging others for theirfallures: the tendency toassume that the behavior ofothers is driven by internalfactors(such as laziness ortack of skill).​


Answer:

The explanation provided is not a complete sentence, but it describes a psychological phenomenon known as the Fundamental Attribution Error. This error occurs when individuals tend to overemphasize dispositional explanations (internal factors) for the behavior of others while ignoring situational explanations (external factors). This means that people often attribute other people's actions to their character or personality traits rather than considering external circumstances that may have contributed to their behavior. For example, if someone is consistently late for work, a colleague may assume that they are lazy or lack punctuality, without considering that they may be dealing with unforeseen circumstances such as transportation issues or family problems.
