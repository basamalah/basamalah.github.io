13. Which food chain is in the correct order? grasshopper → grass → snake → frog → hawk grasshopper → frog → hawk → snake → grass grass → grasshopper → frog → snake → hawk grass → grasshopper → snake → frog → hawk


The food chain that is in the correct order is: grass → grasshopper → frog → snake → hawk

Remember that no food chain can start from a consumer since there will be no source of energy for the primary consumers. In this case, the producer is the grass, it produces its own food via photosynthesis; the grasshopper is the primary consumer, it consumes the producer; the secondary consumer is the frog which in turn eats the primary consumer; and finally the tertiary consumer is the hawk, which eats the secondary consumer.

Do note though that energy received per level in the food chain and energy pyramid is decreasing as you ascend. This is because some of the energy is lost by the organisms to the environment as heat while some is used by the organism.

Some snakes are eaten especially those snakes that stand out. They move into a new area where the rocks are very dark, but still, there are snakes that have been eaten by the hawk until there is only one variation of snakes left. The black snake have a higher chance of living because of their adaptability to the environment. What Theory explains this?


Answer:

The theory that explains the scenario described is natural selection, which is a key concept in the theory of evolution. Natural selection is the process by which certain characteristics or traits that are advantageous to an organism's survival and reproduction become more common in a population over time, while traits that are disadvantageous become less common.

Explanation:

In this case, the black snake has a higher chance of survival because its dark coloration allows it to blend in with the dark rocks in the new area, making it less visible to the hawk predator. The white snake, on the other hand, stands out against the dark background, making it an easier target for the hawk. As a result, the white snake population has been reduced by the predator, while the black snake population has survived and adapted to the new environment.

This scenario illustrates how natural selection operates in response to changes in the environment and the presence of selective pressures such as predation. The black snake's adaptation to the new environment is an example of how natural selection can lead to the evolution of new traits and characteristics in a population over time.
