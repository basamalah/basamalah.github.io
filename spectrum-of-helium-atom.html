6. A mixture of helium and neon gases is held in a container at 2.4 atmospheres. If the mixture contains twice as many helium atoms as neon atoms, what is the partial pressure of helium?


Answer:

4.8 atm

Explanation:

The partial pressure of one individual gas within the overall mixtures according to Dalton's Law of Partial Pressure is described as  :

Pi=Ptotal xi

PHe = Ptotal xHe

According to the problem, the mixture contains twice as many Helium atoms as Neon atoms, so x=2

*atm= atmosphere

Given:

Ptotal =2.4 atm

xHe= 2

Solution:

PHe = PtotalxHe

PHe =(2.4)*2

PHe = 4.8 atm

The molar mass of Helium is 4 g/mol. This means that in every 1 mole of helium, it has a mass of 4 grams.

Conversion:

1 mole = 4 grams Helium atom

Given:

0.55 grams of Helium

Solution:

0.55 grams of He × 1 mole / 4 grams = 0.14 moles of Helium atom
