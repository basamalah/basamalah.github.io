19. what causes poverty and high-rate of jobless individual in the Philippines?​


1. Education status and attainments:

If an individual is not holding a college degree, the chances are they will resort to low-paying jobs or even not be accepted at all. Educational attainments are also not an assurance of a job because in the industry lots of talented people are coming each year. Some of them could surpass the skills of an individual, hence, when this happens, the other loses the job. This lead to the individual trying out new companies. However, time and money will be spent on that journey and if the individual cannot sustain this, he will fall below the poverty line.

2. Health status and complications:

An individual can become poor if his health is not in good condition or environment. Medical bills, medicine, and other healthcare needs may be costly over time. For jobs, a person will be rejected if they fail the health test or have other issues.

3. Connections and biases:

People with connections can escape being poor while people who took up the effort may become poorer. Using connections, they are one step ahead of the other, they have the benefits of being called up for a job, and they can use this connection to expand more, which increases their chances. Some jobs are given because of biases, which increases the unequal opportunities to other people.
