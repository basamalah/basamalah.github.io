29. Complete the concept map below. Write descriptions or related concepts around the main concept politics. Write your answer on a separate sheet of paper.PAHELP PO​


Politics is everything about the process of formulating and implementing government public policies.

Political relations with several other aspects:

Social

Political science is closely related to these social sciences whose objects are humans as members of groups. These sciences study human behavior and the ways in which humans live and work together.

The concepts discussed in political theory include, among others, society, social class, the state, power, sovereignty, rights and obligations, independence, state institutions, change in social development, political development, modernization, and so on.

Ideology

Ideology is a set of goals and ideas that lead to a goal, hope, and action. So, political ideology can be interpreted as a set of goals and ideas that explain how people work, and how to organize power. Several types of ideology in politics are liberalism, capitalism, and socialism.

Culture

Politics and culture become a kind of two sides of a coin that cannot be separated because the two aspects are always related and there seems to be a process of mutualism where a mutually beneficial phenomenon arises between the two parties when there is an interactive process.

It's not infrequency aspects or behaviors in culture are contradictory to the development of political behavior so they are not useful or even give each other negative effects.

Economy

Economics is related to the available resources in a country/region. By using the existing resources in a country, the leader who has the power has the right to determine what types of goods and services are produced.

Furthermore, power holders can also determine where and when goods and services are produced and distributed. The determination of imports of strategic commodities is also determined by the power holders. Based on the description, it can be seen that political power affects how the economic system in a country works.

For example, liberal, guided, or mixed economies. If the economy is liberal, the allocation of resources will be determined by market forces. On the other hand, in a guided economy system, the allocation of resources is determined by the government.
