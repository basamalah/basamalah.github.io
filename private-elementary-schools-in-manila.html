6. If a random sample of 48 private elementary schools is selected,what is the probability that the number of students enrolled is between 545 and 585?​


Answer:

Without knowing the distribution of the number of students enrolled in private elementary schools, it is impossible to determine the exact probability of the number of students being between 545 and 585. However, assuming that the distribution is approximately normal with a mean of μ and a standard deviation of σ, we can use the standard normal distribution to estimate the probability.

Let's assume that the mean number of students enrolled in private elementary schools is μ = 565 and the standard deviation is σ = 30 (these are just hypothetical values for the purpose of this example).

To calculate the z-scores for the lower and upper limits, we use the formula:

z = (x - μ) / σ

For x = 545:

z = (545 - 565) / 30 = -2/3

For x = 585:

z = (585 - 565) / 30 = 2/3

Using a standard normal distribution table or a calculator, we can find the probabilities associated with these z-scores:

P(z < -2/3) = 0.2525

P(z < 2/3) = 0.7475

The probability of the number of students being between 545 and 585 is the difference between these two probabilities:

P(545 ≤ x ≤ 585) = P(z < 2/3) - P(z < -2/3) = 0.7475 - 0.2525 = 0.495

Therefore, if the mean number of students enrolled in private elementary schools is 565 and the standard deviation is 30, the probability of the number of students being between 545 and 585 is approximately 0.495.

Grading in education is the attempt to apply standardized measurements of varying levels of achievement in a course. Grades can be assigned as letters (for example, A through F), as a range (for example, 1 to 6), as a percentage, or as a number out of a possible total (for example, out of 100).[1]

17. PROBLEM: Of an elementary school's 420 graduates, 128 plan to enrol in a private school for Junior High School. What is the ratio of the number of pupils who plan to enrol in a private school to those who do not? a. 128 : 105 b. 32:73 c. 73 : 32 d. 73 : 105 Bone​


Answer:

B. 32: 73

Step-by-step explanation:

Problems involving ratios

In this problem, we are given a total number of graduates of 420, and 128 of them plan to enroll in a private school. We need to calculate the ratio of students who plan to enroll in private school and those who do not.

Step 1: Let N be the number of pupils who will not enroll in private school.

420 = 128 + N

420 - 128 = N

N = 292

Step 2: Put the resulting number into a ratio with those pupils who will enroll in private school.

128:292

We can further simplify the answer by a factor of 4 and express it to its lowest term.

128/4: 292/4

32:73

Therefore, the ratio of the number of pupils who plan to enroll in a private school to those who do not is 32:73.
