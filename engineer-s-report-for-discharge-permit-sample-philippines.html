15. A pipe of diameter 2.4 cm discharges water at a rate of 2.8 m/s. Find the volume of water discharged in half an hour, giving your answer in litres.


Answer:

2280 litres

Step-by-step explanation:

The diameter of the pipe is 2.4cm=0.024m.

Therefore radius of the pipe=0.012m

Therefore surface area of the opening of pipe or crossectional area of the pipe= π(0.012)²

Speed of water discharge=2.8m/s.

Volume of water discharge per second=π(0.012)²×2.8 =0.001266meter cube.

Now half hour=30 minutes=30×60 seconds= 1800 seconds.

Volume of water discharged in 1800 seconds =  0.001266 × 1800  = 2.28 meter cube.

Now we know that 1meter cube = 1000 litres

Therefore 2.28 meter cube = 2.28× 1000litres = 2280 litres.

24. A car manufacturer claims that its latest car model has a combustion engine with 40% efficiency. If the said engine is supplied with 10 000 J of heat in a cycle, how much heat will be discharged by the engine through its exhaust?​


Answer:

Waste thermal energy, that is not converted to kinetic energy in the engine, will be roughly split between exhaust and cooling system (say 3000 j. in the exhaust, in your hypthetical situation). This will vary continuously as engine speed and load vary. - That advertised 40% efficiency represents a ‘peak’ that occurs only under ideal conditions and does not represent the average efficiency in normal driving, which may do well to exceed 10%.
