Answer:

3.How long can a liver store a supply of vitamin B12?

The liver stores vitamins and minerals for the times when they may be lacking in the diet. It can store enough vitamin A and vitamin B12 for four years, and enough vitamin D for four months.

4.How does the liver help clear toxic substances?

The liver turns the toxic ammonia into a substance called urea. The liver releases this into the blood where the kidneys excrete it via the urine. The liver also removes alcohol from the blood, as well as affects many medications a person takes.

5.What is the first sign of having a liver ailment?

If signs and symptoms of liver disease do occur, the may include: Skin and eyes that appear yellowish (jaundice) Abdominal pain and swelling. Swelling in the legs and ankles.

6.How can a liver cells be scarred and e?

Cirrhosis is a late stage of scarring (fibrosis) of the liver caused by many forms of liver diseases and conditions, such as hepatitis and chronic alcoholism. Each time your liver is injured — whether by disease, excessive alcohol consumption or another cause — it tries to repair itself.

Diseases caused by viruses, such as hepatitis A, hepatitis B, and hepatitis C

Diseases caused by drugs, poisons, or too much alcohol. Examples include fatty liver disease and cirrhosis.

Liver cancer

Inherited diseases, such as hemochromatosis and Wilson disease
