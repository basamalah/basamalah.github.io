21. The principal of Magtibay High School wants to estimate the average score of its students in the National Achievement Test with 90% confidence. He is willing to incur an error of only up to ten with the knowledge that the past results yield a standard deviation of 22.5. Find the minimum sample size that he must obtain to construct this confidence interval.


Answer:

To estimate the average score of the students in the National Achievement Test with 90% confidence and an error of only up to 10, we can use the formula for sample size calculation in estimating a population mean.

The formula is:

n = (z^2 * σ^2) / E^2

where:

n = minimum sample size

z = the z-score corresponding to the desired level of confidence (90% confidence corresponds to a z-score of 1.645)

σ = the population standard deviation (22.5)

E = the maximum error margin or tolerance level (10)

Plugging in the given values, we get:

n = (1.645^2 * 22.5^2) / 10^2

n = (2.722 * 506.25) / 100

n = 136.805 or 137 (rounded up)

So the minimum sample size that the principal must obtain to construct this confidence interval is 137.

28. Rodrigo earned a score of 940 on a nationalachievement test. The mean test score was850 with a standard deviation of 100. Whatproportion of students had a higher scorethan Rodrigo? (Assume that test scores arenormally distributed.) If there were 100,000students who took the test, how manywould be expected to have a higher scorethan Rodrigo?​


Answer:

The sample mean is 18.6 and the sample standard deviation is 0.68

Step-by-step explanation:

1. In any math problem, it is very important to identify what the given information are.

Based on what we have:

The population mean (represented by the Greek letter μ) is 18.6.

The  population standard deviation (represented by the Greek letter σ) is 5.9.

The size of the sample/sample size of test taker from Federico Rural High School is 76.

2.Let's answer the question.

a. Sample mean

For the mean of the sample mean of the 76 students, the mean is equal to the population mean.

That is, μ (population mean) = μ (sample mean)

Since μ = 18.6, then μ = 18.6

b. Sample Standard deviation

To solve for the standard deviation (σ), use the formula σ = σ/

σ = 5.9/√76 = 0.68

The sample mean and sample standard deviation of the 76 students who took the National Test from Federico Rural High School are 18.6 and 0.68, respectively.
