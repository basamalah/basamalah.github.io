16. does the noise create so much impact in the conversation of two persons over the phone?​


Answer:

Yes

Explanation:

There various types of noises that may impact conversations may it be on the phone or face-to-face. Claude Shannon and Warren Weaver (1949), designed a model of communication originally for telephone communication. According to Shannon-Weaver's Model, noise is anything that interferes with or distorts the intended meaning of communication. Noise can be physical, psychological, physiological, or semantic.

1. Physical noises are those distractions in the environment. (i.e. seatmates talking, sound of the air conditioner, or the traffic heard from outside the room)

2. Psychological noises are thoughts that run through the communicator's mind during the interaction (i.e. psychological noise can be in form of a student worrying about the exam in the next class)

3. Physiological noises are disturbances within the communicator's body (i.e. the speaker is experiencing headache or is feeling hungry)

4. Semantic noises are differences in meaning or interpretation of words or messages.

Noises create disturbances and interference or distorts the intended meaning of communication. As a result, communicators become distracted or confused. Noise in the communication transaction must be avoided.

reference: Jamandre, N. & Pinzon M. (2017). An Overview of Communication: Models of Communication. POWER SPEAK: Oral Communication In Context, ABIVA Publishing House (2017).
