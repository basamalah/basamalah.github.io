8. Is a medical exam required before beginning a fitness program? In this season of Covid-19 pandemic, will it be fine to do physical fitness activities without having a doctor checking on you?


Answer:

It is generally recommended that individuals consult with a healthcare provider before starting a new fitness program, particularly if they have a pre-existing medical condition or are over the age of 40. A medical exam can help to identify any potential risks or contraindications to physical activity and ensure that an individual is able to safely participate in a fitness program.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, it may be more challenging to schedule a medical exam in person, but it is still important to speak with a healthcare provider before beginning a new fitness program. Depending on your location and the availability of telehealth services, it may be possible to have a virtual consultation with a healthcare provider to discuss your fitness goals and determine whether it is safe for you to start a new program.

It is also important to follow local guidelines and recommendations for physical distancing and other precautions to reduce the risk of COVID-19 transmission while participating in physical activity. This may include wearing a mask, maintaining a safe distance from others, and avoiding activities that involve close contact or shared equipment.

19. Name three requirements for taking the TESDA OQCST exam​


￼TESDA pursues the assessment and certification of the competencies of the middle-level skilled workers through Philippine TVET Competency Assessment and Certification System (PTCACS). The assessment process seeks to determine whether the graduate or worker can perform to the standards expected in the workplace based on the defined competency standards. Certification is provided to those who meets the competency standards. This ensures the productivity, quality and global competitiveness of the middle-level workers. TESDA has a Registry of Certified Workers which provides information on the pool of certified workers for certain occupations nationwide. TESDA also has accredited assessment centers as well as the competency assessors who conduct competency assessment process for persons applying for certification.
