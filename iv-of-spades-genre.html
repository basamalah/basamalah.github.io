6. how to use a spade?​


Answer:

1.Mark your edge with a chalk line or spray paint designed for turf. This makes the edging simpler and gives you a line to follow.

2.Push the edge of your square-edged spade approximately 3 to 4 inches-deep onto the far corner of the line to be edged.

3.Push the spade forward and then pull it backward to create a V-shaped trench. The trench doesn't have to be extremely wide; 2 to 3 inches wide suffices.

4.Dig out the soil from the trench as much as possible.

5.Continue this process while moving down the marked line of the beds. Each time you move the spade, place the corner slightly overlapping the part of the trench you just made.

17. A card is drawn at random from a standard deck of cards. What is the probability of getting a jack or a spade? a. How many jack cards are there in the deck of cards? b. How many spade cards are there in the deck of cards? c. Is there a juck card that is also spade card? If there is, how many cards are jack card that are also spade card? d. What is the probability of drawing a jack card? e. What is the probability of drawing a spade card? f. What is the probability of drawing a jack card that is also a spade card? g. Find the probability of getting a jack or a spade. Let A be the events of getting a jack cad and B the event of getting spade card.​


Answer:

a. There are 4 jack cards in a deck of cards.

b. There are 13 spade cards in a deck of cards.

c. Yes, there is a jack card that is also a spade card. There is only one jack card that is also a spade card, which is the jack of spades.

d. The probability of drawing a jack card is 4/52 or 1/13, since there are four jack cards in a deck of 52 cards.

e. The probability of drawing a spade card is 13/52 or 1/4, since there are 13 spade cards in a deck of 52 cards.

f. The probability of drawing a jack card that is also a spade card is 1/52, since there is only one jack of spades in a deck of 52 cards.

g. To find the probability of getting a jack or a spade, we need to add the probability of getting a jack card to the probability of getting a spade card, and then subtract the probability of getting both a jack and a spade card (since we would be double-counting that scenario).

So, P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)

= 4/52 + 13/52 - 1/52

= 16/52

= 4/13

Therefore, the probability of getting a jack or a spade is 4/13.
