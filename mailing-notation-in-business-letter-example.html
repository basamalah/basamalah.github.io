Used to automatically add mailing address or personalized information to your publication-mail merge

Use Mail Merge to add mailing addresses and other tailored information to your Publisher publications automatically.

Mail merge allows you to create a batch of customized documents for each recipient. A standard letter, for example, might be customized by addressing each recipient by name. The document is linked to a data source, such as a list, spreadsheet, or database. Merge fields are placeholders that tell Word where in the document to include data from the data source.

In Word, you work on the main document, inserting merge fields for the customized content you want to include. When the mail merge is finished, each name in the data source will receive a customized version of the merging document.

19. Martin and Claire ,hospital nurses,are both front liners of theCovid-19 pandemic crisis.Everyday they received letters from people who really appreciate their self-sacrificing spirits of love and dedication for their work.Today they count the number of mailed letters they got. Together they received 50 mailed letters .Claire received 10 more mailed letters than Martin.How many mailed letters did each one get?


Answer:

Claire received 30 while Martin received 20

Step-by-step explanation:

They have 50 letters in total, the given is that Claire has 10 more letters than Martin. To simply solve that we need to subtract 10 from the total number of letters in order to get the letter not including the 10 letters that is surely from Claire.

50-10=40

Now we have confirmed that there are 40 letters which the receiver is unconfirmed. Since we already set aside the 10 more of Claire, this means that the other 40 will be split in half since there are two receivers. So,

40 ÷ 2= 20

This confirms that Martin has 20 letters and the remaining 30 is Claire's.
