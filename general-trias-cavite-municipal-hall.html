1. what is the history of general trias cavite​


Answer:

General Trias is a first-class urban municipality in the province of Cavite, Philippines. It is located in the eastern part of Cavite and is approximately 30 kilometers away from Metro Manila. The town has a rich history, dating back to the Spanish colonial period.

During the Spanish period, the town was known as San Francisco de Malabon. It was a part of the town of Tanza and was later renamed General Trias in honor of General Mariano Trias, a native of the town who was one of the leaders of the Philippine Revolution against Spain.

In 1897, General Emilio Aguinaldo declared the town as the capital of the newly-established province of Cavite. During the Philippine-American War, General Trias served as a strategic location for both the Philippine revolutionary forces and the American forces. The town was also the site of several battles during World War II.

In 1946, after the Philippines gained independence from the United States, General Trias became a regular municipality. Over the years, the town has experienced significant development, especially in the areas of commerce and industry. It has become a popular destination for investors and businesses due to its strategic location and favorable business environment.

Today, General Trias is home to various industries, including electronics, manufacturing, and food processing. It has also become a residential hub for people working in Metro Manila, thanks to its accessibility and relatively affordable housing options. The town is also known for its historical landmarks and tourist destinations, such as the General Trias Monument, the San Francisco de Malabon Church, and the Tejeros Convention Site.

8. what's the description of balagtas municipal hall​


Explanation:

With the continuous expansion of Metro Manila, the municipality is now part of Manila's built up area which reaches San Ildefonso in its northernmost part. Balagtas is 30 kilometres (19 mi) from Manila and is 15 kilometres (9.3 mi) from Malolos City.

Formerly known as Bigaa, it was renamed in honor of Filipino poet Francisco Balagtas. The MacArthur Highway bisects the municipality of Balagtas as this national road cuts northward to the Ilocos region. At the southern approach of the town from Manila is a concrete bridge that crosses the Balagtas River. The river, navigable by banca and motor boats, empties into Manila bay after snaking through the town of Bulacan to the West which provides Balagtas' townsfolk with fish, shrimp, and other fresh water food.

At the foot of the bridge, along the highway towards the North, is the town hall. In front of the town hall is a park where the monument of its hero, Francisco "Balagtas" Baltazar is located. The town hall is surrounded by Spanish colonial type houses. Two hundred meters from the municipal hall is the barrio of Panginay, the birthplace of Francisco Balagtas, for whom also "Balagtasan", a form of debate in versified Tagalog, was named. In 1946 the Historical Society of the Philippines placed a marker at the birthplace of Balagtas. In reverence to the hero's deeds, the official name of Bigaa was changed to Balagtas through the legislative act sponsored by then Congressman, Teodulo Natividad.

Before you can start operating your business in the Philippines, you need to secure a Mayor’s Permit or Business Permit from the Local Government Unit (LGU) where your company office is located.
