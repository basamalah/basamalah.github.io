13. Mr. Santos, a Sheriff of the Regional Trial Court, while serving a summon to Crisanto, the offender, he was attacked by the latter using a bolo. Fortunately, Mr. Santos was able to dodge the first attack. However, Crisanto swang his bolo towards Mr. Santos again, this time Lando, a bystander, pushed down Crisanto before he could strike Mr. Santos with his bolo. The offender managed to get back up and punched Lando a couple of times resulting to serious injuries. What crime or crimes has been committed by Crisanto?


Answer:

Based on the given scenario, Crisanto has committed the crime of Direct Assault under Article 148 of the Revised Penal Code. Direct Assault is defined as an attack, employing force or intimidation, upon a person in authority, or his agents, while in the performance of official duties, which may or may not result in physical injuries.

In this case, Mr. Santos, being a Sheriff of the Regional Trial Court, is considered a person in authority. Crisanto attacked him while he was performing his official duties of serving a summon. The fact that Crisanto used a bolo as a weapon shows that he employed force in his attack.

Furthermore, Crisanto also committed the crime of Physical Injuries under Article 265 of the Revised Penal Code when he punched Lando resulting in serious injuries. This is a separate offense from Direct Assault and is punishable under the law.
