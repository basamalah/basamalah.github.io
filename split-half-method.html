Answer:

1. Selection is the process of choosing the most suitable candidate for a job vacancy. The steps in the selection process include job analysis, sourcing candidates, screening resumes and applications, conducting interviews, checking references and backgrounds, and making a job offer.

2. The purpose of issuing application forms to potential applicants is to gather information about their qualifications, skills, and work experience. This information is used to screen candidates and determine if they meet the minimum requirements for the job.

3. The major items included in the application forms are personal information, educational background, work experience, skills, references, and any other relevant information required for the job.

4. Psychological tests are used to assess the mental abilities, personality traits, and emotional stability of candidates. These tests can provide valuable information about a candidate's suitability for a job.

5. Psychological tests can be used in various areas such as employee selection, career counseling, organizational development, and clinical assessment.

(c) Split-half method is a technique used to measure reliability by dividing a test into two halves and comparing the results. (d) Equivalent form method is a technique used to measure reliability by administering two different versions of the same test to the same group of people and comparing the results.

16. Which measure of central tendency refers to the middle value that splits the dateset into half?​


Answer:

Median

The median is the middlemost number. In other words, it's the number that divides the distribution exactly in half such that half the cases are above the median, and half are below. It's also known as the 50th percentile, and it can be calculated for ordinal and interval/ratio variables.
