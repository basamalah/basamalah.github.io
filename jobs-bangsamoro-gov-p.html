6. bangsamoro basic law advantages and disadvantages?


The Bangsamoro Basic Law (BBL) provides autonomy for the Muslims in certain parts of Mindanao such as Basilan, Lanao del Sur, Maguindanao, Sulu, and Tawi-Tawi. There are Bangsamoro Basic Law advantages and disadvantages. An advantage is that it provides focus in providing solutions to the concerns of the Muslims in the region, and Mindanao rebel groups are more satisfied with it. On the other hand, a disadvantage is that according to some lawmakers, the BBL does not follow the Philippine Constitution because it was not consulted to concerned communities. In connection with this, other details that are related to the Bangsamoro Basic Law (BBL) advantages and disadvantages are indicated below.

I. What is Bangsamoro Basic Law? Bangsamoro Basic Law is also called Bangsamoro Organic Law (BOL). It is previously known as ARMM (Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao) Bangsamoro Basic Law is the result of the long talks and negotiations between the government and rebel groups in Mindanao such as MILF. The official name of BBL is Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (OLBARMM). The Bangsamoro Basic Law provides autonomy for the Muslims in certain parts of Mindanao such as Basilan, Lanao del Sur, Maguindanao, Sulu, and Tawi-Tawi. II. What are the Advantages of Bangsamoro Basic Law?

Here are some of the advantages of the Bangsamoro Basic Law:

It provides solutions to the concerns of Muslims in specific regions. Compared with ARMM, MILF and MNLF are more satisfied with BBL in terms of political structure, justice system, physical autonomy, budget, territory, identity and defense and security. III. What are the Disadvantages of Bangsamoro Basic Law?

Here are some of the disadvantages of Bangsamoro Organic Law or Bangsamoro Basic Law:

According to some lawmakers, BBL does not follow the Philippine Constitution. It is because according to them, BBL did not pass through a consultation process across affected communities. However, according to Major Floor Leader Rodolfo Farinas, it did pass through a consultation period across affected communities.

9. The history of the Bangsamoro​


Answer:

The establishment of Bangsamoro was the culmination of several years of peace talks between the Philippine government and several autonomist groups; in particular the Moro Islamic Liberation Front or MILF, which rejected the validity of the ARMM and called for the creation of a region with more powers devolved from the national government. A framework agreement known as the Comprehensive Agreement on the Bangsamoro was negotiated between the Aquino administration and the MILF in 2014. After continued negotiations and debates over certain provisions, the Congress of the Philippines created and ratified a basic law for the region, now referred to as the Bangsamoro Organic Law; the bill was signed into law by President Rodrigo Duterte on July 26, 2018. Despite questions on the region's constitutionality, as it would have adopted a parliamentary system in an area of a country with a presidential system of government, no judicial ruling was made against the organic law and consequently the COMELEC held two-part plebiscite: one by ARMM citizens determining whether to dissolve the ARMM and immediately replace it with the Bangsamoro and, following the victory of the yes vote on the first part, and the second part taken by neighboring municipalities and barangays in the provinces of Lanao del Norte and Cotabato regarding their cession to the Bangsamoro region. As a result of the second part of the plebiscite, 63 barangays of Cotabato province were handed over to the Bangsamoro government, adding to the autonomous region's territory.

The Bangsamoro took the place of the ARMM as the only Muslim-majority autonomous region in the Philippines. Currently in transition until 2022, the Bangsamoro government has been considered a testing ground for the wider debate on constitutional reform and federalism in the Philippines.

21. What is the advantages of bangsamoro organic law ?


BANGSAMORO ORGANIC LAW (BOL)

The signed Bangsamoro Organic Law has its advantages and disadvantages, of course. But as we go to that, let's first have a view on the law itself.

BOL is the result after hundreds of peace talks between the Philippine Government and the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF) which started in 2014 and was only signed into law in January 2019.

The Bangsamoro Organic Law is under Republic Act 11054 which is the act that allows the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao to have and create a new political entity called Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao. This law pertains to Bangsamoro people. Meaning, all who are considered as natives or inhabitants of Mindanao, the Sulu archipelago and its adjacent islands.

GOVERNMENT

The new Bangsamoro Autonomous Region will have the parliamentary-democratic government, headed by a Chief Minister and 80 parliament members.  

GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS

a. Chief Minister  

Considered the head of the Bangsamoro government. His power includes appointing heads of different agencies, bureaus and other offices that he has power over with. He can also issue executive orders and policies as long as it is within jurisdiction.

b. Wali

Also called as the ceremonial head of the Bangsamoro region. His duties include opening the session of the parliament, administer oaths of different offices and in attending ceremonies as representative of the region.

c. Parliament

The parliament is composed of 80 members and will have the power to enact laws and conduct inquiries in aid of legislation among its members. A member has a 3-year long term.
