Stork Balance Stand Test

The stork balance test requires the person to stand on one leg



Purpose

: To assess the ability to balance on the ball of the foot.



Equipment required:

flat, non-slip surface, stopwatch, paper and pencil.



Procedure:

Remove shoes and place hands on the hipsPosition non-supporting foot against the inside knee of the supporting legThe subject is given one minute to practice the balance.The subject raises the heel to balance on the ball of the foot.The stopwatch is started as the heel is raised from the floor The stopwatch is stopped if any of the follow occur:the hand(s) come off the hipsthe supporting foot swivels or moves (hops) in any directionthe non-supporting foot loses contact with the knee.the heel of the supporting foot touches the floor.



Scoring

:The total time in seconds is recorded. The score is the best of three attempts.



22. learning test number one a die is tossed what is the probability of getting​


Answer:

1. 1/6 or 16% or unlikely

2. 3/6 or 50% or as likely as unlikely

3. 0/6 or 0% or impossible

4. 3/6 or 50% or as likely as unlikely

5. 3/6 or 50% or as likely as unlikely

Step-by-step explanation:

1. There is only one number that is "3" in the die and there are 6 numbers.

- So it is considered as ONE OUT OF 6 (1/6).

-1/6 as percentage is 16%, and if you look at the Probability Line, it is more closer to ZERO than ONE. But since it is not completely zero, it is considered as Unlikely Probability.

Therefore, the probability of getting 3 is unlikely.

2.There are 3 even numbers in the die and there are 6 numbers.

- So it is considered as THREE OUT OF 6 (3/6).

-3/6 as percentage is 50%, and if you look at the Probability Line, 50% is in the middle.It is considered as As Likely As Unlikely Probability. This means that there is 50% chance of getting an even number when the die is tossed.

Therefore, the probability of getting 3 is as likely as unlikely.

3. There are only 6 numbers in the die:

1,2,3,4,5, and 6. There is no number 7 in the die so it is impossible to get a 7 when a die is tossed. There is 0% chance of getting a 7.

4. There are 3 odd numbers in the die and there are 6 numbers.

- So it is considered as THREE OUT OF 6 (3/6).

-3/6 as percentage is 50%, and if you look at the Probability Line, 50% is in the middle.It is considered as As Likely As Unlikely Probability. This means that there is 50% chance of getting an odd number when the die is tossed.

5. There are 3 prime numbers in the die and there are 6 numbers.

- So it is considered as THREE OUT OF 6 (3/6).

-3/6 as percentage is 50%, and if you look at the Probability Line, 50% is in the middle.It is considered as As Likely As Unlikely Probability. This means that there is 50% chance of getting a prime number when the die is tossed.

(Note: 1 is not considered as a prime number)

28. a coin is tossed three times .find the probability that head and tail show alternately. show ur solution po ​


Answer:

¼ or 25%

Step-by-step explanation:

Coin toss three times, possible outcomes:

{(H,H,H),(H,H,T),(H,T,H),(H,T,T),(T,H,H),(T,H,T),(T,T,H),(T,T,T)}

Total outcomes= 8

Thus, favourable outcomes are {(H,T,H),(T,H,T)} = 2

Probability of getting a head and tail alternately = 2/8 = 1/4
