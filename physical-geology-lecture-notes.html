4. what is the difference between physical and historical geology?​


Answer:

14. Explain the importance of physical, structural geology & petrology in civil engineering?


Physics, structural geology and petrology are integrated sciences applied in civil engineering.

Detail of explanation:

Civil engineering is an applied science that studies how to design, manage, maintain, manage, and repair buildings. Almost all types of buildings are related to civil engineering, ranging from houses, buildings, roads, bridges, reservoirs, offices, markets, airports, power plants and many more. The scope of civil engineering is very broad, including studying mathematics, physics, chemistry, biology, geology, petrology, environment and informatics.

Physics is a pure science in the application of civil engineering related to building structures. The building structure must consider the laws of physics. Like a building for sanitation, a sloping floor is needed to facilitate the flow of water to the disposal. Another example is how to make stairs that have a slope according to the building. Making roads in hilly and flat areas by considering the speed and safety for the driver.

Structural geology is concerned with designing civil engineering buildings considering the geological conditions of an area. Such as fault areas, folds or soil characteristics in an area. This relates to the construction to be built, the construction must be sturdy and safe to support a building.

Petrology is a branch of geology that studies rocks and their formation. Rocks have different characters such as igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic. Civil engineers need to understand how to handle developments faced with certain rock formations. Either use it as a building material or as a consideration in building.

24. Explain the importance of physical, structural geology and petrology in civil engineering.


Answer:

Geology is the study of earth, the materials of which it is made, the structure of those materials and the effects of the natural forces acting upon them and is important to civil engineering because all work performed by civil engineers involves earth and its features. Fundamental understanding of geology is so important that it is a requirement in university-level civil engineering programs. For a civil engineering project to be successful, the engineers must understand the land upon which the project rests. Geologists study the land to determine whether it is stable enough to support the proposed project. They also study water patterns to determine if a particular site is prone to flooding. Some civil engineers use geologists to examine rocks for important metals, oil, natural gas and ground water.

26. Search about the each HISTORY of 5 Branches of Physical Science: 1. ASTRONOMY 2. METEOROLOGY 3. CHEMISTRY 4. PHYSICS 5. GEOLOGY​


1. ASTRONOMY is the branch of physical science that studies celestial objects such as stars, planets, and galaxies. Its history can be traced back to ancient civilizations such as the Babylonians and Egyptians who observed and recorded the movements of celestial bodies.

2. METEOROLOGY is the branch of physical science that deals with the study of the Earth's atmosphere and its phenomena such as weather and climate. Its history dates back to ancient times when people observed and recorded weather patterns.

3. CHEMISTRY is the branch of physical science that deals with the composition, structure, properties, and reactions of matter. Its history can be traced back to ancient civilizations such as the Greeks and Egyptians who studied the properties of materials.

4. PHYSICS is the branch of physical science that deals with the study of matter, energy, and their interactions. Its history can be traced back to ancient Greece where philosophers such as Aristotle and Archimedes studied motion and mechanics.

5. GEOLOGY is the branch of physical science that deals with the study of the Earth's structure, composition, and processes. Its history can be traced back to ancient civilizations such as the Chinese and Greeks who studied rocks and minerals.
