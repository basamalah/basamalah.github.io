7. which phase occurs directly after G2


Answer:

The phase which occurs after G2 of the cell life cycle is the M phase, also known as Mitotic or Meiotic phase, depending on the type of cell.

Explanation:

The cell life cycle consists of the G1 phase, S phase, G2 phase, and the M phase. However, cells which do not divide become suspended in the G0 phase after obtaining substantial development and growth. A few examples include the nerve cells and the skeletal muscle cells.

The events occurring during the entire life cycle of the cell are summarized below:

Interphase

G1 phase, or Gap 1 phase, is the phase when the cell grows and prepares for DNA replication. When the cell has reached an appropriate size and the conditions support for DNA replication, the cell leaves the G1 phase and enters the next phase of the interphase.S phase, or Synthesis phase, is the phase in which DNA is replicated. A successful cellular division needs accurate duplication of the entire genome. Hence, the processes occurring during this phase are tightly regulated.G2 phase, or Gap 2 phase, is directly preceding Mitosis (or meiosis, in the case of sex cells). This phase follows the successful replication of the entire DNA during the S phase. Rapid cell growth and protein synthesis occurs during the G2 phase as the cell prepares for Mitosis. The G2 phase ends with the prophase of the M phase.

M phase (Mitotic phase)

The cell divides its replicated DNA and cytoplasmic components to two new cells. The M phase has two main processes namely mitosis and cytokinesis.

During mitosis, the DNA condenses into chromosomes and are pulled apart by mitotic spindle. Mitosis occurs in four stages namely, prophase, metaphase, anaphase, and telophase. The DNA condenses into chromosomes during prophase. The sister chromatids align in the metaphase plate during metaphase. Spindle fibers attach the sister chromatids through their centromere to the opposing ends of the cell. During anaphase, the sister chromatids are pulled away from each other and towards the opposite poles of the cell. In telophase, the sister chromatids reach the opposite poles and a nuclear membrane reappears at each of the opposite poles.Cytokinesis occurs when the cytoplasm of the cell is split into two, resulting in two new cells. Cytokinesis occurs differently in animal and plant cells.

Independent Events

Two events are independent if one of the following are true:

P\left(A|B\right)=P\left(A\right)

P\left(B|A\right)=P\left(B\right)

P\left(A\cap B\right)=P\left(A\right)P\left(B\right)

Two events A and B are independent if the knowledge that one occurred does not affect the chance the other occurs. For example, the outcomes of two roles of a fair die are independent events. The outcome of the first roll does not change the probability for the outcome of the second roll. To show two events are independent, you must show only one of the above conditions. If two events are NOT independent, then we say that they are dependent.

Sampling may be done with replacement or without replacement.

With replacement: If each member of a population is replaced after it is picked, then that member has the possibility of being chosen more than once. When sampling is done with replacement, then events are considered to be independent, meaning the result of the first pick will not change the probabilities for the second pick.

Without replacement: When sampling is done without replacement, each member of a population may be chosen only once. In this case, the probabilities for the second pick are affected by the result of the first pick. The events are considered to be dependent or not independent.

If it is not known whether A and B are independent or dependent, assume they are dependent until you can show otherwise.

30. what happens during the g2 phase?​


Answer:

G2 phase is a period of rapid cell growth and protein synthesis during which the cell prepares itself for mitosis. Curiously, G2 phase is not a necessary part of the cell cycle, as some cell types (particularly young Xenopus embryos and some cancers) proceed directly from DNA replication to mitosis.
