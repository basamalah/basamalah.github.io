10. (SHOW SOLUTION)Myrna went to the supermarket to buysardines and pancit carton for their food stock. She paid 250.00 pesos on 6 pieces sardines and & 8 pieces pancit Canton. Suppose the total cost of a sardines and a pancit canton is 37.00 pesos how much is each item cost?Let x be the cost of sardines Let y be the cost of pancit canton(Elimination or substitution method)


Step-by-step explanation:

Using the substitution method:

Let x be the cost of sardines

Let y be the cost of pancit canton

We can set up the following system of equations to represent the problem:

6x + 8y = 250

x + y = 37

x = 37 - y

We can substitute this expression for x in the first equation to get:

6(37 - y) + 8y = 250

This simplifies to:

222 - 6y + 8y = 250

Combining like terms, we get:

2y = 28

Dividing both sides by 2, we get:

y = 14

Substituting this value for y in the second equation, we get:

x + 14 = 37

Solving for x, we get:

x = 23

Therefore, the cost of a sardine is 23 pesos and the cost of a pancit canton is 14 pesos.

21. er the following problems: Bill has 5 balls of different colors. In how many possible combination can he choose two balls?​


Answer:

10

Step-by-step explanation:

To calculate the number of combinations, we can use the formula for combinations:

nCr = n! / r!(n-r)!

where n is the total number of items, and r is the number of items chosen.

In this case, n = 5 and r = 2, so we have:

5C2 = 5! / 2!(5-2)! = 10

Therefore, there are 10 possible combinations that Bill can choose two balls out of five.
