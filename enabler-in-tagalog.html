7. The cloud enables people in different locations to work on the same project. This enables _____.


The cloud enables people in different locations to work on the same project

The cloud may seem confusing, but essentially, it’s a term used to describe a worldwide network of servers, each with an exclusive function. The cloud is not an individual, but instead is a group of networks of isolated servers around the world which are link together and meant to operate as a single mind. These servers are intended to either store and manage data, run applications, or deliver content or a service such as streaming videos, web mail, office productivity software, or social media. Instead of reading files and data from a local or personal computer, you are reading them online from any Internet-capable device—the information will be available anywhere you go and anytime you need it.

Four different methods usage of cloudPublic cloud -  shares information and offers services to the public over the Internet, Private cloud -  It is not shared and offers services over a private internal network typically presented on-premises Hybrid cloud - shares services between public and private clouds depending on their purpose Community cloud - shares resources only between organizations, such as with government institutions.

The three layers of cloud computing and how it helps your project:

Infrastructure Platform Application

The infrastructure or IAAS is where it all starts, it handles hosting which removes the burden off putting everything on a server which people used to buy or rent which cost plenty of money and time. The cloud server gets rid of all of this by providing more computing power for any project. The platform or PAAS is the domain of the developers. The application layer is delivered by your regular web browser, they are also known as SAAS providers and they are universally available in any platform.

Your bill only covers what you’ve used, compare that to regular hosting which has a fixed cost, regardless of how often you use it. It is scalable Whatever you need is instantly available. Shared resources

24. Explanation of Enabling Michanisms


Enabling mechanisms are tools, processes, or systems that facilitate the achievement of a goal or objective. They provide the necessary support and resources to enable individuals or organizations to perform tasks or activities more effectively and efficiently.

In the context of organizational change, for example, enabling mechanisms might include training programs, communication systems, or performance management systems that help employees adapt to new ways of working. In the context of technology adoption, enabling mechanisms might include user manuals, technical support, or online tutorials that help users learn how to use new tools or software.

Overall, enabling mechanisms play a critical role in supporting the successful implementation of new initiatives or changes by providing the necessary resources and support to help individuals or organizations achieve their goals.
