A bond is a debt instrument issued by a corporation or a government entity to raise capital. It represents a loan made by an investor to the issuer, who agrees to pay periodic interest payments to the bondholder and return the principal amount when the bond matures. A bond is a certificate representing a loan from an investor to a corporation or government entity. It is a debt security, where the issuer borrows money from the investor and promises to pay back the principal amount with interest over a specified period.
