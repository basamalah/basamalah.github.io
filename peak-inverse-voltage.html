11. The turns ratio of a high-voltage transformer is 700:1 and the supply voltage is peaked at 120 volts. how much secondary voltage is supplied to the x-ray tube


Answer:

A transformer with a primary winding of 1000 turns and a secondary winding of 100 turns has a turns ratio of 1000:100 or 10:1. Therefore 100 volts applied to the primary will produce a secondary voltage of 10 volts.

Step-by-step explanation:

Answer:

[tex] \frac{vp}{vs} = \frac{np}{ns} [/tex]

Vp = 120 V

Vs = ?

np = 700

ns = 1

[tex] \frac{120}{vs} = \frac{700}{1} [/tex]

Vs = 0.171 V ≈ 171 mV


12. how are voltage and current related? are they directly proportional or inversely proportional?why?​


Answer:

Directly proportional

Explanation:

Ohm's law states that the electrical current (I) flowing in an circuit is proportional to the voltage (V) and inversely proportional to the resistance (R). Therefore, if the voltage is increased, the current will increase provided the resistance of the circuit does not change. Similarly, increasing the resistance of the circuit will lower the current flow if the voltage is not changed.
