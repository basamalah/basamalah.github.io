13. Galvanic cell is very important in the study of electrochemistry. Through an essay, explain the reactions occurring in the parts of a galvanic cell. ​


A galvanic cell is an electrochemical cell that produces direct current by converting chemical energy to electrical energy. It is composed of two electrodes, usually made of different metals, connected externally by a wire and immersed in a solution containing ions that serve as the medium of electron transfer. A galvanic cell relies on a redox reaction in order to operate. A redox reaction is a reaction in which electrons are transferred from one atom or molecule to another.

In a galvanic cell, one of the electrodes, the anode, acts as an electron acceptor, meaning it gains electrons during the reaction. The other electrode, the cathode, acts as an electron donor, meaning it gives up electrons during the reaction. This process creates a reaction in the cell that causes electrons to flow from the anode to the cathode. As the electrons move between the electrodes, a current is created and electrical energy is produced.

The reactions occurring in the parts of a galvanic cell can be divided into two categories: oxidation and reduction reactions. Oxidation reactions occur at the anode where electrons are lost and oxidation occurs. This occurs when an atom “gives up” electrons to the anode, forming ions which then enter the solution. The ions - usually metal cations - carry a positive charge and move away from the anode due to its electrical potential.

On the other hand, reduction reactions occur at the cathode which is where electrons are gained and reduction occurs. In this reaction, atoms in the solution “accept” electrons from the cathode, forming metal cations that move toward it. As the electrons move between the electrodes, a current is produced. This current is the galvanic cell’s main output and is used to power any device connected to it.

In conclusion, galvanic cells are essential for the study of electrochemistry. They rely on oxidation and reduction reactions occurring between two electrodes to produce a current that can be used to power any device connected to it. Through their ability to convert chemical energy into electrical energy, galvanic cells are widely used in various applications such as fuel cells, batteries, and portable generators.
