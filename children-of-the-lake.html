4. The angle of depression of one side of a lake, measured from a hot air balloon 2600 feet above the lake, is 42°. The angle of depression to the opposite side of the lake is 28°. Find the width of the lake​.


Answer:

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The width of the lake is approximately measures 7777.47

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the width of the lake, we must use the formula:

[tex] \boxed{\bold{tan \theta = \frac{Opposite}{Adjacent}}} [/tex]

and seperate the two angle elevation. Afterwards, add up the result two result to get the width of the lake.

[tex] \begin{array}{c|c} \text{Let x be the Adjacent}&\text{Let y be the Adjacent}\\ \tan(28) = \frac{2600}{x} & \tan(42) = \frac{2600}{y} \\ \tan(28)x = \frac{2600}{x} \cdot x & \tan(42)y = \frac{2600}{y}\cdot y \\ \tan(28)x = 2600&\tan(42)y=2600\\ \frac{\tan(28)x}{\tan(28)}=\frac{2600}{\tan(28)}& \frac{\tan(42)y}{\tan(42)}=\frac{2600}{\tan(42)}\\ x \approx 4889.88 & y \approx 2887.59\\ \end{array} [/tex]

Now, add up both results to get the width

4889.88 + 2887.59 ≈ 7777.47

5. Which statements explain how ponds and lakes differ? Check all that apply. Ponds are shallower than lakes. Ponds are larger than lakes. Pond water moves more than lake water. Lakes have more plants at the bottom than ponds. Lakes are colder and darker at the bottom than ponds.


The statements that explain how ponds and lakes differ are the following:

Ponds are shallower than lakes.

Lakes are colder and darker at the bottom than ponds.

If the water is deep the light cannot enter to the bottom, and photosynthesis is limited to the top layer, the body of water is considered a lake. Lake is a body of water with 2 hectares  or more in area. A pond is a shallow body of water that does not support rooted plants. Many times plants grow at the corner of the pond. The aquatic plants receive  the necessary nutrients because the sunlight is available, as a result of which they can flourish and grow. Both lakes and ponds are slow-moving bodies of water.

Famous Ponds and Lakes

Lakes are freshwater bodies that are surrounded by land on all sides, here is the list of world-famous lakes:

Lake Baikal (Russia) Lough Neagh (Ireland) Caspian Sea (Russia and Iran) Lake Tanganyika (Africa) Lake Superior (North America) Loch Lomond (Scotland) Lake Victoria (Africa) Loch Ness (Scotland) Great Slave Lake (Canada) Lake Malawi (Africa)

A pond that occurs naturally may be on a valley close to a river, here are worlds famous ponds:

Walden Pond, Concord, Massachusetts, USA Antonelli Pond, Santa Cruz, California, USA Christian Pond, Grand Teton National Park, Wyoming, USA Barycz River Valley, southwestern Poland Pete’s Pond, Mashatu Game Reserve, Botswana

Conservation of Ponds and Lakes

To maintain the ecological balance of ponds and lakes, it is humans are accountable to conserve these bodies of water by doing the following:

If you live on a lake, stream or wetland, place aquatic plants native plants along the water and don’t use fertilizers and pesticides. If you have a septic system make sure it functions properly and meets current standards. When you buy a boat motor, choose a 4-cycle engine because it is more eco-friendly to water bodies. Never dump wastes into ponds and lakes.  Don’t use the lake as a bathtub because soaps and shampoos contain pollutants that are harmful to the lake and organisms living in it.

25. Why is Taal volcano in Taal Lake referred to as the largest lake on an island in a lake on an island?


Answer:

Taal Volcano, located in the Philippines, is often referred to as the "largest lake on an island in a lake on an island" due to its unique geographical features. The volcano is situated on the island of Luzon, which is the largest island in the Philippines. Within Luzon, there is a lake called Taal Lake, which is the second largest lake in the country. Inside Taal Lake, there is an island called Volcano Island, which is the location of Taal Volcano, an active stratovolcano.

What makes Taal Volcano unique is that within the caldera, or the large volcanic crater, of Taal Volcano, there is another smaller lake called the Crater Lake. This smaller lake is also sometimes referred to as "Main Crater Lake" or "Volcano Island Lake". So, in summary, Taal Volcano is located on an island called Volcano Island, which is located in Taal Lake, which is situated on the larger island of Luzon in the Philippines. This nested arrangement of lakes within an island within a lake within an island makes Taal Volcano a fascinating geological feature and earns it the distinction of being the "largest lake on an island in a lake on an island."
