7. Compare the number and type of cells that result from meiosis vs. mitosis.


Comparison of the processes of mitosis and meiosis. Mitosis produces two diploid (2n) somatic cells that are genetically identical to each other and the original parent cell, whereas meiosis produces four haploid (n) gametes that are genetically unique from each other and the original parent (germ) cell.

Which type and number of cells result from meiosis?

four

Meiosis is a type of cell division that reduces the number of chromosomes in the parent cell by half and produces four gamete cells. This process is required to produce egg and sperm cells for sexual reproduction.

What type of cells result from mitosis?

Mitosis results in two cells that are identical to the cell you started with. Mitosis, unlike meiosis, occurs in somatic cells and creates daughter cells with a full set of chromosomes. So, 2 diploid somatic cells (that are exact copies of the parent cell) result from mitosis.

How many cells are produced in mitosis and meiosis?

Meiosis produces 4 haploid cells. Mitosis produces 2 diploid cells. The old name for meiosis was reduction/ division. Meiosis I reduces the ploidy level from 2n to n (reduction) while Meiosis II divides the remaining set of chromosomes in a mitosis-like process (division).

15. differentiate the following terms:(a) unicellular cells vs. multicellular cells(b) prokaryotic cells vs. eukaryotic cells(c) cyptoplasm vc. karyoplasm(d) rough endoplasmic reticulum vs. smooth endoplasmic reticulum​


Answer:

(a) unicellular cells vs. multicellular cells

- Unicellular organisms are made up of only one cell that carries out all of the functions needed by the organism, while multicellular organisms use many different cells to function.

(b) prokaryotic cells vs. eukaryotic cells

- eukaryotic cells have a membrane-bound nucleus and prokaryotic cells do not.

(c) cyptoplasm vc. karyoplasm

- cytoplasm is (cytology) the contents of a cell except for the nucleus it includes cytosol, organelles, vesicles, and the cytoskeleton while sarcoplasm is (anatomy) the interfibrillar cytoplasm of striated muscle.

(d) rough endoplasmic reticulum vs. smooth endoplasmic reticulum

​- Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum is known for stocking the lipids and proteins. It is not bounded by ribosomes. While, the Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum is bounded by the ribosomes and also stores proteins.

22. identify the specipic types of research for each given topic(action,pure,applied,descriptive,explanatory,correlation). 1 .theory of relativity 2.university belt street foods 3.landline vs.cellphone​


The specific types of research for each given topic are:Relationship → C, GJustification → D, HDetailed → B, IApplying → K and EPure → A, J, and LAffect → F

Correlation = C and G

When two variables are used in a correlation study, their connection to one another is examined. Thus, correlation research on topics like C. Landline vs. Cell Phone and G. Critical Thinking and Creative Thinking are necessary because they have two different variables yet allow for connections to be made between them. Like a landline and a cell phone are both communication tools, so are critical and creative thinking.

Explanatory = D, H

An explanatory study reveals why a particular variable occurs. H. discusses Why and How the Internet Must Be Used, as well as the D. Reasons for the Increase in Tuition Fees.

Descriptive = B, I

Results from descriptive research are qualitative. It gives a description of the variable or its results. B. University Belt Street Foods is one example. It speaks of the cuisine in University Belt. It specifies whether the cuisine is delectable, reasonably priced, etc. Additionally, the study "Impacts of Korean Telenovelas on TV Viewers" is a descriptive one because it explains if the impact of Korean Telenovelas is positive or negative.

Applied = K, E

Applied research is conducted to address a particular problem. K.  Using PowerPoint presentations to teach, and e Because K. offers solutions in instruction and letter E. offers solutions on Hawaii Flash cuisine, these solutions are examples of applied research.

Pure = A, J, and L

Pure research strives to advance scientific hypotheses that can help us comprehend and anticipate many events, whether they be natural or man-made. Because the Theory of Relativity helps us understand gravity, the Digital Age, and Archimedes' Principle of Buoyance are all examples of pure study. Second, modernization is made clearer to us by the digital era. Third, the concept of buoyancy helps us comprehend why some objects float.

Action = F

The researcher's actual actions are a variable in action research. College Assessment Practices is an empirical study since it examines how students actually conduct themselves during a college assessment.
