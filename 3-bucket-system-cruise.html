4. A hose fills a 3-gallon bucket in 9 seconds. At this rate, how long will it take to fill a bucket that is 67% larger than the 3-gallon bucket? Round to the nearest second.


Answer:

It took 15.03 seconds to fill the 67% larger than the 3-gallon bucket

Calculating the time required to fill the bucket:

→ To solve this problem, use the concept of proportionality to calculate     the time required. Use the concept of percentage increase to find the volume of the larger bucket, which was 67% larger than the 3-gallon bucket.

Here we have:

A hose fills a 3-gallon bucket in 9 seconds.

The volume of the bucket which is 167% more than 3 gallons

= 167% of 3 gallons = [167/100] × 3 = 5.01 gallons

It took 9 seconds to fill 3 gallons

The time required to fill 5.01 gallons = 9/3 × [5.01]

= 15.03 seconds

Therefore,

It took 15.03 seconds to fill the 67% larger than the 3-gallon bucket

21. There are two buckets: A and B. There are 11 kg of pure alcohol in bucket A and 15 kg of water in bucket B. Pour a part of the pure alcohol in bucket A into the bucket B and mixed it with water. Pour part of the mixture in bucket B into bucket A, then the pure alcohol content in bucket A become 62.5% while the pure alcohol content in bucket B will be 25%. In the second mixing, how many kg is the mixture poured from bucket B into bucket A?


Answer:

See below.

Explanation:

When pouring pure alcohol from bucket A into bucket B, the content of bucket B becomes an alcohol solution. After pouring alcohol solution from bucket B to bucket A, the concentration of bucket B has not changed.

However, when pouring pure alcohol from bucket A into bucket B, the 15 kg of water in bucket B turns into a 25% concentration of alcohol solution, and subsequently, the solution in bucket B will be 15 ÷ (1 - 25%) = 20 kg.

So bucket B contains 20 - 15 = 5 kg of pure alcohol. The remaining pure alcohol in bucket A is 11 - 5 = 6 kg.

Let the alcohol solution poured from bucket B to bucket A be x kg, then it contains pure alcohol of 25% of x = x/4. Because 6 kg of pure alcohol in bucket A has an alcohol solution with a concentration of 62.5%,

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{6+\frac{x}{4}}{6+x}=\frac{62.5}{100}\implies x=6\end{aligned}[/tex]

Thus, the mixture poured from bucket B to bucket A is 6 kg on the second time.
