5. What is the percent by mass concentration of a sugar solution that contains 10g of sugar dissolved in 30g of water? Given: Required: Formula: Solution: ​


Given:

mass of solute = 10 g

mass of solvent = 30 g

Required:

percent by mass

Formula:

[tex]\text{percent by mass} = \dfrac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} \times 100\%[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\text{percent by mass} = \dfrac{\text{10 g}}{\text{10 g + 30 g}} \times 100\%[/tex]

Final Answer:

percent by mass = 25%

24. 2. What is the general formula in determining the % concentration regardless of the phase of solute and solvent?​


Percent Concentration

% concentration = [tex]\frac{amount of solute}{amount of solution}[/tex] x 100%

   

Explanation:

A solution is made of solute and solvent. The solute is the substance being dissolved in a solution while the solvent is the substance that does the dissolving.

A solution can be described by its concentration. A concentrated solution is one that has a relatively large amount of dissolved solute. A dilute solution, on the other hand,  is one that has a relatively small amount of dissolved solute. Also, we can describe more specifically the concentration of solution through percent concentration.

Percent concentration is the ratio of the amount of solute in relation to the amount of solution multiplied by 100%. It can be percent mass or percent volume depending on the phase of the solute and solution.
