18. An orange [YR] snap dragon is a result of a cross between a yellow [YY] snap dragon and a red [RR] snap dragon where yellow is incompletely dominant over red. Using a Punnett square, show the cross between a yellow and an orange snap dragons. ​


F1 (first generation): An orange [YR] snap dragon is a result of a cross between a yellow [YY] snap dragon and a red [RR] snap dragon where yellow is incompletely dominant over red.

F2(second generation): orange [YR] snap dragon cross with yellow [YY] snap dragon resulting yellow YY] snap dragon 50% and orange [YR] snap dragon 50% (1:1)

Explanation of Incomplete Dominance

Incomplete dominance is an intermediate form of inheritance in which one allele for a particular trait is not fully expressed over its paired alleles. This generates a third phenotype in which the physical properties expressed are a combination of the phenotypes of both alleles. Unlike the inheritance of complete domination, one allele does not predominate or cover the other.

Explanation of Punnet Square

Punnett square is a square diagram that is used to predict the genotype of a particular crossing or breeding experiment. It is named after Reginald C. Punnett, who designed the approach. The diagrams are used by biologists to determine the probability of an offspring having a certain genotype. Punnett's Square is made from simple square which is divided into grids of 2x2 (or more)
