7. Explain the importance of observing the standards and regulations of watch keeping in accordance to the STCW Code.​


Answer:

The STCW Convention

STCW was first implemented in 1978. Following some important amendments over the years, notably in 1995, it came into full effect in 2002. In June 2010, at the Diplomatic Conference in Manila, Philippines, the latest revision of the STCW will be debated and formalised into adocument that will set the international standards for seafaring best practice now and in the future.

STCW certification was created to promote safety of life and property at sea and to protect the marine environment. It establishes internationally accepted standards of training and certification of seafarers, ensuring that crew are qualified and fit for duties at sea.

STCW Basic Safety certificates are a requirement for virtually all professional seafarers.

The level of certification and training you are required to have is based on the capacity in which you serve and the type of vessel you work on.

This includes ensuring that the vessel is properly maintained, crew members are adequately trained, and safety equipment is in good working order.

2. Environmental Protection: Ship superintendents must ensure that the vessel is operated in a manner that minimizes environmental impacts. This includes following all applicable laws and regulations related to air emissions, water discharges, and waste management.

3. Security: Ship superintendents must ensure that the vessel is operated in a secure manner at all times. This includes following all applicable laws and regulations related to security, such as those related to port security, cargo security, and crew security.

4. Quality Assurance: Ship superintendents must ensure that the vessel is operated in a manner that meets or exceeds industry standards for quality assurance. This includes ensuring that the vessel is properly maintained, crew members are adequately trained, and safety equipment is in good working order.

5. Regulatory Compliance: Ship superintendents must ensure that the vessel complies with all applicable laws and regulations related to maritime operations, such as those related to navigation, communications, pollution prevention, labor standards, etc.
