2. compare and contrast in-text citation and reference citations in terms of context, purpose, style, and format. And the similarities of in-text citation and reference citation.


Answer:

Context:

In-text citation is used to refer to a specific source within the body of a paper. It is used to provide evidence for an argument or point being made. Reference citations are used at the end of a paper to provide a list of all sources used in the paper.

Purpose:

The purpose of in-text citation is to give credit to the original author of the source material and to provide readers with a way to locate the source material. The purpose of reference citations is to provide readers with a comprehensive list of all sources used in the paper.

Style:

In-text citation is typically written in parentheses and includes the author's last name, year of publication, and page number (if applicable). Reference citations are typically written in a specific style such as APA, MLA, or Chicago.

Format:

In-text citation is typically written in parentheses and includes the author's last name, year of publication, and page number (if applicable). Reference citations are typically written in a specific format such as APA, MLA, or Chicago.

Similarities:

Both in-text citation and reference citation are used to give credit to the original author of the source material and to provide readers with a way to locate the source material.
