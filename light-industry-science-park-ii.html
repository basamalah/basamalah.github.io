21. What do you think industry and science refers to? ​


Answer:

In our country, the pharmaceutical industry can be considered as a "true" chemical industry which has become reputable & succeeded in selling medicines to Europe as well as covering many local market demands.

While teaching industrial chemistry, I explain that most of the pharmaceutical industries "worldwide" are of the batch type so as our industries here. Students are required to understand, through examples, the characteristics of an industrial batch plant together with what items of equipment it will have & how these tools are operated.

A typical chemical industry must have a section for R&D "research & development" and our local pharmaceutical industries do have that from information I gathered from our graduates who work there. Therefore, students are required to know examples of the duties of the R&D section in an industry and the type of "applied" research that is carried out there.

Students of the industrial chemistry course ought to know the chemical names, the structural formulas, and the function of the most famous drugs such as Aspirin, Panadol, paracetamol , penicillin, amoxicillin…etc.

Even though it is an introductory course, students do not appreciate its value until they graduate & become fortunate enough to be employed in a chemical industry "pharmaceutical or other industries such as paints, plastics, soaps, detergents…etc.".

25. Industrial science deals with which of the following?​


Answer:

How the technology that arises from science is applied in industries

Explanation:

Without science and technology most of our industries today would not even exist. The thousands of hours put into Research and Development ensures that the products we have are of the highest quality (ideally, at least). But industrial science, or how technology arising from science is applied in the different industries is the backbone of product development.

There are three parts here that are critical:

Development and Design - the early stages start out with designing the product. At this stage, a product is hypothesized, designed and prepared for subsequent testing. Legal, societal and moral ramifications are also considered at this stage.Testing and Troubleshooting - at this stage a product has already been created. But it is not perfect yet so testing and troubleshooting will be done. Human trials are done at this state, in applicable industries. Redevelopment and Adaptation - one data comes in and is analyzed from the second stage, then redevelopment and adaptation is done, Stage 2 is critical because without that data, the last stage is bound not to happen. For so long as there are points of improved, Stage 3 iterates.

So, that being said, the role of an industrial scientist is critical both to the industry and to the community because without them, then it would be difficult to come up with new products and inventions.
