1. In a batch of 300 students, 73 aremembers of the Sports Club, 220 aremembers of the Music Club, and 70students are in both clubs. How manystudents are:a. in either the Sports Club or MusicClub?b. in neither of the clubs?​


Students (U) = 300

Let, S = sports, M = music

n(S) = 73

n(M) = 220

n(S) ∩ n(M) = 70

To solve for the number of students who are into sports club only:

n(S) = 73

73 - 70 = 3

To solve for the number of students who are into music club only:

n(M) = 220

220-70 = 150

To solve for the number of students who are in neither of the clubs:

300-(30+70+150) = 77

14. two seventh of the sports club members are girls.If there are 84 members of the club, how many are boys?​


If two sevenths of the sports club members are girls, then the remaining members, which represent five sevenths of the total, must be boys.

Let's use algebra to solve for the number of boys (B):

250 students in a batch62 are members of Sports Club170 are members of Drama Club40 are members of both clubs

To obtain the number of students who are members of Sports Club only, simply subtract 40 from 62, and we will get 22.

To obtain the number of students who are members of Drama Club only, simply subtract 40 from 170, and we will get 130.

Then we can obtain the number of students who are not members of the Sports and Drama Club by this expression:

250 - (22+130+40)250 - 192 = 58 students

Therefore, there are 58 students who are not members of any of the two clubs.

20. Josephine's sport shop gives a discount of 10% off the list price of all sports equipment to members of the sports club. What discount would a club member get on a tennis racket with a list price of Php 7 835.00?​


Answer:

A club member would get a discount of:

10% of Php 7 835.00 = (10/100) x Php 7 835.00 = Php 783.50

Therefore, the discount a club member would get on a tennis racket with a list price of Php 7 835.00 is Php 783.50.

The discounted price would be:

Php 7 835.00 - Php 783.50 = Php 7 051.50

So, the club member would pay Php 7 051.50 for the tennis racket after the 10% discount.

27. Association of non-profit clubs, inc., et al. vs. bureau of internal revenue scribd


Answer:

Thanks for the reference

Explanation:

Answer:

The Facts

On August 3, 2012, respondent the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) issued RMC No. 35-2012, entitled "Clarifying the Taxability of Clubs Organized and Operated Exclusively for Pleasure, Recreation, and Other Non-Profit Purposes,"6 which was addressed to all revenue officials, employees, and others concerned for their guidance regarding the income tax and Valued Added Tax (VAT) liability of the said recreational clubs.7

On the income tax component, RMC No. 35-2012 states that "[c]lubs which are organized and operated exclusively for pleasure, recreation, and other non-profit purposes are subject to income tax under the National Internal Revenue Code [(NIRC)] of 1997,8 as amended [(1997 NIRC)]."9 The BIR justified the foregoing interpretation based on the following reasons:

According to the doctrine of casus omissus pro omisso habendus est, a person, object, or thing omitted from an enumeration must be held to have been omitted intentionally. The provision in the (1977 Tax Code] which granted income tax exemption to such recreational clubs was omitted in the current list of tax exempt corporations under [the 1997 NIRC], as amended. Hence, the income of recreational clubs from whatever source, including but not limited to membership fees, assessment dues, rental income, and service fees are subject to income tax.10 (Emphasis and underscoring supplied)

Likewise, on the VAT component, RMC No. 35-2012 provides that "the gross receipts of recreational clubs including but not limited to membership fees, assessment dues, rental income, and service fees are subject to VAT."11 As basis, the BIR relied on Section 105,12 Chapter I, Title IV of the 1997 NIRC, which states that even a nonstock, nonprofit private organization or government entity is liable to pay VAT on the sale of goods or services.13

On October 25, 2012, ANPC, along with the representatives of its member clubs, invited Atty. Elenita Quimosing (Atty. Quimosing), Chief of Staff, Operations Group of the BIR, to discuss "specifically the effects of the said [C]ircular and to seek clarification and advice from the BIR on how it will affect the operational requirements of each club and their members/stakeholders."14 During their meeting, Atty. Quimosing discussed the basis and effects of RMC No. 35-2012, and further suggested that the attendees submit a position paper to the BIR expressing their concerns.15


28. 40 out of the 60 members of a sports club are boys. What percent of the club members are girls?


Given:
           60 = the total number of members in the sports club
           40 = number of boys of the club

Find:  Percent of the club that are girls.

Solution:
              There are 60 total members, 40 boys, so the number of girls is obviously 20, because 60 - 40 = 20
  
             This time we must find the percent of the club that are girls.
This also means - what percent of 60 is 20.

Percentage ÷ Base = Rate
20    ÷    60   = 0.33333...
Now, change  0.33333... to percent by multiplying by 100 and inserting the percent symbol

0.33333...  x 100 =      33. 333333......% or just 33.33%

Final Answer: 
                          33.33%
