All organisms share certain characteristics.All levels of life have systems of related parts.Structure and function are related in biology.Organisms must maintain homeostasis to survive in diverse environments.Evolution explains the unity and diversity of life.

Unity and Diversity

Evolution is a unifying theme of biology because it accounts for both the diversity and the similarities, or the unity, of life. As you study biology, you will see time after time that organisms are related to one another. When you read about cells and genetics, you will see that all organisms have similar cell structures and chemical processes. These shared characteristics result from a common evolutionary descent.

Adaptation

One way evolution occurs is through natural selection of adaptations. In natural selection, a genetic, or inherited, trait helps some individuals of a species survive and reproduce more successfully than other individuals in a particular environment. An inherited trait that gives an advantage to individual organisms and is passed on to future generations is an adaptation
