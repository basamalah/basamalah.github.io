4. What are the various form of office correspondence?


What are the various form of office correspondence?

Business correspondence is a means of written communication that is used for business purposes. It is typically performed between organisations, inside the organisation, or between customers and the organization. When used to represent an organization or for the intent of a corporation, e-mail may be viewed as a means of business communication.

Written communication is important in a business for a variety of reasons. It acts as a structured means of sharing knowledge while retaining professional relationships between organisations, staff and customers. It can also act as a potential guideline for the information to be shared.

Forms of office correspondence

1.Business letter - Business letters typically include critical problems that the organization desperately needs. There are many ways to write a business message.

2. Business memo - Business memo is an announcement that is being relayed from management to its staff. It acts as a reminder of laws or something that needs urgent attention and needs to be relayed to a wide audience.

3. Business email - This is another type of letter, except it is written electronically. That is an immediate transfer of a message from one individual or company to another person or company.

4. Business voice mail -There are calls made between the company's management.

 

5 most common types of business correspondence

1. Internal Correspondence

Internal correspondence is written contact between the same organization's staff, offices and branches. Internal mail may be formal or less formal. Internal regular communications, such as fast orders between the supervisor and the workers, are typically less formal and they may be usually e-mailed.

2. External Correspondence

External contact between the various entities or an entity and its individual customers takes place. This is a way of writing from a business to someone that don't belong to the company.

3. Sales Correspondence

Compliance with sales applies to the communications related to sales. It does not only include the selling of a product or service, but also other promotional practices. Includes publicity documents, bid letters, coupon letters, promotional plans, bills, account records, sales accounts, order confirmation, payment requests, authorisation letters, letters of compilation, and so on.

4. Personalized Correspondence

Personal and emotional considerations were involved in personal communications. This form of correspondence can also be used for commercial purposes, although it is classified as 'personalized.

 

5. Circulars

Circulars are notices communicated inside the company to a wide variety of individuals. It is sometimes called office orders or notes. General notifications are also shared (for example, adjustments to contact information, details on shareholders meetings, guidance on particular protocols, etc.) through circulars

28. what type of chart can be used to show the offices together with the corresponding personnel that function in a school​


Answer:

Organizational charts (or hierarchy charts) are the graphical representation of an organization’s structure. Its purpose is to illustrate the reporting relationships and chains of command within the organization. Employee names and titles and/or job positions are generally depicted in boxes or circles with lines linking them to other employees and departments. By looking at the company organizational chart, people can gain a quick understanding of how the organization is designed, its number of levels, and where each employee fits into the organization.
