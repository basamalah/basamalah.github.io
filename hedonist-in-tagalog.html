Hedonistic Theories of Art

According to one kind of theory, the function of art is to produce just one kind of effect upon its audience: Pleasure . It may also inform or instruct, represent or express, but first and foremost it must please. The more pleasure it gives, the better the art. As a theory of value, hedonism states that all and only pleasure is intrinsically valuable and all and only pain is intrinsically not valuable. Hedonists usually define pleasure and pain broadly, such that both physical and mental phenomena are included.

12. this hedonistic concept of the good life arguably dominant in Western culture?​


The hedonistic concept of the good life, which places pleasure and personal enjoyment at the center of one's goals and values, has certainly been influential in Western culture throughout history. Ancient Greek philosophers such as Epicurus and later philosophers such as Jeremy Bentham and John Stuart Mill developed ethical systems that emphasized the pursuit of pleasure as the key to a fulfilling life. This idea has continued to be popularized in literature, art, and popular culture, with a focus on individual gratification and material success.

However, it's important to note that Western culture is not monolithic, and there have been and continue to be many other philosophical, religious, and cultural traditions that have emphasized different values and goals. For example, Judeo-Christian and Islamic traditions often emphasize the importance of self-restraint and the pursuit of virtue, while Eastern traditions such as Buddhism and Taoism have a more contemplative and inward-facing approach to the good life.

Moreover, even within the Western tradition, there have been many thinkers who have critiqued the hedonistic concept of the good life and argued for alternative approaches. For example, Aristotle's concept of eudaimonia (flourishing) emphasizes the importance of living a life of moral and intellectual virtue, rather than seeking pleasure for its own sake.

Overall, while the hedonistic concept of the good life has certainly been influential in Western culture, it is not the only or even the dominant approach to the good life, and there have always been alternative perspectives and critiques.

17. Why do hedonist focus on the attainment of pleasure


Answer:

Hedonists focus on the attainment of pleasure because they believe that pleasure is the ultimate goal in life. Hedonism is a philosophical theory that suggests that the pursuit of pleasure and the avoidance of pain are the only intrinsic goods in life. According to this view, pleasure is the ultimate aim of human existence and should be sought after above all else.

Hedonists believe that pleasure can take many forms, including physical, emotional, and intellectual pleasure. They believe that pleasure is the highest good because it is something that everyone desires and it is something that can be enjoyed for its own sake.
