5. what are the comparisons of karl marx and david harvey theory?​


Answer:

Karl Marx and David Harvey are both known for their contributions to Marxist theory and have both offered critiques of capitalism. However, there are also some differences between their theories.

Explanation:

One key difference is their approach to historical materialism. Marx argued that historical materialism is the fundamental concept that explains the development of society and that it is driven by the contradictions between the forces of production and the social relations of production. Harvey, on the other hand, argues that historical materialism is not enough to explain the complexities of social change and that other factors, such as political struggles, cultural changes, and ideological shifts, also play important roles.

Another difference is their views on capitalism. Marx famously described capitalism as a system of exploitation that is inherently unstable and will eventually be overthrown by the working class. Harvey, while agreeing with Marx's critique of capitalism, argues that capitalism is also highly adaptive and resilient, and that it has been able to survive and adapt to crises by shifting its focus to new areas of growth and expanding its reach into new markets.

Despite these differences, both Marx and Harvey share a common commitment to the idea that capitalism is a fundamentally unjust and unequal system that perpetuates exploitation and alienation. They both emphasize the importance of collective action and social movements in challenging and ultimately overcoming capitalism.
