4. what is the meaning of incineration in science?


Incineration is a waste treatment process that involves the combustion of organic substances contained in waste materials. Incineration and other high-temperature waste treatment systems are described as "thermal treatment". Incineration of waste materials converts the waste into ash, flue gas, and heat.

5. What principle are we using if we see marine fossils in Ohio and assume that Ohio was once under ocean water?​


Answer:

What principle are we using if we see marine fossils in Ohio and assume that Ohio was once under ocean water? The principle of uniformitarianism. Since marine animals live in the sea today, we would assume they also did in the past. And since we found marine fossils in Ohio, we can assume that Ohio was once underwater.

20. Differentiate the principles of operation for various marine propulsion plans


Answer:

such as inboard, outboard, water jet, and sail

Inboard: Inboard marine propulsion is a system which utilizes an engine, usually a diesel or gasoline motor, arranged within the hull of a boat to provide propulsion.

Outboard: Outboard marine propulsion is a form of propulsion which utilizes an engine, typically a gasoline or diesel motor, attached outside of the hull on the transom of the boat in order to provide propulsion.

Water Jet: Water Jet marine propulsion is a system which utilizes an engine, usually a diesel or gasoline motor, to power a pump which then creates a jet of water, in order to propel the boat forward.

Sail: Sail marine propulsion is a system which utilizes the wind in order to move a boat. This is achieved through the use of sails, attached to the boat, which catches the wind and directs it in a particular direction, allowing the boat to move in the opposite direction.
