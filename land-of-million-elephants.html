19. Based on the information in the article, describe how an elephant seal's movements are different on land than in water.​


Answer:

Elephant seals are large marine mammals that spend most of their time in the ocean. In water, they are agile swimmers and use their powerful flippers to navigate through the water and catch prey. Their streamlined bodies and ability to control their buoyancy allow them to dive to great depths and stay underwater for long periods.

On land, however, elephant seals are not as graceful. They move by flopping and sliding on their bellies, using their flippers and body weight to propel themselves forward. This is because their bodies are adapted for life in the water, and their flippers are not designed for supporting their weight on land. Additionally, their large size and thick blubber layer make them less agile and more cumbersome on land.

Overall, the movements of elephant seals on land are much different than in water, as they have adapted to their respective environments in different ways.
