Advantages and Disadvantages of the Market Approach

All methods under the Market Approach come with their own advantages and disadvantages. However, as a whole, the Market Approach offers the following benefits and weaknesses:

Advantages

•It is straightforward and involves simple calculations.

•It uses data that is real and public.

•It is not dependent on subjective forecasts.

Disadvantages

•It is difficult to identify transactions or companies that are comparable. There is usually a lack of a sufficient number of comparable companies or transactions.

•It is less flexible compared to other methods.

•The method raises questions on how much data is available and how good the data is.

Key Uses of the Market Approach

Determining the value of your business using the market approach is particularly suitable in the following situations:

When you want to set the offer price or asking price for a business purchase.

When there is a need for you to defend the valuation of your business before the tax authorities or in a legal dispute.

When you want to justify the value of your business when there is a dispute such as a buyout or partner disagreements.

14. Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of process layout.​


DIRECTION

Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of process layout.

ANSWER

Advantages of process layout

The important advantages offered by a process layout are as follows:

1. Flexibility

It is more flexible when compared to a product layout. Changes in operations as well as their order can be made without disturbing the existing layout. Any new operation can be added.

2. Lower investment

General purpose machines which are usually of low cost are used. Duplication of machinery is avoided. Further general purpose machines do not become obsolete as rapidly as special purpose machines.

3. No stoppage of production

In case of breakdown of any machine, the whole process does not come to a standstill. Work of the machine which suffers from breakdown can be transferred to the other machines.

4. Scope for expansion

Different capacity lines can be expanded under this type of layout. New machines and labor can be added without upsetting the existing order of arrangement.

5. Full utilization of equipment

Process layout facilitates full utilization of equipment. General purpose machines are used in each department which can perform a variety of jobs. There is no need to provide a separate machine for each product line.

6. Better supervision

Because of specialization in operation, an efficient and Better supervision is possible.

====================================

Disadvantages of process layout

The following are the disadvantages of process layout:

1. Inefficient material handling

Materials have to be carried forward and backward quite frequently. Mechanization of material handling becomes difficult. Fixed path material handling equipment like conveyor belts, chutes etc cannot be used and cost of material handling is quite.

2. High space requirement

Space requirement is more than product layout. More storage space is to be provided around machines for waiting material to be processed.

3. High investment in inventory

Due to the lack of continuous flow of production there is high in-process inventory. Frequently materials have to be carried back and forth. This results in delays and therefore the investment in inventory is high.

4. High supervision cost

Cost of supervision is high because the number of employees per supervisor is less resulting in reduced span of control. Further, the work is to be checked after each operation.

5. Longer production time

Time required for production is more in the case of product layout.

6. Skilled labor required

Skilled labor needs to be employed to perform variety of operations in general purpose machines.

23. What are some of the advantages and disadvantages of the transnational approach advocated by Bartlett and Ghoshal?​


Advantages:

1. Transnational approach allows companies to take advantage of global opportunities and resources. Companies can access new markets, tap into new sources of capital, and benefit from the expertise of different cultures.

2. Companies can also benefit from economies of scale by leveraging their global presence to reduce costs and increase efficiency.

3. The transnational approach also encourages innovation and creativity by allowing companies to draw on the best practices from different countries and cultures.

4. Finally, the transnational approach allows companies to develop a competitive edge in the global marketplace by taking advantage of different markets and resources around the world.

Disadvantages:

1. The transnational approach requires significant investments in infrastructure, personnel, and technology in order to be successful. This can be costly for companies that are not prepared for such an undertaking.

2. The transnational approach also requires a high degree of coordination between different departments and divisions within a company in order to ensure that all operations are running smoothly across multiple countries or regions. This can be difficult to achieve without proper planning and communication between departments or divisions.

3. Finally, the transnational approach may lead to cultural clashes between employees from different countries or regions due to differences in language, customs, values, etc., which can lead to misunderstandings or conflicts within the organization if not properly managed or addressed.

A play-based approach in early childhood education emphasizes the importance of learning through exploration, creativity, and self-directed play, while a desk policy approach emphasizes a more structured approach to teaching and learning, with a focus on completing academic tasks and sitting at desks for longer periods.

Advantages of Play-Based Approach:

Enhances creativity and imagination.

Develops problem-solving and critical thinking skills.

Encourages socialization and communication skills.

Provides opportunities for physical development.

Develops self-regulation and self-confidence.

Disadvantage of Play-Based Approach:

Can be difficult to measure or quantify learning outcomes.

May not provide enough structure or discipline for some children.

May not prepare children for more formal educational settings.

Advantages of Desk Policy Approach:

Provides structure and discipline.

Helps children develop early academic skills.

Prepares children for more formal educational settings.

Encourages independence and self-directed learning.

Disadvantages of Desk Policy Approach:

Can be less engaging and less motivating for some children.

May not provide opportunities for physical development and creative exploration.

May not address individual learning styles or needs.

Practical Examples

Play-Based Approach:

Children are encouraged to engage in imaginative play, such as pretending to be doctors, firefighters, or teachers.

Children are given open-ended materials, such as blocks, paints, and clay, to create and explore.

Children are allowed to choose their own activities and playmates, with minimal adult guidance or intervention.

Desk Policy Approach:

Children are expected to sit at desks for longer periods, completing academic tasks such as writing, reading, and math worksheets.

Children are provided with structured learning activities, such as teacher-directed lessons and group work.

Children are required to follow rules and routines, such as lining up quietly or raising their hands to speak.

30. The advantages and disadvantages when teaching smaw students​


Answer:

Teaching students Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW), also known as Stick Welding, has both advantages and disadvantages. Here are some of them:

Advantages:

Versatility: SMAW can be used to weld a variety of metals and alloys, including steel, cast iron, and stainless steel. This makes it a versatile process that can be used in a variety of industries.

Accessibility: SMAW is a widely used welding process, and equipment and consumables are readily available. This makes it easier and more affordable to teach SMAW compared to other welding processes that require more specialized equipment and consumables.

Portability: SMAW equipment is generally portable, and the process can be used in a variety of settings, including outdoor and remote locations. This makes it ideal for teaching in vocational schools or community colleges that may not have a dedicated welding shop.

Low-cost: Compared to other welding processes, SMAW has relatively low setup and operating costs. This makes it a more affordable option for students who are just starting out in welding.

Disadvantages:

Safety concerns: SMAW produces a lot of heat and requires the use of protective gear, including helmets, gloves, and clothing, to prevent burns and other injuries. This can be intimidating for some students, and proper safety training is essential.

Difficulty: SMAW requires a high degree of skill and precision to produce quality welds. This can be challenging for some students, particularly those who are new to welding.

Limited control: SMAW produces a lot of spatter and slag, which can make it difficult to see the weld pool and control the welding process. This can make it challenging for students to produce quality welds.

Environmental concerns: SMAW produces a lot of smoke and fumes, which can be harmful to the environment and human health. Proper ventilation and safety precautions are necessary to minimize these risks.
