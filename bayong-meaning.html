4. What are the similarities of Tingkop and Bayong bags?​


Short answer: Tingkop and Bayong are the same, but with a different price.

Longer answer: If you’re in the middle of a road trip through a developing country or in an emergency situation and want to carry your camera safely, the Tingkop bag (aka as a backpack, duffel bag, day bag or handbag) or Bayong bag (aka as a fanny pack) will be your lifesaver.

With the Tingkop or Bayong bags, you’ll have all of the comfort of a full-size backpack but with the benefits of a fanny pack: it has multiple pockets (with enough space for your digital camera), it’s light-weight (you can carry it around for a long period of time), and you can wear it anywhere (no need for a bulky, heavy backpack or for wearing an under- or over-shirt).

I also like how the Tingkop or Bayong bag has a belt loop attached, which helps you attach the strap to it more securely.

One big difference between Tingkop and Bayong bags is that Tingkop bags come with one strap to go around your body (Tingkop, fanny pack) while Bayong bags come with two straps (Bayong, duffel bag or day bag).

Tingkop and Bayong are the same, but with a different price

Tingkop and Bayong bags are both manufactured in Malaysia.

6. what is materials features of bayong​


Answer:

The leaves used for making the bayong vary but the traditional bayong is made from buri leaves in the Visayas and pandan leaves in Luzon. Abaca, bacbac, karagumoy, sabutan, romblon and tikog are among other organic materials used in making the bayong all of which are derived from plants native to the Philippines
