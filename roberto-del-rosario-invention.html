5. Sino si Robert del rosario? Paano sya produktibo at nag tagumpay?


Answer:

Si Roberto L. del Rosario ay isang kilalang Pilipinong imbentor ng Sing-Along System na kilala bilang Minus-One na kalaunan ay kilala bilang Karaoke. Naipaglaban niya sa Kongreso ng Pilipinas ang pagpasa ng panukala para sa insentibo sa mga imbensiyon ng mga Pilipino na kalaunan ay naging batas RA 7459 o Inventors and Invention Incentives Act noong 28 Abril 1992. Siya ay nahalal bilang miyembro ng Executive Board ng International Federation of Inventors Association (IFIA). Siya ay ginawaran ng World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO) Gold Medal sa kanyang natatanging imbensiyon noong 1985.

Sa edad na pito ay mahusay na siyang magpiyano at lalo pang nahasa nang maging katulong sa pagawaan ng piano ng kanyang tiyuhin na Trebel. Ang nakita niyang hirap sa pagtotono ng piano eng nagbunsod sa kanya upang maimbento ang Piano Tuner's Guide at Piano Keyboard Stressing Device. Taong 1972, nang makilala si Roberto dahil sa kanyang One-Man Band (OMB) na nakakatugtog ng buong orkestra (kumpletong wind, string at brass instruments) sa tugtog ng chacha, boogie, disco, swing, tango at marami pang iba.

15. Ano ang ginampanan ni Rafael del rosario sa kasaysyan ng Angeles city


Rafael D. Del Rosario is a senior associate who is working with the Banking & Finance, and Labor & Immigration departments. His practice focuses on banking and finance, corporate, labor, and litigation.

 

Raffy advises clients in a broad range of issues arising from debt and equity financing structures, project financing and refinancing, security arrangements for single-lender and syndicated bank facilities, sundry regulatory compliance, and banking capital recognition for domestic and international banks. He also regularly assists foreign companies in resolving issues regarding doing business in the Philippines. This includes establishing Philippine presence and providing counsel to a legal reform initiative designed to benefit the banking and financial services sector.

 

Raffy’s labor and employment work runs the gamut from representing clients in labor disputes before the National Labor Relations Commission to structuring employment transition programs resulting from corporate mergers and acquisitions, consolidations, and dissolutions. He also represents clients in proceedings before the Court of Tax Appeals, Court of Appeals, and Supreme Court in a broad range of legal matters from tax amnesty to intra-corporate disputes.

 

Raffy was previously assigned by the firm on legal consultancies with a leading telecommunications company in the Philippines and a multilateral financial institution headquartered in Manila.

 

Before joining the firm, Raffy was court attorney and assistant to the Presiding Justice of the Court of Appeals and a political science lecturer at De La Salle University.

 

Raffy received his bachelor’s degree in Management from the Ateneo de Manila University in 2004 and his juris doctor degree from the same university in 2009. He was admitted to the Philippine bar in 2010 and joined the firm in 2011.
