6. kadayawan festival musical instruments.at costume​


Answer:

The main event is the “Indak-Indak sa Kadalanan” roughly means Street Dancing. It is performed by different tribes or schools all over Mindanao showing unique dance choreographies foretelling stories of the Mindanao’s natives and the bountiful harvest including some superstitious beliefs of the Filipinos. Every Contestants/Participants were very competitive in their own way, dancing under the heat of the sun without showing any signs of tiredness. You would be amazed of their unique, bright, and colorful costumes showing the beauty of what tribe they represent. Loud drum beats and other musical instruments inspired by ethnic music will be heard from one street to the other. Traffic were re-routed for the purpose of the said “Indak-Indak sa Kadalanan” and also cash prizes and awards are given to all the winning groups

13. differentiate the ati-atihan and kadayawan festival​


Answer:

The Ati-Atihan Festival is a Philippine festival held annually in January in honor of the Santo Niño (Holy Child or Infant Jesus) in several towns of the province of Aklan, Panay Island. The biggest celebration is held during the third Sunday of January in the town of Kalibo, the province’s capital. The name Ati-Atihan means "to imitate Ati", the local name of the Aeta people, the first settlers of Panay Island and other parts of the archipelago. The festivity was originally a pagan celebration to commemorate the Barter of Panay, where the Aeta accepted gifts from Bornean chieftains called Datu, who fled with their families to escape a tyrannical ruler, in exchange for being allowed to dwell in the Aeta’s lands. They celebrated with dancing and music, with the Borneans having painted their bodies with soot to show their gratefulness and camaraderie with the Aeta who had dark skin. Later on, the festivity was given a different meaning by the church by celebrating the acceptance of Christianity, as symbolized by carrying an image of the Holy Child or Infant Jesus during the procession.

The Kadayawan Festival is an annual festival in the city of Davao in the Philippines. Its name derives from the friendly greeting "Madayaw", from the Dabawenyo word meaning good, valuable, superior or beautiful.

14. ano ang kadayawan festival​


Kadayawän Festival

Ang Kadayawän Festival ay isang pangunahing pagdiriwang at selebrasyon sa Davao City, Philippines. Ito ay isang linggong selebrasyon na ginaganap tuwing buwan ng Agosto, at ito ay isa sa mga pinakapopular na festival sa Pilipinas.

Ang pangunahing layunin ng Kadayawän Festival ay upang ipagdiwang ang kabutihan ng kalikasan, ang uri ng mga mamamayan ng Davao, at ang kanilang mga kultura at tradisyon. Ito ay nagpapakita ng mga uri ng bulaklak, prutas, at mga pampublikong kagamitan na magagamit sa mga selebrasyon at pagdiriwang.Ang Kadayawän Festival ay naglalaman ng iba't ibang mga palabas, parada, mga sayawän, at mga pagkain na nagpapakita ng mga tradisyunal na mga tula, awit, at mga sayaw ng mga tribo sa Davao City. Ito ay nagsisilbing pagkakataon upang ipakita ang mayamang kultura ng mga tao sa rehiyon at upang itaguyod ang pagkakaisa sa pagitan ng mga iba't ibang tribo at komunidad sa lugar.

Sa kabuuan, ang Kadayawän Festival ay isang makulay, masaya, at makabuluhan na pagdiriwang na nagpapakita ng mga kagandahan ng kalikasan, mga kultura, at mga tradisyon ng mga tao sa Davao City.
