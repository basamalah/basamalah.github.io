3. classification of bok choy​


Answer:

Bok choy, botanically classified as Brassica rapa var. chinensis, is a variety of non-heading Chinese cabbage that is a member of the Brassicaceae or mustard family.

Bok Choy, also called white Chinese cabbage, belongs to the leafy vegetable pak choi family of Chinese brassicas. Bok Choy has white or green, thick, crunchy stems with light to dark green wide leaves. Size can vary from 4-12 inches tall! All parts, stems and leaves, are eaten.

30. characteristic of bok in the story of keesh


In the story, Story of Keesh, it was narrated that Keesh was Bok's and Ikeega's son. Ikeega lives alone with Keesh because Bok has already died. The reason he died was because he had brought himself to hunt for the people because the people were growing hungry. Although he died, he was able to kill the bear and with it, the people had something to eat. Bok was appreciated and honored but as time passed by the people forgot about his great deed. It can be concluded from this detail that Bok is an honorable person willing to risk his safety for the welfare of many.
