14. How to create a pie chart in thesis


Assuming you are not aware that you can create a pie chart using Excel.

Steps:
1)  Group your data (quantitative data) into categories.
     Example: If your thesis is about average monthly household expenses of sample data of family below poverty threshold in Malabon (in Pesos):
     Food expenses = 2,500
     Transportation = 2,000
     Rent = 800
     Others = 1,200

2)  Find the total of the expenses.
     In the example above:  6,500

3)  Divide each category by the total, to get the value in decimal:
     Food = 2,500 ÷ 6,500 = 0.39 
     Transportation = 2,000 ÷ 6,500 = 0.31
     REnt = 800 ÷ 6,500 = 0.12
     Others = 1,200 ÷ 6,500 = 0.18
    
     The total should be 1.00.

4)  The pie chart is a circle, and it has a full 360 degrees full/complete angle.
     Multiply each decimal number per category by 360 to get the measurement        of each angle that correspond to each category:
     In example:
     Food = 0.39 × 360 = 140.4 or  140 degrees
     Transportation = 0.31 × 360 =  111.6 or 112 degrees
     Rent = 0.12 x 360 = 43.2 degrees or 43 degrees
     Others = 64.8 degrees  or 65 degrees

5)  Draw a draft using the angle measurements.  Then use Excel application.  by entering the values needed.

29. how do we interpret data using a pie chart​


Answer:

Interpret the key results for Pie Chart

When you interpret one pie chart, look for differences in the size of the slices. The size of a slice shows the proportion of observations that are in that group.

When you compare multiple pie charts, look for differences in the size of slices for the same categories in all the pie charts.
