We can use this information to answer various questions related to the probability of finding defective transistors in a randomly chosen carton. Here are a few examples:

What is the probability that a randomly chosen carton contains no defective transistors?

From the information given, we know that 89% of cartons contain no defective transistors. Therefore, the probability of choosing a carton with no defective transistors is 0.89 or 89%.

What is the probability that a randomly chosen carton contains at least one defective transistor?

To find the probability of a carton containing at least one defective transistor, we can subtract the probability of choosing a carton with no defective transistors from 1 (since the probabilities of all possible outcomes must add up to 1).

Probability of choosing a carton with no defective transistors = 0.89

Probability of choosing a carton with at least one defective transistor = 1 - 0.89 = 0.11 or 11%

Therefore, the probability of choosing a carton with at least one defective transistor is 0.11 or 11%.

What is the expected number of defective transistors in a randomly chosen carton?

To find the expected number of defective transistors, we need to multiply the probability of each possible outcome (i.e., the number of defective transistors in a carton) by the corresponding number of defective transistors, and then add up these products.

Expected number of defective transistors = (0 x 0.89) + (1 x 0.04) + (2 x 0.03) + (3 x 0.02) + (4 x 0.02)

= 0 + 0.04 + 0.06 + 0.06 + 0.08

= 0.24

Therefore, the expected number of defective transistors in a randomly chosen carton is 0.24.

13. Why npn-transistor is preferred over pnp-transistors?


Answer:

The majority charge carriers in an NPN transistor are electrons and the majority carriers in a PNP transistor are holes. The electrons have better mobility than holes. Therefore, NPN transistors are preferred over PNP transistors.
