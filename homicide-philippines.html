2. simulated interview of homicide​


Answer:

Homicide is the act of one human killing another.[1] A homicide requires only a volitional act by another person that results in death, and thus a homicide may result from accidental, reckless, or negligent acts even if there is no intent to cause harm.[2] Homicides can be divided into many overlapping legal categories, including murder, manslaughter, justifiable homicide, killing in war (either following the laws of war or as a war crime), euthanasia, and capital punishment, depending on the circumstances of the death. These different types of homicides are often treated very differently in human societies; some are considered crimes, while others are permitted or even ordered by the legal system.

Ang 'evident premeditation' o ang maliwanag na paghahanda sa pagpatay ay isa sa tinitignan upang masabing murder ang isang krimen. Ang pagpaplano, pagdedesisyon sa pagpatay, at ang mismong aksiyon ng pagpatay ay ang tatlong kinikilalang panahon.

Sa ilalim ng Article 248 sa Revised Penal Code, Nagiging murder ang isang kaso sa ilalim ng Artikulo 248 sa Revised Penal Code kung:

mayroong isang tahasang pagpapakita ng pagtataksil o treachery

ginawa ang pagpatay dahil o para sa pabuya at premyo

ang isang tao gamit ang kahit na anong sasakyan  ay nanunog, nambomba, nanlunod, nanlason, pumatay

ang pagpatay ay isinagawa sa kasagsagan ng kalamidad tulad ng bagyo, lindol at iba pa

ang paghahanda sa pagpatay ay klaro

may sobrang pagpapahirap na ginawa sa biktima bago patayin

Sa ngayon, ang reclusion perpetua ang pinakamataas na parusa sa sinumang mapapatunayan na nagkasala ng murder. 19. Reckless imprudence resulting to homicide elements


Reckless impruduce must be proved to state that reckless impruduce resulting to homiced elements.

Explanation:

Reckless imprudence consists of voluntarily, but without malicious intent, performing or failing to perform an act that results in material loss due to an unforgivable lack of precautions on the part of the person committing or failing to perform the act, taking into account the occupation or occupation, degree of intelligence, physical condition and other circumstances regarding people, times and places.

The prosecution must demonstrate a direct causal relationship between the driver's negligence and the injury suffered to support the allegation for reckless imprudence resulting to homicide.

According to a G.R. No. 210816. December 10, 2018 there are several elements of reckless imprudence are :

That the offender does or fails to do an actThat the doing or the failure to do that act is voluntaryThat it be without maliceThat material damage results from the reckless imprudenceThat there is inexcusable lack of precaution on the part of the offender, taking into consideration his employment or occupation, degree of intelligence, physical condition, and other circumstances regarding persons, time, and place.

29. ways to prevent homicide​


Answer:

Preventing homicide is a complex issue that requires a multi-faceted approach. Here are some ways that can help prevent homicide:

1. Gun control: A significant number of homicides are committed with firearms. Implementing stricter gun control measures, such as background checks, waiting periods, and limiting access to assault weapons, can help reduce the number of homicides.

2. Addressing poverty and inequality: Homicide rates are often higher in areas with high levels of poverty and inequality. Addressing these issues through policies that promote economic growth and social mobility can help reduce the incidence of homicide.

3. Investing in education: Education is a powerful tool for preventing homicide. Providing access to quality education can help individuals develop critical thinking skills, empathy, and conflict resolution skills.

4. Addressing mental health issues: Many homicides are committed by individuals with mental health issues. Improving access to mental health services and reducing the stigma associated with seeking help can help prevent homicides.

5. Strengthening law enforcement: Effective law enforcement is essential for preventing homicides. This includes increasing the number of police officers, improving training, and using data and technology to target high-risk areas.

6. Promoting community involvement: Encouraging community involvement and engagement can help prevent homicides. This includes promoting neighborhood watch programs, community policing, and other initiatives that bring community members together to address crime and violence.

7. Addressing domestic violence: Domestic violence is a significant contributor to homicides. Implementing policies and programs that address domestic violence, such as restraining orders and counseling services, can help prevent homicides.

In conclusion, preventing homicides requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the root causes of violence. By implementing a combination of policies and programs that address poverty, inequality, mental health, and access to firearms, we can work towards reducing the incidence of homicide and creating safer communities.
