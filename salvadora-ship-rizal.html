This riddle is usually recited orally as to confuse the guesser of what is really the answer since one can play with the intonation of the last line making it ambiguously asking or revealing. In writing, you may intentionally put a period at the end, or just simply write it as a question. Either way is acceptable though some would argue once the answer is revealed.

15. There are two ships that are sailing form General Santos City. Ship A is sailing east,and ship B is sailing 43° south of east. after an hour, ship A has travelled 115 km and ship B has travelled 98 km how far are the two ships? ​


Given:

θ = 43° south of east

OA = 115 km

OB = 98 km

time = 1 hour

Formula:

Use cosine law

[tex] {AB}^{2} = {OA}^{2} + {OB}^{2} - 2(OA)(OB) \cos(θ) [/tex]

Solution:

[tex] {AB}^{2} = {(115)}^{2} + {(98)}^{2} - 2(115)(98) \cos(43) [/tex]

[tex] {AB}^{2} = 6344.29[/tex]

[tex]AB = \sqrt{6344.29} [/tex]

Final answer:

[tex]AB = 79.65 \: km[/tex]
