It is the three elements make up augmented reality (AR): a fusion of the digital and physical worlds, in-the-moment interactions, and precise 3D object recognition.

Answer:

A characteristic of augmented reality is that it involves the superimposition of digital information or content on top of the real world. Augmented reality technology uses sensors and algorithms to detect and track the physical environment, and then adds virtual elements to the real-world view. This creates a composite, enhanced view of the world that combines real and virtual elements. This allows users to interact with the digital content in a more immersive and engaging way than is possible with traditional forms of media. Other characteristics of augmented reality include the ability to manipulate and interact with the digital content, and the ability to share and collaborate with others in real time.
