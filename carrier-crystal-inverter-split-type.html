13. type of mirror that show an inverted image of you


ANSWER: Concave Mirror

A concave mirror can produce a real inverted image whose size depends on screen placement. If the real image and object are the same distance from the concave mirror, then they are both the same size. But if the real image is farther than the object is from the mirror, then the image is larger. So it follows that if the real image is closer to the mirror than the object then it would be smaller. Also note that there is no real image with a concave mirror if you place the object inside the focal length of mirror, in which case the image in virtual, upright, and bigger.

22. karen is a carrier of heterozygous blood type b while kevin is a carrier of heterozygous blood type a. karen is expected to give birth to their first child on the third week of the month. could their child possibly inherit blood type 0? ​


Karen and Kevin's children have blood type O with a 25% chance.

The blood group gene has two alleles of the gene. If a person has homozygous blood type A, it means that the allele of the blood group gene is AA, and if someone has heterozygous blood type, it means that the allele of the blood group gene is AO.

people with heterozygous genotype AO will have blood type A because the O allele has a recessive trait and the A allele has a dominant trait. Homozygous blood type B has a BB genotype, while heterozygous blood group B has a BO genotype where B also has the dominant trait.

The pair had offspring with genotypes AB, BO, AO, and OO with a ratio of 1:1:1:1, respectively.

The AO genotype means that the child will have a heterozygous blood type A, because A is dominant over the recessive O. The BO genotype means that the child will have a heterozygous blood type B, because B is dominant over O.

Genotype AB means the child will have blood type AB. The emergence of blood type AB is a deviation from Mendel's law called codominance. This is because the A and B alleles are both dominant and do not want to give in to each other so that both alleles are expressed simultaneously into AB blood group. So the pair of women with blood type A heterozygous and men with blood type B heterozygous have a 25% chance of having children with blood type A, 25% blood type B, 25% blood type AB, and 25% blood type O.
