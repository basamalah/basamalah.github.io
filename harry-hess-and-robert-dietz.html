5. Who were the two scientists who proposed the theory of seafloor spreading in the early 1960s? a. Charles Darwin and James Hutton b. Harry Hess and Robert Dietz c. John Butler and Arthur Smite d. f. Vine and d. Mathews


Before we answer the question, “Who were the two scientists who proposed the theory of seafloor spreading in the early 1960s?

Charles Darwin and James Hutton Harry Hess and Robert Dietz John Butler and Arthur Smite F. Vine and D. Matthews

We must first briefly discuss what the theory of seafloor spreading is. This theory indicates that the ocean floor is spreading as hot magma rises at the rift or opening in the mid-ocean ridge. The magma then cools down and becomes the new seafloor. Meanwhile, the old sea floor will then be destroyed in the so-called subduction zone and melt inside the earth’s mantle. Although we do not see this happen, the existence and the age of rocks and magnetic strips on the ocean floor support the theory. To note, relatively young rocks can be found at the mid-ocean ridge. Another definition for the mid-ocean ridge can be found here: https://brainly.ph/question/391723.

Going back to the question, the answer is Harry Hess and Robert Dietz. Dietz and Hess’ theory are related to the plate tectonics and the continental drift theories (to know more about plate tectonics, plate boundaries, and continental drift theory, you may click these links: https://brainly.ph/question/585940 and https://brainly.ph/question/369575).  Try to find out more about plate tectonics by searching our Search Bar with the following keywords “plate tectonics test questions and answers” or “20 questions plate tectonics.”

In the 1960s, the two scientists presented an alternative to the Continental Drift Theory, which supposes that continents moved through unmoving oceans and that larger continents broke through the oceanic crust. While on the other hand, the theory of sea floor spreading shows that the ocean is the actual site of tectonic activities. As new seafloor emerges, new bodies of water also emerge. For example, the Red Sea was created after the African and Arabian plates diverged. On one hand, the East Pacific Rise, which moves more than 14 centimeters every year, is pulling the continents of Australia, South America and the Antarctica away from each other.
