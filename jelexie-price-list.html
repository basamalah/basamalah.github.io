10. how to find list price if the only given is the discount rate and net price​


[tex]QUESTION:[/tex]

how to find list price if the only given is the discount rate and net price

[tex]ANSWER:[/tex]

Subtract the discount percentage from 100%. Multiply the difference by the original price to get the sale price. For example, given a 10% discount applied to a list price of $50.00, subtract 10% from 100% = 90%. Multiply the list price $50.00 by 90% = $45.00, which is the sale price.

29. when is a price list allowed?​


Answer:

A price list is generally allowed in business transactions as long as it is transparent, accurate, and does not violate any applicable laws or regulations. In some industries, such as healthcare, the prices of goods and services must be disclosed to patients or customers in advance to ensure transparency and prevent surprise billing. Similarly, some governments require businesses to provide consumers with a price list or menu of services offered to protect them from unfair pricing practices. In general, a price list can be a useful tool for businesses to provide information to customers and facilitate transactions, as long as it is used in a fair and ethical manner.
