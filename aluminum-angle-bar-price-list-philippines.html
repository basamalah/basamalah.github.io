                324 =  k 1/2 * 12
                  k   = 324/6  =  54
       then  substitute it into the new equation
              P  =     54 *2/3*10 =   360 dollarsLet:
Constant - k (Unit price per ft²)
P - price
r - radius
L - length

Variation equation:

P = k r L

Find the constant:
(Convert inch to ft. ⇒  6 inch = 0.5 ft)

$324 = k (0.5 ft) (12 ft)
$324 = k (6 ft²)
k = $324/6 ft²
k = $ 54/ft²

If length is 10 ft and radius is 8 inch:
(Convert 8 inch to ft = 2/3 ft)

Price = k r L
Price = ($ 54/ft²) (2/3 ft) (10 ft)

12. An aluminum bar is 12 cm at 20 oC. if it is heated to 90 oC, What is the new length?​


Answer:

The new length of the aluminum is 12.021 cm

Explanation:

In this problem, we will use the concept of thermal expansion to solve the requirement.

Formula to use

ΔL = L₀αΔT

where

ΔL     is the change in length

L₀      is the original length

α       is the linear coefficient of thermal expansion

ΔT     is the change in temperature

Given information

L₀ = 12cm

T₀ = 20°C

T₁ = 90°C

L₁ = ?

Solving the problem

Let us solve the new length using the given formula

ΔL = L₀αΔT

L₁ - L₀ = L₀α(T₁ - T₀)

L₁ - 12cm = 12cm(25 x 10⁻⁶/°C)(90°C - 20°C)

Take note that the linear expansion for aluminum is 25 x 10⁻⁶/°C.

L₁ = 12cm + 12cm(25 x 10⁻⁶/°C)(90°C - 20°C)

L₁ = 12.021 cm

Therefore, the new length of the aluminum is 12.021 cm
