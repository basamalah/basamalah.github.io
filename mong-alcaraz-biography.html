11. what is a BIOGRAPHY? BIOGRAPHY- an account of someone's lifewritten by someone else. write your biography on the space provided.


Answer:

Explanation:

A biography, or simply bio, is a detailed description of a person's life. It involves more than just the basic facts like education, work, relationships, and death; it portrays a person's experience of these life events. Unlike a profile or curriculum vitae (résumé), a biography presents a subject's life story, highlighting various aspects of their life, including intimate details of experience, and may include an analysis of the subject's personality.

Biographical works are usually non-fiction, but fiction can also be used to portray a person's life. One in-depth form of biographical coverage is called legacy writing. Works in diverse media, from literature to film, form the genre known as biography.

An authorized biography is written with the permission, cooperation, and at times, participation of a subject or a subject's heirs. An autobiography is written by the person himself or herself, sometimes with the assistance of a collaborator or ghostwriter.

16. biography of Euclid​


Euclid was a Greek mathematician who lived around 300 BCE. Not much is known about his personal life, but his contributions to the field of mathematics are significant and have had a lasting impact.

Euclid is best known for his work "Elements," which is a compilation of mathematical knowledge from the time, including theories and theorems about geometry, number theory, and algebra. "Elements" consists of 13 books, with each book focusing on a particular aspect of mathematics. The book covers a wide range of topics including plane and solid geometry, proportion and similarity, number theory, and the geometry of conic sections.

Euclid's work had a significant influence on mathematics and science for centuries after his death. His work was translated into Arabic, and then into Latin, which led to the widespread dissemination of his mathematical ideas throughout Europe. The approach to mathematics used in "Elements" was so influential that it remained the primary way of teaching geometry for more than 2,000 years.

In addition to "Elements," Euclid also wrote several other works on mathematics, including "Data," "Optics," and "Phaenomena." However, only "Elements" has survived in its entirety.

Euclid is considered one of the most important mathematicians of all time and is often referred to as the "father of geometry." His ideas and methods continue to be taught and studied by mathematicians and students around the world.


17. biography of anaximander​


Answer:

Anaximander was a pre-Socratic Greek philosopher who lived in Miletus, a city of Ionia. He belonged to the Milesian school and learned the teachings of his master Thales. He succeeded Thales and became the second master of that school where he counted Anaximenes and, arguably, Pythagoras amongst his pupils.

Anaximander was the author of the first surviving lines of Western philosophy. He speculated and argued about “the Boundless” as the origin of all that is. He also worked on the fields of what we now call geography and biology. Moreover, Anaximander was the first speculative astronomer.
