26. It is a method in which figures are manipulated to appear as moving images.A animationB. audioC. puzzleD. sketch​


Answer:

A. Animation

Explanation:

Animation is a technique in which figures are manipulated to appear as moving images. In traditional animation, images are hand-drawn or hand-painted on transparent celluloid sheets to be photographed and displayed on film. Today, most animations are made by computer-generated imagery (CGI). Computer animation can be very detailed 3D animation, while 2D computer animation (which may look like traditional animation) can be used for stylistic reasons, low bandwidth or faster real rendering -time. Other common animation techniques apply a stopping motion technique to two and three-dimensional objects such as cutout paper, puppets or clay figures.

27. main proponent of dialectic method​


Hegel is the main proponent of the dialectic method. The dialectic method is a way of thinking that involves making a thesis, an antithesis, and then a synthesis. The thesis is an idea or proposition. The antithesis is the opposite of the thesis or the negation of the thesis. The synthesis is the resolution of the conflict between the thesis and antithesis, or the Aufhebung.

Hegel's dialectical method was first introduced in his 1807 work The Phenomenology of Spirit. In this work, Hegel sought to explain the nature of human consciousness. He argued that consciousness is not fixed or static, but rather is in a constant state of flux. This flux is caused by the dialectical process, which pits one idea against its opposite. Through this process, a new, more comprehensive idea emerges.

Hegel expanded on Socrates' use of the dialectic method by positing that history progresses through a series of conflicts between opposing forces. Hegel believed that history itself was the result of the dialectical process at work.
