14. Solve the quadratic equation using appropriate method.


APPROPRIATE METHOD TO SOLVE THE QUADRATIC EQUATIONS.

For the quadratic equation [tex]x^{2} -81=0[/tex], both 9 and -9 are the solutions.

How could you solve such a quadratic equation?

A quadratic equation is an equation with a variable to the second power as its highest power term. There are three basic ways to solve the quadratic equations:

to factor the quadratic equationto taking the square rootsto use the quadratic formula

Usually you will not be told which method to use. You will have to make that decision yourself.

However, here are some guidelines to which methods are better in different situations.

Try Factoring first. If the quadratic factors easily, this method is very quick.Look at the side of the equation containing the variable. Is that side a perfect square? If it is, then you can solve the equation by taking the square root of both sides of the equation. Don't forget to include a ± sign in your equation once you have taken the square root. Finally, use the Quadratic Formula. Any quadratic equation can be solved by using the Quadratic Formula.

Let's solve [tex]x^{2} -81=0[/tex]

This equation is factorable, and the side containing the variable is  a perfect square. So, this can be solved by factoring and taking the square roots.

Let’s take the square root in this case since the equation is a perfect square.

First isolate [tex]x^{2}[/tex], add 81 for the both sides of the equation: [tex]x^{2} =81[/tex].Take the square root of both sides of the equation [tex]x=\sqrt{81}[/tex] and [tex]x=-\sqrt{81}[/tex].Simplify: [tex]x=9[/tex] and  [tex]x=-9[/tex].

So, the following values of [tex]x[/tex], satisfy the equation [tex]x^{2} -81=0[/tex]:

9 and -9

When solving quadratic equations by taking square roots, both the positive and negative square roots are solutions to the equation. This is because when we square a solution, the result is always positive.

25. solve the quadratic equation using appropriate method x²-81=0


Answer:

Solve it using the extracting the square root method.

Step-by-step explanation:

x²-81=0

Firstly, transpose the -81 on the other side.

[tex]x^2-81+81=0+81[/tex]

[tex]x^2=81[/tex]

Then, extract the square root on both sides to leave x isolated.

[tex]\sqrt{x^2} =\sqrt{81}[/tex]

Find the square root of 81 which is ±9.

x = ±9 or x=9 & x=-9
