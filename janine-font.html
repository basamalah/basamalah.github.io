19. What font is best suited for the contents of a printed book?A Serif fontB. Sans Serif fontC. Decorative fontD. All of the choices​


Answer:

A. Serif font

Step-by-step explanation:

Serif fonts help with readability, and are therefore preferable in the body of a book. The “serif” is the decorative stroke that finishes each end of a letter (think Times Roman). Serif fonts are easier on the reader's eye than sans-serif fonts; the stroke leads the reader's eye from one letter to the next.

27. Rock, paper, scissors In 31 tries, Eli beat Janine 11 times. What is the probability that Janine won?


The probability that Janine won is ‌20⁄31‌. It can also be expressed as 0.65 or 65%. To find the probability, it is events over outcomes. The number of events to happen is 20 since Janine won 20 times. The total number of possible outcomes is 31 since they had 31 tries.

Finding The Probability

Probability tells the chance of an event to happen. It shows the likelihood of an event to occur. It can be written in three ways: fraction, decimal and percent. Remember to always simplify the fraction, if possible. The formula to find the probability is the one below:

Probability = number of events to happen / total number of possible outcomes
