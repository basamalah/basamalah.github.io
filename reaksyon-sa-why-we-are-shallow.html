4. why do shallow focus earthquakes cause greater damage and deep focus earthquakes​


Explanation:

SHALLOW FOCUS earthquakes are commonly occurring “crustal” earthquakes, caused by faults and movements of the continental plates. These are earthquakes with their focus nearer the surface of the earth. Shallow focus earthquakes are usually of large spread, causing greater damage at the surface or the earth's crust.

28. why do ponds seem to be shallow when in fact they are deep (sorry if this is kinda hard)​


Answer:

Ponds may seem shallow because of the way light reflects off the surface of the water. When light passes through the water, it is refracted and bent, making objects appear closer to the surface than they actually are. Additionally, the clarity of the water can also affect how deep a pond appears. If the water is murky or cloudy, it can be difficult to see the bottom, making the pond seem shallower than it actually is. Finally, the size of the pond can also affect how deep it appears. A large pond may look shallower than a small pond because of its size, even if it is actually deeper.

Ponds can sometimes appear to be shallow when in fact they are deep due to a phenomenon known as refraction. Refraction occurs when light waves pass through a medium of different density, such as air and water.

When light waves pass from air into water, they slow down and bend, causing objects in the water to appear distorted and displaced from their actual position. This is why objects in a pond appear to be closer to the surface than they actually are.

The degree of refraction depends on the angle at which the light waves enter the water, as well as the temperature and clarity of the water. In some cases, the refraction can be so severe that objects in the water appear to be in a completely different location than they actually are.

Therefore, it is important to exercise caution when swimming or diving in ponds, as the actual depth of the water may be different than it appears.
