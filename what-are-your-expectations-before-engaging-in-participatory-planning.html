What could be the implication of Participatory Action Plan?

Participatory planning means involving the entire community in the process of urban or rural planning  to promote community development. It addresses conflicts between conflicting parties with the aim of harmonizing views in a constructive way and promoting ownership by all involved.

Engaging stakeholders in a plan means making sure that it meets their needs and avoids wasting resources. Participation planning also bridges the gap between those who are planning and those who are planning by someone else. participatory planning creates a  sense of shared responsibility and ownership. Conflicts that  inevitably arise for the different interests of the parties can be resolved constructively and the hurdles can be overcome  together.

Participatory planning is a type of  planning that puts the inhabitants at the center of community decision making. This can be done in different ways, but the end result should be the same. The community feels responsible for the process and  results. They see their efforts reflected in  community visions and projects  built for their dedication. Participatory planning addresses some of the common issues that exist  in participating in modern community planning.
