This is an example of ungrouped data.

The formula to get the percentile is P = [tex]\frac{k(n+1)}{100}[/tex]

where k is the percentile you are looking for

          n is the number of shirts (observation)

Therefore

[tex]P_{50} = \frac{50(10+1)}{100} = \frac{50(11)}{100} = \frac{550}{100} = 5.5[/tex]

Your 50th Percentile lies at 5.5th observation

What does that mean?

Let's arrange your data  from least to greatest

100, 110, 170, 180, 200, 200, 210, 250, 250, 300

In this case your going to count how much your percentile is base from your observation an that lies between

100, 110, 170, 180, 200 (5.5 is here) 200, 210, 250, 250, 300

So, the 5th observation is 200

            6th observation is 200

Whether or not we meet in the middle of two (2) prices which are Php 200 and Php 200, we can easily say that:

Base from your data, the 5th and 6th observation is 200. Therefore we can conclude that the average (which refers to P50) of the price of the shirts is Php 200 or the Php 200 peso shirts are the most in sales.

8. The jeepney fare for the first 4 kilometer is P9.00 and an additional P1.00 for every kilometer. Euri will go to Cubao from Antipolo and the distance is about 28 km. How much does he need to pay What is the correct expression to find the total amount of fare? ​


Answer:

The jeepney fare for the first 4 kilometer is P9.00 and an additional P1.00 for every kilometer. Euri will go to Cubao from Antipolo and the distance is about 28 km. How much does he need to pay What is the correct expression to find the total amount of fare? 

Solution:

In 4 kilometers is P9.00 after that 5 to 28km will add additional P1.00

4 (first four km) × 9 (Php of jeepney fare)

= 36 (Php for only 4km)

28 (km distance between Cubao from Antipolo) - 4 (first 4km with only P9.00)

= 24 (km that is left)

24 (km remaining) × 10 (P10.00 for every km)

= 240 (Php)

36 + 240 = 276 (the total amount Euri need to pay)

14. tree diagram there are 6 roads leading from antipolo to cubao and 3 roads leading from cubao to batangas. How many possible routes from antipolo to batangas through cubao


Answer:

18 possible routes

Step-by-step explanation:

In this problem, there are various of ways to solve this. One would be this.

First we label the places as

A = Antipolo

B = Batangas

C= Cubao

When there are x ways to do something and y ways to do another thing dependent to the first event, then we simply multiply these two values. That is xy = (6)(3)=18. Therefore there are 18 possible routes.

ALTERNATIVE EXPLANATION:

From A to C, we have 6 choices.

From C to B, we have 3 choices.

Now we have to find the number of possible routes from A to B but of course passing through C.

x = {1,2,3,4,5,6 }= routes from A to C

y={a,b,c}= routes from C to B

We start from A, we can take route 1 out of 6, then when we reach C, we can take route a. That is one possible route, namely route 1a.

Now for everytime we use the route 1, there are 3 possible routes to pair up with route 1, that is, route a, b, or c. So we have routes 1a, 1b, 1c.

It means that for every route from A to C, there are 3 possible routes. There are six routes, which makes it 6 x 3 = 18.

Listing all the elements and getting the cartesian product:

x·y= {1,2,3,4,5,6} · {a,b,c}

x·y= {1a,1b,1c,2a,2b,2c,3a,3b,3c,4a,4b,4c,5a,5b,5c,6a,6b,6c} = 18 routes.

23. The prices of a souvenir T-shirt in Tagaytay from 10 souvenir shops located at Sky Ranch are as follows: 250, 100, 180, 110, 200, 250, 170, 200, 210, and 300. What is the value of the 50th percentile and what does it mean?


Answer:

200 is the 50th percentile. This means thar 200 is also considered the median of the given data set.

Step-by-step explanation:

What is asked?

The 50th percentile of the prices of the souvenir T-Shirt in Tagaytay.

What are given?

10 shops with the following prices (arranged in ascending order)

100

110

170

180

200

200

210

250

250

300

How to solve?

The data should be arranged from lowest to greatest. We will then find the 50th percentile and then interpret the result.

Formula:

First:

50% * N = k

Where N is the total number of observations. k will indicate the place (kth value) of the data we will use. Then we proceed to:

(kth + (kth+1)) ÷2 =x

x will the 50% percentile.

Solution:

50%*N=k

0.5(10)= 5

This means that k=5. The 5th place in ascending order is 200 and the 6th (kth+1) place is also 200, we have

(kth + (kth+1)) ÷2 =x

(200+200)÷2=x

400/2=x

x=200

CONCLUSION:

The 50th percentile is 200.

What does this mean?

This means that 200 is also the median of the given data set. This also means that 50% of the data falls behind 200 and the rest 50% falls above 200.
