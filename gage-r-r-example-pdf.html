2. If a 3 m3 tank contains 45 kg of oxygen at 50°c, what is the gage pressure in the tank? the specific gas constant of oxygen is r=0.25983 kj/kg-k.


Answer:

1370.5 kPa.

Explanation:

To calculate the gauge pressure in the tank, we can use the ideal gas law, which relates the pressure, volume, temperature, and number of moles of a gas:

PV = nRT

where P is the absolute pressure of the gas, V is the volume of the tank, n is the number of moles of the gas, R is the specific gas constant, and T is the absolute temperature of the gas.

First, we need to calculate the number of moles of oxygen in the tank. We can use the mass of the oxygen and its molar mass to find the number of moles:

n = m/M

where m is the mass of the oxygen and M is the molar mass of oxygen (32 g/mol).

m = 45 kg = 45000 g

M = 32 g/mol

n = 45000 g / 32 g/mol = 1406.25 mol

Next, we need to convert the temperature to absolute temperature in Kelvin:

T = 50°C + 273.15 = 323.15 K

Now we can use the ideal gas law to solve for the absolute pressure:

P = nRT/V

P = (1406.25 mol)(0.25983 kJ/kg-K)(323.15 K)/(3 m^3) = 1471.8 kPa (absolute pressure)

Finally, we can convert the absolute pressure to gauge pressure by subtracting the atmospheric pressure. Assuming standard atmospheric pressure of 101.3 kPa, we get:

P_gauge = P_absolute - P_atm = 1471.8 kPa - 101.3 kPa = 1370.5 kPa

Therefore, the gauge pressure in the tank is 1370.5 kPa.
