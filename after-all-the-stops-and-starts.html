Answers with Explanation:

1. What made up your mixture?

• The mixture is made up of rice grains, water and other unwanted particles.

2. How did you separate the impurities from the rice before cooking?

• In order to separate the impurities from the rice before cooking, water is added to the mixture and as we stir the mixture, the impurities in the middle or in the bottom slowly floated above the water surface.

3. What happened to the rice? Why?

• The rice remained in the bottom because it is denser than unwanted particles.

4. What happened to the impurities (husk etc)? Why?

• The impurities or unwanted particles such as the empty husks floated on the water because they are less denser than the rice grains.

5. Can the solid particles be separated from water right after mixing without letting it settle at the bottom of the bowl? Why?​

• Yes. The rice grains are denser than water and unwanted particles. As we poured out the water that contains impurities, the impurities flowed out with the water, thus, they were successfully separated from the rice grians before cooking.
