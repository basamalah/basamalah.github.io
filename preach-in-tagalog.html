9. The three basic themes of Hosea's preaching​


Answer:

First, that God suffers exceedingly because of human rebellion against him:

second, that God loves unconditionally; and third, that God forgives completely. It is apparent that the prophet Hosea learned these timeless truths out of the fire of personal experience. It was his tragic lot in life to have an unfaithful wife and a broken home.

While Hosea was too modest to give us a detailed ac­count of his personal struggle, he shared enough to let us know that in the profound tragedy of his broken marriage, his message took on flesh and life

11. what did the prophet preach​


Answer:

Muhammad and the Rise of Islam

Explanation:

Salam

Actually, the Prophet (S) did not preach to people secretly rather he preached the Qur'an to the people of Mecca individually during the first three years of his mission. In other words, the Prophet (S) recited the verses of the Qur'an to those he thought that their hearts were ready to accept it and invited them to Islam privately. Thus, It is better to say that Muhammad (S) preached the Qur'an in two ways: individually and publically. Polytheists particularly and people of Mecca generally were informed about what the Prophet (S) was saying from the outset of his mission but they did not take it as a serious trend. Islam seemed to be an insignificant or temporary effort against Mecca's elite's authority.

From another hand, the Prophet (S) did not insult or offend the Idols and indeed, he concealed the social functions of his preaching. He did not challenge the power of the elites of Mecca during these three years. Very soon people in every corner of Arabia heard about Muhammad. After that he publicized his mission and stood against injustice. Worshiping the Idols was the core and origin of social injustice and ignorance in Mecca in that age. So he fought with them saying there is no sense behind worshiping the Idols.
