14. Why use the term "bullying prevention" instead of anti bullying​


Answer:

PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center uses the term “bullying prevention” instead of “anti-bullying” to place the emphasis on a proactive approach and philosophy, framing bullying as an issue to which there is a solution. While the use of “anti” does appropriately indicate the concept of being against bullying, the focus on “prevention” recognizes that change is ultimately about shifting behavior and attitudes, which can happen through the positive approach of education, awareness, and action.

ArticleAt PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center, our vision is “to make every child safe and to change the culture in our society so that bullying is no longer viewed as acceptable behavior.” The language framing the goal is an important part of this change. It has the potential to influence the view of the issue and, in the best cases, direct ideas that promote positive behavior.

Using the term “bullying prevention” instead of “anti-bullying” places the focus where it should be: on understanding the actions that help prevent bullying from occurring. A term, such as “anti,” which focuses on the negative, often doesn’t allow for the opportunity to change or indicate what society can do about bullying.

Acting to change the behavior is vitally important to preventing bullying. Bullying should not be considered a childhood rite of passage. Instead, bullying behaviors should be viewed as changeable and preventable through education and awareness. Discussions with children that focus on inclusion over exclusion, and showing respect and kindness toward others need to occur frequently as children grow up. When children are brought to better awareness of these issues, they have a greater opportunity to develop empathy and an increased understanding that can ultimately disrupt or interrupt bullying dynamics.

In these ongoing conversations with children or throughout communities, to create a social environment where bullying is not accepted, include discussions on how bullying harms everyone involved — targets, witnesses, and the people who use these damaging behaviors. While “anti” does appropriately express being against bullying, the conversation must include preventative efforts. Discussing the steps necessary to resolve conflicts and promote healthy social interactions between people will lead to a culture focusing on a positive, proactive approach to preventing bullying.

19. Cyber Bullying should be controlled to prevent a negative influence on youth using a pursuasive essay.


Typed this one for ya 'lad

Cyberbullying is a form of harassment that occurs online or through electronic devices such as smartphones, computers, and tablets. This type of bullying can have a profound negative impact on the lives of young people, and it is important to take steps to control it to prevent any further harm. While some may argue that cyberbullying is simply a part of modern life and that children should just toughen up, it is essential to acknowledge the severity of the issue and work to prevent it from happening in the first place.

One of the most significant concerns about cyberbullying is the impact it has on young people's mental health. Constant harassment and humiliation can lead to depression, anxiety, and other mental health problems. Cyberbullying can also lead to physical harm, including self-harm and suicide. The rise in cyberbullying-related incidents over the years is a clear indication that this is a serious issue that needs to be addressed.

Some may argue that cyberbullying is just a part of growing up, and children need to learn to deal with it. However, this is not a valid argument. Bullying of any kind should never be accepted, and we should work to prevent it from happening altogether. Children should not be subjected to the pain and suffering that comes with cyberbullying, and it is our responsibility to protect them.

The issue of cyberbullying can also have a ripple effect on the community. Children who are bullied may become bullies themselves, perpetuating the cycle of harassment and humiliation. This can create a negative environment, where children are afraid to speak out and seek help.

One way to prevent cyberbullying is to regulate online behavior. Parents, teachers, and other adults should monitor online activity and take action when necessary. Schools and organizations should also provide education on the dangers of cyberbullying and how to prevent it. We must create a culture where bullying of any kind is unacceptable, and there are severe consequences for those who engage in it.

In conclusion, cyberbullying is a serious issue that needs to be controlled to prevent a negative influence on youth. The impact of cyberbullying can be devastating, and it is our responsibility to protect our children from harm. While some may argue that cyberbullying is just a part of modern life, we must work to prevent it from happening and create a safe and positive environment for our youth. By taking action and regulating online behavior, we can make a difference and create a better future for our children.
