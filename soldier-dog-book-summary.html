Answer:

The soldier who fired and killed a Filipino soldier is American Private William Grayson of First Nebraska Volunteer brigade who started the Philippine-American War.

Explanation:

In the dark of night on February 4, 1899 in the Sta. Mesa, Manila, a gunshot broke the silence and caused the war between the Philippines and the United States to begin. The aforementioned first gunshot that started the war came from the rifle held by American Private William Grayson of the First Nebraska Volunteer brigade. Grayson fired in the direction where the Filipino soldiers were positioned which he suspected was plotting because he allegedly heard seven signals and a flash of light. In that first shot by Grayson, the first Filipino soldier killed in the Philippine-American war, Corporal Anastacio Felix of the 4th Company of the Morong Battalion, was recorded. Felix's two other soldiers were killed that night. The war between the Philippines and the United States ended in 1902 and an estimated half a million Filipinos were killed, most of them civilians.
