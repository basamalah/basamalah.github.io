1. 1. How is the cell wall different from the cell membrane? A. The cell membrane and cell wall are found in all cells. B. The cell membrane is found in plant cells, while the cell wall in all cells. C. The cell membrane is found in plant cells, while the cell wall in animal cells. D. The cell membrane is found in all cells, while the cell wall in plant cells ONLY​


Answer:

D. The cell membrane is found in all cells, while the cell wall in plant cells ONLY.

Explanation:

The cytoplasm of every cell is surrounded and contained by the cell membrane, which is a thin, semi-permeable layer.

It serves as a barrier that regulates the flow of substances into and out of the cell and is essential for preserving the cell's shape and structure.

The cell wall is a stiff, shielding covering that envelops the cell membrane of certain organisms such as plants, bacteria, fungi, and some protists.

It serves as a supporting framework, safeguards the cell, maintains its shape, and prevents it from bursting as a result of osmotic pressure. Unlike plant cells, animal cells lack cell walls.
