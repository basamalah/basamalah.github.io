28. True or False: Documenting the decision is the last action needed to be taken to solve the problem.


Answer:

True

Explanation:

Documented problem solving is a teaching and learning technique that generates metacognition. This causes students to shift their focus from merely getting the correct answer to the thought process or steps they use in order to find the answer. As students' think about their learning and their problem-solving awareness increases, they begin to transition from the "steps used to solve a problem" to the application of analytical and critical thinking skills. Documented problem solving also helps to identify missteps in students' thinking, and thus provides valuable feedback for students and instructors. The feedback is more informative than what can be gleaned from typical multiple choice, true/false, or short answer questions. The approach is easy to implement in a number of academic disciplines.
