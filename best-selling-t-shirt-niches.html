2. Jayden and Carson are selling T-shirts and sweatshirts with the school logo. Jayden sells 9 T-shirts and 3 sweatshirts for $288. Carson sells 1 T-shirt and 6 sweatshirts for $270. Find the selling price of each item. Write a system of equations to represent this situation.


Answer:

Let

[tex]x - selling \: price \: of \: t - shirt \: \: \: \: \: \: \\ y - selling \: price \: of \:sweatshirts[/tex]

Equation 1,

[tex]9x + 3y = 288[/tex]

Equation 2,

[tex]x + 6y = 270[/tex]

Solve for x and y using either Substitution Method or Elimination Method.

By Substitution Method,

Choose one between the two equations and solve for either x or y then substitute to other equation.

For instance,

(In this case I will choose equation 2)

[tex]x + 6y = 270 \\ x = 270 - 6y[/tex]

Substitute to equation 1,

[tex]9x + 3y = 288[/tex]

[tex]9(270 - 6y) + 3y = 288[/tex]

[tex]2430 - 54y + 3y = 288[/tex]

[tex] - 51y = 288 - 2430[/tex]

[tex] - 51y = - 2142[/tex]

[tex] \frac{ - 51y}{ - 51} = \frac{ - 2142}{ - 5} [/tex]

[tex]y = 42[/tex]

Substitute y = 42 to equation 1 or 2 to solve x.

For Equation 2,

[tex] \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: x + 6y = 270 \\ x + 6(42) = 270 \\ \: \: \: x + 252= 270 \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: x = 270 - 252 \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: x = 18[/tex]

Therefore, the selling price of T-shirt and Sweatshirt is $18 and $42, respectively.

Biconditional Statement

A biconditional statement is a combination of a conditional statement and its converse written in the if and only if form.

It is a combination of two conditional statements, “if two line segments are congruent then they are of equal length” and “if two line segments are of equal length then they are congruent”.

A biconditional is true if and only if both the conditionals are true.
