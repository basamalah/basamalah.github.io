1. describe bhumisparsa mudra​


Answer: Bhumisparsha mudra

This mudra, formed with all five fingers of the right hand extended to touch the ground, symbolizes the Buddha's enlightenment under the bodhi tree, when he summoned the earth goddess, Sthavara, to bear witness to his attainment of enlightenment.

Answer:

Bhumispara mudra is a hand gesture, thought to be adopted by Siddhartha Gautama (Buddha) in the moment of his enlightenment. As such, this mudra is commonly depicted in statues and carvings of Buddha in meditation. Translated from Sanskrit as the "earth touching gesture," bhumispara mudra is symbolic of Buddha's triumph over temptation by the demon king Mara.

It is believed that the earth bore witness to this event as a result of Buddha lowering his right hand, and so statues of Buddha in bhumispara mudra are known as the "earth witness".

Bhumisparsha mudra

This mudra, formed with all five fingers of the right hand extended to touch the ground, symbolizes the Buddha's enlightenment under the bodhi tree, when he summoned the earth goddess, Sthavara, to bear witness to his attainment of enlightenment.

9. if LE = 3× +4, OV =4, find x, LE and OV​


The given equation is:

LE = 3x + 4

And OV = 4.

To solve for x, we need more information or an additional equation. However, if we assume that OV is a point on the line LE, then we can find the value of x by substituting the value of OV into the equation for LE:

OV = LE

4 = 3x + 4

Subtracting 4 from both sides gives:

0 = 3x

Dividing both sides by 3 gives:

0 = x

So, x = 0.

Substituting x = 0 into the equation for LE gives:

LE = 3(0) + 4 = 4

Therefore, LE = 4 and x = 0 and OV = 4.
