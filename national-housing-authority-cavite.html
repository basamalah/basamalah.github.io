25. explain how the cavite mutiny and the Gomburza martyrdom contributed to the growth of the Filipino nationalism and decline the spanish rule?​


Here the following contributed to the growth of Filipino Nationalism and the decline of Spanish Rule:Cavite Mutiny and the Gomburza martyrdomAbuses and immoralities of the friars

The Philippines were colonized by Spain in the 19th century, and the church friars were influential at this time. Because of their position of power, some friars develop avarice and mistreat others. In his book "The Friars in the Philippines," LeRoy claims that some of the abuses committed by the friars included simony, exorbitant church dues, and outdated educational practices. Padre Gomez, Padre Burgos, and Padre Zamora are currently working for Spanish friars. At that same time, the Cavite mutiny took place.

Due to the mutiny that took place, the friars had to round up suspects; unfortunately, because GomBurZa is fighting for the right to keep their church, they are falsely accused of being the mutiny's leader. They are then given a garotte sentence, which results in death. When little Rizal witnessed this, he suddenly became aware of his surroundings. Later, the three priests' deaths sparked the Ilustrado movement, and in his second book, El Filibusterismo, Rizal paid respect to them.

Furthermore, we are aware that the two books Noli me Tangere and El Filibusterismo were the catalysts for the revolution against the Spaniards. These two books stoked the nationalism of every Filipino, inspiring them to oppose Spanish colonization and work for their country's independence.
