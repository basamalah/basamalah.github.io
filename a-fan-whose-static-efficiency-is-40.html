   The resistance to airflow in ductwork is known as static pressure. Cooling and heating systems distribute warm or cold air across rooms via ducts. Some elements, however, obstruct airflow. It is impossible to have no resistance, but the goal is to keep it under control. External Static Pressure measures all of the resistance in the duct system that the fan must overcome. Filters, grills, A/C coils, and ducting are among examples. It is the total of the suction (negative) and discharge (positive) pressures produced by the equipment blower.

Although static and dynamic pressures are expected to vary greatly across the fluid, total pressure remains constant along each streamline. Total pressure is the same on all streamlines in irrotational flow and is hence constant throughout the flow.

24. A box takes 40 n to start moving when the coefficient of static friction is .54. what is the weight of the box?


The weight of the box is 74.08 kg.

The solution is here:

F = 40 N

μf = 0.54

frictional force = static friction coefficient × weight of the box

(F = μf X W)

Therefore, on substituting values:

40 = 0.54 x W

W = 40/0.54

W = 74.08 kg

30. A desktop computer is to be cooled by a fan whose flow rate is 0.30 m3 /min. determine the mass flow rate of air through the fan at an elevation of 3400 m where the air density is 0.7 kg/m3 . also, if the average velocity of air is not to exceed 95 m/min, determine the minimum diameter of the casing of the fan.


Answer:

63.5 mm pabrainliest po thanks

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Flow rate , V = 0.30m³/min

Density of air, p (rho) = 0.7 kg/m³

average velocity of air, v = 95m/min

Mass flow rate,

m = p V

m = 0.7 (0.3) = 0.21 kg/min

Flow rate can be written as,

V= A v

Where A is the cross section of the fan

0.30 = A (95)

0.30 = (π/4) d² (95)

d² = [0.30] / [(π/4)(95)]

d = 0.0634 m

d = 63.41 mm approximately equal to 63.5 mm
