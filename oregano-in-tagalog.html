15. common parts of oregano


Answer:

Oregano is a herb that is commonly used in cooking, particularly in Mediterranean and Mexican cuisines. The parts of the oregano plant that are most commonly used are the leaves and the essential oil, which is extracted from the leaves.

Explanation:

The leaves of the oregano plant have a strong, pungent flavor and aroma that is often described as being similar to a combination of thyme and mint. They have been used to flavor a wide variety of dishes, including meats, poultry, fish, soups, stews, and sauces.

The essential oil of oregano is also commonly used in cooking and as a flavoring agent. It is also used in the production of perfumes, soaps, and other personal care products. Oregano essential oil is also known to have medicinal properties and is sometimes used to treat respiratory issues, skin conditions, and other health problems.

The dried leaves and ground oregano are also commonly used as a spice.

Please note that there are different species of Oregano and the chemical composition may vary.
