11. Mervin wants to find the distance his circular garden. it has a radius of 12 m. what is the circumference of his circular garden . wheres solution


The circumference of a circle can be found using the formula:

Circumference = 2 × π × radius

where π (pi) is a mathematical constant approximately equal to 3.14159.

Substituting the given value of the radius into this formula, we get:

Circumference = 2 × π × 12
Circumference = 24 × π
Circumference ≈ 75.398 m (rounded to 3 decimal places)

Therefore, the circumference of Mervin's circular garden is approximately 75.398 meters.

Note: The distance around the circle is called the circumference, not the diameter. To find the distance around the garden, we need to calculate the circumference, not the diameter.
