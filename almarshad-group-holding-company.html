3. what bond hold the atom of the element in group1 and group 2 of the periodic table of elements​


Answer:

What bond holds the atoms of elements in groups 1 and 2 of the periodic table?

All atoms of any element are held together by two forces.

The electrons are held to the atom by the electrical force of the protons in the nucleus.

The protons and neutrons in the nucleus are pushed apart by the electrical force of the protons, but they are held together by the Strong Nuclear Force. This force operates over very small distances (around the size of nuclei) and creates an attraction between both protons and neutrons.. In stable atoms, the Strong Nuclear Force overcomes the electrical force that pushes the protons apart.

We speak of the bonds between ATOMS….the interatomic bonds. And the bonds that operate for the alkali, and the alkaline earth metals, Groups 1 and 2 respectively, are METALLIC bonds. In the metallic bond, each metal atom contributes one or more of its valence electrons to the entire lattice; these electrons are said to be delocalized. The result is often described as “positive ions in a sea of electrons”, and this is certainly an example of non-molecular bonding.

And the given description of metallic bonding accounts nicely for typical metallic properties, i.e. malleability, the ability to be hammered into a sheet, ductility, the ability to be drawn into a wire, and electrical and thermal conductivity.. The later transition metals, which have MORE valence electrons, tend to have higher melting and boiling points due to the enhancement of metallic bonding afforded by delocalization of more electrons
