Explanation:  

Saying “po” or “opo” is more of a tradition. “Po” or “Opo” is used as word of courtesy and respect towards the elderly or for someone with a higher rank (like your boss, CEO). Filipino children also say “po” or “opo” to their parents or anyone older than them.

4. as a citizen, do you view by saying "po" and "opo" as significant of gesture of friendship and confidence? why?


Answer:

We only use “po” and “opo” for our parents and other older people, except our siblings.

We use “kuya” for any older brother and “ate” for any older sister.

Traditionally, we used to have more according to birth order:

Kuya/ate = 1st child (boy/girl)

Diko/ditse = 2nd child

Sangko/sanse = 3rd child

Siko/sitse = 4th child

Bunso = youngest child, regardless of gender

These are very rarely used nowadays.

13. We use the words po and opo or we say mano po to show respect to people. This shows________.A. ResiliencyB. AdaptabilityC. GratefulnessD. Politeness ​


Answer:

The correct answer is C. Gratefulness.

Explanation:

The use of "po" and "opo" and the gesture of "mano po" in Filipino culture is a way of showing respect to elders, authority figures, and people in higher positions. It is a way of acknowledging their wisdom, experience, and contributions.
