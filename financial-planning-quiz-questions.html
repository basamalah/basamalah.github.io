8. Financial plan of a business​


Answer:

A financial plan is a document that outlines a business's projected income, expenses, and profits over a specified period of time. It is an important tool for businesses, as it helps them to understand their financial position and make informed decisions about how to allocate their resources. A financial plan typically includes a profit and loss statement, a balance sheet, and a cash flow statement. It may also include information about the business's assets, liabilities, and equity, as well as its projected sales and costs. A financial plan can help a business to identify potential financial risks and opportunities, and to develop strategies for addressing them. It can also be used to track the business's financial performance over time, and to make adjustments to the plan as needed in response to changes in the business's financial situation.
