Answer:

What's the Difference Between RFID and Barcode? While barcode scanners require a line of sight to scan each code individually, RFID scanners can read multiple codes at once. 7. To improve package tracking at a UPS transfer facility, conveyor equipment was upgraded with RFID sensors at a cost of $345,000. The operating cost is expected to be $148,000 per year for the first 3 years and $210,000 for the next 3 years. The salvage value of the equipment is expected to be $140,000 for the first 3 years, but due to obsolescence, it won’t have a significant value after that. At an interest rate of 10% per year, determine (a) The economic service life of the equipment and associated annual worth (b) The percentage increase in the AW of cost if the equipment is retained 2 years longer than the ESL.


Answer:

To determine the economic service life (ESL) of the equipment and the associated annual worth (AW), we can use the following formula:

AW = (C - S) / [(1 + i)^n - 1]

Where:

C is the annual cost of operating the equipment

S is the annual salvage value of the equipment

i is the interest rate

n is the number of years the equipment is in service

In this case, we are given that the operating cost is expected to be $148,000 per year for the first 3 years and $210,000 for the next 3 years, and the salvage value is expected to be $140,000 for the first 3 years. We are also given that the interest rate is 10% per year.

First, let's calculate the AW for the first 3 years:

AW = ($148,000 - $140,000) / [(1 + 0.10)^3 - 1] = $8,000 / [(1.10)^3 - 1] = $8,000 / 0.331 = $24,073.50

Next, let's calculate the AW for the next 3 years:

AW = ($210,000 - $0) / [(1 + 0.10)^3 - 1] = $210,000 / [(1.10)^3 - 1] = $210,000 / 0.331 = $632,632.75

Since the AW increases significantly after the first 3 years, we can conclude that the ESL of the equipment is 3 years.

To calculate the percentage increase in the AW of cost if the equipment is retained 2 years longer than the ESL, we can use the following formula:

% increase in AW = (AW(n+2) - AW(n)) / AW(n) * 100%

Where:

AW(n) is the AW of the equipment after n years

AW(n+2) is the AW of the equipment after n+2 years

In this case, we are given that the ESL of the equipment is 3 years and we want to calculate the percentage increase in the AW if the equipment is retained for 5 years.

% increase in AW = ($632,632.75 - $24,073.50) / $24,073.50 * 100% = $608,559.25 / $24,073.50 = 25.25 = 2525%

Therefore, the percentage increase in the AW of cost if the equipment is retained 2 years longer than the ESL is 2525%.
