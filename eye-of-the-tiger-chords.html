3. what is message on the eye of the tiger?​


Answer:

DEATH

Explanation:

In the wild, seeing the "eyes" of the tiger signifies death, because right before a tiger attacks, it turns its ears forward so that the spot on the back of each ear faces nearer its prey. The "eye spots" on the back of a tiger's ear serve to confuse predators and reduce the risk of attack from behind.

Answer:

The message of the song "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor is about perseverance, determination, and resilience in the face of adversity. The lyrics encourage the listener to rise up to the challenge and fight to achieve their goals, using the metaphor of a tiger and its instinct to survive as a symbol of strength and courage.

The song uses several figurative language devices to convey its message, including:

Metaphor: The entire song is built around the metaphor of a tiger, representing strength, power, and determination.

Personification: The lyrics describe the tiger as having "the eye of the tiger," personifying the animal and giving it a human characteristic of determination.

Hyperbole: The lyrics use hyperbole to emphasize the strength and power of the tiger, such as "rising up, straight to the top" and "face to face, out in the heat."

Imagery: The lyrics use vivid imagery to paint a picture of the physical and emotional struggle, such as "hanging tough, staying hungry."

Allusion: The song alludes to the movie "Rocky III" and its protagonist's struggle to regain his fighting spirit, adding a layer of meaning and context to the lyrics.

Overall, the figurative language used in the song helps to convey its message of resilience and determination, using powerful symbols and vivid imagery to inspire and motivate the listener.

In line 10, "it's the thrill of the fight," figurative language of metonymy is used to represent the excitement and challenge of facing obstacles and overcoming them.

In line 9, "it's the eye of the tiger," figurative language of synecdoche is used to represent the animal's strength and determination as a whole.

In line 6, "you trade your passion for glory," figurative language of metaphor is used to represent the idea that some people sacrifice their true passion in pursuit of fame and success.

It symbolizes intense focus and commitment towards achieving goals, even in the face of obstacles. This phrase is widely used in sports, business and other fields that require perseverance, resilience and a strong mindset. In essence, "Eye of the Tiger" represents an unyielding spirit that refuses to be defeated and remains resolute in achieving its objectives.
