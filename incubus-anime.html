2. What is the difference between incubation and gestation ? Give examples of incubation and gestation periods of some animals.​


Answer:

Incubation is the process of keeping eggs warm so they can hatch. Gestation is the period of time when a pregnant animal carries its young before birth.

Examples of incubation:

A chicken's egg takes about 21 days to incubateA crocodile's egg takes about 80 days to incubate

Examples of gestation:

A dog's gestation period is about 63 daysA horse's gestation period is about 11 months

B. process of producting their own kind.

Explanation:

During sexual reproduction, the haploid gametes of the male and female individuals of a species combine in a process called fertilization. Typically, the small, motile male sperm fertilizes the much larger, sessile female egg. This process produces a diploid fertilized egg called a zygote.
