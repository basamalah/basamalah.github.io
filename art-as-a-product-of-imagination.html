19. art is a product of imagination give atleast 5 sentences​


Answer: Art is the product of a desire to express something. Imagination is a tool that may or may not be used to do so.

In some cases, the expression comes through response to the external world without directly employing imagination. Think of a collage, it’s made of existing pieces of material, not preconceived through imagination. In acting, improvisation is the ultimate mastery and requires no imagination, only response.

So, some art is created by the in-out method, other by the out-in-out method. In case of the in-out method, some of it is a product of imagination (ideation, visualization, play, daydreaming) some of it of direct inspiration (a vision that is then followed, channeling).

In case of the visionary-type work, it may feel closer to a detective story or putting together a puzzle, because the original vision or idea, which in essence feels external, holds answers that must be uncovered. It’s not something you made up, it’s something that came (internally or externally) and haunts you until you crack the code.

For example, I’m writing non-fiction books based on external stimulation, questions or compilations of ideas upon which I reflect: out-in-out method. I’m also writing fiction books - sort of fun-read romance where I get to make up stories set in fancy environments: in-out method based on imagination.

I’m also writing a serious book prompted by a single vivid dream I had in 1996 that appears to be an insight into a past life. I’m still discovering details of that story 24 years later. It took me 23 years to finish the first draft and I only did this when I finally met the person the dream described. That’s so not about imagination! I was given that information in my sleep and played a detective ever since: in-out based on direct inspiration without ideation.

Same with my digital art. I do close-up scanographies of natural materials. I get inspired by the plants or rocks I find, I explore them in detail, I find the best way to portray them. No imagination need there. Keen eye - yes. Knowledge of composition, colors, etc. - sure. But not imagination.

These methods are essentially the same across all creative endeavors, not just visual arts or writing. I also do creative problem solving for my business clients, which requires ideation (imagining possible solutions): out-in-out. These principles go far beyond arts.
