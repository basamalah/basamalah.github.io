12. how to add disimilar fraction


Example, we have 1/2 + 1/3

Change 'em to similar fractions first. Do so by getting the LCD (it's simply the LCM of the denominator). LCM of 2 and 3 is 6.

new numerator = (LCD / denominator)*numerator
new denominator = LCD

Following whatever is written above, we'll have:
1/2 = 3/6
1/3 = 2/6

Now, add. Then reduce to lowest term, of course.
3/6 + 2/6 = 5/6

Answer is 5/6You can add dissimilar fractions by finding the LCD or Least Common Denominator of the two denominators, divide the original denominators to the LCD, multiply the quotients to the numerators, then add it the same way as you add similar fractions.

Ex: 1/2 + 3/4= 5/4 or 1 1/4

Solution:
Denominators: 2 and 4
The LCD is 4 
Therefore, 4 ÷ 2 = 2 and 4 ÷ 4 = 1 
2x1 = 2 and 1x3 = 3 
Our new numerators are 2 and 3  
So, 2/4+3/4 = 5/4 or 1 1/4

29. addition and subtraction of disimilar fractions​


Answer:

To add or subtract fractions they must have the same denominator (the bottom value). If the denominators are already the same then it is just a matter of either adding or subtracting the numerators (the top value). If the denominators are different then a common denominator needs to be found.
