2. what will happen to a sealed balloon that contains dry ice if the balloon is left at room temperature​


Answer:

What will happen to a sealed balloon that contains dry ice if the balloon is left at room temperature?

dry ice, a solid, when placed into a balloon in room temperature will sublimate--dry ice passes through the liquid phase and readily becomes a gas. Therefore, going from a solid to a gas, the carbon dioxide will now have much greater kinetic energy compared to the attractive forces of the molecules causing them to spread out. Since the balloon is sealed and the gas cannot escape, the carbon dioxide gas will force the balloon to expand.
