13. Describe segment AW, segment EW, and segment AE of triangle AWE using Triangle Inequality Theorem 3


Answer:

The Triangle Inequality Theorem states that the length of one side of a triangle must be less that the sum of the other two sides. Based on this theorem, we can describe the segments of triangle AWE as follows:

segment AW < segment EW + segment AE

The length of segment AW is less than the sum of the lengths of segments EW and AE.

segment EW < segment AW + segment AE

The length of segment EW is less than the sum of the lengths of segments AW and AE.

segment AE < segment EW + segment AW

The length of segment AE is less than the sum of the lengths of segments EW and AW.

Furthermore, if one side is longer compared to the sum of the other two sides it means that the segments cannot make triangle.
