An intact hymen is a sign of virginity, this statement is a mistake and just a myth.

 Discussion

Talking about the issue of virginity will certainly be directly related to the human hymen. The hymen, or hymen, is a fibrous tissue located in the vagina. Usually, society interprets the virginity of a woman by the condition of the hymen, or a hymen that is not damaged or is still intact. In fact, many factors cause the hymen to be damaged.

The hymen itself has a function to filter dirt so it doesn't directly enter the Miss V. In addition, the hymen grows along with the development of a woman's intimate organs. It can be said that the hymen is not a membrane that marks whether a woman is still a virgin or not. In fact, the function of the hymen has nothing to do with sexual activity.
