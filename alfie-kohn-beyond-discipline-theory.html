2. the use of word in addressing the public or people who are beyond a discipline​


Answer:

Regardless of the academic discipline, according to Melzer’s study, students in different disciplines researched and wrote for a variety of audience which included not only the instructor of their class, but also their classmates and for wider audiences outside of their classes (95). Despite this variety of goals, methods, and approaches, there are several key principles of source-based writing which span different academic disciplines and professions. These principles are:

The purpose of academic writing is to generate and communicate new knowledge and new ideas.

Academic writers write “from sources.” This means that new ideas, conclusions, and theories are created on the basis of existing ideas and existing research

Academic writers examine their sources carefully for their credibility and appropriateness for the writer’s goals and objectives.

Academic writers carefully acknowledge all their research sources using source citation and documentation systems accepted in their disciplines.

One religious issue that has been the subject of much debate and conflict is the question of whether individuals have the right to freedom of religion, and whether this includes the right to not believe in any religion at all. From a structural functionalist perspective, religion plays a vital role in society by providing a sense of community and shared values, as well as a sense of meaning and purpose in life. However, this perspective also recognizes that individuals have the right to choose their own beliefs and practices, and that diversity of beliefs can be a source of strength for a society.

Marxism, on the other hand, views religion as a tool used by the ruling class to maintain their power and control over the working class. According to this theory, religion serves as a means of ideological control, as it provides justification for the existing social and economic order and distracts people from challenging it.

To address the issue of religious freedom, one approach could be to promote education and dialogue around the importance of respecting the beliefs and practices of others, while also working to dismantle systems of power and oppression that may be perpetuated through religion.
