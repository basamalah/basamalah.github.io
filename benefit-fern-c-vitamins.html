4. Commensalism - what benefit does a tree get from the fern.​


Answer:

follow

Explanation:

Since the symbiotic relationship between the fern and its host tree results in the fern benefiting (a place to live, gaining nourishment from elements collecting and moving along the tree’s bark) while the host is not affected at all, the relationship is called commensalism (if the fern benefited and the host was damaged, the relationship would be called parasitism; if both benefited, it would be mutualism).

29. One example of a commensalism is when the plant like fern is attached on a branch of a tree. Why is the interaction called commensalism? A. both a branch of a tree and a fern benefit from each other b. only branch of a tree is benefited from the reletionship C. a fern benefits from the relationship while a branch of a tree is neither harmed nor benefited. D. they complete with each other.


Answer:

C. a fern benefits from the relationship while a branch of a tree is neither harmed nor benefited.

Explanation:

The answer is A because, commensalism is defined as the interaction between two organisms wherein one is benefitted while the other one is neither benefitted nor harmed. For this scenario, the fern is benefitted as it takes nutrients from the tree by anchoring itself onto it. At the same time, the tree is neither benefitted nor harmed as the fern is attached to it.
