2. principles of arts rhythm and movement​


Answer:

Rhythm is a principle of design that suggests movement or action. Rhythm is usually achieved through repetition of lines, shapes, colors, and more. It creates a visual tempo in artworks and provides a path for the viewer's eye to follow.

Movement is the path the viewer's eye takes through the work of art, often to focal areas. Such movement can be directed along lines, edges, shape, and color within the work of art. Pattern is the repeating of an object or symbol all over the work of art.

4. Recognize the difference and uniqueness of the art styles of thevarious art movements (elements and principles of art).​


Answer:

Art Movements are the collective titles that are given to artworks which share the same artistic ideals, style, technical approach or timeframe.

There is no fixed rule that determines what constitutes an art movement. The artists associated with one movement may adhere to strict guiding principles, whereas those who belong to another may have little in common.
