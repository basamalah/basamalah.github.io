4. Characteristics of faith and paradoxical qualities of faith


According to the book of Hebrews in the Bible, faith has six distinct characteristics:

Faith involves a bold action. This involve a confident action that emerges from one’s faith. Faith is a response to God and to His Word.  This is the act of believing someone who is unseen but is manifested. Faith is God’s working on miracles for His people.  God reveals Himself to those who believe in his great works and the truth of His Word. Faith is manifested through various real life situations. This shall include our deliverance from tragedy and disaster. Faith comes in different outcomes.  Others have received an immediate response from God through their faith, while some may have it delayed. Faith is rewarded by God.  God commends those who are faithful to Him just as how faithful He is to us.

While faith is distinct, it has some underlying paradoxical truths:

Faith is certain, but not everyone understands it well. Faith is free, but we are to respond to His unconditional love. Faith is believable, but its reasoning is intangible that requires deep and personal understanding.
