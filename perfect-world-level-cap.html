14. why do you need to place the cap on a flat level surface when measuring ingredients ​


Answer:

To make sure that the measurement is accurate

Explanation:

Place the Cup on a Flat Level Surface When Measuring Ingredients

When it comes to cooking and, especially, baking, or any activity that requires measuring ingredients, you need to place the cup on a flat level surface when measuring ingredients to make sure that the measurement is accurate. Otherwise, placing the cup on a bumped or uneven surface will likely lead to an incorrect measurement of the ingredients. The line or level at which you need to look at will be slanted and any ingredient placed on the cup will have an inconsistent reading. Doing  so will result to a product - quite to highly - different from the original recipe.

Other Tips When Measuring Ingredients

For more tips in making products aside from placing the cup on a flat level surface when measuring ingredients, check the list below:

Use proper measuring cups and spoon.Invest in digital scale if you can as this can minimize the error when measuring ingredients.Use a dry measuring cup for measuring dry ingredients such as flour and sugar. Likewise, use a liquid measuring cup for wet ingredients such as cream and water.Don't use the measuring cup to scoop out flour. Use spoon to transfer the flour into the measuring cup.Read the measuring cup at an eye level. As you look at the side of the cup, the surface will be curved. The correct measurement is at the bottom of the curve.

16. 7. What is the importance of drop caps and initial caps​


Answer:

A drop cap is a large initial letter that drops below the first line of a paragraph, much bigger in size than the rest of the letters that follow. It is a stylistic choice often used as a marker for the beginning of a section or chapter of a book.

Meanwhile, initial caps have three main purposes: to let the reader know a sentence is beginning, to show important words in a title, and to signal proper names and official titles.
