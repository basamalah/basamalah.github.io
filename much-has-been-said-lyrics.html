18. “From everyone who has been given much, much will be demanded, and from the one who has been entrusted with much, much more will be asked.”more explanation


Answer:

This quote comes from the Bible, in the Gospel of Luke, chapter 12, verse 48. It is part of a larger teaching by Jesus about being prepared for the coming of the kingdom of God and being a faithful and wise servant.

The quote emphasizes the idea of responsibility and accountability. It suggests that people who have been given much in terms of resources, talents, or opportunities have a greater responsibility to use those gifts wisely and to serve others. They will be held to a higher standard and will be expected to make the most of what they have been given.

On the other hand, the quote also suggests that those who have been entrusted with much will be given even more opportunities and responsibilities. This implies that the more we use our gifts and talents wisely, the more we will be entrusted with and the more we will be able to accomplish.

Overall, this quote encourages us to be grateful for the gifts we have been given and to use them to the best of our abilities, always striving to serve others and make a positive impact in the world.
