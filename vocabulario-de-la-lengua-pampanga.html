1. vocabulario de la lengua tagala


Answer:

Vocabulario de la lengua tagala was the first dictionary of the Tagalog language in the Philippines. It was written by the Franciscan friar Pedro de Buenaventura and published in Pila, Laguna in 1613. He most probably used the Vocabulario tagalo left by fray Juan de Plasencia as a source.


2. the first dictionary, Vocabulario de la lengua tagala came out in​


Answer:

The first extant dictionary of Tagalog, and indeed of any Philippine language, is the Vocabulario de la lengua tagala of Pedro de San Buenaventura, published in 1613, offering about 16,350 Spanish headwords and about 14,500 Tagalog equivalents.

13. what does lengua symbolize in lengua para diablo


Lengua Para Diablo:

Lengua symbolizes the words of the father in the story Lengua Para Diablo.

This story is about a family who suffers poverty after the head of the family lost his job. Since then, his right to decide and even to express his opinion regarding family issues almost lost its essence. The father literally became so silent for two days wondering how he would tell his family the bad news. Any father of course would not want his family to suffer. However, the narrator who is also the daughter of the father in the story wonders how her father lost his tongue and was not able to talk. She even thinks that her father's tongue was cooked before the devil got to eat it.

The girl also thinks that after her father's tongue, he will soon sell his esophagus or his stomach because he is no longer eating since he wanted his family to have enough for themselves so he just tell them that there is something wrong with his taste buds or he is not hungry. He felt that he should not eat because he does not earn for the family. In the end, the girl realized that every time the father tells that the devil ate his words, the father only wanted to tell them that he cannot say anything or he does not have to tell anything.
