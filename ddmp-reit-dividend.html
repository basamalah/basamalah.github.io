2. Each dividend is blank times the dividend to the left of it


Answer:

The phrase "Each dividend is blank times the dividend to the left of it" is likely referring to the process of long division, where a dividend is divided by a divisor to get a quotient and a remainder. In this process, each digit of the dividend is used to determine a partial quotient.

To find each partial quotient, you would typically take the digit of the dividend that is directly above the space for the quotient, and divide it by the divisor. The result of this division is then written as the partial quotient above the dividend digit being used, and multiplied by the divisor. The product of this multiplication is then subtracted from the digit of the dividend being used to get the remainder, which is then carried down to the next digit of the dividend to continue the process.

So, the "blank" in the phrase would be replaced by the value of the partial quotient, which is determined by dividing the dividend digit by the divisor. The "dividend to the left of it" would refer to the digit of the dividend that is directly to the left of the current dividend digit being used to determine the partial quotient.
