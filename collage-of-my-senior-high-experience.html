8. Exploring the experiences of students in dimasalang senior high school computer Laboratory in the absence of INTERNET CONNECTION ?​


Answer:

The experiences of students in a senior high school computer laboratory in the absence of internet connection can vary depending on the activities they are supposed to do in the laboratory. Here are some possible scenarios:

1. Basic computer skills: If the students are learning basic computer skills, such as typing, using Microsoft Office tools, or navigating the file system, the absence of internet connection may not have a significant impact on their experience. However, they may miss out on opportunities to search for online tutorials or resources to enhance their learning.

2. Programming: If the students are learning programming languages, the absence of internet connection may significantly affect their experience. Many programming tools and platforms require an internet connection to download necessary libraries and packages. Without internet connection, students may not be able to run or test their programs, limiting their learning opportunities.

3. Research and projects: If the students are working on research projects or assignments that require online resources, the absence of internet connection can be a significant hindrance. Students may have to rely on offline resources, which may not be as comprehensive as their online counterparts. This can affect the quality of their work and limit their ability to explore topics in depth.

4. Collaboration: If the students are working on group projects or collaborating with other students online, the absence of internet connection can limit their ability to communicate and share files. This can lead to delays in completing projects and limit the quality of their work.

In summary, the absence of internet connection in a senior high school computer laboratory can have a significant impact on students' experiences, depending on the activities they are supposed to do. Basic computer skills may not be affected much, but programming, research, and collaboration may be significantly hindered
