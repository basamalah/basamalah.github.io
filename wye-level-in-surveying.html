5. A three-phase balanced load has 10 ohm resistance in each of its phases. the load is supplied by a 220 v, three-phase source. the load is connected in wye. what is the power absorbed by the load


The answer is 14520 W.

Three-phase load balancing occurs when the power supply load, such as a three-phase rack PDU, is evenly distributed across all three phases (L1/L2, L2/L3, and L3/L1). This is achieved when you have the same number of devices plugged into the PDU outlet for each phase and draw the same power load in each phase.

In a balanced three-phase system, it is known that the phase voltages must be the same or close to the same. Unbalance is a measurement of the unequal phase voltages. The voltage will measure the voltage difference between the phases of a three-phase system.

In a single phase:

V = 220 / sqr root 3 = 127Phase 1 (I) = (127 angle of -30) / 10 ohm = 12.7 ang -30S total = (3) x (127 angle of -30) x (12.7 angle of -30) * = 4838.7 VA = 4838.7 W

The answer is 14520 W.

11. A three-phase, wye-connected, 6.25kVA, 220-synchronous generator has a reactance of 8.4 ohms per phase. Using the rated KVA and voltage as base values, determine the per – unit reactance. Then refer this per – unit value to a 230 – V, 7.5 kVA base. Answer: 0.688 pu


Answer:

To find the per-unit reactance of the generator, we need to first determine the base values. The rated KVA and voltage are given as 6.25 kVA and 220 V, respectively. Therefore:

Base KVA = 6.25 kVA

The per-unit reactance is calculated as:
