3. How are you going to prevent corruption?


Several ways to prevent corruption

Corruption is an abuse of power to take what does not belong to us or to do something that benefits us but harms others.

Here are some ways to prevent corruption:

1. Practice honesty.

Corruption begins with dishonesty in a person. With dishonesty, people will easily abuse their power to use or receive money that does not belong to them. The first thing that can be done to prevent corruption is to practice honesty in everyday life. Starting from simple things like being honest in taking exams or not lying to others about small things.

2. Knowing the forms of corruption.

Corruption is not just limited to using or receiving things that don't belong to us. There are many other forms of corruption that you may not know about, such as accepting bribes, cheating in exams, forcibly asking friends for food or money, and others.

By knowing the forms of corruption we can prevent ourselves from doing corruption.

3. Take an active role in preventing corruption.

An example of what we can do is to report to the person who has the authority if we find any

corrupt practices around us.

4. Carry out a task responsibly.

If we are trusted to carry out a task, such as being the treasurer of a class or organization, make sure we carry out the task carefully and responsibly.

5. Prioritizing needs over wants.

Corruption occurs because a person is greedy and wants all the things he wants to be fulfilled at all costs. That's why it's important for us to prioritize what we need so that we don't become greedy people and justify everything to achieve it, including corruption.

Those are some ways to prevent corruption. As students, we can prevent corruption by starting by educating ourselves and practicing it.

Answer:

Explanation:

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) laws and the Statement of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth (SALN) are both important tools for combating corruption and preventing the flow of illicit funds in the country. However, their effectiveness in preventing graft and corruption depends on a number of factors, including the level of enforcement, the strength of the legal system, and the commitment of public officials to transparency and accountability.

Moreover, corruption is often deeply entrenched in political systems and can involve complex networks of actors, making it difficult to detect and prosecute. It is important to ensure that AML laws and SALN requirements are accompanied by strong legal and institutional frameworks, as well as a culture of transparency and accountability in government and society as a whole, to effectively combat corruption and prevent the flow of illicit funds.

29. how can we control the power of the government for us to prevent corruption?


Answer:

Corruption stops when those who run the government demand that it stop. Only when they demand those things needed to stop corruption will it stop. Those things include:

1. Free press and electronic media

2. Open access to most government records by individuals and the media.

3. An active investigation capability by the media.

4. Independent auditors for all government agencies (not appointed by the heads of those agencies).

5. Independent judicial, investigative, and prosecution from the executive branch

6. Strong anti-bribery laws for them to enforce.

7. Active prosecution of those participating in corruption.

8. Limits on campaign contributions and campaign spending on elected offices.

9. Conflict of interest laws preventing investments by government people in companies they regulate, tax, or buy from in their official capacity.

10. A whistleblower reward and protection law

What is corruption?

Corruption is the abuse of office for personal gain, and it takes many forms. It’s the politician taking a bribe before awarding a building contract to the briber. It’s the city council member paying for his family vacation with public funds. It’s the official demanding bribes from citizens in exchange for access to clean water.

Who does it affect?

Corruption affects everyone, especially minorities and vulnerable populations. As the biggest obstacle to economic and social development it harms societies in these ways:

It undermines democracy and human rights by weakening governments.

It diverts funds from public services such as health care, education, and sanitation.

It discourages foreign investment, leading to fewer jobs.
