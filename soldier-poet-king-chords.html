1. The poet of "England in 1819" by Percy Bysshe Shelley characterized the king as being elderly, insane, blind, loathed, and nearing the end of his life.

2. The monarch is looked down upon since the king does not exhibit any genuine human feelings. He relies on the hard work of the country's underprivileged citizens to provide for the country's ruling class.

3. Since this is the case, the prince is just as ineffective as the king, and the same style of leadership will be experienced by future generations. When the king passes away, it is not necessary for there to be any changes made because you are the heir apparent.

4. A leader who is callous and corrupt and who is completely insensitive to the screams of the people.

5. It is a symbol of leadership that draws its sustenance from other people; in other words, the rulers are parasites on their subjects.

6. The poet thinks of England as a kingdom that is on the verge of collapsing because of the insanity and blindness of the king, the muddled genetic line that includes the prince, the foolishness of the nation's authorities, and the absence of moral standing in law and religion. Shelley's profound wrath against the political system of his home country is expressed in "England in 1819" which paints a nightmare vision of English society at the time. An outburst of frustration, but it won't give in to hopelessness even as it vents its ire. Instead, it pivots, in the nick of time, to a vision of guarded hope that predates the particular problems that served as the poem's impetus.

47. Who served as the judge in the story?

c. King Solomon

King Solomon acted as the judge in the story. The two mothers went to him to settle their dispute.

48. What is the possible ending of the story?

b. The second mother ran in shame

Although that may be the story's conclusion, it is not the right one. King Solomon returns the infant to the real mother at the conclusion of the tale. However, since the problem only pertains to the second mother, the most likely outcome is that the second mother flees in shame because King Solomon doesn't come off as a tyrant figure in this story.

49. What is the general mood of story?

a. serious

In the narrative- two moms are fighting over a baby, and the infant is in danger of being split in half. It displays a serious and tense mood.

What is the correct order of the events in the story?

c. III-I-II-IV

First, two mothers approach King Solomon and each claim the baby (III). Then, King Solomon calls a soldier and tells him to cut the baby into two (I). The true mother asks the king not to cut the baby into two (II). Finally, King Solomon gives the baby to the true mother (IV).
