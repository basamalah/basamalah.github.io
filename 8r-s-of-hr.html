24. A car is moving in straight path from 60 km/hr to 45 km/hr in 0.8 hr. What is the acceleration of the car? [A] km/hr Convert your answer in m/s: [B] m/s


We can use the formula for acceleration:

a = (v_f - v_i) / t

where
a = acceleration
v_i = initial velocity
v_f = final velocity
t = time

In this problem, we have:
v_i = 60 km/hr
v_f = 45 km/hr
t = 0.8 hr

Substituting the values into the formula, we get:

a = (45 km/hr - 60 km/hr) / 0.8 hr
a = -15 km/hr / 0.8 hr
a = -18.75 km/hr^2 (Note that the negative sign indicates deceleration)

To convert the answer to m/s^2, we need to multiply by a conversion factor:
1 km/hr^2 = 0.2778 m/s^2

So, we have:

a = -18.75 km/hr^2 x 0.2778 m/s^2/km/hr^2
a = -5.2083 m/s^2

Therefore, the acceleration of the car is -18.75 km/hr^2 or -5.2083 m/s^2 (rounded to four decimal places).

Answer:

1. 89 hr = 3 da & 17 hr

2. 870 s = 14 min & 30 s

3. 47 hr = 2, 820 min

4. 7, 990 min = 5 da, 13 hr & 10 min

5. 37 min = 2, 220 s

Step-by-step explanation:

1. 89 hr x (1 da/24 hr) = 3 da

* 3 da x (24 hr/1 da) = 72 hr

89 hr - 72 hr = 17 hr

2. 870 s x (1 min/60 s) = 14 min

* 14 min x (60 s/1 min) = 840 s

870 s - 840 s = 30 s

3. 47 hr x (60 s/1 hr) = 2, 820 min

4. 7, 990 min x (1 hr/60 min) x (1 da/24 hr) = 5 da

* 5 da x (24 hr/1 da) x (60 min/1 hr) = 7, 220 min

* 7, 990 min - 7, 220 min = 790 min

790 min x (1 hr/60 min) = 13 hr

* 13 hr x (60 min/1 hr) = 780 min

790 min - 780 min = 10 min

5. 37 min x (60 s/1 min) = 2, 220 s
