16. III. Guide Questions: 1. Where will the object be placed on a spotlight to form no image and only an infinite light will occur? 2. How will you describe the image formed on the side mirror of a vehicle? 3. How will you construct an image on a curved mirror using the ray diagramming? IV. Conclusion:​


Answer:

In order for a spotlight to form no image and only an infinite light to occur, the object must be placed at the focal point of the spotlight. The focal point is the point at which parallel rays of light are focused after being reflected by the spotlight.

The image formed on the side mirror of a vehicle is a virtual image. This means that the light rays do not actually converge at the location of the image, but rather appear to come from that location when viewed by an observer. The image is also smaller and laterally inverted compared to the object, meaning that the left side of the object will appear on the right side of the image and vice versa.

To construct an image on a curved mirror using ray diagramming, one must first draw a diagram of the mirror and the object being reflected. Then, the rays of light emanating from the object should be traced as they reflect off of the mirror and converge at the location of the image. This can be done using three rays of light: one ray parallel to the axis of the mirror, one ray that passes through the center of curvature of the mirror, and one ray that reflects off of the mirror and passes through the focal point. These three rays will intersect at the location of the image.
