Her background as a counselor was an asset in her new role as a human resource supervisor, which sometimes involved mitigating conflicts between employee.

When answering questions similar to this, we should make sure that we understand the context. Filling the right vocabulary in the blank requires a thorough knowledge on the meaning of the words as well as ensuring that the vocabulary fit the context of the sentence(s).

In this question, there are two phrases separated by a comma. Each phrase contains a blank with ranges of choices we must choose from. Judging by understanding the whole sentence overall, the answer for the phrase "Her background as a counselor was an [blank] in her new role as a human resource supervisor" is asset.

Here's the breakdown:

Obstacle in option A is not the correct answer because having a background as a counselor and the role of supervisor is a benefit. Meanwhile, option B, C, and D are not the correct options due to their definition: accolade is more like an award, while onus is some sort of of duty or responsibility, and affirmation means asserting.

Meanwhile, the correct vocabulary for the second phrase is mitigating due to the fact that conflict mitigation is an example of a role of human supervisor, and the fact that the subject (her) having an experience in this is a benefit as well. Others answers' definition don't match when used because the context would be less understandable.

Second breakdown:

Option A, negotiating conflict is not the correct answer because conflicts are meant to be solved, not negotiated, and this applies to option B, C, and D as well (managing means exercising control, overseeing means supervising, and this is the role of supervisor, nor human resource, while punishing is to assign penalty). In addition, the closest meaning to solving is mitigating, hence the answer.
