9. If line segment AD bisect angle BAC, the measure of angle B is 60 degrees and the measure of angle C is 30 degrees, what are the measures of:1. Angle BAC2. Angle ADC3. Angle BDA4. Angle DAC5. Angle BAD​


Answer:

1. 90°

2. 105°

3. 75°

4. 45°

5. 45°

Step-by-step explanation:

1. First lets visualize a triangle whose angles are A,B and C respectively. Since we already know that the sum of the interior angles of a triangle is 180° we can just solve it using algebra.

Note that we'll represent Angle BAC as Angle A for this number.

A = 180 - B - C

A = 180 - 60 - 30

A = 90

Angle BAC is 90°

2. To solve for angle ADC we should consider the given values first. That Angle A in ∆ADC is 45° since line segment AD bisects Angle A in ∆ABC which is 90°. and Angle C is 30°. now to solve for Angle ADC we'll just use the same formula in no.1

D = 180 - A - C

D = 180 - 45 - 30

D = 105

Angle ADC is 105°

3. Since Angle BDA and Angle ADC are supplementary angles, we could just use the value at no. 2 to solve this :

BDA + ADC = 180

BDA + 105 = 180

BDA = 180 - 105

BDA = 75

Angle BDA is 75°

4. Since Angle A in ∆ABC is bisected we could just divide Angle A by 2 to get the measurement.

90°/2 = DAC

45° = DAC

Angle DAC is 45°

5. Same proof as in no. 4

∠CAF is the obtuse angle. Other than that, ∠EAC, ∠BAD, and ∠BAF are also obtuse angles. Not so sure about ∠FAD and ∠FAE but they seem to be obtuse too.

Step-by-step explanation:

Obtuse angles are angles that are higher than 90 degrees. Just note that the opposite of obtuse is 'acute'

Tip to remember why an angle is called in such way:

Always think of the keyword 'cute' in 'acute' which will mean that the angle is smaller than 90 degrees, where 'small' is 'cute'

Having this in mind, you'll know the opposite of acute: obtuse. Now for obtuse, think of the keyword 'obese' for 'obtuse' which will mean that the angle is bigger than 90 degrees, where 'obese' is 'big'
