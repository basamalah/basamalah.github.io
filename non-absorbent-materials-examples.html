Answer:

QUESTION:

3. Which of the following statements is true about porous and non- porousa?

A. Porous and non-porous materials are materials that float and sink.

B. Porous materials have the ability to absorb water while non porous do not have the ability to absorb water.

C. Porous and non-porous materials are materials that undergo decay.

D. Porous and non-porous materials undergo chemical change.

ANSWER:

B. Porous materials have the ability to absorb water while non porous do not have the ability to absorb water.

EXPLANATION:

The big difference between porous and non-porous surfaces are their ability to hold water, air, or other liquids. A porous surface has pores which allow things to flow through them more readily versus non-porous materials, which have a much tighter cell structure preventing ease of flow.

CHARACTERISTICS OF NON-POROUS MATERIAL:

Non-porous Surfaces

These are mainly smooth surfaces of which the latent print resides on the surface. Examples of non-porous surfaces include glass, plastics, metals, and varnished wood. Latent prints on non-porous surfaces tend to be fragile, so they must be preserved as soon as possible.

CHARACTERISTICS OF POROUS MATERIAL:

The characteristics of a porous material vary depending on the size, arrangement and shape of the pores, as well as the porosity (the ratio of the total pore volume relative to the apparent volume of the material) and composition of the material itself.

WHAT IS THE NON-POROUS MATERIALS:

The term non-porous means that any kind of liquid and air cannot penetrate the material, and they just remain on the surface.

WHAT IS THE POROUS MATERIALS:

A porous medium or a porous material is a materiao containing pores (voids). ... Many natural substances such as rocks and soil (e.g. aquifers, petroleum reservoirs), zeolites, biological tissues (e.g. bones, wood, cork), and man made materials such as cements and ceramics can be considered as porous media.
