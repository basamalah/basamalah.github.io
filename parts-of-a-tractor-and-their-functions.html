30. Parts/Aapects of a Hand Tractor Purpose and Reason​


Answer:

A hand tractor, also known as a power tiller or a walking tractor, is a versatile agricultural tool used for various farming operations. The main purpose of a hand tractor is to prepare the soil for planting and cultivation by breaking up and tilling the soil. It can also be used for tasks such as plowing, harrowing, seeding, and cultivating crops.

Here are some of the parts and aspects of a hand tractor:

Engine: The engine is the heart of a hand tractor, providing the power needed to operate the machine. Hand tractors typically have gasoline or diesel engines.

Tiller blades: The tiller blades are attached to the engine and are responsible for breaking up the soil and preparing it for planting. The number of blades and their shape can vary depending on the model of the hand tractor.

Wheels: The wheels provide mobility for the hand tractor and allow it to move around the field. They can be adjusted for different row widths and soil conditions.

Handlebars: The handlebars allow the operator to steer the hand tractor and control its movements. They also contain the throttle and other controls for the engine.

Transmission: The transmission is responsible for transferring power from the engine to the wheels and tiller blades. It can be adjusted for different speeds and torque requirements.

Hitch: The hitch is used to attach implements such as plows, cultivators, and seeders to the hand tractor.

The reason for using a hand tractor is to increase efficiency and productivity in farming operations. Hand tractors are more affordable and easier to maintain than larger farm equipment such as tractors. They are also more versatile and can be used for various tasks throughout the growing season. Additionally, hand tractors are useful for small-scale and subsistence farming operations where larger equipment is not practical or necessary.
