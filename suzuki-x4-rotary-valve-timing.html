1. timing diagram of the valve is opening and closing of the valve​


Answer:Valve Timing

A valve timing diagram is a graphical representation of the opening and closing of the intake and exhaust valve of the engine, The opening and closing of the valves of the engine depend upon the movement of piston from TDC to BDC, This relation between piston and valves is controlled by setting a graphical representation between these two, which is known as valve timing diagram.

The valve timing diagram comprises of a 360 degree figure which represents the movement of the piston from TDC to BDC in all the strokes of the engine cycle, Which is measured in degrees and the opening and closing of the valves is controlled according to these degrees.

6. Which of these is an incorrect statement concerning the heartbeat? a. The atria contract at the same time. b. The ventricles relax at the same time. c. The atrioventricular valves open at the same time. d. The semilunar valves open at the same time. e. First the right side contracts, and then the left side contracts.​


e. First the right side contracts, and then the left side contracts is an incorrect statement concerning the heartbeat.

During the cardiac cycle, the atria contract together (atrial systole), and then the ventricles contract together (ventricular systole). This allows for coordinated blood flow through the heart and into the circulatory system. The atrioventricular valves and semilunar valves also open and close in a coordinated manner to ensure efficient blood flow.

Thus, option (e) is incorrect because both sides of the heart contract simultaneously during ventricular systole, rather than one side contracting before the other.
