1. What does the verse Romans 8:28 KJV mean? Explain


Answer:

Romans 8:18–30 talks about the participation of Christians in the everyday suffering experienced by all of creation. We all groan together as a woman in labor while we wait for God to reveal His children. As His children, we are waiting for the Father to complete our adoption by redeeming our bodies so that we can be with Him. God's Spirit helps us in the season of waiting by taking our unformed prayers to God. We trust that God uses every circumstance in our lives for His purposes and that He has chosen us long ago to be His children.

There's the gospel of the kingdom of God, and then there's the gospel of salvation, which is the one Paul preached, and the one we should be living by today. The message of this gospel is that christ died on the cross for the penalty of your sins, was buried and rose again the third day to give you everlasting life. Paul taught that, if you just believe that, you shall be saved. Nothing else required! That's the gospel of today. 22. Lesson # 1 1. The Word of God is active in our salvation. Briefly explain how the following verses apply to this. a. 1 Peter 1:23 b. Romans 10:17 C. James 1:21​


Explanation:

a. 1 Peter 1:23 - This verse states that we are born again not by corruptible seed, but by the incorruptible Word of God which lives and abides forever. It implies that the Word of God has the power to bring spiritual life and salvation to those who believe.

b. Romans 10:17 - This verse declares that faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the Word of God. It emphasizes the importance of hearing and believing the Word of God as a means to obtain salvation.

c. James 1:21 - This verse instructs us to receive with meekness the engrafted Word which is able to save our souls. It highlights the need to not only hear, but also receive and apply the Word of God in our lives for our salvation.
