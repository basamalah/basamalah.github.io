1. rizal's execution reflection in CERA format​


CERA (Context, Experience, Reflection, Action) is a reflective framework that helps individuals to explore their experiences and develop insights. Here's a possible reflection on Rizal's execution using the CERA format:

Context: Dr. Jose Rizal was a national hero of the Philippines who was executed by the Spanish colonial government on December 30, 1896, for his role in the Philippine Revolution.

Experience: As a Filipino, I feel a deep sense of sadness and anger whenever I think about Rizal's execution. It was a brutal act of injustice that robbed the Philippines of one of its greatest minds and patriots. Rizal was not just a writer, but also a doctor, a scholar, a linguist, and a visionary who believed in the power of education and enlightenment to uplift his people. He used his pen and his voice to expose the injustices and abuses of the Spanish colonial regime and to inspire his fellow Filipinos to fight for their rights and freedom. His death was a tragic loss for the Philippines and a stark reminder of the atrocities committed by the colonial oppressors.

Reflection: Rizal's execution reminds me of the importance of courage, resilience, and sacrifice in the face of oppression and adversity. Rizal could have chosen to live a comfortable and privileged life as a doctor and a writer, but he chose to use his talents and his voice to fight for his people's rights and dignity. He knew that his actions could lead to his arrest, imprisonment, and execution, but he did not back down from his principles and his mission. His bravery and selflessness inspire me to stand up for what is right, even if it means going against the tide or risking my own safety. Rizal's execution also highlights the power of ideas, words, and symbols in shaping the course of history. Rizal's writings and teachings continue to influence generations of Filipinos and to challenge the status quo of ignorance, apathy, and oppression. His death may have silenced his voice, but it amplified his message and his legacy.

Action: As a Filipino, I vow to honor Rizal's memory and legacy by continuing his fight for justice, freedom, and dignity. I will strive to educate myself and others about Rizal's life, works, and ideals, and to apply his lessons and values to my own life and community. I will also work to promote the values of critical thinking, creativity, and empathy that Rizal espoused, and to resist the forces of ignorance, intolerance, and authoritarianism that threaten our democracy and our human rights. Finally, I will support the efforts of my fellow Filipinos to build a better and more just society, one that upholds the dignity and well-being of all its citizens, regardless of their social status, gender, religion, or ethnicity.

Answer:

Content:

Confucianism is a philosophy and ethical system that originated in China around 500 BCE. It is based on the teachings of Confucius, a Chinese philosopher who emphasized the importance of personal and governmental morality, justice, and sincerity. Confucianism also stresses the importance of education and the cultivation of virtue, particularly the virtue of ren, which involves treating others with respect, kindness, and compassion.

Experience:

After learning about Confucianism in my world religions class, I was intrigued by the philosophy's emphasis on morality and virtue. I was particularly struck by the concept of ren and the importance of treating others with kindness and compassion. As someone who strives to be a kind and compassionate person, I found this aspect of Confucianism to be very appealing.

Reflection:

Upon further reflection, I also appreciate the emphasis that Confucianism places on education and the cultivation of virtue. In a world that often values material possessions and personal gain above all else, it is refreshing to be reminded that true happiness and fulfillment come from being a virtuous person who contributes positively to society. I also think that Confucianism's emphasis on personal and governmental morality is particularly relevant in today's world, where corruption and dishonesty seem to be all too common.

Application:

As I think about how to apply the principles of Confucianism to my own life, I am reminded of the importance of treating others with kindness and compassion. Whether it's interacting with friends and family, coworkers, or strangers on the street, I can strive to be more mindful of how my actions impact others and to always treat people with respect and empathy. Additionally, I can focus on cultivating virtues such as honesty, sincerity, and humility, and strive to be a positive influence in my community. By incorporating these principles into my life, I can strive to live a more virtuous and fulfilling life, in line with the teachings of Confucianism.

In conclusion, Confucianism is a rich and complex philosophy that emphasizes the importance of personal and governmental morality, education, and the cultivation of virtue. By reflecting on and applying these principles to our own lives, we can strive to be better people and to make a positive impact on the world around us.
