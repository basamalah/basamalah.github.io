2. What are the Standard requirements for tourist air transport?


Answer:

1. Valid Passport: Passengers must have a valid passport to travel by air. The passport should be valid for at least six months beyond the date of departure.

2. Health Requirements: Some countries may require tourists to provide proof of vaccination against certain diseases or undergo a medical examination before entry.

3. Luggage Limitations: Airlines typically impose weight and size limits for carry-on and checked luggage.

4. Check-In Time: Passengers are expected to arrive at the airport at least 2-3 hours before their scheduled flight departure time to complete check-in and security screening.

5. Airport Security: Passengers must go through airport security screening, which includes body scanning and baggage screening, to ensure safety onboard the flight.

6. Flight Ticket: Passengers must have a valid ticket to board the flight.

7. Travel Insurance: It is recommended that tourists purchase travel insurance to cover unforeseen circumstances such as flight cancellations or medical emergencies while abroad.

4. How does transportation links tourists to different tourist attractions?​


Answer:

Transportation plays a crucial role in connecting tourists to different tourist attractions. It provides the physical means for tourists to travel from one place to another, allowing them to access and explore various attractions efficiently. Here are some ways transportation links tourists to different tourist attractions:

Accessibility: Transportation networks such as roads, railways, airports, and waterways provide access to tourist attractions that may be located in different geographical locations. They allow tourists to reach destinations that may be otherwise difficult or impossible to reach without adequate transportation options.

Convenience: Transportation provides convenience to tourists by offering various modes of transport such as buses, trains, ferries, and flights that cater to different preferences, budgets, and schedules. This makes it easier for tourists to plan their trips and visit multiple attractions in a region or country.

Efficiency: Efficient transportation links, such as well-connected highways, railways, or air routes, enable tourists to travel quickly and easily between different tourist attractions, saving time and effort. This enhances the overall travel experience and allows tourists to visit more attractions within a limited timeframe.

Flexibility: Transportation options allow tourists to choose the most suitable mode of transport based on their preferences, budget, and itinerary. For example, tourists can opt for public transportation for budget travel or rent a car for more flexibility and convenience. This flexibility enables tourists to visit various attractions at their own pace and convenience.

Economic impact: Transportation links tourists to different tourist attractions, creating economic opportunities for local communities. Improved transportation infrastructure can boost tourism-related businesses, such as hotels, restaurants, tour operators, and souvenir shops, providing livelihoods for local residents and supporting the local economy.

In summary, transportation serves as a critical link between tourists and different tourist attractions by providing accessibility, convenience, efficiency, flexibility, and creating economic impacts. It plays a significant role in facilitating travel and enhancing the overall tourist experience. So, reliable and well-connected transportation networks are essential for the development and sustainability of tourism in any destination.

Could You Own a Franchise?

Franchisors are seeking individuals with the ability to soak up their knowledge and systems, and develop a successful business through a mixture of hard work and the proper implementation of their business concept.

MOTIVATION

Franchisors seek franchisees that are looking to run their businesses in a hands-on fashion because they want to take advantage of that desire for business success. This is a desire that just cannot be matched by a salaried manager and this is why franchised operations will tend to offer greater quality of service and place more emphasis on customer satisfaction. Franchisors are, therefore, seeking prospective franchisees that have a genuine enthusiasm for their industry, their service or product, and for building a successful business. This enthusiasm is usually prized more highly than industry experience – the franchisor's training and support should overcome any disparity in this area.

OPERATING THE SYSTEM

In buying a franchise, you are investing in a proven and fine tuned business blueprint. It is, therefore, important that you are willing to work within the confines of the franchisor's successful systems and procedures. This is partly because the franchisor has already gone through the trial and error development, learned how to avoid mistakes and performed the fine tuning in developing its business package on behalf of the franchisee, and partly to encourage standardisation of the franchisor's products and services nationwide. This ensures that no franchisee is producing products or services to a different standard to the rest of the network, which could damage the brand. A franchisee that comes to resent the authority of the franchisor is one that was never suited to becoming a franchisee in the first place, and these situations are easier to avoid than to remedy. If you are the type of entrepreneur who wants total control to tinker with the business, you may be better off considering other avenues of business ownership.

WORK ETHIC

The decision to become self-employed involves a great deal of soul-searching – what prompts it can as often be the desire to escape employment as the drive to build a successful organisation. However, if you're interested in becoming a business owner in order to cut down your hours, think again. With the future of your business in your own hands, you won't be able to switch off at 5pm – you may find yourself working six days a week, until well past this time, in the initial stages. Franchisors realise this but, while many will offer plenty of support to help you get your business off the ground, they can not do it for you.

FINANCIAL CONSIDERATIONS

Franchises are very affordable, with some superb opportunities available for an initial investment under £15,000. Additional funding is readily available from the banking industry, within which there is a great deal of recognition for the reduced risk inherent in franchising. Like you, many bank managers will be well aware of the survival rate of franchises compared with that of small businesses. Many banks have now set up specialist franchise departments and have developed relationships with franchise brands seeking to assist their franchisees in winning loans. However, the income derived from owning your own business will prove much more irregular than that which is earned through employment. There is no pension or sick pay – barring what you set up for yourself – and much of what the business does earn may be required for reinvestment as you grow your operation. Can you deal with this insecurity, and are you able to support yourself through it until your business start-up finds its feet? Many franchisors will help you develop your business plan, but it will take determination on your part to see it through.

29. this the primary factor of requirement when planning to franchise with renowned beauty salon​


Answer:

The primary factor of requirement when planning to franchise with a renowned beauty salon is the budget. You should have enough capital to invest in franchising. Franchising is a type of business where there is a contractual relationship between a licensor (franchisor) and a licensee (franchisee) that allows the business owner to use the licensor's brand. Normally, a franchisee needs a huge capital investment because the business to be franchised is already established in the market. There is also a number of documents to be processed before putting up a franchise business.

Explanation:

DOCUMENTS NEEDED IN FRANCHISING A BUSINESS IN THE PHILIPPINES

Required Documents to Be Submitted to the Franchisor

1. Letter of Intent to Franchise. This Letter of Intent is used for sketching the different terms and conditions of the deal and acts as an agreement between two parties, in this case, the franchisor and the franchisee.

2. Application Form. Interested franchisee must accomplish an application form that usually comes from the company he is interested in doing business and must be filled out completely and correctly.

3. Site Location Proposal. The owner usually wants to know the location of the business and sets a criterion. It needs to be accessible and with high market value.

Business Documents You Need to File Before Starting Your Franchise Business

1. Business Name Registration (Department of Trade and Industry). Your business name needs to be registered with DTI. DTI is the government’s hand in overseeing and facilitating the creation of business and it governs the registration of business and trade names in the Philippines.

2. Barangay Clearance. After DTI, it is time to get a clearance from the barangay where your business is located. To apply for the Barangay Clearance, you need to show the Business Name Registration from the Department of Trade and Industry and other requirements like the Copy of Lease (if you are renting) , or a copy of the Land Title and Tax Declaration (if you own the property).

3. Business or Mayor’s Permit. Business or Mayor’s Permit is a license from the Local Government Unit that is deemed important and a prerequisite for a business to start operating. In applying for a Mayor’s Permit, you must have completed the following basic requirements like:

i. Barangay Clearance

ii. Business Name Certificate from the DTI

iii. Lease of Contract or Tax Declaration/Land Title

iv. SSS Certification

v. Community Tax Certificate or CEDULA
