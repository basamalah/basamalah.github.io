3. in terms of architectural designs, what are the similarities of chinese, japanese, and korean architecture?​


East Asia has a vast variety of environments, and it would be a disservice to not discuss them briefly to understand their impact on architecture. While China inarguable possesses the widest range of diverse environments, what is often considered quintessential Chinese civilization was based in the Central Plains, or Zhongyuan region, which is defined by large alluvial plains that allowed for large scale agricultural. Though the region is also bordered by significant mountain ranges, most of the nations human population has resided in these plains, which in turn had the strongest influence on their architectural development. For this reason, earth and brickwork are significant in China’s architectural history.

Korea, on the other hand, is a very mountainous region, providing a high availability of timber and very rich granite deposits. Though large cities were often based in plains or river valleys (true enough for any civilization), stone and woodworks were more definitive for their national architecture.

Japan is somewhat similar to Korea in its environment, being very mountainous with larger settlements being based in plains and valleys. Unfortunately, being so close to a tectonic boundary makes Japan considerably less geologically stable than Korea or China. Therefore, Japan’s architecture is defined primarily on woodworks which were more suited to standing up to frequent earthquake activity.

23. japanese colonization in the philippines


Around 1942 and 1945, when Imperial Japan invaded the Commonwealth Philippines during World War II, the Japanese invasion of the Philippines took place. It was the shortest occupation/colonization but was considered the most gruesome.

The Philippine invasion began on December 8, 1941, ten hours after the Pearl Harbor attack. As at Pearl Harbor, the initial Japanese attack caused severe damage to American aircraft. The American Asian Fleet in the Philippines, without air cover, returned to Java on December 12, 1941. General Douglas MacArthur was ordered to leave, leaving his men for Australia, at Corregidor on the night of March 11, 1942. Bataan's 76,000 starving and wounded American and Filipino defenders surrendered on April 9, 1942, and were forced to endure the notorious Bataan Death March of which 7,000–10,000 died or were killed. Corregidor's 13,000 prisoners surrendered on May 6.

For more than three years, Japan controlled the Philippines before Japan's surrender. Philippine resistance forces ' highly effective guerrilla campaign occupied 60% of the islands, mainly jungle and mountain areas. The Filipinos remained loyal to the Americans with the hope that they gain independence from their colonizers. The women also became "comfort women" for the japanese soldiers. Over a thousand women, some of them under the age of 18, were incarcerated as "comfort women" throughout the Philippines, held in sexual slavery for Japanese military personnel during the occupation. During the invasion, each of the Japanese military facilities in the Philippines had a site where the women were kept and they called it a "comfort station," like Bahay na Pula.

24. Architectures in 1898 in the philippines


Answer:

After the Philippines was ceded to the United States as a consequence of the Spanish–American War in 1898, the architecture of the Philippines was dominated by American aesthetics. In this period, the plan for the modern City of Manila was designed, with many neoclassical architecture and art deco buildings by famous American and Filipino architects. During World War II, large portions of Intramuros and Manila were destroyed. Many heritage districts in the provinces were burned down by the Japanese before the end of the war. In the reconstruction period after the Second World War, many of the destroyed buildings were rebuilt, however, a majority of heritage structures, especially in the provinces, were lost and never rebuilt. Most of the structures that were lost are considered a focal properties of former heritage towns.
