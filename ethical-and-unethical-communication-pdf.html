10. To be wealthy by all means, is it ethical or unethical? Why​


The statement is unethical.

The phrase “by all means” implies any act or behavior that can lead to being wealthy.  That must include all sorts of acts whether good or evil.

To be wealthy by all means may signify a person’s desire to work hard and work more just to acquire higher pay or compensation.  However, working hard alone at the expense of one’s health capacity, for example, is still unethical because a person neglects his or her health just to have wealth.

In addition, the statement may also imply that a person can do evil to have wealth in his or her possession, and that maybe is to steal from others, which obviously is unethical.

Things like water and air pollution, the use of natural resources, loss of biodiversity, destruction of the community of living creatures, and global climate change are all part of the environmental ethics.
