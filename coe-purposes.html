9. Abe can paint the room in 15 hours, Bea can paint 50% faster than Abe, and Coe can paint twice as fast as Abe. Abe begins to paints the room and work alone for the first hour and a half. Then Bea joins Abe, and they work together until half the room is painted. Then Coe joins Abe and Bea, and they work together until the entire room is painted. WORK-RELATED PROBLEMS

Problem:

Abe can paint the room in 15 hours, Bea can paint 50% faster than Abe, and Coe can paint twice as fast as Abe. Abe begins to paints the room and work alone for the first hour and a half. Then Bea joins Abe, and they work together until half the room is painted. Then Coe joins Abe and Bea, and they work together until the entire room is painted. Find the number of minutes after Abe begins for the three of them to finish painting the room.

Answer with Step-by-step explanation:

In order to solve this word problem involving work, it is important to know the formula for work which is: W = rt, where W refers to the work, r refers to the rate of work and t refers to the time.

Since Abe can finish painting the room alone in 15 hours, her rate can be computed by manipulating the formula above which is: r = W/t. We consider the room as 1, so Abe can finish 1/15 of the room in an hour. Since Bea can work 50% faster that Abe, her rate is 1.5 of Abe's rate. 1.5 is 3/2 in fraction. By multiplying: 1/15 × 3/2 = 3/30 = 1/10. So Bea's rate is 1/10 which means that in an hour, she can finish 1/10 of the room to paint. For Coe, since he is twice as fast as Abe, just simply multiply 2 to Abe's rate. So Coe's rate is 2 × 1/15 = 2/15 which means that he can finish 2/15 of the room. Now let us do the analysis.

For 1 and a half hours, Abe did the work alone. So:

[tex]\frac{3}{2}[/tex] × [tex]\frac{1}{15}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{3}{30}=\frac{1}{10}[/tex]

So Abe finished 1/10 of the room for 1 and 1/2 hours.

When Bea joined Abe, they worked together to paint the half of the entire room. Let us subtract 1/10 from 1/2 which is 2/5 because this is the remaining part of the room that both of them finished painting. We can expressed this as:

[tex]t(\frac{1}{15} + \frac{1}{10})=\frac{2}{5} \\t(\frac{2+3}{30} )=\frac{2}{5} \\2t + 3t = \frac{30(2)}{5} \\5t = \frac{60}{5}\\5t = 12\\\frac{5t}{5}=\frac{12}{5}\\t = \frac{12}{5}[/tex]

So, Bea and Abe finish painting the half of the entire room in 12/5 hours.

When Coe joined them, there is only 1/2 of the room remaining to complete painting it so:

[tex]\frac{1}{2}=t(\frac{1}{15}+\frac{2}{15}+\frac{1}{10})\\\frac{1}{2}=t(\frac{3}{15}+\frac{1}{10})\\\frac{1}{2}=t(\frac{6+3}{30})\\\frac{1}{2}=t(\frac{9}{30})\\t=\frac{1}{2}(\frac{30}{9})\\t= \frac{30}{18}\\t=\frac{5}{3}[/tex]

So, the three of them finish the remaining half in 5/3 hours.

Now we have to add up all the the time: 1 1/2 + 12/5 + 5/3 = 3/2 + 12/5 + 5/3 = [(3×15)+(6×12)+(10×5)]/30 = (45+72+50)/30 = 167/30 hours

But we need to convert this to minutes, using the conversion factor: 1 hours = 60 minutes.

[tex]\frac{167}{30}[/tex] hours × [tex]\frac{60 minutes}{1hr}[/tex] = 167 × 2 minutes = 334 minutes

Therefore, starting from Abe, the entire room was finished painting in 334 minutes.
