Why did iron filings stick to magnets?

Answer:

Since iron is a ferromagnetic material, a magnetic field induces each particle to become a tiny bar magnet.

The south pole of each particle then attracts the north pole of it's neighbors, and this process repeated over a while area creates chains of fillings parallel to the direction of magnetic field.
