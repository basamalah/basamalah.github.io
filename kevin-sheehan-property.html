5. Kevin’s father is 3 times as old as Kevin. In 10 years, the sum of their ages is 76. How old was his father when Kevin was born?pls show explanation


✏️AGES===============================

Problem: Kevin’s father is 3 times as old as Kevin. In 10 years, the sum of their ages is 76. How old was his father when Kevin was born?

Solution: Let x and y as the ages of Kevin’s Father and Kevin respectively. Make equations of the given statement.

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 3y & \green{(eq. \: 1)} \\ (x + 10) + (y + 10) = 76& \green{(eq. \: 2)}\end{cases}[/tex]

- Substitute x from the first equation to the second equation in terms of y.

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 3y \\ (3y + 10) + (y + 10) = 76\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 3y \\ 3y + 10 + y + 10 = 76\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 3y \\ 4y + 20 = 76\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 3y \\ 4y = 76 - 20\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 3y \\ 4y =56\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 3y \\ \begin{gathered} \frac{ \cancel4y}{ \cancel4} = \frac{56}{4} \end{gathered}\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 3y \\ y =14\end{cases}[/tex]

- The age of Kevin is 14 years old. Substitute it to the first equation to find the age of his Father.

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 3(14) \\ y =14\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 42 \\ y =14\end{cases}[/tex]

- The age of Kevin’s Father is 42 years old. Find his age when Kevin was born by subtracting his age and Kevin’s age.

[tex]42 - 14 = 28[/tex]

- Therefore, the age of Kevin’s Father when Kevin was born is:

[tex] \large \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 28 \: years \: old \: }}[/tex]===============================

18. Kevin is 33 times as old as Daniel. 44 years ago, Kevin was 55 times as old as Daniel. How old is Kevin now?


Here is the answer and the steps in determining how old is Kevin now.

Answer:

Kevin is 3,564 years old now.

Step-by-step explanation:

1. Based on the given information, determine the equations.

Let K = age of Kevin Let D = age of Daniel

2. Equation 1 will be K = 33D for "Kevin is 33 times as old as Daniel."

3. Equation 2 will be K - 44 = 55 × (D - 44) for "44 years ago, Kevin was 55 times as old as Daniel."

4. Substitute the value of K of equation 1 to equation 2.

33D - 44 = 55 × (D - 44)

5. After distributing 55 of the right side, resulting equation is 33D - 44 = 55D - 2,420.

6. Transpose similar terms. Resulting equation will be -22D = -2376.

7. Divide both sides by -22 to derive the value of D. D = 108 (age of Daniel)

8. Substitute this value of D to equation 1. K = 33 × 108

9. K = 3,564 age of Kevin

That is the answer and the steps in determining how old is Kevin now.

26. Kevin is four years younger than Nicole. In 7 years, Kevin will be 4/5 the age of Nicole. How old is Kevin?(Write your answer in this format: ___ years old) ​


AGE PROBLEMSProblem:

Kevin is four years younger than Nicole. In 7 years, Kevin will be 4/5 the age of Nicole. How old is Kevin?

Solution:

With the given data, we can first list them down and analyze in order to solve for the required data. Let us put the present and future.

• Present

Let Kevin's age be "x" and Nicole's age be "x+4".

• Future (After 7 years)

Kevin's age will be "x+7" and Nicole's age be "x+4+7" which can be simplified to "x+11".

Using this, we can formula an equation.

Since Kevin's age is 4/5 of Nicole's age 7 years later, then the equation will be: K = 4/5N, where K is Kevin's age and N is Nicole's age in this equation.

But to recall, 7 year later, Kevin's age is also x+7 while Nicole's age is x+11. With this, we will substitute it to the derived equation above, so:

K = 4/5N

x+7 = 4/5(x+11)

Let us multiply both side by 5 to get:

5(x+7) = 4(x+11)

And distribute each to simplify:

5x + 35 = 4x + 44

Transpose 4x to the left side of the equation and 35 to the right side:

5x - 4x = 44 - 35

x = 9

Remember that x is equal to Kevin's age.

To check if our answer is correct:

• Present

Kevin = x = 9

Nicole = x+4 = 13

• Future

Kevin = x+7 = 9+7 = 16

Nicole = x+11 = 9+11 = 20

16/20 = 4/5

So 16 is 4/5 of 20.

Therefore, Kevin's age is 9 years old.

28. Kevin is jumping to a height of 5m in a 3rd floor building.Determine the take off speed of Kevin.


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

Kevin is jumping to a height of 5m in a 3rd floor building.Determine the take off speed of Kevin.

Given Data:

Vf (final velocity) = 0 m/s

a (acceleration) = -9.8 m/s²

d (distance) = 5 m

Vi (initial velocity) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can use the formula Vf² = Vi² + 2ad and to find Vi, we can use the formula Vi = √ - 2ad since Vf = 0m/s.

Vi = √ - 2ad

   = √ - 2 (-9.8 m/s²)(5 m)

   = √98 m²/s²

   = 9.9 m/s

Answer:

The take off speed of Kevin is 9.9 m/s

Kevin will have an initial velocity of 9.9 m/s. His velocity will be decreased by 9.8 m/s every second since it is decelerating due to gravity. As it reaches the maximum height, its velocity will be zero since it will momentarily stops at maximum height before he falls back to the ground.
