1. ecological solid waste management


Answer:

Details about the Ecological Solid Waste Management

Explanation:

Ecological Solid Waste Management is the systematic management of policies and activities that allow for segregation of solid waste at the source, transport of segregate waste, storage, transferring, processing, treating and disposing of solid waste in ways that would not harm the environment.

The Philippines generate on average 35,000 tonnes of solid waste daily. The bulk of this is of course from Metro Manila. A good percentage of these wastes are burned by people and this leads to an increase in greenhouse gases, not considering the toxic fumes that people inhale. Without a proper waste management policy and practice in place, these garbage would end up harming humans and the environment.

The most effective strategy in ecological solid waste management is through Reduce, Reuse and Recycle. If we reduce our consumption, there would be less to dispose of. We can also reuse things we already have. Plastic bags could be reused as garbage bags.

We also need to sort our wastes so that those we can use as compost are separate from those that we can recycle.

“Ecological Solid Waste Management Act of 2000”.

•Section 2. Declaration of Policies. -- It is hereby

declared the policy of the State to adopt a systematic,

comprehensive and ecological solid waste management

program which shall:

¤(a) Ensure the protection of public health and environment;

¤(b) Utilize environmentally-sound methods that maximize the

resources conservation and recovery;

¤(c) Set guidelines and targets for solid waste avoidance and

volume reduction through source reduction and waste

re-use, recovery, green charcoal process, and others,
