4. This type of mountain rage is not formed the Earth's internal activity, but through rock weathering.A.Dome mountain rageB.Erosion-formed mountain rageC.Fault-block mountain rageD.Folded mountain rage​


The answer is B. Erosion of the mountains that form Wrath. Starting from erosion, which then results in weathering.

Erosion of the mountains. This occurs during and after an uplift, where a newly formed mountainous region is subjected to the effects of wind, water, ice, and gravity. These forces actively shape the surface of mountain ranges, wearing down the exposed surfaces, depositing sediment in alluvial flows, and leading to the formation of characteristic landforms.

The other three options are less precise because:

Volcanic mountains are formed when a tectonic plate is pushed beneath another (or above a mid-ocean ridge or hotspot) where magma is forced to the surface. When the magma reaches the surface, it often builds a volcanic mountain, such as a shield volcano or a stratovolcano.As the name suggests, fold mountains occur when two tectonic plates collide at a convergent plate boundary, causing the crust to over thick. This process forces the less dense crust to float on top of the denser mantle rocks, with material being forced upwards to form hills, plateaus, or mountains while a greater volume of material is forced downward into the mantle.Block mountains are caused by faults in the crust, a seam where rocks can move past each other. Also known as rifting, this process occurs when rocks on one side of a fault rise relative to the other. The uplifted blocks become block mountains (also known as horsts), while the intervening dropped blocks are known as graben.

SPEED OF A REACTION

ANSWER:

The agents that have the potential to increase the speed of a reaction are catalysts, a larger contact surface and heat. Therefore, the correct answer is letter C.

EXPLANATION:

Chemical kinetics

The speed of a reaction is defined by phenomena that contemplate the concept of chemical kinetics. It is this area that studies not only the chemical reactions themselves, but also the factors that can influence their realization. These reactions are defined as the transformations that occur between two or more substances that react with each other.

Chemical reactions, in turn, can be classified according to the speed at which they occur. We have slow reactions, like a burning candle, and fast reactions, like explosions, for example. This speed can be influenced by a number of factors, such as the contact surface, reagent concentration and heat.

11. what is a catalyst? explain how a catalyst will speed up a chemical reaction​


Answer:

what is catalyst?

a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change.

how a catalyst will speed up a chemical reaction?

A catalyst is a substance that can be added to a reaction to increase the reaction rate without getting consumed in the process. Catalysts typically speed up a reaction by reducing the activation energy or changing the reaction mechanism. Enzymes are proteins that act as catalysts in biochemical reactions.
