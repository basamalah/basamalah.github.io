1. who is Alejandro R. Roces ​


Answer:

Alejandro Reyes Roces (13 July 1924 – 23 May 2011) was a Filipino author, essayist, dramatist and a National Artist of the Philippines for literature. He served as Secretary of Education from 1962 to 1965, during the term of Philippine President Diosdado Macapagal.

27. What message does the writer wanted to send us in the short story something to crow about by alejandro roces


Answer:

The short story "Something to Crow About" by Alejandro Roces is a satirical commentary on the Filipino obsession with social status and the lengths that people will go to maintain their position in society. The story centers around a group of wealthy families who compete with each other to have the loudest and most impressive roosters for cockfighting. The protagonist, Kiko, is a poor farmer who dreams of owning a rooster that can help him win enough money to support his family.

Throughout the story, Roces highlights the absurdity and cruelty of cockfighting, as well as the destructive effects of social climbing on individuals and families. The story ultimately suggests that the pursuit of social status and wealth can lead people to lose sight of what is truly important in life, such as family, community, and personal values.

In summary, the message that the writer wanted to send in "Something to Crow About" is a critique of the Filipino obsession with social status and the negative consequences that can result from this obsession, such as the exploitation of animals and the loss of personal values and relationships.
