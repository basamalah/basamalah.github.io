2. What is formal education, elementary education, secondary education, tertiary education, vocational education, non formal education and special education?


Hey dear

So your answer is

•Formal education - It is a kind of education normally delivered by trained teachers in a systematic intentional way within a school, higher education and University.

•Elementary education - it is typically the first stage of formal education coming after preschool and before •Secondary Education.

Secondary education - it is the final and second basic phase of the education which comes before tertiary education.

•Tertiary education - it is commonly referred to as post Secondary Education referred to academic persuits undertaken after High School.

•Vocational education - it is a kind of education that prepares people to work in various jobs, such as trade ,craft or as technician.

•Non formal education - non formal education is about acknowledging the importance of education, learning and training which takes place outside a recognised institution.

•Special education - it is a practice of educating students in a way that addresses their individual differences and needs.

Mabuhay Elementary School Nutrition Month

The essay above shows a news article about Mabuhay Elementary School Nutrition Month which include headline, lead, secondary lead, and others. In the article, the headline is "Mabuhay celebrates nutrition month." The lead here is the entire first sentence of the essay. Meanwhile, the secondary lead is the sentence where it is explained that the "highlight of the program was the coronation of the "Nutri King and Queen"." Others include mentioning the activities that took place during the celebration, the duration, and the promotion of healthy lifestyle among children.

Locating the Parts of a News Article in Mabuhay Elementary School Nutrition Month Essay

In order to locate the headline, lead, secondary lead, and others, here are important details to remember:

Headline - the title of the storyLead - usually an opening paragraph that determines the five Ws and 1 H questions; contains the most important information about the articleSecondary lead - another feature worth mentioning in the news (ex. highlights of the event).Others - additional information about the headline or the story in focus.
