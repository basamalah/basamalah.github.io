4. the pressue of gas varies directly as its temperature and inversley as its volume​


Answer:

The pressure, P, of a gas varies directly with its temperature, T, and inversely with its volume, V, according to the equation P=nRT/V, where n is the number of molar units and R is the universal gas constant.

[tex] \begin{gathered} c = \frac{k}{d} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} 7 = \frac{k}{4} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} 4⋅7 = \frac{k}{4} ⋅4 \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} 4⋅7 = \frac{k}{\cancel4} ⋅\cancel4 \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} 4⋅7 = k\end{gathered}[/tex]

22. In newton's second law of motion what does directly proportional and inversley proportional means?


Newton's 2nd Law of Motion: The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the force exerted upon it and inversely proportional to the mass of the object.

Directly proportional: When x increases, y also increases.
When force increases, acceleration also increases, and vice versa.

Inversely proportional: When x increases, y decreases.
When mass increases, acceleration decreases, and vice versa.
