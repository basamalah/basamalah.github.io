22. Suppose that a set of exam scores has a mean of 75 and a variance of 25 Assume that the exam scores are normally distributed. What is the probability that an exam score is between 75 and 77?


PROBLEM:

Suppose that a set of exam scores has a mean of 75 and a variance of 25. Assume that the exam scores are normally distributed. What is the probability that an exam score is between 75 and 77?

SOLUTION:

Step 1: Identify the parts of the word problem. The word problem will identify:

The mean (average or μ) — 75

Standard deviation (σ) — 5

Standard deviation is the square root of variance. [tex]\rm \sqrt{25} = 5 [/tex]

X: the numbers associated with “between” — 75 and 77

Step 2: Figure out the z-scores and Find the area in the z-table. Plug the X values into the z value formula and solve. The μ (the mean), is 75.

[tex]\small\underline{\boxed{z=\frac{X-μ}{σ}}}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{75-75}{5}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{0}{5}[/tex]

[tex]z=0=0.00000[/tex]

NOTE: If a Z-score is 0, it indicates that the data point's score is identical to the mean score.

[tex]\small\underline{\boxed{z=\frac{X-μ}{σ}}}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{77-75}{6}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{2}{5}[/tex]

[tex]z=0.4=0.15542[/tex]

See the attached pictures for the z-table and the graph (illustration).

Step 3: Add the computed z-scores to get the probability or area between 0 and 0.4. You can also convert them in percent.

[tex]\boxed{0.00000+0.15542=0.15542=15.54\%}[/tex]

ANSWER:The probability that an exam score is between 75 and 77 is 0.15542.

28. Suppose your grades on three exams are 80,93, and 91, what grade will you need on your next exam to have an average of 90 on the four exams?


Hello, reapthereaper2322! I will be answering your question today...

---------------------------------------------------------

Your question is:

Suppose your grades on three exams are 80, 93, and 91. What grade will you need on your next exam to have an average of 90 on the four exams?

We will use the formula for calculating the mean average to solve for x where x is the required grade on the next exam to have an average of 90 on all the four exams.

[tex] \large \text{Average} = \frac{\text{sum of the values}}{\text{number of the values}} [/tex]

[tex] 90 = \frac{ 80 + 93 + 91 + x }{4} [/tex]

[tex] 4( 90 = \frac{ 80 + 93 + 91 + x }{4}) 4 [/tex]

[tex] 360 = 80 + 93 + 91 + x [/tex]

[tex] 360 - 80 - 93 - 91 = x [/tex]

[tex] \large \green{x = 96} [/tex]

Therefore, you will need a grade of at least 96 on your last exam to have an average of at least 90 on all the four exams.
