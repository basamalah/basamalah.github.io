7. Essay about cause and effect about cyber bullying


Most people know that bullying is wrong. Calling someone names has absolutely no beneficial purpose. Moreover, hitting someone makes a bully feel good in the moment while doing permanent damage to the person being victimized. With the Internet, people now have even more opportunities to bully through cyberbullying. This includes sending crude pictures, posting fake web pages, or tweeting slanderous messages. Cyberbullying has subsequently led to a rise in a completely new kind of bullying.

One of the effects of bullying is that it can change the victim’s personality. It can cause people who are normally confident and happy to become self-conscious, shy, and unsure. Additionally, victims of bullying may also become sad or depressed. Their confidence might completely disappear, keeping them from trying new things or trusting people. Once a person has been bullied, they may hesitate to participate in situations where he or she might be ridiculed, such as in public speaking or in sports. A bullying victim might even begin to possess previously absent anxious
behavior.

Despite all the negative effects of bullying, there are even far more serious consequences. People who have been bullied sometimes become so upset, scared, or depressed that they see no worth in themselves and no way out of their torment. There have been countless reports over the past few years of students committing suicide because they were bullied. Meanwhile, there are times when victims see no recourse but to seek revenge by serious acts of violence against the bully and instigators. As a result of bullying, people can lose their ability to love and trust, denying them the chance to experience a quality relationship later in their life. They might find themselves as a submissive partner or they may want to be completely alone. Compounding all of these problems, victims often develop eating disorders, begin to self-injure, or require extensive counseling. Social bullying can also leave people without a supportive group of friends that they can lean on and spend time with.

Another unfortunate consequence of this is that bullying is often cyclical. People who have been bullied can, in an attempt to gain their power and self-esteem back, become bullies themselves. In relation to this, bullies who are not confronted or stopped may find themselves in future positions where they can bully as adults. This is where manipulative bosses and child abusers come from.

Aside from its long-term effects, some consequences of bullying can be seen and felt immediately. When one child calls another child names, the victim might cry and a bruise might appear after a punch to the arm. However, some effects of bullying are not always obvious to the naked eye. The results of bullying might grow and appear over time, damaging a person in profound ways for the long term. There are so many effects of bullying that they are impossible to count or predict. This is why it is so important to stop bullying.

Informative Essay on Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is a growing problem that affects people of all ages, but it is particularly prevalent among young people. It involves using the Internet, social media, or other electronic communication platforms to harass, threaten, or intimidate others. Cyberbullying can take many forms, including sending threatening or cruel messages, posting embarrassing pictures or videos, or spreading rumors or lies about someone online. It can also involve excluding someone from online social groups or engaging in other forms of online social aggression.

The consequences of cyberbullying can be severe. It can lead to feelings of depression, anxiety, and low self-esteem in those who are targeted. It can also cause problems with schoolwork and relationships, and in extreme cases, it can even lead to suicide.

There are several factors that can contribute to cyberbullying. One is the anonymity that the Internet provides. It is easier for someone to harass or threaten others online when they can do so without revealing their identity. The lack of face-to-face contact can also make it easier for people to be more aggressive or mean online because they do not have to see the immediate consequences of their actions.

Another factor that can contribute to cyberbullying is the prevalence of smartphones and other devices that allow people to access the Internet and social media at all times. This constant connectivity can make it more difficult for people to escape from cyberbullying, as it can occur at any time and in any place.

There are several steps that individuals can take to protect themselves from cyberbullying. One is to be cautious about what personal information they share online. This includes not sharing their full name, address, phone number, or other sensitive information. It is also a good idea to use privacy settings on social media accounts to control who can see their posts and personal information.

Another step that individuals can take is to think carefully before posting anything online. It is important to consider how their words and actions might affect others and to treat others with respect. It is also a good idea to be aware of the "digital footprint" that they are leaving behind and to be mindful of what they post online.

There are also steps that parents and schools can take to help prevent cyberbullying. Parents can educate their children about the risks of cyberbullying and the importance of being kind and respectful online. They can also monitor their children's online activities and talk to them about how to handle cyberbullying if it occurs. Schools can also educate students about cyberbullying and the importance of being kind and respectful online. They can also implement policies to address cyberbullying and provide resources to help students who are being bullied.

In conclusion, cyberbullying is a serious problem that affects people of all ages, but it is particularly prevalent among young people. It involves using the Internet, social media, or other electronic communication platforms to harass, threaten, or intimidate others. The consequences of cyberbullying can be severe and can include feelings of depression, anxiety, and low self-esteem. There are steps that individuals, parents, and schools can take to help prevent cyberbullying, including being cautious about what personal information is shared online, thinking carefully before posting anything online, and educating others about the risks and consequences of cyberbullying.
