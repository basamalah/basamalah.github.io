2. steps in sewing a blouse? ​


1. Gather the materials needed: fabric, thread, scissors, pins, and a sewing machine.

2. Cut out the pattern pieces from the fabric according to the instructions on the pattern envelope.

3. Pin together all of the pattern pieces that need to be sewn together and mark any darts or pleats with tailor’s chalk or a marking pen.

4. Sew all of the pinned pieces together using a straight stitch on your sewing machine and following any special instructions for darts or pleats if necessary.

5. Finish off raw edges with an overlock stitch or zigzag stitch if desired for extra durability and neatness of seams.

6. Try on your blouse to check fit before hemming it at bottom edge and sleeve cuffs if necessary; make adjustments as needed before continuing with next step(s).

7 . Sew buttons onto front placket (if applicable) according to instructions included in pattern envelope; use buttonholes for closure instead if desired instead of buttons/snaps/hook-and-eye closures etc..

8 . Hem bottom edge and sleeve cuffs (if applicable) using a narrow hem stitch on your sewing machine; press hems flat after stitching is complete for neat finish

8. identify at least 5 steps in cutting final pattern for ladies blouse.​


Answer:

1. Selvage– This is the finished side edges of the fabric.

2. Lengthwise grain– This is known as the warp which runs parallel to the selvage and the strongestgrainline with least amount of stretch.

3. Crosswise grain– This is known as the weft. This runs from selvage to selvage.

4. Bias. This is the line that runs diagonally across the fabric. The true bias is always at a 45-degree angle to the crosswise and lengthwise grains and is the fabric’s stretchiest point.
