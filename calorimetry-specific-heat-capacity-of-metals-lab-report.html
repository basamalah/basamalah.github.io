Report your final answer in correct number of significant figures.​


Answer:

The answer is 0.279 J/g°C

Explanation:

The specific heat capacity of the unknown metal can be calculated using the following equation:

Specific Heat Capacity = (4.184 J/g°C * 75 g * (297.3K - 294.7K)) / (20.8 g * (372.5K - 294.7K))

16. What is the Specific heat capacity of silver metal if 55.00 kg of the metal absorbs193875 joules of heat and the temperature rises 15.0°C? (Q = MC∆T)​


The specific heat capacity of silver is 235 J/kg°C

or 0.235 J/g°C

Explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

m = 55 kg

∆T = 15°C

Q = 193,875 J

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Specific heat capacity

Step 3: Solve for Energy

Equation:

The formula for Heat Energy is given by:

Q = mcp∆T

where: Q - Heat Energy

m - mass

cp - specific heat capacity

∆T = change in temperature, T2 - T1

Solution:

Substituting the value of m, Q and ∆T in the formula,

193,875 J = (55 kg)(cp)(15°C)

cp = (193,875 J)/(55 kg)(cp)(15°C)

cp = 235 J/kg°C or 0.235 J/g°C

18. a 2.00 kg metal object requires 1.00 x 10(4) J of heat to raise its temperature from 20.0 celsius to 40.0 celsius. What is the specific heat capacity of the metal?


Answer:

The specific heat capacity is c) 250 J/kg.°C

Explanation:

Specific heat capacity is a quantity needed to raise a temperature per unit mass. It is a constant, with different values for different materials.

Using the formula for heat capacity:

Δ[tex]Q=mc[/tex]Δ[tex]T[/tex]

Δ[tex]Q[/tex]     is the heat capacity, unit is in Joules (J)

[tex]m[/tex]       is the mass, unit is in kg

[tex]c[/tex]        is the specific heat capacity, unit is in J/kg.°C

Δ[tex]T[/tex]    is the change in temperature, unit is in °C

        where Δ[tex]T=T_{f} -T_{i}[/tex]

        [tex]T_{f}[/tex]     final temperature, unit is in °C

        [tex]T_{i}[/tex]      initial temperature, unit is in °C

For the given information:

[tex]m=2.00kg[/tex]

Δ[tex]Q[/tex][tex]=1.00x10^{4} J[/tex]

[tex]T_{f}[/tex][tex]=40[/tex]°[tex]C[/tex]

[tex]T_{i}[/tex][tex]=20[/tex]°[tex]C[/tex]

[tex]c=?[/tex]

Solving for the problem, use the formula for heat capacity, we get:

Δ[tex]Q=mc[/tex]Δ[tex]T[/tex]

Substitute the given information, we get:

[tex]1.00x10^{4} =(2.00kg)c(40[/tex]°[tex]C-20[/tex]°[tex]C)[/tex]

Simplifying, we get:

[tex]1.00x10^{4} =(2.00kg)c(20[/tex]°[tex]C)[/tex]

Solving for the specific heat capacity, divide both sides of the equation:

[tex]\frac{1.00x10^{4} J}{2.00kg(20C)}=\frac{(2.00kg)c(20C)}{2.00kg(20C)}[/tex]

[tex]c=250J/kg.[/tex]°[tex]C[/tex]
