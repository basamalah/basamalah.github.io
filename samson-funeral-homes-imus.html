7. reflection to the story of Samson ​


Answer:

The story of Samson is a powerful example of how trusting in one’s own strength and abilities can lead to success and victory, but also how pride and arrogance can lead to destruction. It teaches us that we should be humble and not let our pride get the best of us. We should not rely solely on our own strength and abilities, but instead trust in the Lord for guidance. It also reminds us to be aware of our weaknesses, and that we should not underestimate our enemies. The story of Samson can also serve as a reminder that no matter how powerful we are, we should always strive to be righteous and humble.

Nazirite

In the Hebrew Bible, a nazirite or nazarite is one who voluntarily took a vow described in Numbers 6:1–21. "Nazarite" comes from the Hebrew word נזיר‎ nazir meaning "consecrated" or "separated". Samson has a unique nazirite status called Nazir Shimshon which permitted him to touch dead bodies, since the angel who imposed the status omitted this restriction.

It forms the rolling tuffaceous plateau. This topography includes steep hills, ridges and elevated inland valley. The plateau is characterized with ground elevation ranging from 30 meters (98 ft) to nearly 400 meters (1,300 ft). Its ground slope ranges from 0.5 to 2%.
