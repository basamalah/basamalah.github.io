11. Lotus characteristics of stem​


Answer:

The stem of lotus is called rhizomes, which serve as anchoring and food storage organs. Fibrous roots grow in bunches from the stem internodes, or spaces between where leaves emerge. The roots are about 4 to 5 inches long and turn from whitish or purplish in young stems to brown in mature stems.
