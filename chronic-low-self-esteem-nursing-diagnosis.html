24. what is the relationship between low self-esteem and verbal communication development in students?​


Low self-esteem can have a negative impact on the verbal communication development of students. When individuals have low self-esteem, they may feel insecure or inadequate, making it difficult for them to express themselves confidently and effectively. This can manifest in different ways, such as speaking softly or hesitantly, using vague or uncertain language, or avoiding eye contact.

In the case of students, low self-esteem can hinder their communication development in several ways. For example:

1. Limited Vocabulary: Students with low self-esteem may have a limited vocabulary, making it difficult for them to express themselves clearly and effectively.

2. Poor Articulation: Students with low self-esteem may struggle with articulation and pronouncing words correctly, which can make it difficult for them to be understood by others.

3. Lack of Confidence: Low self-esteem can lead to a lack of confidence in one's communication skills, causing students to avoid speaking up in class or participating in group discussions.

4. Social Isolation: Low self-esteem can also lead to social isolation, causing students to withdraw from social interactions and opportunities to practice their communication skills.

Overall, low self-esteem can have a negative impact on the verbal communication development of students, hindering their ability to express themselves confidently and effectively. It is important for educators and parents to be aware of this relationship and take steps to support students in developing their self-esteem and communication skills.
