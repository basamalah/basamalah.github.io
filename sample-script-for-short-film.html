1. suggest short film script about bullying ​


Answer:

Title: Alone

Opening shot of a school playground. The camera focuses on a group of kids playing basketball. One of the kids, JASON (12), is watching from a distance. He looks lonely and sad. Suddenly, a group of boys approach him.

BULLY 1: Hey, what are you doing here? You're not cool enough to play with us.

JASON: (nervously) I wasn't trying to play. I was just watching.

BULLY 2: (pushing Jason) Well, you shouldn't be here. You're a loser.

The bullies continue to taunt and push Jason until he runs away. The camera follows him as he runs to the bathroom and locks himself in a stall. He's crying.

Flashback to Jason at home. He's sitting on his bed, looking at a picture of himself and his old best friend. He remembers the good times they had together.

Back to the present. Jason is still crying in the bathroom stall. Suddenly, he hears a knock on the door. It's a boy named TYLER (12).

TYLER: Hey, are you okay in there?

JASON: (hesitantly) Y-yeah, I'm fine.

TYLER: (suspicious) Are you sure? You don't sound fine.

JASON: (sighs) No, I'm not okay. Those guys were bullying me.

TYLER: (sympathetically) I know how you feel. They used to bully me too.

Tyler talks to Jason and tells him about how he stood up to the bullies and made new friends. He convinces Jason to come out of the stall and face the bullies.

The camera cuts to Jason and Tyler walking out of the bathroom. The bullies are waiting for them.

BULLY 1: (sarcastically) Oh, look who's back. The crybaby and his new friend.

TYLER: (firmly) Leave him alone. He hasn't done anything to you.

The bullies try to start a fight, but Tyler stands up to them and scares them away. Jason is amazed at Tyler's bravery.

JASON: (grateful) Thank you for standing up for me.

TYLER: (smiling) No problem. We're friends now, right?

Jason smiles and nods. The camera follows Jason as he walks away, feeling happy and confident for the first time in a long time.

Closing shot of the school playground. The camera pans to the basketball court. Tyler and Jason are playing basketball together, and the other kids are cheering them on.

3. Short Script/Film for Youngest Sibling actions and the topic is Can you buy me some soap and shampoo from the supermarket, please?​


Answer:

Open sidebar

Soap & Shampoo Request.

Short Script/Film for Youngest Sibling actions and the topic is Can you buy me some soap and shampoo from the supermarket, please?

Title: The Youngest Sibling's Request

Fade in:

EXT. SUBURBAN HOUSE - DAY

We see a small house with a white picket fence, surrounded by lush green trees and a neatly trimmed lawn.

INT. LIVING ROOM - DAY

We see a young boy, JASON, playing video games on the couch. His older sister, KAREN, enters the room.

KAREN: Hey, Jason. Mom asked me to run some errands. Do you want to come with me to the supermarket?

JASON: (excitedly) Yes, please! Can you buy me some soap and shampoo from the supermarket, please?

KAREN: Sure thing, kiddo. But why do you need soap and shampoo? Don't we have plenty of those at home?

JASON: (sheepishly) Well, I may have used them all up.

KAREN: (smiling) I see. Alright then, let's go get you some soap and shampoo.

INT. SUPERMARKET - DAY

Karen and Jason walk down the aisles, picking up various items. Jason is holding a basket, while Karen is pushing the shopping cart.

JASON: (pointing to the toiletries aisle) Karen, can we go there? That's where the soap and shampoo are.

KAREN: (nodding) Sure thing, Jason. But let's make a quick stop at the snack aisle first. I want to get some chips and soda for us.

JASON: (excitedly) Yay, snacks!

Karen and Jason head over to the snack aisle, and Karen picks up a few bags of chips and a bottle of soda. They then head over to the toiletries aisle.

JASON: (picking up a bottle of shampoo) Can I get this one, Karen?

KAREN: (reading the label) Sure thing, Jason. But do you want to get some soap too?

JASON: (nodding) Yes, please.

Karen helps Jason pick out a bar of soap, and they head to the checkout.

INT. LIVING ROOM - DAY

Karen and Jason enter the living room, carrying the groceries.

MOM: Hey, you two. How was the supermarket trip?

KAREN: It was good. We got everything on the list, plus some snacks.

JASON: And Karen got me some soap and shampoo, Mom!

MOM: (smiling) That's great, Jason. Did you remember to thank your sister?

JASON: (hugging Karen) Thank you, Karen! You're the best.

KAREN: (laughing) Anytime, Jason. Just remember to use them sparingly this time, okay?

JASON: (grinning) I will, I promise.

Fade out.

Good morning/afternoon everyone, and welcome to today's college webinar. My name is [Moderator Name] and I will be your moderator for this session. Before we begin, I would like to go over a few ground rules to ensure that we have a productive and respectful discussion.

Please mute your microphones when you are not speaking to minimize background noise.

Please raise your hand if you would like to ask a question or make a comment. I will call on you when it is your turn to speak.

Please be respectful of others and their opinions. We want to create a safe and inclusive space for all participants.

If you have any technical issues, please let me know and we will do our best to resolve them.

Now, let's begin today's discussion on [topic of discussion]. Our guest speaker for today is [Guest Name], who is an expert in the field of [expertise]. [Guest Name], would you like to start by introducing yourself and giving a brief overview of the topic?

26. short script for the restaurant reservation​


Answer:

"Making a Reservation"

A: Shogun Restaurant.

B: Hi, I would like to make a dinner reservation.

A: Of course, what evening will you be joining us on?

B: We will need the reservation for Tuesday night.

A: What time would you like the reservation for?

B: We would prefer 7:00 or 7:30.

A: How many people will you need the reservation for?

B: There will be 4 of us.

A: Fine, I can seat you at 7:00 on Tuesday, if you would kindly give me your name.

B: Thank you. The last name is Foster.

A: See you at 7:00 this Tuesday, Mr. Foster.

B: Thank you so much. I appreciate your help.
