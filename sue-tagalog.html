2. Sue and Joan are sisters. Joan is 7 years older than Sue. In 3 years Joan's age will be twice Sue's age. What are their present ages? With solution and x=Sue's age


Answer:

Sue is 4 years old and Joan is 11 years old.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be Sue's age

Let y be Joan's age

y = x + 7   ---equation 1

(y + 3) = 2(x + 3)

y + 3 = 2x + 6

y = 2x + 6 - 3

y = 2x + 3 ---equation 2

Equate 1 and 2

x + 7 = 2x + 3

2x - x = 7 - 3

x = 4

Plug in to equation 1

y = x + 7

y = 4 + 7

y = 11

7. Paul and Sue share $325 in the ratio 9:4 how much more money did Paul have than Sue


Answer:

Paul have $124 more than Sue

Step-by-step explanation:

9+4=13

325÷13=25

25×9=225

25×4=100

Thus, when $325 is divided in the ratio of 9:4 it will be 225:100. Then to substract 224 to 100 to find how much more Paul have than Sue.

225-100=125

23. Mary is 4 years older than Sue. The sum of their age is 16. How old is Mary and Sue?


Answer:

Mary is 10 years old while Sue is 6 years old.

Step-by-step Explanation:


Let x and x + 4 be their ages.

x + (x + 4) = 16

Move the constant to the right and change its sign.

x + x = 16 - 4

Combine like terms.

2x = 12

Divide both sides by 2.

2x/2 = 12/2

Calculate for the quotient.

x = 6


Checking:

x + (x + 4) = 16

6 + (6 + 4)= 16

6 + 10 = 16

16 = 16
