5. summary of le miserable


Explanation:

In Les Misérables, Jean Valjean spends nineteen years in prison for stealing a loaf of bread. After release, he's plagued by a suspicious police inspector, Javert. Valjean and his adopted daughter Cosette face much hardship, but finally find peace at the end of the novel.

After Valjean is released from prison, he attempts to steal some silver candlesticks from a priest. To keep Valjean from being arrested, the priest gives Valjean the candlesticks as a gift so he can start his new life.

Valjean becomes a wealthy manufacturer and is elected the mayor of a small town. Later, he's forced to flee when Javert finds him and grows suspicious.

Cosette falls in love with a young revolutionary who's injured in a fight. Valjean carries him to safety through the sewers, but continues to be plagued by his past until the day he dies.

Answer:

Victor Hugo was born in 1802 in the French town of Besançon. His father was a general in Napoléon’s army, and much of his childhood was therefore spent amid the backdrop of Napoléon’s campaigns in Spain and in Italy. At the age of eleven, Hugo returned to live with his mother in Paris, where he became infatuated with books and literature. By the time he was fifteen, he had already submitted one poem to a contest sponsored by the prestigious French Academy.

Hugo wrote prolifically in all genres, but his plays proved to be his earliest critical and commercial successes. France’s 1830 July Revolution opened Hugo’s creative floodgates, and he began producing a steady stream of work, most notably the novel The Hunchback of Notre-Dame (1831). Hugo also began to cultivate his interest in politics and was elected to France’s National Assembly after the revolution of 1848. As Hugo grew older, his politics became increasingly leftist, and he was forced to flee France in 1851 because of his opposition to the monarch Louis Napoléon. Hugo remained in exile until 1870, when he returned to his home country as a national hero. He continued to write until his death in 1885. He was buried with every conceivable honor in one of the grandest funerals in modern French history.

19. reflection Les Miserables 500 words essay​


Les Misérables is a novel written by Victor Hugo that has captured the hearts of millions of readers worldwide. The story is set in early 19th-century France and follows the life of Jean Valjean, a former convict who is trying to redeem himself and live a life of virtue. The novel explores themes such as justice, love, redemption, and sacrifice, and it has been adapted into numerous films, musicals, and plays.

One of the most significant themes in Les Misérables is the concept of redemption. The character of Jean Valjean is a prime example of this theme, as he begins the novel as a hardened criminal who has spent 19 years in prison for stealing a loaf of bread. However, he is given a second chance at life when he is shown mercy by a kind bishop. This act of kindness sparks a transformation in Valjean, and he dedicates his life to helping others and living a life of virtue.

Valjean's journey towards redemption is not an easy one, as he is constantly pursued by Javert, a police inspector who is determined to bring him to justice. However, Valjean's commitment to living a virtuous life allows him to overcome the challenges that come his way. His willingness to sacrifice himself for the sake of others, such as when he saves the life of Marius, a young revolutionary, is a testament to his commitment to redemption.

Another significant theme in Les Misérables is the power of love. The novel presents several examples of the transformative power of love, such as the love that Cosette, Valjean's adopted daughter, has for Marius. This love inspires Valjean to make the ultimate sacrifice to ensure Marius's safety, even though it means putting his own life in danger.

The novel also explores the idea of sacrifice, both in terms of the sacrifices that individuals make for the sake of others and in terms of the sacrifices that are necessary to achieve a greater good. The revolutionaries in the novel are willing to sacrifice their lives for the sake of a better future for their country, and Valjean is willing to sacrifice his own happiness to ensure the happiness of those he loves.

In conclusion, Les Misérables is a powerful novel that explores themes such as redemption, love, and sacrifice. The character of Jean Valjean serves as a model for the transformative power of redemption, and the novel's exploration of the power of love and sacrifice is a testament to the human spirit. The novel's enduring popularity is a testament.
