5. 1. Given the equation, 3FeO + 2Al 3Fe + Al2O3, if 50 grams of FeO reacted with 25 grams of Al, a. Which is the limiting reagent? How many moles of the excess reagent will be left? b. How many grams of Al2O3 will be formed? (atomic mass : Fe = 55.58 amu, O= 16 amu, Al= 27 amu)


a. Which is the limiting reagent? How many moles of the excess reagent will be left?Solution (Limiting Reagent)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of each reactant.

For FeO

molar mass = (55.58 g/mol × 1) + (16 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 71.58 g/mol

For Al

molar mass = 27 g/mol × 1

molar mass = 27 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratios needed.

Using FeO

mole ratio = 3 mol FeO : 1 mol Al₂O₃

Using Al

mole ratio = 2 mol Al : 1 mol Al₂O₃

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of Al₂O₃ produced by each reactant.

Using FeO

[tex]\text{moles of Al₂O₃ = 50 g FeO} × \frac{\text{1 mol FeO}}{\text{71.58 g FeO}} × \frac{\text{1 mol Al₂O₃}}{\text{3 mol FeO}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of Al₂O₃ = 0.23284 mol}[/tex]

Using Al

[tex]\text{moles of Al₂O₃ = 25 g Al} × \frac{\text{1 mol Al}}{\text{27 g Al}} × \frac{\text{1 mol Al₂O₃}}{\text{2 mol Al}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of Al₂O₃ = 0.463 mol}[/tex]

Step 4: Determine the limiting reagent.

Since FeO produces less number of moles of Al₂O₃

[tex]\boxed{\text{FeO is the limiting reagent.}}[/tex]

Solution (Moles of Excess Reagent Left):

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of Al consumed.

[tex]\text{moles of Al consumed = 0.23284 mol Al₂O₃} × \frac{\text{2 mol Al}}{\text{1 mol Al₂O₃}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of Al consumed = 0.47 mol}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the initial number of moles of Al.

[tex]\text{initial moles of Al = 25 g Al} × \frac{\text{1 mol Al}}{\text{27 g Al}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{initial moles of Al = 0.93 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of Al left.

moles of Al left = initial moles of Al - moles of Al consumed

moles of Al left = 0.93 mol - 0.47 mol

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of Al left = 0.46 mol}}[/tex]

b. How many grams of Al₂O₃ will be formed?Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Al₂O₃.

molar mass = (27 g/mol × 2) + (16 g/mol × 3)

molar mass = 102 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of Al₂O₃ formed.

Since FeO is the limiting reagent

[tex]\text{mass of Al₂O₃ = 0.23284 mol Al₂O₃} × \frac{\text{102 g Al₂O₃}}{\text{1 mol Al₂O₃}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of Al₂O₃ = 23.75 g}}[/tex]

10. How many grams of Fe are needed to react with sufficient oxygen to produce 28.8 moles FeO


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

How many grams of Fe are needed to react with sufficient oxygen to produce 28.8 moles FeO

Balanced Chemical Equation:

                         2 Fe  +   1 O₂  ⇒  2 FeO

                               ↓            ↓              ↓

                               2     :      1       :       2

Note:

The mole ration of Fe, O₂ and FeO is 2:1:2 which means, 2 moles of Fe will react to every 1 mole of O₂ to produce 2 moles of FeO.

Identifying molar mass:

Fe = 56 grams per mole

O₂ = 32 grams per moleFeO = 72 grams per moleBase on the mole ratio, 112 grams of Fe will react with 32 grams of  O₂ to produced 144 grams of FeO.

How many grams of Fe are needed to react with sufficient oxygen to produce 28.8 moles FeO?

Answer:

Since the mole ratio of Fe and FeO are the same, therefore you need 28.8 moles of Fe to produce 28.8 moles of FeO and 28.8 moles of Fe is converted to grams to get the answer.

Conversion of 28.8 moles of Fe =  ________grams

28.8 moles × 56 g / 1 mole = 1,612.8 grams

Answer:

You need 1,612.8 grams of Fe to produced 28.8 moles of FeO.

1. Caffeine, C8H10N4O2

To calculate the percentage composition of caffeine, we need to find the molar mass of the molecule first:

Molar mass of Caffeine = (8 x 12.01 g/mol) + (10 x 1.01 g/mol) + (4 x 14.01 g/mol) + (2 x 16.00 g/mol) = 194.19 g/mol

Now we can find the percentage composition of each element in caffeine:

Percentage of Carbon = (8 x 12.01 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 49.48%

Percentage of Hydrogen = (10 x 1.01 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 5.19%

Percentage of Nitrogen = (4 x 14.01 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 28.85%

Percentage of Oxygen = (2 x 16.00 g/mol) / 194.19 g/mol x 100% = 16.48%

2. Ammonium nitrate, NH4NO3

To calculate the percentage composition of ammonium nitrate, we need to find the molar mass of the molecule first:

Molar mass of Ammonium Nitrate = (1 x 14.01 g/mol) + (4 x 1.01 g/mol) + (3 x 16.00 g/mol) = 80.04 g/mol

Now we can find the percentage composition of each element in ammonium nitrate:

Percentage of Nitrogen = (1 x 14.01 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 17.50%

Percentage of Hydrogen = (4 x 1.01 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 5.03%

Percentage of Oxygen = (3 x 16.00 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 37.51%

Percentage of Ammonium ion (NH4+) = (4 x 1.01 g/mol + 1 x 14.01 g/mol) / 80.04 g/mol x 100% = 40.96%

3. Benzene, C6H6

To calculate the percentage composition of benzene, we need to find the molar mass of the molecule first:

Molar mass of Benzene = (6 x 12.01 g/mol) + (6 x 1.01 g/mol) = 78.11 g/mol

Now we can find the percentage composition of each element in benzene:

Percentage of Carbon = (6 x 12.01 g/mol) / 78.11 g/mol x 100% = 92.26%

Percentage of Hydrogen = (6 x 1.01 g/mol) / 78.11 g/mol x 100% = 7.74%

4. Iron(II) oxide, FeO

To calculate the percentage composition of iron(II) oxide, we need to find the molar mass of the molecule first:

Molar mass of Iron(II) oxide = 55.85 g/mol + 16.00 g/mol = 71.85 g/mol

Now we can find the percentage composition of each element in iron(II) oxide:

Percentage of Iron = 55.85 g/mol / 71.85 g/mol x 100% = 77.67%

Percentage of Oxygen = 16.00 g/mol / 71.85 g/mol x 100% = 22.33%
