Answer:

1. Scissors: a. simple machine

2. Bulldozer: c. complex machine

3. Axe: a. simple machine

4. Wheelbarrow: b. compound machine

5. Pulley: a. simple machine

Explanation:

The classification of these machines as simple, compound, or complex is based on their mechanical structure and the number of simple machines they use.

1. Scissors: Scissors are a simple machine known as a lever. They have two arms that pivot around a fulcrum to cut objects, making them a simple machine.

2. Bulldozer: Bulldozers are considered complex machines as they typically have multiple systems working together, such as an engine, hydraulics, gears, and tracks or wheels. They are used for heavy-duty construction and excavation tasks, and their mechanical structure is more intricate and involves multiple components, making them a complex machine.

3. Axe: An axe is a simple machine known as a wedge. It consists of a handle with a sharp blade at the end, which is used to split wood or other materials. It operates by applying force to the blade, which creates a wedge shape, allowing it to penetrate and split the material.

4. Wheelbarrow: A wheelbarrow is considered a compound machine as it combines two or more simple machines. It typically consists of a wheel, axle, handles, and a bucket for carrying objects. The wheel and axle act as a lever, and the handles provide a mechanical advantage, making it easier to transport heavy loads.

5. Pulley: A pulley is a simple machine that consists of a wheel with a grooved rim and a rope or chain that runs along the groove. It is used to lift or move heavy objects by changing the direction of force applied. Pulleys can be used individually or in combination to create compound machines such as block and tackle systems, which provide mechanical advantage for lifting heavy loads.
