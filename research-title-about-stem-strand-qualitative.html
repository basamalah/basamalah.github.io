29. qualitative research title related to STEM​


Answer:

Perceptions developed and choices made during the preadolescent and early adolescent years may restrict or enrich youth's future career aspirations. These years are critical for acquiring and exploring academic and career related interests. Addition, outcome expectations what youth believe will happen if they pursue certain interests, tasks, or goals are important predictors of eventual career choice. With national, state, and local initiatives advocating science, technology, engineering and mathematics ( Stem ) knowledge and career goals there is a need to understand youth's outcome expectations of 95 youth age 10-14 using focus group data. Several of the identified outcome expectations had negative connotations. Results supported outcome expectations identified by Bandura ( i.e.., physical, social, and self) two additional types of girls, women and minorities in STEM occupation.
