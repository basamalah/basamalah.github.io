13. use the venn diagram to compare and contrast media literacy, information literacy and digital literacy​


Three similarities and differences comparing and contrasting media literacy, information literacy, and technology literacy are discussed below in detail.

The three styles of education all aim to adapt students to the realities of modern life, which are present in all professional domains, which is where the parallels between them arise. The variances may be seen in the unique goals and interests that each of them has.

→ You may find the Venn diagram you're referring to below.

In the context of your three specific literacy courses, we are thinking:

Media Literacy – Aims to acclimate people to the many media forms utilized in modern culture by letting them understand how they work and what they can do. Knowledge about media, how it works, the approach it presents, and the knowledge that not everything is always objective or perfect. Many of these may contain misunderstandings and prejudices.

Technology literacy – Its goal is to provide users with the skills necessary to operate technical tools proficiently, comprehend their significance in modern society, and follow best practices for doing so. Refers to your understanding of technology, your proficiency with it, your ability to use it to your daily activities or job, etc. As in my case, I can typically guess the operation of the majority of gadgets without a handbook. My father can accomplish almost anything with a PowerPoint presentation, while my mother is adept with Excel spreadsheets.

Information Literacy – Its goal is to acclimate the user to the available informational tools, enabling them to identify reliable sources of information and comprehend how various forms of information work. Combine this with learning literacy knowing that it is the awareness/understanding of the information provided in any configuration, be it learning, video, audio, etc.

17. how media literacy, information literacy and technology literacy and media and information literacy differ in terms of use​


•Information Literacy: The ability to know when there is a need for information, to be able to identify, locate, evaluate, and effectively use that information for the issue or problem at hand.

•Media Literacy: Media literacy is an informed, critical understanding of the mass media.

•Technology Literacy: The ability to use new media such as the Internet to access and communicate information effectively.

•Global Literacy: Understanding the interdependence among people and nations and having the ability to interact and collaborate successfully across cultures.

•Visual Literacy: Based on the idea that visual images are a language, visual literacy can be defined as the ability to understand and produce visual messages.

•Computer Literacy: The ability to use a computer and its software to accomplish practical tasks.

•Mathematical Literacy or Numeracy: Numeracy is a mastery of the basic symbols and processes of arithmetic.

•New Media Literacies: Particularly literacies in digital mediums and on the Internet, involving the new tools of hypertext, multimedia and electronic forms of synchronous and asynchronous communication.
