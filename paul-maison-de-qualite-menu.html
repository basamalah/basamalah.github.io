10. Bakit naging santo si st vincent de paul


Si St. Vincent de Paul ay isang Katolikong Pari na ginugol ang kanyang buhay sa pangangaral ng Salita ng Diyos at pagtulong sa mahirap. Noong panahong kakaunti pa lamang ang mga pari sa Pransya, si St. Vincent de Paul ang nagbago sa paraan ng pagsasanay ng mga nagpapari sa pamamagitan ng kanyang mga programa na nakahikayat sa mga lalaki na pumasok sa seminary upang maging pari. Itinatag niya ang Ladies of Charity, isang samahan ng mga kababaihan na tumutulong upang ipahayag ang ebanghelyo at ang Congregation of Priests of the Mission na kilala ngayon bilang mga Vicentinians.

Bukod sa mga nabanggit, naging santo si St. Vincent de Paul dahil sa mga himalang inuugnay sa kanya. Isang madre ay gumaling sa ulcer at isang babae nagamot ang leukemia. Kinilala rin siya sa pagkontra sa Jansenism, isang paniniwala na ang mga tao ay likas na makasalanan kaya tanging ang Diyos lamang ang makakapili ng ililigtas nito at walang magagawa ang mga tao para sa kanilang kaligtasan.

Namatay si St. Vincent de Paul noong ika-21 ng Setyembre 1660 ngunit hanggang sa ngayon ay ramdam pa rin ang impluwensya niya sa pamamagitan ng pagkakatatag ng Society of St. Vincent de Paul isang samahang ang pangunahing layunin ay ang tumulong sa mahihirap.

"St Vincent de Paul" redirects here. For other uses, see Vincent de Paul (disambiguation).

Vincent de Paul (24 April 1581 – 27 September 1660) commonly known as Saint Vincent de Paul was a French Catholic priest who dedicated himself to serving the poor. In 1622 Vincent was appointed a chaplain to the galleys. After working for some time in Paris among imprisoned galley slaves, he returned to be the superior of what is now known as the Congregation of the Mission, or the "Vincentians" (in France known as "Lazaristes"). These priests, with vows of poverty, chastity, obedience, and stability, were to devote themselves entirely to the people in smaller townser and villages. Vincent was zealous in conducting retreats for clergy at a time when there was great laxity, abuse, and ignorance among them. He was a pioneer in clerical training and was instrumental in establishing seminaries, and founder of the Congregation of the Mission and Daughters of Charity of Saint Vincent de Paul.

A la carte menu refers to when a diner orders one type of dish that is separate from a plate of an original menu. The term carries the meaning of going opposite of the menu. It came into English in the 1830s. It is the opposite of the word table d'hote, which means according to the menu.

Table de Hote

In restaurant terminology a table d'hôte menu is a menu where multi-course meals with only a few choices are charged at a fixed total price. Such a menu may be called prix fixe. The terms set meal and set menu are also used. The cutlery on the table may also already be set for all of the courses.
