5. Activity 2. Apply moral and ethical principles in the teaching profession by making a decision on the following situations. Think of yourself as a teacher in this situation in order to decide rationally on this ethical dilemma. 2. A learner submits his/her junior thesis in partial fulfillment of graduation requirements. You are appointed to the panel of examiners, and when you review his/her thesis, you find that approximately five pages of one of his/her chapters are paraphrased from your lecture notes. There is no acknowledgement of your notes, nor was your consent ever secured. When you bring this to the attention of the department chair, the latter urges you to be more benevolent, reminding you that any issue in respect to the integrity of the thesis would compromise the learner’s chances of graduating. You would not like to be the cause of the delay of the learner’s graduation, but neither would you not like to countenance intellectual dishonesty. How should you deal with the matter?


It is important to address this situation carefully and fairly, as it involves issues of academic integrity and the potential for negative consequences for the student. Here are some steps that you could consider taking:

Document the issue: Make a record of the pages in question and the specific content that has been paraphrased from your notes without proper attribution.

Discuss the issue with the student: Meet with the student to discuss the issue and explain your concerns about the lack of attribution for the material that was paraphrased from your notes. It may be helpful to provide the student with examples of proper paraphrasing and citation.

Consider the possibility of a revised submission: If the student was unaware of the need to properly attribute the material, they may be willing to revise their submission to include the appropriate citations. This could be a way to resolve the issue without causing undue delays in the student's graduation.

Consult with the department chair and other relevant parties: If the student is unwilling or unable to revise their submission, or if the issue cannot be resolved through discussion with the student, it may be necessary to bring the matter to the attention of the department chair or other relevant parties. It is important to approach this in a fair and impartial manner, and to consider the potential consequences for the student.

Ultimately, the most important thing is to ensure that the academic integrity of the thesis is maintained, while also being mindful of the impact on the student's academic progress.
