Answer:

The Leydig cells produce testosterone, which also is responsible for the secondary sexual characteristics of males. In females, FSH and LH cause estrogen and progesterone to be produced.

Females

LH causes the egg to be released from the ovary, a process called ovulation.

16. 13. What happens to the level of FSH and Progesterone after fertilization? A. Progesterone and FSH both increase. B. Progesterone and FSH both remain low. C. Progesterone remains low, but FSH increases. D. Progesterone increases but FSH remains low.​


Answer:

The correct answer is D. Progesterone increases but FSH remains low.

After fertilization, the fertilized egg (embryo) implants into the uterine lining, and the placenta begins to form. The placenta is responsible for producing hormones to support pregnancy, including progesterone. Progesterone is essential for maintaining the uterine lining and preventing it from shedding, which helps support the pregnancy.

FSH (follicle-stimulating hormone) is a hormone that is responsible for stimulating the growth and development of follicles in the ovaries, which eventually leads to ovulation. After fertilization and implantation, the hormonal feedback mechanisms change, and FSH levels typically decrease to prevent further ovulation and to support the maintenance of pregnancy.

Therefore, the correct answer is D. Progesterone increases but FSH remains low after fertilization.
