Answer:

1. A doctor attending to the patients in the emergency room Thinking: Intuitive Why: In an emergency room, doctors have to make quick decisions based on their instincts and experience to save lives.

2. A scientist finding a cure for an infectious disease Thinking: Strategic Why: Finding a cure for a disease requires extensive research, data analysis, and testing. It requires a strategic approach to identify the most effective treatment.

3. A group of researchers solving climate change Thinking: Strategic Why: Solving climate change requires a long-term plan and a strategic approach that involves research, policy-making, and collaboration among various sectors.

4. A firefighter rescuing victims from a burning building Thinking: Intuitive Why: In a life-threatening situation, firefighters have to make quick decisions based on their instincts and experience to save lives.

5. A lawyer solving a case Thinking: Strategic Why: Solving a case requires a strategic approach that involves analyzing evidence, researching legal precedents, and developing a persuasive argument.

6. A street vendor trying to sell and generate good profit within a day Thinking: Strategic Why: Generating profit requires a strategic approach that involves identifying the target market, setting prices, and developing a marketing plan.

7. A teacher wanting to solve poor motivation among her students Thinking: Strategic Why: Solving poor motivation among students requires a strategic approach that involves identifying the root cause, developing a plan to address it, and implementing strategies to improve motivation.

8 A lady trying to figure out whether to bring or not an umbrella in anticipation of rain Thinking: Intuitive Why: Deciding whether to bring an umbrella or not is a quick decision that is based on intuition and past experience.

9. A couple wanting to buy a house in a subdivision Thinking: Strategic Why: Buying a house requires a strategic approach that involves researching the real estate market, identifying the best location, and considering financial factors.

10. A mother attending to the whining of her child Thinking: Intuitive Why: Attending to a child's needs requires a quick decision based on intuition and past experience.

Sure, here are five examples of intuitive thinking and five examples of rational thinking:

Intuitive Thinking:

Deciding what to wear based on how we feel or what we think will look good, without necessarily analyzing all the options and factors.

Making quick decisions based on gut feelings or instincts, such as choosing which route to take while driving or which food to order at a restaurant.

Forming opinions or judgments about people or situations based on initial impressions or intuition, rather than analyzing all available information.

Knowing what to say or how to act in social situations based on social cues and past experiences, rather than consciously thinking through every decision.

Creatively problem-solving by trusting our instincts and coming up with unique solutions based on our intuition.

Rational Thinking:

Making a budget based on our income and expenses, and analyzing the pros and cons of different financial decisions.

Planning a project or event by breaking down the steps, setting goals, and considering the resources and constraints available.

Analyzing data and statistics to draw conclusions and make informed decisions, such as in business or scientific research.

Making a grocery list based on meal planning and nutritional needs, and considering the prices and availability of different items.

Evaluating the credibility and reliability of sources before making decisions or forming opinions, such as in journalism or academic research.
