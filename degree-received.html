15. a volleyball is served at a speed of 8.0 at an angle of 35 degrees above the horizontal what is the speed of the ball when received by the opponent at the same height​


Explanation:

Assuming air resistance is negligible, we can use the conservation of energy principle to determine the speed of the ball when received by the opponent. At the moment of serving, the ball has the following energy:

Kinetic energy = (1/2)mv^2, where m is the mass of the ball and v is its speed

Potential energy = mgh, where h is the height of the ball above the ground

At the highest point of the ball's trajectory, all of its initial kinetic energy is converted to potential energy, so we can set these quantities equal:

(1/2)mv^2 = mgh

Solving for v, we get:

v = sqrt(2gh)

where g is the acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s^2). To find the height h, we can use trigonometry:

h = (8.0 m/s)^2 * sin^2(35°) / (2 * g)

h ≈ 2.24 m

Plugging in the values, we get:

v = sqrt(2 * 9.8 m/s^2 * 2.24 m)

v ≈ 6.7 m/s

So the speed of the ball when received by the opponent is approximately 6.7 m/s.

24. Martin Luther King Jr. received in 1948 from Morehouse College his B.A. degree. Is the use of adverb correct?


The usage of the adverbs in this sentence "Martin Luther King Jr. received in 1948 from Morehouse College his B.A. degree." is something that can be improved.


Adverbs are words, phrases or clauses that are used to modify verbs, adjectives or other adverbs. They can be placed at the beginning, middle or the end part of the sentence.

However, there are some specific adverbial positions that we commonly follow in constructing more organized sentences.


Adverbs of time and place can generally be found at the beginning or at the end of sentences. In spoken English, we normally put them at the end. But, if we want to emphasize these adverbs, we can put them at the beginning. This is possible when we

construct short and simple sentences.


Examples:


1.1 Adverb of time at the end


We had a class yesterday.


1.2 Adverb of time at the beginning

Yesterday, we had a class.


2.1 Adverb of place at the end


The children are sleeping in the room.


2.2 Adverb of place at the beginning

In the room, the children are sleeping.



Sometimes, we need to use more than a single adverb in a sentence. In cases like this,adverbs of place usually come before adverbs of time. Therefore, the more appropriate format of the sentence should be:


Martin Luther King Jr. received his B.A degree at Morehouse College in 1948.


Notice that the preposition "at" is used instead of "from". We use "at" to generally refer to the place where he had received his degree.
