Similarities between sociology, anthropology and political science:

The object of scientific study is humans or society at large.In the category of social science.Theory oriented.

Difference between sociology, anthropology and political science

sociology studies human behavior in the environment. Anthropology studies the origin of humans. Political science focuses on political theory and practice.Sociology deals with social changes that occur as a result of social processes. Anthropology deals with culture and social meanings that are more abstract. Political science is concerned with political behavior.

Explanation:

Sociology is a science that discusses various aspects of society and their influence on human life. The main object of study in sociology is the structure of society, social elements, socialization and social change. The branches of sociology are a combination of the science of social phenomena that occur in society with other sciences.

Sociology is a field of social science that studies society. Sociology as a science has fulfilled all the elements of science. The main characteristic of sociology as an empirical, theoretical, and non-ethical science. Empirical, which is based on observations and common sense whose results are not approximate.

Theoretical, which is trying to compile an abstraction from the results of real observations and the abstraction is a framework of elements that are arranged logistically and aims to carry out the relationships caused by the theory. Cumulative, which is compiled on existing theories, then corrected, thus strengthening the old theories.

Anthropology is the science of humans. The purpose of studying anthropology is to find out patterns of human behavior in social life universally as well as patterns of human behavior in each society (ethnic group).

Political science is the branch of social science that deals with political theory and practice as well as the description and analysis of political systems and political behavior. This science is oriented to academics, theory, and research. The purpose of studying political science is to provide an integral understanding of politics and the values ​​it contains. Political science aims to prosper the nation, educate the nation's life, and maintain world peace.

25. perspective of anthropology sociology and political science diagram​

Between Sociology and anthropology, the connection goes thus. Basically, we study bits of anthropology in sociology. We study culture in sociology and anthropology. We learn how people behave, how what they do defines them. We want to know what separates the Malawians from the Nigerians. We want to know why the Yoruba culture is almost totally different from the Igbo culture.

Between anthropology and political science, we learn power. We learn that in the old Yoruba empire, we had the Oba (the king) and his chiefs, even to the subjects. We learn that most parts of Europe ran on Monarchy for a while. We learn that power moves from the top to the bottom, in one culture and that in some cultures, it's vice versa. You see elements of political science and sociology in that?

Lastly, in sociology, we also learn how power is distributed in the society. We learn democracy, oligarchy, anarchy, a state of anomie and all. These things are also replicated in political science.

Anthropology is the scientific study of humanity, concerned with human behavior, human biology, and societies, in both the present and past, including past human species.[1][2][3] Social anthropology studies patterns of behaviour and cultural anthropology[1][2][3] studies cultural meaning, including norms and values. Linguistic anthropology studies how language influences social life. Biological or physical anthropology[1][2][3] studies the biological development of humans. Visual anthropology, which is usually considered to be a part of social anthropology, can mean both ethnographic film (where photography, film, and new media are used for study) as well as the study of "visuals", including art, visual images, cinema etc. Oxford Bibliographies describes visual anthropology as "the anthropological study of the visual and the visual study of the anthropological".[4]

Archaeology, which studies human activity through investigation of physical evidence, is considered a branch of anthropology in the United States and Canada, while in Europe it is viewed as a discipline in its own right or grouped under other related disciplines, such as history.
