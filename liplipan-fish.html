Plating Techniques

Conceptualize plating as an art form: you are the artist; the plate is your canvas, and the food is your medium. Master the following plating techniques to perfect your craft.

1. Plate Presentation Techniques

how to choose a plate for food presentation

Selecting the right plate for your meal is the first step in the food presentation process. Consider the following to choose the ideal plate for your food presentation:

Plate Size - Your plate should be big enough to make your food stand out and petite enough to prevent your portions from looking small.

Light vs Dark Plates - Use light and dark plates to make your meal stand out. White plates are popular because they offer a neutral background for brightly colored foods. Dark plates lend beautifully to light-colored dishes, such as a whitefish or creamy polenta.

Plate Color - Plate color can stimulate or reduce appetites. Red increases the appetite, so serving appetizers on red plates keeps customers interested in ordering large entrees and desserts. Conversely, professional platers typically consider blue dinnerware unappetizing because there are few naturally occurring blue foods.

Restaurant Style - If you operate a fine dining establishment, classic china dinnerware will pair well with traditional plating styles. A trendy gastropub should invest in unique plates with unconventional shapes that allow for maximum plating creativity.

26. fish/seafood dishes name & description. ​


Answer:

Sushi: Sushi is a Japanese dish made with vinegared rice and various ingredients such as raw fish, vegetables, and egg. The most common types of sushi are maki (rolls) and nigiri (fingers).

Ceviche: Ceviche is a dish popular in Latin America, particularly Peru, that is made with raw fish marinated in lime juice and seasoned with chili peppers, onions, and cilantro.

Fish and chips: Fish and chips is a British dish that consists of deep-fried fish (usually cod or haddock) and chips (French fries).

Paella: Paella is a traditional dish from Valencia, Spain. It is a rice dish cooked with saffron, paprika, and various meats and seafood like chicken, rabbit, and shellfish.

Fish curry: Fish curry is a dish that is popular in India, Sri Lanka, and other parts of South Asia. It is made with fish cooked in a spicy sauce made from a variety of herbs and spices.

Crab cakes: Crab cakes are a dish made from crabmeat, breadcrumbs, and various seasonings. They are popular in the United States, particularly in the Eastern Seaboard.

Fish Tacos: Fish Tacos is a popular dish in Mexico, and the United States. It's a corn tortilla filled with fish that is battered and fried, and then topped with various ingredients such as salsa, avocado, and sour cream.
