9. Describe the following Holy Sacraments. ​


Answer:

There are seven Sacraments: Baptism, Confirmation, Eucharist, Reconciliation, Anointing of the Sick, Matrimony, and Holy Orders.

Answer:

The Holy Sacraments (Seven Sacraments)

(according to order manifested by the Catholic Church)

1. Baptism - a Christian sacrament marked by ritual use of water and admitting the recipient to the Christian community.

2. Confirmation - is a sacrament, ritual, or rite of passage practiced by several Christian denominations. The word means strengthening or deepening one's relationship with God.

3. Communion (Receiving the Holy Eucharist) -  is a symbolic reminder of Christ's death. This is where the consecrated bread (body/eucharist) is received.

4. Confession (Penance and Reconciliation) -  to say sorry for the wrongdoings in their lives and to experience God's healing through forgiveness. People consider confession to repent and receive and seek reconciliation to God and its church.

5. Holy Matrimony - also known as marriage. Represents the change of vows, unity of two individuals in love, united by the divine grace of church, and God's sacredness.

6. Holy Orders - any of several grades in the ordained ministry of some of the Christian churches. Where men on seminaries get ordained,  comprising at various times the major orders of bishop, priest, deacon, and subdeacon and the minor orders of porter (doorkeeper), lector, exorcist, and acolyte.

7. Anointing of the Sick - This sacrament confers a special grace that unites the sick person more intimately to the Passion of Christ for his good and for the good of all the Church.

The English word "sacrament" is derived indirectly from the Ecclesiastical Latin sacrāmentum, from Latin sacrō ("hallow, consecrate"), from sacer ("sacred, holy"). This in turn is derived from the Greek New Testament word "mysterion". In Ancient Rome, the term meant a soldier's oath of allegiance
