So, by using the formula of Permutation, we have,

nPk = [tex] \frac{n!}{(n-k)!} [/tex]

9P5 = [tex] \frac{9!}{(9-5)!} [/tex]

22. How many different combinations of management can there be to fill the positions of manager, secretary, and advisor of a company knowing that there 12 eligible candidates?​


Step-by-step explanation:

How many different combinations of management can there be to fill the positions of manager, secretary, and advisor of a company knowing that there 12 eligible candidates?

To find the number of different combinations of management positions that can be filled by 12 eligible candidates, we can use the permutation formula:

nPr = n! / (n - r)!

where n is the total number of candidates and r is the number of positions to be filled.

In this case, we need to fill 3 positions (manager, secretary, and advisor) from a pool of 12 eligible candidates. Therefore, n = 12 and r = 3.

nPr = 12! / (12 - 3)! = 12! / 9!

= (12 × 11 × 10) / (3 × 2 × 1)

= 220

Therefore, there are 220 different combinations of management positions that can be filled by 12 eligible candidates.
