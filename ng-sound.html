Answer:

The amplitude of a loud sound is described as high or large.

The amplitude of a quiet sound is described as low or small.

Yes, the loudness of sound is determined by the amplitude of sound waves. The greater the amplitude, the louder the sound will be.

1. The amplitude of a loud sound is high. It means that the sound wave has a high intensity, and the particles in the medium vibrate with a large displacement.

2. The amplitude of a quiet sound is low. It means that the sound wave has a low intensity, and the particles in the medium vibrate with a small displacement.

3. The loudness of sound is determined by the amplitude of sound waves because the greater the amplitude of a sound wave, the more energy it carries. This energy causes the eardrum to vibrate more intensely, which sends a stronger signal to the brain, resulting in a perception of a louder sound. The loudness of a sound is measured in decibels (dB), which is a logarithmic scale that relates the intensity of a sound wave to the threshold of human hearing.
