28. Aphostrophe in romeo and juliet


Apostrophe is one of punctuation mark used to show possession. The sign (‘) is uses to indicate omission of letters in a word, whether it is pronounced or unpronounced.

Examples of apostrophe (‘) in Romeo and Juliet act:

1. Gregory, o' my word, we'll not carry coals. (Sampson, Act 1)

2. At thy good heart's oppression. (Benvolio)

3. Why, such is love's transgression. (Romeo)

4. Being purged, a fire sparkling in lovers' eyes; Being vex'd a sea nourish'd with lovers' tears (Romeo)

5. Tut, man, one fire burns out another's burning, One pain is lessen'd by another's anguish (Benvolio)
