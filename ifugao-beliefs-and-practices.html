9. why do the ifugao in the province of benguet are able to preserve their beliefs and traditions and remain unharmed by the foreign influences? does geography have a connection with this? explain.​


Answer

How did the Ifugao in the province of Benguet preserve their beliefs and traditions while remaining untouched by foreign influences?

In 1601, the Ifugao resisted the proselytization of the Spaniards, which is called the Igorot Revolt or La Revuelto del Igorot.  In Nov. 1601, the Spaniards sent Fray Esteban Marín, a prior of Laoag, Batac, and Bantay, to convert the Ifugao peacefully. Fray Esteban was Martyred in the process.

Does geography have a connection with this?

Yes. After the martyrdom of Fray Esteban, the Spaniards sent a sizeable expedition commanded by Lt. Aranda. The terrain(geography) made it easier for the Ifugao to ambush the expedition successfully. The Spaniards stopped proselytization after their defeat.

22. 3. What is a narrative chant performed by the Ifugao as a practice during rice sowing season, at harvest time, and funeral wakes and rituals? A HudhudC. Salidumay B. Marayaw D. Tuitul ​


Answer:

3.A

Explanation:

The Hudhud consists of narrative chants traditionally performed by the Ifugao community, which is well known for its rice terraces extending over the highlands of the northern island of the Philippine archipelago. It is practised during the rice sowing season, at harvest time and at funeral wakes and rituals. Thought to have originated before the seventh century, the Hudhud comprises more than 200 chants, each divided into 40 episodes. A complete recitation may last several days.

27. ano ang ifugao?saan matatagpuan ang ifugao?​


Answer:

Ifugao (Ilocano: Probinsia ti Ifugao; Tagalog: Lalawigan ng Ifugao) is a landlocked province of the Philippines in the Cordillera Administrative Region in Luzon. Its capital is Lagawe and it borders Benguet to the west, Mountain Province to the north, Isabela to the east, and Nueva Vizcaya to the south.
