Answer:

C.

Explanation:

No sons of a man with hemophilia will have hemophilia. All daughters of a man with hemophilia will be carriers (called obligate carriers). If a carrier has a son, the son has a 50% chance of having hemophilia. If a carrier has a daughter, the daughter has a 50% chance of being a carrier.

16. If all the daughters of Harry and Meghan are carriers of hemophilia genes and the sons are normal, what are their genotypes?​


Answer:

Hemophilia is an X-linked recessive genetic disorder, which means that the gene responsible for hemophilia is located on the X chromosome. Females have two X chromosomes while males have one X and one Y chromosome. If a female inherits a hemophilia gene on one of her X chromosomes, but has a normal gene on her other X chromosome, she is said to be a carrier of the hemophilia gene. Males, on the other hand, only have one X chromosome, so if they inherit a hemophilia gene on that chromosome, they will develop the disorder.

In this scenario, if all the daughters of Harry and Meghan are carriers of hemophilia genes, it means that they inherited a copy of the hemophilia gene from one of their parents and a normal gene from the other parent. The sons, on the other hand, are normal because they inherited a normal gene from their mother and a Y chromosome from their father, which does not carry the hemophilia gene.

Therefore, we can assume that Harry does not have the hemophilia gene because if he did, he would have the disorder himself. This means that he must have a normal gene on his X chromosome. Meghan, on the other hand, must be a carrier of the hemophilia gene, as she is able to pass it on to her daughters.

With this information, we can construct the following Punnett square to show the possible genotypes of the offspring:

| Xh | X

---- | --------------

Xh | XhXh | XhX

---- | --------------

X | XhX | XX

In this Punnett square, "Xh" represents the hemophilia gene, and "X" represents the normal gene.

From this Punnett square, we can see that there are two possible genotypes for the daughters: XhX and XhXh. The XhX genotype indicates that the daughter is a carrier of the hemophilia gene, while the XhXh genotype indicates that she has the disorder. The sons, on the other hand, can only have one genotype: XY, which means they do not have the hemophilia gene.
