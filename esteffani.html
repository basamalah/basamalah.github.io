18. The sum of the ages of Negradas and Stiffany are 47 in 7 years, Negradas was more than 4 times as old as Stiffany. How old are they now?


Answer:

The age of Stiffany is 6.6 years old while Nagradas is 26.4.

Step-by-step explanation:

Since we don't know the age of Stiffany yet, we will use x to represent it. It is stated in the given that the age of Negradas is 4 times older than Stiffany. Their ages in the present time can be written as:

Stiffany: xNegradas: 4x

According to the problem, the sum of their ages 7 years from now will be 47. To represent their ages 7 years from now, will be the following:

Stiffany: x + 7Negradas: 4x + 7

Now, we must form an equation equating the sum of their ages 7 years from now and 47.

x + 7 + 4x + 7 = 47

*It's fine if you don't put a parenthesis since the operation is addition.

Next, combine like terms.

5x + 14 = 47

The next thing to do to find the value of x is to isolate it. First, we will transpose the 14 or subtract 14 on both sides.

5x = 47 - 14

5x = 33

Lastly, to totally isolate x, we will divide both sides by 5.

[tex]\frac{5x}{5}= \frac{33}{5}[/tex]

x = 6.6

Next part, to find their present ages, we will use the expressions we made first. We will substitute the value of x now.

Stiffany: x = 6.6Negradas: 4x = 4 (6.6) = 26.4

Therefore, the age of Stiffany is 6.6 years old while Nagradas is 26.4.

29. Ms. Estenfan wants to put a fence around 3 sides of a square garden that has an area of 225ft^2. how much fencing does she need? ​


Question:

Ms. Estenfan wants to put a fence around 3 sides of a square garden that has an area of 225ft^2. how much fencing does she need?

Answer:

45ft

Step-by-step explanation:

✓225ft² = 15ft

15ft • 3 sides = 45ft of fence Ms. Estenfan needs

[tex]\red{45 ft.}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the measurement of the sides of the square, we substitute the given, to the formula of its area. Thus,

[tex] \: \: \: \: \: a = {s}^{2} \\ 225 = {s}^{2} \\ \sqrt{225} = \sqrt{ {s}^{2} } \\ 15 = s[/tex]

Ms. Estefan only needs to put a fence around the three sides of the square. Thus, we only need to multiply the side by 3. That is,

[tex]15 \times 3 = 45[/tex]

Therfore, Ms. Estefan needs only to fence [tex]\red{45 ft.}[/tex] of the square garden.
