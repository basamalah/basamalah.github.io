La Jota Cagayana originated from Enrile, Cagayan. This is done with stamping or stepping heavily on feet of every measure of waltz step. Female wears maskota and camisa with stiff panualo or Maria Clara dress. Male wears barong tagalog andblack or dark colored trousers.

15. What is the history of Jota Batanguena?


What is the history of Jota
Batanquena?

The history of Jota Batanquena is closely intertwined with the history of the Portuguese fado. This type of music originated in the 19th century in the city of Coimbra, Portugal. It was initially known as "Cantigas d'amigo" (songs of friendship), and was usually performed by two singers who would improvise lyrics about love, longing, and nostalgia. Over time, the lyrical content of these songs began to change, and eventually the genre was renamed "fado." As the fado style evolved, so too did the Jota Batanquena, which began to incorporate elements of fado, giving it a unique flavor. As it spread throughout Portugal, the style grew in popularity, becoming an important part of the country’s musical heritage. Today, Jota Batanquena is still performed in Portugal, and has become a symbol of the country’s rich musical tradition.

27. what is La jota Moncadena?​


Answer:

The Jota Mocandena is the interpretation and adaptation by the old people of Moncada, Tarlac. Hence, the dance is named after the place from where it originated. It is a combination of Spanish and Ilocano dance steps and music. The use of bamboo castanets makes the dance lively, gay and delightful.

28. 17.Where was La JotaMoncadeñaoriginated in?​


Answer:

Moncada, Tarlac,

Explanation:

Early Filipinos evolved their own simple versions of the jota. The jota moncadeña, named after its place of origin, Moncada, Tarlac, combines Spanish and Ilocano movements. Its initial movements are lively and fast, accented by the scintillating rhythm of elongated bamboo castanets.


29. What makes la jota isabela unique from other dances?


Answer:

La Jota Isabela *

Origin: Cuayan, Isabela

Explanation:

La Jota dances were the most popular during and after the Spanish regime. Named after Queen Isabela of Spain, Isabela province in northwestern Luzon is home to a very lively adaptation of the Spanish Jota. It was said to originate in the grand ballrooms of elegant mansions as guests dances to the rondalla while wearing their finest gowns and suits.

A fine example of a filipinized spanish jota, but unlike other jotas this dance does not use elongated bamboo castanets. This Ilocano dance was first performed b the Ilocano settlers of the woodlands of old Isabela.
