9. Fs 6 episode 6 the teacher as a professional


The teacher as a professional uphold certain values and responsibilities.
1. Value of excellence
As a professional, the teacher should be committed to excellence.Proper education and certification is needed to carry out quality work. A teacher, as a professional, is responsible to equip herself/himself with the relevant knowledge, skills and other qualification.
2. Value of ethics
As a professional, the teacher is bound by moral standards and professional etiquette that will dictate how to operate in and out of the classroom. A teacher is responsible to conduct himself/herself properly in terms of appearance, words, actions, interactions with students, colleagues and parents.
3. Value of self-development
As a professional, the teacher must aim for consistent learning. A teacher must strive to update his/her knowledge and upgrade his/her skills.
4. Value of compliance
There are policies and laws that teachers have to abide by. As a professional, this should be observed by the teacher at all times.

15. Fs 2 episode 6 deductive and inductive methods of teaching answer


Two very distinct and opposing instructional approaches are inductive and deductive method of teaching. Both approaches can offer certain advantages, but the biggest difference is the role of the teacher.

Deductive - The teacher conducts the lesson by introducing and explaining concepts; this approach is very teacher-centered. This means that teacher gives the student new concept, explains it, and then has the student practice using the concept: For example, when teaching a new grammar concept, the teacher will introduce the concept, explain the rules related to its use, and finally the students will practice using the concept in a variety of ways.

Inductive - Is much more student-centered approach and makes use of strategy known as "noticing". Instead of explaining a given concept and following his explanation with examples, the teacher presents students with many examples showing how the concept is used. The intent is for the students to notice, by the way of the examples, how the concept works.

Reporting Students Performance

Teachers must have accurate data about the grading system; student performance must be recorded base on its achievement and effort. A comprehensive report of grades to each learner must observe at all times. Teachers’ judgments reflect on learners’ capability and intelligence; therefore, critical evaluation on the given data must apply appropriately and honestly.

Tools and ways of reporting students’ performance Parent-Teachers Meeting, this is the perfect time to report learners’ achievements; teachers and parents’ communication must be well presented and organized. The problems and conflicts must be discussed during the meeting; confidential information about the learners must be revealed to the authorized person only. Student Portfolio is a collection of student’s output such as quizzes, examinations, oral examinations, and the likes. This is one of the effective ways to report learners’ achievements wherein student outputs were organized and filed accordingly. Types of Assessment Diagnostic Assessment, the aim of this evaluation was to know learners’ prior knowledge about the topic. Formative Assessment, it aims was to identify learners’ understanding during the teaching-learning process. Summative Assessment, this is the most important part of the evaluation in which the assessment intends to measure and validate learners’ understanding. The following were the assessment tools used to provide precise information about student performance: Multiple Choices, this is the use of pen and paper; the learners choose appropriate answers among the choices. Project, this can be performance or output base; interpretation of grade must be assessed base on a reasonable scale. Performance Examination, this is the actual demonstration of acquired knowledge and understanding; teachers must use rubric during the evaluation. Rubric, is a tool used to measure accurately the students’ performance.

26. Fs 2 episode 3 the three domains of knowledge/learning activities


The three domains of learning include the following:
1. Cognitive
2. Affective
3. Psychomotor
The goal of education is to produce well-rounded individuals. Cognitive domain pertains to the aspect of learning that shapes the cognition or intellectual abilities of the students. Affective refers to the areas of emotional growth or the molding of attitude. Psychomotor includes the physical movements and the use of motor skills.
As a teacher, when you plan lessons, it is important to come up with learning activities that will support these domains.
Examples of cognitive activities include solving a problem or making an experiment. This will compel the students to brainstorm and analyze. Examples of affective activities include asking students for their opinion, having them work in a group or making them self-reflect. Psychomotor activities include engaging them in a physical game or going on a field trip.
