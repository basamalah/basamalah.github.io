10. Find the meaning of the following 1. Mass 2. Volume 3. Temperature 4. Pressure 5. SI Unit for Volume Pressure 6. Metric Unit, English Unit 7. Volume units and their equivalents. 8. Pressure unit and their equivalents 9. Temperature units and their equivalent10. Boyles Law


HELLO FOLK

Find the meaning of the following.

1. Mass - Mass is the amount of matter present in an object, typically measured in kilograms (kg) or grams (g).

2. Volume - Volume is the amount of space occupied by an object or substance, typically measured in cubic meters (m3) or liters (L).

3. Temperature - Temperature is the degree of hotness or coldness of a substance, typically measured in Celsius (°C) or Fahrenheit (°F).

4. Pressure - Pressure is the force exerted per unit area, typically measured in Pascals (Pa) or pounds per square inch (psi).

5. SI Unit for Volume Pressure - The SI unit for volume is cubic meters (m3) and for pressure is Pascals (Pa).

6. Metric Unit, English Unit - Metric units are based on the International System of Units (SI), while English units are based on the British Imperial System.

7. Volume units and their equivalents - Some common volume units and their equivalents include liter (L) = 1,000 milliliters (mL) = 1 decimeter cubed (dm3), gallon (gal) = 3.785 liters, and cubic foot (ft3) = 28.317 liters.

8. Pressure unit and their equivalents - Some common pressure units and their equivalents include Pascals (Pa) = 1 Newton per square meter (N/m2), pounds per square inch (psi) = 6894.75 Pascals, and atmospheres (atm) = 101,325 Pascals.

9. Temperature units and their equivalent - Some common temperature units and their equivalents include Celsius (°C) = (5/9) x (°F - 32), Kelvin (K) = °C + 273.15, and Fahrenheit (°F) = (9/5) x °C + 32.

10. Boyles Law - Boyle's Law is a gas law that states that at a constant temperature, the volume of a gas is inversely proportional to its pressure. This means that as pressure increases, volume decreases, and vice versa.
