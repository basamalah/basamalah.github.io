8. What is a stethoscope


Answer:
Stethoscope is an acoustic medical device for auscultation, or listening to the internal sounds of an animal or human body. It typically has a small disc-shaped resonator that is placed against the chest, and two tubes connected to earpieces. It is often used to listen to lung and heart sounds.

10. why was the stethoscope invented


The stethoscope was invented in France in 1816 by René Laennec at the Necker-Enfants Malades Hospital in Paris. It consisted of a wooden tube and was monaural. Laennec invented the stethoscope because he was uncomfortable placing his ear on women's chests to hear heart sounds.

12. How to use a stethoscope?​


Answer:

How do you properly use a stethoscope?

Five tips for using your Stethoscope properly

Align the headset. Before placing the eartips in your ears, make sure they point forward, away from you.

Use the right eartip size.

Rotate to the correct side.

Remove potential obstructions.

Check the seal.

Which side of a stethoscope do you use?

The diaphragm (larger) side is ideal for detecting breathing, as well as normal heart rhythms. The bell (smaller) side is better for detecting abnormal heart sounds and bruits, as well as bowel sounds. A cardiac exam should begin with use of the diaphragm, followed by use of the bell.

30. How to use a stethoscope?​


Answer:

A stethoscope is a medical instrument used to hear sounds made by the heart, lungs, and intestines. Using a stethoscope to hear sounds is called auscultation[1] Medical professionals are trained to use stethoscopes, but you can learn how to use one too. Keep reading to learn how to use a stethoscope.

Explanation:

Get a high-quality stethoscope. A high-quality stethoscope is important. The better the quality of your stethoscope, the easier it will be for you to listen to your patient’s body.

Single tubed stethoscopes are better than double tubed ones. The tubes in double tubed stethoscopes can rub together. This noise can make it hard to hear heart sounds.[2]

Thick, short, and relatively stiff tubing is best, unless you plan to wear the stethoscope around your neck. In that case, a longer tube is best.[3]

Make sure that tubing is free of leaks by tapping on the diaphragm (the flat side of the chest piece). As you tap, use the earpieces to listen for sounds. If you don’t hear anything, there may be a leak.

Adjust your stethoscope’s earpieces. It is important to make sure that the earpieces are facing forward and that they fit well. Otherwise, you might not be able to hear anything with your stethoscope.[4]

Make sure that the earpieces are facing forward. If you put them in backwards, you won’t be able to hear anything.[5]

Make sure that the earpieces fit snugly and have a good seal to keep out ambient noise. If the ear pieces don’t fit well, most stethoscopes have removable earpieces. Visit a medical supply store to purchase different earpieces.[6]

With some stethoscopes, you can also tilt the earpieces forward stethoscopes to ensure a better fit.[7]

Check the earpiece tension on your stethoscope. In other words, make sure that the earpieces are close to your head but not too close. If your earpieces are too tight or too loose, readjust them.[8]

If the earpieces are too loose, you may not be able to hear anything. To tighten the tension, squeeze the headset near the earpieces.[9]

If the earpieces are too tight, they might hurt your ears and you might have a hard time using your stethoscope. To reduce the tension, pull the headset apart gently.[10]

Choose an appropriate chest piece for your stethoscope. There are many different types of chest pieces available for stethoscopes. Choose one that is appropriate for your needs. Chest pieces come in different sizes for adults and children.
