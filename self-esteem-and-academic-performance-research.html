12. research hypothesis about the impact of colorism to the self esteem​


Individuals who face colorism, or discrimination based on the perceived lightness or darkness of their complexion, may have poorer self-esteem compared to those who do not face colorism, according to a research hypothesis about the impact of colorism on self-esteem. This theory's premise is that exposure to prejudice or discrimination can have a destructive effect on a person's sense of worth and mental health. Experimental studies in which participants were randomly assigned to receive either positive or negative messages about their skin color and then measuring the impact of these messages on self-esteem would be two examples of how we could test this hypothesis.
