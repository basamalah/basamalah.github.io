14. A pulley of 5 cm radius on a motor is turning at 30 rev/s and slows down uniformly to 20 rev/s in 2 seconds. Calculate the angular acceleration of the motor. a. -10 rev/s^2 b. 5 rev/s^2 c. -5 rev/s^2 d. 10 rev/s^2


The angular acceleration of the motor : c. -5 rev/s

Further explanation

The speed of the rotating object is called angular velocity. This value is part of circular motion.

The angular velocity is denoted by w including the vector quantity. The unit used is usually rpm (rotation per minute) or radians/sec

Average angular velocity is the ratio of angular displacement to the time interval

[tex]\rm \omega = \dfrac {\Delta \theta} {\Delta t}[/tex]

Circular motion with constant acceleration (angular acceleration denoted by α) can be formulated:  

[tex]\rm \omega=\omega o+\alpha t\\\\\alpha=\dfrac{\omega-\omega o}{t}[/tex]

ωo = initial angular velocity  

ω = angular velocity after t  

A motor is turning at 30 rev/s and slows down uniformly to 20 rev/s in 2 seconds, so :

ωo = 30 rev/s

ω = 20 rev/s

t = 2 s

The angular acceleration :

[tex]\rm \alpha=\dfrac{20-30}{2}\\\\\alpha=\boxed{\bold{-5~rev/s}}[/tex]

22. It was designed to replace PCI, PCI-X, and AGP​


PCI Express (PCIe) was designed to replace PCI, PCI-X, and AGP.

PCI (Peripheral Component Interconnect) is a standard for connecting peripheral devices to a computer's motherboard. PCI-X (PCI Extended) is an enhanced version of PCI that supports faster data transfer rates. AGP (Accelerated Graphics Port) is a specialized version of PCI designed specifically for video cards.

PCIe, on the other hand, is a high-speed serial computer expansion bus standard that is designed to replace the older PCI, PCI-X and AGP standards. It offers faster data transfer rates, increased bandwidth, and improved scalability compared to its predecessors. It allows for more efficient use of a computer's resources and enables faster communication between the computer's processor and peripheral devices.
