20. List the factors that influence employee compensation and benefits. Describe the different performance appraisal methods. Written Essay Critical Incidents Graphic Rating ScalesBehaviorally Anchored Rating Scales (BARS) Multiperson Comparison Management by Objectives 360 Degree Feedback ​


Factors that influence employee compensation and benefits:

Job responsibilities and tasksEducation, skills, and experienceIndustry and market trendsCompany size and revenueGeographic locationCollective bargaining agreementsGovernment regulations and lawsSupply and demand for certain skills and rolesCost of living and inflationCompany culture and values

Different Performance Appraisal Methods:

Critical Incidents: This method involves recording and evaluating specific events or incidents where an employee demonstrated exceptional or poor performance. It provides a detailed analysis of an employee's actions and behaviors.Graphic Rating Scales: This method involves rating an employee's performance on a scale, typically from 1 to 5, for various job-related criteria such as quality of work, productivity, and teamwork.Behaviorally Anchored Rating Scales (BARS): This method combines elements of the critical incidents and graphic rating scales methods. It involves identifying specific behaviors or actions that align with job responsibilities and using them to evaluate employee performance.Multiperson Comparison: This method involves ranking employees within a department or team, based on their performance. It provides a relative evaluation of employee performance, rather than an absolute evaluation.Management by Objectives: This method involves setting specific, measurable, and achievable goals for employees and evaluating their performance based on their ability to meet these goals.360 Degree Feedback: This method involves gathering feedback from multiple sources, including managers, peers, subordinates, and customers. It provides a comprehensive view of an employee's performance from different perspectives.
