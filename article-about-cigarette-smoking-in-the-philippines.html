16. opinion about cigarette smoking and alcohol consumption​


Tobacco and alcohol both have the potential to harm the heart. While the negative impact of smoking on the risk of cardiovascular disease is well-known and obvious (the risk of heart disease increases as one smokes more), the influence of alcohol is more complicated.
Moderate drinking (3–14 drinks per week) has been linked to a lower risk of heart attack, but heavy drinking has been linked to an increased risk of heart attack, heart failure, stroke, and high blood pressure.
Because smokers and drinkers often share similar behavioural and lifestyle patterns, it's uncertain if the combined or independent effects of smoking and alcohol greatly increase cardiovascular risk.
Nonetheless, the health issues persist.
