1. explain what is assertion of fact, assertion of convention, assertion of opinion,and assertion of preference ​


Answer:

Assertion of Fact: An assertion of fact is a statement that can be proven to be true or false. It is based on evidence and can be verified. Examples include: The sky is blue, Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.

Assertion of Convention: An assertion of convention is a statement that reflects the accepted behavior or practice in a particular society or culture. Examples include: It is polite to say please and thank you, In the United States, people drive on the right side of the road.

Assertion of Opinion: An assertion of opinion is a statement that expresses an individual's beliefs or feelings about something. It cannot be proven to be true or false and may vary from person to person. Examples include: Chocolate ice cream is the best flavor, I think rap music is underrated.

Assertion of Preference: An assertion of preference is a statement that expresses an individual's preference for something over another option. It cannot be proven to be true or false and may vary from person to person. Examples include: I prefer coffee over tea, I like apples more than oranges.

• This type of assertion is a statement used to express the feelings, belief and opinions directly by the writer. This type of assertion is usually used in writing formal papers like thesis and dissertation.

Emphatic Assertion

• This is a statement used to express empathy  

• It is included on the writings on how a person understands the feelings and emotions of the literary author.  

• This type of assertion is can be used on:

1. writing a formal  

2. informal paper

It is usually composed of two parts:

1) First part  

is the statement that recognizes the situation or even the feelings of the characters on the text being read followed by

2.) Second statement

2) This is where the writer states his stand about the situation.

I -Language Assertion

• This is a statement used to express the feeling and preference of the writer.

•  It is called I-Language because it focuses on the writer and is using the pronoun ‘I’. This type of assertion is

1.  Recommended if the author wants to express negative feelings and opinion.  

2. This is best used in writing a review or reflection paper.

3 parts of the I language Assertion

1.  Accurate information from literary work, especially the topic the writer disagrees with (When you. . .).  

2. The effect or feeling of the writer towards the topic (It affects/I feel...).  

3. The preference or recommendation of the writer (Therefore, I prefer/I want...).

Convention

• These are the belief that are socially accepted but cannot be verified objectively.

Fact

• The statement that can objectively proven by proofs such as experiences, witnessessurveys, testimonies.observation or conclusions of studies of research.  

      Opinion

• The statements that are based on facts. This type of assertion is open to disputes, debates or arguments..

Preference  

• This is based on personal choice thus it is subjective but still cannot be verified objectively.
