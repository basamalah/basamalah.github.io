2. A landscaper is putting in a brick patio. The area of the patio is 110 square meters. If the bricks measure 10cm by 20cm, how many bricks will it take to make the patio?​


The landscaper needs 5,500 bricks to make the patio.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Explanation:

[tex]\\[/tex]

Given:

Area of patio = 110 m²

Area of bricks = 10 cm × 20 cm

[tex]\\[/tex]

Required:

Number of bricks needed, n

[tex]\\[/tex]

Equation:

[tex]n\:=\:\frac{Area\:of\:patio}{Area\:of\:bricks}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Solution:Convert 10 cm and 20 cm to meters

[tex]=\:10\:cm\times\frac{1\:m}{100\:cm}[/tex]

[tex]=\:0.10\:m[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]=\:20\:cm\times\frac{1\:m}{100\:cm}[/tex]

[tex]=\:0.20\:m[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Solve for the number of bricks needed

[tex]n\:=\:\frac{Area\:of\:patio}{Area\:of\:bricks}[/tex]

[tex]n\:=\:\frac{110\:m²}{0.1\:m\times0.2\:m}[/tex]

[tex]n\:=\:\frac{110\:m²}{0.02\:m²}[/tex]

[tex]n\:=\:5,500\:bricks[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Final answer:The landscaper needs 5,500 bricks to make the patio.

18. The length of Kara’s rectangular patio can be expressed as 2x − 1 and the width can be expressed as x + 6. In terms of x, what is the area of her patio?​


Answer:

2x² + 11x - 6 units

Step-by-step explanation:

A = LW

Given,

L = 2x - 1W = x + 6

Substitute the given value of L and W.

A = (2x-1)(x+6)

Expand (2x-1)(x+6) using FOIL method

A = 2x² + 12x - x - 6

A = 2x² + 11x - 6

∴ The area of her patio is 2x² + 11x - 6 units.

27. A landscaper is putting in a brick patio. The area of the patio is 110 square meters. If the bricks measure 10 cm by 20 cm, how many bricks will it take to make the patio? with solution ​


Answer:

it will take 5,500 bricks to make the patio.

Step-by-step explanation:

1. Find the area of each brick

Each brick is 10 cm × 20 cm

Each brick is 10 cm × 20 cmThat is 200 cm² per brick.

2. Find the area of the patio in square centimeters

110 m² = 1,100,000 cm²

3. Find out how many 200 cm² bricks it would take to fill in an area

1,100,000 cm² ÷ 200 cm² = 5,500
