9. Benevolent Assimilation​


Answer:

Benevolent assimilation refers to a policy of the United States towards the Philippines as described in a proclamation by U.S. President William McKinley issued in a memorandum to the U.S. Secretary of War on December 21, 1898, following the signing of the treaty which ended the Spanish-American War.[1] It stated that future control, disposition, and government of the Philippine islands had been ceded to the United States and that the U.S. military government was to be extended over the whole of the ceded territory.

About six months earlier, on June 12, 1898, Emilio Aguinaldo had declared the Philippines independent and established a revolutionary government. The Filipino revolutionary armed forces were deployed surrounding American Army positions occupying Manila, creating a stand-off between opposing forces that would erupt in fighting in early 1899 to ignite the Philippine–American War.

28. The main principle of Confucianism is ____, which means "humaneness" or "benevolence."


answer: REN

The Confucian virtue of REN ("benevolence" or "humaneness") means that a person will always do what is right, regardless of the consequences.

***********more information about REN below***********

Ren Confucianism Definition:

Confucius was apprehensive with the human beings' development and with the relationships of humans to each other. Filial piety is one of the ways in which an individual must act towards others from underlying attitude of humaneness. Another way is through rituals.

Thus, Confucius's concept of humaneness is perhaps the best articulated in Confucian version of Golden Rule phrased in the negative: "Don’t do unto others what you’d not like others do unto you".


This Confucius’ rén has a political dimension. It states that if a ruler lacks rén, it will be very challenging if not impossible for his subjects to behave benevolently. Ren is the basis of Confucian political theory as it presupposes autocratic ruler, urged to renounce from acting inhumanely to anyone.

A disgusting inhumane leader runs risk of losing mandate of heaven - the right to rule. Such a person need not be followed. But a leader who can reign humanely and can also take care of the people around her or him is to be obeyed for the magnanimity of his power shows that he has been assigned by the heaven.

Well, the worldly wise concern of this belief rests upon the belief that human beings are fundamentally good, teachable, and perfectible through self-cultivation and self-creation. Confucian philosophy’s thought in fact focuses on the virtues and its cultivation in a morally organised realm.


***********more information about REN below***********

Ren Meaning in English:

Some basic Confucian ethical practices are rén, yì, and lǐ, zhì and xin.

It holds one in contempt for failure to sustain cardinal moral values of rén and yì.

Here are the The Five Constants or Wuchang:

·         Rén Confucianism (benevolence, humaneness)

·         Yì Confucianism (righteousness or justice)

·         Lǐ Confucianism (proper rite)

·         Zhì Confucianism (knowledge)

·         Xìn Confucianism (integrity)

  

These philosophy’s ethical codes are described as “humanistic”.

Again, Rén (or ren) is translated as the “benevolence” or "humaneness" – this is the essence proper of a being, – as the character of compassionate mind.

Ren is popularly known as the virtue endowed by heaven. It states that an individual may achieve oneness with heaven realizing her or his origin in heaven and consequently, her or his divine essence.

This Confucian virtue denotes that the worthy sensation and virtuous human experiences could occur if the person is doing some altruistic deeds.

 

There was a story that once, Confucius was asked by one of his students, he was asked to define the rules of rén. He replied, "One should see nothing improper, hear nothing improper, say nothing improper, do nothing improper."

Confucius also defined it as a "wishing to be established oneself - seeks also to establish others; wishing to be enlarged oneself - seeks also to enlarge others." Thus, scholars explained it ren as a virtue that is close to human beings and never leaves them. As a saying goes, "Ren isn’t far off. He who seeks ren has already found it."
