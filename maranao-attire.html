5. compare the ballroom attire in the 16th century and the attire in the 20th century. Describe each


Answer:

The ballroom attire in the 16th century was characterized by grand and elaborate styles. Women would wear large, flowing gowns made of expensive materials such as silk and adorned with intricate embellishments such as lace and jewels. Men would wear high-collared shirts and doublets paired with breeches, hose, and shoes with large buckles.

In contrast, ballroom attire in the 20th century became more streamlined and simple. Women would often wear sleek, form-fitting dresses in solid colors or with simple patterns, while men would wear tuxedos or suits in classic styles. The focus shifted from elaborate embellishments and accessories to clean lines and understated elegance.

Overall, the evolution of ballroom attire reflects changes in fashion and cultural attitudes over time, with a move towards simplicity and practicality in the 20th century.

13. How are these attires and fabrics different form each other? 1.Traditional Clothing B'laan2. Maranao's Traditional Clothing 3. Bagobos Traditional Clothing ​


The traditional clothing of the B'laan, Maranao, and Bagobo ethnic groups in the Philippines are distinct from each other in terms of their style, design, and materials used.

B'laan Traditional Clothing:

The B'laan tribe, which is mostly found in Mindanao, wears clothing made of abaca fibers, a type of plant that is common in the region. The fabric is made by hand-weaving and has a rough texture. The clothing is usually loose and flowing, with colorful patterns and designs that are symbolic of their culture and beliefs. The B'laan women wear long skirts called "lufid" that are decorated with beads and shells.

Maranao Traditional Clothing:

The Maranao, who are also from Mindanao, have a more elaborate and ornate traditional clothing. They wear a long-sleeved shirt called "barong a labba" which is embroidered with intricate designs using silk or gold threads. The shirt is paired with a loose-fitting pants called "sirwal" and a headscarf called "kandit" which is adorned with pearls and beads. The Maranao women wear a long, flowing dress called "malong" which is also embroidered with intricate designs.

Bagobo Traditional Clothing:

The Bagobo tribe, which is found in the southern part of Mindanao, wears clothing made of abaca fibers, similar to the B'laan tribe. However, their clothing is characterized by geometric patterns and bright colors. The Bagobo women wear a long skirt called "inabal" which is handwoven and decorated with intricate designs. They also wear a sleeveless blouse called "lufitan" which is embroidered with colorful threads.

In summary, these three traditional clothing of B'laan, Maranao, and Bagobo are unique from each other in terms of their design, materials, and cultural significance. They reflect the distinct cultural heritage and practices of each tribe.

The Blaan people, alternatively spelled as "B'laan," are one of the indigenous peoples of Southern Mindanao in the Philippines. The Maranao people (Maranao: ['mәranaw]; Filipino: Maranaw), also spelled Meranao, Maranaw, and Mëranaw, is the term used by the Philippine government to refer to the southern indigenous people who are the "people of the lake", a predominantly-Muslim Lanao province region of the Philippine island of Mindanao.
