22. Which coastlines most closely match each other and are, therefore, used as evidence to support Wegener’s theory? Africa and Eurasia Australia and Africa Africa and South America South America and North America


Alfred Wegener proposed the continental drift theory. He believed that the continents were once joined together and called it Pangea which means “all earth”. This Theory were then be supplemented by a lot of evidence. One of the geological evidence of continental drift is the jigsaw puzzle. Wegener observed the continents and found out that the coastline of one continent matches the coastline of the other continents as if it were once joined together and just break apart.

The coastline of Africa and South America, India, Antarctica and Australia fit one another, Eurasia and North America also fit together.

Among the choices given, the coastline most closely match each other and are therefore used as evidence to support Wegener’s theory is South America and Africa.
