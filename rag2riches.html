6. RA The Rags2Riches team ensured that its products were 100 percent consistent with their values. All the materials were upcycled and, thus, "eco-friendly" (e.g., organic materials that did not use harmful dyes or chemicals). Also, expansion resulted in engaging the other nearby communities. Clearly, the women-and other communities as well-had become empowered and enriched. Essentially, this social business enterprise sought to improve livelihood outcomes of the poor. Apart from ensuring attainment of the 3P's-people, planet, profits-, Rags2Riches sought to exert positive influence on the market, on the communities it works with, and eventually on the world. This case highlights the good effects that can be achieved by the mere desire to serve people and communities. Guide Questions: What is the main vision and mission of Rags2Riches? Explain your answer based on the business model of Rags2Riches. 1. 4. Do you know of any other enterprise in the Philippines that has achieved what Rags2Riches has achieved, i.e., serve a social function and, at the same time, make money? If so, describe the nature or operations of that enterprise.​


Answer:

1. The main vision and mission of Rags2Riches is to alleviate poverty by creating sustainable livelihood opportunities for marginalized communities, particularly women, through ethical fashion. The enterprise seeks to achieve social, environmental, and economic sustainability, which it calls the 3P's: people, planet, and profits. Rags2Riches aims to provide a fair and dignified income to its partner artisans while promoting sustainable fashion and minimizing its environmental impact.

2. Rags2Riches employs a social business model that empowers marginalized communities by providing them with skills training, design support, and access to the market. The enterprise works with partner communities to collect textile scraps and upcycle them into high-quality fashion and home accessories. Rags2Riches ensures that its products are consistent with its values, using only eco-friendly and organic materials that do not harm the environment or the people who produce them. By expanding its operations, Rags2Riches engages nearby communities, thus providing more opportunities for marginalized groups to participate in the value chain and earn a sustainable income.

3. Yes, it is possible for a business enterprise to fulfill a social function and earn a profit at the same time. Rags2Riches is an excellent example of this. By providing sustainable livelihood opportunities to marginalized communities, the enterprise is able to produce high-quality fashion products that appeal to consumers who value ethical and sustainable fashion. Rags2Riches has been successful in creating a market for its products by emphasizing the social and environmental benefits of its business model, which in turn generates profits that are reinvested in the enterprise and its partner communities.

4. There are other enterprises in the Philippines that have achieved a similar goal of serving a social function while making a profit. One example is Hapinoy, a social enterprise that provides microfinance and business development services to sari-sari store owners in rural areas. By helping these small business owners improve their operations and access to capital, Hapinoy is able to promote entrepreneurship and financial inclusion while generating revenue from its services. Another example is Human Nature, a natural and organic personal care and household products company that sources its ingredients from local farmers and employs community-based workers. Human Nature promotes ethical and sustainable sourcing while providing fair and dignified employment opportunities to its workers.
