4. Intentional choosing of sample size


What is sampling?
Sampling is a technique of selecting individual members or a subset of the population to make statistical inferences from them and estimate characteristics of the whole population. Different sampling methods are widely used by researchers in market research so that they do not need to research the entire population to collect actionable insights.

It is also a time-convenient and a cost-effective method and hence forms the basis of any research design. Sampling techniques can be used in a research survey software for optimum derivation.

For example, if a drug manufacturer would like to research the adverse side effects of a drug on the country’s population, it is almost impossible to conduct a research study that involves everyone. In this case, the researcher decides a sample of people from each demographic and then researches them, giving him/her indicative feedback on the drug’s behavior.

7. letter of intent applying a new job​


Answer:

A letter of intent, also known as a cover letter, is a document that you can include with your job application to provide additional information about your qualifications, experience, and interest in the position you are applying for. Here are some tips for writing a letter of intent for a new job:

1.Start with a strong introduction that explains who you are and why you are interested in the job.

2.Mention any relevant experience or qualifications that make you a good fit for the position.

3.Explain why you are interested in working for this particular company.

4.Use specific examples to illustrate your skills and achievements.

5.Keep the tone of your letter professional and avoid using overly casual language.

6.Close the letter by thanking the employer for considering your application and expressing your hope to be invited for an interview.

7.Edit and proofread your letter carefully to ensure that it is error-free.

A letter of intent (LOI) is a document that outlines the terms and conditions of a proposed agreement or transaction. In the context of applying for a new job, an LOI may be used to express interest in a particular position and to highlight your qualifications and experience.

Here is an example of what an LOI for a new job might look like:

Dear [Employer],

I am writing to express my strong interest in the [Position] role at [Company]. As a highly skilled and motivated [Industry] professional, I am confident that I would be a valuable asset to your team.

With [X years/months] of experience in [Industry/Field], I have developed a strong foundation of knowledge and skills that I believe make me an ideal candidate for this position. In my previous roles at [Company 1] and [Company 2], I have consistently demonstrated my ability to [List relevant skills/achievements].

I am excited about the opportunity to contribute my skills and experience to the success of [Company] and am confident that I can make a positive impact in this role. Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to the opportunity to discuss this position further and how I can contribute to the success of your team.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]
