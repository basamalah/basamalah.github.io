3. A square field near Valenzuela Town Center has an area of 2000 square feet. To the nearest foot, how much fencing would be needed to enclose the field?


Interesting question!

Now, see the figure.

The yellow region is 2000 sq. feet. Therefore, Area of square is 2000 sq feet.

Remember your formula:

[tex]\boxed{\sf{\blue{SIDE=\sqrt{AREA} }}}[/tex]

Here,

[tex]\to \sf Side=\sqrt{2000}[/tex]

[tex]\to \sf Side=20\sqrt{5} \ feet[/tex]

⊱ ──────  ✯  ────── ⊰

Now, to enclose the field, we need to cover the boundary of the field. In other words, We need to cover the perimeter of the field.

[tex]\boxed{\sf{\blue{Perimeter=4 \big(Side \big)}}}[/tex]

Here,

[tex]\sf \to Perimeter=4 \big(20\sqrt{5} \big)[/tex]

[tex]\sf \to Perimeter= \big(80\sqrt{5} \big) \ feet[/tex]

√5 = 2.2360

[tex]\sf \to Perimeter= \big(80 \times 2.2360 \big) \ feet[/tex]

[tex]\sf \to Perimeter \approx \big(178.88 \big) \ feet[/tex]

Approx value is 178.88 feet.

9. difference betweem emissive and non emissive display​


Answer:

Emissive displays are devices that convert electrical energy into light; where the image is produced directly on the screen. Non-emissive displays use optical effects to convert sunlight into graphical patterns and shapes; where the light is produced behind the screen and the image is formed by filtering this light.

16. sino si pio Valenzuela?​


Answer:

Pío Valenzuela y Alejandrino (July 11, 1869 – April 6, 1956) was a Filipino physician and revolutionary leader. At the age of 23, he joined the society of Katipunan, a movement which sought the independence of the Philippines from Spanish colonial rule and started the Philippine Revolution. Together with Andrés Bonifacio and Emilio Jacinto, they formed the secret chamber of the society called Camara Reina. He took charge of the publication of Ang Kalayaan, Katipunan's first and only official publication.[1][2] He was the one who tried to convince the exiled José Rizal to join the revolutionary movement.[3]

When the Katipunan was discovered, he fled to Balintawak (now part of Quezon City) on August 20, 1896, but he later availed of an amnesty that the Spanish colonial government offered and he surrendered on September 1, 1896. He was deported to Spain where he was tried and imprisoned in Madrid. He was later transferred to Málaga, and then to a Spanish outpost in Africa. He was incarcerated for about two years.

He returned to the Philippines in April 1899 and resumed his medical practice.[1] He was immediately arrested by the Americans in fear of inciting insurrection. While still in prison, Valenzuela was elected the municipal president in his hometown Polo which forced the Americans to release him. From 1921-1925, he served as the governor of the province of Bulacan.[4]

23. Give 5 example of Alpha Emission, Beta Emission, Positron Emission


Answer:

Many alpha emitters occur naturally in the environment. For example, alpha particles are given off by radionuclides such as uranium-238, radium-226, and other members of the naturally occurring uranium, thorium and actinium decay series which are present in varying amounts in nearly all rocks, soils, and water.

Many beta emitters occur naturally in the radioisotopes found in the natural radioactive decay chains of uranium, thorium and actinium. Examples include lead-210, bismuth-214 and thallium-206. Beta emitters are also commonly found in the radioactive products of nuclear fission.

In positron emission, the atomic number Z decreases by one while the mass number A remains the same. Magnesium-23 has 12 protons and 11 neutrons. The neutron:proton ratio is 11:12 or 0.92:1. It undergoes positron emission to form sodium-23.

26. 1. Describe the material emission of Shield Volcano. 2. Describe the material emission of Cinder Cone Volcano.3. Describe the material emission of Composite Volcano. ​


[tex] \huge\green{Answer:}[/tex]

1. Describe the material emission of Shield Volcano.

Shield volcanoes have small amounts of pyroclastic material, most of which accumulates near the eruptive vents, resulting from fire fuming events. Thus, shield volcanoes typically form from nonexplosive eruptions of low viscosity basaltic magma. Shield volcanoes have low slopes and consist almost entirely of lavas.

2. Describe the material emission of Cinder Cone Volcano.

Cinder cones are the simplest type of volcano. They are built from particles and blobs of congealed lava ejected from a single vent. As the gas-charged lava is blown violently into the air, it breaks into small fragments that solidify and fall as cinders around the vent to form a circular or oval cone.

3. Describe the material emission of Composite Volcano.

Stratovolcanoes/Composite Volcano show inter-layering of lava flows and pyroclastic material, which is why they are sometimes called composite volcanoes. Due to the higher viscosity of magmas erupted from these volcanoes, they are usually more explosive than shield volcanoes.
