SINGULAR and PLURAL NOUNS

A singular noun is simply a noun having only one person, thing, place, or idea. From the word “single”, singular infers a single noun. On the other hand, a plural noun is one that has multiple or many persons, things, places, or ideas. Plural nouns are usually noted with -s or -es at the end.

Some of the singular and plural nouns are:

S: girl; P: girls

S: market; P: markets

S: toy; P: toys

S: teacher; P: teachers

S: sock; P: socks
