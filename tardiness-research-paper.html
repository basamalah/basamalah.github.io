Answer:A. Conceptual FrameworkExplanation:

A conceptual framework is a tool that researchers use to guide their inquiry; it is a set of ideas used to shape the research, a map that may include the research question, the literature review, methodology, and data analysis. Researchers use a conceptual framework to guide data collection and analysis. If, for example, the researcher wanted to discover whether males performed better than girls in a particular subject, he may look at the literature on the development of both sexes, as well as on the methods of socialization of boys and girls, as this could impact what subjects were of interest. The researcher would then study existing literature on male and female development and socialization to help explain what questions she should ask. Such as if girls are more interested in history when it involves actual people, whether guys prefer battle history, etc. The perspectives of boys and girls on a subject could influence their success in that area.
