22. Show how to used toolbox and controls


How to use toolbox and controls

Controls that you can add to Visual Studio projects are displayed in the Toolbox pane. Choose View > Toolbox from the menu bar, or press Ctrl+Alt+X to launch Toolbox.

You can resize and place controls by dragging and dropping them onto the surface of the designer you're using.

Toolbox appears in conjunction with designer views, such as a XAML file's designer view or a Windows Forms App project's designer view. Only the controls that can be utilized in the current designer are displayed in the Toolbox. You can use Toolbox's search function to narrow down the objects that show. Toolbox is hidden by default along the left side of the Visual Studio IDE and only appears when the cursor is moved over it. Toolbox can be pinned (by clicking the Pin icon on its toolbar) so that it stays open when the pointer moves. The Toolbox window can also be undocked and dragged to any location on your screen. By right-clicking Toolbox's toolbar and selecting one of the options, you may dock, undock, and hide it.
