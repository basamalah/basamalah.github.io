Hooke's Law formula

To be able to work on each example problem, a formula is needed to calculate the magnitude of the force. A mathematical formula for Hooke's law is found as follows:

F = -kx

Information:

F = force (N)

k = spring constant (N/m)

x = distance of spring movement from normal position (m)

The length of the new playground is 20 meters longer than the width. The area of the playground is at least 2,925 square meters. a. What would be the minimum width of the playground? b. What would be the minimum length of the playground?


Answer:

Let's first set up an equation to represent the area of the playground. We can call the width of the playground w and the length l. Since the length of the playground is 20 meters longer than the width, we can write the equation as:

Area = w * (w + 20)

Since the area of the playground is at least 2,925 square meters, we can set this equation equal to 2,925 and solve for w:

2,925 = w * (w + 20)

We can use the quadratic formula to solve for w:

w = (-b +- sqrt(b^2 - 4ac)) / 2a

Where:

a = 1

b = 20

c = -2,925

Substituting these values into the formula and solving, we get:

w = (-20 +- sqrt(20^2 - 4 * 1 * (-2,925))) / 2 * 1

w = (-20 +- sqrt(400 + 11,700)) / 2

w = (-20 +- sqrt(12,100)) / 2

w = (-20 +- sqrt(110 * 110)) / 2

w = (-20 +- 110) / 2

w = (-130, 90) / 2

Since the width of the playground must be a positive number, we can disregard the negative solution, and our final answer is:

w = 90 / 2

w = 45

Therefore, the minimum width of the playground is 45 meters.

To find the minimum length of the playground, we can use the equation l = w + 20, where w is the width of the playground that we just found. Substituting our value of w = 45 into this equation, we get:

l = 45 + 20

l = 65

Therefore, the minimum length of the playground is 65 meters.

27. IT INCLUDES THE ANVIL, STIRRUP AND HAMMER.​


Answer:

Ossicles

Explanation:

The ossicles (also called auditory ossicles) are three bones in either middle ear that are among the smallest bones in the human body. They serve to transmit sounds from the air to the fluid-filled labyrinth (cochlea). The absence of the auditory ossicles would constitute a moderate-to-severe hearing loss. The term "ossicle" literally means "tiny bone". Though the term may refer to any small bone throughout the body, it typically refers to the malleus, incus, and stapes (hammer, anvil, and stirrup) of the middle ear.
