8. 1. The spirit of communal unity and cooperation.A. bayanihanB. hospitalityC. pakikipagkapwa-taoD. utang na loob​


Bayanihan shows the spirit of communal unity and cooperation.

What is Bayanihan?

Bayanihan is a Filipino tradition that comes from the word "bayan", which means nation, city, or community.

The term "bayanihan" itself means "to be in the bayan". refers to the spirit of living as a unified group and working together to achieve a certain goal.

 

The concept of bayanihan can be traced back to the traditions of the country. Bayanihan is usually found in rural areas. Residents in the area—especially men—were asked to lend a helping hand to families who were about to move to a new place.

This relocation process does not only move the goods, but the entire building of the house.

 

Traditional Filipino houses (Bahay Kubo) are made of native materials such as bamboo and palm leaves.

So that the residents could carry and lift the house, the bamboo poles on each side of the house were tied tightly. It takes about 15-20 people to move the house to a new place.

 

As a token of gratitude, the owner of the house will serve food to the volunteers after the move is completed.

This spirit of bayanihan demonstrates the Filipino concept of helping others without expecting anything in return. Filipinos believe they should help the 'kababayan' (fellow citizens) as much as they can.

 

Bayanihan is still alive today. There are still people in the countryside who move their houses with the help of residents.

In this modern era, the spirit of the bayanihan is not only in the form of helping to move the house but also shown by other forms of help. especially when natural disasters strike. The Filipino people must immediately help those in need.

21. Do you understand the meaning of what "bayanihan" is? What is bayanihan?​


Answer:

Yes, Although bayanihan can manifest itself in many forms, it is probably most clearly and impressively displayed in the old tradition of neighbors helping a relocating family by getting enough volunteers to carry the whole house, and literally moving it to its new location. They do this by placing long bamboo poles length-wise and cross-wise under the house (traditional Filipino houses were built on stilts), and then carrying the house using this bamboo frame. It takes a fairly large number of people -- often 20 or more -- working together to carry the entire house. All this is done in a happy and festive mood. At the end of the day, the moving family expresses their gratitude by hosting a small fiesta for everyone.
