Solution:

List down the given data from the problem.

•The total amount registered on the taxi's meter

•The constant is P 20

•P 5 in every kilometer traveled by the taxi

Now we have to express each of the following to mathematical expressions. Let's start with 5 pesos every 1 kilometer. We can express this one as 5k where k represents the total distance covered in kilometers. So every time k increases, the amount to be paid will increase also. The constant 20 is still 20 since it is fixed.

T(k) is the dependent variable which can be equivalent to "y" in a function. Therefore, combining all terms we can form:

T(k) = 20 + 5k

28. A taxi driver drove 62kph for 3 hours what is the distance traveled by the taxi? ​


Answer:

186 kilometers

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the distance traveled by the taxi, we can use the formula:

distance = speed × time

where speed is in kilometers per hour (kph) and time is in hours. Substituting the given values, we get:

distance = 62 kph × 3 hours

distance = 186 kilometers

Therefore, the taxi traveled 186 kilometers.
