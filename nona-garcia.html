Answer:

Nona is 10 years old today.

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Assigning variable.

The age of Nona's mother will be expressed in "x". On the other hand, Nona's age will also be expressed as a fraction of x (or a fraction of her mother's age).

Always remember that it will be easier if both Nona and her mother is expressed in both variable.

Step 2: Interpret the first statement "Nona is one-third as old as her mother"

At present: [tex]Age of Nona= \frac{1}{3} Age of Nona's mother[/tex]

Since the age of Nona's mother is expressed in x, then equation one and two will be

[tex]Age of Nona's mother=x\\ Age of Nona= \frac{1}{3}x[/tex]

Step 3: Interpret the second statement "Five years ago, she was only one fifth of the age of her mother"

Five years ago: [tex]Age of Nona=\frac{1}{5} Age of Nona's mother[/tex]

Age of Nona's mother= x -5 (where -5 represent "five years ago")

Since Nona's age is expressed as a fraction of her mother's age, then the equation three and four will be

[tex]Age of Nona's mother=x-5\\ Age of Nona=\frac{1}{5} (x-5)[/tex]

Note: If the situation stated is in the future, then use "+" instead of "-".

Step 4: Get the difference of equation 1 and 2 as well as for equation 3 and 4

The format should be uniform for the two (i.e. older-younger or younger-older). In this problem, I will illustrate the older-younger age difference or Age of Nona's mother - Age of Nona.

Equation 1 and 2: [tex]x- \frac{1}{3}x[/tex]Equation 3 and 4: [tex](x-5)-\frac{1}{5}(x-5)[/tex]

Step 5: Equate the two difference and solve for "x".

Note: All throughout the years the only thing that will remain the same is their age difference.

[tex]x-\frac{1}{3}x=(x-5)-\frac{1}{5}(x-5)[/tex][tex]\frac{2}{3}x=x-5-\frac{1}{5}x+1[/tex][tex]\frac{2}{3}x=\frac{4}{5}x-4[/tex][tex]\frac{2}{3}x-\frac{4}{5}x=-4[/tex][tex]-\frac{2}{15}x=-4[/tex][tex]x=30 years old[/tex]

Nona's mother is 30 years old.

Step 6: Find Nona's present age.

Nona's age at present is 1/3 of her mother's1/3 of 30 is 10

Therefore, Nona is 10 years old today.

10

First make a equation out of the question.

X is Nona’s age and Y is mother’s age

x=y/3

x is one third of y

x-5 = (y-5)/5

-5 means 5 years ago

Substitute the first value of x to the second equation

y/3-5=(y-5)/5

Transpose

y/3 - (y-5)/5=5

Get the LCD

5y/15 - 3(y-5)/15=5

Compute for the left side

5y-3y+15/15=5

2y+15/15=5

Multiply 15 to both sides

2y+15=75

Transpose

2y=75-65

2y=60

y=30

Go back the the first equation

x=30/3

Nona is 10 years old
