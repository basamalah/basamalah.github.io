20. Give the defects or malfunctions detected in each picture of tools, equipment, and utinsils. Then, write the procedures for reporting the detected defects.​


Answer:

1. Scissors

Defect: Broken tension screw, pivot loosens

The looseness in the pivot of scissors is the most typical flaw. The rivet that forms the pivot gradually wears out, loosens, and the two blades have free play one on top of the other.

Report: Any equipment damage should be immediately reported to your parent, instructor, or supervisor. Remove dangerous equipment from use as soon as possible so that it can be repaired. If they can't be mended, properly dispose of them.

2. Knife

Defect: Poor Polishing

The most prevalent quality flaw in metal cutlery is poor polishing. Excessive or inadequate polish on the surface of a metal spoon, fork, or knife can be perceived as poor polishing. Some metal cutlery faults look duller, while others have a higher gloss. The polishing of metal cutlery is sometimes done manually by individuals and is often done by automated machinery in factories. When you apply polish by hand, you're more likely to have an uneven finish.

Report: Remove the knife from easy access of individuals. Report it to people who commonly used it. Sharpen the knife before using it again. Sharpening a knife can be done in a variety of techniques, from steel to stone. However, not all of them are interchangeable, and each one has a distinct function. Remember to sharpen all sides of your knife, regardless of whatever method you use.

3. Can

Defect: With hole causing a spill

Report: Small spills must be reported to the supervisor. Do not use the faulty can or container and mark it with the words "spill" or "defective" to keep others from using it. Any leak of more than 25 gallons from a storage tank into soil or another previous surface must be notified. A leak of more than 100 gallons from a storage tank to an impervious surface or more than 500 gallons into secondary containment must also be notified to the government.

4. Weighing scale

Defect: Error in calibration; the unit was calibrated with an erroneous weight.

Report: If a weighing scale is subjected to erroneous weight reading, propose a test of known materials with their weight. Let's say a 1kg sugar, it should weigh 1kg on aweighing scale, if not then the weighing scale of subject for calibration and more testing. It shouldn't be used until it is correctly calibrated.
