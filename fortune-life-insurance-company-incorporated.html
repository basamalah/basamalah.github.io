3. Describe how life insurance companies earn their profits.​


Answer:

Most insurance companies generate revenue in two ways: Charging premiums in exchange for insurance coverage, then reinvesting those premiums into other interest-generating assets. Like all private businesses, insurance companies try to market effectively and minimize administrative costs.

The insurance company primarily makes money in two ways. One, from the profit it makes on premium payments. And two, from investing those premiums. To figure out what premiums should be, insurance companies employ thousands of actuaries that specialize in advanced statistics and probability.

17. health insurance companies in the philippines


Answer:

HMO and Health Insurance

Explanation:

There are two kinds of health insurance providers in the Philippines. We have HMOs or health maintenance organizations where they provide coverage for outpatient, in-patient and emergency services. The top companies are Intellicare, Maxicare, ValuCare, MediCard and PhilCare. The way it works is that you pay a premium per year and if you get sick within the year, you could get coverage. Usually pre-existing condition are not covered, depending on the rules of the company. If you do not use the services for the year, no money back is to be expected.

The other type is the investment-lined health insurance where investment companies provide health benefits to those who subscribe to their funds. The top companies are SunLife, Manulife, Axa, PruLife and Insular Health. What they do is they provide coverage for dreaded diseases. If you are diagnosed with any of the dreaded diseases, a set amount is given to you for medical purposes.

Another rising type of medical insurance is the prepaid insurance. This is where people could purchase health insurance by "tingi". There are packages that only cater to outpatient, laboratory or dental procedures.

19. an insurance company insures a clients car worth php 1,000,000 for an insurance policy worth php 50,000. the probability of the car getting stolen was determined to be 0.05. what is the company's expected profit?​


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

The expected profit can be calculated using the following formula:

Expected Profit = (Probability of Loss x Amount of Loss) - Cost of Insurance

In this case, the expected profit is:

Expected Profit = (0.05 x 1,000,000) - 50,000

Expected Profit = 50,000 - 50,000

Expected Profit = 0

So, the expected profit is 0 PHP. This means that, on average, the insurance company can expect to neither make nor lose money if they insure the client's car for this amount.
