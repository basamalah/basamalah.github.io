8. TO ALLOW OR NOT ALLOWAlberto Flores, a student of qualitative research is publishing his firstresearch article on therapeutic communication as intervention amongvictims of Yolanda super-typhoon. In this paper, Alberto uses themethodology from a psychologist-author Dr. Sansa even without securingpermission to use it for his own research although the work was cited.Aside from this, the Yolanda victims were forced to be interviewed withthe promise that solutions to the problems will be granted.1. Is there unethical practice in this situation? Why?2. Does Alberto commit an ethical practice in his methodology? Why?3. Is Alberto entitled to become the author of the research withoutmentioning Dr. Sansa's contribution? Explain.4. Why do you need to seek permission from the Yolanda victims duringthe in-depth interviews with the survivors of the catastrophe?​


Answer:

1. Yes, there is an unethical practice in this situation. Using someone else's methodology without securing permission is a violation of intellectual property rights and constitutes plagiarism. Also, forcing people to participate in research with a promise of solutions to their problems is a violation of informed consent, which is a fundamental principle of ethical research.

2. No, Alberto has not committed an ethical practice in his methodology. The failure to secure permission to use someone else's methodology and the use of deceptive practices in obtaining informed consent are both serious ethical violations.

3. No, Alberto is not entitled to become the author of the research without mentioning Dr. Sansa's contribution. Failing to acknowledge the contribution of others is also a form of plagiarism and is considered unethical.

4. You need to seek permission from the Yolanda victims during the in-depth interviews with the survivors of the catastrophe to ensure that they understand what they are agreeing to and to protect their rights and well-being. Informed consent is an essential aspect of ethical research and helps to ensure that participants are treated with dignity and respect.

28. determine the characteristics of Alberto sevesto​


Answer:

Alberto Seveso is a graphic artist, born in Milan and now working in Bristol, UK as a freelance artist. His passion for graphic art started in the early 90’s when he became fascinated with the skate decks and album covers for metal bands. He’s always been creative, and many years ago started playing with the first graphic design softwares just for fun. Not knowing how to draw pushed him to express himself with other tools. From here, he started combining his digital art and photography to create his own unique style, combining the real and digital worlds. Seveso has since created a name for himself as the pioneer of the ‘dispersion’ technique, a deconstructed rendering technique where he takes pictures of faces and bodies and blends them with vector patterns. He is drawn to people’s portraits, especially faces and expressions and enjoys, in a sense, giving people a new face. His process starts from black, and then adds vector objects to the image. “Basically, this face sort of creates itself”, he says. He is now a featured artist of Adobe Photoshop and is his unique style can be found on the cover image he created for Adobe Photoshop CS6.

Another mesmerising style by Seveso can be seen in his recently released collection,  ‘Heavy Metals’ - a series of underwater ink photographs, achieved by mixing ink with metallic powders which are then suspended in different fluids and photographed at high-speed.  

He believes that even if you’re not able to draw or paint, you’re still able to make something beautiful, creative and interesting. When asked why he chose this career, the answer is easy. “I do this because I love it. That’s all.”
