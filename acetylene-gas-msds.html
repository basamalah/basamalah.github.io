9. Acetylene gas, C2H2 is used for welding. At what temperature would 4 moles of acetylene, placed in a 10-liter container, produce a pressure of 1,260 mm Hg?


Answer:

T = 50.51 K

Explanation:

Convert 1260 mm Hg to atm.

P = 1260 mm Hg × (1 atm / 760 mm Hg)

P = 1.6579 atm

Use ideal gas law to solve for the temperature.

PV = nRT

T = PV / nR

T = (1.6579 atm)(10.00 L) / [(4.000 mol)(0.082057 L•atm/mol•K)]

T = 50.51 K

20. Acetylene gas, C2H2 is used for welding. At what temperature would 4 moles of acetylene, placed in a 10-liter container, produce a pressure of 1,260 mm Hg?


Given:

[tex]V = \text{10 L}[/tex]

[tex]P = \text{1260 mm Hg} × \frac{\text{1 atm}}{\text{760 mm Hg}} = \text{1.65789 atm}[/tex]

[tex]n = \text{4 mol}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]T[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]PV = nRT[/tex]

[tex]T = \frac{PV}{nR}[/tex]

[tex]T = \frac{\text{(1.65789 atm)(10 L)}}{(\text{4 mol})(0.0821 \: \frac{\text{L • atm}}{\text{mol • K}})}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{T = \text{50.5 K}}[/tex]

24. 1. An acetylene tank for an oxyacetylene welding torch provides 9340 L of acetylenegas, C2H2, at 0 °C and 1 atm. How many tanks of oxygen, each providing7.00 xx 103 L of Oz at 0 °C and 1 atm, will be required to burn the acetylene?2 C2H2 + 5 O2 -> 4CO2 + 2 H2O​


Answer:

number of O2 tanks = 3 tanks

Explanation:

Solve for the number of O2 tanks.

number of O2 tanks = 9340 L C2H2 × (1 mol C2H2 / 22.414 L) × (5 mol O2 / 2 mol C2H2) × (22.414 L O2 / 1 mol O2) × (1 O2 tank / 7.00 × 10³ L)

number of O2 tanks = 3 tanks

25. Stoichiometric calculations involving gas calculate the mass of o2 (g) at STP required for the complete combustion of 26.4L of acetylene (c2h2) at STP?​


Solution:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

2C₂H₂ + 5O₂ → 4CO₂ + 2H₂O

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of O₂.

molar mass = 16.00 g/mol × 2

molar mass = 32.00 g/mol

Step 3: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 2 mol C₂H₂ : 5 mol O₂

Step 4: Calculate the mass of O₂ required using the mole ratio.

Note: The volume of 1 mole of any ideal gas is 22.414 L at STP.

[tex]\text{mass of O₂ = 26.4 L C₂H₂} × \frac{\text{1 mol C₂H₂}}{\text{22.414 L C₂H₂}} × \frac{\text{5 mol O₂}}{\text{2 mol C₂H₂}} × \frac{\text{32.00 g O₂}}{\text{1 mol O₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of O₂ = 94.2 g}}[/tex]

26. A tank of acetylene holds 14.0 liters under a pressure of 200 psi. what is the volume of the gas at 1 atm​


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{200 psi}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{14.0 L}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{1 atm = 14.696 psi}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{14.0 L} × \frac{\text{200 psi}}{\text{14.696 psi}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{190 L}}[/tex]
