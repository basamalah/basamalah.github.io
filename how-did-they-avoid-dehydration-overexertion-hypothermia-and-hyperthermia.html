4. WHAT IS HYPERTHERMIA? • Hyperthermia is a result of the body being over heated. Causes can be physical activity, moderate to high ambient air temperature, onset of dehydration as a result of losing too much sweat without adequate water intake to replenish your system, solar or reflected radiation, improper clothing which does not allow for evaporation [cooling] of sweat, a low fitness level and or additional workload of carrying excessive weight.

RULES OF THUMBS TO AVOID HYPERTHERMIA: • Avoid activities in the heat of the day, higher than the low 90s Fahrenheit, if possible. • Wear cotton next to skin - it absorbs water and will help draw-down the body temp as it evaporates. • Wear a broad brimmed hat, to lessen the heating of your head. • Rehydrate by drinking lots of water. The brighter the yellow, the more alarming is the dehydration with subsequent over heating. • Take salt tablets as needed. The body sweats to induce temperature reduction and sweat includes salt in solution. The more a person sweats, the more salt their body loses. Taking small salt tabs with water replenishes the lost salt.

The condition can develop rapidly and is caused by cold, wet and/or windy weather which cools the body at a rate faster than the body can produce heat. 28. how to prevent dehydration, overexertion, hypothermia and hyperthermia?​


Answer:

PREVENTING DEHYDRATION

1. Pay attention to the possible symptoms of dehydration

There are physical signs of dehydration including headaches, fatigue, vomiting and a flush complexion.

2. Respond to thirst when the feeling strikes

The no 1 sign that your dehydrated is feeling thirsty. Keep a water nearby and take a swig whenever thirst strikes. Try to drink water regularly throughout the day so you never reach that level.

3. Check you urine color for signs that your dehydrated

A dark yellow color of your urine is a definite sign of dehydration.

4. Assess the inside of your mouth for dehydration symptoms

Check the mucus membrane but in other words, if it's starting to get dry, you know your running low on water.

5. Eat hydrating foods throughout the day

Foods such as melons, berries, grapes and lettuce.

6. Drink a glass of water when you wake up

PREVENTING OVEREXERTION

1. Stretching and warming up before heavy lifting or strenuous activity

2. Lifting with your legs bent and objects held close to your body

3. Avoid bending, reaching and twisting when lifting

PREVENTING HYPERTHERMIA

1. Avoid exercise in the middle of the day

2. Avoid strenuous activities

3. Wear lightweight clothes that are white. Dark colors absorbs light and attracts heat.

4. Use sunscreen

5. Drink plenty of water

PREVENTING HYPOTHERMIA

1. Dress in several layers of loose, warm clothing

2. Wear a hat or headband that covers your ears

3. Wear socks that wick moisture and provide insulation

4. When travelling in cold weather, carry emergency supplies

5. Keep moving to keep blood flowing and help stay warm
