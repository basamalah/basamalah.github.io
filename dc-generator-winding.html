20. what is the general principle of dc generator? ​


Answer:

DC Generator working principal is based on Faraday's Laws of electromagnetic induction.

Explanation:

According to Faraday’s laws of electromagnetic induction, whenever a conductor is placed in a varying magnetic field (OR a conductor is moved in a magnetic field), an emf (electromotive force) gets induced in the conductor. The magnitude of induced emf can be calculated from the emf equation of dc generator. If the conductor is provided with a closed path, the induced current will circulate within the path. In a DC generator, field coils produce an electromagnetic field and the armature conductors are rotated into the field. Thus, an electromagnetically induced emf is generated in the armature conductors. The direction of induced current is given by Fleming’s right hand rule.

25. what are the top uses of commutator and shaft in dc generator?​


What are the top uses of commutator and shaft in dc generator?

The AC is converted to pulsing DC via the commutator on the DC generator. The commutator ensures that the generator's current flows in just one direction. The brushes ride on the commutator and ensure that the generator and the load are properly connected electrically.

In DC and most AC motors, the commutator's purpose is to guarantee that the current flowing through the rotor windings is constantly in the same direction and that the right rotor coil is activated in respect to the field coils. By physically arranging the brushes on the commutator, the magnetic force of the field windings and the magnetic force of the rotor windings may be adjusted at an angle of displacement.
