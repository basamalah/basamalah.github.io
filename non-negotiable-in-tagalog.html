13. What is an example of a promissory note that is negotiable and why is it considered negotiable or what makes it negotiable? ​


Answer:

A negotiable instrument is a commercial document in writing. There are of three types, namely, bills of exchange, promissory notes and cheques. Section 13 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 2000 defines “negotiable instrument” as a promissory note, bill of exchange or cheque payable either to order or to bearer.

27. what is the relations of negotiating with hotels and other establishment with conference service contract? what is the effect of negotiation to the contract content?


Negotiating with hotels and other establishments for a conference service contract involves discussing the terms and conditions of the contract, including the services that will be provided, the cost of those services, and any other relevant details. The negotiation process can have a significant effect on the content of the contract, as it determines the final agreement between the parties involved. For example, if the negotiation results in a lower price for the conference services, this may be reflected in the contract as a discounted rate. Additionally, any additional services or amenities that are agreed upon during the negotiation process can be included in the contract. Ultimately, the negotiation process can help to ensure that the contract accurately reflects the needs and expectations of both parties, and can help to prevent misunderstandings or disputes down the road.
