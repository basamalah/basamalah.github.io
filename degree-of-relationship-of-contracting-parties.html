8. The contract between A and B provides: " That the term or period of this contract shall be as long as the party of the first part has need for the electric light posts of the party of the second part." Is the contract valid? Why?


Answer:

The contract is potentially invalid due to vagueness and uncertainty in its terms. The phrase "as long as the party of the first part has need for the electric light posts of the party of the second part" is not specific or definite enough to create a legally enforceable obligation. The contract fails to provide any objective standard or criteria for determining when the first party's need for the light posts has been fulfilled.

Courts generally require contracts to be clear and specific in their terms so that both parties can understand their respective obligations and duties. A contract that is too vague or ambiguous is not enforceable because it does not provide a clear indication of the parties' intentions or obligations.

Therefore, it is likely that a court would find this contract invalid because it lacks a clear and objective standard for determining when the contract term ends. If the parties wish to create a valid and enforceable contract, they should include specific terms and conditions that clearly define the parties' respective obligations and duties, including a specific duration or term for the contract
