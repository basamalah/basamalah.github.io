Answer:

Under RA 9344, the minimum age of criminal liability is 15 years old. This means that those within the age of 15 to 18 years old may be detained in youth centers and go through rehabilitation programs while those under 15 years old are exempted from criminal liability but must undergo intervention.


25. Explain The main principle of the Bill on Lowering the Age of Criminal Responsibility.​


Answer:

It has been proposed that lowering the minimum age of criminal responsibility will discourage adults from using children as vehicles for illegal activity, but this logic is flawed: there is a risk that lowering the minimum age of criminal responsibility will encourage them to target and exploit even younger children.

Explanation:

The Coalition for Street Children is deeply concerned about legislative efforts in the Philippines to lower the age of criminal responsibility. The current legal minimum age for a child to be held criminally responsible is 15. House Bill 8858 proposed lowering the minimum age to nine years old at first. It was later amended to lower the proposed minimum age to 12 years old, and the House of Representatives approved it on second reading. The bill only needs one final reading before it can be approved and signed into law.
