1. Instruction: Expound the quote below. Beware of little expenses. A small leak will sink a great ship. - Benjamin Franklin Activity: In at least five (5) sentences, expound the quote above.


Answer:

Benjamin Franklin's quote "Beware of little expenses. A small leak will sink a great ship" is a warning that small expenses, if left unchecked, can have a significant impact on one's financial stability. Just like a small leak in a ship can lead to its sinking, small and seemingly insignificant expenses can add up over time and lead to financial ruin.

The quote emphasizes the importance of being mindful of one's spending habits and keeping track of even the smallest expenses. It also highlights the need for individuals to prioritize their spending and focus on saving for important long-term goals.

To avoid falling into financial trouble, it is essential to keep track of all expenses, no matter how small they may seem. This requires creating a budget and sticking to it, as well as regularly reviewing one's spending habits to identify areas where cuts can be made.

In conclusion, Benjamin Franklin's quote serves as a reminder that financial stability requires vigilance and attention to detail when it comes to managing expenses, no matter how small they may seem.
