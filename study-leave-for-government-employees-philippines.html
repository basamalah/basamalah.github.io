Identify the classification of law violated by each of the following situation.1.muder2.breach of contract3.Grave misconduct of a government employee4.gross neglect of duty of a government employee5.assault6.tax evasion7.the employer not paying required overtime fee8.unethical action of a government employee9.not ensuring a safe workplace10.theft​


Explanation:

Murder- the unlawful premeditated killing of one human being by another.

Breach of contract- breach of contract occurs when one party to the agreement fails to fulfil an obligation or breaks the 'Terms and Conditions' as set out in that agreement.

Grave misconduct of a government employee -is defined as the transgression of some established and definite rule of action, more particularly, unlawful behavior or gross negligence by a public officer coupled with the elements of corruption, willful intent to violate the law or to disregard established rules.

Gross neglect of duty of government employee -There is gross neglect of duty when one's actions, even if not willfully or intentionally done to cause harm, are characterized by want of even slight care and a blatant indifference to the consequences of one's actions to other persons.

Assault-The definition of assault varies by jurisdiction, but is generally defined as intentionally putting another person in reasonable apprehension of an imminent harmful or offensive contact. Physical injury is not required.

Tax evasion- is an illegal activity in which a person or entity deliberately avoids paying a true tax liability. ... To willfully fail to pay taxes is a federal offense under the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) tax code.

The employer not paying required overtime fee- Under the Labor Code and as expounded by other laws, overtime pay must be paid to all employees, except: Government employees, which are governed by separate rules such as the Civil Service Law and other rules and regulations of the Civil Service Commission (CSC)

Unethical action of a government employee-These Rules shall cover all officials and employees in the government, elective and appointive, permanent

or temporary, whether in the career or non-career service, including military and police personnel, whether or not

they receive compensation, regardless of amount.

Not ensuring a safe workplace -As an employer, it is your legal responsibility to make your workplace a safe environment for your employees. If you neglect your legal responsibility, you may face high legal costs, hefty fines, and the possibility of a jail sentence.

Theft- is the taking of another person's property or services without that person's permission or consent with the intent to deprive the rightful owner of it.

21. As a future employer, how will you empower your employees to lead your management in quality?​


Answer:

Employee empowerment is defined as the ways in which organizations provide their employees with a certain degree of autonomy and control in their day-to-day activities. ... A key principle of employee empowerment is providing employees the means for making important decisions and helping ensure those decisions are correct.

Empowering employees means giving your team members permission to take action and make decisions within your organization. It also means there is trust and understanding in place to ensure these actions are in line with company goals.

Empowering employees is important for growing a sustainable business. While many companies may grow ground-up from the hard work and dedication of one or two entrepreneurs, true growth is the product of multiple people working together. “Multiplying” yourself (as opposed to a strict leader-follower mindset) multiplies your organization’s strength and capabilities.

Empowering employees is a culture. It takes an increase in trust, clear communication, and strategic delegation. To empower employees, consider the following # steps:

1. Deligate to develop

2. Set clear expectations

3.Give employees autonomy over assignments

4. Provide necessary resources

5. Give constructive feedback

6. Accept ideas and input

7. Communicate the vision of the organization

8. Recognize employees for hard work
