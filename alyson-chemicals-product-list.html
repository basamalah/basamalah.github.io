2. Four students in the class weighed themselves. Alyson was 15 kilograms lighter than Veniz. Elijah was twice as heavy as Alyson and Thaddy was seven kilograms heavier than Elijah. If Thaddy weighed 71 kilograms what was the weight of Veniz?


Answer:

47 kg

Step-by-step explanation:

Let A be Allison's weight

Let V be Veniz's

Let T be Thady's

Let E be Elija's

Making equations

Alyson was 15 kilograms lighter than Veniz:

A=V-15 (eqn 1)

Elijah was twice as heavy as Alyson:

E=2A (eqn 2)

Thaddy was seven kilograms heavier than Elijah

T=E+7 (eqn 3)

Given: T=71, find V

Using eqn 3:

E=T-7

E=71-7

E=64

Using eqn 2:

E=2A

A=E/2

A=64/2

A=32

Using eqn1:

A=V-15

V=A+15

V=32+15

V=47

Therefore, Veniz is 47 kg

4. Alyson deposits $500 in the bank for 12 years. The bank offers her a 4% interest rate compounded monthly. How much money will be in her account at the end of the 12 years? (Remember to round your answer to the nearest cent.)​


ANSWER:

$807.39

SOLUTION:

Compound Interest Formula:

[tex]A=P\left(1+\frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}[/tex]

where:

A = final amountP = principal amountr = interest rate (in decimal form)n = number of times interest is applied per yeart = time (in years)

Given:

P = $500r = 4% = 0.04n = 12 (monthly)t = 12 years

Solving:

[tex]A=P\left(1+\frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}[/tex]

[tex]A=500\left(1+\dfrac{0.04}{12}\right)^{12 \cdot 12}[/tex]

[tex]A=500\left(1.00333333...\right)^{144}[/tex]

[tex]A=500\left(1.61478492...\right)[/tex]

[tex]A=807.392461...[/tex]

[tex] \therefore[/tex] Alyson will have $807.39 (nearest cent) in her account at the end of 12 years.
