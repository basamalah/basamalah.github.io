10. Which of the following statement is false about the different situation where people can pick up germs?


Answer:

Nose, mouth, or eyes to others' hands:

Via sneezing, coughing, or rubbing the eyes, germs can spread to the hands and then can be passed to other family members or friends. It can help avoid such diseases as the common cold or eye infections by simply washing your hands.

Hands to food:

Usually, germs are spread to food by a contaminated food preparer who did not wash his or her hands after using the bathroom. Then the germs are transmitted to those who consume the food. This can be easily avoided by washing your hands after using the bathroom and before cooking food.

Food to hands:

During the preparation of a meal, germs are transferred from raw foods, such as chicken, to hands. The germs on the hands are then moved, such as salad, to other uncooked foods. The initial germs are destroyed by cooking the raw food, but the salad remains contaminated.

14. what is germ mutation​


Answer:

A germline mutation, or germinal mutation, is any detectable variation within germ cells. Mutations in these cells are the only mutations that can be passed on to offspring, when either a mutated sperm or oocyte come together to form a zygote.
