Explanation:

Conifers or conifers are a group of open seed plants that have conifers as seed-bearing organs. This group is now placed as a separate division after it is known that the cladistic division of Gymnosperms and Angiosperms is polyphyletic. There are approximately 550 species of members, in the form of shrubs, shrubs or trees. Most of its members have a conical crown and have leaves that are elongated (lanceolate) or needle-shaped (thus they are also known as needle-leaved plants). This kind of leaf shape is considered as an adaptation to the habitat of almost all of its members which are often found in areas with relatively cool temperatures, such as around the poles (circumpolar) or in the highlands.

Conifers are mostly spread in areas that have a temperate climate. The shape of the narrow conifer leaves is very adaptive to the low temperatures that characterize the area. In the tropics only a few species grow in nature and naturally like cool mountainous areas. In Indonesia there are several representatives, such as tusam (Pinus merkusii), a number of Araucariaceae such as damar (Agathis alba) and male resin (Araucaria cunninghamii), as well as several Podocarpus.

One of the types of plants that spread and spread at the end of the Carboniferous Age were conifers. It is a seed plant and is so named because it has a conical or cushion shape and currently has more than 550 species. Most of all the species included in this group are trees or shrubs. They dominate many areas of the highlands, forming forests of evergreen, needle-shaped trees. Among the most famous members of the family are the pines, firs, cedars and redwoods.
