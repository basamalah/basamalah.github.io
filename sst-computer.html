10. The depletion of ozone (03) in the stratosphere has been a matter of greatconcern among scientists in recent years. It is believed that ozone can reactwith nitric oxide (NO) that is discharged from the high altitude jet plane, theSST. The reaction is,O3 + NO + O2 + NO2.If 0.740 g of Og reacts with 0.670 g of NO, how many grams of NO 2 will beproduced? Which compound is the limiting reagente Calculate the numberof moles of the excess reagent remaining at the end of the reaction.​


Answer:

0.709 g

O3

0.0069 mol

Explanation:

Molecular weights

N → 14.01 g/mol

O → 16.00 g/mol

MW O3 = 3(16.00) = 48.00 g/mol

MW NO = 14.01 + 16.00 = 30.01 g/mol

MW NO2 = 14.01 + 2(16.00) = 46.01 g/mol

We need to get the mass of NO2 produced using the two given masses of reactants to determine the limiting reagent:

Using 0.740 g of O3

mass NO2 = 0.740 g O3 × (1 mol O3 / 48.00 g O3) × (1 mol NO2 / 1 mol O3) × (46.01 g NO2 / 1 mol NO2)

mass NO2 = 0.709 g NO2

Using 0.670 g of NO

mass NO2 = 0.670 g NO × (1 mol NO / 30.01 g NO) × (1 mol NO2 / 1 mol NO) × (46.01 g NO2 / 1 mol NO2)

mass NO2 = 1.03 g NO2

Since O3 produces less amount of NO2 than NO, O3 is the limiting reagent.

Since O3 produces less amount of NO2 than NO, O3 is the limiting reagent.Since O3 is the limiting reagent, 0.709 g NO2 will be produced.

To solve for the moles of excess reagent remaining, we will start at the mass of NO2 produced.

n NO = 0.709 g NO2 × (1 mol NO2 / 46.01 g NO2) × (1 mol NO / 1 mol NO2)

n NO = 0.0154 mol

Then, get the number of moles of 0.670 g NO

n NO = 0.670 g NO × (1 mol NO / 30.01 g NO)

n NO = 0.0223 mol

Subtracting the number of moles obtained

n of excess reagent remaining = 0.0223 - 0.0154

n of excess reagent remaining = 0.0069 mol
