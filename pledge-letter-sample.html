3. One of the distinctions between pledge and mortgage is that pledge:


One of the distinctions between pledge and mortgage is that pledge requires actual delivery of the goods given as collateral to the creditor or a third person by mutual agreement.

Explanation

According to Article 2093, there is a statement "In addition to the requirements specified in Article 2085, it is necessary, in order to form a pledge contract, that the pledged goods be placed in the possession of the creditor, or a third person by mutual agreement. (1863)" Thus, in the formation of a pledge contract, the goods are delivered to the creditor or a third person by mutual agreement.

Article 2085 contains an important statement regarding pawn and mortgage contracts. The article reads: "(1) That they are formed to secure the fulfillment of a primary obligation; (2) That the pledgee or mortgagor is the absolute owner of the thing pledged or mortgaged; (3) That the persons forming the pledge or mortgage are at liberty to dispose of their property, and if they are not, that they are legally authorized for that purpose." Thus, a pledge contract is formed with the requirement of actual delivery of the goods given as collateral to the creditor or a third person by mutual agreement.
