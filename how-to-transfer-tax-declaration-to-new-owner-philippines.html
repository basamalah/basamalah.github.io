28. ipaliwanag ang tax declaration of real property


Answer:

Declaration of Real Property ( Tax Declaration ) is a property record, which is a traditional assessment document maintained by the provincial, city or municipal assessors, showing, among others the market and assessed values of the property as the basis for the collection of real property tax.

Other uses of the Declaration of Real Property is as follows :

1) The zonal value of the property is based on the Barangay where the property is located.

2) The tax declaration keeps track of prior ownership of the property and is useful for “tracebacks” ( when research is being done on history of a property )

3) Assessment for the value of the improvement is also used by the the Bureau of Internal Revenue when figuring out the zonal value of the property.

4) The Declaration of Real Property and Transfer Certificate of Title or Condominium Certificated of Title are two important documents that show proof of ownership.

30. what are the current tax policies in the philippines and how do they impact the economy and society?​


Answer:

The current tax policies in the Philippines have been a topic of discussion for quite some time now. The country's tax system is composed of national and local taxes, which are collected by the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) and the Bureau of Customs (BOC). The government has implemented several tax reforms in recent years, including the Tax Reform for Acceleration and Inclusion (TRAIN) law, which aims to generate more revenue for the government and provide relief for low-income earners.

The impact of these tax policies on the economy and society has been significant. The TRAIN law has increased the take-home pay of low-income earners, but it has also resulted in higher prices for basic goods and services. The government has also implemented measures to combat tax evasion, which has improved revenue collection and helped fund infrastructure projects and social services.

Despite these efforts, there are still challenges that need to be addressed. The tax system remains complex and difficult to understand, which makes compliance difficult for many businesses and individuals. There is also a need to improve the efficiency and transparency of tax collection and administration to ensure that the government is able to collect the revenue it needs to fund essential services and programs.

The Philippines has recently ratified the final version of the proposed Corporate Recovery and Tax Incentives for Enterprises Act (CREATE), which reduces the income tax rate to 25% from 30% for corporations with a net taxable income of up to PHP 5 million ($99,000) and 20% for those with over PHP 5 million ($99,000). The new law also provides a five-year income tax holiday for new enterprises and a reduced rate of 15% for those located in less developed areas. The government hopes that these measures will help businesses recover from the economic impact of COVID-19.
