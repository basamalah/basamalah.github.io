27. 3Year over, company XYZ is consistently filling the same position with external hires due to voluntary turnover. Though it is a critical sales role, a proper evaluation of underlying issues and a plan to address the attrition issue has not been established. What, specifically, can be done in this situation to identify factors leading to high turnover? What staffing strategies could be best suited to fit the above need? Choose one and explain how this could develop an improvement to the scenario.


Answer:

To identify the factors leading to high turnover, the company could conduct an exit interview with departing employees to understand their reasons for leaving. The company could also gather feedback from current employees about their experiences and potential areas of improvement. This information can be analyzed to identify common patterns or themes that may be contributing to the turnover.

One staffing strategy that could be effective in addressing the attrition issue is to focus on employee retention by implementing a comprehensive employee engagement program. This program could include regular check-ins with employees to understand their needs, providing opportunities for professional development and growth, offering competitive compensation and benefits packages, and fostering a positive work culture.

Additionally, the company could consider implementing a referral program to incentivize current employees to refer potential candidates for the sales role. This strategy could help to identify high-quality candidates who are more likely to stay with the company long-term.

Overall, by identifying the underlying factors leading to high turnover and implementing effective staffing strategies, the company can improve its retention of sales staff and reduce its reliance on external hires.
