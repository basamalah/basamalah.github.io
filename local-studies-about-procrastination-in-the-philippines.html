1. Background of the study about academic procrastination


The background of the study in a thesis should include the following discussion:

A brief review of the research topic Current information or relevant literature that talks about the research topic Relevant history about the research topic

Its basic aim is to lead the readers to be knowledgeable of the purpose of the research topic, and why and how the topic arises for conducting the research.

If your topic is about academic procrastination, make a preliminary research on this.  Read on the current and relevant issues and literature that tackle academic procrastination.  Use the access of books and journals in the library.  Through the information that you have gathered, create a solid research question as the base of your background.  You may use the format provided above.

Here are some studies about academic procrastination that you may read in formulating your background of the study:

1. Reasons of Academic Procrastination: Self-Regulation, Academic Self-Efficacy, Life Satisfaction and Demographics Variables by Mehmet Kandemir, 2014

“Students cannot show their real performances in learning processes and they fail because of procrastination.”

2. Academic Procrastination in College Students: The Role of Self-Reported Executive Function by Laura Rabin, et al., 2010

“Procrastination is increasingly viewed as involving failures in self-regulation and volition, processes commonly regarded as executive functions.”

19. 2 Literature about Procrastination for students FOR RESEARCH PO NAMIN PLZZ GIVE ME ANSWER​


Answer:

Procrastination is a widespread phenomenon in academic settings. It has been studied from many different theoretical angles, and a variety of causes and consequences have been suggested. Recent studied support the notion that academic procrastination can be seen from a setuational perspective and as a failure in learning self-regulation. It suggests that interventions should address situational as well as deficits in self-regulation to help students overcome their procrastinating tendecies. The present review examined the recent literature on causes and consequences of academic procrastination and the limited number of studies of academic interventions for academic procrastination. Findings of this review strengthen the need to further study the topic of academic interventions for academic procrastination and to develop effective interventions.

29. Example of scope and delimitation about procrastination


The scope of the research refers to the coverage of the study including its sample size, time and geographic.  On the other hand, the delimitation is definite and broader description of the scope.

Here is an example of a scope and delimitation about procrastination:

The study aims to understand why procrastination is common among students, and to identify the causes and effects of procrastination among them.  Therefore, the research will include 50 students from the university, 50 from high school, and another 50 from grade school.  There will be no gender preference to be observed, and a random sampling technique will be used as a research design.  An open interview questionnaire which comprises of 40 questions about procrastination will be administered.  The interview will take place inside their respective schools at noon breaks.  There will be two interviewers, where one will ask the questions and the other will record the conversation with the consent of the participants.
