10. What is chemistry? What are the branches of chemistry? ​


Answer:

Fundamentally, chemistry is the study of matter and change. The way that chemists study matter and change and the types of systems that are studied varies dramatically. Traditionally, chemistry has been broken into five main subdisciplines: Organic, Analytical, Physical, Inorganic and Biochemistry

What is chemistry?

1. the field of science concerned with the identification of the constituents of which matter is comprised; the study of their characteristics and the processes by which they interact, combine, and change; and the application of these processes to the formation of new substances

a substance's or body's chemical makeup and qualitieschemistries (plural) "the soil chemistry"

2. two people's complicated emotional or psychological relationship

"Intense sexual chemistry began their affair"

What are the branches of chemistry?

1. Biochemistry - The study of chemical processes that occur within living organisms is known as biochemistry. Proteins, nucleic acids, and other important compounds are examples.

2. Thermodynamics - The study of the underlying physical principles that control the behavior of atoms, molecules, and other chemical systems is known as physical chemistry.

3. Organic chemistry - The study of carbon and its compounds is known as organic chemistry. It is the study of the chemistry of life and the reactions that occur in living things.

4. Inorganic chemistry - Inorganic chemistry is the study of chemicals that are not covered by organic chemistry. It is the investigation of inorganic substances.
