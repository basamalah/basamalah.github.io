27. what role did Romeo play in the play, Romeo and Juliet?


Answer:

Romeo is the male lead in the play and acts as the play's hero. He is central to the plot through his relationship with Juliet, the heroine, and many of Romeo's choices govern the action in the play. For example, his choice to go to the Capulet feast against his better judgement leads to him meeting Juliet.

1. Describe Romeo

Romeo is handsome, intelligent, and sensitive. Though impulsive and immature, his idealism and passion make him an extremely likable character. He lives in the middle of a violent feud between his family and the Capulets, but he is not at all interested in violence.

2.What is the biggest obstacle that confronted Romeo's love for Juliet?

The family feud between the Capulets and the Montagues is the chief obstacle to Romeo and Juliet's love. The main obstacle that Romeo and Juliet face is the feud that divides their two families. On the broadest level, the feud makes it impossible for them to go to their families and request to marry each other, as they know it would never be allowed. In Romeo and Juliet, Romeo and Juliet's marriage could be considered unwise due to the conflict between their families, their young ages, and their unwillingness to openly act against their families.
