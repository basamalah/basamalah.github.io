1. (Listen to Cecile Licad performing Frédéric Chopin's Etude op. 10 no. 12) Describe briefly the manner in which the pianist performed (movements, appearance, gestures, etc.)​


Answer: In most Piano pieces the right hand seems to do more work in comparison to the left hand. But in this etude, the left hand is constantly playing arpeggios throughout the piece while the right hand is playing big chords and very sorrow melancholic melody. Her left hand is just shifting and dragging it in incredible speed through many notes from left to right vice versa. It is a very sorrowful and moving piece, Chopin himself dedicated it to his beloved hometown as of knowing that it was conquered by Russians thus giving its name the 'Revolutionary'. Finally, In the last bar of the Etude it didn't even resolve to its tonic and it gives of quite an ominous and impulsive mood. But in conclusion, her performance was amazing.
