18. Use the combined gas law to solve the following problem. If P1 = 5 atm, V, = 1 L, T, = 300 K, P2 = 10 atm and T2 = 600 K, what is the volume V2? 0 V2 = 10.02 O V2 = 2.0L O V2 = 1.0L O V2 = 0.5 L


COMBINED GAS LAW

[tex]\normalsize{\blue{\overline{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad}}}[/tex]

[tex]\large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:}[/tex]

We know that the combined gas law is:

[tex]\boxed{\sf{\dfrac{P_{1} V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \dfrac{P_{2} V_{2}}{T_{2}} }}[/tex]

Where:

P₁ = initial pressure.V₁ = initial volume.T₁ = initial temperature.P₂ = final pressure.V₂ = final volume.T₂ = final temperature.

Temperature must be in Kelvin. To convert Celsius to Kelvin, add 273.15.

So our givens are:

P₁ = 5 atmV₁ = 1 LT₁ = 300 KP₂ = 10 atmT₂ = 600 K

Rearrange the equation to isolate V₂:

[tex]\sf{\implies V_{2} = \dfrac{P_{1} V_{1} T_{2}}{P_{2} T_{1}} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\implies V_{2} = \dfrac{5 \times 1 \times 600}{10 \times 300} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\implies V_{2} = \dfrac{5 \times 600}{10 \times 300} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\implies V_{2} = \dfrac{3,000}{3,000} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\implies V_{2} = 1.0L}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of V₂ is 1.0 L.
