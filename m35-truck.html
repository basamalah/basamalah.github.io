7. PROBLEM SOLVING1. Consider a 28,000 kg truck travelling at 8 m/s towards another stationary truck. After the two truck collide, they couple together and move along at 5 m/s. What is the mass of the second truck?2. A Blue Truck and Green Truck are identical truck that are on a flat, horizontal smooth surface. The blue truck is motionless, while the green truck is moving 3.5m/s to the left and collides to blue truck. the toy trucks have a mass of 25g. After the collision, the blue truck is moving at 3m/s to the left. What is the final velocity of the green truck?================================NONSENSE/INCOMPLETE = REPORTCORRECT ANSWER = BRAINLEIST================================​


1.) Given:

m1 = 28,000 kgs.

v1 = 8 m/s

v2 = 0 m/s (stationary)

Vt = 5 m/s

Solution:

[tex] (mv)_{1} + (mv)_{2} = (mv)_{t} [/tex]

[tex](28000 \times 8) + ( m_{2} \times 0) = (28000 + m_{2})(5) [/tex]

[tex]224000 + 0 = 140000 + 5 m_{2}[/tex]

[tex] m_{2} = \frac{224000 - 140000}{5} [/tex]

[tex] m_{2} = 16800 \: kgs.[/tex]

2.) Given:

m1 & m2 = 25 kgs.

v1 = 0 m/s (motionless)

v2 = 3.5 m/s (to the left)

vt1 = 3 m/s (to the left)

Solution:

[tex] (mv)_{1} + (mv)_{2} = (mv)_{t1} + (mv)_{t2}[/tex]

[tex](25 \times 0) + (25)( - 3.5) = (25)( - 3) + (25) (v)_{t2}[/tex]

[tex]0 - 87.5 = - 75 + 25 v_{t2}[/tex]

[tex] v_{t2} = \frac{ - 87.5 + 75}{25} [/tex]

[tex] v_{t2} = - 0.5 \: \frac{m}{s} \: [/tex]

or

[tex] v_{t2} = 0.5 \: \frac{m}{s} (to \: the \: left)[/tex]

9. Problem #1 Benjamin loves to play his toy truck. This toy truck is 15 kg made up of steel. He puts sand in his truck and deliver it at their backyard. In pulling the truck he exert 370 N. What would be the truck's acceleration then?


Acceleration

[tex]____________________________________[/tex]

[tex]\huge\blue{\underline{\green{\mathscr{Question}}}}[/tex]

Benjamin loves to play his toy truck. This toy truck is 15 kg made up of steel. He puts sand in his truck and deliver it at their backyard. In pulling the truck he exert 370 N. What would be the truck's acceleration then?

[tex]\huge\color{blue}{\underline{\overline\color{hotpink}{\rm{ Solution}}}}[/tex]

Given data:

Mass (m) - 15 kgForce (f) - 370 N

Required:

Acceleration (a) - m/s²

Formula:

[tex]\rm{Acceleration = Force ÷ Mass}[/tex]

Where;

[tex]\rm{Acceleration = m/s^2}[/tex]

[tex]\rm{Force = Newtons}[/tex]

[tex]\rm{Mass = kilograms}[/tex]

Solve:

[tex]\rm{A = f ÷ m}[/tex]

[tex]\rm{A = 370 \: N ÷ 15 \: kg}[/tex]

[tex]\rm{A = \red{\boxed{\bold{24.66 \: m/s^2}}}} \: \longleftarrow[/tex]

[tex]\huge\star{\underline{\mathtt{\red{A}\pink{N}\green{S}\blue{W}\purple{E}\orange{R}}}}[/tex]

Hence, the acceleration of the truck is 24.66 m/s²

24. a truck is travelling with a velocity of 10.5 m/s if the trucks mass is 3458 kg,what will be the kinetic energy possessed by the truck​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The kinetic energy of a moving object is given by

[tex]\boxed{KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}}[/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = speed

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the kinetic energy

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}mv^{2}[/tex]

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}(\text{3,458 kg})(\text{10.5 m/s})^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{KE = \text{190,622.25 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the kinetic energy of the truck is 190,622.25 J.
