Answer:

Explanation:

The youngest volcano, Hibok-Hibok (also known as Catarman), has been active during historical time and lies at the northern-west end of the island. Major eruptions during 1871-75 and 1948-53 formed flank lava domes at Hibok-Hibok and produced pyroclastic flows that devastated coastal villages.
