4. Affirmative Positive Death Penalty ​


Answer:

The affirmative positive argument for the death penalty is that it serves as a deterrent to crime. Supporters of the death penalty argue that the threat of execution can prevent potential criminals from committing serious crimes such as murder. Additionally, they argue that the death penalty is a just punishment for heinous crimes such as murder, providing closure for victims' families and society as a whole.

Another argument is that it is a way of protecting society by removing dangerous criminals from circulation, as they will no longer be able to commit crimes.

However, It should be noted that there are multiple perspectives and opinions on the death penalty and it remains a controversial issue with valid arguments on both sides.

7. pa help position papertopic : death penalty​


Death penalty, also known as capital punishment, is the practice of executing individuals convicted of certain crimes, usually murder or treason. The death penalty has been a controversial topic for many years, and opinions on its use vary greatly.

In this position paper, I argue that death penalty should be abolished.

Firstly, the death penalty is not an effective deterrent for crime. Studies have shown that the threat of the death penalty does not significantly reduce the rate of violent crime. Furthermore, the possibility of executing an innocent person is a real concern. Since the reinstatement of the death penalty in the United States in 1976, over 160 people have been released from death row with evidence of their innocence.

Secondly, the death penalty is a costly and inefficient system. The appeals process for death penalty cases is lengthy and expensive, and it often takes many years for a death sentence to be carried out. Moreover, the death penalty disproportionately affects marginalized and minority communities, and it is often applied in a discriminatory manner.

Lastly, the death penalty is inhumane and goes against the values of a civilized society. It is a cruel and inhumane punishment that violates the right to life and the prohibition of cruel, inhuman, and degrading treatment or punishment. Furthermore, it does not allow for the possibility of rehabilitation or redemption.

In summation, the death penalty should be abolished. It does not effectively deter crime, it is costly and inefficient, and it is inhumane and goes against the values of a civilized society. Instead of relying on the death penalty, we should focus on implementing effective and humane alternatives such as life imprisonment without parole and restorative justice programs.

Both rights are protected under the Universal Declaration of Human Rights in 1948. 24. What is the position of the United Nations about the issue of death penalty as policy of sovereign countries?​


Answer:

The United Nations system as a whole, including UNODC, opposes the use of the death penalty in all circumstances (UNODC, 2010; UNODC, 2012; UNODC, 2016). The UN recognizes that because it is irreversible, the death penalty is opposed even when backed by legal process.

The plenary session of the UN General Assembly (UNGA) today adopted a resolution on a moratorium on executions, with a view to fully abolishing the death penalty, after 123 states voted in favour.
