14. A tube of sweets contains redsweets, 7 blue sweets, 8 greensweets, and 5 orange sweets. If asweet is taken at random fromthe tube, what is the probabilitythat it is a. redb. greenc. orange d. not bluee. not red​


Answer:

The probability of getting a certain color of sweet from the tube can be calculated by dividing the number of sweets of that color by the total number of sweets in the tube.

a. The probability of getting a red sweet is 4/24 or 1/6.

b. The probability of getting a green sweet is 8/24 or 1/3.

c. The probability of getting an orange sweet is 5/24.

d. The probability of not getting a blue sweet is the same as the probability of getting a sweet that is not blue. This can be calculated by adding the number of sweets that are not blue (red, green, and orange) and dividing by the total number of sweets in the tube. Therefore, the probability of not getting a blue sweet is (4 + 8 + 5)/24 or 17/24.

e. The probability of not getting a red sweet is the same as the probability of getting a sweet that is not red. This can be calculated by adding the number of sweets that are not red (blue, green, and orange) and dividing by the total number of sweets in the tube. Therefore, the probability of not getting a red sweet is (7 + 8 + 5)/24 or 20/24.
