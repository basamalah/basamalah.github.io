What words were used in the poems that suggest sounds? 2. How do those words contribute to the effect of the poems? 3. Why are sound devices important in a poem? ENGLISH 5​


1. The words "cheered", "cleared", "dropped", "loud bassoon" suggest sounds in the poem.

2. These words contribute to the effect of the poem by creating a lively and bustling atmosphere as the ship sets off on its journey. The sound of the bassoon also adds to the festive and celebratory mood.

3. Sound devices such as rhyme, rhythm, alliteration, and onomatopoeia are important in a poem because they create a musical quality that enhances the meaning and emotional impact of the words. They also help to make the poem more memorable and enjoyable to read or recite.
