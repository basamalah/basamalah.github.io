6. Which must heparin bind to in Oder to exert its anticoagulant effect? A. Gp iib/iia receptor B. Thrombin C. Anti-thrombin iii D. Von Willebrand factor


Answer:

C.antithrombin III.

Explanation:

Heparin exerts its anticoagulant effect by binding to and activating the enzyme inhibitor protein, antithrombin III (ATIII). The binding of heparin to ATIII increases the ability of ATIII to inhibit clotting factors, including thrombin and factor Xa. Therefore, the correct answer is C, antithrombin III.

16. Which of the following is the preferred anticoagulant for preparing blood smears for diagnosing malaria?


Which of the following is the preferred anticoagulant for preparing blood smears for diagnosing malaria?

A. EDTA

B. Heparin

C. Sodium citrate

D. Sodium fluoride

Answer: A. EDTA

Since the early 1950s, Ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid (EDTA) has been used as an anticoagulant, and it offers several advantages over other anticoagulants. By eliminating or chelating calcium from the circulation, it prevents clotting. Anticoagulants are commonly used to keep blood from clotting during diagnostic tests. The anticoagulant activity of EDTA is based on the chelation of free Ca2+ ions, which inhibits thrombocyte aggregation and numerous hemostatic cascade processes
