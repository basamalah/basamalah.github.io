17. what is negotiating rate​


Answer:

Negotiating rate is a term used in the finance and banking industries to refer to the interest rate that a borrower and a lender agree upon in a loan or credit agreement after negotiating and considering various factors, such as the borrower's creditworthiness, the loan amount, and the loan term. Negotiating rate is often different from the initial or advertised rate because it takes into account the borrower's specific financial situation and the lender's risk assessment. A borrower who has a good credit score and a stable income may be able to negotiate a lower interest rate than someone with a lower credit score or less income. The negotiating rate is an important component of a loan or credit agreement because it determines the total cost of borrowing for the borrower, including the interest charges, fees, and other costs.

26. what is the relations of negotiating with hotels and other establishment with conference service contract? what is the effect of negotiation to the contract content?


Negotiating with hotels and other establishments for a conference service contract involves discussing the terms and conditions of the contract, including the services that will be provided, the cost of those services, and any other relevant details. The negotiation process can have a significant effect on the content of the contract, as it determines the final agreement between the parties involved. For example, if the negotiation results in a lower price for the conference services, this may be reflected in the contract as a discounted rate. Additionally, any additional services or amenities that are agreed upon during the negotiation process can be included in the contract. Ultimately, the negotiation process can help to ensure that the contract accurately reflects the needs and expectations of both parties, and can help to prevent misunderstandings or disputes down the road.
