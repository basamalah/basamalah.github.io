10. is quasi-contract an implied contract?


Answer:

Also called a contract implied in law or a constructive contract, a quasi contract may be presumed by a court in the absence of a true contract, but not where a contract—either express or implied in fact—covering the same subject matter already exists.

Maria is a recent college graduate who has been offered a job at a marketing firm. After her successful interview, the HR representative sends her an employment contract to review and sign. The contract outlines the terms and conditions of her employment, including her salary, job responsibilities, benefits, and termination procedures.

Maria carefully reads through the contract and notices a few clauses that she has questions about. She contacts the HR representative and they have a conversation about the clauses, clarifying any misunderstandings. Maria is satisfied with the responses and decides to sign the contract.

After signing the contract, Maria starts her job and begins to familiarize herself with the company's policies and procedures. She quickly discovers that one of the policies in the employee handbook conflicts with a clause in her employment contract. Maria brings this to the attention of her supervisor, who investigates and realizes that the employee handbook needs to be updated to reflect the terms of the employment contract.

Overall, the employment contract provides clarity and protection for both Maria and the marketing firm, ensuring that both parties understand their obligations and rights.
