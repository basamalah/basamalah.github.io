Answer:

Kuwentong Milagro ni Apung Bastian

Pintakasi ng Kabisera ng Tarlak

Noong panahon ng simula ng Tarlak bilang isang independiyenteng bayan ng Pampanga, isang kwentong milagro ni Apung Bastian (San Sebastian) ang umikot sa taumbayan. Isang grupo ng mga tulisan ang dumating sa bayan na may masamang balakin. Sa baybayin ng ilog, nakakita sila ng isang batang naglalaro. Tinanong ng pinuno ang bata kung saan sila maaaring dumaan papunta sa bayan. Tinugon sila ng bata na ang kanyang pinanggalingan lamang ang kanyang alam na daan. Gayunpaman, tumuloy pa din ang mga tulisan ngunit laking gulat nila nang ang pinuno, sakay ng kanyang kabayo, ay nilamon ng kumunoy. Nang mailigtas ang sarili ay hinanap niya ang bata upang sitahin. Ngunit wala na sa kinaroroonan ang bata. Sa lawak ng lupain ay imposibleng mawala na lamang siya na parang bula. Isa sa kanyang tauhan ang nagsabi na maaring ang bata ay si Apung Bastian, ang tagapagtanggol ng bayan ng Tarlak. Sa takot ng mga ito, nagpasya ang mga tulisan na huwag nang magpatuloy sa kanilang masamang balak.

7. trinidad,san antonio,concepcion,elena ships of magellan?


Answer:

The ships of Magellan's expedition were the Trinidad, San Antonio, Concepcion, Victoria, and Santiago

Explanation:

Magellan's expedition was a historic voyage that aimed to find a new trade route to the Spice Islands in the East Indies. The expedition consisted of five ships, namely the Trinidad, San Antonio, Concepcion, Victoria, and Santiago. The Trinidad was the flagship of the expedition, and it was the largest and most heavily armed ship. It was commanded by Magellan himself and carried most of the crew and supplies. The San Antonio was the second-largest ship and was commanded by Juan de Cartagena. The Concepcion was a smaller ship commanded by Gaspar de Quesada, and it was used to transport supplies and provisions.

The Victoria was a small caravel commanded by Luis Mendoza, and it was used for scouting and exploration. The Santiago was also a small caravel commanded by Juan Serrano, and it was used for reconnaissance and mapping.

During the expedition, the ships faced many challenges, including storms, mutinies, and hostile encounters with indigenous peoples. Despite these difficulties, the expedition managed to reach the Spice Islands and complete the first circumnavigation of the globe, with only the Victoria returning to Spain with 18 survivors.

Answer:

Culion Church used to be a fortress. The first Agustinian missionaries arrived in the island in 1622 to establish a parish but the repeated moro raids pushed them to build a fortress as defense in 1740. Almost two centuries later, Culion was declared a leper colony and became home to Filipinos afflicted with the illness. In 1933, the Jesuits reconstructed the fort and transformed it into a place of worship, now known as La Inmaculada Concepcion Church.

28. Who is Marcelo De Gracia Concepcion?​


Answer:

M. de Gracia Concepcion was listed on the staff of the Three Stars as an editor in 1931. He was the first Filipino poet to have his collected poems published as a book in the U.S. Published in 1925 by G.P. Putnam's Sons, the book was entitled Azucena, named after a lily found in the Philippines.
