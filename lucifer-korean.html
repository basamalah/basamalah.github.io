Why did Lucifer rebel against God?

I believe the myth goes something like this:

There was once an all powerful, all knowing being called God. He decided to make other beings and one of them in particular, named Lucifer, he made more beautiful and powerful than the rest. Then he gave Lucifer free will so that Lucifer would do EXACTLY as God commanded or be punished (don’t ask, it didn’t work on humans either but he keeps going with it).

So Lucifer, who intimately and personally knew this all powerful and all knowing God, decided to rebel against God to…overthrow him? I guess? The all powerful and all knowing God was surprised and angry when Lucifer rebelled even though he already knew everything that would happen and he himself had meticulously created Lucifer’s personality.

Lucifer lost because God was all powerful and all knowing (notwithstanding the fact that he didn’t see Lucifer’s rebellion coming). So as punishment for this action that God should’ve known would happen when he created Lucifer, he cast him down to a place of darkness and suffering.

BUT somehow Lucifer managed to escape this all powerful and all knowing God’s imprisonment and decided to continue his rebellion against a being that he knew was invincible because…I have no idea. Anyway, he goes to this new place called Earth where he teams up with God to prove that humans don’t really love God. Take this guy Job as an example.

This is really the most insane part of the story. The all knowing and all powerful God, who knows what everyone in existence is thinking and feeling in real time, takes a bet with Lucifer that Job doesn’t really love him. Is it Lucifer with the pride problem? I digress. So God, who already knows the answer, allows Lucifer to subject Job to repeated and horrific torture, taking away everything Job has in his life.

Job thanks God for this.

God is satisfied that he has rubbed in Lucifer’s face how much Job loves him by pointing out that he can hurt Job as much as he wants (no, really, please do) and Job will still love him. Satisfied smirk.

Lovely.

Let’s fast forward a few thousand years though. The all knowing God realizes that humanity has become terrible and that his old rules aren’t working (not for the first time, mind you, just ask Noah). Why didn’t he know that when he set the earlier rules? Or when he designed every single thing about every single person created? God is mysterious. Or maybe despite being all knowing, there are things he doesn’t know right away. But I digress again.

Humanity is now terrible. So God decides to create himself as his own son and go to Earth, but only a really tiny portion of Earth with a small population so that he can preach to 0.00001% of humanity. Lucifer pops back up again and tries to tempt God, who is in human form as his own son, to forsake himself. This, shockingly, fails to work. God then kills himself as a sacrifice to himself to forgive the sins of the people he made predisposed to commit them.

Fast forward to the present day where every temptation we have to do a bad thing is Lucifer whispering in our ears instead of the scientifically studied and well tested knowledge of Human brain chemistry. Or maybe Lucifer IS brain chemistry? In any case, the all powerful and all knowing God allows this to happen because free will, even though he specifically designed some people (sociopaths as an example) with psychogical defects that prevent or overpower their ability to feel compassion (that good ol’ conscience).

So why did Lucifer rebel against God? If that’s the one question you want to ask about this whole story, I have no idea either.

For the record, the story you’re mentioning isn’t really in the Bible. Satan/Lucifer is a very ambiguous character that for most of his (very few) mentions in the Bible is cast as the Prosecutor of Heaven more than the Prince of Darkness. The word Satan comes from the Hebrew word for “accuser”. It would be more in line with the Old Testament especially to describe him as a tester or tempter working for God than his arch nemesis.

19. What did lucifer think of God's law? Why? ​


ANSWER

According to the Old Testament, Satan (or Lucifer, as he was called when he was an angel) was one of God's creations. Two Old Testament passages (Isaiah 14:12-15 and Ezekiel 28:11-19) allude to the angel Lucifer's original position as a very exalted being, and likely the highest ranking of all the angels. Ezekiel 28:12-15 also states that in addition to being powerful, it's likely that Lucifer was the most beautiful of all of God's creations.

But Lucifer was not satisfied with his lot in heaven as "God's favorite angel." Instead, Lucifer wanted to become God himself and planned what could essentially be called a military coup to overthrow God and take over as leader of the universe. In essence, Satan rebelled against God because he wasn't happy as an assistant to God but instead wanted to have God's job.
