6. Discuss the Maranao's malong. T'Boli bracelets and anklets.​


Answer:

The Maranao are an ethnic group in the Philippines and they are closely associated with the culture of Lanao del Sur–a province in Mindanao. One of the most recognizable pieces of Maranao culture is the malong, a colorful, tubular handwoven textile that is commonly used as a sarong or wrap skirt. Malongs come in a variety of traditional colors and patterns, and are often decorated with handmade lace.

The Maranao also create other accessories from the same fabrics as the malong, such as bracelets and anklets. These pieces often feature intricate designs and beading, such as floral or leaf motifs. Some of these pieces, known as T'boli bracelets and anklets, are made with flowers and beads that are said to bring luck to the wearer. These pieces are usually worn to special occasions and may carry some spiritual meaning as well.

26. weaving is one of the finest works of maranao people. one or their finest works in weaving is malong. if you look at the design of their malong, it has a combination of different colors and pattern called_______.​


Answer:

The malong is a traditional Filipino rectangular or tube-like wraparound skirt bearing a variety of geometric or okir designs. The malong is traditionally used as a garment by both men and women of the numerous ethnic groups in the mainland Mindanao and parts of the Sulu Archipelago. They are wrapped around at waist or chest-height and secured by tucked ends, with belts of braided material or other pieces of cloth, or are knotted over one shoulder. They were traditionally hand-woven, with the patterns usually distinctive to a particular ethnic group. However, modern malong are usually machine-made or even imported, with patterns that mimic the traditional local designs.
