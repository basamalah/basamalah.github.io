Answer:

Globalization has brought significant changes to our daily lives, from the foods we eat to the clothes we wear and the way we interact with people from different cultures. It has created opportunities for individuals and businesses to connect and trade on a global scale. However, it has also led to cultural homogenization and the loss of unique identities.

The collage activity is an excellent way to visualize and reflect on how globalization affects our lives. The images selected can be related to food, fashion, media, technology, and other areas influenced by globalization. The collage can also include pictures of people from different cultures to showcase the diversity brought about by globalization.

The brief explanation accompanying the collage should highlight both the positive and negative effects of globalization. It is important to acknowledge the opportunities it has created while also recognizing the challenges it poses to cultural identities and local economies. Ultimately, whether we continue to adapt to globalization or maintain our identity as one nation is a complex and ongoing debate. Finding a balance between embracing global connections and preserving cultural diversity is crucial.

In conclusion, the collage activity and the accompanying explanation can help individuals gain a better understanding of how globalization affects their lives and the world around them. It is an opportunity to reflect on the benefits and drawbacks of globalization and to consider how we can shape its future impact.

15. collage and decollage​


Answer:

Collage is a visual art form, where pieces of other images are grouped together to create a new piece of art. Collage comes from the French word Coller, which means to glue. A decollage is created by removing part of the larger image to create something new. It is the opposite of collage where the image is built up by adding pieces.
