2. what is the meaning of giantism?


Answer:

Gigantism (Greek: γίγας, gígas, "giant", plural γίγαντες, gígantes), also known as giantism, is a condition characterized by excessive growth and height significantly above average. In humans, this condition is caused by over-production of growth hormone[2] in childhood, resulting in people up to 2.7 m (9.0 ft) in height.

Giantism

Giantism is a medical condition in which a person grows to an unusually tall stature due to an excess of growth hormone during childhood. This condition is known as gigantism.

Giantism can be caused by a tumor on the pituitary gland, which is a small gland located at the base of the brain that produces growth hormone. The excess growth hormone can cause the person to grow much taller than average, often reaching heights of over 7 feet.

Giantism can also be caused by other medical conditions, such as acromegaly, which is a condition that occurs when the body continues to produce growth hormone after the bones have stopped growing.

Treatment for giantism typically involves removing the tumor or other underlying cause of the excess growth hormone, as well as using medications to control hormone production.

Urban Habitat is a term used to describe the physical and social environment created in urban areas. It includes the physical infrastructure such as buildings, roads, and parks, as well as the social aspects such as the culture, economy, and population density. An ideal urban habitat is one that is designed to be livable, efficient, and equitable, while promoting sustainability and resilience.
