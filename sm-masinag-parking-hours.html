10. B] Solve the following problems. Show complete solution. #1) The fee to park in SM Baguio cost 50 pesos for the first 3 hours and an extra 15 pesos for each hour or a fraction of it. If you park more than 12 hours, you will pay an exact rate of 200 pesos. a) Represent your parking fee using function f(t) where t is the number of hours you parked in SM Baguio. b) How much will you pay if you park your car from 8AM to 6:45PM. (10pts) Pahelp


Answers with step-by-step explanation:

a.) 50, t ≤ 3

f(t) = { 50 + 15t, 3 < t < 12

200, t ≥ 12

This is an example of a piecewise function. For the first part, we put f(t) = 50 when t ≤ 3 since you will pay 50 pesos for the first 3 hours of parking fee. For the next part, we put f(t) = 50 + 15t when t is more than 3 hours but less than 12 hours. For the last part of the piecewise function, we put f(t) = 200 when t is equal to or more than 12 hours of parking. So whatever will be the parking fee for the time spent parking in SM Baguio, it depends on the duration of the time.

b.) We have to solve for the parking fee from 8 AM to 6:45 PM but first we need to get how many hours spent by the car in parking.

We need to convert first 6:45 PM to 24-hour time format which is 6:45 + 12:00 = 18:45. So, 6:45 PM can also be expressed as 18:45.

Now we have to subtract 18:45 and 8:00.

18:45

– 8:00

----------------------

10:45

Therefore, the car was parked in SM Baguio for 10 hours and 45 minutes. However, we cannot solve for the time given 45 minutes so we need to convert it first to hours. So 45 minutes is 45/60 = ¾ or 0.75. Therefore, 10 hours, 45 minutes is 10.75 hours. We can already substitute it to the piecewise function.

Since t = 10.75, it lies between 3 < t < 12, so it will be substituted to the second part of the piecewise function.

f(10.75) = 50 + 15(10.75)

f(10.75) = 50 + 161.25

f(10.75) = 211.25

Therefore, you will be paying a parking fee of P 211.25 when you parked from 8 AM to 6:45 PM in SM Baguio.

17. 4. Noah drove his car to SM. His initial velocity is 60 kilometers per hour (kmph) and his final velocity is 20 kmph.his time travel is 1 hour. What is his acceleration? with solution ​


Answer:

We can use the formula for acceleration:

acceleration = (final velocity - initial velocity) / time

Plugging in the given values, we get:

acceleration = (20 kmph - 60 kmph) / 1 hour

Simplifying the expression, we get:

acceleration = -40 kmph / 1 hour

Therefore, Noah's acceleration is -40 kmph / 1 hour or simply -40 kmph/hour.

Note that the negative sign indicates that Noah's acceleration is in the opposite direction to his initial velocity, which means he is decelerating or slowing down.

When you look at your analog clock and try to make a quarter (which is also equivalent to one fourth) using the long arm and the short arm, it will be either pointing at 3 which is equivalent to 15 minutes or 9 which is equivalent to 45 minutes but since we are talking about the time travel in minutes, it's best to use the 15 minutes.

3. Add 60 and 15 and that will give you the answer.
