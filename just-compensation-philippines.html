7. own definition1. Direct compensation2. Indirect compensation3. Nonfinancial compensation4. Pay equity theory5. Expectancy theory​


Answer:

1.Direct compensation refers to the compensation that an employee receives directly from his or her place of work. This includes the base salary and any incentive pay. Direct compensation can be in the form of wages, salaries, commissions and bonuses that an employer provides regularly and consistently.

2.Indirect compensation includes non-monetary benefits provided to workers, such as pension funds, mobile phones, company cars, health and life insurance, overtime pay, and annual leave. In fact, it includes everything from legally obligated health insurance to social security, child care, and more.

3.Non-monetary compensation differs from direct and indirect pay as it is has no monetary value. Non-financial incentives are the types of rewards that are not a part of an employee's pay.

4.Equity theory is all about whether or not employees feel they are being paid fairly. “It's important to note that this is nothing to do with whether you are or are not paying them fairly” explains Simon. “It's about whether or not they feel they are being paid fairly.

5.Expectancy theoryproposes that an individual will behave or act in a certain way because they are motivated to select a specific behavior over others due to what they expect the result of that selected behavior will be.

19. Compute for the tax using the following data using the NIRC: Jayson is an american citizen who currently earning compensation income both in the philippines and in the US as employee. Income $2000 from the US, while P250,000 in the Philippines.​


The total amount of tax deducted using the NIRC is ₱20,000.

NIRC is also known as the National Internal Revenue Code of the Philippines, as declared by the law itself.

Further explanations:

First, in order to determine the tax incurred by a certain person, we should calculate the gross income which can be done by adding all the compensations earned before the tax and other sorts of deductions are made.

The income in the US is $2,000, and ₱250,000 in the Philippines.

Let us say that $1 = ₱50

$2,000 × (50) = ₱100,000

₱100,000 + ₱250,000 = ₱350,000 (gross income)

As stated in the NIRC Chapter 3 Section 2, tax deduction should be applied for incomes of over ₱250,000 but not over ₱400,000. 20% of the excess of the ₱250,000 must be deducted.

₱ 250,000 is fr ee of taxes, only the excess must be incurred with tax. We will have ₱100,000 of the ₱350,000 gross income deducted by 20%.

Solutions are as follows:

₱100,000 × (20%) = ₱20,000

Or

₱100,000 - (20%) = ₱80,000

₱100,000 - ₱80,000 = ₱20,000

Therefore the total amount of tax deducted is ₱20,000.

27. . What is your idea about compensation in the Philippines? Explain and cite some examples


Compensation in the Philippines is an important factor in determining the success of a business. It is important to ensure that employees are adequately compensated for their work, as this will help to motivate them and increase their productivity.

The Philippine government has implemented several laws and regulations to ensure that employees are fairly compensated. The Labor Code of the Philippines sets out minimum wage rates for different industries, and employers must comply with these regulations. Additionally, the Social Security System (SSS) provides benefits such as health insurance, retirement benefits, and other forms of social protection for employees.

In addition to these government-mandated regulations, employers can also provide additional compensation packages such as bonuses, stock options, and other incentives. These can be used to reward employees for their hard work and dedication or to attract new talent. For example, some companies offer performance-based bonuses or stock options to reward high-performing employees. Other companies may offer flexible working hours or telecommuting options as an incentive for employees who are willing to go above and beyond in their roles.

Overall, it is important for employers in the Philippines to ensure that they are providing fair compensation packages that meet both legal requirements and employee expectations. This will help create a positive working environment where employees feel valued and motivated to do their best work.
