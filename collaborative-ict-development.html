Collaboration is the process of two or more people working together to achieve a common goal. ICT (information and communications technology) can be used to facilitate collaborative tasks in many ways. 29. what are the different types of platforms for collaborative ict design? ​


What are the different types of platforms for collaborative ict design?  

Different Platforms for Collaborative ICT Design

Collaboration platform, Groupware is a category of forum or environment in which users can collaborate, transfer information, and exchange data and documents in order to accomplish a mission or complete a job. The following are few examples of shared platforms:

Cloud Computing -

cloud refers to a storage which can only be accessed by users with the right log in credentials via Internet. Managers can store their files on the cloud and employees can view, download and edit the file using their employee log ins and thus, they can continue to work even at home. A good example of cloud environment is GDrive and Dropbox.

Social Media

Social media is a kind of website where people can share messages and files. They may even make phone calls and have video conferences for a community of people.

Filestage

Filestage is a design collaboration platform that allows project managers to have full control of the evaluation and acceptance process. In only a few taps, project managers can easily invite internal and external testers. This users will then leave useful in-context reviews, which eliminates the uncertainty that is all too common for artistic feedback. An integrated to-do list creates projects for the artistic team as well. This guarantees you'll never ever lose a vital piece of customer input, and your projects will always be on point.

Basecamp

Basecamp, also known as project management program, is a fantastic platform for working internally and externally on all stages of the design project. The fact that it works with so many different resources (including graphic markup and QA) makes the possibilities for what you will do with it seem limitless.

Binfire

is a self-proclaimed "virtual workspace" where designers can work more easily with all those participating in the design process. And since it includes Agile, Waterfall, and a combination of the two project management methods, the program can be used however you like.
