13. Seatwork Construct a monohybrid testcross and a dihybrid test cross. Choose from the listed sets of traits. Then, answer the question for each test cross. 1) Give the genotype and phenotype of the parental, Fl and F2 progenies. 2, Give the genotypic ratios and phenotypic ratios for each cross. Flower position A- Axial a-terminal Stem length T-tall t-dwarf Pod shape H-inflated h-constricted Pod color D-green d-yellow Flower color F-purple f-white Seed shape R-round r-wrinkled


Answer:

Monohybrid Testcross:

One parent is homozygous recessive (aa) for the flower position (axial vs. terminal) trait, and the other parent is heterozygous (Aa). The resulting offspring will all have the dominant phenotype (axial) but will have varying genotypes.

1.Genotype and Phenotype of the Parental, Fl and F2 Progenies:

Parent 1: aa (terminal)

Parent 2: Aa (axial)

Fl offspring: Aa (axial)

F2 offspring:

1/4 AA (axial)

1/2 Aa (axial)

1/4 aa (terminal)

Genotypic and Phenotypic Ratios:

Genotypic ratio: 1 AA : 2 Aa : 1 aa

Phenotypic ratio: 3 axial : 1 terminal

Dihybrid Testcross:

One parent is homozygous recessive (aabb) for the flower color (purple vs. white) and seed shape (round vs. wrinkled) traits, and the other parent is heterozygous (AaBb) for both traits. The resulting offspring will have varying genotypes and phenotypes.

2.Genotype and Phenotype of the Parental, Fl and F2 Progenies:

Parent 1: aabb (white, wrinkled)

Parent 2: AaBb (purple, round)

Fl offspring: AaBb (purple, round)

F2 offspring:

1/16 AABB (purple, round)

2/16 AABb (purple, round)

1/16 AAbb (purple, wrinkled)

2/16 AaBB (purple, round)

4/16 AaBb (purple, round)

2/16 Aabb (white, wrinkled)

1/16 aaBB (white, round)

2/16 aaBb (white, round)

1/16 aabb (white, wrinkled)

Genotypic and Phenotypic Ratios:

Genotypic ratio: 1 AABB : 2 AABb : 1 AAbb : 2 AaBB : 4 AaBb : 2 Aabb : 1 aaBB : 2 aaBb : 1 aabb

Phenotypic ratio: 9 purple, round : 3 purple, wrinkled : 3 white, round : 1 white, wrinkled

19. These are two pea-shaped organs located beneath the prostate gland. a. Prostate gland b. epididymis c. Bulbourethral gland d. testes


Answer:

Urethra or A. Prostate gland

Explanation:

Bulbourethral glands Pea-sized structures located on the sides of the urethra just below the prostate gland. These glands produce a clear, slippery fluid that empties directly into the urethra. Fluid produced by these glands lubricates the urethra and neutralizes acidity associated with residual urine.
