25. Scope of the study,Delimitations,Limitation,Scope Where?,Who?,Size?,Topic Limits?,Delimitation You will just explain this‚Äã


Scope of the study refers to the breadth and depth of the research topic that a researcher has chosen to explore. It defines the extent to which the researcher intends to investigate a particular phenomenon or subject matter. It determines the boundaries of the research and sets the direction for the study.

Delimitations are the specific factors or parameters that the researcher sets in advance in order to narrow the scope of the study. These are constraints that are put in place to define the boundaries of the research, and to ensure that the study remains focused and manageable. Delimitations can be determined by a variety of factors, such as time, location, sample size, or methodology.

Limitations, on the other hand, refer to the external factors that may affect the research outcomes, and are beyond the control of the researcher. These can include factors such as lack of resources, access to data, or external events that may impact the research.

The scope of a study can be defined by answering the following questions:

Where: What geographical or spatial boundaries will the study cover?

Who: What specific population or group will be included in the study?

Size: What will be the sample size of the study?

Topic Limits: What specific aspect or aspects of the topic will be explored?

Delimitation: What specific factors or parameters will be set in advance to narrow the scope of the study?

Delimitations and limitations are important to consider when conducting research, as they help to ensure that the study remains focused and feasible, and that the results are reliable and valid. By being clear about the scope, delimitations, and limitations of the study, the researcher can better communicate the significance and relevance of their research to the broader academic community.

27. scope and delimitation‚Äã


Answer:

Scope and delimitations are two elements of a research paper or thesis. The scope of a study explains the extent to which the research area will be explored in the work and specifies the parameters within which the study will be operating. For example, let's say a researcher wants to study the impact of mobile phones on behavior patterns of elementary school children. However, it is not possible for the researcher to cover every aspect of the topic.

Thus, delimitations are the characteristics that limit the scope and describe the boundaries of the study, such as the sample size, geographical location or setting in which the study takes place, population traits, etc. Additionally, the researcher might also choose to use some research tools and methodologies to collect data but not others. These delimitations might be imposed for practical reasons, such as lack of time or financial resources to carry out a more thorough investigation. The delimitation section of the study should explain why specific choices were made while others were excluded and how this might affect the outcome of the research.
