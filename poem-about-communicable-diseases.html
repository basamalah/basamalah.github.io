Answer:

Communicable diseases are those that can spread from one person to another through various modes of transmission, such as direct contact, airborne droplets, or contaminated objects. Here are some preventive measures to avoid communicable diseases:

1. Wash your hands frequently - Use soap and water to wash your hands for at least 20 seconds, especially after using the toilet, before eating, and after coughing, sneezing, or blowing your nose.

2. Cover your mouth and nose - Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or the inside of your elbow when coughing or sneezing, and dispose of used tissues properly.

3. Avoid close contact with sick people - Stay away from people who are sick or have symptoms of communicable diseases.

4. Practice good hygiene - Bathe regularly, brush your teeth twice a day, and keep your nails clean and trimmed.

5. Stay home if you are sick - If you have symptoms of a communicable disease, stay home from school or work to avoid spreading the infection to others.

6. Get vaccinated - Get vaccinated for diseases such as flu, measles, and chickenpox to protect yourself and others from communicable diseases.

7. Avoid sharing personal items - Do not share personal items such as towels, razors, or eating utensils that can spread communicable diseases.

8. Keep your environment clean - Regularly clean and disinfect surfaces that are frequently touched, such as doorknobs, light switches, and countertops.

By following these preventive measures, we can reduce the spread of communicable diseases and protect ourselves and others from getting sick.
