12. ASSIGNMENT Give the beliefs and business relations of different religions to business ethics. Religion 1. Judaism 2. stianity 3. Buddhism 5. Confucianism Beliefs Business Relations to Business Ethics​


Here are some beliefs and business relations of different religions to business ethics:

1. Judaism:

Beliefs: Honesty, fairness, and justice are fundamental values in Judaism. The concept of "tzedek" (righteousness) is central to Jewish ethics, and requires individuals to act justly and fairly in all business dealings.

Business Relations: Jews believe that business should be conducted with fairness and honesty. They emphasize the importance of treating employees and customers fairly, and avoiding deceit and exploitation in business dealings.

2. Christianity:

Beliefs: Christians believe in the importance of ethical behavior, which is grounded in the teachings of Jesus Christ. They are taught to treat others as they would like to be treated, to be honest, and to respect the dignity of all individuals.

Business Relations: Christians believe that business should be conducted with integrity and compassion. They emphasize the importance of treating employees and customers with respect, and avoiding practices that harm the environment or society.

3. Buddhism:

Beliefs: Buddhism teaches the importance of ethical conduct and the concept of "karma" (the idea that actions have consequences). Buddhists are encouraged to act ethically and to avoid causing harm to others.

Business Relations: Buddhists believe that business should be conducted in a way that benefits society as a whole. They emphasize the importance of avoiding harm to the environment, and of treating employees and customers with respect and compassion.

4. Confucianism:

Beliefs: Confucianism emphasizes the importance of ethical behavior and the concept of "ren" (benevolence). Confucians are taught to act ethically and to cultivate virtues such as honesty, loyalty, and respect.

Business Relations: Confucians believe that business should be conducted in a way that benefits society and promotes the common good. They emphasize the importance of treating employees and customers with respect, and of avoiding practices that harm the environment or society.
