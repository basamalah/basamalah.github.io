20. Can a smart tv have both WI-FI and LAN connectivity? If not, why? If so, can it both work at the same time?​


QUESTION

======

Can a smart tv have both WI-FI and LAN connectivity? If not, why? If so, can it both work at the same time?​

ANSWER

======

Depends on your POV, and your Router.

ETHERNET is the LOCAL wiring, or wireless, to your local router / gateway.

ONLY the local router / gateway is on the internet (hopefully). It’s a DOOR, allowing only OUTGOING, not INCOMING messages on your local ethernet.

For your TV to be connected to the Internet, it has to have 3 things. A local ethernet address (192.168.x.x), a SUBNET Mask the Router supports (Usually 255.255.x.x., but this can vary), and a gateway address (the router’s internal address). So, if your TV is not “Connected to the Internet”, it doesn’t have one of these three parameters correct.

Probably, it doesn’t have the gateway address of the router.

23. what are the different wireless connection of Smart TV​


Answer:

There are several different types of wireless connections that a smart TV may support, including:

1. Wi-Fi: Most smart TVs have built-in Wi-Fi, which allows them to connect to your home network and access the internet. This allows you to stream content from online services, such as Netflix or Hulu.

2. Bluetooth: Many smart TVs also support Bluetooth, which allows you to connect other devices, such as wireless headphones or speakers, to your TV.

3. Infrared: Some older smart TVs may use an infrared connection, which requires a direct line of sight between the TV and the device you are trying to connect.

4. HDMI: High-Definition Multimedia Interface (HDMI) is a common type of wired connection that is often used to connect a smart TV to other devices, such as a cable box or Blu-ray player.

5. Ethernet: Some smart TVs also have an Ethernet port, which allows you to connect the TV to your home network using a wired connection. This can provide a faster and more stable internet connection than Wi-Fi.

Overall, the specific types of wireless connections supported by a smart TV will vary depending on the make and model of the TV. It's important to check the specifications of your TV to determine which connections are supported

26. How can we connect to the internet using copper cables similar to the ones used for cable TV?


Answer:

Copper cables can be used to connect to the internet in a few different ways. One way is through a technology called coaxial cable, which is commonly used for cable TV service. Coaxial cable uses a copper core surrounded by insulation and a protective outer layer to transmit data. To connect to the internet using coaxial cable, you would need a coaxial cable modem, which converts the digital signals transmitted over the cable into a form that can be used by your computer or other device.

Explanation:

Answer:

You can connect to the internet using coaxial cables similar to the ones used for cable TV by using a cable modem. The cable modem connects to the coaxial cable, which is then connected to a device like a router or computer. The router or computer will then be able to access the internet.
