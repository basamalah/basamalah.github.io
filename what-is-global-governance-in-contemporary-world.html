7. what is the global interstate system in contemporary world? ​


Answer:

The global interstate system is a vast network of highways, roads, and pathways that connect countries and regions around the world. This system is designed to facilitate the movement of people, goods, and services in a safe and efficient manner. The system is managed by the United Nations and other international organizations and is continuously being developed and improved upon.

The global interstate system consists of major highways and roads, many of which are interconnected. These roads are designed to provide direct access to locations all over the world, allowing for easy and efficient travel between countries. In addition, the system makes use of bridges, tunnels, and other infrastructure to further facilitate the flow of traffic. Many highways also feature rest stops, gas stations, and other facilities to serve travelers' needs.

The global interstate system is an integral part of the international economy, allowing for the rapid and safe movement of goods and services across borders. This system is also beneficial for tourism, allowing people to explore new and exciting locations. As the global interstate system continues to develop and improve, it will undoubtedly play an even greater role in the international community.

11. what is the Important you learned about globalization in contemporary world


Answer:

One of the most important things I have learned about globalization in the contemporary world is that it has had a significant impact on our economies, societies, and cultures. Globalization refers to the increasing interconnectedness and interdependence of the world's economies, societies, and cultures. This process has been facilitated by advances in technology, transportation, and communication, which have made it easier for people, goods, and ideas to move across borders.

Globalization has led to many benefits, such as increased economic growth, higher living standards, and greater cultural exchange. However, it has also created challenges, such as rising income inequality, environmental degradation, and cultural homogenization.

Another important aspect of globalization is that it has created both winners and losers. While some people and countries have benefited greatly from globalization, others have been left behind or have experienced negative consequences, such as job losses or cultural displacement.

Overall, the key takeaway from globalization in the contemporary world is that it is a complex and multifaceted phenomenon that has both benefits and challenges. As such, it requires careful management and policy solutions to ensure that its benefits are maximized and its challenges are mitigated.
