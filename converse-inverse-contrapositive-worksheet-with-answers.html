Inverse, Converse and ContrapositiveA. Definition

1. Conditional Statement is a statement in "if p then q form, such that p is hypothesis and q is conclusion. They are defined as statement true if and only if the conclusion satisfies the hypothesis.

2. Inverse Statement-is a statement in "if not p, then not q form."

3. Converse Statement-is a statement in "if q then p form.

4. Contrapositive Statement -is a statement in "if not q then not p form."

B. Symbolic Form

1. Conditional statement: pq 2. Inverse statement: p

3. Converse statement: q-p

4. Contrapositive statement:qp

Transforming Conditional Into Converse, Inverse & Contrapositive

Transform the statement to converse, inverse, and contrapositive.

If you are a human, then you are a vertebrate.

Answers:

Converse:

If you are a vertebrate, then you are a human.

Inverse:

If you are not a human, then you are not a vertebrate.

Contrapositive:

If you are not a vertebrate, then you are not a human.

Explanation:

Recall the conditional, converse, inverse, and contrapositive statements.

The given statement is an example of a conditional statement. A conditional statement is a statement that consists of a hypothesis and a conclusion. This could be express as 'p → q' or 'p ⇒ q' where p is the first hypothesis and q for the conclusion.

A converse statement is a statement that interchange the hypothesis and the conclusion of a conditional statement. Hence, "if q, then p". The conclusion implies the hypothesis.

An inverse statement is a statement that negating both hypothesis and conclusion in a conditional. Hence, "If not p, then not q." or '~p → ~q'. The symbol ~ is used when using the word 'not'.

Lastly, a contrapositive statement is a statement that interchange the hypothesis and the conclusion of an INVERSE. Hence, "If not q, then not p." or '~q → ~p'.
