1. definition manometer​


Answer:

Manometers are precision instruments that are used to measure pressure, which is the force exerted by a gas or liquid per unit surface area owing to the effects of the weight of that gas or liquid from gravity.

MA-NO-ME-TER

an instrument for measuring the pressure acting on a column of fluid, especially one with a U-shaped tube of liquid in which a difference in the pressures acting in the two arms of the tube causes the liquid to reach different heights in the two arms.

14. When 0.5 g of a liquid is completely evaporated and collected in liter manometer, the pressure is 0.25 atm and the temperature is 27oc. assume ideal gas behavior, find the molecular weight if the gas constant is r?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P = \text{0.25 atm} \\ & V = \text{1 L} \\ & T = 27^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{300.15 K} \\ & mass = \text{0.5 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of gas using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{PV}{\text{RT}} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.25 atm})(\text{1 L})}{\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{300.15 K})} \\ & = \text{0.01015 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molecular weight of the gas.

[tex]\begin{aligned} MW & = \frac{mass}{n} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.5 g}}{\text{0.01015 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{49.26 g/mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molecular weight of the gas is 49.26 g/mol.

The pascal (Pa) or kilopascal (kPa) is a popular unit of pressure measurement, especially in nations that use the metric system. (the majority of Europe) and in other industries, the metric system has largely replaced the pounds per square inch (psi) measure. 1 kilogram per square meter is equal to 0.145038 pounds per square inch.

Other pressure units include pounds per square inch and bar, the unit of atmospheric pressure is atm, centimetres of water, millimetres of mercury or inches of mercury (used as the unit of blood pressure), torr, MSW, and FSW.

The pascal (Pa) represents pressure or stress (SI) in the International System of Units. It is named after the French mathematician and physicist Blaise Pascal. One pascal equals one newton (N) force applied to one square meter of area (m2).

Zero-referenced to atmospheric pressure, vehicle tire gauges and many other pressure gauges detect the pressure above atmospheric pressure (around 1 BAR). Consequently, the following terms may be helpful when working with your gauge:

In the United States, pounds per square inch (PSI) is the standard tire pressure measurement.

kPa (Kilopascal) is a unit of measurement in Europe and other metric-based regions.

BAR is a measurement unit defined as 100 Kilopascals in Europe and other regions that use the metric system. It is roughly equivalent to the atmospheric pressure at sea level on Earth.

The pascal (Pa) or kilopascal (kPa) as a unit of measuring device is widely used throughout the world and has largely replaced the pounds per square inch (psi) unit, except for a few countries, including the United States, that still use the imperial measurement system or the US customary.

Geophysicists measure and calculate tectonic stresses and pressures within the Earth using the gigapascal (GPa).

Using ultrasonic or magnetic resonance imaging, medical elastography analyzes tissue stiffness non-invasively and typically displays Young's modulus or shear modulus of tissue in kilopascals.

27. A chemist heats an amount of baking soda (NaHCO3) and collects the carbon dioxide gas (CO₂) emitted. Using a closed-end manometer, the pressure of CO₂ measured was 159.5 mmHg. Convert this measurement to atm and kPa.​


Given:

[tex]P = \text{159.5 mmHg}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]P \: \text{in atm}[/tex]

[tex]P \: \text{in kPa}[/tex]

Solution: (Pressure in atm)

[tex]P \: \text{in atm} = \text{159.5 mmHg} × \frac{\text{1 atm}}{\text{760 mmHg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P \: \text{in atm} = \text{0.2099 atm}}[/tex]

Solution: (Pressure in kPa)

[tex]P \: \text{in atm} = \text{159.5 mmHg} × \frac{\text{1 atm}}{\text{760 mmHg}} × \frac{\text{101.325 kPa}}{\text{1 atm}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P \: \text{in kPa} = \text{21.26 kPa}}[/tex]
