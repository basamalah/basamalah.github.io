9. As compared with total absorption costing profit over the entire life of a company


There are benefits and drawbacks to both absorption costing and variable costing, which businesses must select for their accounting systems. Full absorption costing, also known as absorption costing, includes all costs associated with production or manufacturing, including direct labor costs, rent, and insurance. On the other side, variable costing excludes direct, fixed overhead costs while including all variable direct costs in the cost of goods sold (COGS). According to generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP), absorption costing is necessary for external reporting.

Absorption costing's key benefit is that it complies with GAAP and records profits more precisely than variable costing. In contrast to variable costing, which solely takes into account variable costs, absorption costing accounts for all production expenses. Absorption costing has limitations in that it cannot be used to compare product lines or enhance operations, and it can distort the perception of a company's profitability.

Anything that is a direct cost of creating a good is included in absorption costing's cost base. Fixed overhead costs are included in the product costs under absorption costing as well. Wages paid to workers who physically produce a product, raw materials required in production, and all overhead expenditures (such as all utility bills) incurred during production are a few of the costs related to product manufacturing.

25. Variable manufacturing overhead costs are treated as product costs under both absorption and variable costing. group startstrue or false


Variable Costing Analysis

In managerial and cost accounting, the notion of variable costing refers to the exclusion of fixed manufacturing overhead from the product-cost of production. The approach stands in contrast to absorption costing, which allocates the fixed production costs to the output of products.

TRUE

Both absorption and variable costing regard variable manufacturing overhead costs as product costs.

Supporting details:

Until the product is sold, all production costs, whether fixed or variable, must be considered product costs and recorded in an inventory amount on the balance sheet. The cost of the item is written down as cost of goods sold on the income statement after it is sold.

One of the most crucial outcomes that a manufacturing company reports on its financial statements is operating income on the income statement. This figure is used by outside parties to assess a company's performance and how it affects them, including creditors, investors, and governmental organizations.

Operating income is a metric used by executives and other company members to assess and enhance operational performance. This fundamental framework has been used by us while creating income statements for manufacturers.

29. Net operating income computed using absorption costing will always be less than net operating income computed using variable costing.


Absorption Costing

Absorption costing is necessary for external reporting. Until the product is sold, all production costs, whether fixed or variable, must be considered product costs and recorded in an inventory amount on the balance sheet.

TRUE

Net operating income determined by absorption costing will never exceed net operating income determined by variable costing.

Supporting details:

When production volume exceeds sales volume, absorption costing releases a portion of fixed manufacturing overhead cost from inventories.

Because absorption costing defers a portion of fixed manufacturing expenses in finished goods inventory, absorption revenue exceeds variable costing income when units produced exceed units sold, like when inventory increases.

The net operating income reported under absorption costing will be higher than the net operating income reported under variable costing since all of the existing fixed production overhead expenditures are expensed under variable costing.

Keep in mind that while fixed costs do not change as activity levels fluctuate, total variable costs do so proportionately. Direct materials, variable manufacturing overhead, and direct labor often make up these variable production expenses.

Direct materials, direct labor, and overhead would be included in the product costs (or cost of goods sold). The selling, general, and administrative costs would be included in the period costs.
