Answer:

The majority of the earthquakes that occur in the world occur near the boundaries of the tectonic plates. The most active regions are the subduction zones, which have produced the largest historic earthquakes on record, Chile 1960 and Alaska 1964. Most of the active volcanoes in the world are located landward from the subduction zones, most outstanding example of which are the string of volcanoes that form the highest active ones along the Andes mountain chain. 19. Why is the location of the epicenter of the earthquake experiences greater damage than the nearby localities?


Earthquake energy is released in seismic waves. These waves spread out from the focus. The waves are felt most strongly at the epicentre, becoming less strong as they travel further away. The most severe damage caused by an earthquake will happen close to the epicentre.

Surface damage

In most earthquakes, the epicenter is the point where the greatest damage takes place, but the length of the subsurface fault rupture may indeed be a long one, and damage can be spread on the surface across the entire rupture zone.
