14. Why is mother ignacia call solitude? ​


Answer:

Mother Ignacia del Espiritu Santo lived during the Spanish colonial era in the Philippines. The precise date of her birth is not known but the record shows the date of her baptism on March 4, 1663. This confirms the statement of Pedro Murillo Velarde, biographer of Mother Ignacia, that she was 21 years old in 1684. Ignacia was the eldest and the sole surviving child of Maria Jeronima, an yndia, and Jusepe luco, a pure Chinese immigrant from Amoy, China. Her father was converted to Catholicism in 1652 and resided in Binondo, Manila.

When Ignacia was 21 years old, her parents wanted her to marry. Heeding a call deep within but not wanting to disappoint her parents, Ignacia sought counsel from Fr. Paul Klein, a Jesuit priest from Bohemia who arrived in Manila in 1682. The priest gave her the Spiritual Exercises of St. Ignatius. After this period of solitude and prayer, Ignacia decided to ('remain in the service of the Divine Majesty/' and to "live by the sweat of her brow." She left home and brought with her only a needle and a pair of scissors. She started to live alone in the house located at the back of the Jesuit College of Manila. Her life of prayer and labor attracted yndias who were also called to religious life but could not be admitted to existing beaterios at that time. Mother Ignacia accepted these women into her company and the first community was born. They became known as the Beatas de la Compafiia de Jesus because they frequently received the sacraments at the Church of St. Ignatius, performed many acts of devotion there and went to the Jesuit Fathers for spiritual direction and confession.

Mother Ignacia centered her life on the suffering Christ and tried to imitate Him through a life of service and humility. She prayed earnestly to God and performed penances to move God to have mercy on them. Her spirituality of humble service was expressed in her capacity to forgive, to bear wrongs patiently and to correct with gentleness and meekness. This spirituality was manifest in peace and harmony in the community, mutual love and union of wills, witnessing to the love of Jesus Christ and the maternal care of Mary our Blessed Mother.

This spirituality sustained the beatas in their moments of difficulties especially during times of extreme poverty, when they even had to beg for rice and salt and scour the streets for firewood. The beatas continued to support themselves by the labor of their hands and sometimes received some financial help from pious people. In all these, they did not cease to thank God and to trust in His divine providence.

24. Make an acronym for Mother Ignacia's name​


Answer:

OPPTIC

Explanation:

The characteristics of M. Ignacia's spirituality, when summarized would form the acronym OPPTIC. Similar to the word optic, which means "related to vision or pertaining to the eye"

O- PENNESS TO THE HOLY SPIRIT

M. Ignacia's spirituality is based on Holy Spirit guidance. Her natural curiosity and love of adventure did not lead to risk-taking because she was sensitive to the Holy Spirit. Bravery grew from M. Ignacia's transparency. Following the Spirit's lead, she gained new insights into events, people, and things in life. She felt God drawing her out of her situation. Her life's circumstances fit into God's loving plan for her, she realized. Everyday, she longed to discover God's will and work in her life.

P- ONDERING HEART OF MARY

Because of her restlessness, M. Ignacia chose a path that defied convention. Her restlessness was not hidden or avoided. She asked an older person for advice. She despised quick fixes. Her whole life she tried to make good decisions. Her role model was Mary. The meaning of the angel's welcome puzzled Mary of Nazareth. “She pondered everything in her heart,” said M. Ignacia. Mary's meditative state soothed M. Ignacia's restlessness. It was during this reflective period that M. Ignacia became more self-aware. Her perception grew as she maintained her calm. She grew to understand her life's events better.

P- RESERVANCE

M. Ignacia's life was a muddled mess. She overcame her own and her beaterio's difficulties. Devotion and loyalty spanned her 85 years in the beaterio Mme Ignacia didn't abandon her mission. With God's help, she stayed faithful to God's work. She exemplified the gospel principle of fidelity shown by the cross This strength came from her faith in Jesus Christ, whose crucifixion symbolizes God's faithful love triumphing over death.

T- RUST IN GOD'S LOVING PROVIDENCE

M. Ignacia and the beatas were poor. They were bad. They were hungry but undeterred. They made a living by working. In their uncertain life, M. Ignacia and her beatas saw God's wonderful providence through their works or donors. Despite their difficult circumstances, M. Ignacia and her beatas' faith grew stronger.

She knew life after the Spiritual Exercise would be difficult. She'd have to abandon her hunt to join a pack. Her seclusion was required. She had to learn to follow God's lead. With God's help, she gained confidence. She perceived God as a caring Father who understood her. This alleviated her need to fit in. She was able to help other native women find their sense of belonging in God as she discovered her own. Her beaterio became a gathering place for local women to pray. A native religious group flourished under M. Ignacia's guidance. Anyone could join her beaterio's apostolic work regardless of race. And so did M. Ignacia and her beatas.

I- NTIMACY WITH CHRIST AND INTEGRITY

Love built M. Ignacia's generosity. Her life revolved around Christ. She gave without waiting to be received. She loved and cared for others before waiting to be loved. As a result of her knowledge of Jesus' unfailing love, M. Ignacia could be charitable Her actions were fueled by Jesus' love on the cross. M. Ignacia's integration developed as she grew in Christ relationship. Her actions mirrored her inner attitude. Her gentle words and kind service demonstrated her purity of heart.

C- OURAGE AND FREATIVE SERVICE ROOTED IN FAITH, LOVE, AND HUMILITY

M. Ignacia took a chance. Her path was unclear. She had to figure it out. She dared to follow her dreams and ideas. She could take chances because her trust in God gave her the courage. Aware of her personal strengths and talents, she embraced and utilised them for the benefit of others as she matured. Her ingenuity matched her boldness. So she used adversity to encounter God and serve others. M. Ignacia and her beatas did not give up their holy life because they were not recognized or subsidized by the government. Their creativity helped them define their community's apostolic character.
