14. 9. The results of a mock board exam for assessing the mathematics skills of realtors were normally distributed with a mean score of 72 and a standard deviation of 12. The realtors who scored in the top 10% are to receive a special certificate, while those in the bottom 20% will be required to attend a remedial workshop. a. What score does a realtor need in order to receive a certificate? b. What score will dictate that the realtor attend the workshop?​


Answer:

To solve this problem, we need to find the z-score for the top 10% and bottom 20% of the distribution. We can then use these z-scores to find the corresponding raw scores using the formula:

raw score = (z-score * standard deviation) + mean

a. To find the z-score for the top 10%, we look up the z-score corresponding to a cumulative area of 0.9 in a standard normal distribution table. This value is approximately 1.28. We can then use this z-score to find the raw score as follows:

raw score = (1.28 * 12) + 72 = 87.36

Therefore, a realtor needs a score of at least **87** to receive a certificate.

b. To find the z-score for the bottom 20%, we look up the z-score corresponding to a cumulative area of 0.2 in a standard normal distribution table. This value is approximately -0.84 (since we want the left tail). We can then use this z-score to find the raw score as follows:

raw score = (-0.84 * 12) + 72 = 61.92

Therefore, a realtor who scores less than **62** will be required to attend a remedial workshop.

Answer:

P = $2,500.00

Step-by-step explanation:

Equation:

P = A / (1 + rt)

Calculation:

First, converting R percent to r a decimal

r = R/100 = 6%/100 = 0.06 per year.

Solving our equation:

P = 3100 / ( 1 + (0.06 × 4)) = 2500

P = $2,500.00

The principal investment required to get a total amount, principal plus interest, of $3,100.00 from simple interest at a rate of 6% per year for 4 years is $2,500.00.
