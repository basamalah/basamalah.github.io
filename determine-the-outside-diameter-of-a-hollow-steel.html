7. 4. A steel tube has an outside diameter of 3.000 cm at room temperature (20°C). A brass tube has an inside diameter of 2.997 cm at the same To what temperature must the ends of the tubes be heated if the steel tube is to be inserted into the brass tube?


To solve this problem, we need to use the coefficient of thermal expansion of each material.

The change in length of a material due to a change in temperature can be calculated using the formula:

ΔL = L * α * ΔT

where:

ΔL = change in length

L = original length

α = coefficient of thermal expansion

ΔT = change in temperature

For the steel tube, the outside diameter is given as 3.000 cm. The radius is therefore 1.500 cm. The coefficient of thermal expansion for steel is approximately 1.2 x 10^-5 /°C.

For the brass tube, the inside diameter is given as 2.997 cm. The radius is therefore 1.4985 cm. The coefficient of thermal expansion for brass is approximately 1.8 x 10^-5 /°C.

We want to find the temperature at which the steel tube will fit inside the brass tube. This occurs when the combined length of the tubes is equal to the sum of their original lengths. Since the brass tube has a smaller coefficient of thermal expansion, it will expand less than the steel tube for a given change in temperature.

Let's assume the tubes are heated to a temperature of T°C. Then the change in length of the steel tube is:

ΔL_steel = 2 * 1.500 cm * (1.2 x 10^-5 /°C) * (T - 20°C)

The factor of 2 accounts for the fact that the change in diameter is twice the change in radius.

The change in length of the brass tube is:

ΔL_brass = 2 * 1.4985 cm * (1.8 x 10^-5 /°C) * (T - 20°C)

The factor of 2 again accounts for the change in diameter.

We want the combined change in length to be zero, so:

ΔL_steel + ΔL_brass = 0

2 * 1.500 cm * (1.2 x 10^-5 /°C) * (T - 20°C) + 2 * 1.4985 cm * (1.8 x 10^-5 /°C) * (T - 20°C) = 0

Simplifying and solving for T, we get:

T = 297.2°C

Therefore, the ends of the tubes must be heated to a temperature of 297.2°C in order for the steel tube to fit inside the brass tube.

20. determine the diameter of a steel rod that will carry a tensile load of 50,000 kg at a stress of 140 kg per square centimeter.


Answer:

The diameter is 21.34 cm

Explanation:

Here, we will be using the concept for stress. Stress is the force acting per unit area in a material. Stress is an effect which deforms a material.

The formula for stress is:

σ = F/A

where

σ     is the stress, unit is in N/m² or Pascal, Pa

F     is the force, unit is in Newtons, N

A     is the cross-sectional area of the material, unit is in m²

Given information

F = 50,000 kg

σ = 140 kg/cm²

d = ?

Take note that the given force is expressed in terms of kg.

Solving the problem

Let us solve for the diameter of the steel rod using the given formula for stress.

σ = F/A

140 kg/cm² = [tex]\frac{50,000kg}{\pi d^2/4 }[/tex]

Take note that we take the cross-sectional area of the tip of a rod which is circle. And the area of a circle is πr² = πd²/4.

Cross-multiply

[tex]d=\sqrt{\frac{4(50,000kg)}{\pi (140kg/cm^2)} }[/tex]

d = 21.34 cm

Therefore, the diameter of the steel rod is 21.34 cm.

26. A cantilever hollow circular bar is 1.5 m long, 5 mm thick and with outside diameter of 75 mm. It is subjected to a torque of 3 kN.m at its free end. What is the resulting angle of twist (degrees) of the bar. G = 78 GPa.​


Answer:

The resulting angle of twist is 0.077 radians

Explanation:

In this problem, we will use the concept similar to elastic deformation which is the deformation due to torsion like twisting.

The formula to be used is:

a. Φ = [tex]\frac{TL}{JG}[/tex]

b. J = π/32(D⁴ - d⁴)

where

Φ    is the angle of twist, the unit is in radians, rad

T     is the internal torque, the unit is in N·m

L     is the length of the segment, the unit is in meter, m

J      is the polar moment of inertia, the unit is in m⁴

D     is the outside diameter, the unit is in meter, m

d     is the inner diameter, the unit is in meter, m

G     is the shear modulus, the unit is in GPa

Given information

L = 1.5 m

D = 75 mm = 0.075 m

d = 75 mm - 5 mm = 70 mm = 0.070 m

T = 3 kN

G = 78 GPa

Φ = ?

Solving the problem

a. Let us solve first the polar moment of inertia using the 2nd given formula

J = π/32(D⁴ - d⁴)

Then substitute the given data

J = π/32(0.075⁴ - 0.070⁴)

J = 7.49 x 10⁺⁷ m⁴

b. Now, let us solve for the angle of twist using the 1st given formula

Φ = [tex]\frac{TL}{JG}[/tex]

Then substitute the given data

Φ = [tex]\frac{(3x10^3N)(1.5m)}{(7.49x10^{-7})(78x10^9N/m^2)}[/tex]

Φ = 0.077 radians

Therefore, the resulting angle of twist of the bar is 0.077 radians.
