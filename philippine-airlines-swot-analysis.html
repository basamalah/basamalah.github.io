The Philippines tourism industry continues to face the impact of Covid-19 on global demand for air travel and leisure tourism. The tourism market will track the wider global tourism industry, which we forecast to contract by high double-digits y-o-y in 2020 owing to widespread border closures and flight suspensions that have extended into Q320. At present, we are hopeful that pent-up demand will be unlocked from Q121 onwards, with international borders potentially expected to open in time for the Q420 high season. The government is highly supportive of the industry and continues to incentivise investments as part of the ambitious National Tourism Development Plan 2016-2022. Risks to our medium-term forecast are weighed to the downside and include global economic weakness, a second wave of Covid-19 infections, environmental challenges and logistical disruptions
