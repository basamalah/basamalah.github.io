7. what are the purposes of the 3 principal okir designs?


Answer:

Okir motif has 3 common designs that depict animals, plants, and mythical figures in highly decorative, long curvilinear with similarities to Arabian art.

These are the purpose of the three common Okir design/motif:Simply for For DecorationFloral patterns are used to show authority, power, prestige, and wealth. In other words, Social StatusMythical Creatures designs are for religious purposes mainly for driving away evil spiritsPossibly, animals designs are used for weapons, hunters, or warriors. An example of this is the Kris Dagger.Explanation:

During the pre-colonial and onward, Okir motifs are mostly associated with the rich and influential people like a Datu or Sultan.

The Okir motif had many foreign influences. For example, the Naga and other Mythical figures came from the Indian influence and the flowing floral patterns came from Muslim influence.

In a "Torogan" (which is the name for a Datu or Sultan's House) there are protruding wood beams called "Panolong" that are designed with Okir motifs.

Answer:

Okir or okil is the term for rectilinear and curvilinear plant-based designs and folk motifs that can be usually found among the Moro and Lumad people of the Southern Philippines, as well as parts of Sabah. It is particularly associated with the artwork of the Maranao and Sama (Badjao) tribes, although it can also be found to a lesser extent among the Maguindanao, Iranun, Tausug, Yakan, and Lumad groups.
