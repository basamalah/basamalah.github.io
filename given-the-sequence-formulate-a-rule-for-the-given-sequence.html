27. given the sequence formulate a rule for the given sequence . 3 6 12 24​


Answer:

an = 3(2)ⁿ⁻¹

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:  

3, 6, 12, 24, . . . an

Where:

First term, a₁ ⇒  3Common ratio, r ⇒ 2

To find r of the geometric sequence:

r = a₂/a₁ = a₃/a₂r = 5/3 = 12/6r = 2

Geometric sequence general rule:

an = a₁(r)ⁿ⁻¹

The rule for the given sequence  3, 6, 12, 24, .  . an

an = 3(2)ⁿ⁻¹

How to use the specific rule?

an = 3(2)ⁿ⁻¹

Check:

a₁ = 3;  n = 1

a₁ = 3(2)¹⁻¹

a₁ = 3(2)⁰

a₁ = 3(1)

a₁ = 3  (True)

a₄ = 24;  n=4

a₄ = 3(2)⁴⁻¹

a₄ = 3(2)³

a₄ = 3(8)

a₄ = 24  (True)

[tex]x_{n} = n^{2}[/tex][tex]x_{n} = 3n[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

1) 1, 4, 9, 16, ...Rule

[tex]x_{n} = n^{2}[/tex]

The numbers in the sequence are squares.

Examples[tex]x_{1} = 1^{2} = 1[/tex][tex]x_{2} = 2^{2} = 4[/tex][tex]x_{3} = 3^{2} = 9[/tex][tex]x_{4} = 4^{2} = 16[/tex][tex]x_{5} = 5^{2} = 25[/tex]2) 3, 6, 9, 12, ...Rule

[tex]x_{n} = 3n[/tex]

The numbers in the sequence are multiples of 3.

Examples[tex]x_{1} = 3(1) = 3[/tex][tex]x_{2} = 3(2) = 6[/tex][tex]x_{3} = 3(3) = 9[/tex][tex]x_{4} = 3(4) = 12[/tex][tex]x_{5} = 3(5) = 15[/tex]
