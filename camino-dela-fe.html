7. Espana llevando a filipinas en el camino del progreso


Spanish Translation

España y Filipinas, que significa "España y Filipinas" en la traducción, es un óleo de 1886 sobre madera del pintor, ilustrado, propagandista y paladín filipino, Juan Luna. Es una representación alegórica de dos mujeres juntas, una representación de España y otra de Filipinas. La pintura, también conocida como España Guiando a Filipinas ("España liderando Filipinas"), es considerada como una de las "piezas duraderas de legado" que los filipinos heredaron de Luna. La pintura es una pieza central de arte en el Salón Luna del Museo López Memorial.


English Translation

España y Filipinas, meaning “Spain and the Philippines” in translation, is an 1886 oil on wood by Filipino painter, ilustrado, propagandist, and paladin, Juan Luna. It is an allegorical depiction of two women together, one a representation of Spain and the other of the Philippines. The painting, also known as España Guiando a Filipinas ("Spain Leading the Philippines"), is regarded as one of the “enduring pieces of legacy” that the Filipinos inherited from Luna. The painting is a center piece art at the Luna Hall of the Lopez Memorial Museum.

17. Fe+AgNO = Fe(NO3)2+Ag


This is a single displacement equation.

Iron + Silver Nitrate = Iron(II) Nitrate + Silver

The balanced equation will be:

Fe + AgNo3 ---> Fe(No3)2  + Ag

Fe + 2AgNo3 ---> Fe(No3)2 + 2Ag

Assuming Fe to be Fe(III):

Fe + AgNo3 ---> Fe(No3)3 + Ag

Fe + 3AgNo3 ---> Fe(No3)3 + 3Ag

Oxidation-reduction equations  

Fe2+ + 2e- = Fe  

2Ag = 2Ag + e-  

Combined this equations to get the reactants and to balance the equation.  

So we the formula for iron (II) and silver nitrate which is:  

Fe(II) + AgNO3  

Now we add the products:  

Fe(II) + AgNO3 ----> FeNO3 + Ag(s)  

Now to balance the equation, must consider the following Ag has two moles (Remember: 2Ag = 2Ag + e-) meaning that Ag needs two electrons from nitrate to from a stable molecule. Iron (II) is also 2+ meaning that it also needs two electrons from nitrate in the products. Therefore, the equation looks like so:  

Fe (II) + 2AgNO3 -----> Fe(NO3)2 + 2Ag

For iron (III) the equations looks like this:  

Fe (III) + 3AgNO3 -----> Fe(NO3)3 + 3Ag
