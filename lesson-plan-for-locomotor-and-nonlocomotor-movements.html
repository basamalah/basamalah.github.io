1. what are the locomotor and nonlocomotor movements


Locomotor Movements - movements where the body moves through space from one location to another. 


Non Locomotor - movements that occur in the body parts or the whole body and do not cause the body to travel to another space/ body is stationary

2. similarities of locomotor nonlocomotor and manipulative movement​


Answer:

Locomotor movements are those that incorporate traveling from one point to another. ... Nonlocomotor movements are body movements without travel, such as bending, swaying, or wiggling. Manipulative movements involve both the body and an object. They are the movements most associated with games and sports

9. Essay explain the differences between axial locomotor and nonlocomotor movements​


Answer:

Axial and Locomotor. Axial movement happens in a stationary place and locomotor movement travels through space. When you raise your arm, bend your knees, or even turn your head, you are performing an axial movement. Simply walk across the room and you are doing locomotor movement!
