6. mga gawain ng medtech?at kaalamang pumukaw sa iyo​


Answer:

Medical Technologists Job Description

Med techs prepare blood, urine, and tissue samples for analysis using special laboratory equipment. They may also use a variety of chemicals and other agents to help find bacteria and other microorganisms. The role a med tech plays may be helpful in a number of ways, including determining a patient's response to medical treatment by testing blood levels, as well as matching blood types when a transfusion is necessary. Many med techs are also trained in phlebotomy, which involves the collection and proper storage of blood. In addition, med techs may need to enter results into a patient's records.

8. PERSONAL HEALTH CAREER PLANCAREER EXPLORATIONHealth Careers-engineer-police-medtech-nursingDescription Of The Health Career----​


Certainly! Here are brief descriptions of the health careers for engineer, police, medtech, and nursing:

1. Health Careers for Engineer: Biomedical Engineer

Biomedical engineering combines biology, medicine, and engineering to create new technologies and develop innovative applications that improve human health, such as prosthetics, biomedical imaging, and medical devices.

2. Health Careers for Police: Forensic Pathologist

Forensic pathologists are medical doctors who specialize in investigating the cause of death in cases of sudden, unexpected, or violent deaths. They work closely with law enforcement agencies to help solve crimes by conducting autopsies and analyzing evidence.

3. Health Careers for Medtech: Medical Technologist

Medical technologists are responsible for running complex medical tests and analyzing the results. They work in hospitals, clinics, and laboratories, using specialized equipment to study cells, tissues, and bodily fluids to diagnose diseases and monitor treatment.

4. Health Careers for Nursing: Nurse Practitioner

A nurse practitioner is a registered nurse who holds an advanced degree in nursing and is licensed to diagnose and treat patients. They work in a variety of healthcare settings, from primary care clinics to hospitals to nursing homes, and provide a range of services, including physical exams, medication management, and patient education.

17. how does globalization affect you as a student or a medtech?​


Answer:

As a student, globalization can provide access to a wider range of educational opportunities and resources, including online courses, international exchange programs, and collaborations with universities and professionals from other countries. This can broaden one's perspective, deepen their knowledge, and enhance their career prospects.

On the other hand, globalization can also increase competition for jobs and resources, especially in fields that are highly interconnected and globally competitive. It can also lead to the homogenization of cultures and the erosion of local traditions and values, which may have a negative impact on identity and social cohesion.

As a medtech professional, globalization can bring new innovations and technologies from other countries, improve access to medical equipment and supplies, and create new markets for products and services. It can also facilitate collaborations between medical professionals and researchers from different parts of the world, leading to the exchange of knowledge and best practices.

However, globalization can also bring challenges, such as increased pressure to reduce costs, standardize practices, and meet global regulations and standards. It can also raise ethical concerns about the distribution of healthcare resources, the exploitation of vulnerable populations, and the potential risks of global pandemics.

In summary, globalization can have both positive and negative effects on students and medical technology professionals, and it is important to be aware of these impacts and work to mitigate any potential negative consequences while maximizing the opportunities for growth and development
