1. A root word is a word or word part that can form the basis of new words through the addition of prefixes and suffixes. ... For example, “egotist” has a root word of “ego” plus the suffix "-ist." “Acting” has the root word “act”; “-ing” is merely the suffix.

2.  A basic word to which affixes (prefixes and suffixes) are added is called a root word because it forms the basis of a new word. ... For example, the word lovely consists of the word love and the suffix -ly. In contrast, a root is the basis of a new word, but it does not typically form a stand-alone word on its own.
