1. What is culture? How does it define moral behavior?​


Answer:

Human Society(Culture)

Explanation:

Culture reflects the moral and ethical beliefs and standards that speak to how people should behave and interact with others. These normative beliefs, together with related cultural values and rituals, impose a sense of order and control on aspects of life that might otherwise appear chaotic or unpredictable.

3. 3. How does different cultures define moral behavior?​


answer :

different cultures have different definitions and standards of moral behavior. moral behavior refers to actions that are considered right or wrong based on the beliefs, values, and principles of a particular society or culture.

for example, in some cultures, honesty and integrity are highly valued, and lying or cheating is considered morally wrong. in other cultures, loyalty to family or community may be emphasized above individual honesty, and lying or cheating may be more acceptable in certain situations.

religion can also play a significant role in defining moral behavior in different cultures. for instance, in many religious traditions, such as christianity, islam, and buddhism, there are specific moral codes and guidelines that believers are expected to follow.

in some cultures, moral behavior may be defined more broadly to include values such as respect for authority, generosity, and hospitality. the specific moral values and principles that are emphasized in a culture are often shaped by its history, social norms, and religious or philosophical beliefs.

it's important to note that while there may be differences in how different cultures define moral behavior, there are also many universal values that are shared across cultures, such as compassion, fairness, and kindness.
