20. 5. Moses as a leader was a. alone b. not a good speaker c. afraid of the wild animals


B. Not a good speaker.

EXODUS 4:10 - Moses now said to Jehovah: “Pardon me, Jehovah, but I have never been a fluent speaker, neither in the past nor since you have spoken to your servant, for I am slow of speech and slow of tongue.” - NWT

Moses had initial fear of public speaking. He was not a proficient speaker and may have had a speech problem, which is why he believed he was unqualified to represent Jehovah God to the people of Israel and before Pharaoh of Egypt. He even tried to justify public speaking by pointing out his speaking difficulties, but God never accepted any of his reasonings.

EXODUS 6:12 - However, Moses replied to Jehovah: “Look! The Israelites have not listened to me; how will Pharʹaoh ever listen to me, as I speak with difficulty?"

Jehovah God helped Moses by letting him bring along Aaron as a spokesperson for Moses, and he assisted him in speaking. Moses addressed the entire nation, not only to individuals and small groups but repeatedly and persuasively.  

What can we learn from this?
Even if we lack fluency in public speaking, we should never lose faith in Jehovah God for he will undoubtlessly help us to be good at using our speech to glorify him and his name.

22. what are the strengths and weaknesses of moses as a leader? ​


Answer:

Some of his strengths.

He was educated, probably as an architect in charge of construction

He was broken by 40 years of working as a shepherd for another man—Egyptians despised shepherds

He showed great faith and obedience to God—this developed over time

He wrote five works, the influence of which cannot be overestimated

He led millions of people whose identities had been stripped from them by a hundred years of slavery

Some of his weaknesses.

Initially, he was timid and faithless

He had a least one fit of rage, where he broke the stone tablets God had given him

He tried to do it all himself, although he listened to advise and appointed assistants

For a moment, he became angry and self-centered, leading him to strike the rock instead of speaking to it, as God had told him

Given the demands of his role, Moses was one of the greatest leaders of all time.
