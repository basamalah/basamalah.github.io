= 10 (9) / 2

A chord subtending 90° angle at centre of circle is equal to side length of a square inscribed in circle.

Diameter of a circle is equal to diagonal of a square inscribed. We can find side length of a square by dividing diameter by 2–√

So chord length of given circle is

Ch=12÷2–√≈8.49cm

Or it can be derived by formula,

Ch=2⋅r⋅Sinθ2

Ch=12⋅12–√

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

[tex] \rm hypotenuse = leg \cdot \sqrt2 [/tex]

» By applying the given on the circle, we have:

[tex] \rm chord = radius \cdot \sqrt2 [/tex]

[tex] \rm chord = \mathcal{6\,cm \cdot \sqrt2} [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The length of the chord is 6√2 cm long.
