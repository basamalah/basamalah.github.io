1. Nichrome ribbon resistor elements has a resistance of 1 ohm. the element is made from sheet of nichrome alloy, 0.025 cm thick. if the width of the ribbon is 0.3 cm, what length is required per element? assume specific resistance of nichrome alloy to be 109 micro ohm-cm.


Answer :
68.80 cm

Explanation:

Given:
R = 1 ohm
Thickness = 0.025 cm
Width = 0.3 cm
p = 109 micro ohm-cm or 109x10^-6

Formula:
R = pL/A

where:
R = resistance
p = resistivity
L = length
A = cross-sectional area

Therefore:
l = RA/p
I = 1 ohm x 0.0075cm^2 / 109x10^-6 ohm-cm

I = 63.80 cm

9. what is a nichrome wire?


Nichrome (NiCr, nickel-chrome, chrome-nickel, etc.) is any of various alloys of nickel, chromium, and often iron (and possibly other elements). The most common usage is as resistance wire, although they are also used in some dental restorations (fillings) and in a few other applications.

Nichrome Wire and Ribbon (80% Ni/20%Cr). Nichrome is a non-magnetic alloy of nickel and chromium. Nichrome is widely used in heating elements. It is wound in wire coils to a certain electrical resistance, and current passed through to produce heat.
