24. identify the different 21st century literacy and what are the skills that are applicable to the 21st century learners ​


Answer:

The main literacies in the 21st century include:

Information and digital literacy - ability to access, evaluate and use information from various digital sources

Media literacy - ability to understand, analyze and critically evaluate various forms of media, such as television, film, and online media.

Technology literacy - ability to use and integrate technology into daily life and work activities.

Financial literacy - ability to understand and manage personal finances and investments.

Health literacy - ability to access and understand health information and services.

Environmental literacy - ability to understand and act on environmental issues.

Cultural literacy - ability to understand and appreciate cultural diversity and global perspectives.

The skills that are applicable to 21st century learners include:

Critical thinking and problem solving - ability to analyze and evaluate information, and make informed decisions.

Communication skills - ability to effectively communicate ideas and information in a variety of contexts.

Collaboration - ability to work effectively with others to achieve a common goal.

Creativity - ability to think outside the box and generate innovative ideas.

Adaptability - ability to adapt to new and changing situations.

Initiative and self-direction - ability to take charge of one's own learning and pursue goals independently.

Digital and technological fluency - ability to use digital technologies to support learning and communication.
