14. Six weights for an analytic balance are in an arithmetic progression.If the weights range from 5 grams to 20 grams,what are the other weights?​


Answer:

8, 11, 14, 17

Step-by-step explanation:

The question is asking us to find the 4 terms (out of 6) between 5 and 20.

We know that the 6th term (20) = 5+5d, where d is the difference.

Let's solve for d:

5+5d = 20

5d = 15 (APE)

d = 3 (MPE)

Now, let's use d to find the 4 missing terms.

5+3=8

8+3=11

11+3=14

14+3=17

21. when do you use analytic rubrics


Answer:

Analytic rubrics are used to assess the quality of student work on a task or assignment. The rubric breaks down the task into several components or criteria and evaluates each one separately. This type of rubric provides a more detailed and nuanced assessment of the work than a holistic rubric, which evaluates the work as a whole based on a single criterion.

Analytic rubrics are commonly used in educational settings to assess student performance in areas such as writing, problem solving, critical thinking, and presentation skills. They are also used to evaluate performance in competitions, exhibitions, and other performance-based events.

In general, analytic rubrics are used when the task being evaluated is complex and requires the assessment of multiple skills, abilities, or aspects of the work. They allow teachers and evaluators to provide specific feedback on individual components of the work, which can help students improve their skills and achieve higher quality results.

The measurement of mass in the laboratory is performed using balances. While there are a great variety of balance types, they are all expensive and delicate instruments that need to be treated with respect. Most balances in use now are electronic balances. If this is not the case at your school, you will need to consult your instructor to obtain directions on how to use your balances.

Balance Rules

In order to protect the balances and ensure accurate results, a number of rules should be followed:

1. Always check that the balance is level before using it.

2. Never weigh directly on the balance pan. Always use a piece of weighing paper to protect it.

3. Never add chemicals to a container or piece of weighing paper while it is on the balance.

4. Do not weigh hot or cold objects.

5. Clean up any spills around the balance immediately.
