19. What is the use of dry steam power plant, How does this work


Answer:

A dry steam power plant is a type of geothermal power plant that generates electricity using geothermal steam that does not require additional processing or treatment. This steam is generated by hot rocks and magma located deep within the earth's crust.

The process of generating electricity from dry steam power plants involves the following steps:

Extraction: Geothermal wells are drilled into the ground to extract the steam from the underground reservoir.

Separation: Once the steam is extracted from the ground, it is separated from any water or other impurities.

Power Generation: The steam is then directed towards a turbine that is connected to an electricity generator. As the steam flows through the turbine, it spins the blades, which in turn rotates the generator to produce electricity.

Condensation: After the steam has passed through the turbine, it is cooled and condensed back into water. This water is then pumped back into the underground reservoir to be reheated and reused in the process.

The advantages of dry steam power plants are that they are reliable and can generate electricity 24/7 with minimal environmental impact. However, they are limited to areas with high underground temperatures and a good supply of dry steam.

26. Where did the steam of dry steam power plant come from


Answer:

Dry steam power plants use steam that occurs naturally in geothermal reservoirs deep beneath the Earth's surface. These geothermal reservoirs are typically found in areas with high volcanic activity, where magma heats underground water to high temperatures and pressures, creating steam. The steam is then extracted from the reservoirs through wells and piped to the power plant, where it is used to turn turbines and generate electricity. Unlike other types of geothermal power plants, which require additional steps to separate steam from hot water, dry steam power plants use pure steam to generate electricity, making them very efficient and environmentally friendly.

29. What is the purpose of injection well of dry steam power plant


Answer:

The purpose of injection wells in dry steam power plants is to reinject the condensed water and any excess steam back into the geothermal reservoir. This helps to maintain the reservoir pressure and sustain the geothermal resource for future use. It also prevents the release of excess water and steam into the environment, reducing the risk of environmental damage. Additionally, reinjecting the water and steam can help to improve the efficiency of the power plant by increasing the amount of available steam for electricity generation.

Geothermal power plants use hydrothermal resources that have both water (hydro) and heat (thermal). Geothermal power plants require high-temperature hydrothermal resources—300 degrees Fahrenheit (°F) to 700° F—that come from either dry steam wells or from hot water wells. We use these resources by drilling wells into the earth and then piping steam or hot water to the surface. The hot water or steam powers a turbine that generates electricity. Some geothermal wells are as much as 2 miles deep.

Explanation:

There are three basic types of geothermal power plants:

•Dry steam plants use steam directly from a geothermal reservoir to turn generator turbines. The first geothermal power plant was built in 1904 in Tuscany, Italy, where natural steam erupted from the earth.

•Flash steam plants take high-pressure hot water from deep inside the earth and convert it to steam that drives generator turbines. When the steam cools, it condenses to water and is injected back into the ground to be used again. Most geothermal power plants are flash steam plants.

•Binary-cycle power plants transfer the heat from geothermal hot water to another liquid. The heat causes the second liquid to turn to steam, and the steam drives a generator turbine.
