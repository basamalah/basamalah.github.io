One of the creatures with which people cross themselves before praying is an example of the Holy Spirit. To accept the Holy Spirit is when you trust God with your heart and believe that God sent his son to die in your place as a sacrifice for your sins. When you put your faith in God, the Holy Spirit is present in you.

As an Augustinian Recollect Steward you need the Holy Spirit to become Christ centered. As defined above, the Holy Spirit is God’s continued presence on Earth. Through the Holy Spirit we are guided by God in our daily devotion to fulfill our mission on earth. That is to pray with greater connection to God, to submit to his commands, and to live according to His glorious will just like the ways St. Augustine and his mother, Saint Monica has lived their lives. They pray to God for relief from their afflictions and acknowledge that only God's will will bring them peace.

When we be Christ-centered as Augustinian Recollect Stewards, we can be a disciplined individual who will reflect not just in our schools and families, but also in our entire community.

The seven gifts of the Holy Spirit are the following:

wisdom, understanding, counsel, fortitude, knowledge, piety, and fear of the Lord.

The gifts of the Holy Spirit assist us in bringing things back to their proper place. It assist us in making sound decisions and delivering sound advice. It aids us in turning terrible situations into positive ones. We can use this gift of the Holy Spirit to guide people to do what is right and good.
