12. A 220-kv, three-phase transmission line is 40 km long. the resistance per phase is 11.15 per km and the inductance per phase is 1.3263 mh per km. the shunt capacitance is negligibl e. use the short line model to find the voltage and power at the sending end and the voltage regulation and efficiency when the line is supplying a three-phase load of 381 mva at 0.8 power factor leading at 220 kv.


Answer:

The voltage and power at the sending end can be found using the short line model, which considers the line to be an ideal voltage source in series with the line impedance.

The line impedance can be calculated by adding the resistance and the reactance (inductance) per phase.

Resistance per phase = 11.15 ohm/km

Inductance per phase = 1.3263 mH/km

Converting inductance to ohms:

1.3263 mH/km = 1.3263 x 10^-3 H/km = 1.3263 x 10^-3 x 2π x 50 = 0.0833 ohm/km

Line impedance per phase = resistance + reactance = 11.15 + 0.0833 = 11.2333 ohm/km

Line impedance = 11.2333 ohm/km x 40 km = 449.33 ohms

Voltage at the sending end = 220 kV

Power at the sending end = (Voltage at the sending end)^2 / line impedance = (220 x 10^3)^2 / 449.33 = 485,988,900 VA = 486 MVA

Voltage Regulation = (Voltage at load / Voltage at sending end) -1 = (220 / 220) -1 = 0

Efficiency = (Power at load / Power at sending end) x 100 = (381 / 486) x 100 = 78.39%

The voltage at the sending end is 220 kV, the power at the sending end is 486 MVA, the voltage regulation is 0% and the efficiency is 78.39%.
