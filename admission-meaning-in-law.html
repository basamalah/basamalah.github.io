10. Definition of admission requirements


Answer:

Admission requirements refer to the minimum requirement that the school has set for any student-applicant upon admission. The admission requirements may include the following:

Report CardGood Moral CharacterHonorable Dismissal for TransfereesTranscript of Record for Transferee College StudentsStudent Visa & Passport for International StudentsPictures in different sizes (1x1 or 2x2)Entrance ExamInitial Interview by the Dean in your preferred department (depending on course)Police / NBI ClearanceBaptismal Certificate (for Catholic Educational Institutions)

Please note that you need to prepare the stated requirements before you proceed to enroll in an educational institution. The indicated requirements are mostly needed by universities / schools upon admission. They strictly need it for assessment since they also have protocols and standards to follow during enrollment period.

How to format a resume?

Set one-inch margins on all four sides.

Pick a 11 or 12pt resume font and stick to it.

Create a proper resume header format for your contact details.

Divide your resume into legible resume sections: Contact Information, Resume Summary, Work Experience, Education, Skills.

Use bullet points to talk about past jobs.

Be consistent with your resume formatting (stick to the same date format: for example 11–2018, or November 2018.)

Use single or 1.15 line spacing.

Add an extra space before and after each section heading.

Make your resume as long as it needs to be.

22. Define admission requirements


The admission requirements refer to the documents that are needed to seek permission or acceptance in a desired institution such as a school or a university.  The institution requires these documents (some examples are shown below) to assess whether the student is qualified to enter or enroll in their chosen university.

The university’s entrance examination High school card Certification of good moral character from the previous school’s principal or the guidance counselor Certificate of eligibility for admission to college NSO/PSA authenticated copy of your birth certificate Medical clearance (if applicable to the university) An interview with the Dean (if applicable to the university) Application letter
