15. How did Age of Enlightenment and free trade help the rise of Filipino nationalism?


Answer:

The Age of Enlightenment and free trade played significant roles in the rise of Filipino nationalism. The Age of Enlightenment was a period of intellectual and philosophical growth in Europe in the 17th and 18th centuries. This period emphasized reason, science, and individualism, and it influenced the development of political and social ideas that challenged traditional authority and promoted democracy and freedom.

The ideas of the Age of Enlightenment spread to the Philippines through Spanish colonial rule, and they inspired Filipino intellectuals to question the legitimacy of Spanish authority and to demand greater political and economic rights. These intellectuals, known as ilustrados, used the ideas of the Enlightenment to argue for the need for a more democratic and just society.

Free trade also played a significant role in the rise of Filipino nationalism. During the 19th century, the Philippines was opened up to international trade, and this led to the growth of a merchant class that became increasingly aware of the economic exploitation of the country by foreign powers. This exploitation fueled nationalist sentiment among Filipinos, who demanded greater economic autonomy and control over their resources.

In conclusion, the Age of Enlightenment and free trade helped the rise of Filipino nationalism by inspiring intellectuals to question traditional authority and by exposing the economic exploitation of the country by foreign powers. These factors contributed to the development of a sense of national identity and a desire for greater political and economic autonomy among Filipinos.
