Phonology, semantics, morphology, syntax, and pragmatics are the basic features of the human language

Discussion

1. Phonetics and Phonology.

This knowledge will make you familiar with the dictionary—I guarantee it. It studies the sounds that appear in spoken language, including how to produce certain sounds and distinguish them from other sounds. You will understand the types of vowels, consonants, and even diphthongs in the language you are learning, complete with how to pronounce and use them.

2.Morphology.

This branch of linguistics studies morphemes, which are the smallest parts of language that have meaning. In other words, this one science is the most curious science, namely researching down to the most detailed pieces in language.

3.Syntax.

This science studies language patterns, including how noun phrases are formed, as well as clauses and sentences. After all, these rules are not always the same in each language. For example, in English, we call it beautiful girl (a noun phrase that comes from a combination of adjective + noun), while in Indonesian we call it beautiful girl, with the reverse rule: noun + adjective.

4. Semantics

in linguistics focuses on the relationship between words, phrases, including how the meaning of these words. In this science too, we know that a word can have many meanings, or simply called polysemy.

5.Pragmatics

Pragmatics study the matter of meaning, except that it is more pragmatic.

7. What is Semantic Load?​


Semantic load is the meaning or connotation of a word or expression.

What is Semantic Load?

It is the set of associations, feelings and knowledge that are linked to a word or expression. The semantic load can vary according to the context, culture and individual experience of each person.

For example, the word "home" may have a positive semantic charge for someone who has a cozy home, but it may have a negative semantic charge for someone who does not have fond memories of their childhood home.

24. Semantic web in English​


Answer:

The Semantic Web is an extension of the World Wide Web through standards set by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). The goal of the Semantic Web is to make Internet data machine-readable. To enable the encoding of semantics with the data, technologies such as Resource Description Framework (RDF) and Web Ontology Language (OWL) are used.

The Semantic Web refers to the practice of adding additional structure or markup to online content that describes what things mean. The motivation for semantic markup is so that data processing applications can better understand the relationships and contexts in which real-world objects exist and interact.
