3. List down different right of PWDs. Have a brief description or reaction on each right.​


Answer:

Persons with disabilities (PWDs) have several rights that protect them from discrimination and ensure their equal treatment and full participation in society. Some of these rights are:

Right to equality and non-discrimination: PWDs have the right to be treated equally and without discrimination on the basis of their disability. This right is enshrined in the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD), which was adopted by the United Nations in 2006.Right to dignity and respect: PWDs have the right to live with dignity and to be treated with respect, regardless of their abilities or disabilities. This right recognizes the inherent value of all human beings and is fundamental to ensuring the full and equal participation of PWDs in society.Right to accessibility: PWDs have the right to access public and private spaces, goods, and services on an equal basis with others. This includes the right to accessible transportation, information, and communication, as well as physical accessibility to buildings and public spaces.Right to education: PWDs have the right to receive an education that is inclusive, accessible, and of the same quality as education received by others. This includes the right to appropriate accommodations and support services, such as sign language interpretation and Braille materials.Right to work: PWDs have the right to work on an equal basis with others, including the right to equal pay for equal work and to a safe and healthy work environment.Right to health: PWDs have the right to access health services, including rehabilitation services, on an equal basis with others.Right to participate in political and public life: PWDs have the right to participate in political and public life, including the right to vote and to stand for election, on an equal basis with others.

In general, these rights are aimed at ensuring that PWDs have the same opportunities and freedoms as others, and are able to fully participate in all aspects of society. However, in many cases, these rights are not fully realized, and PWDs continue to face significant barriers and discrimination. It is important for governments, organizations, and individuals to take action to ensure that these rights are protected and upheld for all PWDs.

8. Paano ba maging pwd?


Ang PWD o Person With Disability ay isang pangngalan sa mga taong may pagkukulang o higit sa pisikal, mental, intelektwal, o senswal na kalagayan.

A PWD or a Person With Disability is a noun that refers to those people with physical, mental, intellectual or sensual impairment.

Ang isang taong PWD ay may katangiang:
