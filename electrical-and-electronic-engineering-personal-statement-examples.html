24. Give examples of electronic motors and electric generators and write their functions


Examples of Electronic Motor:

* Electric fans use an electric motor to turn a rotating shaft connected to their blades.

* Blenders and food processors use motors attached to a shaft, which is then connected to a blade. The blade is used to crush, grind, shred, and slice objects.

* Washing and drying machines use motors to rotate clothing to dispel water and dirt.

* Machine or drilling tools use electric motors to drive a rotating chuck that can be connected to several accessories. These can be used to drill holes, drive screws, and grind various materials.

* Car motors, such as electric cars, use motors to turn the car's gears and wheels.

* Conveyors, elevators, and escalators use motors to transport people and objects.

* Heavy equipment or machinery uses motors to move gears and pistons that are essential in bulldozers, excavators, and compactors, to name a few.

* Robots use multiple motors to complete a complex task in a series of small steps.

Examples of Electric Generators:

* Portable Generator are handy during natural disasters or calamities when grid power breaks down. They provide power outages.

* Inverter generators produce AC power and by using a rectifier which converts the AC power into DC power and then inverts it to AC to provide steady current to appliances. These are very useful for appliances such as air conditioners, refrigerators, automobiles, boats and recreation vehicles which need particular values of voltage and frequency, which inverter generators are capable of providing. They are also light in weight and compact, therefore highly suitable for such applications.

* Standby generators serve the purpose of switching on automatically when grid power fails. These are very useful for residential apartments, hotels, restaurants, hospitals and commercial establishments connected to grid power.

29. 1. what is electrical Engineering? 2. what is an electrical engineer?3. what are the jobs of an electrical engineer?​


Answer:

1.Electrical engineering deals with the study and application of physics and mathematics combined with elements of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism to both large and small scale systems to process information and transmit energy.

2.Electrical engineering is an engineering discipline concerned with the study, design, and application of equipment, devices, and systems which use electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism.

3.Electrical engineers design, develop, and test electrical devices and equipment, including communications systems, power generators, motors and navigation systems, and electrical systems for automobiles and aircraft. They also oversee the manufacture of these devices, systems, and equipment.
