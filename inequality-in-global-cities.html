Among the 17 goals of the SDGs (Sustainable Development Goals), 5 goals that are related to health are goals number 2, 3, 4, 5 and 7:

Number 2: Zero hunger

Number 3: Good health and well-being

Number 4: Quality education (health education)

Number 5: Gender equality

Number 6: Clean water and Sanitation

EXPLANATION OF THE GLOBAL EFFECTS OF THE GOALS ABOVE:

Goal 2: Zero Hunger

The global effect of SDGs number 2 is to end hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition and promote sustainable agriculture. This goal is in line with Indonesia's development priorities which are included in the priorities of food security and job creation.

Goal 3: Good health and well-being

The global effect of goal 3 is to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all people at all ages. In addition to the problems that have not been fully addressed, including efforts to reduce maternal mortality (MMR) and infant mortality (IMR), control of HIV/AIDS, TB, malaria and increase access to reproductive health (including family planning), there are new things that become attention, namely:

1) Death from non-communicable diseases (PTM);

2) Drug and alcohol abuse;

3) Deaths and injuries due to traffic accidents;

4) Universal Health Coverage;

5) Contamination and pollution of water, air and soil; and crisis and emergency management.

Goal 4: Quality education (health education)

The purpose of this goal is to ensure inclusive and equitable quality (health) education and to increase lifelong learning opportunities for all by 2030. In order to achieve the goal of a healthy and prosperous life in 2030, 10 targets have been set which are measured through 23 indicators.

Goal 5: Gender equality

The global effect of this goal is to achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls. There are 9 targets in Goal #5 as the main requirement for achieving this goal. Of the 17 Goals, 169 Targets in the SDGs, there are 16 goals and 91 targets related to gender equality, women's and girls' human rights.

Goal 6: Clean water and Sanitation

The global effect on the points in the sustainable development goals (SDGs) in the environmental sector is to ensure that people achieve universal access to clean water and sanitation.
