2. Read The Story SEVENTH GRADE by Gary Soto and summarize it.


Answer:

The story is taken in a middle school. The conflict is that Victor, the protagonist, is trying to impress a girl named Teresa, but he doesn’t know how to. He was in homeroom and saw that Teresa was there too. After all his classes and lunch, he goes to French class, which Teresa is there as well. At the same time, he wanted to impress Teresa. As a result of this, he pretended to speak French to the teacher and he felt really embarrassed for doing that. After class, Teresa was actually impressed and asked Victor for help, so Victor, feeling determined, went to the library after his classes and took some French books so he can help Teresa. Victor goes through events that he has never faced before. He is a dynamic character because he grows from being awkward to daring to determined.

**Miss Yeyeng enters the hallway, wearing her best dress and putting on her lipstick.**

**Mrs. Nunal peeks in from the doorway.**

MRS. Nunal: Miss Yeyeng, where are you going?

MISS Yeyeng: I'm going to the town fiesta. I heard there will be music and dancing.

MRS. Nunal: Don't you know what happened to Maria and Consuelo after they went to the fiesta last year?

MISS Yeyeng: What happened?

MRS. Nunal: They were both harassed by the men there. It's not safe for girls to go alone.

MISS Yeyeng: Oh, I didn't know that. But I really want to go.

**Mr. Peralta enters.**

MR. Peralta: Good evening, ladies. Miss Yeyeng, I heard you're planning to go to the fiesta tonight.

MISS Yeyeng: Yes, sir. I want to have some fun.

MR. Peralta: That's good to hear. But please be careful. We don't want anything bad happening to you.

MISS Yeyeng: Thank you, sir. I'll be careful.

MRS. Nunal: Mr. Peralta, can't you do anything about the men who harass women at the fiesta?

MR. Peralta: We're doing what we can. We have assigned more policemen to patrol the area. But we also need the cooperation of the people.

MRS. Nunal: What do you mean?

MR. Peralta: If you see someone being harassed, report it to the authorities immediately. And parents should warn their children about the dangers of going out alone at night.

MISS Yeyeng: Thank you for your concern, sir. I'll be careful and I'll tell my friends to be careful too.

MR. Peralta: That's good to hear. Have a good time at the fiesta.
