PROBLEM:

Aljon ran 5 3/5 kilometers. John ran 6 1/4 kilometers. How many kilomiters when the two runners cover when combined?

We're going to add two mixed numbers as their running distance.

Convert them first into improper fraction using this steps:

1 - Multiply the denominator by the whole number.

2 - Add the quotient to the numerator.

3 - The result will be the numerator and just copy the denominator.

[tex]5 \frac{3}{5} + 6 \frac{1}{4} = \frac{28}{5} + \frac{25}{4} [/tex]

Since this is addition of fraction, we need to get the LCD of both denominators.LCD of 5 and 4 is 20, that'll be the denominator of the answer.

[tex] \frac{28}{5} + \frac{25}{4} = \frac{112 + 125}{20} = \frac{237}{20} [/tex]

[tex] \frac{237}{20 } [/tex] or [tex]11 \frac{17}{20} [/tex] is the answer.

30. when do you say that a chord is a diameter?​


A chord is a diameter when it passes through the center of the circle.

About diameter

Diameter is a straight line that connects two points on a circle through the center point. Diameter length is symbolized by "d".

Diameter is one of the important elements of a circle

Circle Elements

The elements of the circle consist of:

1. Center Point (P): The point which is the center of the circle which is located right in the middle of the circle

2. Radius (r): the distance between the center of the circle and the point on the circle

3. Diameter (d): a line connecting two points on a circle through the center point

4. Circle Arc: a curved line on the edge of a circle

5. Bowstring: a line that connects two points on a circle

6. Sector of a Circle: area bounded by an arc and two radius of a circle

7. Section: the area bounded by a bow and bowstring

8. Apothem: a line connecting the center point with the chord (perpendicular to the chord)

Circumference of circle

The circumference of a circle is the longest arc on a circle. In calculating the circumference of a circle is not difficult.

We can use two ways to calculate the circumference of a circle, namely if you know the radius (r) or if you know the diameter (d). The diameter of a circle is twice the radius of the circle.

So, therefore, the formula for the circumference of a circle is:

C= π.d or C=2πr
