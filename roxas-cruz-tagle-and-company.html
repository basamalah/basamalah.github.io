1. Mr. Cruz owns half of the company and his best friend owns the other half. If Mr. Cruz would give 250,000 pesos from his share of company to his best friend, Mr. Cruz would own only one-third of the company. What is the total worth of the company?


Answer:

To find the total worth of the company, you need to determine how much Mr. Cruz owns before giving his friend 250,000 pesos and how much his friend owns.

If Mr. Cruz owns half of the company and he gives 250,000 pesos to his friend, he would own only one-third of the company. This means that the total worth of the company is equal to three times the amount that Mr. Cruz owns before giving his friend the 250,000 pesos.

To solve for the total worth of the company, you can set up the following equation:

Total worth of company = 3 * (Mr. Cruz's share before giving 250,000 pesos)

Since Mr. Cruz owns half of the company, his share before giving his friend 250,000 pesos is equal to half of the total worth of the company. You can substitute this expression into the equation above to get:

Total worth of company = 3 * (1/2 * Total worth of company)

Solving this equation gives you the total worth of the company:

Total worth of company = 750,000 pesos

So, the total worth of the company is 750,000 pesos.

Let X be the total worth of the company.

Since Mr. Cruz owns half of the company, his share is worth X/2.

After Mr. Cruz gives 250,000 pesos to his best friend, he will own only one-third of the company, so his share will be worth X/3.

The total worth of the company is equal to the sum of Mr. Cruz's share and his best friend's share, or X/2 + X/2 = X.

Therefore, X/3 = X/2 - 250,000.

Solving for X, we find that X = 750,000 pesos.

Therefore, the total worth of the company is [tex]750,000[/tex] pesos.

8. What are the weaknesses and strengths of Luis Antonio tagle?


Answer/Explanation:

During the morning of October 23, Tagle received an anonymous note with a phone number and a request to call it during the coffee break.

He did so and was told to go to the Secretariat of State, the Vatican's central administration body, at 4:50pm that day.

I immediately started wondering: Why 4:50pm? Why not five? And why are they calling me? What did I do wrong? Tagle said amid laughter from the crowd.

That afternoon the Filipino archbishop asked the president of his synod working group – Archbishop Diarmuid Martin of Dublin, who has spent many years in the Roman Curia – what could be the reason for his convocation: Maybe they are sending you to Syria, was the answer.

I immediately started thinking that I would have to change my plane ticket, Tagle said.

When he arrived at the Secretariat of State, he found Cardinal Tarcisio Bertone, Pope Benedict's 'prime minister,' waiting for him.

Bertone started reading him a letter – and Tagle recalled that it was only slowly he started realizing that he was being made a cardinal. The official announcement would come the next day, on October 24.

There were no questions. Bertone only said 'I will tell the Holy Father that you have already been informed'.

The surprise at the unexpected promotion was among the reasons that led to the tears during the Consistory of November 24.

I cry easily, even if I don't want to. I don't know if it's weakness or strength but [tears] come easily for me. I don't hide my emotions, Tagle explained.

Those were the tears of a man who knows himself, his sins and his limits, but that received a call you can't say 'no' to, but can only answer 'yes' with joy and trust. Those were tears of fear – but also of joy, Tagle added almost as an afterthought.

21. Mrs. dela Cruz invested P 100. 000.00 in a company officers 6% compounded anually define an exponental model for this investment be worth at end of each year for the next five years?​


The model to be used is the Compound Interest Model, an exponential model used when there is an interest that is compounded let it be annually, monthly, or daily.

The model:

A=P(1+r/n)^nt

Where:

A is the accumulated value

P is the initial investment

r is the interest rate

n is the number of times the interest is compounded

t is the number of days, months, or years

Let A be P100,000 as the accumulated value

Let r be 6% or 0.06 as the interest rate

Let n be 1, since the clue word there is "annually", meaning it gets compounded only once a year, and

Let t be 5 years

A=100,000(1+0.06/1)^1(5)

A=100,000(1.06)^1(5)

A=100,000(1.06)^5

A=100,000(1.338225578)

A=133,822.5578 and must be rounded off to two decimals

A=133,822.56

Hence, Mrs. dela Cruz's investment will be worth approximately P133,822.56 for the next five years.
