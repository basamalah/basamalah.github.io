19. 12. Which statement does NOT describe the windward side of a mountain?A. It has abundant life forms.B. It receives a lot of precipitation,C. It has lots of vegetation due to abundance of precipitation.D. It has less vegetation because the moist in the air is accumulated in the leewardside​


The correct answer is D. It has less vegetation because the moist in the air is accumulated in the leeward.

When air collides with a mountain, the side that it strikes first is known as the windward side. This is where the air is pumped to rise, and it is often the side of the mountain that receives the most precipitation. The opposite side of the mountain is known as the leeward side because it receives far less precipitation. The reason for this is that air is descending on the mountain's leeward side, and descending air is warmer and drier than ascending air.

Leeward and windward are technical phrases in meteorology that indicate the direction of the wind in relation to a certain point of reference. These points of reference can include ships at sea, islands, buildings, and, as this article will discuss, mountains. The windward side of an island faces the prevailing, or trade, winds, whereas the leeward side faces away from the wind, protected from prevailing winds by hills and mountains. Trade winds gather up moist air from the ocean as they sweep over it. One exception to the concepts of windward and leeward is that while most snow falls on the windward side, powder can still be found on the top leeward slopes of mountains because wind can drift snowflakes from the windward side to the leeward side.
