3. What Ilocano culture/practices/traditions do you know of? Will you share at least two? plss paki-answer ​


Answer:

There are a lot of Ilocano culture/practices/traditions that has been going around since then and until now. However, these are some of the Ilocano practices that I came to see as I grow up.

First:  Dayu-Dayu, Bari-Bari

Dayu-Dayu, Bari-Bari basically is a culture that is said to excuse yourself to beings that are not residing as humans in this world. Ilocanos say this, too, whenever they are going to places that they are unfamilair with (bantay or bukid), and when they are pouring out hot water from their thermos to warn those unerthly beings getting hurt.

Second: Atang (Offering)

Ilocanos making Atang is also a tradition that is believed to cast away evil spirits that is lurking with them whenever there are celebrations. Giving offering or atang is a way used to avoid getting sick because of those unearthly beings.

Third: Putting a Knife, Garlic and Salt near the window of a pregnant's room.

Ilocanos strongly believe on the exitence of aswang (witches) and mannamay (sorcerers). So, as a precautionary measure, they put garlic to be used as protection from Aswang ()witches) and Knife against Mannamays (sorcerers)

7. Ano ang evolution ng tradition ng Ilocano? Compare and Contrast the tradition of Ilocano before and now


Answer:

Ang evolution ng tradition ng Ilocano ay nagbago mula sa kanilang mga sinaunang paniniwala at gawi hanggang sa kasalukuyang panahon. Sa nakaraang panahon, ang mga Ilocano ay mayroong mga tradisyunal na paniniwala tulad ng pagpapakasal sa murang edad, pagtitiwala sa mga mangkukulam at albularyo, at pagpapakain sa mga patay. Ngunit sa kasalukuyan, mayroon nang mga pagbabago sa kanilang mga paniniwala at gawi.

Isa sa mga nabago ay ang kanilang paniniwala sa pagpapakasal. Sa kasalukuyan, mas pinapahalagahan ng mga Ilocano ang edukasyon kaysa sa pagpapakasal sa murang edad. Mas pinapahalagahan na rin ng mga Ilocano ang pagpapakain sa mga bata at hindi na sa mga patay.

Sa kasalukuyan, mas nagiging moderno na rin ang mga Ilocano sa kanilang mga gawi. Halimbawa, sa pagdiriwang ng kanilang mga tradisyunal na fiesta, mas nagiging moderno na rin ang mga aktibidad na kanilang ginagawa. Mayroon nang mga palaro, mga parada, at mga kompetisyon na ginagawa sa mga fiesta.

Sa kabuuan, ang evolution ng tradition ng Ilocano ay nagpapakita ng pagbabago sa kanilang mga paniniwala at gawi mula sa kanilang mga sinaunang paniniwala at gawi hanggang sa kasalukuyan.

10. What makes ilocano culture interesting


Answer:

Ilocano, also spelled Ilokano, or Ilokan, also called Iloko, or Iloco, third largest ethnolinguistic group in the Philippines. When discovered by the Spanish in the 16th century, they occupied the narrow coastal plain of northwestern Luzon, known as the Ilocos region. The growth of their population later led to much migration to neighbouring provinces, to the southern Philippine island of Mindanao, and to Hawaii. Their language is closely related to others of northern Luzon, all of which belong to the Austronesian (Malayo-Polynesian) family of languages. In the early 21st century the Ilocano (Iloko) language was spoken by roughly 10 million people.

The Ilocanos are known for being hardworking, appreciative, simple and determined. However, they are also known as stingy or “kuripot”. Of course, the Ilocanos being called stingy and cheap is just treated as a joke or pun among Filipinos and not as a negative regional stereotype. It is a regional characteristic that their Filipino brethren have grown to understand.

The body is placed in a coffin in the middle of the house parallel to the slats of the floor.
