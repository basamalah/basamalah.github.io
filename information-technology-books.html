5. Write the best summary you can give in this article. On this age of technology, sources of information include both print and non-print materials. You have the choice which to use, so you should know how these materials are different from one another. Print materials refer to all paper publications in printed form. Examples of these are published books, journals, magazines, newspaper, dictionaries and encyclopedias all these you can hold and grab. You can scan through their pages and read the contents conveniently. It is said that they are reliable and affordable for most people who valued these resources. On the other hand, non-print materials are those that have special formats. The information they give only can be accessed or reached by using machines or digital equipment such as computers, tablets and cellular phones. Examples of these materials are e-books, e-journals, e-photographs, compact disc, records, slides, videos and films. These can relay information faster.


Answer:

This article explains the difference between print and non-print materials as sources of information. Print materials are physical publications such as books, magazines, and newspapers that can be held and read at one's convenience. They are considered reliable and affordable. Non-print materials, on the other hand, are accessed through digital equipment such as computers, tablets, and phones. Examples of non-print materials include e-books, videos, and photographs. Non-print materials are faster in relaying information but require machines to access them.
