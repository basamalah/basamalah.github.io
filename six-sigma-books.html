1. what is six sigma and give the characteristics of six sigma​


Answer:

Six Sigma is a disciplined and in demand method, which uses statistical based data driven approach and also a continuous improvement methodology for eliminating defects in a product, process or service

Along with being process and data driven, the whole Six Sigma philosophy is about being consistent. It is about sustained intensity rather than short bursts of power. Six Sigma focuses on developing the capability to deliver the same results over and over again with zero variance rather than delivering it once.

2. How do Six sigma and Lean work together and is Lean Six Sigma and six sigma work? (please help)​


Answer:

Lean Six Sigma combines the strategies of Lean and Six Sigma. Lean principles help to reduce or eliminate process wastes. Six Sigma focuses on variation - reduction in the process. Thereby, the principles of Lean Six Sigma helps to improve the efficiency and quality of the process.

27. Suppose that your business is operating at the 4.5 sigma quality level. if projects have an average improvement rate of 50 annually, how many years will it take to achieve six-sigma quality?


Needs 11.75 years or 11 years 9 months to achieve six-sigma quality.

Explanation :

If we ignore the fact that "Six-sigma" quality means the error rate corresponds to about -4.5σ (3.4 ppm) then we will use the reference ...

P(z ≤ -6) ≈ 9.86588×10^-10

and

P(z ≤ -4.5) ≈ 3.39767×10^-6

this error rate ratio is about 0.000290372.

In the next step we will multiply the error rate by 0.5 (representing each year), so as to calculate the result to the power of 0.50 which gives this value:

0.50^t = 0.000290372

t•log(0.50) = log(0.00290372) . . . . take logarithms

t = log(0.000290372)/log(0.50) = -3.537045/-0.301030

t = 11.75

That is, it takes about 11.75 years or 11 years and 9 months to reach Six Sigma quality (error rate 0.99 ppb).
