9. Appetizer Salad essay​


Answer:

An salad appetizer is a small dish served before a meal in European cuisine. Some hors d'oeuvres are served cold, others hot.[4] Hors d'oeuvres may be served at the dinner table as a part of the meal, or they may be served before seating, such as at a reception or cocktail party. Formerly, hors d'oeuvres were also served between courses. There are two types of hors d'oeuvre from service point of view:

General hors d'oeuvreClassical hors d'oeuvre

General hors d'oeuvres include cold preparations such as salad, cold meat, and fish. Classical hors d'oeuvres include fruit juice and soft drinks, grapefruit, shellfish cocktail, and so on.[6]

Typically smaller than a main dish, an hors d'oeuvre is often designed to be eaten by hand.

21. what are the defferences and similarities between appetizer and appetizer salad?​


Answer:

An appetizer is a small dish served before the main course of a meal. It is typically savory and designed to stimulate the appetite. Examples of appetizers include shrimp cocktail, bruschetta, cheese platters, and dips.

An appetizer salad, on the other hand, is a type of appetizer that includes greens and other vegetables as its primary ingredients. It is usually dressed with vinaigrette or other light dressings and may also include proteins such as chicken, shrimp, or tofu. Examples of appetizer salads include Caesar salad, Greek salad, and spinach salad.

In terms of similarities, both appetizers and appetizer salads are served before the main course of a meal and are intended to whet the appetite. They can also be used to showcase the chef's creativity and culinary skills.

One main difference between the two is that appetizers can take many forms, while appetizer salads are typically composed of greens and vegetables. Another difference is that appetizers may be hot or cold, while appetizer salads are typically served cold.

Overall, the main similarity between appetizers and appetizer salads is that they are both meant to be light and refreshing dishes that prepare the palate for the main course of the meal.
