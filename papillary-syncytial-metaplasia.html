Answer:

The upper, papillary layer, contains a thin arrangement of collagen fibers. The papillary layer supplies nutrients to select layers of the epidermis and regulates temperature. Both of these functions are accomplished with a thin, extensive vascular system that operates similarly to other vascular systems in the body.

8. Why is it necessary to stimulate papillary muscles in the ventricle slightly earlier than the rest of the ventricular wall myocardium?


Answer: It is necessary to stimulate papillary muscles in the ventricles slightly earlier than the rest of the ventricular wall myocardium because contraction of the papillary muscles exerts tension on the AV valve flaps through the chordae tendineae, preventing valve prolapse and subsequent backflow of blood into the atria.
