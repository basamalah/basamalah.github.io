In Geology and Minerology, Minerals are a Solid Chemical Compound with a fairly, well defined chemical composition and a specific crystal structure that occurs naturally in pure form.

The International Mineralogical Administration has established the following requirements for a substance to be considered a distinct Mineral:

1.) It must be a natural occuring substance formed by natural geological processes

2.) It must be a solid substance in its natural occurence

3.) It must have a well-defined crystallographic structure

4.) It must have a fairly, well-defined chemical composition
