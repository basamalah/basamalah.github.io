3. levinas idea on otherness​


Answer:

The face-to-face relation (French: rapport de face à face) is a concept in the French philosopher Emmanuel Lévinas' thought on human sociality. 23. Do you agree to Levinas that it is our duty to show concern to “others”? Why or why not? ​


I agree. Because In the struggle of life in this world, humans do not live alone. Will always with other people. Like it or not, the existence of other people takes part in shaping the process of human reflection in living their existence together with other people in the world.

About Emmanuel Levinas

Emmanuel Levinas was born on January 12, 1906 in Kovno (Kaunas), Lithuania. During the First World War, the Levinas family fled to Kharkov, Ukraine. In that city Levinas entered the Russian gymnamsium. After the first world war ended, the Levinas family returned to Lithuania. In 1923, Levinas studied at the University of Strasbourg, France. After completing his studies at the University of Strasbourg, Levinas married Raissa Levi. Then Levinas continued his career by teaching at the Alliance Israelite Universalle. Levinas died on December 25, 1995 after a long illness.

It should be noted that in the history of western philosophy, the ego has the highest priority. This results in blurring and the loss of important aspects of the others. The priority emphasis on the ego gives rise to the view that the other is part of the ego. Others are unknown and unnoticed. Therefore, others reveal themselves and appear in the form of a bare face. Related to this, a bare face knocks on a person's heart to be kind to him. Others are described as strangers, widows, orphans, poor, weak, and helpless.

Levinas states that the ethical transcendent relationship pattern occurs when a person is disturbed and touched by the presence of another. In encounters with others, one feels that one's freedom and comfort are questioned and interrupted. This is referred to as an encounter with a bare face. Through these meetings, one person responds, behaves respectfully, and feels responsible for the existence of the other.

Levinas sees responsibility for the existence of others as the basis of social justice. Because Levinas believes that every meeting with others creates an ethical space. Related to this, the ethical space presupposes a transcendent relationship pattern. Patterns of relationships that value, respect, acknowledge, and uphold the existence of others. There is a sense of belonging and responsibility for the existence and life of others.
