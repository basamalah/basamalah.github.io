Step-by-step explanation:

Problem 1:

A car travels from City A to City B, a distance of 120 km, at a speed of 60 km/h. It then returns from City B to City A at a speed of 80 km/h. What is the average speed of the car for the entire journey?

Solution:

We can use the formula: average speed = total distance / total time.

Let's first find the time it takes for the car to travel from City A to City B:

time = distance / speed = 120 / 60 = 2 hours

Now let's find the time it takes for the car to return from City B to City A:

time = distance / speed = 120 / 80 = 1.5 hours

The total distance is 240 km (120 km each way), and the total time is 2 + 1.5 = 3.5 hours.

Therefore, the average speed is:

average speed = total distance / total time = 240 / 3.5 = 68.57 km/h (rounded to two decimal places).

Answer: The average speed of the car for the entire journey is 68.57 km/h.

Problem 2:

A boat travels upstream on a river at a speed of 10 km/h. On the return journey downstream, the boat travels at a speed of 20 km/h. If the distance of the round trip is 60 km, how long does it take for the boat to complete the journey?

Solution:

Let's first find the speed of the river by using the formula: speed of the river = (speed downstream - speed upstream) / 2

speed of the river = (20 - 10) / 2 = 5 km/h

Now we can find the time it takes for the boat to travel upstream and downstream:

time upstream = distance / speed upstream = 60 / 10 = 6 hours

time downstream = distance / speed downstream = 60 / 20 = 3 hours

The total time for the round trip is 6 + 3 = 9 hours.

Answer: It takes the boat 9 hours to complete the round trip.

Problem 3:

A cyclist travels at a speed of 15 km/h. He starts at point A and cycles towards point B. At the same time, another cyclist starts from point B and cycles towards point A at a speed of 20 km/h. If the distance between point A and point B is 150 km, how long will it take for the two cyclists to meet?

Solution:

Let's first find the combined speed of the two cyclists: combined speed = speed of cyclist A + speed of cyclist B

combined speed = 15 + 20 = 35 km/h

Now we can use the formula: time = distance / speed

time = 150 / 35 = 4.29 hours (rounded to two decimal places)

Answer: It will take the two cyclists 4.29 hours to meet

Answer:

Two vehicles are in motion. Vehicle A is 500kg moving at 10m/s. Vehicle B is 1000kg moving at 5m/s. Which vehicle has greater kinetic energy?​

Given:

Vehicle A:

mass, ma = 500kg

velocity, va = 10m/s

Vehicle B:

mass, mb = 1000kg

velocity, vb = 5m/s

Required:
Kinetic Energy, KE = ?

Equation:
KE = 1/2 mv^2

Solution:

Vehicle A:

KEa = 1/2 (500 kg)(10 m/s)^2

KEa = 25,000 Joules

Vehicle B:

KEb = 1/2 (1000 kg)(5 m/s)^2

KEb = 12,500 Joules

Vehicle A has greater kinetic energy.

14. What does the Second Law of Motion state? Write down two word problems with answers and solutions using this law.


Answer:

SIMPLE

Newton's first law of motion predicts the behavior of objects for which all existing forces are balanced. The first law - sometimes referred to as the law of inertia - states that if the forces acting upon an object are balanced, then the acceleration of that object will be 0 m/s/s. Objects at equilibrium (the condition in which all forces balance) will not accelerate. According to Newton, an object will only accelerate if there is a net or unbalanced force acting upon it. The presence of an unbalanced force will accelerate an object - changing its speed, its direction, or both its speed and direction.

 

Newton's second law of motion pertains to the behavior of objects for which all existing forces are not balanced. The second law states that the acceleration of an object is dependent upon two variables - the net force acting upon the object and the mass of the object. The acceleration of an object depends directly upon the net force acting upon the object, and inversely upon the mass of the object. As the force acting upon an object is increased, the acceleration of the object is increased. As the mass of an object is increased, the acceleration of the object is decreased.
