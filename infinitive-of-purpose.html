28. Modals are always followed with a bare infinitive — an infinitive without "to".​


Answer:

Can infinitive verb be without TO?

In some cases we use the infinitive without to.The infinitive is used without to after modal auxiliary verbs will, shall, would, should, can, could, may, might and must. It might rain later in the evening. (NOT It might to rain later in the evening.)

What is to-infinitive and bare infinitive?

The infinitive without 'to' is called the bare infinitive, or the base form of a verb.The infinitive with to is called the full infinitive, or the to-infinitive
