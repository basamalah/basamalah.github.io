25. Differences in terms of elements of doodle fiction, Manga, graphic novel​


The elements of doodle fictions:

The theme of this type is its fictional nature. Usually the results of this type of doodle art are in the form of unreal characters that exist in the imagination of the creator. A fictional doodle is a fictional drawing or doodle that is painted abstractly (although sometimes has a certain pattern), unfocused, and spontaneously created (later it has been developed in a planned way). But even so, fictional doodles have their own beauty value.

The Elements of Manga:

Manga has many constituent elements. These elements are actually the basic ingredients of manga and are the hallmark of manga compared to other media. The elements in the manga are as follows:

1. Panel

Panels are boxes containing illustrations and text that will later form a storyline. In general, panels are square and have a reading order from left to right, top to bottom, or clockwise.

2. Trench

Trench refers to the space between the panels which is usually in the form of a white dividing line. However, now the shape of the trench is different depending on the creativity of the manga artist. The trench serves to unite the imaginative boxes of separate panels. Therefore, the party can be said to be the heart of a manga.

3. Word Balloons

Word balloons are speech representations or narratives of events that are happening in a panel. Word balloons consist of three forms, namely speech balloons, thought balloons, and captions. Speech balloons are representations of dialogue utterances in the form of thick circles with their tails pointing towards the character who said the words. While the thought balloon is a representation of the mind of the character, but only in the mind and not spoken. Then, captions refer to narrative explanations instead of dialogues from the illustrations in the panel which are usually presented in the form of boxes.

4. Illustration or Image

Illustrations are used to represent story scenes in detail and intact. According to the type, illustrations are divided into two, namely realist and cartoon. A realist image is an icon that most closely resembles the real person or object. While cartoons refer to abstract and simple shapes so that they are further away from the original photo story.

5. Story

Stories are one of the basic strengths in comics other than illustrations. Comics can choose a variety of themes ranging from superhero stories, martial arts, puppets, and others.

27. Differences in terms of element doodle fiction, graphic novel,manga


Answer:

Much as Supreme, Pepperoni and Cheese are all kinds of Pizza, comics (I know you didn’t specifically ask), manga, and doodle fiction are all kinds of Graphic Novels.

Graphic Novel is also American shorthand for a collection of single issue comics, or a longer American-comic style work, so this can get confusing.

Manga is drawn in a heavily stylized way and usually oriented for reading left to right as opposed to the American right to left way of reading.

Doodle fiction seems to be closer to a traditional novel, but takes on a severely hand-drawn aesthetic to its art and lettering, whereas the other two begin with hand-drawn art and then use shortcuts such as tone and digital editing to finish the job. While I have no doubt that Doodle Fiction does this as well, American Comics and Manga seem to use them more heavily, opting for a more refined look.

Some people will tell you that Manga is purely Japanese, made by Japanese authors for a Japanese audience. That’s poppycock. When I ask them “Well, if a Japanese artist draws Batman in an American style, oriented left to right for reading, is the 20 page full color comic he made then Manga instead?” and their argument crumbles away.

Likewise, comics can be drawn in Japan or America or anywhere on earth, same with Manga, so don’t let people define it by where you happen to put pen to paper, that’s inane.

So long as both Americans and Italians can make Pizza, so long as Americans and Germans can both brew beer, so to can anyone on earth make Manga, Comics, and Doodle Fiction.

So while Manga and Comic art drift closer to one another steadily as time goes by, I would say it all comes down to: Eastern style art vs Western style art, how the book is laid out to be read, who is publishing said book, and most importantly: what the author classifies their creation as.
