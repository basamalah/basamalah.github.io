6. farmlands are converted into housing projects what effects do these problem create on the ecosystem


In this phase of urbanization, a lot of farmlands are being converted into housing projects and are also used for commercialization. It is beneficial to build more houses so people can have place to live in, and the problem of illegal settlement is resolved. But these activities also have disadvantages to our ecosystem.

 

With a lot of farmlands being converted into housing projects, people can have a place to live in but on the other hand, the animals natural habitat are greatly affected. Also, one of the bad effects of these urbanization can manifest in the natural air that we get from the atmosphere. Metro Manila is one of the best example of air pollution since you can rarely see trees, plants and even farmlands, hence, no more fresh air that you can breathe in. And most of our provinces are also coping up with this urbanization stage, that’s why our ecosystem is being threatened.

10. What Are the cause And Effect farmland are Converted into housing project?


The cause of converting a farmland into a residential zone, particularly into a housing project is due to the continually increasing population or just a mere commercialization of a vacant farmland. The result or the effect of this conversion is the loss of tillable farmland and loss of income on the end of the farmers.
