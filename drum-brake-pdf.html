10. Explain the difference between Drum Brake type and Disc Brake type in terms of Performance, Economical Value and most importantly Safety.ill report nonsense answer ​


Answer:

THE DIFFERENCE OF DRUM BRAKE AND DISC BRAKE

So which brake type is better? As with most things in life, the answer is rarely clear-cut. Drum brakes have some major design flaws: they overheat too quickly, take longer to dry off, and are typically heavier than disc brakes.

At the same time, disc brakes cannot be used as a parking brake because they expand when hot and contract when cold. If we relied on them for a parking brake after using them, they would eventually cool off, shrink, and lose contact with the brake disc. Obviously we’d have a problem here.

The two brakes are just different. Disc brakes are the more effective and reliable choice, but they have their limitations. Drum brakes are not very practical, but they are crucial to parking a car – unless of course you’d like to go back to wooden blocks on sticks.

Therefore, drum brakes are often still found in modern cars. Manufacturers will usually outfit the front wheels with disc brakes since they have to work the hardest, and drum brakes in the rear. Some sports cars will use disc brakes on all four wheels, but have one additional drum brake for parking purposes.

30. Applying the brakes on the car causes it to slow down because the brakes


Answer:

When you apply the brakes on a car, the brake pads or shoes press against the rotating brake rotor or drum, which creates friction and slows down the car. This happens due to the conversion of kinetic energy (the energy of motion) into thermal energy (heat) through the process of friction.

The brake system of a car typically includes a brake pedal, a master cylinder, brake lines, brake calipers, and brake pads or shoes. When you press the brake pedal, it pushes a piston in the master cylinder, which pressurizes the brake fluid. This pressure is transmitted through the brake lines to the brake calipers or wheel cylinders, which then apply pressure to the brake pads or shoes, creating friction against the brake rotor or drum and causing the car to slow down or stop.

In summary, the application of brakes on a car slows it down by converting kinetic energy into heat energy through the process of friction.
