18. the rate of the flow of water through a pipe is 1/3 varies directly with the square of the radius of the pipe which is 30. what is the rate of the flow of water through a pipe if the radius of the pipe is 12?​


Answer:

So the rate of flow when the radius is 12 is 8/45.

Step-by-step explanation:

We can use the formula: rate of flow = k * (radius^2), where k is a constant of proportionality.

Given that the rate of flow is 1/3 when the radius is 30, we can write:

1/3 = k * (30^2)

Solving for k, we get:

k = 1/3 / (30^2)

Now, we can find the rate of flow when the radius is 12:

rate of flow = k * (12^2) = 1/3 / (30^2) * (12^2) = 144 / 900 = 8/45
