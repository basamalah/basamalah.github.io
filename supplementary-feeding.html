13. Corresponding angles are Supplementary or Complementary? Give meaning of Supplementary and Complementary? ​


Answer:

Corresponding angles can be supplementary if the transversal intersects two parallel lines perpendicularly (i.e. at 90 degrees). In such case, each of the corresponding angles will be 90 degrees and their sum will add up to 180 degrees (i.e. supplementary).

Two angles are said to be complementary if they add up to 90 degrees. Two angles are said to be supplementary if they add up to 180 degrees.

20. An angle that is supplementary to one of two congruent angles is supplementary to the other also


Answer:

The statement stated above is TRUE.

Step-by-step explanation:

Congruent angles are angles that are of the same measure, even though they are not facing the same direction. Two angles of different measure can be joined together to make either a complementary (two angles summing up to 90 degrees) or supplementary (two angles summing up to 180 degrees) angles. The supplementary or complementary of one of the two congruent angles is also the supplementary or the complementary of the other congruent angle. The measure of the supplementary or complementary angle of the congruent angles is just the same.
