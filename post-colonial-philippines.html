17. Learning Tasks 4: What are the Philippine Contemporary Art Forms? Define each in your own words based on the timeline presented. PHILIPPINE ART PERIOD TIMELINE Pre-Colonial Art Period Spanish Colonial Art Period Post War Colonial Art Period American Colonial Art Period Contemporary Art Period ​


Answer:

There are five Philippine contemporary art forms: Pre-Colonial, Spanish Colonial, Post War Colonial, American Colonial, and Contemporary. Each art form has its own unique features and characteristics.

Pre-Colonial art is the earliest form of Philippine art. It is characterized by simple designs and geometric shapes. The most common pre-colonial art forms are pottery and weaving.

Spanish Colonial art is more ornate and elaborate than pre-colonial art. It is often characterized by religious imagery and themes. Common Spanish Colonial art forms include painting, sculpture, and architecture.

Post War Colonial art emerged after the Philippines gained independence from Spain in 1898. This art form is a mix of pre-colonial, Spanish colonial, and American influences. The most common post war colonial art forms are painting and sculpture.

American Colonial art emerged after the United States acquired the Philippines from Spain in 1898. This art form is characterized by realistic depictions of everyday life. Common American Colonial art forms include painting, photography, and architecture.

Contemporary art is the most recent form of Philippine art. It is characterized by abstract or experimental designs. The most common contemporary art forms are installations, performance arts, and digital arts.PA BRAINLY THANKS

19. what defines pre-colonial, colonial, post-colonial or contemporary literature?​


Post colonialism literature is the literature by people from formerly colonized countries.[1] It exists on all continents except Antarctica. Postcolonial literature often addresses the problems and consequences of the decolonization of a country, especially questions relating to the political and cultural independence of formerly subjugated people, and themes such as racialism and colonialism.[2] A range of literary theory has evolved around the subject. It addresses the role of literature in perpetuating and challenging what postcolonial critic Edward Said refers to as cultural imperialism.[3]

29. during post colonial examples in Philippines literature ​


The postcolonial era in the Philippines is generally considered to have begun after the country gained independence from the United States in 1946. During this period, Filipino writers and artists sought to assert their cultural identity and express their experiences of colonialism, nationalism, and social change through literature and other forms of art.

Here are some examples of postcolonial literature in the Philippines:

1.)"Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo" by Jose Rizal - These two novels are considered to be among the most important works of Filipino literature. They were written by Rizal, a Filipino nationalist and reformist, during the late 19th century under Spanish colonial rule. The novels exposed the injustices and abuses of the Spanish colonial system and called for social reform.

2.)"Po-on" by F. Sionil Jose - This novel tells the story of a Filipino family during the early 1900s, a time of great social and political change. It explores themes such as identity, poverty, and the struggle for independence.

3.)"Dogeaters" by Jessica Hagedorn - This novel is set during the Marcos dictatorship of the 1980s and explores the lives of various characters in Manila's elite society. It critiques the corruption and violence of the regime and highlights the ways in which the country's colonial history has shaped its present.

4.)"Smaller and Smaller Circles" by F.H. Batacan - This crime novel is set in Manila and follows two Jesuit priests as they investigate a series of murders. The novel explores the social and political realities of contemporary Philippines and critiques the country's justice system and class divisions.

These are just a few examples of the many works of literature that emerged during the postcolonial era in the Philippines. They reflect the country's complex history and the ways in which writers and artists sought to understand and articulate their experiences.
