2. The first computer virus in the philippines is?


The ILOVEYOU virus or love bug is the first computer virus in the Philippines that is sent as an attachment in an email with the word "ILOVEYOU" in the subject line. When opened, the attachment causes the message to be sent again to everyone in the recipient's Microsoft Outlook contact book. More gravely, all JPEG, MP3, and specific other files on the hard drives of all receivers are lost.

The ILOVEYOU virus can spread quickly within a company because Microsoft Outlook is frequently set up as the default email management tool on business networks around the world. In actuality, on May 4, 2000, this is exactly what took place. ILOVEYOU reportedly gained 45 million users in just around 10 days and cost the economy $10 billion in losses.

Although widely referred to as a computer virus, ILOVEYOU is actually a worm. It goes by the names "love letter virus" and "love bug worm." A worm is a sort of malware that can duplicate itself and spread from system to system without human interaction or intervention, in contrast to a virus, which is malicious code that replicates after human intervention. It doesn't even have to connect to any software. Email, and more specifically a malicious email attachment, is how ILOVEYOU operates. The moment the victim opens the attachment, the worm is silently downloaded into their system and begins to propagate over the network.

When a recipient opened their email, the virus sent copies of itself to everyone in their address book. The email had the subject line "ILOVEYOU" and the straightforward message "kindly check the attached LOVELETTER coming from me." These recipients clicked on the email out of curiosity, thinking it was a sincere love letter or an amusing joke, and unintentionally assisted in its propagation.

3. What is the first computer in the philippines?


The first computer in the philippines is in the first generation (1940-1959), during World War II.

EXPLANATION:

When World War II took place, the countries involved tried to develop computers to exploit the potential of computers. In 1941, a German engineer, Conrad Zuse, created a computer called Z3, which serves to design an airplane and missiles. Two years later, in 1943, England succeeded in completing a secret code-breaking computer called the Colossus Computer. The Colossus computer functioned to decode a secret code that was then used by Nazi Germany.

Unfortunately, the existence of the Colossus computer did not really affect computer development, because Colossus was not a versatile computer, but was only designed to decode a secret code. The first computer from the Philippines began to appear in 1946, which is called the first generation of computers. The first generation of computers was created using vacuum tubes as a basic component of manufacture. These vacuum tubes are known to be inefficient in several aspects because they get hot very easily when in use.

This is caused by the vacuum tube which requires very large electrical power to operate. One example of the first generation of computers or also called the first computer in the world is the Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer (ENIAC).

4. "what is the first computer virus in the philippines"​


The first computer virus in the Philippines was ILOVEYOU.

More detailed explanation:

A computer virus is a computer program that can replicate or copy itself and spread by inserting itself into other programs or documents. Computer viruses according to the biology of viruses that spread embedded in living cells. Computer viruses can be destructive (such as corrupting data in documents) can be annoying to computer users, or have no effect at all.

The ILOVEYOU virus was first detected on May 4, 2000. Onel de Guzman and Reomel Ramones are Filipinos, the brains of this shocking virus. In early 2000, the ILoveYou virus attacked 45 million people in just one day. In addition, ILoveYou caused losses of up to $5.5 billion. This is an impressive amount of damage caused by the virus. This virus is spread via email with the subject ILOVE YOU and followed by the attachment Love-Letter-For-You.TXT.vbs. When the user opens the "ILOVEYOU" link, the virus automatically attacks JPEG, MP3, and other data receiving formats. Messages are also sent directly to all Microsoft Outlook recipient contacts.

In fact, at that time Microsoft Outlook was being used by companies all over the world, so it is undeniable that this virus spread quickly. Worse, all information about e-mail taken from the computer's address book, such as usernames and passwords will be sent to the address of the author of the virus.  

Before the ILOVEYOU virus, there was the Melissa virus to be precise in 1999. The Melissa Virus attacked through documents sent via email. Messages like "This is the document you requested, don't show it to anyone else." Once the link is activated this virus will replicate itself automatically and send the same message to 50 recipients' email contacts. But this virus is not made by Filipinos.
