1. The lateral area of a right circular cone is 386 square centimeters. If the diameter is one-half its altitude, determine the altitude in centimeters.


In mathematics, solving word problem required knowledge and skills in basic mathematics that will improve your analytical and critical thinking skills.

In the given problem, you need to apply your knowledge and skills in alegbra and basic math specially in solving word problem related on geometry.

Given:

lateral area of a right circular cone is 386 square centimeters.

Diameter is one-half its altitude

Problem

Determine the altitude in centimeters.

Solution

A = πrl

where

π is a constant (3.14)

r is radius

l is a slant height

Remember: Altitude (h) is different from slant height (l)

Based from phytagorean theorem.

c² = a² + b² → l² = r² + h²

d = 2r

d = ½h → r = ¼h

π = 3.14

A = πrl

386 = 3.14(¼h)[√(¼h)² + h²)]

Then simplify.

386 = 3.14(¼h)[√(¼h)² + h²)]

1544 = 3.14(h)[√(5/4h²)]

1544 = 3.14(h)(1.12h)

1544 = 3.52h²

h² = 438.64

h = √438.64

h = ± 20.94

Use the positive one, since there is no negative dimension.

Answer: Altitude is 20.94 cm.

Answer:

Acetyl-CoA (acetyl coenzyme A) is a molecule that participates in many biochemical reactions in protein, carbohydrate and lipid metabolism. Its main function is to deliver the acetyl group to the citric acid cycle (Krebs cycle) to be oxidized for energy production.

As a result, acetyl-CoA is generated in the mitochondria for oxidation or other possible fates. In the liver, mitochondrial acetyl-CoA is used to synthesize ketone bodies (acetoacetate and β-hydroxybutyrate) as alternative fuel sources for the brain and heart under conditions of carbohydrate scarcity.

August 20, 2021

HISTORY OF COA

The Commision on Audit (COA) has been a long-standing institution in the Philippines. Its roots date back to the Spanish Era of the Philippines where the books of accounts of our country are being audited by representatives of the Spanish crown. During the American colonial period, a similar function was done by the Office of the Auditor of the Philippine Islands, as established by then President William Mckinley. Later on, the 1936 Constitution of the Philippines established the General Auditing Office or GAO to perform the audit function of the Government. During 1972, GAO, under Martial Law, was renamed to become the Commission on Audit (COA). The biggest change in the audit function of the government was the change from a single Auditor General, to a three-man collegial Commission on Audit. Years later, the EDSA Revolution ensued. This signaled the move of our country from a dictatorship to the establishment of a democratic country. This also signaled the move of the Commission on Audit to a new government.

29. Is COA plugging a propaganda or not?why?​


Answer:

No, I do not believe that the Commission on Audit is pushing any type of propaganda. There is no reason for the government commission to do such a thing. It was instituted to provide a system of checks and balances of the Philippine government.

First of all, it is an independent commission. This means that it does not fall under the three branches of the government: executive, judiciary, and legislative. There is a good reason for this. As the team tasked to audit, examine, and settle the funds of the government, it has to be separate from all of the branches to do its job without any ulterior motive.

It does not matter who the current people in power are. The duty of the commission is to check where the money of the taxpayers go. These funds are meant to be used for the development of the country. Most of the time, the biggest allocations go to the Department of Public Works and Highways, the Department of Education, and the Department of the Interior and Local Government.

Unfortunately, the Philippine government is infamous for how plagued it is by corruption on all levels. If there is no one to keep all the branches accountable, there is no doubt that the funds will be channeled into the hands of nefarious entities and individuals.

According to the law, every single government branch and agency must undergo this assessment each year. Transparency is something that every person and agency in power should have. This is the only way to win the trust of the constituents. On top of that, the taxpayers are the ones who contribute to these funds. It is their right to know what the government is doing with their money.

Commission on Audit members have meetings with the representatives of government agencies. If the said representative can explain the anomalies discovered by the auditors, these issues will not appear in the annual audit report. However, failure to properly explain these problems is a sign of corruption.

There is no reason for any government agency to be afraid of the Commission on Audit as long as it is not hiding anything. The Constitution established the creation of the commission to ensure that the money is used for what it is allocated for. The commission is simply doing its job, as it always does year after year. If the current administration is scared, maybe this is because they are using the funds improperly.
