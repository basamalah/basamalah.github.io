Functions

1. To grant parole to qualified prisoners;

2. To recommend to the President the grant of pardon and other forms of executive clemency;

3. To authorize the transfer of residence of parolees and pardonees, order their arrest and recommitment, or grant their final release and discharge.

Basis for Grant of Parole

1. The prisoner is fit to be released;

2. There is a reasonable probability that, if released, he or she will live and remain at liberty without violating the law; and

3. His or her release will not be incompatible with the welfare of society.

25. 3. Discuss the different correctional institutions in the.​


Answer:

Correctional institutions are facilities that are used to house individuals who have been convicted of a crime. These institutions can range from minimum security prisons to maximum security prisons, and they can also include juvenile detention centers, halfway houses, and boot camps.

Minimum Security Prisons: These prisons are typically for inmates who have committed less serious offenses and pose a low risk of escape or violence. Inmates in these facilities usually have more freedom than those in higher security prisons, such as the ability to work outside the prison walls or participate in educational programs.

Medium Security Prisons: These prisons are for inmates who have committed more serious offenses and pose a moderate risk of escape or violence. Inmates in these facilities typically have fewer freedoms than those in minimum security prisons, but more than those in maximum security prisons.

Maximum Security Prisons: These prisons are for inmates who have committed the most serious offenses and pose a high risk of escape or violence. Inmates in these facilities typically have very limited freedoms and are closely monitored at all times.

Juvenile Detention Centers: These centers are for juveniles who have been accused or convicted of a crime. Juveniles in these centers typically receive educational services as well as counseling and other rehabilitative services.

Halfway Houses: These houses provide temporary housing for individuals who have recently been released from prison but still need assistance transitioning back into society. Halfway houses typically provide job training, counseling, and other services to help individuals reintegrate into society successfully.

Boot Camps: Boot camps are short-term correctional programs that focus on discipline and physical activity as a means of rehabilitation for juvenile offenders. Inmates at boot camps typically receive military-style training and must adhere to strict rules and regulations while participating in the program.
