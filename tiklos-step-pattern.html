6. What are the basic steps of Tiklos?​


The basic steps of Tiklos are as follows:

Stand with your feet together, arms at your sides.Take a step to the left with your left foot, bending your knees slightly.Move your right foot to the left, and then move your left foot to the right.Repeat the steps, alternating your feet and moving in a circular motion.

Answer:

Tiklos is a lively folk dance from the Philippines that is commonly performed at festivals and social gatherings. The basic steps of Tiklos are:

Start by standing in a circle with your hands on the shoulders of the people next to you. The circle should be facing inwards.

The dance starts with a slow step to the right, followed by a quick hop on the right foot. This step is then repeated to the left, followed by a hop on the left foot.

After the initial steps, the dancers will then start to move in a counter-clockwise direction around the circle, continuing to do the same basic step of slow step and quick hop to the right, then to the left.

As the music and tempo increase, the dancers may add variations to the basic step, such as stamping their feet or clapping their hands.

The dance may also include partner interactions, such as hand holding or arm swinging, as well as more intricate footwork and patterns.

The dance ends with a final step and hop, and the dancers may finish with a bow or salute.

Overall, Tiklos is a fun and energetic dance that celebrates Filipino culture and community.

1.) Tiklos is a partner dance,how does this folk dance improve our socialization?

Tiklos is a partner dance that originated from the Visayas region in the Philippines. This folk dance involves two or more people dancing together in a synchronized manner. Dancing Tiklos can improve our socialization skills because it involves interacting and communicating with our dance partner. Through the dance, we learn to work together with our partner, establish a connection, and develop trust and cooperation. Dancing Tiklos can also help us build confidence and improve our self-esteem, especially when we successfully execute the steps with our partner. Overall, Tiklos can help us develop social skills that are essential in building relationships and communicating with others.

2.) As a student, why is it important to learn the steps or figures of tiklos?

As a student, learning the steps or figures of Tiklos is important because it is part of our cultural heritage. By learning Tiklos, we are also learning about Philippine history, culture, and traditions. Moreover, learning Tiklos can help us develop a sense of pride and appreciation for our cultural identity. Tiklos can also be a form of physical activity and can promote a healthy lifestyle. Additionally, learning Tiklos can improve our coordination, balance, and rhythm, which are essential in other physical activities and sports. Overall, learning Tiklos can enrich our knowledge, skills, and appreciation of our cultural heritage and promote our physical and mental well-being.

What is Gallop step:

Filipino/Tagalog:

Ang Galloping ay isang pasulong na kilusan ng slide: mga hakbang sa harap ng paa pasulong na may isang maliit na spring na sinusundan ng paglipat ng timbang ng katawan sa likod na paa. Habang tumatanggap ang paa sa paa ng timbang ng katawan, inuulit ng harap ng paa ang kilusan ng hakbang.

English:

Galloping is a forward slide movement: front foot steps forward with a little spring followed by the transfer of body weight to the back foot. As the back foot receives the body weight, the front foot repeats the forward step movement.
