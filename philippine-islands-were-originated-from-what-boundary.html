1. philippine islands were originated from what boundary?​


Answer:

Sorong Fault System

Explanation:

The Philippine Sea Plate is the one major plate whose Tertiary motion is poorly constrained and whose origin is problematic. Its southern boundary is the Sorong Fault system which is part of a major left-lateral fault system at the northern margin of the Australian plate.

Answer:

The Philippine Sea plate is tectonically unusual in that almost all the boundaries are convergent.

Convergent and Divergent Boundaries

1. Philippine islands originated from Convergent and divergent boundaries.

The Philippine Islands are formed due to convergent and divergent boundaries. The Philippine Islands are above the Philippine Plate where the divergent boundary between the Philippine Plate is the Mariana Plate which includes the Mariana Islands in the United States.

While the convergent boundary of the Philippine Plate in the north is the Okhotsk Plate in the Nankai Trench, in the east is the subducted Pacific Plate at the Izu-Ogasawara Trench, in the south, it is bordered by the Bird's Head Plate and the Caroline Plate, in the northwest it is bordered by the Okinawa Plate (China). and the Ryukyu Islands above it), and the Amur Plate (Japan) to the south.

2. What type of plate boundary occurs between the Eurasian plate and the North American plate?

Answer: Divergent plate boundaries

This divergent plate boundary is an area where plate points are far apart due to magma pushing out so that the earth's crust is widening or far apart and will trigger the formation of new oceans and mid-ocean ridges.

One of the divergent plate boundaries is the Eurasian plate and the North American plate. Divergent boundaries also produce volcanic islands that appear as plates move away, creating fissures that are then filled with lava.

3. Movement of the plate boundary causes shaking of the Earth’s surface called ____________.

Answer: Earthquake.

The earth consists of tectonic plates that are constantly moving due to convection currents. Tectonic plates move in a relative direction and if they collide with each other it will produce a shock or called an earthquake.

4. It is consists of Earth’s crust and upper mantle.

Answer: Earth's lithosphere.

Earth's lithosphere consists of the Earth's crust and the upper part of the Earth's mantle which results in the hardness of the outermost layer of the planet Earth. The lithosphere is a layer of rock that forms the earth's crust and is the top layer of the earth as thick as 66 km consisting of rocks and minerals.

5. What geologic feature is formed between the diverging oceanic plates?

Answer: MOR (Mid-Ocean Ridge).

MOR (Mid-ocean ridge) is a series of volcanoes and seafloor lava flows that form at divergent plate boundaries. MOR is formed by plate tectonic activity that moves divergently so that there is a gap or void at the boundary of two separate oceanic plates filled with lava/magma which produces a new crust.

MOR is a chain of underwater volcanic clusters that surrounds the earth where the new earth's crust is formed from melting magma and volcanic activity, it is more than 40,000 miles (60,000 km) long.

6. It is a transform fault boundary between the Pacific plate and North

American plate which extends through California.

Answer: San Andreas Fault (San Andreas Fault)

The San Andreas Fault is the boundary between two tectonic plates, the Pacific Plate and the North American Plate. This fault stretches across the state of California from Mendocino to the Mexican border, dividing it into two parts:

San Diego, Los Angeles, and Big Sur lie on the Pacific PlateSan Francisco, Sacramento, and the Sierra Nevada are on the North American Plate.

7. It is the process wherein the leading edge of one plate bends down into the mantle beneath the other plate.

Answer: Subduction

Subduction is a geological process in which one plate that meets each other will sink or bend under another plate, resulting in folding mountains.

Subduction zones can occur between two continental plates, between two oceanic plates, and between continental and oceanic plates.

8. What type of plate boundary is formed between two plates sliding past each other?

Answer: Transform boundaries

Transform boundaries, or transform faults (transform faults; transform faults), are boundaries between plates formed by the presence of two tectonic plates that pass each other and rub against each other horizontally in a sideways and opposite direction.

9. Another term for molten rock.

Answer: magma.

Magma is a hot molten rock located in the bowels of the earth or below the earth's surface that will be released when a volcano erupts.

10. Convergent boundary is formed between Caribbean plate and ______plate.

Answer: North American Plate

The Caribbean is a seismically active area because the Caribbean and North American tectonic plates meet here, and fault lines occur where these tectonic plates move against each other. In places where one plate moves under another (subduction), the rock can melt what is known as magma, and pressure can push this molten lava to the surface, causing volcanic eruptions.
