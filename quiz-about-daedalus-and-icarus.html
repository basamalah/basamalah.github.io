15. the story of icarus and daedalus is all about what?


The story of Icarus and Daedalus talks about the line between the mortal and the gods.

Daedalus is often stepping on the boundary between mankind and nature, always building inventions and questioning philosophies the gods had given them. His intelligence was immeasurable and that made for a dangerous weapon, as the Greeks believed that the line between mortals and gods are stiff and must not be crossed.

Daedalus often crossed this belief, and his meddling caused his demise of having to be stuck within the labyrinth that King Minos built, along with his son, Icarus.

When trying to escape, Daedalus built wings that could help them fly in hopes of escaping the tower. Though he warns Icarus about the dangers: Fly too close to the sun and the wax will melt, fly too low and the water will remove the feathers and drag you into the ocean -- which is why we must fly in the middle, where we are neither too close nor too far.

Flying too high could mean in being the overachiever, where your goals are set unrealistically. This is why it correlates with the idea of mankind wanting to be like a god, because it should never happen. Those who dare to go against the gods meet a tragic fate (like Arachne with Athena), and will suffer dire consequences. Meanwhile, flying too low could mean that setting your goals in a manner of easy ways could only drag you down and hold you back from showing true potential.

When Icarus flew to high, Daedalus could do nothing to stop his son from meeting his fate. His body soared, and vanished into the ocean right after. The story of this myth teaches us about how easy it is for an individual to crash and burn, if one is not too careful.
