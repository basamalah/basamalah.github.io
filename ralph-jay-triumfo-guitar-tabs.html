20. 12) Ralph, Royce, and Randolf weigh 119 kgs altogether. Royce weighs twice as heavy as Ralph. Ralph weighs 7 kg less than Randolf. What is the mass of Randolf?​


Answer:

Let's start by assigning variables to each person's weight.

Let's call Ralph's weight "R", Royce's weight "O", and Randolf's weight "D".

From the problem, we know that:

R + O + D = 119 (they weigh 119 kgs altogether)

O = 2R (Royce weighs twice as heavy as Ralph)

R = D - 7 (Ralph weighs 7 kg less than Randolf)

We can use the second equation to substitute "2R" for "O" in the first equation:

R + 2R + D = 119

Simplifying:

3R + D = 119

We can also use the third equation to substitute "D - 7" for "R" in the above equation:

3(D - 7) + D = 119

Simplifying:

4D - 21 = 119

Adding 21 to both sides:

4D = 140

Dividing both sides by 4:

D = 35

Therefore, Randolf weighs 35 kgs.

Jay's father is twice as old as Jay. In 20 years , Jay will be two-thirds as old as his father. How old is Jay now? What is the age of Jay's father now?​

Answer:

Jay is 20 and his Father is 40

Solution:

Let f = Father's age now

Let j = Jay's age now

Given:

f = 2j

j + 20 = (2/3) x (f + 20)

By substitution:

j + 20 = (2/3) x (2j + 20)

j + 20 = (4/3) x j + 40/3

Multiply both sides by 3

3j + 60 = 4j + 40

j = 20

and

f = 2j

f = 40

Jay is 20 and his Father is 40
