2. In our current time, can you say that the filipinos are lazy? Why? ​


Answer:

I think some people here in the Philippines are lazy .

Filipinos are hardworking, and lazy at the same time. Filipinos would work for very long hours if needed, and could also just sit down on the couch for long hours while drinking liquor, if there isn’t a need to exert extraneous effort. Environment plays a vital role in this attitude. We are living in a tropical country where the burning heat of the sun would make you soaked in sweat with just minimal movement. The weather in the Philippines is also very unpredictable, this hour it is very sunny, rain with strong winds would blow on another. In spite of these, Filipinos in the rural areas are very productive when it comes to agriculture and fisheries.

There is one example to this type of attitude. The Spaniards thought that Filipinos are lazy, because they saw them just sitting in their huts during noon until 3 in the afternoon. In the mind of the Spaniards, they believe that Filipinos should be working and not wasting any time. What the Spaniards failed to see is that Filipinos woke up early (about 3 in the morning) and even if it’s still dark, they are already farming and tilling the ground, while the Spaniards are still at their deep slumber. Filipinos did this, because they want to avoid the burning heat of the sun that can literally damage your skin and drain your reserved energies during noontime. So, by starting the work before dawn, most of the time, the tasks that were assigned to the farmers have already been finished by 10AM, and will just rest in their huts during the time when the heat of the sun is scorching hot. By 3 in the afternoon, they would return to their fields to finish the remaining work to be done until 7 in the night. The body clock of the said colonizers are different to that of Filipino agriculturists, because they are only doing light manual labor and white collar jobs compared to the native laborers. So, they would woke up by about 8 in the morning, when most Filipino laborers are already midway in finishing their work in the fields. So the Spaniards thought that we are lazy, because they are only witnessing the time when Filipinos are already resting, because they aren’t still awake during the time of their hard labor. They thought Filipinos are just resting and that they hate working.

Another reason for the laziness of Filipinos is because of the lack of motivation, especially on the sector of blue collar workers and minimum wage earners. They usually receive a very small amount of salary, and there are hardly any benefits and freebies from the companies on which they work for. Even a foreigner would not do the work with perfection and precision if they are not being treated well and given importance by their employers.

Most Filipinos who work overseas are the most hardworking, because most of the salaries that they would receive would be given to their families as a form of support. These overseas workers are very versatile, coping up with difficult situations alone with no one to turn to. They are brave, hardworking and patient and I admire them for their contributions for the growth of this country. The sad part here is most of the families who receive monthly assistance from their overseas relatives would be completely dependent to the said person and they would not work hard since there is no forced necessity. This attitude certainly must go with the times.

Geographical environment also plays a huge role in the work ethic of Filipinos. The Ilocanos of North Luzon are much hardworking than most Filipinos because they live in a region where there’s scarcity in fertile lands, being hemmed by forbidding mountain ranges in one side and the endless sea on the other. That’s the main reason Ilocanos are frugal and disciplined at work, because this is how their environment have shaped them. The most different ethnic group from the Ilocanos are the Kapampangans of the Central Plains of Luzon. Because they are living in a region blessed with fertile farms with rivers teeming with edible fishes, they are mostly laidback and would not work as hard as the Ilocanos do. They are also big spenders, because their environment offers no reason for them to be frugal. This is why the many ethnic groups here in the Philippines also have different work ethics from one another, because geography have shaped their attitudes and outlook in life differently from one another.

So to answer this question, Filipinos are a combination of both, lazy but still hardworking.

25. How to get rid of laziness to something that you really want to do but your too lazy?


Answer:

1. Make your goals manageable

Setting unrealistic goals and taking on too much can lead to burnout. While not an actual clinical diagnosis, the symptoms of burnout are recognized by medical professionals. Job burnout can cause exhaustion, loss of interest and motivation, and a longing to escape.

Avoid overloading by setting smaller, attainable goals that will get you where you want to be without overwhelming you along the way.

2. Don’t expect yourself to be perfect

Perfectionism is on the rise and it’s taking a psychological toll.

One 2017 study that looked at college students between 1989 and 2016 found an increase in perfectionism over the years. Researchers noted “young people [are] now facing more competitive environments, more unrealistic expectations, and more anxious and controlling parents than generations before.”

This rise in perfectionism is causing people to be overly critical of themselves and others. It’s also led to an increase in depression and anxiety.

Another smaller study of college students concluded that expecting perfection was related to avoidant coping, which causes you to avoid dealing with stressors.

3. Use positive instead of negative self-talk

Negative self-talk can derail your efforts to get things done in every aspect of your life. Telling yourself that you’re a lazy person is a form of negative self-talk.

You can stop your negative internal voice by practicing positive self-talk. Instead of saying, “There’s no way I can get this done,” say, “I’ll give it my all to make it happen.”

4. Create a plan of action

Planning how you will get something done can make it easier to get there. Be realistic about how much time, effort, and other factors are needed to meet your goal and create an action plan. Having a plan will provide direction and confidence that can help even if you hit a hurdle along the way.

5. Use your strengths

Take a moment to think about what your strengths are when setting goals or gearing up to tackle a task. Try to apply them to different aspects of a task to help you get things done. Research has shown that focusing on strengths increases productivity, positive feelings, and engagement in work.
