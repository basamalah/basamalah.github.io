23. Cite situations or events where you present the following modes of speech delivery: reading from manuscript, memorized, impromptu, and extemporaneous


Answer:

Extemporaneous speaking is the presentation of a carefully planned and rehearsed speech, spoken in a conversational manner using brief notes. By using notes rather than a full manuscript, the extemporaneous speaker can establish and maintain eye contact with the audience and assess how well they are understanding the speech as it progresses. The opportunity to assess is also an opportunity to restate more clearly any idea or concept that the audience seems to have trouble grasping.

Impromptu speaking is the presentation of a short message without advance preparation. Impromptu speeches often occur when someone is asked to “say a few words” or give a toast on a special occasion. You have probably done impromptu speaking many times in informal, conversational settings. Self-introductions in group settings

The option C. Formal speech is incorrect answers. Because formal speech is a speech made at a formal event that is usually attended by important people, such as officials, dignitaries, or important figures (which simply needs to use outlines).

The option D. Memorized speech is incorrect answers. Because memorized speech is a method of speech that is carried out without text or is completely memorized. Usually someone who uses this method will be given time and careful preparation until the time to deliver the speech.

EXPLANATION:

There are several methods used in giving speeches, namely memoriter, manuscript, impromptu, and extemporaneous.

The memoriter method is a method of giving a speech by memorizing and writing down the speech first. This method is used by people who are just learning to speak.Manuscript method is a method of giving speeches by bringing and reading the script when giving a speech. This method is used by officials in official state events to avoid mistakes.The impromptu method is a method of making speeches in a spontaneous way and only relying on the experience and insight of the speaker. This means that the speaker in this method is appointed suddenly and is usually the right person to give a speech at an event. This method is used in informal events.The extemporaneous method is a method of giving a speech by bringing a small note as a reminder of the order in which the content of the speech will be delivered. This method is usually used by people who are already proficient in delivering speeches in order to speak systematically.

27. 1. How do you deliver a speech by reading from a manuscript? What are the advantages anddisadvantages of using this method?Answer pls​


Answer:

How do you deliver a speech by reading from a manuscript?

•Practice reading it out aloud several times, or as many times as you can.

•Try keeping a smile on your face while reading.

•Keep in mind that a manuscript speech does not mean 'mere reading out'. Maintaining frequent eye contact with the audience helps involving them into the subject matter.

What are the advantages and disadvantages of using this method?

Advantages:

(1) control of time,

(2) control of word choice, and

(3) when style is critical—elegant language.

Disadvantages:

(1) loss of eye contact and

(2) loss of natural speaking style.

The two disadvantages typically far outweigh the advantages of this style. Memorized: Speech is memorized word-for-word.
