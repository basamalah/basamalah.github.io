14. what is forensic chemistry​


Answer:

Forensic chemists analyze non-biological trace evidence found at crime scenes in order to identify unknown materials and match samples to known substances. They also analyze drugs/controlled substances taken from scenes and people in order to identify and sometimes quantify these materials.

19. research paper about toxicology in college forensic ​


RESEARCH:

Toxicology is a field of study that deals with the adverse effects of chemicals on living organisms. It is a crucial aspect of forensic science, as it helps to determine the presence and concentration of toxic substances in the body, and can provide valuable information about the cause of death in criminal cases.

There are several methods used in toxicology to detect and quantify the presence of toxins in the body. These include blood and urine testing, tissue analysis, and the use of specialized instrumentation such as gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS).

One of the main challenges in toxicology is the wide range of potential toxins that can be present in the body. These can include prescription and over-the-counter medications, illegal drugs, alcohol, and various environmental contaminants. It is essential for forensic toxicologists to have a comprehensive understanding of the chemical properties, metabolism, and potential effects of these substances in order to accurately interpret their presence in the body.

Overall, toxicology plays a vital role in forensic science and is an essential tool in helping to solve criminal cases and seek justice for victims. As the field continues to evolve, it is essential for forensic toxicologists to stay up-to-date on the latest techniques and technologies in order to accurately and effectively detect and analyze toxic substances in the body.

Forensic toxicologists perform scientific tests on bodily fluids and tissue samples to identify any drugs or chemicals present in the body. Working in a lab, the forensic toxicologist performs tests on samples collected by forensic pathologists during an autopsy or by crime scene investigators. They use highly sophisticated instruments, chemical reagents and precise methodologies to determine the presence or absence of specific substances in the sample.

As part of a team investigating a crime, a forensic toxicologist will isolate and identify any substances in the body that may have contributed to the crime, such as:

Alcohol

Illegal or prescription drugs

Other chemicals

Poisons

Metals

Gases, such as carbon monoxide
