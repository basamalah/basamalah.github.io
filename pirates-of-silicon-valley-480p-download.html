6. A cartoon of 20 compact disks contains 5 pirate and 15 original disks. In how many ways can an inspector choose 3 of the compact disks and a. get one that is pirated? b. get two of the pirated disks? c. none of the disks is pirated?


a. To get one pirated disk, we have to choose 1 disk from the 5 pirated disks and 2 disks from the 15 original disks. This can be done in C(5,1) * C(15,2) ways (where C(n,r) denotes the combination of n items taken r at a time). So, the total number of ways to choose 3 disks with one pirated disk is:

C(5,1) * C(15,2) = 5 * (15 choose 2) = 5 * 105 = 525

Therefore, there are 525 ways to choose 3 disks with one pirated disk.

b. To get two pirated disks, we have to choose 2 disks from the 5 pirated disks and 1 disk from the 15 original disks. This can be done in C(5,2) * C(15,1) ways. So, the total number of ways to choose 3 disks with two pirated disks is:

C(5,2) * C(15,1) = (5 choose 2) * 15 = 10 * 15 = 150

Therefore, there are 150 ways to choose 3 disks with two pirated disks.

c. To choose none of the disks as pirated, we have to choose 3 disks from the 15 original disks. This can be done in C(15,3) ways. So, the total number of ways to choose 3 disks with none of the disks as pirated is:

C(15,3) = (15 choose 3) = 455

Therefore, there are 455 ways to choose 3 disks with none of the disks as pirated.

The first step is to calculate the mass of silicon dioxide that contains 10 g of silicon. Since silicon makes up 46.7% of silicon dioxide by mass, we can use the following equation to find the mass of silicon dioxide:

mass of silicon dioxide = mass of silicon / fraction of silicon in silicon dioxide

mass of silicon dioxide = 10 g / 0.467

mass of silicon dioxide = 21.41 g

So, 10 g of silicon will combine with 21.41 g of silicon dioxide.

Now, we can use the molecular formula of silicon dioxide (SiO2) to calculate the mass of oxygen that is present in 21.41 g of silicon dioxide.

The molecular weight of SiO2 is 60.08 g/mol (28.09 g/mol for silicon and 31.99 g/mol for oxygen), so we can use the following equation to calculate the mass of oxygen:

mass of oxygen = (mass of silicon dioxide x fraction of oxygen in SiO2) / molecular weight of SiO2

mass of oxygen = (21.41 g x 0.533) / 60.08 g/mol

mass of oxygen = 0.190 g

Therefore, 10 g of silicon will combine with 0.190 g of oxygen to form 21.41 g of silicon dioxide.
