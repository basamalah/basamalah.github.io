2. what are the benefits and beneficiaries of my study in research​


Answer:

Researchers are encouraged to consider who will benefit from their research, especially when the research may involve or affect multiple individuals or groups (eg beneficiaries, non-academic users, participants and their associates, researchers or research organisations) - particularly where benefit to one individual or group may lead to increased risk to another. Researchers should aim to achieve a positive risk-benefit ratio, but should also ensure they safeguard the independence and excellence of the research.

Benefit can be defined as the positive impact from the research to the parties directly involved (eg research participants and those associated with them, researchers and research organisations), as well as the demonstrable contribution of research to knowledge, our economy, individuals and society. During the development of the research the maximisation of research benefits should hold central position when considering ethics issues. A study may be considered ethical when there is a positive risk-benefit ratio - that is, the risks and intrusions for people taking part in research are minimised and justified by the expected benefits for the participants, or for science and society.

24. Significance of the Study sought the main beneficiaries of the research and their benefits including their advantages Topic: Financial Problems​


Answer:

The significance of a study on financial problems may have a broad range of beneficiaries, including individuals, families, businesses, and society as a whole.

Individuals who are struggling with financial problems may benefit from a study that provides insights into effective financial management strategies, such as budgeting, saving, and debt management. Such a study may also offer guidance on how to access financial resources and support systems, such as financial counseling, credit counseling, and government assistance programs.

Families may also benefit from a study on financial problems, particularly in terms of understanding how financial stress can impact family relationships and dynamics. The study may provide guidance on how to communicate about finances effectively and how to work together as a family to overcome financial challenges.

Businesses may also benefit from a study on financial problems, particularly in terms of understanding how financial difficulties can impact business operations and profitability. The study may provide guidance on how to manage cash flow, reduce expenses, and access financing to support business growth and sustainability.

Society as a whole may also benefit from a study on financial problems, particularly in terms of understanding the broader economic and social implications of financial stress. The study may provide insights into the impact of financial difficulties on poverty, social inequality, and public health, as well as offer guidance on policies and programs that can support financial stability and wellbeing.

In summary, a study on financial problems may benefit a wide range of individuals, families, businesses, and society as a whole by providing insights into effective financial management strategies, guidance on accessing financial resources and support systems, and understanding the broader economic and social implications of financial stress.
