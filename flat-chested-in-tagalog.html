7. A client with chest injury has suffered flail chest. a nurse assesses the client for which most distinctive sign of flail chest


Answer:

Symptoms of Flail Chest

The most telling symptom is paradoxical movement of a portion of the chest wall—that is, the affected area draws in when the patient breathes in and the rest of the chest expands, and the affected area moves outwards as the patient exhales and the rest of the chest contracts.

16. What happens when you inhale? a. Chest cavity expands and diaphragm relaxes. b. Chest cavity expands and diaphragm contracts. C. Chest cavity compresses and diaphragm relaxes, d. Chest cavity compresses and diaphragm contracts.​


Answer:

When the lungs inhale, the diaphragm contracts and pulls downward. At the same time, the muscles between the ribs contract and pull upward. This increases the size of the thoracic cavity and decreases the pressure inside. As a result, air rushes in and fills the lungs.
