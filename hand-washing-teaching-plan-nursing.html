29. Nurse julia plans to instruct the postpartum client about methods to prevent breast engorgement. which of the following measures would the nurse include in the teaching plan?


Breast engorgement is the sensation where the postpartum mother experience a heavy, painful, hard, and sensitive breast due to expansion and pressure exerted by the lympathic engorgement of the breast which synthesize the breast milk production (lactation).

The primary measures that a nurse should include in her teaching plan is the initiation of breastfeeding.

Encouraging breastfeeding can alleviate the pain, tenderness, and soreness of the breast engorgement. It can also provide bonding between the mother and the newborn.

Here are some other measures that a nurse should also include in her teaching plan:

Application of cold compress helps to alleviate breast discomfort.Avoid breast pumping. Engorged breast tissue is sensitive, hence, breast pumping is harmful and it make more susceptible to damage. Wear a snug-fitting bra. It offers comfort and helps in diminishing the amount of engorged milk caused by blocked milk ducts. Ask the attending physician for oral analgesics to provide a pain relief. Encourage the mother to don't delay the breast feeding sessions and don't use any formula milk.
