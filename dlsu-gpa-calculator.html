5. The pressure in different layers of earth increases as the depth increase. The sum of the pressure in all layers of the earth is 523.5 GPa. If the mantle is 5.79 GPa times the crust while core is 15.49 GPa times the pressure in the crust. Find the pressure in all layers of the Earth.​


answer:

We are given that the sum of the pressure in all layers of the earth is 523.5 GPa and that the pressure in the mantle is 5.79 times the pressure in the crust, and the pressure in the core is 15.49 times the pressure in the crust.

Let Pc be the pressure in the crust, Pm be the pressure in the mantle, and Pcore be the pressure in the core

From the information given, we know that:

Pc + Pm + Pcore = 523.5 GPa

Pm = 5.79 * Pc

Pcore = 15.49 * Pc

We can substitute the values of Pm and Pcore in the first equation:

Pc + 5.79Pc + 15.49Pc = 523.5 GPa

we can simplify this to:

22.28*Pc = 523.5 GPa

Then we can find the pressure in the crust by dividing both sides by 22.28:

Pc = 523.5 GPa / 22.28

Pc = 23.7 GPa

Now that we know the pressure in the crust, we can use the formulas for the pressure in the mantle and core to find their values:

Pm = 5.79 * Pc = 5.79 * 23.7 GPa = 138.6 GPa

Pcore = 15.49 * Pc = 15.49 * 23.7 GPa = 364.7 GPa

So the pressure in the crust is 23.7 GPa, the pressure in the mantle is 138.6 GPa and the pressure in the core is 364.7 GPa

9. The pressure in different layers of the earth increases as depth increases. The sum of the pressure in all layers of the Earth is 523.5 GPa If the mantle is 5.79 GPa times the crust while the core is 15.49 GPa times the pressure in the crust. Find the pressure in all layers of the Earth.I need a translation, a solution and a final answer


Answer:

The pressure in different layers of the Earth is represented by the variables: Pc = pressure in the crust, Pm = pressure in the mantle, and Pk = pressure in the core.

The sum of the pressure in all layers of the Earth is given as: Pc + Pm + Pk = 523.5 GPa

The pressure in the mantle is 5.79 times the pressure in the crust, Pm = 5.79 * Pc

The pressure in the core is 15.49 times the pressure in the crust, Pk = 15.49 * Pc

We can substitute the values of Pm and Pk in the first equation to get: Pc + (5.79 * Pc) + (15.49 * Pc) = 523.5 GPa

Solving for Pc: Pc = 523.5 / (1 + 5.79 + 15.49) GPa

Pc = 69.08 GPa

We can then use this value to find the pressures in the mantle and core:

Pm = 5.79 * Pc = 5.79 * 69.08 GPa = 399.35 GPa

Pk = 15.49 * Pc = 15.49 * 69.08 GPa = 1064.66 GPa

The pressure in the crust is 69.08 GPa, the pressure in the mantle is 399.35 GPa and the pressure in the core is 1064.66 GPa.
