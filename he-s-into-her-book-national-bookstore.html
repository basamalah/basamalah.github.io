10. Katie and Becca each bought a new book for $50. Katie sold her book to the used bookstore for 25% less than the original price. Becca sold her book to the used bookstore for 40% less than the original price. Enter how much more money, in dollors, Katie received for her book than Becca received for her book.


Answer:

$3.50

Step-by-step explanation:

First, find how much Katie sold her book for by multiplying the original price by the percentage discount: $50 * 0.25 = $12.50

Then subtract that amount from the original price to find the sale price: $50 - $12.50 = $37.50

Next, find how much Becca sold her book for by multiplying the original price by the percentage discount: $50 * 0.40 = $20

Then subtract that amount from the original price to find the sale price: $50 - $20 = $30

Now subtract the sale price for Becca's book from the sale price for Katie's book to find the difference: $37.50 - $30 = $7.50

So Katie received $7.50 more for her book than Becca did.
