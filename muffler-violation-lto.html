11. 1. Minimizing the use of car horns 2. Putting silencers in motorcycle mufflers 3. Turning up the volume of stereos in full blast 4. Using ear mufflers for protection 5. Putting noise reduction materials at home 1​


Answer:

Minimizing the use of car horns: This is a great way to reduce noise pollution, especially in residential areas. Drivers can limit the use of their car horns to only when it is necessary, such as in emergency situations or to warn other drivers of danger. Excessive honking can cause annoyance and even health problems for people living nearby, so it is important to use car horns judiciously.

Putting silencers in motorcycle mufflers: This is another effective way to reduce noise pollution. Motorcycles are notorious for their loud exhaust noise, which can cause hearing damage to riders and nearby pedestrians. Silencers or mufflers can help reduce the decibel level of the exhaust sound and make the ride quieter and more pleasant for everyone.

Turning up the volume of stereos in full blast: This is a behavior that contributes to noise pollution and can be harmful to hearing. Loud music can cause hearing damage and annoy neighbors. It is important to be considerate of others when listening to music, especially in shared living spaces.

Using ear mufflers for protection: This is a great way to protect hearing from excessive noise, such as at concerts, airports, or construction sites. Ear mufflers or earplugs can help reduce the decibel level of noise and prevent hearing loss. It is important to choose the right type of ear protection for the situation and wear it correctly.

Putting noise reduction materials at home: This is a way to reduce noise pollution within a living space. Noise reduction materials such as acoustic panels or curtains can help absorb or block out noise from outside or from other rooms. This can create a more peaceful and comfortable living environment.
