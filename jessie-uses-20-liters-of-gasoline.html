3. Jessie uses 20 liters of gasoline to travel 200 kilometers, how many liters of gasoline will he use on a trip of 700 kilometers?


Proportion Problem:

Let x be the liters of gasoline required for a 700-km trip.

20 is to 200; x is to 700.

20/200 = x/700

(200) (x) = (20) (700)

200x = 14,000

200x/200 = 14,000/200

x = 70 

The answer is 70 liters.

1. Use variables to get the equation of variation

Jean uses 20 Liters (L) to travel 200 km (T).

2. Use direct variation formula

[tex]20= k200[/tex]

3. Solve for K

[tex] \frac{20}{200} = \frac{k200}{200} [/tex]

The constant of variation is K=0.1

4. Apply the constant rto the new equation

[tex]T = (0.1)(900)[/tex]

The answer is 90 Liters(L)
