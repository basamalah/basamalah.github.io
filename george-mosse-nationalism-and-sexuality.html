3. What do mosses need to reproduce sexually?


Answer:

Mosses can actually reproduce sexually and asexually. Remember, when we are talking about sexual reproduction in plants, it involves female and male structures while in asexual reproduction no mixing of gametes takes place.

For it to reproduce sexually, the presence of a female structure called archegonium (which produces the egg cells) and a male structure called antheridium (which produces sperm cells) are needed.

You may notice that mosses grow in a humid place or a slightly moist environment like near a river, wet rock structures, or most cement. That is because WATER is needed to fertilize the egg cell. Water droplets help the released sperm cells by antheridium (male part) to swim and reach the archegonium (female part). Then, fertilization takes place until a sporophyte is produced. It contains spores that are released in a dry condition where the wind carries the spores to the other areas. Then, a new sprout of mosses begins.

-----------------------------------------

CONCLUSION

In conclusion, mosses need a female structure called archegonium, a male structure called antheridium, and water droplets as abiotic pollinators.

Here are my answer:

Plants reproduce sexually through the fusion of male and female gametes. In most plants, this occurs through the formation of flowers, which contain both male and female reproductive organs. Pollen from the male stamen is transferred to the female pistil, where it fertilizes the female gametes and forms a zygote. The zygote then develops into a seed, which is capable of growing into a new plant. However, there are some plants, such as mosses and liverworts, which reproduce sexually through the fusion of male and female gametes that develop into a sporophyte, which produces spores that can grow into new plants.
