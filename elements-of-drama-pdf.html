14. Compare and contrast the literary elements and the technical elements of a drama.​


Answer:

Technical elements

The areas of technical theatre are scenery,lighting, properties,costuming,and sound. All of these areas work together in a production to establish the place, time period, and mood of the production. If successful the audience will not even notice many of the technical elements of the show.

Literary elements

literary elements include story line (plot), character, story organization (beginning, middle, end), plot structures (rising action, turning point, falling action), conflict, suspense, theme, language, style, dialogue, monologue. technical elements include scenery (set), costumes, props, lights, sound, music, makeup.

16. what are the elements of drama?


PLOT

...the series of events that comprise the whole story that is told in a novel, play, movie, TV show, etc.

DIALOGUE

...the "lines" (words, facial expressions, body language) "spoken" by characters in a drama or fiction intended to convey intent, feeling, action or thought

CHARACTER

...the "people" in a novel, play, movie, TV show, etc. that exhibit characteristics (attributes and experiences that determine or influence moral, ethical, intellectual and emotional actions, reactions and thought processes)

AUDIENCE

...a gathering of spectators or listeners at a (usually public) performance

STAGECRAFT

...the art and craft of establishing the physical environment of a production

GENRE
(A Sound of Thunder)

...a kind of literary or artistic work that follows a particular technique or includes certain characteristic forms

CONVENTION

...the set of practices or characteristics within a drama or artistic work that are expected to be included in order to establish the genre

THEME

...the unifying idea that is reflected through recurrent elements within a literary or artistic work

20. it is considered as literary element of drama​


The setting is considered as a literary element of drama as it refers to the physical and social context in which the play takes place. The setting can include the time period, the geographical location, the atmosphere, and the cultural and historical background of the characters or the events portrayed in the play. The setting can also influence the mood and tone of the drama and can play a significant role in developing the plot and the characters.
