9. how water wind and steam make electicity through generator​


Answer:

In a turbine generator, a moving fluid—water, steam, combustion gases, or air—pushes a series of blades mounted on a rotor shaft. The force of the fluid on the blades spins/rotates the rotor shaft of a generator. The generator, in turn, converts the mechanical (kinetic) energy of the rotor to electrical energy.

30. 1. A nuclear power reactor has pressurized water at 300°C. The steam produced in the steam generator is used to drive the turbine generators. Eventually the steam is condensed to water at 27°C and then heated again to start the cycle. Compute for the efficiency of the engine that operates in this nuclear power reactor. ​


Answer:

n = 0.486

Explanation:

[tex]T1 = 300C+273 = 573K[/tex]

[tex]T2 = 27C+273 = 300K[/tex]

[tex]n = (T1-T2)/T1 = (573-300)/573 = 273/573[/tex]

[tex]n = 0.486[/tex]
