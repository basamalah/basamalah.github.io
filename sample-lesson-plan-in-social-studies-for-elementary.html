1. sample detailed lesson plan in elementary level​


Answer:

Subject: English

Grade level: 4th grade

Duration: 45 minutes

Objectives:To introduce the concept of nounsTo identify common and proper nounsTo practice writing sentences using proper nouns

Materials:Whiteboard and markersChart paper and markersHandouts (worksheet on nouns)

Introduction (10 minutes):Greet the students and ask them how their day is going.Write the word "nouns" on the whiteboard and ask the students if they know what it means.Explain to the students that nouns are words that describe people, places, things, or ideas.Write a few examples on the whiteboard (e.g. dog, city, love, book, etc.).Ask the students if they can guess which ones are proper nouns.

Direct Instruction (20 minutes):Explain the difference between common nouns and proper nouns.Write "Common Nouns" and "Proper Nouns" on the chart paper and place it on the board.Write examples of common nouns (e.g. cat, street, happiness, etc.) under "Common Nouns".Write examples of proper nouns (e.g. Siamese, Main Street, Joy, etc.) under "Proper Nouns".Explain that proper nouns always start with a capital letter and refer to a specific person, place, or thing.Write a few sentences on the board and ask the students to identify the proper nouns in each sentence.

Guided Practice (10 minutes):Distribute the handouts to the students and explain that they are going to practice writing sentences using proper nouns.Give the students 5-7 minutes to complete the worksheet.Walk around the classroom to monitor the students and offer help if needed.

Closure (5 minutes):Call on a few students to share their sentences and highlight the proper nouns in each sentence.Summarize what the students learned during the lesson.Remind the students that proper nouns are important to use in writing to make their sentences clear and specific.Assessment:Observation of students during the guided practice activityCompleted handoutsTeacher-made quiz on proper nouns next lesson.

6. What are the competencies in teaching social studies in the elementary?


Answer:

This booklet outlines specific competencies for social studies education in grades one through six in the Philadelphia school system. The focus of social studies education is seen to be the transmission of knowledge and inculcation of skills and attitudes essential for good citizenship in an interdependent world. Five important areas which contribute to good citizenship are self-realization, governing, producing and consuming, utilizing environments, and critical thinking and decision making. These areas form the basis for the competencies listed for each of six grade levels. For each grade level at least 10 competencies are listed. Each competency is followed by at least one behavioral objective which can be used to evaluate mastery of the competency. For example, a key competency for grade three is, "Students will show awareness of the neighborhood as a geographic entity." A behavioral objective for this is "Identify or draw a map of the neighborhood." Other key competencies include understanding that people have feelings and emotions (grade one), recognizing the importance of city services (grade two), appreciating ethnic and cultural diversity (grade four), and understanding the importance of political and geographic boundaries (grade six). Some of the behavioral objectives involve activities related to the city of Philadelphia. (AV)

9. how will you apply jigsaw in the elementary social studies?​


Answer:

Jigsaw is a cooperative learning strategy that involves each student of a 'home' group specialising in one aspect of a learning unit. Students meet with members of other groups who are assigned the same aspect and, after mastering the material, return to their 'home' group and teach the material to their group members.
