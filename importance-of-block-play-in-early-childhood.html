15. briefly describe a play based approach and desk policy approach toward teaching and learning in early childhood education.


Early childhood education that prioritizes a play-based approach highlights the significance of play as a tool for young children to learn and grow. This method views play as a fundamental and organic aspect of children's growth and employs it to promote education.

Teachers establish environments that stimulate exploration, discovery, and creativity, and provide opportunities for children to learn by doing. Play-based learning incorporates activities such as building with blocks, imaginative play, exploring nature, and creating art.

On the other hand, an approach to early childhood education that prioritizes a desk policy emphasizes more structured activities directed by teachers that occur at desks or tables. This approach expects children to sit still and concentrate on teacher-led activities such as lectures, drills, and worksheets. It focuses on academic skills such as reading, writing, and math, with less emphasis on social and emotional development.

While both approaches have their advantages and disadvantages, research indicates that a play-based approach is more suitable for young children's development as it better aligns with their natural curiosity, creativity, and desire to learn through exploration. Play-based learning also promotes crucial social and emotional skills like problem-solving, communication, and self-regulation that are vital for success in school and beyond.

23. briefly describe a play based approach and desk policy approach toward teaching and learning in early childhood education


Answer:

A play-based approach to teaching and learning in early childhood education is centered around the idea that young children learn best through exploration and play. Play-based learning encourages children to be active participants in the learning process, using their natural curiosity and interests to guide their learning. This approach provides opportunities for children to develop social skills, problem-solving skills, and creativity while building their knowledge and understanding of the world around them.

On the other hand, the desk policy approach is a more traditional approach to teaching and learning in early childhood education, which is focused on structured lessons and formal instruction. This approach emphasizes classroom management and following set rules and procedures. Children are often expected to sit quietly at their desks and listen to the teacher, following a prescribed curriculum. The focus of this approach is on memorization and repetition rather than hands-on exploration and learning through play.

Overall, while the play-based approach offers a more child-centered and engaging learning experience, the desk policy approach may be more suitable for some children who require more structure and guidance. It is important for teachers and parents to recognize the unique needs and learning styles of each child and to adapt their teaching approach accordingly.


24. how do development theorists such as piaget, vygotsky view the role of play in early childhood?​


Answer:

Jean Piaget and Lev Vygotsky both believed that play is an important aspect of early childhood development. Piaget believed that play is a form of children's problem-solving and reasoning, allowing them to test and construct their understanding of the world around them. On the other hand, Vygotsky viewed play as a means for children to engage in social and imaginative experiences that help to develop higher-level thinking and reasoning skills. Both theorists emphasized that play is essential for children's cognitive, emotional, and social growth and development.
