17. 1. Why is it important to have a good research instrument?2. Differentiate validity and reliability. Explain how they complement each other to makea good research instrument.​


Research

Research is defined as the creation of new knowledge and/or the creative application of existing knowledge to generate new concepts, methodologies, and understandings. This could include synthesizing and analyzing previous research to the point where it results in new and creative outcomes.

Difference between validity and reliability

The concepts of reliability and validity are used to assess the quality of research. They indicate the accuracy with which a method, technique, or test measures something. The consistency of a measure is referred to as its reliability, whereas the accuracy of a measure is referred to as its validity.

What is a research instrument?The term research instrument refers to any tool that you can use to collect or obtain data, measure data, and analyze data relevant to the topic of your research.In the social sciences and health sciences, research instruments are frequently used. These tools are also available in education for patients, staff, teachers, and students.A research instrument may take the form of questionnaires, surveys, interviews, checklists, or simple tests. The researcher will make the decision on which specific research instrument tool to use. It will also be closely related to the specific methods that will be used in the study.

What Characteristics Characterize a Good Research Instrument?A good research instrument has been validated and proven to be reliable. It should be capable of gathering data in a manner appropriate to the research question at hand.The research instrument should be able to help answer the research aims, objectives, and research questions, as well as prove or disprove the study's hypothesis.It should not be biased in the way data is collected, and it should be clear how the research instrument should be used correctly.
