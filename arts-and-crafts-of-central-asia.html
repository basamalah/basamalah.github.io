17. Characteristics of arts and crafts from central and west asia world pool​


Answer:

Answer and Explanation:

Arts and crafts from West Asia are mostly Islamic art. It covers a wide range of forms and styles, reflecting the large geographical area and a wide variety of cultures influenced by the Islam religion.

Design

The distinct style of Islamic art uses intricate geometry and floral as designs and patterns. This is because the Islamic religious law forbids Idolatry or Worship of Idols. Artists felt that using figures of animals and people would be considered idolatry instead of honoring Allah. A pattern commonly used by Islamic artists is called "arabesque." Arabesque uses intricate patterns of leaves and flowers. These patterns are common in wooden carvings, stone reliefs on buildings, textiles, and decorated books.

Islamic artists also used geometric patterns called "tessellation" and "calligraphy" in their designs.

Ceramics

Ceramics was a major form of Islamic art. They created beautiful pieces of pottery, often glazed. Some artists were influenced by Chinese porcelain, while others created their unique ways of glazing pottery. Glazing in pottery is the fusion of substance to the body of the ceramic using fire.

Islamic artists also created great pieces of art using ceramic tiles. These tiles would sometimes be used to cover walls or the entire outside of religious buildings with bright patterns and designs.

Carpets

Probably the most famous and recognizable of Western Asia arts and crafts. These carpets often used colorful repeating geometric designs or arabesque patterns.

Carvings

Islamic Artists incorporated the same arabesque and geometric patterns used in other forms of Islamic art. Carvings were often made using wood, but could also be made from ivory, stone, or plaster. They were used to decorate important buildings, like mosques, including the ceiling, doors, and wall panels. Stands for the Islamic holy book, the Quran, were often pieces of art carved from wood.

Painting

The most popular paintings of Islamic art were the "miniature painting." These paintings were small and were used in books called "illuminated manuscripts." These paintings often contained pictures of animals and people. This was because they were illustrations for story books.

Calligraphy

Most Western Asia art use Calligraphic designs as patterns to objects and architecture. Mostly, the writing would be a religious verse from the Quran. Calligraphy is usually combined with geometric and arabesque patterns.

20. why is it important to understand the arts and crafts from southeast asia,east asia,south,west and central asia​


Southeast Asian arts, the literary, performing, and visual arts of Southeast Asia. Although the cultural development of the area was once dominated by Indian influence, a number of cohesive traits predate the Indian influence. Wet-rice (or padi) agriculture, metallurgy, navigation, ancestor cults, and worship associated with mountains were both indigenous and widespread, and certain art forms not derived from India—for example, batik textiles, gamelan orchestras, and the wayang puppet theatre—remain popular.

The term Southeast Asia refers to the huge peninsula of Indochina and the extensive archipelago of what is sometimes called the East Indies. The region can be subdivided into mainland Southeast Asia and insular Southeast Asia. The political units contained in this region are Myanmar (Burma), Thailand, Laos, Cambodia, Vietnam, Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The Philippines originally was not included, because Philippine history has not followed the general historical pattern of Southeast Asia, but, because of its geographic position and the close affinities of its cultures with the cultures of Southeast Asia, it is now usually regarded as the eastern fringe of Southeast Asia.


21. 16. Roots of the traditions of Arts and crafts in South, West, and Central Asia​


Answer:

Handicrafts.

Explanation:

The traditions of Arts and crafts in South, West, and Central Asia can be traced back to ancient times. These regions were home to various civilizations, such as the Indus Valley Civilization, Persian Empire, and Islamic Empires. These civilizations had a rich tradition of arts and crafts, including pottery, weaving, embroidery, leatherwork, metalwork, and woodwork.

One of the earliest examples of arts and crafts in South Asia can be seen in the pottery made by the Indus Valley Civilization, which dates back to around 2500 BCE. These were finely crafted with intricate patterns and designs, and were used for both ceremonial and everyday purposes.

In West Asia, the Persian Empire had a significant impact on the arts and crafts of the region. Under the patronage of the Persian kings, a number of crafts such as carpet weaving, metalsmithing, and ceramics flourished. The intricate patterns and designs seen in Persian rugs, metalwork, and pottery continue to influence craftspeople in the region today.

Central Asia has also been home to many ancient civilizations, including the Mongol Empire and the Islamic Caliphates. These civilizations brought with them various techniques and styles of art and crafts, such as calligraphy, miniature painting, and carpet weaving. These traditions continue to be practiced in the region, with a focus on preserving and promoting traditional techniques and styles.

Overall, the traditions of arts and crafts in South, West, and Central Asia have been shaped by the various civilizations that have inhabited the region throughout history. These traditions have also evolved over time, with modern craftspeople blending traditional techniques and styles with contemporary trends to create new and unique works of art.
