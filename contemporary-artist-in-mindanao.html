11. research an artwork made by the contemporary artists


Answer:

Andy Warhol, Campbell’s Soup Cans, 1962

This famous piece was one of Andy Warhol’s first ventures into using images from consumer culture and mass media in his work, a trend which would later come to define his distinctive style. Although the careful repetition of the piece echoes market mass production, Warhol didn’t start employing his famous silk screen printing technique until after completing this work, so each of these identical cans is actually hand-painted. It became a symbol of contemporary artworks.

17. Contemporary art is an art of today, as a senior high school student do you consider yourself a contemporary artist or a traditional artist. How and why?​


Answer:

As a senior high school student, contemporary art refers to the art produced in the present time, while traditional art typically refers to art that has been passed down from generation to generation and often adheres to established conventions and techniques. As a high school student, you may have been exposed to a variety of art forms, styles, and techniques. You might have experimented with different media and techniques, and developed your own artistic voice.

If you consider yourself a contemporary artist, you may be interested in exploring new ideas and pushing the boundaries of art. You might be drawn to contemporary art movements like conceptual art, installation art, or performance art. On the other hand, if you consider yourself a traditional artist, you might be more interested in mastering traditional techniques and creating works that adhere to established styles and conventions.

Ultimately, whether you consider yourself a contemporary or traditional artist depends on your personal artistic vision and the type of art that you create. You can also draw inspiration from both contemporary and traditional art forms to create works that reflect your own unique perspective and style.

Region 10, also known as Northern Mindanao, has produced many talented contemporary artists.

Lito Mondejar - Mondejar is a mixed media artist who has exhibited his works in various galleries and museums in the Philippines and abroad. He is known for his socio-political commentary and use of found objects in his pieces.

Ian Carlo Jaucian - Jaucian is a painter and printmaker whose works often feature elements of nature and the environment. He has received numerous awards for his art, including the grand prize at the 2013 Metrobank Art and Design Excellence Awards.

Vicente "Enting" Navarro - Navarro is a sculptor who creates large-scale installations and public artworks. He is known for his use of indigenous materials such as bamboo, rattan, and abaca in his works.
