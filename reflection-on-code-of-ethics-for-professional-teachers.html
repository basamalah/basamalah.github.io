9. Direction: What is the importance of identifying the code of ethics of a professional teacher? (15 points)​


Answer:

Identifying the code of ethics of a professional teacher is important for several reasons. Firstly, it helps establish a set of guidelines and principles that teachers should adhere to in order to maintain professional conduct and integrity. The code of ethics outlines the ethical responsibilities of teachers towards their students, colleagues, and the profession as a whole. By following these principles, teachers can build trust and respect with their students and colleagues, and maintain a positive reputation within the community.

Secondly, the code of ethics serves as a framework for professional development and growth. By identifying and understanding the ethical standards that govern their profession, teachers can better reflect on their own practices and identify areas for improvement. This self-reflection can help teachers become more effective in their roles, as they are able to continually learn and develop their skills.

Lastly, identifying the code of ethics of a professional teacher is important for legal and regulatory purposes. In many countries, teachers are required to adhere to a code of ethics in order to maintain their teaching license. By understanding and following these guidelines, teachers can ensure that they are in compliance with the law and avoid any legal or disciplinary issues.

Overall, identifying the code of ethics of a professional teacher is an important step toward ensuring that teachers maintain high standards of professionalism, ethics, and quality in their practice.

14. 5.Explain briefly the Five codes of Ethics for teachers.Integrity.Objectivity.Professional competence.Confidentiality.Professional behavior.​


INTEGRITY-Literally, integrity means having honesty and moral principles. ... In turn, an integrity-based code of ethics is one that defines an organization's guiding values while giving employees the leeway to interpret and define how they will internalize these values and behave according to their own standards of ethics.

OBJECTIVITY-Objectivity refers to being impartial and unbiased. ... It requires the avoidance of situations and circumstances that would result in personal feelings and opinions affecting decisions and actions. In other words, the avoidance conflicts of interest.

PROFESSIONAL COMPETENCE- Professional Competence and Due Care – to maintain professional knowledge and skill at the level required to ensure that a client or employer receives competent professional services based on current developments in practice, legislation and techniques and act diligently and in accordance with applicable technical.

CONFIDENTIALITY- Confidential information is one of our most valuable assets. ... “Confidential information” means any information, whatever its form, that is not in the public domain, and that we must protect from any inappropriate use or disclosure, because such use or disclosure could potentially harm the Company.

PROFESSIONAL BEHAVIOR- A code of ethics and professional conduct outlines the ethical principles that govern decisions and behavior at a company or organization. They give general outlines of how employees should behave, as well as specific guidance for handling issues like harassment, safety, and conflicts of interest.
