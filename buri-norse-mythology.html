Answer:

In the land of ancient Scandinavia, the gods and goddesses of Norse mythology ruled over the nine realms. Among them was Odin, the all-powerful ruler of Asgard and the god of war and wisdom. He had two sons, Thor and Loki, who were both known for their strength and bravery.

One day, the giants of Jotunheim, the realm of the giants, declared war on the gods. The giants were led by a fierce warrior named Hrungnir, who was made of stone and nearly indestructible.

Thor, the god of thunder, was determined to protect Asgard and its people. He armed himself with his powerful hammer, Mjolnir, and set off to face Hrungnir in battle. The two clashed in an epic showdown, but in the end, Thor emerged victorious.

Despite his defeat, Hrungnir's stubborn spirit lived on, and he continued to haunt Thor in the form of a terrible curse. But with the help of Loki, the trickster god, Thor was able to break the curse and rid himself of Hrungnir's malevolent presence.

And so, peace was restored to the nine realms, thanks to the bravery and cunning of Thor and Loki. The gods and goddesses rejoiced, knowing that they were safe once more, thanks to the valiant efforts of their greatest heroes.

18. enumerate the three parts of the world according to norse mythology


In Norse Mythology, there are nine worlds, but three levels.
1) The Upper level which contains Asgard, Alfheim, and Vanaheim. These worlds are resided by Nordic gods, elves, and Vanir (groups of gods, which includes Freyja).
2) The Middle lands, which has Midgard, Jotunheim, Svart Alfheim, and Nitha Vellir. Midgard is famous since this is the land of humans. Jotunheim is the land of giants, where Loki originated.
3) The Lowest level consists of Muspelheim, the realm of fire, and Niflheim, the land of the dead ruled by Hel - daughter of Loki.
