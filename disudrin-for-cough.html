7. description of a coughs and sneezes​


Answer:

A cough is a sudden and forceful release of air from the lungs to clear the throat and airways of mucus, foreign particles, or irritants. A sneeze is a sudden and forceful expulsion of air from the nose and mouth due to irritation in the nasal passages. Both are normal bodily reflexes that help to protect the respiratory system.

Cough - A cough is your body's way of responding when something irritates your throat or airways. An irritant stimulates nerves that send a message to your brain. The brain then tells muscles in your chest and abdomen to push air out of your lungs to force out the irritant.

Sneezes - A sneeze is a sudden, forceful, uncontrolled burst of air through the nose and mouth.

The cough medicine is in a 'dark proof bottle. B. The cough medicine should be hidden away from children. c. The cough medicine should be stored in the refrigerator. D. The cough medicine is affected by heat and light.​


Making an Inference

D. The cough medicine is affected by heat and light

Explanation:

An inference is an explanation or an interpretation of an observation. Observations are made with the use of the different senses. We have to be careful with our observation in order to make a good inference. To make an inference, a connection has to be made between the observation to one's prior knowledge or previous observations.  In the question, the observation is the cough syrup's label which says,  "Store away from light, below 30° C". We can make an interpretation out of this observation by making an inference. Since the label has something to do with the instruction regarding exposure of the cough syrup to certain light and temperature, therefore, we can interpret that the syrup is sensitive to light and heat.
