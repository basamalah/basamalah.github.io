15. What is hots question in obtaining measurement dressmaking?


Measurement dressmaking

In need of assistance? Prepare to be assisted by someone standing in front of a mirror (especially for those back measurements.)

Use the appropriate tape. Use a flexible tape measure to do so (rather than a fabric one, as those tend to stretch out over time.)

Examine your posture. With your feet together, stand erect in a comfortable manner. When taking measurements, take a deep breath and make sure the tape is securely fastened to your body. As a way to measure myself, I use a tight spandex dress that I put on and pin 1/8" of broad elastic material to my natural waist. Then, I mark the center front and center back, then tie the elastic around my natural waist to measure it.

17. What is the importance of taking accurate body measurements in dressmaking?


Answer:

Accurate measurements are important because precise amounts are required for reactions to take place, for a recipe to turn out and to keep correct records of a measurement. When measurements are not accurate, this provides incorrect data that can lead to wrong or even dangerous conclusions or results. Without taking body measurements, the fabric material can run out of supply if the tailor uses it too much. And more material is required to fix small dresses or suits. So when body measurements are taken accurately, the tailor will know the exact amount of material to be used.

25. differentiate between a dressmaker's gauge and tape measure​


Answer:

A tape measure or measuring tape is a flexible ruler used to measure size or distance.

It consists of a ribbon of cloth, plastic, fibre glass, or metal strip with linear-measurement markings. It is a common measuring tool. Its design allows for a measure of great length to be easily carried in pocket or toolkit and permits one to measure around curves or corners. Today it is ubiquitous, even appearing in miniature form as a keychain fob, or novelty item. Surveyors use tape measures in lengths of over 100 m.
