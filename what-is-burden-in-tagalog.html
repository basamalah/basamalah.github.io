10. WHITE MENS BURDEN poem in tagalog (summary)


Tagalog: "Hiling sa Pagsasalin"

"White Man's Burden" is a poem by the English poet Rudyard Kipling, written in 1899. It is a controversial poem that reflects the imperialist attitudes of the time. The poem essentially argues that it is the duty of white, European nations to civilize and uplift the "savage" peoples of the world, particularly those in Asia and Africa.

A Tagalog summary of the poem would convey the main idea that the white man has a responsibility to bring civilization and progress to other peoples who are seen as backward or uncivilized. However, the poem's message is often criticized for promoting a colonialist and racist worldview that justifies the subjugation of other cultures and the imposition of European values on non-European peoples.
