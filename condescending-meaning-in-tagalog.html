Among those characters adapted into a film, one stands out whose film version is a vast improvement of the comic version. This is the Black Panther. Black Panther’s film version, portrayed by Chadwick Boseman, is a much better rendition than the comic book version because of better characterization, a more feminist ideology, and a more significant social impact. First, the film version of the Black Panther is a better character than the comic book version. While their identity as T’Challa—the King of the fictional African nation Wakanda—is consistent, film T’Challa is a more level-headed ruler, who rarely lets his emotions cloud his better judgment. He also treats his subjects and tribe chieftains with respect. Integrity dictates may of his actions, even against his foes. Comics T’Challa, on the other hand, while generally a wise and strong-willed ruler, tends to be arrogant and impetuous. He is also more condescending towards people he deems inferior in stature. Second, the social milieu of the film Black Panther’s Wakanda is very feminist in ideology. His personal army of defenders, the Dora Milaje, are esteemed warriors unmatched by anyone in the country, man or woman. The roles of women are portrayed as though it is natural for them to be at par with the men; in fact, one would note no social distinction between the sexes. On the other hand, the Dora Milaje in the comics is not just a personal army but also a harem for T’Challa, from whom he may select a bride of his choosing. Third, the film version of Black Panther had a more far-reaching effect on its fans. Black Panther was the first African superhero to be portrayed in comics, which made the character very significant at the time of release. However, the relatively limited reach of comics, as well as the somewhat problematic nature of his character, made him more polarizing among comics fans. On the other hand, thanks to the mainstream scope of the MCU, the film version of Black Panther was able to reach a wider audience. Thus he became a symbol of greatness for the marginalized. The MCU has breathed new life into both the comic book industry and the film industry. Fans who have enjoyed the comics are treated to a spectacle beyond anything they had once imagined. Fans who have been exposed to the characters through the films find themselves seeking more material, the original comic books included. Prominent among these is the King of Wakanda, the Black Panther, who through the films has become an inspiration to those who once upon a time had no heroes representing them on a grand stage.
