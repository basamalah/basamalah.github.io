1. A parallel plate capacitor consists of two rectangular plates, each with an area of 4.5 cm2 and are separated from each other by a 2.00 mm thick dielectric with a dielectric constant of 5.26. The capacitor is connected to a 12.0 V battery. How much energy is stored in the capacitor?


Ec = 7.541 x 10⁻¹⁰ J

Further explanation

A capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor with a dielectric constant can be formulated :

C

=κε₀A/d

C = capacitance = Farad(F)

k = dielectric constant

ε₀ = the permittivity of free space; ε0 = 8.85 × 10⁻¹² F/m

A = surface area, m²

d =  the distance between the plates, m

The energy stored in a capacitor (E) :

Ec=QV/2 = CV²/2 = Q²/2C

Q =the charge on a capacitor

V = voltage applied

Given

Area = A = 4.5 cm²=4.5 x 10⁻⁴ m²

Distance = d = 2 mm = 2 x 10⁻³ m

Dielectric constant=κ= 5.26

Voltage = V = 12 V

Required

The energy stored

Solution

Ec= κε₀AV²/2d

Ec = 5.26 x 8.85 × 10⁻¹² x 4.5 x 10⁻⁴ x 12²/(2 x 2 x 10⁻³)

Ec = 7.541 x 10⁻¹⁰ J

Q = CV

29. What is input coupling capacitor and output coupling capacitor?


To overcome the challenge of creating necessary DC bias voltage for an amplifier’s input signal without resorting to the insertion of a battery in series with the AC signal source, we used a voltage divider connected across the DC power source. To make this work in conjunction with an AC input signal, we “coupled” the signal source to the divider through a capacitor, which acted as a high-pass filter. With that filtering in place, the low impedance of the AC signal source couldn’t “short out” the DC voltage dropped across the bottom resistor of the voltage divider. A simple solution, but not without any disadvantages
