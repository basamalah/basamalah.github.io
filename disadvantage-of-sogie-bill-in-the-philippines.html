                   Sogie means Sexual Orientation and Gender Identity Expression. This bill is also known as the Anti-Discrimination Law.

                    It is the proposed legislation of the Congress of the Philippines. It is intended to prevent a number of economic and public accommodation-related acts of discrimination against people on the basis of sexual orientation, gender identity or language.

                 In general, Sogie will not make same-sex marriage legal, it will also not take away the rights of those who does not belong in the category. The bill is being proposed to protect them from discrimination, be treated equal among others.

22. Purpose of SOGIE bill


Sexual Orientation or Gender Identity or Expression or simply called SOGIE bill is meant to fulfill the rights outlined in the 1987 constitution for the equal protection clause. This bill is an anti-discrimination bill.  It acknowledges that many people experience discrimination based on their SOGIE. The bill seeks to protect all people from this kind of discrimination. According to the law expert, this bill applies to all gender and identity.  The SOGIE Equality Bill does not grant LGBTQIA with special rights." With the SOGIE bill, certain activities can now be reported discriminatory, and you can file charges if any person, corporation, or organization commits these acts.

SOGIE Bill

The Sexual Orientation and Gender Identity and Expression Equality is also known as the Anti-Discrimination Bill (ADG).

The first filing of the anti-discrimination bill was during the 11th Congress. The bill itself was a policy directly proposed by the Lesbian and Gay Legislative Advocacy Network or LAGABLAB. For all LGBT-related bills that has been filed, they are the first LGBT policy advocacy network here in the Philippines.

LAGABLAB has been revived as a community platform for the SOGIE bill and the human rights in the area of policies and legislation.

Here are the similarities of the comprehensive version and the SOGIE Bill:

Both versions are being supported by different LGBT groups and it was only the Sogi-specific version that made a little progress.

Both versions penalize discrimination on an internationally recognized standard.
