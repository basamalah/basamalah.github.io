14. calculate the molarity of a solution prepared by dissolving 1.78g of gaseous HCI into enough water to make 27.2 ml of solution.​


Molarity (M)Discussion:

Molarity is one of the commonly used units in chemistry. Molarity is defined as the number of moles of solute per liter of solution --- that is,

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}}[/tex]

For example, a 1.46 [tex]M[/tex] glucose solution contains 1.46 mol of the solute (glucose) in 1 L of the solution. Of course, we do not always work with solution volumes of 1 L. Thus, a 500-mL solution containing 0.730 mol of glucose also has a concentration of 1.46 [tex]M.[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

Calculate the molarity of a solution prepared by dissolving 1.78 g of gaseous HCI into enough water to make 27.2 mL of solution.

Given:

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 1.78 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution = 27.2 mL = 0.0272 L}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solute = HCl}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\text{molarity}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass of solute = (1.008 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 36.458 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{1.78 g}}{\text{36.458 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 0.048823 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{0.048823 mol}}{\text{0.0272 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = 1.79 \: M}[/tex]

26. A dilute sulfuric acid (H2SO4) solution is being prepared by the workers of an oil refinery. They had 105 grams of sulfuric acid used to prepare 175 L of solution. What is the molar concentration or molarity of the solution they are preparing? solve this problem.​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute (H₂SO₄).

molar mass = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (32.07 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass = 98.086 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 105 g H₂SO₄} × \frac{\text{1 mol H₂SO₄}}{\text{98.086 g H₂SO₄}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 1.0705 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{1.0705 mol}}{\text{175 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity = 0.00612 M}}[/tex]

28. A chemist prepares a 1.5L solution by adding 25grams of NaCl to water. Calculate the molarity concentration of this NaCl solution.


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of solute (NaCl).

Note that the molar mass of solute (NaCl) is 58.44 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of solute} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}} \\ & = \frac{\text{25 g}}{\text{58.44 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.43 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Solve for the molarity of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molarity} & = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.43 mol}}{\text{1.5 L}} \\ & = \text{0.29 mol/L} \\ & = \boxed{0.29 \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molarity of NaCl solution is 0.29 M.
