26. jason had $48 more than halina. Halina had $25 more than Bill. after bill spent $45. halina had three times as much as what Bill had left. how much money did they have altogether at first?


Let's make this simpler by using J to be the amount Jason has, H to be the amount Halina has, and B the amount Bill has.

Now at the start: J = H+48 and H = B + 25

Then since H is 3 times B, when we subtract 25 from B, that is H = 3 (B-25).

The question asks for the total of the 3 at the beginning, so J + H + B.

We first use the 2 equations with H and B, H = B+25 and H = 3(B-25).  So both left sides are H, we can equate both right sides:

B+25 = 3(B-25)

B+ 25 = 3B - 75

B- 3B = -75 -25

-2B = -100

B = 50

Then since H = B + 25, H = 75. We can also use that H = 3(B-25), we would also get that H =75.

Now J = H+48 = 75 + 48 = 123

Adding all together:

J + H + B = 123 + 75 + 50 = 248
