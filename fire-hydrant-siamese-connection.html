9. When she is outdoors, tasha, the dog, is tied to a stake in the center of a circular area of radius 24 feet. the angle between her dog house and her favorite hydrant is 165 degrees. what is the length of the arc from her dog house to the hydrant, following minor arc dg, to the nearest foot?


Answer:

Length of the minor arc dg = 69 feet

Explanation:

Step by step calculation:

The formula for determining the length of an arc is expressed as

Length of the arc = θ/360 × 2πr

Solutions:

The θ is the central angle

The r is the radius of the circle.

π is pi with constant whose value is approximately 3.14

Given,

r = 24 feet

θ = 165 degrees

In a circle, there is 360 degrees angle

To calculate below:

Length of the minor arc dg = θ / 360 degrees * 2 + π * r

Length of the minor arc dg = 165degress /360 × 2 × 3.14 × 24

Length of the minor arc dg = 69.08 feet or rounding off to one decimal place of 69 feet

Length of the minor arc dg = 69 feet

Answer:

The Siamese or Thai language contains a great variety of compositions of every species. Their poems and songs are very numerous, as are their Cheritras, or historical and mythological fables. Many of the Siamese princes have been celebrated for their poetical powers, and several of their historical and moral compositions are still preserved. In all their compositions they either affect a plain, simple narrative, or an unconnected and abrupt style of short, pithy sentences, of much meaning.
