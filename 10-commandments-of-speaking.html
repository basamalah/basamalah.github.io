21. When speaking, How do you know that a person is instructing, commanding, asking or expressing a strong feeling?​


Answer:

When speaking, you would know that a person is instructing, commanding, asking, or expressing a strong feeling when:  (see below)

Explanation:

When speaking, you would know that a person is instructing, commanding, asking, or expressing a strong feeling when:

Instructing when the speaker uses words to teach or educate on how she/he wants you to respond to an essay, assignment, or exam question.

Commanding when the speaker uses imperative verbs because they tell or request someone to do something. Example: Sit down.

Asking when the speaker uses words to ask something and usually has "please".  

Expressing a strong feeling when the speaker uses interjections like congrats or alas or words to express a strong feeling and sudden emotion.
