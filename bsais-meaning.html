Type of set-up which serve as a basis of all measurements and a means of comparison.

Any of the systems employed in the process of linking numbers with measures physical quantities and events is referred to as a measurement system. Although temperature, brightness, pressure, and electric current are now included in the idea of weights and measures there were formerly only four basic measurements: mass (weight), distance or length, area, and volume (liquid or grain measure). Of course, the final three are intertwined.

The principles of uniformity, units, and standards are fundamental to the entire concept of weights and measures. Uniformity, the cornerstone of every weights and measures system, necessitates accurate, repeatable mass and length standards as well as agreed-upon units. The name of a number, such as kilogram or pound, is referred to as a unit.A standard is the physical representation of a unit, such as the platinum-iridium cylinder used as the standard kilogram by the International Bureau of Weights and Measures in Paris.
