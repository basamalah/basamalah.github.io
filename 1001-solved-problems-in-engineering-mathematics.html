21. Find the value of 1001*1001


Both those numbers are 1 on either side of 1000. The answer is to square 1000 and subtract 1 and I'll show you why. First try in your head with some smaller numbers. 10 x 10, then 9 x 11, then 8 x 12. Noticing a pattern? When you multiple 2 numbers together that are plus and minus the same amount from another number (8 and 12 are both 2 from 10 etc) you can obtain the answer by squaring the middle number and subtracting the square of the distance from the first numbers. Eg 8 x 12 = 10^2 - 2^2 It may look like magic but expand (n-a)(n+a) and see what you get. So 1001 × 999 = 1000^2 - 1^2 = 1000000 - 1 = 999999

25. Determine the units digit of 4^1001 or 4 exponent 1001?​


Solution:

For 4 with increasing exponent by 1, determine each last digit:

[tex]4^1=4\rightarrow 4\\4^2=16\rightarrow 6\\4^3=64\rightarrow 4\\4^4=256\rightarrow 6[/tex]

and so on.

Notice that odd exponent will result the last digit of 4 whereas even exponent will result the last digit of 6. Since 1001 is an odd exponent, therefore the last digit is 4.
