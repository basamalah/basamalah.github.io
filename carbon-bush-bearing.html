1. What literary device is used in this sentence, “The big, bad bear scared all the baby bunnies by the bushes.”


Answer:

Alliteration

Explanation:

In literature, alliteration is the conspicuous repetition of identical initial consonant sounds in successive or closely associated syllables within a group of words, even those spelled differently. As a method of linking words for effect, alliteration is also called head rhyme or initial rhyme.

7. defferent rose bushes are grown in a greenhouse for two months . the number of flowers on each bush is counted at the end of the experiment​


Answer:

Independent Variable - The type of rose bush.

The independent variable is something you change. In a good experiment, you should only have one independent variable so things don't get confusing. In this experiment, the type of rose bush would be the independent variable. It is the one thing that is being changed.

Dependent Variable - The number of flowers.

The dependent variable is the data that is obtained in an experiment. In this experiment, the number of flowers would be the dependent variable. The amount of flowers is the data from the experiment.

Constant Variable - Soil, light, water, greenhouse, amount of time.

The constant variable is something in the experiment that stays constant and doesn't change. In this experiment, it mentioned having the timespan of two months and being in the same greenhouse, those are constants. Some other things that aren't mentioned but would be constants are the type of soil, color and amount of light, amount of water, etc.

16. Gilbert has 95 rose bushes to plant in rows. He wants to put the same number of bushes in each row with no bushes left over. How many rows of bushes could Gilbert make?


Answer:

To find the number of rows of bushes that Gilbert could make with 95 rose bushes, you can divide the number of bushes by the number of bushes per row to find the number of rows.

Since Gilbert wants to put the same number of bushes in each row with no bushes left over, he will need to find the greatest whole number that is less than or equal to 95 and divides evenly into 95.

One way to do this is to start with the highest possible value and work your way down, checking to see if it divides evenly into 95. For example, if Gilbert starts with 95 bushes per row, he would have 1 row of 95 bushes. If he tries 94 bushes per row, he would have 95 / 94 = 1.010638298, which is not a whole number, so 94 bushes per row would not work. If he tries 93 bushes per row, he would have 95 / 93 = 1.034482759, which is also not a whole number. However, when he tries 92 bushes per row, he has 95 / 92 = 1.038043478, which is a whole number. Therefore, Gilbert could make 1.038043478 rows of 92 bushes each.

Therefore, Gilbert could make 1 row of 92 bushes.
