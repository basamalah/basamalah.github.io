28. List down the 10 steps (chronological) of wearing the Personal Protective Equipment of a Fire Personnel.


List down the 10 steps (chronological) of wearing the Personal Protective Equipment of a Fire Personnel.

1. A sort of headgear is a hard hat.

Hard helmets protect industrial workers from head injuries caused by falling, flying, or fixed objects.

Protective headgear must be resistant to penetration, shock, and water, as well as be slow-burning.

2. Foot Guards, Leggings, and Safety Shoes

Workers are protected in the workplace by leggings, foot guards, and safety shoes against a number of hazards, including falling, rolling, or sharp objects; damp, slippery, or hot surfaces; and electrical hazards (headgear)

3. Earplugs and earmuffs

Employees wear earplugs and earmuffs to protect themselves from excessive noise, which can cause permanent hearing loss and stress (headgear)

4.Gloves

Gloves, finger guards, and arm coverings will protect employees from cuts, chemical and thermal burns, and punctures. (headgear)

5.Eye protection is essential.

In some workplaces, eye protection is required to protect personnel from flying particles, hot liquids, molten metal, chemical fumes, and dangerous radiation.
