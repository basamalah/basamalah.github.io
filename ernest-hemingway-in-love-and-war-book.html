7. Summary for Ernest Hemingway’s “Hills Like White Elephants” need it ASAP


Answer:

Ernest Hemingway’s “Hills Like White Elephants” is a short story that depicts a conversation between an American man and a woman, who is referred to as Jig. The two are waiting at a train station in Spain and discussing the possibility of Jig having an abortion. The tension between the two characters is palpable, as they struggle to communicate their feelings and desires. Jig is torn between her love for the American man and her desire to keep the baby, while the American man is focused on his own desires and unwilling to commit to anything. Through the dialogue, Hemingway explores themes of communication, decision-making, and the complexities of human relationships. The story ends with the train arriving, and the two characters making their decision as they prepare to embark on a new journey.

Explanation:

The story’s title, ‘A Day’s Wait’, refers to the boy’s day spent waiting to die, as he believes he will. However, the father, who narrates the story, doesn’t realise that boy believes he is approaching imminent death, and simply ascribes his son’s strange behaviour (such as staring straight ahead) to the illness. The father tries to read a book to keep his son entertained in his sickbed, but the kid doesn’t seem to be listening. Then the father goes out with his dog for a walk.

When the boy’s father returns and talks to his son, he realises his son’s fault and explains it to him – using the analogy of confusing kilometres with miles to explain the difference between Celsius and Fahrenheit temperatures – and the son feels relieved and begins his recovery.

Through their dialogue, Hemingway explores the themes of communication, control, and the choice between freedom and responsibility, ultimately leaving the reader to interpret the couple's fate

In Ernest Hemingway's short story "Hills Like White Elephants," the author employs minimalism and symbolic language to explore the complexities of communication, emotional detachment, and personal choice, as a couple navigates a life-altering decision within the seemingly mundane setting of a train station, ultimately reflecting on the challenging nature of human relationships and the importance of individual agency.

Answer:

In the short story "The Killers" by Ernest Hemingway, the characters of Nick Adams and Al are directly characterized through the descriptions given by the narrator, while the characters of Ole Andreson and the two killers are indirectly characterized through their actions and dialogue.


24. Ernest Hemingway is a renowned author.He is famous all over the world​


Answer:

Ernest Hemingway served in World War I and worked in journalism before publishing his story collection In Our Time. He was renowned for novels like The Sun Also Rises, A Farewell to Arms, For Whom the Bell Tolls and The Old Man and the Sea, which won the Pulitzer Prize in 1953. In 1954, Hemingway won the Nobel Prize.

Hemingway produced most of his work between the mid-1920s and the mid-1950s, and he was awarded the 1954 Nobel Prize in Literature. He published seven novels, six short-story collections, and two nonfiction works.


25. Summary for Ernest Hemingway's “Hills Like White Elephants” need this ASAP


Answer:

Ernest Hemingway's “Hills Like White Elephants” is a short story about a couple's conversation at a train station in Spain. The story follows the couple as they discuss an operation that the woman, Jig, is considering having. The man, who is never named, is trying to convince her to get the operation, while Jig is ambivalent and uncertain. Throughout their conversation, they discuss the implications of the operation, the relationship between them, and the views of their society. Ultimately, the story ends with the couple's decision left unresolved, with the man saying, "It's all right. We'll be all right."
