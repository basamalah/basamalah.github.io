7. tell the relationship between the two. termites and cellulose? ​


Answer:

Not all termites consume cellulose, but those that do have a specialized midgut teeming with microbes (primarily protists, bacteria, and archaea) that break down the cellulose. The relationship between the termites and the microbes is a mutualism, because both species benefit from the relationship.

21. The foundation of your house has about 1,200 termites. The termites grow at a rate of about 2.4% per day. How long until the number of termites doubles?​


Answer:

To solve this problem, we can use the formula for exponential growth:

N(t) = N0 * (1 + r)^t

where:

N(t) is the number of termites at time t

N0 is the initial number of termites (1,200)

r is the growth rate per day (2.4% or 0.024)

t is the time (in days) that has elapsed

To find out how long it takes for the number of termites to double, we need to solve for t when N(t) = 2N0.

2N0 = N0 * (1 + r)^t

Dividing both sides by N0, we get:

2 = (1 + r)^t

Taking the natural logarithm of both sides, we get:

ln(2) = t * ln(1 + r)

Solving for t, we get:

t = ln(2) / ln(1 + r)

Substituting the values of N0 and r, we get:

t = ln(2) / ln(1 + 0.024) ≈ 29.2 days

Therefore, it takes about 29.2 days for the number of termites to double


22. What is the role of termite mounds in ecosystem? ​


Answer:

Termites help plants grow by enriching the soil with nutrients and digging tunnels that improve water flow to roots. As a result, ecosystems with termite mounds may be more resistant to losing vegetation, especially during droughts.

Termite mounds retain more water than the surrounding soil. The retained moisture
results in more vegetation than would otherwise grow in this climate, reducing the chances of desertification. Termites bring organic material to their mounds, concentrating nutrients and fertilizing the soil on and around mounds.

27. What is the Termite mound​


Answer:
