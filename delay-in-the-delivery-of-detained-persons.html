4. what is the difference between detain and detention?​


Answer:

The main difference between detention and arrest is whether a person is charged with a crime or not. In case of a person being detained, he is not formally accused of committing a crime but is simply restricted and kept in police custody on a reasonable suspicion. ... Thus, detention may be seen as a lesser form of arrest.

23. if you are arrested or detained,what are your're rights?explain.​


Answer:

If you are arrested or detained, you have the following rights:

1. Right to remain silent: You have the right to remain silent and not incriminate yourself. This means you do not have to answer any questions or provide any information that could be used against you in a court of law.

2. Right to an attorney: You have the right to consult with an attorney before answering any questions. If you cannot afford an attorney, one will be appointed for you.

3. Right to know the charges: You have the right to be informed of the charges against you. This means that you must be told what crime you are being accused of.

4. Right to a fair and speedy trial: You have the right to a trial without undue delay. This means that you cannot be held in detention for an extended period of time without being brought to trial.

5. Right to habeas corpus: You have the right to challenge your detention in court. This means that you can ask a judge to review the reasons for your detention and determine if it is lawful.

It is important to note that these rights may vary depending on the jurisdiction and the circumstances of the arrest or detention. It is recommended to seek the advice of an attorney to fully understand your rights in such situations.
