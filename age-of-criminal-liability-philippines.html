Lowering the age of criminal liability from 15 years old to 9 years old is a controversial issue that has been discussed and debated in many countries. Proponents of lowering the age argue that children as young as 9 years old can already distinguish right from wrong, and therefore, should be held accountable for their actions. They also argue that lowering the age will deter children from committing crimes and protect society from the actions of young offenders.

On the other hand, opponents of the proposal argue that children under the age of 15 are not yet fully matured and do not have the same level of judgment and responsibility as adults. They also argue that punishing young children for their actions will not address the root causes of the problem, such as poverty, lack of education, and neglect.

It is important to note that different countries have different laws regarding the age of criminal liability. In some countries, the age is as low as 7 years old, while in others, it is as high as 18 years old. The decision to lower or maintain the age of criminal liability should be based on a careful consideration of the specific context and circumstances of each country

25. Ano ang iyong pananaw tungkol sa pagbaba ng Age of Criminal Liability sa Pilipinas?


Tinutulan ng ilang grupong nagsusulong sa karapatan ng mga kabataan ang planong ibaba ang minimum age sa criminal liability.

Ang minimum age sa criminal liability ay ang pinakamababang edad kung saan ang isang tao ay maaaring sampahan ng kaso sa korte at makulong.

Dito sa Pilipinas,  batay sa ilalim ng Juvenile Justice and Welfare Act of 2006 na inamyendahan ng Republic Act 10630  ang isang batang ang edad ay labinlimang taong gulang o mas bata pa na lumabag sa batas ay ipinalalagay na walang kriminal na pananagutan pero kailangang sumailalim sa isang intervention program.

Sa kasalukuyan may mga panukalang batas ang nakahain ngayon sa Kongreso na naglalayong ibaba ang minimum age ng criminal liability sa siyam na taong gulang, bagay na tinututulan ng ilang Child Rights Group.

Ayon sa UNICEF Philippines, Child Rights Network at Philippine Action for Youth Offenders (PAYO), hindi maaaring ibaba ang  minimum age sa criminal liability ng mga menor de edad dahil nakalagda ang Pilipinas sa United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child.

Ibig sabihin, kinakailangang sundin ng gobyerno ng Pilipinas ang mandato na protektahan ang karapatan ng mga bata.
