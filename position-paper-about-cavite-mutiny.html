The mutiny was quickly crushed, but the Spanish regime under the reactionary governor Rafael de Izquierdo magnified the incident and used it as an excuse to clamp down on those Filipinos who had been calling for governmental reform. A number of Filipino intellectuals were seized and accused of complicity with the mutineers. After a brief trial, three priests—José Burgos, Jacinto Zamora, and Mariano Gómez—were publicly executed. 30. Reaction paper for “There will be no 1896 Philippine Revolution if there was no 1872 Cavite Mutiny.”


The 1872 Cavite Mutiny is an important event in Philippine history as it is considered to be the spark that lit the flame of revolution. Led by Filipino soldiers of the Spanish garrison, the revolt was an expression of Filipinos’ dissatisfaction towards Spanish rule. The Cavite Mutiny marked the beginning of what would eventually become the Philippine Revolution of 1896.

This event set the groundwork that would allow Filipino revolutionaries such as Andres Bonifacio and Emilio Aguinaldo to further lead the struggle against Spanish rule. Bonifacio formed the Kataas-taasang, Kagalang-galangan, Katipunan ng mga Anak ng Bayan in 1892, a group that fought actively for independence. In 1896, Aguinaldo declared Philippine independence and led the Philippine forces during the Philippine-American War.

Without the 1872 Cavite Mutiny, Philippine independence and the Philippine Revolution of 1896 may have never occurred. It was a catalyst for Filipino leaders to organize and fight for Philippine independence. The act of defiance shown by the Filipino soldiers served as an inspiration for others to take part in the revolution against Spanish rule.

In conclusion, the 1872 Cavite Mutiny was the beginning of the Philippine Revolution of 1896, and without it the revolution may have never occurred. It was a symbol of Filipino courage, determination, and perseverance which further served as an inspiration for other Filipino revolutionaries. The 1872 Cavite Mutiny is thus a significant part of Philippine history and its legacy will remain as a reminder to all Filipinos of the power of collective action.
