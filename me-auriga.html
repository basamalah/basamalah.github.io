STARS

The word "star" refers to a huge luminous heavenly body, which is generally composed of different gas elements and produces its own light. One of the best examples of a star is the Sun, the center of the Solar System.

Composition of a star:

Stars are made mostly of hydrogen (75%) and helium (24%), and also have a small amount (1%) of other gaseous elements.

What is a constellation?

The word "constellation" refers to the group or cluster of stars that commonly forms a distinct pattern or shape that can be seen from the sky at night.

Why are some stars not visible in September but visible in March? 

- This is because planet Earth revolves around the sun. As it rotates on its own axis and travels around the sun at the same time, the night side of the planet faces a different part of outer space.

That is why the position of the stars throughout the globe changes because of lateral motion depending on our location or position on the planet. Due to this, different constellations are visible during March, but not during September or other times of the year.

Describe the movement of the Stars in the night sky Q8. How is the motion of the Stars similar to the motion of the Sun?​


Answer:

When observing the stars in the night sky from 7pm to 11pm, it is noticeable that their position changes. One or two constellations, such as Auriga and Orion, can be focused on to better observe this movement. Comparing the position of the stars at 7pm to their position at 11pm, it can be seen that they are no longer in their original position, having moved across the sky. The stars appear to move in a circular motion around the North Star, with some stars even disappearing from view as they dip below the horizon. This movement is similar to the motion of the sun, which rises in the east and sets in the west due to the Earth's rotation on its axis. Therefore, the apparent movement of the stars through the night sky is a result of the Earth's rotation on its axis.
