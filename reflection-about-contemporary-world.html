14. Reflection essay about contemporary moral problems on human life


Answer:

Our capacity for impersonal reflection, for looking at our own perspective from without, as part of a world that exists independently of us, is our most distinctive trait as human beings. It finds its most striking expression in our moral thinking. For we are moral beings insofar as we stand back from our individual concerns and see in the good of others, in and of itself, a reason for action on our part. It is not, to be sure, in morality alone that we exercise this power of impersonal reflection. We do so too, whenever we set about weighing the evidence for some belief without regard for what we would like to be true or for what common opinion would say. Yet nowhere does this self-transcendence show forth more vividly than when we turn our attention from our own happiness to that of another, taking the same immediate interest in that person's good—just because it is his or hers—as we naturally harbor for our own. In this essay, I explore the way that the moral point of view is shaped by the nature of impersonal reflection and thus constitutes a signal expression of our humanity.

This process has been facilitated by advances in technology, transportation, and communication, which have made it easier for people, goods, and ideas to move across borders.

24. Reflection Paper about Traditional Arts and Contemporary Arts.​


Answer:

Traditional arts and contemporary arts are two distinct yet interconnected forms of artistic expression. Traditional arts are often rooted in cultural practices and beliefs that have been passed down through generations, while contemporary arts are more recent forms of artistic expression that reflect the current social and cultural context.

One of the main differences between traditional and contemporary arts is their approach to innovation. Traditional arts tend to value the preservation of the past, often maintaining the same techniques and materials used by previous generations. In contrast, contemporary arts prioritize experimentation, pushing boundaries and exploring new techniques and media.

However, while traditional arts may appear stagnant on the surface, they are in fact constantly evolving, adapting to new influences and challenges. This is particularly evident in the way traditional arts are passed down from one generation to the next, with each new generation adding their own interpretations and innovations to the art form.

Contemporary arts, on the other hand, are often more focused on individual expression and pushing the boundaries of what is considered art. Contemporary artists are not bound by tradition or conventions, and are free to experiment with new techniques and materials.

Despite these differences, traditional and contemporary arts are not mutually exclusive. In fact, many contemporary artists draw inspiration from traditional arts, incorporating elements of traditional techniques, materials and themes into their work. This fusion of traditional and contemporary styles has resulted in a vibrant and diverse artistic landscape, where artists are constantly exploring new ways to express themselves.

In conclusion, both traditional and contemporary arts have their own unique strengths and value. While traditional arts are deeply rooted in culture and history, contemporary arts are more focused on individual expression and innovation. However, rather than seeing these two forms of artistic expression as separate entities, it is important to recognize their interconnectedness and the ways in which they inform and influence each other.


25. make a speech about contemporary world​

As we gather here today, I want to talk to you about the state of our contemporary world. We live in a time of great change and uncertainty, with challenges and opportunities that are unprecedented in human history.

One of the most significant developments of our time is the rapid pace of technological advancement. In just the last few decades, we have seen the rise of the internet, mobile phones, and other technologies that have transformed the way we communicate, access information, and do business. These technologies have brought us closer together and have opened up new possibilities for human progress.

But with these advances also come new challenges. The rapid spread of technology has led to the proliferation of fake news, online harassment, and other forms of digital manipulation. It has also contributed to the growing gap between the rich and the poor, as well as the erosion of privacy and other personal rights.

Another key issue facing our world today is the ongoing threat of climate change. The scientific evidence is clear: human activities, such as the burning of fossil fuels and deforestation, are contributing to the warming of the planet and the disruption of natural ecosystems. If we do not act now to address this crisis, we risk catastrophic consequences for ourselves and future generations.

In addition to these challenges, we must also confront the reality of inequality and injustice. Despite the progress we have made in recent years, many people around the world still suffer from discrimination, poverty, and other forms of oppression. We must work together to create a more equitable and fair society for all.

So where do we go from here? The first step is to recognize the gravity of the situation and to take action. We must use our collective power as citizens, consumers, and voters to demand change from our leaders and institutions. We must also do our part as individuals to reduce our own impact on the environment and to support the causes we believe in.

We are at a crossroads in human history. The choices we make today will determine the course of the future. Let us work together to build a better world for ourselves and for generations to come.

Thank you.
