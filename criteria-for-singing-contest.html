•Stage Presence/Audience Impact- 10%

•Diction- 20%

•Vocal Quality- 35%

•Rhythmic Interpretation- 10%

3. criteria for judging dance contest


Answer:

This is an example of criteria for judging a dance contest:

•Choreography (Creativity, Artistry, Style & Originality) - 40%

•Execution - 10%

•Audience Impact- 10%

•(Timing & Coordination, Projection & Expression) - 30%

•Costume - 10%

Why is criteria for judging important?

•It is important for judges to know how to score and important for contestants to know what they should improve and focus on their performance.

#AnswerForTrees

In judging a dance contest, it's either you or the organizers should have a prepared criteria. However, here are the basic criteria for judging a dance contest:

A) Choreography - this includes the sequence of dance steps in a dance act or performance. This criteria must include the creativity, artistry, style, and originality of dance steps.

B) Execution - this includes the projection and expression, timing and coordination of each member during the performance

C) Costume - this is also a vital part in a dance act. Costumes make the performance come to life depending on what they are portraying/depending on the concept of the dance act/performance

D) Audience impact - audience's claps, chants and cheers is also part of the criteria because performances are made for an audience that is why their reaction matters as well.

Criteria for judging should be divided in percentages totaling 100 percent. For example, Choreography is 40 percent, Execution is 30 percent, Costume is 20 percent, Audience impact is 10 percent for a total of 100 percent.

Criteria matters for it to be fair to all the performances being judged.

18. Criteria for judging zumba dance contest


A zumba dance is a form of exercise fitness program that involves dance and aerobic movements performed through energetic music. The program was developed by Colombian dancer and choreographer Alberto Perez in the 1990s. A zumba dance can also be held as a competition among group of dancers. The criteria for judging will depend on the organizers of the contest. Among the criteria that they could use are choreography (30%), choice of music and costumes (20%), mastery and execution (30%), and audience impact (20%).
