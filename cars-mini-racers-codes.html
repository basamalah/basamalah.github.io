22. A car racer was initially at rest. He accelerated uniformly at 11 m/s² for a distance of 200 meters. How long did it take the car to cover this distance?​


Answer:

We can use the equation:

d = 1/2 * a * t^2

where d is the distance, a is the acceleration, and t is the time.

We are given that the car accelerated uniformly at 11 m/s² for a distance of 200 meters, so we have:

d = 200 m

a = 11 m/s²

Plugging these values into the equation above, we get:

200 = 1/2 * 11 * t^2

Simplifying this equation, we get:

t^2 = 400/11

Taking the square root of both sides, we get:

t = sqrt(400/11) ≈ 6.43 seconds

Therefore, it took the car approximately 6.43 seconds to cover a distance of 200 meters while accelerating uniformly at 11 m/s².

A Nissan Sentra is stopped at a traffic light. When the light turns green, the driver accelerates so that the car's speedometer reads 10m/s after 5 seconds. What is the car's acceleration assuming it is constant? 3. A racer accelerates from rest at a constant rate of 2.0m/s^2 (a) How fast will the racer be going at the end of 6.0s? (b) How far has the racer traveled during this time?​


Answer:

1. The velocity of the car at the end of the race can be calculated using the equation:

v = at, where v is the velocity, a is the acceleration, and t is the time.

Since the car starts from rest, we can assume that the initial velocity v0 = 0.

The time it takes to cover a distance of 250m while accelerating at 9 m/s2 is :

t = (v - v0) / a = (v - 0) / 9 = v / 9

To find the distance covered, we can use the equation:

d = v0t + (1/2)at^2

d = 0 * t + (1/2) * 9 * t^2

As we know,

d = 250m

We can substitute this into the above equation, solve for time and then use the time to find velocity:

t = sqrt (2d / a) = sqrt (2 * 250 / 9) = 15 m/s

v = at = 9 * 15 = 135 m/s

2. To find the acceleration of the car, we can use the equation:

a = (v - v0) / t = (10 - 0) / 5 = 2 m/s^2

3. (a) The final velocity of the racer can be found using the equation:

v = v0 + at = 0 + 2 * 6 = 12 m/s

(b) To find the distance traveled, we can use the equation:

d = v0t + (1/2)at^2 = 0 * 6 + (1/2) * 2 * 6^2 = 36 m
