The Ecosystem is composed of Biotic (Living) and Abiotic (Non-Living) components in the environment that are connected to each other in a form of the food web.

        There are 3 Main Classifications of Ecosystem:FreshwaterMarineTerrestrial

Wider and collaborative biodiversity in ecosystems, living and nonliving species, and individuals leads to more excellent stability.A High Biodiversity has a greater advantage in the stability of an ecosystem since it has a wide variety of living organisms that can adapt to any kind of weather or natural disturbance and yet was able to continue their reproductions and survival.Biodiversity is important for the Ecosystem since it defines the connection and relation of different kinds of Ecosystems to one another, it serves as the collection of territorial habitats where every organism can survive and reproduce.
