to find the cost of one cell phone, all you have to do is divide the total cost by the number of units.

you'll have:

[tex] \frac{6a^2 + 5ab - 6b^2}{3a-2b} \\ factor~the~numerator \\ \frac{6a^2+5ab-6b^2}{3a-2b} = \frac{(3a-2b)(2a+3b)}{3a-2b} \\ cancel~out~(3a-2b) \\ therefore~the~cost ~of~one~cell~phone~is~ (2a+3b)[/tex]

24. more Sample Problem: the cost of phone calls varies directly as the total number of minutes of calls. If the duration of calls in a month is 120 min, the cost is P750, At this rate, how much will 80 min of calls cost? How many minutes of use will cost P1375?​

As the cost is proportional to the time you can set up a direct proportion using equivalent fractions:

=1.35

Answer:

a) how much will 80 min of calls cost?

Answer: P500

b) how many minutes of use will cost P1375?

Answer: 220 minutes of calls

Step-by-step explanation:

Let y be the cost of phone calls.

Let x be the number of minutes of calls.

Equation: y = kx

Find k (constant).

y = kx

(750) = k(120)

750/120 = 120k/120

k = 6.25

Hence, each minute cost of call costs P6.25.

New Equation: y = 6.25x

Finding Unknown:

a) how much will 80 min of calls cost?

Solution:

Substitute the given from the equation.

y = 6.25x

y = 6.25(80)

y = 500

Therefore, 80 minutes of calls cost P500.

b) how many minutes of use will cost P1375?

Solution:

Substitute the given from the equation.

y = 6.25x

(1375) = 6.25x

1375/6.25 = 6.25x/6.25

x = 220

Therefore, 220 minutes of calls will cost P1375.
