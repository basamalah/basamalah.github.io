5. a basic element of western and eastern system of musical notation prior to the five line staff.​


A neume (/ˈnjuːm/; sometimes spelled neum) is the basic element of Western and Eastern systems of musical notation prior to the invention of five-line staff notation. The earliest neumes were inflective marks that indicated the general shape but not necessarily the exact notes or rhythms to be sung.

I believe that the essay "How Much is Too Much" by Sharon Palmer highlights the important issue of overconsumption in our society. In the essay, Palmer discusses how our society has become obsessed with accumulating more and more material possessions, and how this constant desire for more has led to negative consequences for both individuals and the environment.

One of the key points that Palmer makes in her essay is that our obsession with consuming more has contributed to the problem of waste and pollution. As she points out, the production of goods requires a great deal of resources, and the disposal of these goods often results in harmful effects on the environment. This is particularly true when it comes to disposable products, such as plastic water bottles or single-use bags, which are often used once and then discarded.

Another point that Palmer emphasizes is that our culture of overconsumption can have negative effects on our mental and physical health. She suggests that the constant pressure to accumulate more and more can lead to feelings of stress and anxiety, and can even contribute to mental health issues such as depression. Additionally, the constant desire to consume can lead to unhealthy eating habits and can contribute to the development of chronic diseases such as obesity and diabetes.

Overall, I believe that Palmer's essay is a thought-provoking exploration of the issue of overconsumption in our society. She raises important questions about the impact that our constant desire for more has on both the environment and our own well-being, and encourages readers to think critically about their own consumption habits. I think that it is crucial for us to consider the effects of our actions on both ourselves and the world around us, and to strive to find a balance between consuming what we need and being mindful of the impact that our choices have on others and the environment.
