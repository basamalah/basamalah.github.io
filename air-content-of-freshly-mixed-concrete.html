12. How do we prepare concrete mix in making hollow blox


Answer:

Mixing, molding, curing, and cubing are the four basic procedures for making hollow blocks.

The first step is to locate a convenient location where you can produce hollow blocks. The area should be as large as possible.

Make a one-sided open box with a 150mm (thickness), 200mm (width), and 300mm (length) inner cavity, which is the common size for hollow blocks.

Water, cement, gravel, and sand make up the concrete mix. Use the largest container that will fit in your workspace. When mixing the cement, sand, and gravel in the container, use a 1:2:3 ratio. Pour water until the concrete mixture is good enough to pour into the mold. In a mixing basin, combine the water. Continuously whisk the mixture with a rod.

The concrete mixture needs to be poured into the mold. Shake or agitate the mold to ensure that the mold and concrete are aligned at the extreme ends.

The hollow blocks should be for curing. The process of curing takes around two weeks to give the hollow blocks the required compression strength. The hollow block must be kept in the mold for 24 hours for drying.

The final section is all about marketing and sales. You can develop a website to promote your hollow block or place a modest advertisement in the newspaper about your hollow block services after you have the finished product.
