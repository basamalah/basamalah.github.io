13. pahelpBased on Rizal's poems and academic performance in Ateneo, how did he view and give importance to himself through education?​


Answer:

Based on Rizal's writings and actions, he placed a high value on education and saw it as crucial for personal growth and development. Through his poems, he expressed his love for learning and the pursuit of knowledge, which he believed would help him fulfill his mission as a social reformer.

In Ateneo, Rizal excelled academically and was known for his exceptional intelligence and academic performance. This demonstrated his commitment to education and his belief in its power to shape his future and the future of others. He saw himself as a role model for young Filipinos and believed that education was the key to unlocking their potential and empowering them to make positive changes in their communities.

Overall, Rizal viewed education as a means to not only improve himself, but also to contribute to the betterment of society. He placed a great deal of importance on education and saw it as essential for personal growth and development.

Rizal wrote the poem "The Song of the Traveler" to express his love for his country and his desire to see it free from the oppression of foreign colonizers. 3

4
