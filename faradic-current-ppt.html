What is environmental ethics/what does it mean?

It is the discipline in philosophy that people study about the truthful relationship of human beings, and also the value/importance of the environment.

What is an example of environmental ethics?

Things like water and air pollution, the use of natural resources, loss of biodiversity, destruction of the community of living creatures, and global climate change are all part of the environmental ethics.

What is the purpose of environmental ethics?

The purpose of environmental ethics is that they maintain to provide moral grounds for social policies aimed at protecting the earth's environment and setting environmental humiliation/shame right.

23. orgin of..1. horsepower2.watt3.farad​


Answer:

Horsepower

The term "horsepower" came about in the 1700's, when inventor James Watt was trying to explain the power of steam engines. He spent hours watching horses and calculating how much power they produced in a minute. Watt calculated that an average pony produced 22,000 foot-pounds of effort every minute.

Watt

The "watt" is named after James Watt who improved the Newcomen engine with his own steam engine in 1776. Watt's invention was fundamental for the Industrial Revolution.


Farad

The term "farad" was originally coined by Latimer Clark and Charles Bright in 1861, in honor of Michael Faraday, for a unit of charge amount, but by 1873, the farad had evolved into a unit of capacitance.
