17. what are the challenges to entrepreneurs in the light of the so-called borderless world? discuss​


What are the challenges to entrepreneurs in the light of the so-called borderless world? discuss

Major advancements in communications and computer technologies, as well as substantial and rapid growth in transportation, international organizations and deals, and firm commitments to globalization, have culminated in significant economic and financial interconnectedness between nations and economies, resulting in a “BORDERLESS WORLD.” Capital, Technology, and Information are no longer nationalities. They literally flow openly in and out of national boundaries.

Multinational organizations such as the World Bank, World Trade Organization, and International Monetary Fund have a critical role to play, particularly if they expand and continue to have the requisite expertise on economic issues such as development, monetary policy, and trade. With these relatively sudden developments come risks, this time with significant repercussions, such as the latest meltdown of stock markets, which has created a recessionary atmosphere due to uncertainty and credit tightness.

I can tell you from my own perspective as a technologist, corporate leader, and entrepreneur:  These global developments necessitate a focus on a variety of topics that affect each and every one of us.  

However, in addition to considering the international influx of goods and services and financial resources, exchange rates, inflation, malnutrition and agriculture, urbanization, and the position of women, we must also remember the following: Businesses that used to be low-uncertainty, “core” businesses no longer exist. Instead, they are under pressure from the same uncertainties that have already existed in new industries.

Unfortunately, companies currently continue to operate using traditional / mainstream business structures and tools that they have used for many years. These templates and tools, in my opinion, are harmful in the advancement of technologies or the establishment of new industries because they place knowledge standards that are unattainable in a constantly evolving and uncertain world. Consider planning.   In a high-uncertainty environment, whether the numbers and other targets in the strategy are met later is a strong indicator of a manager's success. This makes no sense because you actually do not have enough information to reliably foresee what will occur. Insisting on managers sticking to the schedule will contribute to a risky escalation of loyalty to a failed operation or unwarranted spending in the core market that exceeds the returns an organization would earn. Furthermore, imposing the assumption that one will'make one's numbers' or 'deliver the earnings' is impractical as the basic market logic for an operation has changed.

26. pahelp poNegative effects of economic globalization are felt by poor countries and become victims of the so-called "borderless world"​


Economic globalization has been a major force that has driven global growth, increased competitiveness, and created opportunities for countries and companies to expand their operations globally. However, this process has not been without its drawbacks, and the negative effects of economic globalization can have significant consequences, particularly for those living in poverty.

One of the primary negative effects of economic globalization has been the concentration of wealth and power in the hands of a few individuals and countries. This concentration results in inequality both between and within countries, with wealthier individuals and countries having access to a greater share of resources and opportunities. This inequality is particularly prevalent in developing countries, where large multinational corporations often exploit cheap labor, often working in hazardous conditions, to increase profits. This situation causes a situation known as exploitation, which is harmful to the poor.

Moreover, economic globalization has brought with it a range of environmental challenges, including pollution, deforestation, and climate change. These challenges disproportionately affect the poor and vulnerable, who are often more exposed to environmental risks, and have fewer resources to cope with environmental disasters.

The rise of multinational corporations has been accompanied by the erosion of local industries and the displacement of local workers, leading to unemployment and underemployment, especially in rural areas. This causes an economic instability that results in a decline in living standards for many individuals, particularly those living in poverty, who have fewer options in terms of education and employment.

In addition to economic instability, cultural and social patterns face a threat. This occurs because economic globalization often involves the spread of Western cultural norms, which can erode local traditions and values. Also, the rate of crime constitutes another critical threat to society, which inevitably affects the poor. Crime escalating in some places as a direct result of increasing unemployment, deprives peoples of their hard-earned possessions, and sometimes inflicts physical pain.

In conclusion, while economic globalization has had a range of positive impacts, including increased growth, employment opportunities, and access to goods and services, it has also had significant negative effects on the poor, including increased inequality, environmental risks, unemployment, cultural erasure, and crime. There is a need for greater regulation of multinational corporations, fair labor practices, policies to promote environmental protection, and measures to protect local industries and workers. Only then can economic globalization promote sustainable and inclusive growth that benefits everyone.
