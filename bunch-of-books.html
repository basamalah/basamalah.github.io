13. What a bunch of grapes?


Answer:

The "Bunch of Grapes" refers to the respiratory system or the breathing system; such as: trachea, bronchi, bronchioles, alveoli, nasal cavity, and nostril.

Parts and Functions:Trachea – this is where air passes to and from the lungs when a person breathes.Bronchi – the primary bronchus where air passes that is connected to the trachea that separates left and right lung.Bronchioles – the secondary bronchus or hairlike tubes that serves as the little passageways of air to enter alveoli.Alveoli – tiny bubble-like bunch of airsacs where oxygen and carbon dioxide exchange in the bloodstream.Nostril – the two openings in the nose through which a person breathe and smell.Nasal Cavity – is the inside of nose where little hairs' located that filter air entering before it reaches the lungs.

"Bunch of Grapes" model represent in relation to the breathing system:

The main stem or the largest stem of the grapes represents the trachea, the two branches represents the bronchi, the smallest stems represent the bronchioles, and the individual grapes represents the alveoli.
