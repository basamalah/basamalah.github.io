Sure! I can explain the concepts of scope of study, delimitations, limitations, and scope in the context of the title "effectiveness of the ABM strand in developing entrepreneurship skills."

Scope of Study:

The scope of the study refers to the boundaries of the research or investigation. It defines the extent to which the research will cover the topic or subject matter. In this case, the scope of the study is limited to the effectiveness of the ABM strand in developing entrepreneurship skills.

Delimitations:

Delimitations are the parameters or constraints that the researcher imposes on the study. It sets the boundaries of the research by specifying what the study will include and exclude. For instance, delimitations for this study could be limited to a specific geographical location, certain age group, specific time frame, or specific ABM curriculum.

Limitations:

Limitations refer to the potential constraints or barriers that may affect the study's validity and reliability. These can be practical, theoretical, or methodological in nature. Some examples of limitations in this study could be the availability of data, access to participants, the reliability of measures, or biases in the data collection.

Scope Where, Who, Size, and Topic Limits:

The scope can be further defined by the following factors:

Scope Where: The study may focus on a specific location, region, or country where the ABM strand is implemented.

Scope Who: The study may target a specific group of individuals who have undergone the ABM strand, such as high school graduates, college students, or professionals.

Scope Size: The study may be limited to a specific sample size, such as a certain number of participants or a particular percentage of the population.

Topic Limits: The study may be limited to certain aspects of the ABM strand's effectiveness in developing entrepreneurship skills, such as its impact on financial literacy, decision-making, risk-taking, or innovation.

Overall, the scope of the study, delimitations, limitations, and scope where, who, size, and topic limits will depend on the research objectives and questions.
