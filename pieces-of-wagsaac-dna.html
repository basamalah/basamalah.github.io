16. Compare the foam DNA and mRNA kit pieces. Identify any similarities and differences.


Answer:


Foam DNA and mRNA kits are both designed to help students understand the basic structure and function of DNA and RNA. However, they differ in some key ways.

Similarities:

Both kits use foam pieces to represent the building blocks of nucleic acids: nucleotides.Both kits have color-coded pieces to represent the four nitrogenous bases found in nucleotides: adenine, guanine, cytosine, and thymine/uracil.Both kits allow students to assemble nucleotides into a complementary sequence to illustrate base-pairing rules (A-T and C-G in DNA, A-U and C-G in RNA).


Differences:

The DNA kit typically includes longer foam pieces that represent the sugar-phosphate backbone of DNA, which is not found in the mRNA kit.The mRNA kit includes pieces that represent the 5' cap and poly-A tail, which are not found in the DNA kit.The mRNA kit uses uracil instead of thymine as one of the nitrogenous bases, which is a characteristic feature of RNA.The DNA kit may include additional pieces to illustrate the double-helical structure of DNA.

Overall, both kits serve as valuable tools for teaching the fundamental concepts of genetics and molecular biology, and can be used in combination to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the similarities and differences between DNA and RNA.

19. It is a small piece of circular DNA called?


Answer:

PLASMIDS

Explanation:

A plasmid is a small, often circular DNA molecule found in bacteria and other cells. Plasmids are separate from the bacterial chromosome and replicate independently of it. They generally carry only a small number of genes, notably some associated with antibiotic resistance. Plasmids may be passed between different bacterial cells.
