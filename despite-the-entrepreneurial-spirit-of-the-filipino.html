1. Despite the entrepreneurial spirit of the Filipinos, why do you think that many Filipinos remain poor and that the disparity between the rich and the poor is still very high?​


Answer:

The Philippines is a country made up of approximately 7,000 islands. It has a wealth of natural resources as well as distinct cultural and historical characteristics.

However, the Philippines remains a very poor country.

Filipinos are one of the poorest Asian countries on the island.

Because of the hierarchical nature of Filipino society, there was extreme inequality between rich and poor. It resulted in significant poverty. Under Spanish rule, Filipino society was divided into different social classes. It gave rise to a slew of social institutions in the Philippines. It resulted in the accumulation of aggregate wealth by certain groups of people in society. It is not the responsibility of society or the government to lift poor people out of poverty. Aside from that, the Philippine government's corruption and incompetence, as well as commonplace problems in the Philippines, have exacerbated the country's already dire poverty situation.
