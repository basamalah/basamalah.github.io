Answer:

While Euclidean geometry seeks to understand the geometry of flat, two-dimensional spaces, non-Euclidean geometry studies curved, rather than flat, surfaces. Although Euclidean geometry is useful in many fields, in some cases, non-Euclidean geometry may be more useful.


2. What is euclidean geometry???

5. Which of the following is an example of using non-Euclidean geometry to solve a real-life problem? A. Determining the shape of the universe B. Designing a rectangular swimming pool C. Calculating the area of a circle D. Building a square pyramid


A. Determining the shape of the universe is an example of using non-Euclidean geometry to solve a real-life problem.

Non-Euclidean geometry is a type of geometry that is based on axioms or assumptions that are different from those used in Euclidean geometry. Euclidean geometry is based on the five postulates of Euclid, which describe the properties of points, lines, and angles in two-dimensional and three-dimensional space. However, in non-Euclidean geometry, these postulates are modified or replaced with other assumptions, leading to different geometric properties and theorems.

One of the most famous examples of non-Euclidean geometry is the study of the shape of the universe. In Euclidean geometry, space is assumed to be flat, meaning that parallel lines never intersect. However, in non-Euclidean geometry, space can have a different curvature, leading to different properties of parallel lines. By using non-Euclidean geometry, scientists have been able to study the shape and size of the universe and make predictions about its behavior.

Therefore, option A is the correct answer.

8. how does euclidean geometry work


Answer:

Euclidean geometry is an axiomatic system, in which all theorems ("true statements") are derived from a small number of simple axioms. Until the advent of non-Euclidean geometry, these axioms were considered to be obviously true in the physical world, so that all the theorems would be equally true.

17. answer of 84 and 112 in Euclidean Algortihm​


ANSWER:

GCF(84, 112) = 28

Solution:

Set up a division problem where a is larger than b.

a ÷ b = c with remainder R. Do the division. Then replace a with b, replace b with R and repeat the division. Continue the process until R = 0.

112 ÷ 84 = 1 R 28 (112 = 1 × 84 + 28)

84 ÷ 28 = 3 R 0 (84 = 3 × 28 + 0)

When remainder R = 0, the GCF is the divisor, b, in the last equation. GCF = 28

Answer:

parallel postulate, One of the five postulates, or axioms, of Euclid underpinning Euclidean geometry. It states that through any given point not on a line there passes exactly one line parallel to that line in the same plane.

28. Use the Euclidean Algorithm to find the GCF 20 and 350​


GCF(20, 350) = 10

Solution

Set up a division problem where a is larger than b.

a ÷ b = c with remainder R. Do the division.

Then replace a with b, replace b with R and repeat the division.

Continue the process until R = 0.

350 ÷ 20 = 17 R 10    (350 = 17 × 20 + 10)

20 ÷ 10 = 2 R 0    (20 = 2 × 10 + 0)

When remainder R = 0, the GCF is the divisor, b, in the last equation. GCF = 10
