6. a airline flies from manila to davao with stop over in cebu. it charges a fare of P 1, 800 manila to cebu and a fare of P3, 500 from manila to davao in one flight, a total of 180 passengers boreded the plane in manila and the collected fare totaled P 494, 000. how many passengers got off the plane in cebu?​


Answer:

80 passengers

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x & y be the no. of passengers from Manila to Cebu and Manila to Davao respectively.

Eq. 1: 1800x + 3500y = 494000

Eq. 2: x + y = 180

We need to determine the number of passengers that got off the plane in Cebu (x).

By substitution,

1800x + 3500(180 - x) = 494000

1800x + 630000 - 3500x = 494000

136000 = 1700x

=> x = 80, it follows that y = 100.

Thus, 80 passengers got off the plane in Cebu.

10. What are the relative and vicinal location of Manila, Cebu and Davao?​


Answer:

Manila, Cebu, and Davao are cities in the Philippines.

Relative location refers to the location of a place in relation to other places. Manila is located north of Cebu and east of Davao. Cebu is located south of Manila and east of Davao. Davao is located west of both Manila and Cebu.

Vicinal location refers to the immediate surroundings of a place. Manila is located in a bay area and surrounded by several smaller islands. Cebu is located on the eastern coast of the island of Cebu and is surrounded by smaller islands as well. Davao is located in a gulf area and surrounded by several smaller islands too.

Michael scheduled a trip to cebu on Christmas season. The plane flew at an average rate of 480 km/h and after travelling 2 850 km, his plane touched down. What was michael's travel time from manila to cebu?

SOLUTION:

• In the problem, the average rate or what we call the speed and distance travelled are known while the time taken is not given.

• We'll use the formula [tex] \rm Speed=Distance÷Time[/tex] to solve for the unknown quantity.

[tex] \rm Speed=Distance÷Time[/tex]

[tex] \rm 480 \: \frac{km}{h} = 2850 \: km \div t[/tex]

[tex] \rm \frac{480 \: km}{h} = \frac{ 2850 \: km } t \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm 480t \: km = 2850 \: kmh[/tex]

[tex] \rm \frac{480t \: km}{480 \: km} = \frac{2850 \: kmh}{480 \: km} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \huge \boxed{ \large \rm t = 5.94 \: h}[/tex]

28. 5. The ferry from Manila left on Tuesday at 1:30 p.m. and arrived in Puerto Princesa, Cebu at 6:30 p.m. the next day. How long was the trip? ​


Answer:

The trip from Manila to Puerto Princesa, Cebu took longer than one day, so we need to calculate the total number of hours between the departure and arrival times.

The ferry left Manila on Tuesday at 1:30 p.m. and arrived in Puerto Princesa, Cebu at 6:30 p.m. the next day.

To calculate the total duration of the trip, we can subtract the departure time from the arrival time.

6:30 p.m. (the arrival time) minus 1:30 p.m. (the departure time) equals 5 hours.

Since the trip took longer than one day, we need to add 24 hours (one full day) to the result we just obtained.

5 hours + 24 hours = 29 hours

Therefore, the total duration of the trip from Manila to Puerto Princesa, Cebu was 29 hours is the answer
