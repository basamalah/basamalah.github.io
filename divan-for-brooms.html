19. Do natural broom bristles(broomcorn) still have proteins after transferring it to a broom?​


Answer:

It is not likely that natural broom bristles (also known as broomcorn) would retain proteins after being transferred to a broom.

Explanation:

Protein molecules are typically quite large and complex, and they are not particularly resistant to degradation by physical or chemical means. As such, it is unlikely that the process of transferring broomcorn to a broom would preserve any proteins that may have been present in the bristles. Additionally, the bristles of a broom are typically subjected to a variety of conditions (including regular use, exposure to water, and cleaning agents) that would further break down any proteins that may have been present. It is possible that small amounts of proteins may still be present in the bristles of a natural broom, but the amount would likely be very small and would not have any significant effect on the broom's performance or durability.
