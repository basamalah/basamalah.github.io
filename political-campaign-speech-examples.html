7. example of a simple campaign speech for presidential.​


A Presidential campaign speech example.

In making a campaign speech text for the president, the important parts that we must include are:

In the opening sentence, greet the audience.Talking about things that concern you as a presidential candidateExpress your vision, mission, and plans to lead the country in the future.

Good morning everyone.

I thank God for allowing us to meet today and I also want to thank everyone who is willing to come today.

Recently, I can feel that economic and technological changes require us to always be able to adapt quickly in order to remain a country that is able to compete on an international scale. And this certainly requires a lot of improvements and also cooperation with various parties. In this regard, my main vision in running the government is to make our country a developed country and able to compete in the international world by implementing the mission of working with all member countries in implementing constructive policies, improving the quality of human resources, creating a clean, effective and trusted government, and keep maintain the noble values and culture that we have.

Why do I want to be president of the Philippines? I think there is no best answer other than that I really want this country to be a developed and safe country for all people. Therefore, I hope that we can all work together, hand in hand, to build a better country in the future. Because I believe that a good country can be realized with good cooperation between the government and the people, and I really appreciate the voice of every people.

Thank you.

Speech Campaign: Public Information Officer

Good morning to all of our professors, classmates, fellow students, and candidates. One thought immediately sprang to mind when I was selected to run for Public Information Officer in Supreme Student Government. "Great responsibility goes along with great power." "Spiderman did cross my thoughts, yes. That's correct! Possession of a position entails both tremendous privilege and considerable responsibility. As the work suggests, accurate information should be spread from us to your end, advocating true, just, and fair information and denouncing false information.

If given the opportunity, I will support my fellow officers in all their undertakings as your public information officer and stand by their side. Additionally, two-way communication will also be implemented through listening to the voices of you, my classmates, and fellow students.

By doing this, I hope to earn your support as your SPG Public Information Officer for the 2019–2020 academic year and to demonstrate my ability to uphold the faith you have placed in me. As I see it, let's work together to create goodness within each other and find the silver lining.

Good morning once again!

Answer:

In 1965, Ferdinand Marcos won the presidential election and became the 10th President of the Philippines. His first term was marked with increased industrialization and the creation of solid infrastructures nationwide, such as the North Luzon Expressway and the Maharlika Highway.

President Marcos imposed martial law on the nation from 1972 to 1981 to suppress increasing civil strife and the threat of a communist takeover following a series of bombings in Manila.

When he declared the martial law in 1972, Marcos claimed that he had done so in response to the "communist threat" posed by the newly founded Communist Party of the Philippines (CPP), and the sectarian "rebellion" of the Mindanao Independence Movement (MIM).

He ruled as a dictator under martial law from 1972 until 1981 and kept most of his martial law powers until he was deposed in 1986, branding his rule as "constitutional authoritarianism" under his New Society Movement.
