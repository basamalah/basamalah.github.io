19. wind beneath my wings(song) what is the song all about?​


Answer:

"Wind Beneath My Wings" is a heartfelt ballad about a person's gratitude and admiration for someone who has supported them throughout their life. The song expresses feelings of appreciation and indebtedness towards that special person who has always been there, offering encouragement, guidance, and love. The lyrics compare this person to the wind beneath the singer's wings, keeping them aloft and enabling them to soar to new heights. The song is a tribute to the power of love and the impact that a supportive person can have on someone's life.

23. Texture of my heart will go on by celine dion ​


Answer:

Musically, this change in attitude is signaled through the form, melody, harmony, tone color, dynamics, texture, tempo, and rhythm.The form of this song is a modification of a standard verse-chorus form. After an introduction, we hear the verse.In the verse, the melody is heard two times, with a different text each time.

The chorus appears immediately after the verse and features a different melodic line that is heard twice with two different texts. After a bridge, we hear another verse (same melody, different text) and another statement of the chorus (same melody, same text). Another bridge leads into a final statement of the chorus which ends the song.

This final chorus is remarkable in that the text is different from the two preceding choruses. The use of the same chorus melody for a different text highlights the different standpoints of the speaker from the beginning of the song to the end.Two distinct melodies are used in this song, one for the verses and one for the chorus. The verse melody does not contain a lot of melodic motion; rather, it consists largely of repeated notes with changes in pitch to emphasize words.

29. the prayer celine dion melody​


"The Prayer" is a song by Canadian singer Celine Dion and Italian tenor Andrea Bocelli. It was written by David Foster, Carole Bayer Sager, Alberto Testa and Tony Renis. "The Prayer" was originally recorded in two solo versions for the 1998 film Quest for Camelot, in English by Dion and in Italian by Bocelli. A duet between Dion and Bocelli later appeared on their respective studio albums, These Are Special Times (1998) and Sogno (1999), and was released as a promotional single on 1 March 1999. "The Prayer" won a Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song and was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Original Song in 1999 and a Grammy Award for Best Pop Collaboration with Vocals in 2000. It received positive reviews from music critics and entered adult contemporary charts in Canada and the United States in 1999. In 2008, Dion released a live version of "The Prayer" as a duet with Josh Groban. This music download entered the Canadian Hot 100 at number 37 and the Billboard Hot 100 at number 70.
