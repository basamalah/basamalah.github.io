11. what is monopolistic competition? ​


Answer:

Monopolistic competition is a type of imperfect competition such that there are many producers competing against each other, but selling products that are differentiated from one another and hence are not perfect substitutes

Answer:

Monopolistic competition is a type of imperfect competition such that there are many producers competing against each other, but selling products that are differentiated from one another (e.g. by branding or quality) and hence are not perfect substitutes. In monopolistic competition, a firm takes the prices charged by its rivals as given and ignores the impact of its own prices on the prices of other firms.

21. halimbawa ng monopolistic competition roleplay​


Answer:

Imagine there are many clothing stores in a mall that sell similar products such as shirts, jeans, and pants, but each store has its unique style and designs. For example, one store may specialize in trendy streetwear, another in formal attire and another in vintage clothing. Consumers have a variety of options to choose from, but each store has a degree of control over the prices they charge. The competition among the stores is based on factors such as style, design, and quality, rather than just price. This is an example of monopolistic competition.

27. monopolistic competition


WHAT IS MONOPOLISTIC COMPETITION ?

Monopolistic competition characterizes an industry in which many firms offer products or services that are similar, but not perfect substitutes. Barriers to entry and exit in a monopolistic competitive industry are low, and the decisions of any one firm do not directly affect those of its competitors. Monopolistic competition is closely related to the business strategy of brand differentiation
