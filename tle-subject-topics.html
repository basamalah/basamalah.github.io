12. Explain the thought, "Hindi bale kanti kita, basta bilis benta." Topic: Entrepreneurship Subject: TLE (Technology Living Education)


The phrase "Hindi bale kanti kita, basta bilis benta" is a colloquial expression in Filipino which means "I don't care if I'm selling to you at a low price, as long as I sell quickly." In the context of entrepreneurship, it is a statement that emphasizes the importance of efficient sales and quick inventory turnover over short-term profit margins.

Entrepreneurs often face the challenge of balancing the need to generate revenue with the need to maintain a sustainable profit margin. While it may be tempting to hold out for higher prices or negotiate harder for better deals, the reality is that inventory that stays on the shelves for too long can eat into profits and impact cash flow.

This is where the concept of "basta bilis benta" comes in. Entrepreneurs who prioritize quick turnover of their inventory are more likely to stay ahead of their competition, maintain a positive cash flow, and minimize the risk of inventory loss due to obsolescence or expiration.

Overall, "Hindi bale kanti kita, basta bilis benta" reflects the entrepreneurial mindset of being agile, adaptable, and focused on the bottom line. By prioritizing efficient sales and inventory turnover, entrepreneurs can build a more sustainable business model and achieve long-term success.
