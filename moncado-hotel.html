28. The number of rooms at Hotel G is 10 less twice the number of rooms at Hotel H. if the total number of rooms at both Hotels is 425, what is the number of rooms at Hotel G?


Answer:

Since G is equal to 10 less than twice H, we get:

G = 2H - 10

Since the total number of rooms at G and H is 425, we get:

G + H = 425

H = 425 - G

Substituting H = 425-G into G = 2H - 10, we get:

G = 2(425-G) - 10

G = 850 - 2G - 10

3G = 840

G = 280
