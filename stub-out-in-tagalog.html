6. the nervous system sends messages back and forth from the body so as for you to react. Number the statements (1-6) below within the correct order to point out how you feeling pain once you stub your toe?


NERVOUS SYSTEM

You stub your toe on a hard object.Pain receptors in the skin of your toe detect the injury and send a signal through sensory neurons to the spinal cord.The signal is relayed through interneurons in the spinal cord to the brainstem.The brainstem processes the signal and sends a response through motor neurons back to the muscles in your toe and foot.The muscles contract and cause you to withdraw your foot from the painful stimulus.Simultaneously, the brain also interprets the sensation of pain and you become aware of the discomfort in your toe.

Further explanation

When you stub your toe, the nerves in your skin, called pain receptors, are stimulated by the impact of your toe hitting a hard object. These pain receptors send a signal, or an electrical impulse, through sensory neurons, which are specialized cells that transmit information from the body to the spinal cord.

The signal then travels up the sensory neurons to the spinal cord, which is a long, tube-like structure that runs from the base of your brain down to your lower back. In the spinal cord, the signal is relayed through interneurons, which are neurons that connect other neurons together, allowing them to communicate with each other.

The signal then continues up the spinal cord to the brainstem, which is the lowest part of the brain. The brainstem receives and processes the sensory information, and sends a response back through motor neurons, which are specialized cells that transmit information from the brain to the muscles and organs in the body.

The motor neurons tell the muscles in your toe and foot to contract, which causes you to withdraw your foot from the painful stimulus. At the same time, the brainstem also interprets the sensation of pain and sends signals to other parts of the brain, such as the thalamus and cortex, where you become aware of the discomfort in your toe and experience the sensation of pain.

So, the nervous system plays a crucial role in allowing you to sense and react to stimuli in your environment, such as the pain of stubbing your toe. By transmitting signals back and forth between your body and your brain, the nervous system helps you to adapt and respond to changes in your environment, ensuring your survival and well-being.

Why does it hurt so much when you stub your toe? When your toe exerts a force on a rock, the rock exerts an equal force back on your toe. The harder you hit your toe against it, the more force the rock exerts back on your toe (and the more your toe hurts).

29. a total of 1,200 stubs of learning packets is to be divided among three barangays A,B and C in the ratio of 2:3:5, how many stubs will barangay c receive​


Answer:

The stubs distributed per barangays that has ratios of 2:3:5 is 240:360:600.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

1,200 stubs

2:3:5 ratio of three barangays

1. Add the ratios, 2 + 3 + 5 = 10

2. Divide the number of stubs to the sum of ratios, 1,200 ÷ 10 = 120

3. Multiply it to the ratios per barangays, 120 × 2 = 240, 120 × 3 = 360, 120 × 5 = 600.

So that the stubs distributed per barangays that has ratios of 2:3:5 is 240:360:600.
