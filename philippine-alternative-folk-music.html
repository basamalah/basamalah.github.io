1. Describe Philippine alternative Folk Music


The Philippine Alternative Folk Music is characterized by its form which is a combination of ethnic instrumentation with an electronic accompaniment. The music usually elicits a thematic outline based on the existing societal and environmental issues. The Kontemporaryong Gamelan Pilipino of which Joey Ayala, Grace Nono, and Edru Abraham are its members who popularized this form of musical genre. Moreover, Freddie Aguilar, Yoyoy Villame, and Gary Granada are also leading Filipino alternative folk musicians known in the country.

Suling

The suling is an end-blown flute that is employed in Indonesian gamelan ensembles. Giant gamelans, which may contain over fifty instruments and include flutes, drums, stringed instruments, large hanging gongs, and ranks of potlike gongs, are used for palace and other major ceremonies.

Sarimanok

The mythological bird known as the Sarimanok has become an icon of Maranao art. It is portrayed as a colorful fowl with a feathered tail and a fish in its mouth or talons. Scroll, leaf, and spiral designs adorn the head in abundance. It's thought to be a sign of good fortune.

Cariñosa

The cariosa, which means "lovely" or "affectionate," is a colonial-era Philippine folk dance from the Maria Clara suite, in which the fan or handkerchief plays an important part in putting the couple in a romantic situation. The cariosa originated on Panay Island and was introduced to the Philippines by the Spaniards during their colonization. It is related to various Spanish dances, such as the bolero, as well as the Mexican jarabe tapatio, or Mexican hat dance.
